# Wilder

- **Role:** Beaver shaman; guardian-watcher of the approach to the blasted lands; voice for the shamans' long plan when the right questions are asked
- **Species:** Beaver (animal-person)
- **Location:** On a hill-top at the edge of the blasted lands — a dwelling he built and has lived in for long enough that the land has come to know him back
- **Status:** Alive; present; always watching

## Summary

Wilder is an old beaver shaman who lives **on a hill-top at the edge of the blasted lands**. The hill is his home; the vantage is deliberate. From it he sees anyone approaching the broken country, and he sees them before they see him. His role — self-assigned over decades, but also the kind of role that picks a shaman rather than the other way round — is to **watch, warn, and (where he can) turn travellers back**.

He calls the country below him **the broken lands**; the formal name elsewhere is *blasted lands*. Same region, different register.

He is not a barrier. He does not stop people who are determined. But he will try to stop people who do not yet know what they are walking into, which is most people, because the blasted lands are what they are (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` for the background).

## What he does when a party arrives

**He notices first.** Unless the party is **deliberately sneaking** — using stealth powers, moving at night, approaching along a concealed route — Wilder will spot them well before they reach his hill, and he will come down to meet them. A party that is deliberately sneaking may slip past him, at the cost of what comes after: he is their best source of warning and guidance for what is inside.

**He warns them.** The blasted lands are not a place a traveller survives by underestimating. Wilder will tell them plainly: what grows here does not live the way living things live elsewhere; the spirits here are different from the spirits a shaman further north has ever negotiated with; the terrain itself will eat an unprepared party. He is not dramatising. He lives here precisely because watching the lands is his work, and he has watched enough travellers fail to return to know the shape of the danger.

**If they press, or ask the right questions, he will tell them more.** Wilder is one of the shamans who has been working on the **long plan** to reopen the portal at the centre of the blasted lands, in a controlled way, to push the chaotic spirits back through it into the ether they came from (see `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"Campaign Threads" → *The shamans' long plan*). A party that comes to him having already grasped *something* — that the chaos-spirit migration is a continental problem, that the desert is being driven by it, that the portal is at the centre of the blasted lands — can draw the plan out of him. A party that asks vaguely (*what are the shamans doing?*) gets vague answers. A party that asks specifically (*can the portal be closed again, and what would it take?*) gets the real story.

He is not gating the plan out of secrecy; he is gating it because it is the work of generations and an underprepared party joining it would be a liability rather than an asset. Ask the questions that show you have been thinking, and he opens up.

## Role in the campaign

Wilder is the **in-world pointer** for two threads at once:

- **The blasted lands as an actionable destination.** Without him, the blasted lands are backdrop — somewhere south, dangerous, where the desert pushes from. With him, they are a place a party can approach deliberately, because there is someone to talk to at the approach who can tell them what they will face and whether they are ready.
- **The shamans' long plan.** Without him, the plan is a threads-table abstraction. With him, a party can ask a specific shaman a specific question and get a specific answer. If the campaign moves toward the shamans' climax, Wilder is most parties' first contact with the shamans as an organised effort.

He can also teach — shaman's work is his life; a party that has earned his respect can learn from him the kinds of things a Luchebert priest, a Mouse-clan priestess, or a druid would teach in their own traditions, with an emphasis on the **spirit-ecology of chaos-affected regions**. See LORE.md §"Spirit Ecology" for the wider framework his teaching sits inside.

## Role as spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," an old shaman — especially one working at the edge of chaos-spirit territory — reads to chaotic spirits as a mighty-adjacent presence, and those spirits back off from the area that shaman holds. Wilder's hill-top, and a modest radius around it, is therefore **a clear-spot on the edge of the blasted lands**: a place where the chaos-pressure eases, which is part of why a traveller can stop at his hill and think clearly before continuing. The further from his hill, the faster that protection fades.

His presence is also part of why the blasted lands' chaos-pressure has not pushed *further* north into Brumal and Allondo faster than it has. He is one of several shamans holding the line; losing him (to age, accident, a chaos-spirit encounter) thins the cover at this part of the frontier.

## Relationships

- Part of the **shamans** as a loose body of practitioners working on the long plan to close the portal. How organised that body is, how they communicate, and who else is in it are DM-fill.
- A fellow old shaman of **Gerhald** (see `doc/npcs/hermit_shaman.md`), though of different species and very different post — both hold difficult territory where most practitioners would not live. Whether they have met, know of each other, or would recognise one another as allies is open. They would almost certainly get on if they did.
- Natural potential ally to **Felicia** (through her hill-country arrangements) and to **Diederik** (druid, ascetic, also holding a spirit-shield post), though none of them have likely ever spoken directly.
