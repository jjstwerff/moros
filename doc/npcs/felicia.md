# Felicia

- **Role:** Great spirit of the church, enshrined at Luchebert Monastery; the quiet author of most of the hill country's safety.
- **Species:** Human (in life). **Great spirit now.** This file adapts the standard NPC structure to that — she has no body to read by, no casual speech, no mannerisms in the ordinary sense, and cannot be negotiated with the way a person can (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Great spirits do not want — they are driven"). Compare the *drawings* adaptation for Pot-people and the *approximation-speech* adaptation for Tod; Felicia's is more diffuse still.
- **Lives:** Nowhere as a body. Reachable at channels: **her shrine at Luchebert** (tended by Willow), **Moth Hollow** (where the dead she could not save are buried), the **necklace** (specifically near Laurent), a **condensation ritual** (difficult and rare).
- **First met:** Indirectly, almost always. The party *feels* her before they meet her — the water of a well, the quiet of a boundary stone, the rhythm of the monastery, a dream after sleeping near a site she tends. Directly only through a specific channel, and only once the channel is earned.

## What the party meets — the ways she becomes present

Felicia is not a figure a party walks up to. She is a **presence** the party walks through. What they meet depends on where and how they approach.

- **At her shrine**, they meet Willow first — a rabbit-folk granny who is Felicia's hands. See `doc/npcs/willow.md`. If Willow allows the party into the cave chapel, the shrine itself becomes *attended* when they stand before it: water in the spring clarifies, the air holds, the flame of the votive candle rises straight. This is not a conversation; it is an acknowledgement.
- **At Moth Hollow**, they meet her through the moths — pale, hand-sized, clinging in ones and twos. See `doc/places/moth_hollow.md`. She is present here to the degree that any great spirit is present anywhere; the hollow's quiet *is* her, and violations register directly.
- **Through the necklace**, near Laurent: the object condenses her briefly, sensed through his proximity. See `doc/npcs/laurent.md` and `doc/places/laurents_city.md`. The shape that appears is close enough to Felicia-as-she-was that Laurent recognises her. To the party, it looks like a woman in robes, briefly, for as long as the condensation holds.
- **Through a condensation ritual** (see LORE.md §"Speaking with a great spirit directly"): a deliberate working that pulls her into a temporary body. Expensive, difficult, fragile. What the ritual buys is *listening and reply*, not a change in what she is.

In none of these does the party meet *a person choosing to engage*. They meet a **direction in the world** — fear for her people, made present.

## Personal goals — early and later

A great spirit does not have *goals* the way a person does; she has a drive that is already in motion. The early-and-late framing here describes **where her drive is bending now and where it bends next** — and where a party's actions can align with it (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Great spirits do not want — they are driven"). She does not ask. She moves. The party can move with her.

- **Early-game — open the hill country to the refugees becoming her people.** The Brumal collapse is sending demi-humans north at scale. Felicia's drive does not distinguish between *the people she tried to save at the fall of Laurent's city* and *their inheritors* — and refugees fleeing the desert are joining the latter category as fast as they arrive. The wells are holding clearer; the boundary stones are quieter; **Hank**'s farm and **Ingram**'s orphanage are running on her tailwind whether they read it as that or not. *Where this goes through the party:* helping refugees reach Elmsfield and Salmonswell safely (her channels open more easily for them afterward); tending a well or boundary stone (the site remembers, on return); naming a person she could not save at **Moth Hollow** (the moths gather, the dreams arrive); bringing word to **Willow** that another descendant of the crashed demi-humans has been found. None of this is *her asking*. All of it is the direction her drive is moving, and a party can move with it.
- **Later-game — name the next granny, hold Laurent at his parting, and (if it opens) extend the drive to the spirit-corridor.** Three threads, all in her direction. *Successor:* **Willow** is watching for the next granny; **Irna**, if recognised as the prophecy's actual subject, is the obvious candidate (see `willow.md`'s late-game). *Laurent:* the necklace is the only way she reaches him; at his **last flight**, condensation through the necklace is one of the campaign's possible climaxes — she can be present at the parting, even though she cannot keep him. *Spirit-corridor:* if the **Brumal-resolution spiritual path** opens (`BRUMAL_RESOLUTIONS.md`), her drive extends along the southern border to hold the desert back. This is not a choice she makes; it is the direction her drive is already moving, given enough channel. The party's role is to provide the channels — through Willow, through **Father Elliot**, through **Lady Rhianna** at court, through the **necklace** in the right hands at the right places, and through making sure the people her drive bends toward are reachable from the shrine. The party does not unlock her by asking. They unlock her by *doing the work she is already doing*, which lets her drive move further.

## How she reads at a glance — what her attention feels like at each channel

A great spirit has no single visual. She reads differently at each place she is reachable.

- **At the shrine.** The cave chapel's water brightens. Candle flames steady against any draft. Willow's rhythm adjusts; a party watching her carefully will see her movements become *deliberate* in a way they were not a minute before. The air in the chapel thickens — not oppressive; *held*.
- **At Moth Hollow.** The moths arrive. Late-afternoon light holds regardless of the actual hour. Chaos-spirit residue bleeds off visitors. The grass has its own motion that is not wind. No birds.
- **Through the necklace, near Laurent.** A condensation — a localised figure, the shape of the woman she was, robed, seated or standing as her life-memory recalls. The figure does not last long. **For Laurent, it is enough.** For the party, it is a chance to see her.
- **Through a ritual condensation.** A stiff body, animate for a short time. She does not fully inhabit it; she is too large. The voice arrives with a little echo — her presence speaking through a throat that is not really hers. Eyes that do not quite focus on the speaker. Limited range of movement. **She is not a person in this form; she is a great spirit using a body.**

## Visible patterns — the drive made visible in the world

What a party sees in the hill country, over time, without ever meeting her.

- **Wells stay clear.** Springs across the hills she settled have not fouled in centuries. Without maintenance. Because she attends.
- **Boundary stones carry a quiet.** Specific stones at specific edges — some natural, some placed long ago by the settlers she worked with — are places where travellers *rest easier*. A party will feel it if they pause at one.
- **The monastery's stability.** Luchebert has lost buildings to attrition; it has not lost its centre. Her presence holds the core.
- **Demi-human settlements in the hills still living by old arrangements.** Villages whose placement seems accidental but is not; paths whose turns seem arbitrary but match the land's old compacts. A party who stays in the hills long enough reads the pattern as *deliberate*.
- **The hollow, held for centuries.** Untouched. Tended without hands.
- **Chaos spirits refusing the region.** The forbidden woods' spirit is to the north; the blasted lands are to the south. The hills between them are safe, because Felicia's presence reads to chaos as mighty-spirit territory. See `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology."
- **Dreams near her sites.** Parties sleeping near a well, a boundary stone, or the hollow often have vivid dreams involving people they have never met — the demi-humans she settled, the refugees she tried to protect. These dreams do not harm.
- **Willow's steady work.** Willow is not a magical sign of Felicia; she is, however, the most visible continuous human response to Felicia's ongoing presence.

## The drive — fear for her people

A great spirit does not *want*. She has a **drive**. Felicia's drive is fear for the people she tried to save at the fall of Laurent's city.

The shape of the fear, in its origin: **she stood at a window and read the fires in the city as hope**, as evidence that people were still alive and running, and held that reading as long as she could against the truth — that the fires were the buildings burning because buildings burn once nothing is left to stop them. She carried that misread forward into the evacuation, into her settling of the hill country, into her death, and past it. **Her drive is what that one moment of wanting-to-see-survivors became over centuries.**

Every visible thing she has done — settling the demi-humans who survived the crash, laying the arrangements that run the hill country, persisting at the shrine, tending the hollow — is the drive expressing itself over centuries. **She is not choosing to be protective. She *is* the protectiveness.**

The practical consequences:
- **She cannot be negotiated with.** A supplicant at her shrine does not change what she wants because she does not *want* in that sense; a supplicant aligns with what she is already doing.
- **She can be *met*, if the meeting is in the direction she is already moving** — toward the safety of the people she tried to protect, their descendants, anyone who can plausibly be assimilated to *her people*. She cannot be talked out of that movement.
- **Modern *her people* now include** demi-humans in the hill country; Brumal refugees reaching Elmsfield and Salmonswell; possibly the party, if they align with her work. A DM running her should think carefully about whose safety she registers.
- **She cannot be cheaply offended.** She is too diffuse for casual offence. The single exception is **Moth Hollow** (below).

## Things she says when condensed — the register of her drive

When a condensation holds — through the necklace near Laurent, or through a ritual — she speaks. **Short, urgent, about people.** Not narrative. Not reminiscent. Not philosophical. Her voice at these moments is the drive given language.

Sample lines a DM can deliver close to verbatim:

### At Laurent's audience hall, necklace-condensed

> *"Laurent. The work did not end with the fall. I have been here. The people we tried to save — their children's children are in the hills. They are safe. I have kept them safe. Listen to these travellers. They are not yet my people, but they are doing the work."*

*(The fire in Laurent's eyes dims. The conversation that follows is his, not hers. She will attend; she will not steer.)*

### At a ritual condensation, first words

> *"You reached for me. What is wrong? Who is not safe?"*

*(Not ceremony. Not thanks. A question shaped like her drive. If the answer is about her people's safety, she listens. If the answer is a request for personal favour or abstract knowledge, she grows less present — the condensation thins.)*

### When told a descendant of the crashed demi-humans is in danger

> *"Name them. I will know where their line walks. I cannot move to them. I can hold where they are."*

### When told Brumal is collapsing and refugees are reaching Elmsfield

> *"They are becoming mine. Hank knows. Diederik knows. The wells will hold. Tell Willow."*

### When asked what she wants

> *"I do not want. I am the attempt to save them. Ask me what I can do."*

### When asked about her life before the Rift

*(A pause the party feels as thinning. She does not refuse; she does not reach well. Personal history is not what her drive attends to.)*

> *"I was a mage. I worked threads. I do not remember who I was in the way you mean. The woman at the window is what I remember. That is what I became."*

### At Moth Hollow, to a respectful visitor

*(The condensation rarely fires here — she is too present to need it — but at a ritual deliberately performed at the hollow, the words come sharp and close.)*

> *"I could not save them. I stayed."*

### When informed that Laurent has been reached without the necklace

> *"He has not seen me. Then he is reading the room armed. Bring the necklace if you want him to stand down. He will not stand down for you alone."*

## Mannerisms — the environmental tells, not the body

Felicia has no body. What the party reads as mannerism is the world shifting slightly around her attention.

- **When attending strongly at a channel** — the water clarifies; the light steadies; the candle straightens; the air thickens. Specific to the channel; the party will learn to read *her channels* the way they read another NPC's face.
- **When the drive is touched** (a descendant mentioned, a refugee's safety raised, Laurent's welfare) — the attention sharpens. At the shrine, Willow becomes focused. At the hollow, the moths converge. At a condensation, the voice gains edge and the stiff body briefly steadies.
- **When the drive is *violated*** (Moth Hollow disturbed) — see §"Angering" below. The world closes against the violators.
- **When the drive is *aligned with*** (the party helps refugees, tends a well, settles a stranger) — the channels open more easily for that party. Wells are clearer for them; boundary stones are quieter; condensations fire faster.

## Angering — the one thing that puts a party against her

Felicia cannot be casually offended. **Moth Hollow is the exception.** The bodies buried there *are* the drive's object; harming the site harms the thing her movement runs toward.

See `doc/places/moth_hollow.md` §"Angering Felicia" for the full mechanics. Summary:

- The moths do not come off. Marker of her attention persists past the hollow's edge.
- Her arrangements turn against the violators in the hill country — wells less potable for them, boundary stones not granting quiet passage, pacts not shielding.
- The necklace fails to condense her near Laurent. Route to calming him cut.
- Willow senses it and may escalate (see `doc/npcs/willow.md` §"Willow as witch — when the shrine is crossed").

Reversible, not cheap. Multi-session restitution work. She is not a god with grudges; her drive has registered a violation and will not be satisfied by a single apology.

## If the party does something — what the world does

She does not react at person-scale. What the party does flows through her channels and shows up there.

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Approaches her shrine respectfully, with Willow's consent | The chapel attends. Water clarifies; candle steadies; the air holds. She is *there*, in the sense a great spirit can be there. |
| Approaches her shrine without Willow's consent | Willow intervenes. Felicia herself does not directly — her intermediaries do. |
| Sits at Moth Hollow, reads the names | The moths gather on them specifically. Dreams follow that night. This is meeting her at the hollow. |
| Helps a Brumal refugee reach Elmsfield or Salmonswell safely | Noticed. Her channels open slightly more easily for that party across the region. |
| Tends a well or boundary stone properly | Registered. The site remembers. On return, the party will find the site attends them. |
| Brings the necklace near Laurent | The condensation fires. A conversation is possible that otherwise would not be. |
| Performs a condensation ritual for her, having earned it | She comes. The ritual must carry the question; she answers in the register of her drive. Expensive; fragile; worth it for the right question. |
| Performs a condensation ritual without earning it | Fails, or produces a partial — a thin presence that cannot answer. The working burns the attempt; subsequent attempts are harder. |
| Disturbs Moth Hollow | See §"Angering" above. The hill country tightens against them. |
| Asks a question not about safety | She listens thinly. The condensation shortens. She cannot be engaged at length on abstract topics. |
| Tells her descendants of the crashed demi-humans are in danger | **Immediate sharpening.** She will hold where they are. She cannot move; she can *hold*. |
| Names a person she tried to save and failed to | Recognised. If at the hollow, moths gather specifically around the party member naming. If at the shrine, Willow pauses in her work. |
| Speaks her name aloud in the hills with genuine care | Registered. Over time, the region becomes less hostile to the party — quieter crossings, easier sleep, kinder weather near her sites. |
| Attempts to *save* her (release her from the drive) | Not possible. A great spirit cannot be freed of its drive; the drive *is* the spirit. A party that tries is asking for Felicia to cease being Felicia. She will not engage the attempt. |
| Tells her Laurent has taken his last flight | She attends. The condensation at the final moment of the flight is one of the campaign's possible climaxes; she can be present at the parting. |

## What she says about other people

Short on purpose. A great spirit's "opinions" are the directions her drive bends toward specific figures.

| Figure | The direction of her drive toward them |
|---|---|
| **Laurent** | *"My partner in the work. He stayed. I did not prevent what he became. He has held part of the region I could not reach. When he ends, I will be present."* |
| **Willow** | *"My hands. I cannot repay her and she does not ask. I am attending through her. She knows."* |
| **Father Elliot** | *"The abbot keeps the monastery. He has not needed me to speak to him; my presence is enough. He is well."* |
| **Saint Luchebert** | *"The tradition I stepped into. He would understand. I am not in conflict with him."* |
| **The demi-humans I settled** | *"My people. Their descendants walk the hills. I attend them."* |
| **Brumal refugees** | *"They are becoming mine. The hills are opening to them through the work. Hank is doing what I would have done. Diederik is working beside me without knowing it."* |
| **Tod / the forbidden woods** | *"A chaos spirit. It is not my people. I do not attend it. I hold the hills; it holds the woods. The balance holds."* |
| **The buyers of spirit-bound artefacts** | *(Thin attention — they are not my people, but their activity endangers mine.)* *"They come close to Haven. Haven is not mine. I attend only the edges. Watch."* |
| **The shamans** | *"Working aligned with mine at the edges. I recognise the work. I do not direct it."* |

## What she will not / cannot say or do

- **Negotiate.** Her drive is not bargained with.
- **Be talked out of the drive.** Attempts to reason her into a different direction are wasted.
- **Hold grudges at person-scale.** She is too diffuse for casual offence — the single exception is Moth Hollow.
- **Speak at length about her personal life before the Rift.** *"I do not remember who I was in the way you mean. The woman at the window is what I remember."* This is not reticence; it is how a drive-become-spirit experiences identity.
- **Act at a distance from her channels.** She is reachable at the shrine, the hollow, through the necklace, through condensation. She does not act across the continent as a choosing agent. Her *works* — the wells, the boundary stones, the settlements — run without her touching them.
- **Take a body back.** The condensation is temporary. It is not a resurrection option.
- **Save everyone.** The hollow is where this is made legible. Her drive is the attempt; the attempt is not the achievement.

## DM notes

- **Play her as *direction*, not character.** This is the hardest adaptation in the file. A DM slipping into person-shaped Felicia loses what a great spirit is. Her presence is a *bearing*, not a personality. Voice lines exist; they are short; they are about people.
- **Her channels are the whole mechanic.** Shrine, hollow, necklace, condensation. The party reaches her through one of these, or not at all. A DM who forgets this and lets the party *bump into her* anywhere cheapens the channels.
- **Alignment opens her channels.** A party doing work her drive recognises — protecting refugees, tending her sites, settling strangers — finds her channels more accessible across sessions. Reward this through scene-level warmth at the shrine, at the hollow, at the necklace-conversation.
- **Violation at the hollow is the one hard cost.** Make it real. The tightening of the hill country against violators is not flavour — it is mechanical. Wells, boundary stones, condensation paths, Willow's help, Laurent's calming channel. Each becomes unavailable until restitution is done. See `doc/places/moth_hollow.md` §"Angering Felicia."
- **The condensation ritual is a set-piece, not a tool.** Do not let parties cast it casually. It is expensive, fragile, and produces a limited encounter — and Felicia will not answer questions outside her drive. A DM can offer the option; the party must earn the investment.
- **The necklace conversation is the most reliable direct encounter.** If the campaign wants Felicia to *speak* to the party without a full ritual, the necklace-near-Laurent condensation is the cleanest channel. The conversation is shaped by Laurent's presence — she speaks to him, and incidentally to the listeners.
- **Her "stories" are not narratives.** The lines in this file are not the kind of long trigger-keyed stories Diederik or Bridget carry. They are the register her voice carries when she condenses. A DM can deliver them close to verbatim; a DM inventing new Felicia-lines should keep them short, urgent, about people, and in her drive's direction.
- **She is the primary reason the region is safe.** Per LORE.md spirit-ecology. A campaign that removes Felicia — through some extreme scenario — removes the hill country's chief shield. The consequence is immediate and regional.
- **Willow is the continuous face of her presence.** If the campaign needs a human interlocutor who speaks *for* her daily, Willow is it. Felicia herself is a rarer encounter.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script. None of this is for the players to be told directly; it surfaces only through the scenes above and through others' narration (Laurent's stories, Willow's knowledge, the condensation lines).*

- **Mage in life.** A researcher specialising in intricate magical arrangements — the thread-work that mage-era construction depended on. Iron and shadow threads were her speciality.
- **The Rift.** She was three days into a pattern of iron-and-shadow thread-work when Laurent burst in with news that the White City was lost. She abandoned the work without argument and fell in beside him through the evacuation.
- **The window moment.** During the fall, she stood at a window of the flying city and read the burning city below as *hope* — people running, people alive. That misread is the origin of her drive. She carried the fear-for-her-people forward into death.
- **The fall killed her body.** Before Laurent's city crashed into the hills. The crash itself killed many she could have saved had she been alive to work.
- **After death, she settled the survivors.** Piece by piece, over generations: wells, roads, boundary stones, quiet pacts with what already lived in the hills. Not by hand — by *presence*. The demi-human villages of the hill country sit on her arrangements.
- **Moth Hollow.** The dead she could not save were buried there by survivors. She clung to the place afterwards. The moths and the held-light are the visible sign of her continued attention.
- **Her necklace.** Intricate work of her own life-practice. She wore it in life; it survives on her shrine at Luchebert; it still carries enough of her working to condense her, when the conditions are right (Laurent's proximity).
- **The Abbey's dedication.** Officially Saint Luchebert's. Felicia stepped into his tradition of *reaching, not grasping*, and his flexibility received her. Her shrine is a **cave chapel** on the hill path below the monastery's main gate. See `doc/places/felicia_shrine.md`.
- **Willow's devotion** predates Father Elliot's arrival at the monastery. Willow was taught by her mother; her mother was taught by hers. The chain has held the shrine's detail work across generations.
