# Laurent

- **Role:** Former mage of the White City; the man whose flying castle carried Rift survivors; now a demi-lich in the wreck of his own crashed flying city.
- **Species:** Human in life. Demi-lich now — preserved, diminished, patient.
- **Lives:** The crashed flying city, in the hills near the Lake of Tears. Bound to it; cannot leave. (For the city as a place — the wreck's layout, the alcove puppets, Felicia's preserved room — see `doc/places/laurents_city.md`.)
- **First met:** When the party reaches him — late mid-campaign, usually. He does not come to them. They have to come through the wreck to reach him, with or without the necklace.

## What the party meets

The party has crossed Corné's hills (or another route), reached the wreck, and pressed in past the alcove puppets. The deeper they go, the more *kept* the wreck looks — the central halls are not random ruin but something tended, by something. They reach what was once an audience hall.

A figure is seated in the chair at the back of the hall.

He is dried, preserved, robed in cloth that has held its shape past any natural span. **His eyes hold actual light** — small flames, steady. He looks at the party for a long moment. The fire in his hands has not been thrown yet; he is deciding.

If they have the **necklace**, the fire goes out before they have spoken. The puppets in the alcoves still. The conversation that follows is *different from any they had crossing the wreck.*

If they don't, he will speak first. *"You came in through my puppets. State your business and I will decide whether to throw fire at you again."* He says this without raising his voice. He has said it before. The words have been waiting.

## How he reads at a glance

- **A demi-lich.** Dried; preserved; robed in cloth from a vanished court. He does not move except when he chooses to. The stillness is total when it holds.
- **Eyes that hold actual light.** Small flames, steady. They are part of how he sees and part of how he warns.
- **Hand gestures still mage-trained.** Precise. When he speaks at length, the hands move with the pre-rift discipline of a working — not casting; remembering the shape.
- **A voice that should not work.** Works. The breath he uses is not breath. The party will adjust to it within a few sentences and then notice it again at the end of every speech.
- **The hall is preserved around him** more carefully than the rest of the wreck. Dust controlled. Cloth maintained. The room is *kept*; the wreck is not.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **He does not stand.** Across the visit, he remains seated. Standing belongs to a body that needs blood; he has put that aside.
- **The puppets in the alcoves twitch when his attention shifts.** Not always — only when something is rising in him. A party watching the alcoves can read his mood from the wood.
- **The fire in his eyes dims when he speaks of Felicia.** This is the most reliable tell of warmth he has.
- **He tracks the wreck around him.** A party that does something elsewhere in the city while he is talking — opens a door, lifts a panel — he knows. He may not stop them; he knows.

## At the audience hall (the wreck's centre, his throne)

The room he holds. Three things stand out:

- **The chair he is in is the chair he was in at the city's height.** Original; preserved. The wood has not aged because he has not allowed it to. The dais is cracked from the fall; the chair sits straight on it anyway.
- **Felicia's empty seat to his right.** Set as it was. Cushion intact. A place at his side that has been waiting for centuries. A party that asks about it gets the *what Felicia did* story.
- **The alcoves around the walls** — wooden puppets standing motionless. They do not move while the party is welcome. They will if the welcome ends.

For Felicia's preserved room (a separate scene location elsewhere in the wreck), see `doc/places/laurents_city.md` §"Felicia's room."

## What he will tell — and when

| Topic | When he'll talk |
|---|---|
| The party's business — why they came | Immediately. He decides whether to throw fire on the answer. |
| His downfall — the towers, the elemental, the fall | **Freely once asked.** The narration is long, agitated, complete. |
| Felicia, in any aspect | Yes — and the fire dims. |
| The faction that brought him down | Yes; with fury. |
| The current overseas mages (heirs of that faction) | Yes; with cooler fury. He distinguishes them from the original. |
| What he wants now | Yes. He has thought about it for centuries. |
| The last-flight option (if the party broaches it) | Yes — and the conversation changes. |
| His own working, the one that made him demi-lich | He will not describe the specific working. He will say *what* he became. |
| His life before the Rift | Sparingly. The personal life is ash. He keeps what he keeps. |
| The skull gatherers | He will speak of them. They have not approached. He has views on why. |

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the burning of the White City — *the pledge*

> *"I stood at the foot of my castle with the city on fire behind me. The mages who could still cast were trying to and failing — the rift was breaking workings the moment they finished them. I could only walk. So I walked, and I shouted that my castle would carry as many as could reach it, and that *every* refugee was welcome — humans, animal-people, the people the mage leadership had spent generations treating as fixtures. I said it because it was true. I said it because saying it loudly meant the leadership could not undo it. They never did. That pledge is the only thing about me history kept correctly."*

### When asked about the slow descent — *the towers I noticed too late*

> *"I noticed the towers ringing the lake before I knew what they were doing. I was carrying refugees; I was busy. By the time I understood that the towers were *for* my elemental — that they were *taking* it from me — the cage was nearly closed. I tried to fly the city further. The lift faltered. I tried to set it down. The lift stopped. The city went down hard, into the hills, where the wreck still is. The faction that built the cage got everything they wanted from the engineering. Felicia got everything they wanted from the people."*

### When asked about Felicia — *what she did from beyond*

*(The fire in his eyes dims.)*

> *"Felicia worked alongside me in the evacuation. She died at the fall — the body did. What she became after is what your monks at Luchebert tend now. I learned, slowly, what she had done with what she had become: she settled the demi-humans my city had carried, in the hills, quietly, persistently, from a place a person should not be able to act from. The faction that brought us down wanted those demi-humans for service overseas. They never got one. That is hers. I tell you this so it is clear that it is hers."*

### When asked about the faction — *the people I want*

> *"They wanted the old order kept. They wanted the demi-humans on my city for service overseas. They built the cage. Their faction does not exist as a body any more — it diminished, fractured, aged out. The current overseas leadership are heirs of its flavour, not the same organisation. I want what is reachable of them. The originals are dead. I have lived with that. The heirs are not."*

### When asked what he wants now — *revival, revenge, and what is actually available*

> *"I want my city to fly again. I want to find the ones who brought it down. The first is not a thing this world has the workings to do any more. The second is a thing I have been chasing for centuries against the slow erosion of who is left to chase. What is *available*, if you decide to make it available, is **one last journey**. The cage at the lake holds the same elemental that lifted me. Free it, and the city rises — once, briefly, for a trip of your choosing. That is not revival. That is a parting. I have not made my peace with it. I will if I have to."*

### When asked why the skull gatherers have not collected him — *what he thinks they see*

> *"The gatherers collect mage-spirits that drift after their wearer dies and confuse a household for a generation. I am not drifting. I am here, in a body that I have arranged to keep me. They cannot collect what is sitting up. They have come close. They have left. I think they are waiting for me to fall. I am not going to fall."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — the voice gets *quieter*, not louder. The fire in the eyes brightens slightly. The hand-gestures still. He is choosing whether to throw fire; the choice is visible to anyone watching.
- **When distraught** — refers to himself as *"Laurent"* in the third person briefly. *"Laurent is — was — that person. He is here now."* The puppets in the alcoves twitch without his meaning them to.
- **When at peace** — fire dims further. Always when speaking of Felicia. Once or twice when remembering a specific refugee whose name he still has. He gets *younger* somehow without changing.

## If the party does something — what he does

| If the party... | He responds by... |
|---|---|
| Approaches with the **necklace** in hand or near | **Fire stops. Puppets still.** Felicia's presence condenses through the necklace; the room becomes one a person can be in. The conversation that follows is the warmest he has. |
| Approaches respectfully without the necklace | Decides whether to throw fire. Most respectful approaches reach the audience hall without being attacked, but the threat is real and the puppets are present. |
| Loots anything from the wreck | **Fire.** This is the surest way to provoke him. Looting is what most prior intruders did; his response is reflexive. |
| Damages the wreck or its preserved areas (especially Felicia's room) | Fire, plus full puppet activation across the city. Escalates fast. |
| Listens fully to his downfall narration without interrupting | The narration completes. The six-shackle mechanism becomes legible to the party. He notices the listening; standing rises. |
| Tells him a shackle has been broken | Pays close attention. Asks specifics. *"Which one. How."* Knows exactly what the party means. The mood improves; the conversation lengthens. |
| Tells him *all* the shackles are broken | The mood changes entirely. He may rise from the chair for the first time in centuries. The last-flight conversation becomes immediate and concrete. |
| Offers him the last flight as the resolution | Considers. *"Where would the journey go."* The party's answer matters. He commits to the flight only when the destination is one he can accept as a parting. |
| Asks him to teach pre-rift mage-craft | Possible — to a serious mage student, gated by sustained respect across visits. Not a one-conversation matter. |
| Tries to bind, dispel, or destroy him | The full city activates. Every alcove puppet animates. The fire is no longer a warning. The party will lose this fight unless they have prepared for it across many sessions. |
| Brings news of Brother Darrel, the shamans, or any old wartime figure | Listens carefully. Files the information. May ask about names he has not heard in centuries. |
| Speaks to him about Felicia *without* the necklace | He will speak. The conversation is good. Bringing the necklace later deepens it. |
| Asks his pre-mage personal history (family, origin) | Polite refusal. *"That belonged to a man who is no longer here."* |
| Asks him to leave the wreck | He cannot. *"I am bound here. I have arranged it. The arrangement is not undone by your asking."* |

## What he can teach

For students who reach him with sustained respect and a serious purpose:

- **Pre-rift mage-craft** — fragments. He cannot teach a working that requires the broken stability of pre-rift conditions, but he can teach the *grammar* of mage-work as it was done before the chaotic spirits made everything unreliable. A mage student of substantial standing who returns repeatedly can learn things no living mage will know.
- **Flying-city engineering** — how the lift was held, how the elemental was bound, how the city's life-systems worked. Useful only if the party is committing to the last-flight option; otherwise scholarly.
- **The history of the Rift from a survivor's perspective** — names, factions, mistakes, what should have been done. Available to anyone who listens; not gated.
- **How a mage and a great spirit can work together** — what he and Felicia did, in the practical terms of two practitioners coordinating across very different scales. Rare and earned.

## What he will not say

- **The specific working that turned him demi-lich.** He will say what he became; he will not describe the working. *"Some doors should not have a how attached to them."*
- **His pre-mage personal history.** Family, origin, the man he was before he held the castle. *"Belonged to someone who is no longer here."*
- **Whether he wants to be released from the wreck after the last flight.** He has not decided. He may decide in the moment, with the city in the air for the last time, and not before.
- **The names of every refugee he carried.** Most he no longer has. The ones he has, he keeps.

## DM notes

- **Play him aged but lucid.** He is not senile; he is not theatrical. Centuries of stillness have *concentrated* him, not eroded him. The wit is still there. The fury is real. The warmth, where it appears, is genuine.
- **The fire is his own; the puppets are remote-animated through the city.** Two distinct systems. The fire is a personal attack — instant, hot, thrown from his hand or his eyes. The puppets are a system he runs across the wreck (see `doc/places/laurents_city.md`); animated when intruders enter, stilled when he chooses. The party should learn the distinction over the visit.
- **The necklace is the cleanest path to the warm conversation.** Without it, the conversation is still possible — respectful approach, no looting, listening — but the room is *armed*. With it, the room is at peace. DMs should reward the necklace path with a more emotionally available Laurent.
- **The downfall narration is the file's load-bearing scene.** It is where the party finally understands the six-shackle mechanism. Take time on it. Let the puppets twitch. Let the fire flicker. The narration completes if the party listens; it breaks if they interrupt with action.
- **The last-flight option is bittersweet, not victorious.** When all six shackles are broken and the party offers Laurent the trip, he is grateful and bitter at once. The destination matters — he will accept the offer only if the destination is something he can frame as *a parting that mattered*. The DM should treat his answer to *"where would the journey go"* as a real test of the party's understanding of what he has been carrying.
- **He is an unintentional spirit-shield.** Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a demi-lich reads as a mighty spirit and the chaotic spirits back off from the hills around the wreck. Destroying him removes that shield; the surrounding hill country pays. Felicia and Luchebert hold part of the region; Laurent alone holds the rest. A party that *kills* him when they could have walked him to the last flight has cost the region something.
- **The skull gatherers are an open thread.** They have come close and left. The DM can develop this if the campaign reaches into the gatherers' arc; otherwise the line stays as it is — a noticed pattern Laurent has views on.

## Role as unintentional spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," chaotic spirits back off from anything that reads as a mighty spirit and cannot tell the difference between actual spirits and sufficiently powerful practitioners. **A demi-lich reads, unambiguously, as a mighty spirit.** Laurent's presence in the wreck is therefore *passively protecting* the hills around the downed city from chaotic-spirit migration — whether he meant to or not, and whether anyone in those hills realises it or not.

Destroying him (or, more gently, releasing him at the last flight) would remove that shield and let whatever is in the blasted lands or the forbidden woods press into the hills. Felicia and Luchebert Monastery would still hold part of the region; Laurent alone holds the rest. A campaign that ends with Laurent's last flight should account for this — the ecological cost of the bittersweet climax is *real*, not metaphorical.

## Relationships

- Worked alongside **Felicia** during the Rift evacuation; she settled the demi-humans his city once carried after he could no longer protect them. Her shrine is at Luchebert; he has preserved her room in the wreck.
- Mortal enemy of the **faction of old-order mages** who took the towers and used them to bring him down. That faction no longer exists as such; the recent overseas leaders are heirs of its flavour, not the same organisation, and are reachable in a way the originals are not.
- Shares a grievance — if not yet a cause — with the **surviving original owners of the seized towers**, who watched their homes and workings taken by force.
- Possibly a target, or curiosity, for the **skull gatherers**.
- His city houses **alcove puppets** — humanoid wooden constructs, cousins in construct-type to Haven's humanoid population and the buried tower's pot-people, physically complete but never bound to spirits. Laurent animates them remotely when intruders enter the city. See `doc/places/laurents_city.md`.

## Open questions

- The exact working he used to become demi-lich.
- Whether the skull gatherers have ever made an explicit approach he refused.
- What he would do *after* the last flight, if the city set down somewhere meaningful and he were still bound to its wreck. Would he choose to release the binding? Continue? The file does not commit; the campaign can decide.
