# Laurent

- **Role:** Former mage and castle-lord; now demi-lich haunting the wreck of his own crashed flying city
- **Species:** Human, undead (demi-lich)
- **Location:** The crashed flying city, in the hills near the Lake of Tears
- **Status:** Active — undead, diminished, patient

## Summary

In life, one of the two mages whose names history kept from the fall of the White City. Where most mages were incapacitated by the Rift's destabilising effect on their workings, Laurent worked on foot, guiding survivors out of the burning city. He pledged publicly that his flying castle would carry as many as could reach it, and that every refugee was welcome, humans and animal-people alike — explicitly rejecting the old mage leadership's exclusionary practices. Watching the White City burn, he predicted the destruction marked the end of the unified mage tradition. He was proven right.

His flying castle carried survivors for years, and grew into a flying city. Its lift came from a great bound air elemental. That made it a target: a faction of his fellow mages, who wanted to preserve the old order and the old way of life — a way in which demi-humans served rather than lived free — decided his city had to come down, and the demi-humans aboard it had to be collected into service overseas. To do it, they seized six existing towers from their previous owners — some of whom they killed in the taking, others of whom survived and have not forgotten — and moved them, whole, to ring a lake in the hills below his flight path. From those towers they captured the bound air elemental that held his city aloft. The city fell. Laurent used some working — no one knows which — to survive the fall. What he became was a demi-lich, bound to the wreck.

The mages who brought him down did not get what they wanted. The demi-humans who survived the crash were settled, quietly and persistently, by **Felicia** from beyond her own death, and never entered the mages' service. But the air elemental remained trapped in its stolen cage of towers, and Laurent has remained in his wreck. The two facts are cousins.

He has two standing projects: **revive the city** and **find the ones who brought it down**. Both are slow; neither is forgotten. He remembers every refugee he carried, and is bitterly conscious that his legacy of inclusion is now a story told about a corpse in a broken palace.

### Potential campaign climax — the last flight

What is actually available to him, if the party makes it happen, is **one last journey** rather than a revival. Freeing the bound air elemental at the Lake of Tears would release the spirit that once held his flying city aloft. With that spirit free, the downed city could **rise again** — but only briefly. It would not endure a full campaign, and it would not resume its old life. A few sessions, led by the players, with the city in the air once more: enough for a single meaningful journey (to the overseas continent, to the shifting lands, to whatever is worth the last trip), and no more.

This is the bittersweet shape of Laurent's ambition actually resolving. Not revival; not revenge sustained; one last flight with the city going somewhere that mattered. Laurent's position on that outcome — grateful, bitter, both, or refusing to see it — is a DM choice when the time comes. It is also the path that costs the Lake of Tears rain-buffer, because the elemental being freed is the same act.

Whether the skull gatherers have ever attempted to collect him, and if not, why not, is an open question.

## Relationships

- Worked alongside **Felicia** during the Rift evacuation; she settled the demi-humans his city once carried after he could no longer protect them.
- Mortal enemy of the faction of old-order mages who took the towers and used them to bring him down. That faction no longer exists as such. The recent Overseas War's mages are themselves old — long-lived senior figures — but Laurent predates them by a much larger span. Their faction is *similar* to the old order in flavour, not the same organisation. The heirs of the old grudge are living, but generations removed; for practical purposes they are the live targets for whatever revenge Laurent can still reach, even if they would not immediately recognise themselves as such.
- Shares a grievance — if not yet a cause — with the surviving original owners of those six towers, who watched their homes and workings taken by force.
- Possibly a target, or curiosity, for the skull gatherers.
- His city houses iron puppets — cousins, in construct-type, of the pot-person in the buried tower at the Lake of Tears.

## Role as an unintentional spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," chaotic spirits back off from anything that reads as a mighty spirit and cannot tell the difference between actual spirits and sufficiently powerful practitioners. A demi-lich reads, unambiguously, as a mighty spirit. Laurent's presence in the wreck is therefore *passively protecting* the hills around the downed city from chaotic-spirit migration — whether he meant to or not, and whether anyone in those hills realises it or not. Destroying him (or, more gently, driving him off or binding him elsewhere) would remove that shield and let whatever is in the blasted lands or the forbidden woods push into the hills. Felicia and Luchebert Monastery would still hold part of the region; Laurent alone holds the rest.
