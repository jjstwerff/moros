# Demilich Laurent Gallagar

- **Role:** Former mage of the White City; the man whose flying castle carried Rift survivors; now a demi-lich in the wreck of his own crashed flying city. The full name **Laurent Gallagar** is on the audience hall's old records and on the deeds of sale for the castle; he has not been called *Demilich* by anyone who knew him alive, but the title fits what he chose to become and he does not refuse it.
- **Species:** Human in life. Demi-lich now — preserved, diminished, patient.
- **Lives:** The crashed flying city, in the hills near the Lake of Tears. Bound to it; cannot leave. (For the city as a place — the wreck's layout, the alcove puppets, Felicia's preserved room — see `doc/places/laurents_city.md`.)
- **First met:** When the party reaches him — late mid-campaign, usually. He does not come to them. They have to come through the wreck to reach him, with or without the necklace.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Demi-lich of the wreck; the pre-rift world's last living voice; partner-of-the-work to **Felicia Vintala**; passive spirit-shield for the surrounding hills. Full name **Laurent Gallagar** — used on his old papers and in moments of weight; he answers to *Laurent* without ceremony.
- **Standouts:** *No body.* **A floating skull at seated head-height** above an empty preserved chair, holding station for centuries. **A shadow beneath the skull** in the rough shape of a robed seated man — illusion, not substance, occasionally tightening for seconds into a glimpse of the living face. **Flame-lit eye sockets** that brighten when he is deciding to throw fire and **dim when Felicia is named** — the most reliable pattern in him. The **alcove puppets** twitch when his attention shifts; with no face reliably visible, the wood is the external tell.
- **Speech tag:** Aged, lucid, stripped — concentrated, not eroded. He does not defend his choices. He does not process his losses. He states what is. *"That belonged to a man who is no longer here."* The wit is intact; the certainty is flat.
- **Quests:** Early — *earn the necklace path and let the open conversation happen — the campaign's clearest window onto the pre-rift world.* Late — *break the six shackles at the lake and choose the destination of the last flight — the bittersweet climax with real ecological cost.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *He is not negotiable on the cages, the pledge, or the destination.* Reaching for the skull triggers full escalation; looting the wreck triggers fire. **He does not emote about the last flight; the bittersweet is the party's, not his.** A party whose answer to *"where would the journey go"* is wrong does not get a half-meeting; he assesses and refuses.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/laurent.md`.*

## What the party meets

The party has crossed Corné's hills (or another route), reached the wreck, and pressed in past the alcove puppets. The deeper they go, the more *kept* the wreck looks — the central halls are not random ruin but something tended, by something. They reach what was once an audience hall.

At the back of the hall, **something is suspended in the air above an empty chair** — a human skull, aged bone, floating at the height a seated man's head would be. **The eye-sockets hold small steady flames.** Below the skull, a shadow fills the chair in the rough shape of a robed man sitting in it — not flesh, not cloth; illusion. The party's eye fills the shadow in as a body before it registers that there is nothing there.

He looks at them for a long moment. The fire that will be thrown has not formed yet; he is deciding.

If they have the **necklace**, the fire goes out before they have spoken. The puppets in the alcoves still. The conversation that follows is *different from any they had crossing the wreck.*

If they don't, he will speak first. *"You came in through my puppets. State your business and I will decide whether to throw fire at you again."* He says this without raising his voice. He has said it before. The words have been waiting.

## Personal goals — early and later

His goals have been fixed for centuries; what changes is which of them are *available*. He does not phrase them as quests for the party — he states what *is*, and the party reads what they can do. The *what he wants now* story below is the canonical statement; the early-and-late framing makes it actionable.

- **Early-game — earn the necklace path and let the open conversation happen.** *What the NPC says* (when the party first reaches the audience hall without the necklace): *"You came in through my puppets. State your business and I will decide whether to throw fire at you again. If you mean to come back, come back differently. Bring her with you, or do not come back at all."* — *Where:* **Willow** at Felicia's shrine (`doc/npcs/willow.md`) — the necklace's keeper, who will release it only to a party she has come to trust; **Father Elliot** at Luchebert if a spiritual frame is wanted on what carrying the necklace means (`doc/npcs/father_elliot.md`); the **wreck of the flying city** (`doc/places/laurents_city.md`) — through the alcove puppets, past the half-replaced lower decks, past **Felicia's preserved room**, into the audience hall. — *What:* earn Willow's standing across multiple visits, take the necklace from her on her terms, walk it through the wreck, and bring it into the audience hall so the room becomes one a person can be in. Listen, then, to the **downfall narration** in full — the pledge, the cages that were not his, the faction that brought him down, the six-shackle mechanism — without interrupting. — *Reward:* the campaign's clearest window onto the **pre-rift world**. Standing earned with Laurent that gates the late-game choice; **the shadow holds the living face long enough that the party will remember what he looked like**. The six-shackle mechanism becomes legible. — *Complications:* the wreck is hostile to looters and to anyone reaching for the skull — full puppet escalation, fire, no second warning. **Felicia must not be alienated first** — a Moth-Hollow violation closes the necklace's condensation and the route is cut until restitution is done. Willow does not give the necklace cheaply; the standing must be real and visible to her. The narration *breaks if interrupted with action*; let it complete.

- **Later-game — break the six shackles, free the elemental, and choose the destination of the last flight.** *What the NPC says* (when the party tells him a shackle has been broken): *"Which one. How."* And, after the sixth: *"Where would the journey go."* (And, if the destination makes sense as a parting, only then:) *"Then we go."* — *Where:* the **six towers ringing the Lake of Tears** — the cage built by the faction that brought him down; the **wreck's audience hall** for the conversation about destination; the **last flight** itself, which leaves the hills and lands once, briefly, somewhere the pledge's weight requires. Possible destinations: **(a)** the **overseas continent**, where the heirs of the faction that brought him down still live — fury cooled by centuries, but reachable; **(b)** the **Skull Gatherers' necropolis** west of the blasted lands at the desert's edge, via **Andras**'s standing offer (`doc/npcs/andras.md`) — *"we will hold your station, in our city, with the others, when you choose to set it down"*; **(c)** somewhere the table proposes that earns the pledge's weight. — *What:* break each of the six shackles in turn — research, travel, working, fight, depending on the tower; carry the count back to Laurent each time so he knows the work is real; once the sixth is down, sit at the table and propose the destination; if Andras's offer is the path, broker the conversation between Laurent and Andras through the party (Laurent will not summon Andras and Andras will not approach uninvited); be present, **with the necklace**, at the parting itself, so the city does not lift alone. — *Reward:* one of the campaign's possible climaxes. The pre-rift world's last living voice ends with company; the city lifts once more; the parting carries the pledge to a destination that completes it. **Felicia is present at the parting through the necklace** — the climax that has been building since their work in the evacuation. — *Complications:* freeing the elemental at the lake **removes the lake's rain-buffer against the desert** (`doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"The rain-buffer"). Laurent's presence at the wreck is a **passive spirit-shield over the surrounding hills** (`doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology"); his last flight removes that shield, and the chaos pressure from the forbidden woods or the blasted lands may press into the hills afterwards. **The cost of the bittersweet is real, not metaphorical** — the campaign must account for what the hills lose. Laurent does not meet the party halfway on a wrong destination: if the answer to *"where would the journey go"* does not earn the pledge's weight, he assesses and refuses, and the city stays on the ground. **A party that kills him when they could have walked him to the last flight has cost the region something.**

## How he reads at a glance

- **A floating skull, human, aged bone.** He does not sit, stand, or touch ground. The skull holds position at seated head-height, turns when he chooses to look, is still when he holds still. The stillness is total when it holds.
- **A shadow beneath the skull** — roughly a robed man seated in the chair, sometimes a sharper outline, sometimes only a dark suggestion filling the seat. **Illusion, not substance.** A hand reaching to touch it passes through and meets nothing (until it reaches the skull). Most of the time the shadow is only a dark shape that the eye fills in as a body.
- **Rare glimpses of real expression.** Perhaps once per visit, perhaps not at all, the shadow tightens into a moment of the living face: the set of a mouth, a raised brow, a hand held a certain way. Seconds at most. The glimpse is not performance — it is the illusion briefly aligning with what he was. *Shamanic* and *Druid* characters see these alignments more readily; everyone else catches only the stronger ones. **Mostly there is no expression visible at all.**
- **Flame-lit eye sockets.** Small fires in the empty orbits, steady. They are part of how he sees and part of how he warns. They dim when Felicia is named; they brighten when he is deciding to throw fire.
- **Illusion-hands that still remember mage-discipline.** When he speaks at length, the shadow's hands move — precise, pre-rift, trained. No less accurate for being illusion; a mage watching can read the grammar of a working in their shape.
- **A voice that should not work.** Works. No throat. No breath. The sound simply arrives.
- **The hall is preserved around him** more carefully than the rest of the wreck. Dust controlled. Cloth maintained. The room is *kept*; the wreck is not.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **He does not move his station.** The skull holds position above the preserved chair at seated head-height. It turns when he looks; it does not rise, descend, or travel. He has held this spot for centuries.
- **The puppets in the alcoves twitch when his attention shifts.** Not always — only when something is rising in him. A party watching the alcoves can read his state from the wood. With no face reliably visible, **the puppets are the main external tell.**
- **The fire in the eye-sockets dims when Felicia is named.** The most reliable pattern in him; the DM can count on it. Read as warmth by the party; not warmth to him.
- **The shadow holds longer when Felicia is in the air.** With the necklace nearby, or during any extended Felicia-themed exchange, the illusion-body sharpens and holds the glimpse of his living face longer than it otherwise would. The party may see him *as he was* for several seconds at a stretch — long enough to notice, not long enough to converse with.
- **He tracks the wreck around him.** A party that does something elsewhere in the city while he is talking — opens a door, lifts a panel — he knows. He may not stop them; he knows.

## At the audience hall (the wreck's centre, his throne)

The room he holds. Three things stand out:

- **The chair beneath him is preserved empty.** The chair he sat in at the city's height, still there, with the shadow of him sometimes filling it as a seated robed man and sometimes not. The wood has not aged because he has not allowed it to. The dais is cracked from the fall; the chair sits straight on it anyway.
- **Felicia's seat beside his.** Set as it was. Cushion intact. A place to his right, also empty — no shadow ever fills it. A party that asks about it gets the *what Felicia did* story.
- **The alcoves around the walls** — wooden puppets standing motionless. They do not move while the party is welcome. They will if the welcome ends.

For Felicia's preserved room (a separate scene location elsewhere in the wreck), see `doc/places/laurents_city.md` §"Felicia's room."

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked how he came to own the castle — *the cages were not mine*

> *"I did not build it. I bought it — at auction, under my own name, **Gallagar**, written in plain ink on papers anyone could read — after its prior owners were broken by their own trade. They had run this continent's market in demi-humans for a generation: seizing, caging, shipping. The law caught up with them late, and only because the politics of the day had turned against them. The castle was their transport. I had the money. I bought it to take it out of their hands. I remade what I could. I had started on the middle decks when the White City fell and every refugee who could reach me came up the ramps into holds I had not yet finished. They slept in the holds because there was nowhere else. They would not sleep in the cages. They built cots where there had been none, and stored their food where the old owners had stored theirs. If you look, the traces are still there. The cages in the lower holds are not mine. The half-replaced walls are. The cots are theirs."*

### When asked about the burning of the White City — *the pledge*

> *"I stood at the foot of my castle with the city on fire behind me. The mages who could still cast were trying to and failing — the rift was breaking workings the moment they finished them. I walked. I shouted that my castle would carry as many as could reach it, and that every refugee was welcome — humans, animal-people, the people the mage leadership had spent generations treating as fixtures. It was true. Saying it loudly meant the leadership could not undo it. They did not. The pledge is the one thing about me history kept correctly."*

### When asked about the slow descent — *the cage closed as I watched*

> *"I noticed the towers ringing the lake before I knew what they were doing. I was carrying refugees; I was busy. By the time I understood that the towers were *for* my elemental — that they were *taking* it from me — the cage was nearly closed. I tried to fly the city further. The lift faltered. I tried to set it down. The lift stopped. The city went down hard, into the hills, where the wreck still is. The faction that built the cage got everything they wanted from the engineering. Felicia got everything they wanted from the people."*

### When asked about Felicia — *what she did from beyond*

*(The fire in his eyes dims.)*

> *"Felicia worked alongside me in the evacuation. She died at the fall — the body did. What she became after is what your monks at Luchebert tend now. She settled the demi-humans my city had carried, in the hills, quietly, persistently, from a place a person should not be able to act from. The faction that brought us down wanted those demi-humans for service overseas. They never got one. That is hers."*

### When asked about the faction — *the people I want*

> *"They wanted the old order kept. They wanted the demi-humans on my city for service overseas. They built the cage. Their faction does not exist as a body any more — it diminished, fractured, aged out. The current overseas leadership are heirs of its flavour, not the same organisation. I want what is reachable of them. The originals are dead. The heirs are not."*

### When asked what he wants now — *revival, revenge, and what is actually available*

> *"I want my city to fly again. I want to find the ones who brought it down. The first is not a thing this world has the workings to do any more. The second I have chased for centuries; the chaseable dwindle. What is *available*, if you decide to make it available, is **one last journey**. The cage at the lake holds the same elemental that lifted me. Free it, and the city rises — once, briefly, for a trip of your choosing. That is not revival. It is a parting. It is what is available."*

### When asked why the skull gatherers have not collected him — *what he thinks they see*

> *"The gatherers collect mage-spirits that drift after their wearer dies and confuse a household for a generation. I am not drifting. I am here, in what I arranged to keep me — skull, hall, will, and the cast of a man beneath me. They cannot collect what is holding station. They have come close. They have left. They wait for me to fall. I am not going to fall."*

## Mannerisms — reactions, not felt states

**Laurent has no self-reflection; that was his human part, and it is gone.** What the party reads as mood is physical reaction in a preserved construct — pattern, not interiority. He does not defend his choices. He does not process his losses. He states what is. The three-state mannerism convention (pressed / distraught / happy) adapts here to stimulus-triggered reactions: what registers externally when a specific kind of topic hits him.

- **When pressed** — the voice gets *quieter*, not louder. The fire in the eyes brightens slightly. The hand-gestures still. He is deciding whether to throw fire; the decision is visible in the stillness. He does not defend; he chooses.
- **When a central fact is touched** (the faction, the cage, the day the city fell, the pledge) — the puppets in the alcoves twitch without him meaning them to. **Overflow into the construct he runs**, not expression. He does not pause, does not raise his voice, does not alter his rate of speech. The wood registers what he will not.
- **When Felicia is named** — the fire in the eyes dims. Always. The dimming is the most reliable pattern in him; the party can count on it. It is not warmth. It is the one stimulus that still pulls a consistent response.

## What he can teach

For students who reach him with sustained respect and a serious purpose:

- **Pre-rift mage-craft** — fragments. He cannot teach a working that requires the broken stability of pre-rift conditions, but he can teach the *grammar* of mage-work as it was done before the chaotic spirits made everything unreliable. A mage student of substantial standing who returns repeatedly can learn things no living mage will know.
- **Flying-city engineering** — how the lift was held, how the elemental was bound, how the city's life-systems worked. Useful only if the party is committing to the last-flight option; otherwise scholarly.
- **The history of the Rift from a survivor's perspective** — names, factions, mistakes, what should have been done. Available to anyone who listens; not gated.
- **How a mage and a great spirit can work together** — what he and Felicia did, in the practical terms of two practitioners coordinating across very different scales. Rare and earned.

## What he will not say

- **The specific working that turned him demi-lich.** He will say what he became; he will not describe the working. *"Some doors should not have a how attached to them."*
- **His pre-mage personal history.** Family, origin, the man he was before he held the castle. *"Belonged to someone who is no longer here."*
- **Whether he wants to be released from the wreck after the last flight.** He has not decided. He may decide in the moment, with the city in the air for the last time, and not before.
- **The names of every refugee he carried.** Most he no longer has. The ones he has, he keeps.

## DM notes

- **Play him aged, lucid, and stripped.** He is not senile; he is not theatrical. Centuries as a demi-lich have *concentrated* him, not eroded him — and have taken the human part that would have reflected on any of this. **He does not defend his choices. He does not process his losses. He knows he was right and states what is.** The wit is still there. The certainty is flat. What the party reads as warmth or fury is pattern in the construct, not emotion working through him; play it as automatic response, not as felt state.
- **No body.** Laurent is a **floating skull** with an **illusion-shadow** filling the chair beneath him. Do not narrate him standing, walking, sitting down, handling objects, or physically interacting with the world. The shadow's hands can make mage-gestures; the skull can turn to look; the eye-sockets can brighten or dim; his voice arrives without breath. **He has no substance below the skull itself.** A party reaching to touch passes through the shadow and meets bone — and meeting bone is the line that triggers full escalation. The rare glimpses of a real expression in the shadow are moments to *hand* the DM (a short pause in narration, a specific physical detail — *"for a moment the shadow tightens: you see the set of a mouth, a raised brow, the shape of a hand"*) and then let pass.
- **The fire is his own; the puppets are remote-animated through the city.** Two distinct systems. The fire is a personal attack — instant, hot, **arriving from the eye-sockets or thrown by the illusion-hand** (the shadow performs the throwing gesture; the fire is real). The puppets are a system he runs across the wreck (see `doc/places/laurents_city.md`); animated when intruders enter, stilled when he chooses. The party should learn the distinction over the visit.
- **The necklace is the cleanest path to the open conversation.** Without it, the exchange is still possible — respectful approach, no looting, listening — but the room is *armed*. With it, the room is at peace, and the shadow holds the living face long enough that the party can see the man. DMs should reward the necklace path with a more readable Laurent, not a more *emotional* one: the patterns are simply clearer.
- **The downfall narration is the file's load-bearing scene.** It is where the party finally understands the six-shackle mechanism. Take time on it. Let the puppets twitch. Let the fire flicker. The narration completes if the party listens; it breaks if they interrupt with action.
- **The last-flight option is bittersweet for the players, not for Laurent.** He does not emote about it. The party offers the trip; he assesses whether the destination makes sense and commits or does not. The bitterness belongs to the party who understood what he once was; Laurent states terms and proceeds. Treat his answer to *"where would the journey go"* as a real test of the party's understanding of what the pledge requires — he will not meet them halfway if their answer is wrong.
- **He is an unintentional spirit-shield.** Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a demi-lich reads as a mighty spirit and the chaotic spirits back off from the hills around the wreck. Destroying him removes that shield; the surrounding hill country pays. Felicia and Luchebert hold part of the region; Laurent alone holds the rest. A party that *kills* him when they could have walked him to the last flight has cost the region something.
- **The skull gatherers are an open thread — and one of the destinations the last flight can resolve to.** They have come close and left. **Andras** (see `doc/npcs/andras.md`), the order's named contact, is authorised to extend a late-game offer the file's prior version did not name: a **resting place for Laurent in the necropolis** — *"we will hold your station, in our city, with the others, when you choose to set it down."* This is not collection. It is one of the parting destinations the last-flight option can carry the city toward. The party becomes the carrier of the offer once it has earned the order's standing (the relic dungeon is the gate). Laurent's response in the audience hall is his own.

## Role as unintentional spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," chaotic spirits back off from anything that reads as a mighty spirit and cannot tell the difference between actual spirits and sufficiently powerful practitioners. **A demi-lich reads, unambiguously, as a mighty spirit.** Laurent's presence in the wreck is therefore *passively protecting* the hills around the downed city from chaotic-spirit migration — whether he meant to or not, and whether anyone in those hills realises it or not.

Destroying him (or, more gently, releasing him at the last flight) would remove that shield and let whatever is in the blasted lands or the forbidden woods press into the hills. Felicia and Luchebert Monastery would still hold part of the region; Laurent alone holds the rest. A campaign that ends with Laurent's last flight should account for this — the ecological cost of the bittersweet climax is *real*, not metaphorical.

## Relationships

- Worked alongside **Felicia** during the Rift evacuation; she settled the demi-humans his city once carried after he could no longer protect them. Her shrine is at Luchebert; he has preserved her room in the wreck.
- Mortal enemy of the **faction of old-order mages** who took the towers and used them to bring him down. That faction no longer exists as such; the recent overseas leaders are heirs of its flavour, not the same organisation, and are reachable in a way the originals are not.
- Shares a grievance — if not yet a cause — with the **surviving original owners of the seized towers**, who watched their homes and workings taken by force.
- Bought the castle, before the Rift, from **a demi-human-trafficking group** broken by legal trouble. He was partway through retrofitting it (middle decks in hand, lower decks still caged) when the White City fell; the pledge *"every refugee welcome"* was made on a vessel built for the opposite. He wants this on the record. See `doc/places/laurents_city.md` §"At the decks — the city's past lives."
- Possibly a target, or curiosity, for the **skull gatherers**.
- His city houses **alcove puppets** — humanoid wooden constructs, cousins in construct-type to Haven's humanoid population and the buried tower's pot-people, physically complete but never bound to spirits. Laurent animates them remotely when intruders enter the city. See `doc/places/laurents_city.md`.
