# King Hannes of Allondo

- **Role:** King of Allondo; veteran of a capture he has not yet forgiven himself for; unexpected heir after his older brother renounced the crown
- **Species:** Human (the royal line of Allondo — humans still tend to hold leadership in the kingdoms)
- **Location:** Scarlet vale (capital of Allondo)
- **Status:** Alive, reigning

## Summary

Rules a small kingdom with his wife **Queen Fienna** — whom he married as a younger prince, when she was an acceptable match for the spare son of the house: the daughter of an important but non-royal house of **World's End**, a wealthy coastal family of forges and mines. A younger prince can marry where he likes. A king's wife is supposed to be royal. When Elliot renounced and Hannes became king, Fienna became queen; the court has not quite forgiven either of them for it, and Hannes has not quite found the voice to defend her. He **was not always expected to be king**: at the time of the Overseas War, his older brother **Elliot** was Crown Prince, and Hannes was a younger prince without the throne in his future. After the war, Elliot renounced the crown and entered monastic service at Luchebert — and Hannes, without the upbringing his brother had, found himself king. He has never entirely stopped feeling that the throne is borrowed. His older brother still lives, and still wears, in a sense, the weight Hannes now pretends comfortably to carry.

In the last war he was captured — more easily than the story his court tells admits. He had been warned beforehand that there were unknown soldiers moving in the region; he ignored the warning, and paid for it. An ox-tauren commander named **Raul** led the forward war band that took him, intending to extract information about the homelands on behalf of **Thorgal**, the overseas mage running the invasion remotely. Hannes was held for long enough to understand that he was about to be interrogated in earnest. He has not yet come to grips with any of it. It sits under everything he does. It is one of the unspoken reasons he held **John Bean** so firmly to the bargain for Ron's enlistment — the full story of his liberation is that **John killed Thorgal** (breaking the scrying mirror Thorgal was using to run the operation), **Raul fell back in confusion**, and **Felix** slipped in during the retreat and extracted Hannes. Hannes owes his freedom to John's arm, Felix's timing, and whichever gods he still prays to. He cannot say any of this aloud without admitting what went wrong.

Whether he has ever understood that Thorgal's war was about *food* — that the overseas continent was starving and Thorgal's "invasion" was a desperate emigration — is a separate question. The court does not like to discuss the overseas mages in terms that make them sympathetic, and Hannes has not insisted that it should.

The bargain itself is his other live concern. John asked only for a farm and the right never to fight again; Hannes agreed only on condition that the crown could enlist John's eldest son in John's place. That promise is active — **Ron** is in training. The crown assumed Ron would carry the Tusk Clan's strong-willed lineage, which would have made the bargain simple; he didn't. **Irna** did, and she is not the one in uniform. Hannes has not yet fully reckoned with that.

His second son **Didrich** was recently attacked, non-lethally, by Irna Bean — a second live embarrassment, on top of an older unhealed one. And a third pressure, still largely invisible at court, is building: **Brumal** is being lost to advancing desert (Steadington is already gone), and a second war is brewing on that border. Hannes cannot afford to be seen unprepared a second time in a decade, and the Ron enlistment — harsh, debt-flavoured — is in part a preparation for what he can feel coming.

Hannes is a king who calls in old favours, often more harshly than the favour warrants, because the alternative is to admit what he owes. The same debt is why he could not say no to **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** when she stormed into his castle over what had been done to Irna — Rhianna was there, she knows what happened, and Hannes could not afford to refuse her. Irna's release from the mine and her exile to Luchebert were the compromise he agreed to in the moment, so that he would not have to agree to more.

## Relationships

- **Younger brother of Father Elliot** (former Crown Prince, now abbot of Luchebert). Became king when Elliot renounced.
- Married to **Queen Fienna**.
- Father of **Prince Corven** (older son; Crown Prince; martial heir, Hannes's preferred version of himself) and **Didrich** (second son). Hannes does not know — or has not let himself know — that Corven is in love with **Princess Penelope of Brumal**. It is the third thing sitting under the surface of the royal household he cannot face, after his own capture and his brother Elliot's renunciation.
- Held **John Bean**'s service and now holds the enlistment right to **Ron**. Likely owes John his life and freedom; this is not in the official record.
- Captured during the Overseas War by **Raul**'s forward war band. Freed by **Felix** during the confusion after John broke Thorgal's scrying mirror.
- Dealing with the fallout of **Irna Bean**'s attack on **Didrich** — an attack that is, by family relation, on his brother Elliot's nephew as much as on his own son.
