# Princess Penelope of Brumal

- **Role:** Royal lady of Brumal; secret love of Prince Corven of Allondo
- **Species:** (open — Brumal's royal structure not yet specified)
- **Location:** Brumal (Cliffside hold presumed, not yet confirmed)
- **Status:** Alive; in active correspondence with Corven

## Summary

A royal lady of the Brumal court — exact rank, line, and standing in the royal house not yet specified and open for the DM to place. She is in love with **Prince Corven of Allondo**. The relationship is real, current, and hidden on both sides of the border.

**In her youth she was sent to Fata morgana** to learn its famous **jewelry craft**. This was a political-economic errand, not an artistic indulgence: the Brumal court hoped to bring the technique home and use it to turn the metals mined at Bockthicket and elsewhere into high-value finished work they could sell rather than export as raw ore. The plan has **not panned out yet**. Penelope learned what she could, returned with what knowledge she could carry, and the uplift the court hoped for has not materialised.

The time away was long. Being kept that far from her own house for that many formative years **did not strengthen her allegiance to it**. She came back changed in ways her family had not accounted for: quieter about Brumal's fortunes, less invested in its politics, more measured when people around her spoke as if the kingdom's cause was self-evidently her own. The thing her court sent her to acquire for them, she acquired in part. The loyalty that was supposed to come with the rest of the errand did not.

Afterwards, the court sent her to **the mine** (presumably Bockthicket or a nearby mining town) to **teach others** the jewelry craft — the next phase of the uplift plan. She was **not particularly good at teaching there**. She knew the work, but the context was wrong: strangers her court had selected, a classroom-by-mandate, a skill being extracted from her rather than given. The attempted training produced little. That is another reason the plan has not panned out: what the court wanted from her, it wanted on its terms.

In the right context, she *can* teach. The craft is real. Her knowledge is real. But the people have to be ones she **fully trusts**, and they have to come to her without **forcing her into it**. Under those conditions — earned trust, no coercion, her own decision to open — she will pass on what she learned in Fata morgana. Under any other conditions she will not, and the court has already demonstrated why.

All of which means her connection to **Prince Corven** is not a detour from an otherwise committed Brumal princess. It is a line being drawn by a woman whose formal attachments to her house have been hollowed out by years of errands that did not land. Her loyalty was never solidified. Her teaching didn't work. Her house sent her away and got less than it spent. The love she has chosen is, quietly, one of the few decisions of her own life that was not handed to her with an expectation attached.

Yet: she does know Fata morgana first-hand. She has met people there. She is the only Brumal royal who has actually *lived* inside the protection the continent will eventually need. That fact outlives the failed uplift plan.

The love crosses a collapsing frontier. Brumal is being eaten by the desert from the south; its royal house is preparing for whatever form the coming Allondan pressure takes, whether that is refugee overflow, border conflict, or open war. Penelope sits at the centre of a royal house that has reason to distrust Allondo specifically because of the old Overseas War and the current silence between the kingdoms. She is in love with the very crown prince her family would most immediately need to negotiate with — or fight.

How long she has been in correspondence with Corven, how they met, and how much of her own standing she is risking to continue the relationship are all open. What is certain is that she is a live political actor in her own right on her side of the border, not merely a mirror of Corven on his side.

## Relationships

- In love with **Prince Corven** of Allondo.
- Member of the Brumal royal court (exact family structure open for DM fill).
- **Trained at Fata morgana in her youth**; knows its famous jewelry craft (whatever the extent of what she mastered), and knows the southern city first-hand in a way no one else in either northern royal household does.
- Implicit counterpart to several Allondan figures she has never met: **Queen Fienna** (her prospective mother-in-law if the love were ever acknowledged), **Father Elliot** (who sheltered the very sort of border-crossing life hers mirrors, in his way), **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** (who has quietly noticed Corven's faltering).

## Role in the campaign

Penelope is the Brumal-side mirror of the Corven tension. She may be:

- **A peace-maker by correspondence** — working from the Brumal end to delay or defuse the coming war, because her future with Corven depends on it.
- **A reluctant loyalist** — her kingdom is collapsing, her family's survival depends on the war going well, and her love is something she is slowly being forced to put down.
- **A lever** — someone on the Brumal side (or on the Allondan side) learns about the relationship and uses her position to force Corven's hand.
- **A fugitive-in-waiting** — ready to cross the border to Corven if the war becomes inevitable, with whatever consequences that causes.

Which of these she is depends on the DM's placement of her standing in the Brumal royal house and on how the campaign handles the coming war.
