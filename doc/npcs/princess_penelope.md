# Princess Penelope of Brumal

- **Role:** Royal lady of the Brumal court; secret love of Prince Corven of Allondo; the only Brumal royal who has lived inside Fata morgana's protection.
- **Species:** Human (Brumal's royal line; exact rank within the line open for DM placement).
- **Lives:** **Cliffside hold**, Brumal's royal seat. **The high north-light room** is hers — a working room she had cleared for jewelry craft in her teens and never gave up. Two other places where the party may find her: **the meeting place she shares with Corven** (a half-ruined roadhouse at the old border crossing — see §"At the meeting place with Corven"), and, rarely, **Fata morgana** if a court errand sends her south again. (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` for Brumal's wider state; `doc/places/fata_morgana.md` for the southern city.)
- **First met:** At a formal court reception in Brumal, where her presence is correct and her attention is not. Or — earned, with risk — at the meeting place with Corven, after the party has been brought into the conspiracy from his end.

## What the party meets

A young human woman, slight, with a faint sun-mark across the bridge of her nose and the high cheekbones that does not belong on a northern face — the year in the south is on her skin and has not faded. She is holding *something*: a piece of unset wire, a pendant in progress, a small file. Her hands continue their quiet work while she greets the room. Her court manners are flawless and slightly ahead of the room, as if she has already decided what to do with the party and is waiting politely for them to catch up.

If they meet her at court, she is performing — courteously, exactly. If they reach her in private — and they will not, the first time — the performance softens. The piece in her hand becomes the conversation: she will turn it as she speaks, weigh it, set it down only when she has decided to commit to what she is saying.

## Personal goals — early and later

She is a live political actor on her side of the border. The early goal is keeping the line to Corven alive without it costing them; the later goals are preventing the war or surviving it on her own terms.

- **Early-game — open a courier line that does not depend on either of us riding.** *What the NPC says* (when the party has been brought to the meeting place by Corven and earned an honest hour with her): *"The night rides are how we have managed this. We will not manage it much longer. I need a hand that can carry between Cliffside and Scarlet Vale that is not his hand or mine. Someone neither court watches. A sealed packet to an inn at Linar harbour, left with a name I will give you, and the answer brought back when it is there. Will you do that for us."* — *Where:* the meeting place at the border crossing for the ask; **Linar harbour back rooms** for the handover (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md`); **Cliffside hold** for the return delivery, slipped to Penelope through the kitchen-yard scribe she trusts. — *What:* take a sealed packet (one or two finger-thick, wax-sealed without a sigil) to a named keeper at a named room; wait for the reply; bring it north. Do not open it. Do not name the destination aloud. — *Reward:* trust, a working channel, and Penelope's honesty about Brumal's actual condition (which she does not give to outsiders before this). On a clean first run she begins to teach a piece of the craft (see *What she can teach*). — *Complications:* Brumal is preparing for war and has begun watching its own. A second packet sent the same way after the first carries more risk than the first; the route needs to vary. Allondo's quartermasters in Linar are noticing strangers; a too-clean handover looks like spycraft. An informer at Cliffside (DM-fill) may already be reading the kitchen-yard.

- **Early-game — get one piece of inner-craft work into Bockthicket without the court owning it.** *What the NPC says* (when teaching has begun, or when the party asks why the back bench in her room has unfinished pieces on it): *"My father's people sent me to that mine to teach and I taught nothing, because the court was the wrong room to teach in. There is a young silversmith there who is better than her shop and her shop will not let her work the inner pattern. If the piece on my bench reaches her hand without my court's stamp on it, she will know what to do with it. Take it for me."* — *Where:* **Bockthicket** (Brumal mining town, named in `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` and `doc/places/southern_desert.md`); the silversmith's back workshop, not the shop counter. — *What:* deliver an unmarked finished piece — a small ward-pendant in Fata-morgana inner-craft pattern — to a named young woman; pass on a single phrase Penelope teaches the party; come back with whatever the silversmith sends in answer. — *Reward:* a craft-trained ally inside Brumal's mining country; a piece of Fata-morgana ward-work loose in the world, doing its actual job; Penelope's standing with the party rises a step. — *Complications:* the shopmaster will read the party as buyers and try to extract a sale; the piece must not change hands at the counter. Bockthicket is watched by both factions of Brumal's court (the one that wanted the uplift to work and the one that did not); the wrong noble overhearing the wrong word costs the silversmith her position.

- **Later-game — choose with Corven, in concert, from the Brumal side.** *What the NPC says* (after the courier line has run for some time and the war is no longer hypothetical): *"Whatever he chooses, I am not going to let him choose it alone. If he tells the courts in Allondo, I tell the courts in Brumal at the same hour, in the same words, so the news cannot be treated as one kingdom's scandal. If he renounces, I have a renunciation already drafted. If we work in parallel without either court ever acknowledging us, we do that and we do not stop until the war does. Tell me which way he is leaning. I will not push him. I will match."* — *Where:* the meeting place at the crossing for the conversation; from there into whichever of Corven's four arcs the campaign opens (`doc/npcs/prince_corven.md` §"Personal goals — later-game"). — *What:* once Corven has chosen, carry word back to her, then carry the matched announcement forward — a sealed letter to the cliffside chamberlain, or to Brumal's heralds, or to the inner practitioners at Fata morgana, depending on the arc. — *Reward:* the war's shape changes. Penelope's choice becomes part of the historical record on the Brumal side. — *Complications:* her family's survival depends on the war "going well"; if she stands with Corven publicly, her own house may disinherit or imprison her before the announcement reaches the crowd. A renunciation in parallel may not be enough; a public reveal may be too much. Two royals must guess at the same hour, from opposite sides of a closed border, what the right shape of the act is. **Prerequisite:** the courier line has run cleanly at least twice.

- **Later-game — bring Brumal's last apprentices to Fata morgana before the desert reaches Cliffside.** *Conditional — opens if the campaign runs long enough that Cliffside is threatened, or if Penelope decides Brumal cannot be saved as a state.* *What the NPC says* (in private, when the party has earned the inner-circle introduction): *"My house will not ask. I will. The shimmer-city has rooms in its inner streets that have sheltered before. They will not take a column of refugees. They will take names — the practitioners they would have taken anyway, the families with craft, the children who can still learn. I want my country's last apprentices walking south while there is still a road. You have walked into Fata morgana on my standing. Walk back south with my list."* — *Where:* **Cliffside hold** to gather names; **Fata morgana**'s inner circle to place them (`doc/places/fata_morgana.md` §"Inner circle"); roadhouses along the way for shelter. — *What:* carry a coded list of named families south; arrange staged passage through the desert with Fata morgana's mighty-spirit-readers running point at each rest; deliver each name to the practitioner who will take them. — *Reward:* an inner-circle relationship at Fata morgana that outlasts the campaign; a piece of Brumal saved that the rest of the kingdom never learns about; Penelope's life choice made and carried. — *Complications:* the Brumal court will name this treason if it learns. The desert between cliff country and Fata morgana is alive with chaos-spirit pressure (`doc/places/southern_desert.md`); guides are essential and not abundant. Fata morgana's inner circle will not take everyone Penelope names; the choosing is hers, and it will haunt her. **Prerequisite:** Fata morgana introduction in hand (see *What she can teach* and *DM notes*).

## How she reads at a glance

- **Sun-marked in winter.** A faint band of darker skin across the bridge of the nose and the high cheekbones, the kind that comes from a year working in a southern courtyard and stays for a long time afterwards. On a Brumal woman it does not match the room.
- **A piece of work in her hand, almost always.** A pendant frame she is filing; a loop of fine silver wire she has been turning while listening; a thin file or a tiny pair of pliers she carries the way another woman carries a fan. The hands continue while she speaks.
- **Court manners ahead of the room.** She has read the situation by the time anyone finishes their introduction. Her courtesies arrive precisely on the beat the court expects, and her real attention has already moved past that surface.
- **A quiet voice.** Not soft — measured. She does not raise it. She waits for the room to come down to where she is speaking.

## Visible patterns

- **Works in the high north-light room every morning.** The room is hers by long standing. A party that has earned access can find her there with the door propped, a piece on the bench in front of her.
- **Attends court only when summoned.** She does not seek the throne room. Her attendance is correct; her presence after the formal hour is rare.
- **Walks the cliff-path at dusk.** Alone or with a single attendant. The path looks south, toward the country the desert is taking. She does this whether the court is watching or not.
- **Receives quietly.** Visitors who have her trust come in by the kitchen-yard rather than the formal stair, and find her in the workshop rather than a receiving room.
- **Does not ride out at night.** Corven is the rider; her own movements are slower, by carriage or by trade-cart with the right people. The risks are different on her side and she has matched them differently.

## At the high north-light room — at Cliffside hold

The room is hers; the hold is Brumal's seat (a place file is forthcoming; for now see `doc/places/southern_desert.md` for the wider region). The standouts on entry:

- **The light.** Cliffside hold sits high on the eastern face of the cliff country; her room takes the cool north-light a metalsmith needs. Broad window, wide sill, stone floor, clean. The light is the room's first impression.
- **The bench.** A long working bench under the window. Files, gravers, a small hand-bellows for soldering, a black-stone slab worn smooth where she draws patterns. **Pieces in progress along the back edge** — three or four, none of them finished, each waiting for a decision the court does not know she has been deferring.
- **The view.** From the window the land falls away south. On clear days the haze on the horizon is the desert's edge. On winter mornings it is closer than it was the winter before. **She works facing it**; the room is arranged so she does not have to turn to see it.

A party brought to this room has already been chosen. She does not work in front of strangers.

## At the meeting place with Corven — the half-ruined roadhouse

Earned. The meeting place is a stone roadhouse at an old border crossing that neither kingdom claims for tax or watch — half a roof, a serviceable hearth, a stable that takes two horses without comment. It is the nearest a place can be to *neither court's*. A party reaches it only by Corven's hand or by careful following of a night ride.

- **A small fire, no light otherwise.** They have learned to keep the building dark from the road. Talk happens at the hearth; sleeping arrangements are spare and prearranged.
- **Two horses, both fast.** Hers, brought up by carriage to the last Brumal posting-house and ridden the final stretch with one trusted attendant. His, the night-ride horse from Scarlet Vale.
- **A specific quiet.** She and Corven, when alone here, are not the prince and princess the courts know. The party walking in on this room walks in on something private.

In this room she is honest. She will receive a courier from the party at this place when she cannot reach them at Cliffside.

## Stories she tells

When the relevant topic comes up and she has decided to answer.

### When asked about her year at Fata morgana — *what the year did to my hands*

> *"I went south at sixteen, with a small retinue and a court letter explaining what they wanted me to bring home. I worked in the front of a shop on the market street for the first season — outer pieces, the work that sells. By the second season the family let me into the back. The patterns there are not for sale. They are the same patterns you see on the door-frames and the well-copings; the work and the wards are one practice. I had two tutors for the inner work and I did not finish with either of them. I had a year. The work needs more. The shop-keepers were kind about the not-finishing; they said it as a kindness, but they were also saying the truth. I came home with what my hands could hold. The rest stayed in their benches."*

### When asked about teaching at Bockthicket — *why nothing came of it*

> *"My father's people sent me to a mining town to teach what I had learned. They had picked the students; they had picked the room; they had named the lesson. I taught what I could. The students were polite. Two were promising. None of them were going to be jewellers in the way the court needed; they were silversmiths, working a different shop, on a different schedule, for a different market. The court wanted the work to come out of that mine and travel as Brumal goods. The people they had chosen could not make that happen and I could not teach them to. I think now I taught it badly because I was angry to be teaching it that way. They sent me back to Cliffside and the plan was, quietly, set down. I do not blame the students. I do not entirely forgive the court."*

### When asked about meeting Corven — *what we did the first time we knew*

*(Private. Earned trust. She does not tell this story above a half-voice.)*

> *"We had met before, the way royals meet — at functions our houses had reasons to attend. The first time we **knew** was at a place I am not going to name to you yet. He had ridden, the way he does now. I had come down from Cliffside with the half-truth I had practised. We spent an evening at a hearth, both of us still in the manners our courts had given us, and somewhere in the second hour the manners stopped being load-bearing. I said something I had not meant to say, and he answered. The reply mattered more than the question. We rode home in opposite directions in the morning and we have not been able to pretend the evening did not happen since. I do not regret it. He does not regret it. The cost is real."*

### When asked about the desert — *what I saw on the way home from Bockthicket*

> *"The road back from the mining country runs along the southern edge for half a day. The summer I came home, the marker stones at the southernmost stretch were under sand at one and a half feet. The next year my brother's traveller-corps reported them at three feet. The villages on that stretch had thinned without anyone announcing it; the people had moved north and the houses had moved south, into the dunes, by the simple action of the desert eating houses. We are not losing a region the way the court's wording wants it. We are losing the country. The court is preparing to call it a war. I am preparing to be honest about what we are actually losing."*

### When asked about Allondo — *what my house thinks of Hannes's kingdom*

*(Court register at court; private register in private.)*

> *"My house remembers the Overseas War as a ledger that did not balance. Allondo got its hero and its proclamation and its quiet decade. We sent men and got fewer men back; the gold did not flow either direction; the silence between our kingdoms after has been called peace, and was not. My house is preparing to call in what the war owed us, with the desert at our back forcing the timing. I have heard this said at council since I was twelve. I do not entirely disagree with the ledger. I do disagree with the proposed payment."*

### When asked whether she will leave Brumal — *the choice if it comes to it*

*(Private. Earned trust. Once the courier line is real.)*

> *"I have considered it. I have not committed. To leave is to forfeit the seat at the table where the war is being decided; to stay is to be inside the house that decides for the war. Corven and I have not asked each other to leave. We have asked each other to remain useful. I will go south, perhaps, with the people who can still travel. I will not go north to him while my house can still be talked out of the war. If the talking ends — that is the conversation we have not had yet."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed at court** — sets her piece of work down. *Then* answers. The pause is small and the room learns nothing it did not already know. The silence before the answer is the answer; the words after are courtesy.
- **When pressed in private** — the file or the wire stays in her hands. She turns it, slower. Looks at the work, not at the speaker. Replies when she has decided what the truth costs to say. Will not be hurried.
- **When distraught** — goes to the window. Hands flat on the sill. Looks south. Will not face the room until the topic has changed or until she has decided she can answer it. The attendant who has served her longest knows not to follow her to the sill.
- **When happy** — a small one-sided smile, slow. Touches the piece in her hand briefly, sets it down, picks it up again. The smile reaches her eyes. Most often when Corven is the conversation, or when a piece has resolved itself in her hand.

| Is brought to her by Corven, vouched for | She receives them at the workshop. The performance is set aside. She offers the seat at the bench, not the chair across the room. |

| Promises discretion | *"Tell me when you are not sure you can keep it. That promise I will trust."* She watches them answer. |

| Carries the courier packet badly — opened, lost, intercepted | Quiet for a long moment. *"Tell me what you saw. All of it."* No anger; assessment. The line closes for that route. Whether she trusts a different route from the same hand is a separate decision; she will take time. |
| Asks to learn the jewelry craft | Yes — once trust is real. *"Sit at the bench. You will start with the file."* The teaching is slow and she will not rush. The inner work is gated past the outer. |

| Threatens her | She does not flinch. *"I am a princess of a house that has been preparing for war for a decade. You are not the first person to threaten me here. The room behind me has more guards than you have noticed. Leave."* |
| Threatens Corven | Cold. *"You have told me what you are, and saved me the discovery. Leave Brumal."* The line closes. |
| Asks for help reaching Fata morgana's inner circle | Trust-gated. With trust, she writes a sealed introduction by name (DM-fill: one of her old tutors). Without it, she will not. The introduction opens the back-bench shop-floors and one inner-circle home. |
| Tells her about the Brumal refugee pressure on the north | Listens carefully. Asks named questions. May, in time, ask the party to carry the list (see her late-game refugee quest). |
| Asks her about her position in the royal line | Briefly. *"I am where I am."* If pressed: *"Specific enough. You did not come here to read the genealogies."* She does not lie; she does not feed the question. |

## What she will not say

- **Corven's name at her own court.** Not under any pressure.
- **The location of the meeting place** to anyone who has not already been brought there.
- **The exact sigil-pattern of Fata morgana's inner ward-work.** Even with full trust, she teaches the craft; she does not loan the patterns out as portable knowledge.
- **What her own father (or whichever cousin holds the seat above her) has said about Allondo in private council.** She will summarise; she will not quote.
- **That she has considered leaving Brumal.** Public denial; private only-with-Corven-or-equivalent-trust.
- **What she has done to delay her family's marriage plans for her.** That work is hers and the court does not know how she did it. She will not explain it now.

## What she can teach

**Trust-gated past the courier work.**

- **Outer jewelry craft** — filing, soldering, simple settings, pattern-drawing on the slab. Open to any serious student once trust is real. A working relationship begins at the bench.
- **Inner ward-craft, in pieces** — the patterns that overlap with Fata morgana's wards. Very slow; not transmittable in single sessions; usable in protective work for the party only after sustained study with her, or with one of her old Fata morgana tutors via her introduction.
- **How to court without losing yourself** — by example. A party member who has watched her at court for a season learns something about *holding two registers at once* that they will not learn from any other Allondan-side noble.
- **Reading the southern country** — how the desert moves, the signs villages give before they fail, what the wells do when the spirits have moved north. Practical; not romantic. She has seen it from the wrong side.

## DM notes

- **Two scenes, same princess.** Court Penelope is the cover; the workshop and the meeting place are the truth. The contrast between them is hers in the same way the contrast at court vs. on the night ride is Corven's. Run both.
- **The exact rank stays open.** The file does not pin Penelope's position in the Brumal royal line. The campaign can place her as a princess of the seat, a royal cousin with a strong claim, or a niece of the current ruler — whichever fits the table best. The behaviours, the year at Fata morgana, the workshop, the meeting place, the courier work are all consistent with any of these.
- **The courier line is the door.** A party that runs a packet successfully becomes part of the conspiracy on the Brumal side. A party that does not, never reaches her in private. Most of the late-game work is gated behind this.
- **Fata morgana access is real and earnable.** Penelope's introduction is the cleanest soft-entry the party has into the city's inner streets (`doc/places/fata_morgana.md` §"Inner circle"). A DM running the southern-desert arc can use this as a structural connection between Brumal's collapse and Fata morgana's role in absorbing what survives.
- **The four arcs are Corven's; Penelope matches.** If the campaign goes into public-reveal, Penelope reveals at the same hour from her side. If renunciation, she has a renunciation drafted. If parallel-collaborator, she keeps her seat and works from inside it. If breakdown, she is the one carrying the consequence on the southern side. Her file is the mirror of Corven's; do not run her as a passive recipient of his decision.
- **She is not a damsel.** A DM playing her as endangered-and-waiting plays a weaker version of her. She is a working political actor whose risks are her own. Players who try to rescue her without consulting her will receive a polite, exact rebuke.
- **The desert is the constant pressure.** Every season the conversation with Penelope leans more toward the refugee work. A DM tracking the campaign's calendar can use her as a quiet barometer of how late the world is.
- Lore cross-references: Brumal's collapse and the desert's shape in `doc/places/southern_desert.md` and `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"Brumal's collapse"; Fata morgana's inner circle and Penelope's old apprenticeship in `doc/places/fata_morgana.md` §"The market — jewelry craft" and §"Inner circle"; Corven's mirror file and the four arcs in `doc/npcs/prince_corven.md`; Linar harbour back-rooms as the Allondan-side handover point in `doc/places/linar_harbour.md`; Nadine as a possible carrier in `doc/npcs/nadine.md`; the Leatherman channel through Fienna in `doc/npcs/queen_fienna.md`.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Royal lady of Brumal**, exact rank within the line open. Brought up at Cliffside hold.
- **Sent to Fata morgana** at sixteen, for a committed year, on a court errand to bring the jewelry craft home and uplift Brumal's mining trade. Learned the outer craft well; the inner work she did not finish.
- **Sent to Bockthicket** afterwards to teach. The plan did not land; her teaching there did not work; the court set the plan aside.
- **In love with Prince Corven of Allondo** for a year longer than is safe. They meet at a roadhouse at a half-abandoned border crossing. They correspond by sealed packet through carriers neither court watches.
- **Remains at Cliffside** by choice, holding her seat at the table where the war is being argued.
