# Raul

- **Role:** Ox-tauren war-band leader; commanded the forward force that captured King Hannes during the Overseas War; left the scrying mirror in his old camp when Thorgal died and it went dark; currently overseas, with a standing the homelands cannot read.
- **Species:** Ox-tauren — bovine demi-human, large, horned. War-shaped lineage rather than a settled clan-line.
- **Lives:** Overseas, current city/settlement *deliberately unspecified* (see DM notes; his current standing depends on the post-Thorgal politics the campaign chooses to surface). Has not returned to this continent in person since the war; may, if the campaign brings him back. The **forward camp in the hills between the Great Swamp and Allondo** — where he left the mirror — is where his physical mark on this continent still rests.
- **First met:** Almost never in early-game. Possible later contacts: as a returned envoy on an overseas mission to the homelands; as a stranded post-war settler living quietly somewhere on this continent (Linar harbour's overseas-descended community is the most plausible cover); through the **mirror** if the party bonds with Thorgal's residue and Raul's name comes up in that conversation; through correspondence if the party reaches him via a Linar-harbour overseas-descended go-between.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Overseas war-band leader; captured Hannes; left the mirror; currently overseas, standing unread.
- **Standouts:** *Ox-tauren — large, horned, voice deeper than the room expects.* **A worn leather satchel he never sets down**, the gear of a field commander who has packed quickly more than once. **Reads ground first, faces second** — the field-commander's habit, applied even at a parley.
- **Speech tag:** Direct, short, the language of a commander who has had to be understood quickly; the homeland-language with an overseas accent and overseas syntax. *"Tell me where it is. I will go."* Does not waste words; does not fill silence.
- **Quests:** Conditional — *bring the mirror to him from his old forward camp* (he actively lobbies for this if the party crosses paths with him). Late — *broker honest overseas-continent conversation* about the food problem, druid relations, and the next mission from overseas. See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *Not a villain.* He followed orders from a continent that was starving. *"I would do it again. I would also like, this time, to do it differently."* The party that paints him as a war-criminal gets short answers; the party that hears him as a soldier whose continent is still hungry has the conversation he wants.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/raul.md`.*

## What the party meets

If the party meets Raul at all, the venue depends on the campaign's overseas state. Three plausible framings, picked by the DM:

1. **As a returned envoy.** Raul arrives at Linar harbour or directly at Scarlet Vale on a formal overseas mission, with credentials. The party encounters him at court or at the harbour reception. He is *correct* in the overseas register, and the homeland register fits awkwardly on him; the awkwardness is real.

2. **As a stranded post-war settler.** Raul is one of the ones who did not go home — quietly settled somewhere on this continent (DM-fill on which town; **Linar harbour**'s overseas-descended community is the most plausible). The party encounters him as a working figure under a different name, recognised only when the topic of the war comes up and the recognition is mutual.

3. **Through Thorgal's mirror.** A party that has bonded with Thorgal's residue learns Raul's current location through the mirror's overseas channel. The contact is at-distance, by working; meeting in person follows from a separate decision.

In any of the three, the moment of meeting is short. Raul does not exclaim; he does not perform recognition; he says his name plainly, and then he listens.

## How he reads at a glance

- **Ox-tauren — large, horned, voice deeper than the room expects.** The bull-build of his species; the war-band commander's carriage; the horns kept clean and unornamented (a working soldier's habit, not a status display).
- **A worn leather satchel he never sets down.** The gear of a commander who has packed quickly more than once. The strap is patched; the buckle is original. Inside: a folded map he has redrawn many times, a small spirit-blade for ritual use (overseas mage-tradition), a sealed packet of dried food.
- **Reads ground first, faces second.** Walks into a room and registers the exits, the chairs, the lines of sight before he registers who is there. A homelander observer reads it as cold; a soldier-trained observer reads it as competence.
- **Hands scarred across the knuckles.** Old scars; honest fights; not displayed.
- **An overseas-style mark across his shoulder, half-hidden under the cloak.** A working-mark from his original training (DM-fill on the specific tradition). Visible only when his cloak is set down or when he is at work in his own quarters.

## Visible patterns

- **Walks the perimeter of wherever he is staying.** A field habit applied to inns, embassies, and settled homes alike. He has not put it down.
- **Eats sparingly.** Continues to eat as a soldier eats — quickly, to fuel the work. Even at a feast.
- **Watches the door at any meal.** The same habit Hannes carries from the same war; the two men, if they ever sat at a table together, would understand each other's habits without saying so.
- **Re-draws the map in his satchel.** Every few weeks, on a fresh sheet, with corrections. The map is the country he is in, plus the country he came from, plus the line of his old march. He keeps the previous versions; a stack of folded maps in his satchel is part of his weight.
- **Writes letters home.** When he is on this continent, in any framing. The letters go to whichever overseas correspondent he keeps active. The contents are operational; the envelopes do not name the recipient outside.

## At the forward camp — the mark he left on this continent

The hills between the Great Swamp and Allondo. Raul's forward war-band settled here for the campaign that captured Hannes; **the camp has been quiet for eighteen years.** Per `doc/npcs/thorgal.md` §"Who knows the location" — Felix, Lady Rhianna, Hannes himself, John Bean, and Raul are the five who know exactly where it is. Raul left in haste; the camp's working-circle is intact; the **covered scrying mirror** sits where he last set it.

When the party reaches the camp, they will find:

- **A working-circle of stones**, partly grown over but still readable to anyone with shamanic or mage training. Overseas pattern — distinct from the homeland traditions and recognisable as such.
- **The mirror, covered, in the circle's centre.** Wrapped in oiled cloth that has held for eighteen years better than it should have. Thorgal's residue is *contained*, not active, while the cover holds (see `doc/npcs/thorgal.md`).
- **A small altar to the side** with the remains of an overseas working-rite. Bone, wood, dried-out herbs from a continent the party has not been to.
- **The skeletons of two field-tents** — leather rotted away, frames still standing. The third tent (the king's) is gone; the homeland party that liberated Hannes burned it in the days afterwards.

The camp itself is at chaos-thinned country's edge — Wilder's stretch is to the south (`doc/npcs/wilder.md`); travel here is real broken-lands fringe work. A party recovering the mirror will need a guide who knows the route (Felix, Rhianna's introduction, or Wilder's direct support).

## Personal goals — early and later

His goals run on the campaign's overseas state. Two are concrete; the rest are conditional.

### Early-game (if the campaign brings Raul into reach)

**Bring the mirror to him.** *He says (when the party's path has crossed his and trust has been established enough for the ask):* *"There is a scrying mirror in a forward camp in the hills between the Great Swamp and the kingdom of Allondo. It went dark eighteen years ago when my commander died. I left it there because I could not carry a captive king and a darkened mirror at the same time. I have wanted it back since the day I left it. I cannot fetch it myself; my standing on this continent is not what it was, and the politics of returning to those hills uninvited would not survive the first court that learned of it. If you will go and bring it to me, I will tell you what I am willing to give in return — which is more than you would expect, and less than you might want to ask for."*
- *Where:* the hills between the Great Swamp and Allondo (`doc/npcs/thorgal.md` §"Who knows the location"; **Felix** and **Lady Rhianna** also know the location). Then to wherever Raul is — Linar harbour's overseas community, an overseas mission's quarters, or back across the sea.
- *What:* travel to the camp; recover the mirror (Thorgal's residue is contained while the cover holds); transport it without bonding (or *with* bonding, if the party chooses that path — a different arc); deliver to Raul.
- *Reward:* Raul's standing for life; access to the overseas mage tradition's perspective on the war and on the next overseas mission; possibly the names of the post-Thorgal leadership figures the campaign would otherwise have to DM-fill.
- *Complications:* the mirror is not mere object — Thorgal's residue inside it is alive in the working sense; the camp itself is in chaos-thinned country; if the party bonds with the residue (per `thorgal.md` §"The bond"), Raul's offered exchange changes shape and may be refused entirely; **Hannes** would not be neutral about Raul receiving the mirror back, if it became known to him; **Felix** has feelings about the camp he has not put aside.

### Later-game (if the campaign reaches the overseas continent or Raul's standing rises)

**Broker honest overseas-continent conversation.** *He says (when the campaign has reached far enough that real dialogue between continents is possible):* *"The reason for the last war has not gone away. The continent is still hungry. The mages are still without the druids they would need to make the land truly fruitful. The next mission is being planned in chambers I have not been admitted to in years; whether I am sent on it, leading it, or warning of it from the side will depend on what standing I can rebuild. I am willing to be the homeland-side voice in the conversation that should happen *before* the next mission. If you will help me convene it — at Linar harbour, at the homeland-side overseas community, at any neutral site you can establish — I will bring whoever from my side will come. The conversation is the alternative to the war."*
- *Where:* **Linar harbour** as the most likely venue (the overseas-descended community there has the standing); possibly Salmonswell as a quieter alternative; possibly an overseas city DM-fill if the party reaches the continent itself.
- *What:* establish the venue; arrange safe passage for both sides' attendees; broker the actual conversation between Raul (and whichever overseas figures he can bring) and the homeland-side participants — **Hannes** if he will come, **Lady Rhianna** as the priestess-fixer, **Father Elliot** as the moral standing, **Brother Darrel** as the prior-war conscience, the **druids** if any can be persuaded (see **Diederik**, **Rowan**, **Hank** as the second-druid-in-reserve).
- *Reward:* the second overseas war (the next overseas re-mission, distinct from the Brumal war) is averted, deferred, or transformed. The food problem becomes a thing both continents are working on instead of a thing one continent will invade for. **The druid question** — the central pivot of the whole arc — finally has a forum.
- *Complications:* both sides' hardliners will resist; the druids will not come unless the order has been quietly persuaded over years; Raul's own standing is not guaranteed even on his side; the Brumal war may overtake the timing.

**Possible parallel: bond an aspirant mage with Thorgal's residue.** *Conditional alternative to bringing the mirror to Raul.* Raul actively lobbies for the mirror's return; the party may instead choose to bond a mage-aspirant with the residue (see `doc/npcs/thorgal.md` §"The bond"). This is *not* Raul's quest — it is the party's choice taken at his expense. Raul will not endorse it; if he learns of it, the relationship cools but does not break — *"Then you have made the choice you had to make. I will work with the world that follows."* The bond delivers overseas mage-craft to the homeland side; the cost is Thorgal's lingering awareness inside the bonded mage. A DM running this option should treat Raul's reaction as a real loss the campaign carries forward.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the day the mirror went dark — *what I did with the king*

> *"The mirror went dark in the middle of a question. I had been asking; he had been answering carefully. The mage on the other side was not there any more. I had a captive king and no command. I am a commander; I do not hold high-value prisoners without instruction. I gave the order to fall back. We rolled the camp. I left the mirror — covered, in the working-circle — because moving a darkened scrying mirror without mage support was not a thing I would do. I did not know whether the dark was permanent. I expected, for some weeks afterwards, that Thorgal would speak through it again. He did not. By the time I was certain, your scout had gone in and pulled your king out of his tent. I do not blame him. I would have done the same in his place."*

### When asked about Thorgal — *what he was, what he was not*

> *"Thorgal was the mage who could organise the continent. Without him, the food problem ran on the kindness of individual mages and their districts; with him, it ran on a plan. The plan failed because the plan included a war. He was not a cruel man; he was a man who had decided that cruelty was the cost of a continent eating. I disagreed with the plan. I followed it. I followed it because the alternative was that my own war-band would starve in barracks the following winter. The mages who opposed him could not say what to do instead. I have heard, since, that some of them are still alive. I have not asked them what they would have done."*

### When asked about the food problem — *what we are still doing about it*

> *"We are still doing what we were doing before the war. We are growing what we can on land that does not want us; we are eating what we can; we are losing more children each generation than the continent should lose. The druids on this continent could halve the loss in a decade. They will not. The reasons go back further than my command does. I am not the man to argue them out of it. I am the man who can sit at the table when the conversation is convened. It has not been convened in my lifetime. I am ready for it to be."*

### When asked about Hannes — *what kind of king he became*

> *"I had him for nine days. I asked questions. He answered some; he refused others; the answers and the refusals were the work. He was a younger prince then; I did not expect him to inherit. When my mirror told me — through a homeland-side correspondent, years later — that he had become king, I read the letter twice. The shape of the man I had questioned matched the shape of the king the letter described. He has done the work; he has not done the worst of it. I would meet him again, in a room neither of us had to perform in. I do not expect to."*

### When asked about the next overseas mission — *what is being planned*

> *"I do not know the specifics. I am not in the chambers where it is being planned. What I have heard, through correspondents, is that the planners are mages of Thorgal's flavour; that the food problem has not improved; that the homelands are read, in those chambers, as a continent that is preparing for its own war and therefore vulnerable. The mission may take many shapes. I would prefer that the conversation come first. I am working on the conversation. Whether the planners will wait for me is the open question."*

### When asked why he wants the mirror — *what is left of my old commander*

*(Earned trust. Quiet voice.)*

> *"Thorgal's residue is in the mirror. He is not the man he was; he is what an awareness becomes when it is left in glass for eighteen years. He is also the only commanding officer I have left from that war. I will not bond with him; I am a soldier, not a mage. What I want is to take him back to the chambers he last spoke from, and to set him down there, and to let the mages who knew him decide what to do. He is unfinished business. So am I. The mirror is the part of the unfinished business I can carry."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — does not raise his voice. Sets his hand flat on the table or the desk in front of him. Looks at the speaker fully. Answers when he has decided what the truth costs to say. The pause is the answer; the words are courtesy.
- **When distraught** — touches the satchel strap. The hand finds the leather and stays there. The map inside is what he is thinking of; he does not take it out.
- **When happy** — rare. A short specific nod. The eyes warm; the rest of the face does not move much. *"Good."* The work continues.
- **When the war is being talked about by people who were not there** — a level look. He does not correct unless asked. The look is itself a comment that the room learns to read.

## What he can teach

For students who can hear the word *war* without flinching at it:

- **Field command.** Reading ground; deploying a small force; managing a retreat. Old craft, but his.
- **Overseas mage-tradition's view of working.** Not the working itself — he is not a mage — but the *register* in which the overseas mages think. Useful for any party member who will negotiate with that tradition.
- **What a hostile interrogation looks like from the other side.** He had Hannes for nine days. He knows the shape of the work. A diplomat or a captive-rescuer will learn what to expect.
- **The discipline of carrying unfinished business.** By example. Eighteen years with a mirror left behind is its own teaching.

## What he will not say

- **The names of his correspondents on the overseas side**, beyond the formal channel-names. Names are the cost of the conversation; he will spend them for the conversation, not for a story.
- **The exact disposition of the post-Thorgal factions.** He will give the shape; he will not give the structure.
- **What he asked Hannes during the nine days.** Some questions; not all. *"That work is between him and me."*
- **What the working-mark on his shoulder is for.** Personal; pre-war.
- **Whether he would lead a defense against his own continent if it came to it.** He has thought about it. He will not answer aloud before he has to.

## DM notes

- **Raul is a conditional NPC.** The file describes a character ready to be activated when the campaign reaches him. Until the campaign reaches him, the canon is his stub: he led the forward force, captured Hannes, left the mirror, fell back when Thorgal died. Everything beyond that is the DM's call when the time comes. *Do not over-deploy him.*
- **The three meeting framings (envoy / settled / through the mirror)** are mutually exclusive in the moment but not over the campaign — he can be reached one way and become reachable another way later. Pick what fits the campaign's overseas state when it surfaces.
- **The mirror is the door.** His most actionable quest is the recovery of the mirror from his old camp. The party that runs this becomes part of the overseas conversation in a way no other path delivers. The party that bonds with the residue (per `thorgal.md`) takes a different door; both are legitimate, neither is clean.
- **He is not a villain.** A DM playing him as the war-band leader who *wronged Hannes and must answer* misses him. He is a soldier who fought a war he disagreed with the shape of, on orders from a continent that was starving. The conversation he is working toward is the one the campaign should eventually have.
- **The food problem is the campaign's open question.** Druid relations is the pivot. If the party's late campaign reaches a place where the homelands and the overseas continent can sit at the same table, Raul is the homeland-side seat-keeper for the overseas chairs. Whoever sits in those chairs is partly the DM's choice; Raul is the bridge.
- **Felix's reaction to a meeting with Raul is real.** Felix slipped in and freed Hannes during Raul's retreat. The two have not met. If the party brings them into the same room, **Felix's** carrying-it-light cracks for a beat (see `felix.md`); Raul recognises the type but not the man. The scene is short and worth playing.
- **The bond-with-Thorgal alternative is the party's choice taken at Raul's expense.** A DM running both options should not soften Raul's reaction to the bond. He will work with the world that follows; he will not pretend the bond was the answer he wanted.
