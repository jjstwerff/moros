# Raul

- **Role:** Ox-tauren war-band leader; commanded the forward force that captured King Hannes in the Overseas War
- **Species:** Ox-tauren (overseas demi-human species)
- **Location:** Overseas — fell back to the bigger forces after the mirror broke; current whereabouts not established
- **Status:** Alive

## Summary

Raul led the small forward-facing war band that the overseas force sent ahead of its main body during the Overseas War. His mission was reconnaissance: to capture high-value figures and bring back information about the homelands to **Thorgal**, the mage commanding the operation from overseas. His band captured **King Hannes** — the warning Hannes ignored was about Raul's scouts — and Raul was in the process of extracting useful intelligence from him when the situation changed.

What changed it was **the breaking of the mirror**. Thorgal had been directing the forward operation remotely through a scrying mirror; when John Bean's ironwood arm severed the connection (and, at the overseas end, killed Thorgal himself), Raul's mirror went dark in the middle of a conversation and did not come back. Without command, and unwilling to hold a captive king in the forward position without mage support, he fell back to join the bigger forces. In the confusion of that retreat, **Felix** got in and freed Hannes.

Raul was a competent commander, not a villain. He was a demi-human officer following orders from a mage faction whose decision to go to war was itself driven by hunger on the overseas continent (see CAMPAIGN.md §"The Overseas War"). The fact that the invasion he was fighting was a famine-response rather than a conquest is not lost on him, even if he has never had reason to say so to anyone on the homeland side.

## Relationships

- **Thorgal** — his commanding mage, now deceased. Raul's last communication with Thorgal was through the scrying mirror that broke at the moment of Thorgal's death.
- **King Hannes** — his prisoner. They know each other's faces.
- **Felix** — the raccoon scout who freed Hannes during Raul's withdrawal. Raul does not necessarily know Felix's name, but he knows *someone* slipped in during the retreat.
- **John Bean** — the homelander responsible for breaking the mirror and killing Thorgal. Raul does not necessarily know which individual that was, but the method (an ironwood-driven force reaching through a scrying connection) is the kind of detail the overseas mage tradition would want studied. If Raul ever returns to this continent, John is the one he will be asked about.

## Raul and the mirror

Raul knows **exactly where his forward war-band's settlement was** — the hills between the Great Swamp and the kingdom of Allondo, well off any patrolled road (see `doc/npcs/thorgal.md` §"Who knows the location"). He left the scrying mirror there when his band fell back after it went dark, and he knows what he left. If the party crosses paths with him in any future arc — diplomatic envoy, returned overseas figure, any of the DM-fill framings below — he will **actively direct them to the location** and **ask them to bring the mirror to him**.

His reasons are not hidden. The mirror is his connection back to overseas mage-work (rare to have on this continent), and the residue inside it — Thorgal's lingering awareness — is his former commander, the man whose death he lived through without finishing the command chain cleanly. The mirror is unfinished business. He wants it, and the party, if they trust him enough to listen, is the simplest means he has to retrieve it.

A party that carries the mirror *to* Raul rather than keeping it themselves gets a different arc from a party that chooses to bond with the residue (see `doc/npcs/thorgal.md` §"The bond"). Both paths are legitimate; Raul is actively lobbying for the first.

## Open questions — for DM fill

The overseas political structure after Thorgal's death is **deliberately unspecified** (see `thorgal.md` and CAMPAIGN.md). Raul's current situation is therefore genuinely unknowable from this continent and should be decided by the DM when a campaign actually needs him. Candidate framings to pick from when the time comes:

- Did Raul go home after the war, or is he among the stranded overseas demi-humans now living quietly on this continent?
- What is his standing with the post-Thorgal overseas leadership? A field commander who lost his scrying link and had to retreat has a specific political position — liability, witness, or promoted for saving what he could.
- If there is a next diplomatic or military mission from overseas, is Raul on it? Is he leading it, resisting it, or being used as a recognisable face by whichever faction has taken power?
