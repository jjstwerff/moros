# Wilfred

- **Role:** Badger farmer and Elmsfield's *almost-blacksmith*; [Bridget](bridget.md)'s husband; the farm half of the Inn-and-farm household.
- **Species:** Badger. Shorter than his wife, wider across the chest; heavier hands.
- **Lives:** The farm on the edge of Elmsfield — his farmhouse, his forge-shed, his fields. Goes into the square on market days and to [the Inn](../places/elmsfield_inn.md) only when Bridget asks.
- **First met:** At his farm when the party needs metalwork done; at his stall on market days; or, on the rare evening, holding up one end of the bar while Bridget runs the other.

## What the party meets

The party comes to the farm because something needs hammering — a shoe, a hinge, a broken hoop, a tool split at the ferrule. They walk up the track past the barn, and the forge-shed is the nearer of the two outbuildings. Wilfred is in the doorway in a leather apron that has seen a generation of use, wiping his hands on a rag. He looks them over, looks at what they are carrying, and says *"Show me."* The smith-greeting. The farmer is somewhere behind the smith today.

He is **shorter and thicker than Bridget**, the way an old oak is shorter and thicker than a young one. Where her body is trader-quick, his is work-heavy. The children call them *mother-and-father-like-two-different-weather-fronts*; he likes the line and will not repeat it.

## How he reads at a glance

- A stocky Badger in an apron that is more scar than leather. Sleeves rolled past his forearms; the fur there singed in places and grown back paler. **Hands twice the size they ought to be**, fingers thick from hammering, nails kept short with a file at his belt.
- **Moves between two speeds.** At the forge he is *fast* — the speed of a man who knows the metal and has the window before it cools. Everywhere else he is *slow* — the farmer's speed, the animal-keeper's speed, the pace that wears out nothing including him.
- **Quiet until asked.** He will not volunteer opinion in a room. Ask him a specific question and the answer is considered, short, and precise.
- **A small pouch of horseshoe nails at his belt**, always. The farmer's habit; a shoe comes loose in the field, you fix it where it fell.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **At the forge most mornings for an hour or two** — the stacked work from the town, done in the morning cool. By mid-morning he is out in the fields unless the pile is high.
- **In the fields the rest of the day.** Plowing, mending, haying, whatever the season wants. A pair of oxen he has had since before Bridget met him.
- **At his barn before dawn.** He mucks his own barn and has never let anyone else do it. *"It's how I wake up."*
- **Elly and Nina pass him the farm's end of the day** — the two daughters run the ferry between farm and Inn most afternoons.
- **In the Inn on specific evenings — never regularly.** Bridget sends a child when she wants him. He comes. He does not stay long. They sit at the same corner of the bar when he is there.

## At the forge-shed

One room, open front, a shingled roof held up on posts the tree-line supplies. A stone forge at the back, a single anvil, a bellows he rebuilt himself, a rack of tongs and hammers on the wall in the order he uses them.

- **The rack is tidy in a way the rest of the farm is not.** He has a smith's order here and a farmer's tolerance of mess everywhere else. The party will notice the discrepancy if they walk the rest of the yard.
- **A coal pile on one side, charcoal on the other.** He buys both because what he burns depends on the work.
- **A shelf of *almost-finished* things** — a knife with no handle, two brackets for a gate he has not hung, a specific hinge he has measured three times and never cut. Elmsfield is too small to keep him busy at one craft; the shelf is what happens in the gaps.
- **A small framed line of iron shaved from the horn of his anvil** on a hook above the forge. It was his father's. He has never remade the frame.

## At the fields

Worked plots behind and beside the farmhouse; a fence line that wants mending every spring; oxen in the far pasture; a vegetable patch near the kitchen door that Bridget's children tend when they're at the farm.

- **The plots run well but plain.** He is not reading farming manuals (contrast Hank); he farms the way his father farmed him to farm, and it works. *"You fix what isn't working. What's working you leave."*
- **The soil-line along the fence is where he does his thinking.** A party that walks a row with him will get the longest answers of any scene at the farm.
- **Winter is for tool-mending.** By first frost the forge-shed's *almost-finished* shelf has nothing on it. He uses the dark months to catch up.

## At the Inn — when he's there

See [`../places/elmsfield_inn.md`](../places/elmsfield_inn.md) for the room. Wilfred is *an occasional presence* inside it — Bridget's summons, a season-change night, a wake, the night before a farm decision she wants to make with him across a table instead of at their own kitchen.

When he is in the Inn he sits at the bar end opposite Bridget's working corner. He nurses one drink for the whole evening. The regulars know not to pull him into the room; the room comes to him when the room wants him.

## What he will tell — and when

| Topic | When he'll talk |
|---|---|
| Smithing work — rates, timelines, what's possible | Immediately. This is the job. |
| Farming, seasons, animals | Freely. He will walk a row with the asker and keep talking. |
| Bridget and the children | Briefly; plainly. He does not dwell. |
| The ironwood he cannot work | With a dry smile, if asked. The irony is not lost on him. |
| Master Darius at World Edge | Freely. The respect is real; the path there is not his. |
| What smithing he was trained for and did not keep doing | Once asked, and only if the asker seems serious about craft. |
| Badger-clan matters | Carefully. Bridget handles most of it; he backs her. |
| The hidden wartime hoard | He does not know specifics. *"Bridget keeps that. I keep the farm."* |
| The upper floors of the Inn | Interested but not pushing. Will help if the party's plan is credible; the labor is his kind of work. |

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up and he chooses to answer.

### When asked why he didn't go to World Edge for the craft — *the road I didn't take*

> *"I was sent up to World Edge for a summer when I was young. Master Darius — he was already a master then — watched me work for a week and said I could come back and train under him if I wanted it. I walked home thinking about it the whole way. When I got to Elmsfield, Bridget was new in the Inn and the horses hadn't been shod properly in a season. I stayed. I have not regretted it, which is a different thing from saying I don't think about it."*

### When asked about Bridget — *how we divide the house*

> *"She runs the Inn. I run the farm. The children run between us. We made the split the first year and we have not had to redraw it. I don't go into her room while she's working; she doesn't come into mine. One evening a week she comes home. Every few weeks I go into town for her. It is the most peaceful marriage in Elmsfield and we both know how we earned it."*

### When asked about the ironwood — *the material I cannot work*

> *"I live inside an ironwood tree and I could not put a mark on it if I worked every tool I own against it for a year. The old mages could. Nobody else can. It is the kind of fact that will humble a man who might otherwise get proud about his trade. I come home from the Inn, I walk past the trunk, I put my hand on it. I remember."*

### When asked about farming — *what the work is*

> *"You fix what isn't working. What's working you leave. You watch the animals. You watch the weather. You put the seed in when the ground wants it, not when the almanac says. Most of the farming is paying attention. The rest is a strong back."*

### When asked about the town — *what Elmsfield is*

> *"A valley. A tree. A road through. Enough to feed us, enough to trade a bit, enough to know everyone. It's not a place for ambition. It is a place for keeping a thing alive. Those are different tasks. I chose the second one."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — wipes his hands on his apron slowly, twice. Sets whatever he is holding down. Looks at the simple thing in the question first, and answers that part. Does not follow the asker around the rest.
- **When distraught** — goes to the forge. The hammer picks up a specific three-beat rhythm that is not work-rhythm. A party member who can hear it from outside knows to leave him alone until it stops.
- **When happy** — a slow chuckle that starts in the chest. Rests a hand on the nearest shoulder — ox, child, customer — and leaves it there a moment before moving on.

## If the party does something — what he does

| If the party... | He responds by... |
|---|---|
| Brings him standard work (shoeing, tool repair, nails, hinges) | Quoted fair, done well, often faster than promised. Pays himself in goodwill at the Inn that evening. |
| Brings him specialised or decorative work | Honest about his limits. *"I can do this. For that you want Master Darius; it's a long trip but it's the right trip."* |
| Offers to pry the planks out of the Inn's upper floor | He is interested. *"I can do the hammering. I can do the watching. Three hands and a week's nights and we're in."* A credible plan earns him; he will go to Bridget with the party to propose it. |
| Asks him to try ironwood | A smile. *"You can watch me dull a chisel on it if you want to pay for the chisel. I will not promise you a chip."* He will not waste real time on it. |
| Insults Bridget in his hearing | Sets the hammer down. *"Say that to her face or don't say it at all. You are not welcome at the forge again tonight."* He does not fight; he does not need to. |
| Asks him to travel — far, long, expensive | Declines. *"Someone has to be here when a shoe comes loose."* He means it kindly. |
| Brings him scrap metal from somewhere interesting | Very interested. Looks it over; asks its history; may offer work for it in trade. The shelf of *almost-finished* things benefits. |
| Threatens his children | One warning, quiet and flat. *"Do not."* If repeated, he and Bridget close the household against the party and the Badger clan hears within a day. |
| Offers him real money for a major custom piece | Takes the commission seriously; quotes plainly; delivers exactly what he promised and no more. He is not undersold; he is also not wasting anyone's time. |
| Wants him to teach | Sees *who* is asking before deciding. A student who shows up at first light and is willing to do the mucking as well as the hammer-work is accepted. Anyone who wants the forge without the barn is turned away. |

## What he can teach

- **Blacksmithing — basic level.** Horseshoes, nails, tool repair, simple hinges, a serviceable knife. Given to students who muck the barn first. He will be honest that the advanced work wants Master Darius.
- **Farming — the pay-attention method.** How to read soil, animals, weather, a plow's complaint. Taught by walking rows; not by lectures.
- **Animal husbandry.** Oxen and the working animals of the farm. Practical, unsentimental.
- **Quiet judgement.** Not a formal lesson; a habit students pick up from being near him long enough.

## What he will not say

- **Specifics of the wartime hoard.** Not his to discuss; he does not ask Bridget and she does not tell him beyond what she needs to.
- **What he thinks about Master Darius's choice to stay at World Edge.** He has opinions; he keeps them. That craft life is not his to judge.
- **Which of the Elmsfield townspeople still owe him for work never paid.** He carries the ledger in his head; he does not read it aloud.

## DM notes

- **He is the Inn file's husband half.** The physical Inn lives in `../places/elmsfield_inn.md`; Wilfred lives on the farm and visits the Inn only when Bridget asks. A DM using the Inn at the table should use Bridget as the Inn's voice and bring Wilfred in for specific moments — a season-change conversation, backing Bridget on a decision, a scene that needs the marriage visible.
- **The upstairs-planks labor problem has his name on it.** A party proposing to reopen the Inn's upper floor needs hands, and Wilfred's are the obvious local hands. He is a credible first recruit; pairing him with party labour solves the Labor half of the problem (the Closure half stays Bridget's). See `../places/elmsfield_inn.md` §"The staircase — and the upstairs problem."
- **Master Darius is his road-not-taken.** A party travelling to World Edge can carry his regards; a party bringing back news of Darius lifts him for an evening. This is quiet texture, not a quest.
- **The ironwood he cannot work is his private humbling.** Do not play it as frustration. Play it as a fact he carries easily, because it keeps him a farmer as much as a smith, which is what he wanted.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Badger-clan Elmsfield native.** Grew up on the farm he now runs; his father was the previous smith, also part-time.
- **Trained at World Edge one summer** under Master Darius. Was invited back to apprentice fully. Chose Elmsfield and Bridget instead, and has held that choice without regret.
- **Married Bridget** the year she gave up on the city and took over the Inn. The two operations — farm and Inn — have been run in parallel ever since, with the children ferrying between them.
- **No secret.** His is the quieter kind of NPC file: a man who has made his choices, knows what they cost, and lives inside them without hiding anything significant. His secret, if he has one, is that he sometimes wonders what the Darius apprenticeship would have made of him.

## Relationships

- Husband of **[Innkeeper Bridget](bridget.md)**.
- Father of many children. Daughters **Elly** and **Nina** work the Inn's morning floor (see Bridget's file); other children divide between farm and Inn.
- Part of Elmsfield's **Badger clan** concentration with **Bridget**, **[Corné Dunham](corne_dunham.md)**, and possibly **[Ralph Overhill](ralph_overhill.md)**. See `../claude/LORE.md` §"Clans and their shape."
- Professional respect for **[Master Darius](master_darius.md)** of World Edge; the party can carry messages in either direction.
