# Wilfred

- **Role:** Badger farmer and Elmsfield's *almost-blacksmith*; Bridget's husband; the farm half of the Inn-and-farm household
- **Species:** Badger
- **Location:** Elmsfield
- **Status:** Alive

## Summary

Husband of **Innkeeper Bridget**. Runs the farm attached to the household — the side of their life Bridget has little interest in. They have a slew of children working between farm and Inn.

He is also **the closest thing Elmsfield has to a blacksmith**. He trained the craft in his youth, has the tools, has the skill. What he does not have is enough work. Elmsfield is too small and too quiet to keep a full-time smith busy — the volume of horseshoes, tool repairs, nails, and farm-implement mending the town generates is not enough to live on. So he is primarily a farmer, and a part-time smith when the town needs him, and that is how his days run.

He is not resentful about it. He is a man who knows what his town is and what it isn't. When someone needs metalwork, they find him. When someone needs a barn mended, that is him too. He is the only person in Elmsfield who can make a new hinge; he is also the one who mucks out his own barn in the morning. He holds both of those facts in his hands without complaining.

There is a small quiet irony in this, though — Wilfred is a modest smith living with his wife *inside an ironwood tree he cannot work*. Even Master Darius of World Edge could not work that material with any tools in use today (only the ancient mages could). If Wilfred thinks about it, he thinks about it in private.

## Relationships

- Married to **Innkeeper Bridget**.
- Shared parent of many children who help at farm and Inn.
- Part of the Elmsfield **Badger clan** concentration (with Bridget, **Corné Dunham**, and **Ralph Overhill**). See LORE.md §"Clans and their shape."
- The party will need him if they need metalwork done in Elmsfield: tool repair, horseshoeing, nail-making, basic crafting. For anything beyond basic, the nearest answer is **Master Darius** in World Edge — which is a long trip, so Wilfred usually wins the work by default.
