# Eric Randell

- **Role:** Fox-folk farmer with a flock of strange birds; *also* a druid in undercover observation of Elder Diederik on behalf of the formal druidic order; a thinking man who will not act against his own judgement to satisfy his sponsors.
- **Species:** Fox folk. Mid-life; quick on his feet; the wrangler's habits visible if a party watches the hands rather than the cover.
- **Lives:** **His own farm**, plausibly half a day from Elmsfield — close enough to keep incidental track of Diederik, far enough that the visit-pattern does not read as surveillance. The farm has a flock of birds, **Gato-walker** (his Dromo transport mount) among them. He travels from the farm on Gato-walker for long journeys and on foot or by cart for short ones.
- **First met:** **At Bridget's Inn**, most often — he is a regular, on a rougher schedule than Nadine, who pays his tab and listens longer than he speaks. Or at his farm, if the party is sent there for a creature he is rumoured to keep. Or *anywhere on the road* if the party has been moving and Eric on Gato-walker has been moving in the opposite direction; he is one of the few NPCs the party may meet without having been directed to him.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Fox-folk farmer with a Dromo transport bird; druidic-order observer of Diederik; his own conscience first, his sponsors second.
- **Standouts:** *A flock of birds visible at his farm — ordinary poultry mixed with the strange ones he has accumulated.* **Gato-walker** the Dromo, big enough to ride, fast over distance. Hands move quietly when a creature is in the room — even when there isn't one, the hands have not put the habit down.
- **Speech tag:** Quiet, observational. Asks more than he tells. *"What did you make of him."* Will not commit to an opinion before he has finished watching; will not be pushed past his own pace.
- **Quests:** Early — *carry a fast message on Gato-walker; let him take their measure of Diederik through the party's account.* Late — *take a side, finally* (act with the order, against the order, or beside the order on terms he sets himself); *open an irregular line into the druidic order from outside* if the party's late campaign needs it. See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *He will not move against his own judgement.* The party that wants him as a tool of the order, *or* against the order, learns quickly that he is neither. The honest answer to *"whose side are you on"* is *"mine, until I know what side is right."*

## What the party meets

Most parties meet Eric at **Bridget's Inn** — corner table, drink not finished, a small bag at his feet that the dog at the next table is interested in and the bag's owner has not addressed. He is in plain travel clothes; the *farmer with strange birds* read is intact; his eyes have already taken the room in.

If the party is introduced (Bridget will, if asked, point him out as *"the Randell man, his farm is up past the second crossroads"*), he greets them politely. **He listens longer than he speaks.** He asks where they have been; he asks what they have seen; he asks one or two questions that reveal he has been listening more carefully than the room expected. *"And how did Diederik seem to you."* — that question, if it lands, is the one that tells the party (in retrospect) that this conversation was not entirely casual.

If the party meets him on the road — Gato-walker striding between hills at a long pace — he stops, pulls Gato-walker up, and reads the party for a beat before he speaks. *"You are travelling. Where to. I may be heading the same way."*

## How he reads at a glance

- **Fox folk, mid-life, weathered.** The fur shows the years a fox-folk's fur shows; the carriage is a working farmer's — easy in the body, ready to move quickly if a bird gets out of a pen. Not a soldier's carriage.
- **A long staff he carries on Gato-walker.** Plain wood; the top end is darker than the rest from being held; he walks with it on the ground when he is on foot. A staff is what a farmer uses to herd birds; a staff is *also* what a druid uses. The staff is honest about both.
- **Hands that move when a creature is near.** A goose on the road; a stray cat in the Inn; a mouse in the corner. The hands do something small — a held position, a tiny reach — that the creature reads. The party may or may not notice; the creature does.
- **A small leather pouch at his belt.** Seeds, salves, a few feathers, things he has used and may need again. Not displayed.
- **Eyes that take a room in once and do not need to take it in again.** A regular at an Inn does this; a hunter does this; a druid in observation does this. Three habits in the same gesture.

## Visible patterns

- **At Bridget's Inn on a rough rotation** — once every three or four weeks when the season permits, less often in winter. He pays his tab; he tips fairly; he is back at the corner table before the next visit.
- **Walks Gato-walker on the road south of the Inn**, mornings, when he is in town. Gato-walker is too big for indoor stables; the Inn's outer yard takes the bird overnight, and Eric walks her at dawn before riding home.
- **Takes a long way around Diederik's tree** rather than passing it directly. He knows the elder is there; the elder, he assumes, has noticed him; the dance is mutual and quiet.
- **Receives correspondence at his farm.** Sealed letters arrive once or twice a season; he reads them, replies on the same day, sends the replies south on the road via whichever traveller he trusts. The letters are not openly from the order; the seals are not the order's; a careful eye would still read them as such.
- **Eats with whatever creature is at the farm that day.** A goose at his elbow at breakfast is normal. The household-bird that does not get sold; the strange specimen he is keeping for whatever reason; Gato-walker if she is in the yard.

## At his farm — the flock and the strange ones

The farm is a small holding — a stone-and-timber farmhouse, a yard, a barn with high beams (Gato-walker fits), pens for various birds at varying scales. The standouts on entering:

- **The flock is visible from the gate.** Ordinary geese and ducks at the front of the yard; strange ones further in. *Strange* is honest — birds the party has not seen before, kept properly, fed correctly; nothing exotic for show.
- **Gato-walker, when she is at the farm.** Tall — twice a tall man; fast; **a Dromo / Gastornis transport bird** with the build for distance and the temperament for a single rider. Eric has trained her over years; she lets him on without ceremony and lets no one else on at all without his explicit hand on her flank first.
- **A small workshop off the barn.** Plant cuttings, drying-racks, a few tools that are not for the birds. **The druidic part of him lives here.** The workshop's contents are not displayed at the farm gate; the workshop itself is open to a party brought to it.

(See §"What he can teach" for what the workshop is for.)

## At Bridget's Inn — the corner table

His regular spot. The corner table with the line of sight to both doors and the bar. Bridget pours his drink without being asked. (See `doc/npcs/bridget.md` §"What she says about other people" for her take.)

- **He listens.** Most of the work he does at the Inn is hearing. Travellers' news, merchants' rumours, the road's report on the road. He files what he hears; he writes some of it down later, at the farm.
- **He does not push for conversations.** A party that sits at his table is welcomed; a party that does not is welcomed to ignore him. He is not lonely in the room.
- **Does not compare notes with Nadine.** The two of them are aware of each other (`doc/npcs/nadine.md` §"What she says about other people"). Neither has started the conversation that would compare what each of them has been carrying. Whichever one starts it has a reason; neither has yet decided to be that one.

## On the road on Gato-walker — the messenger scene

When the party hires (or is given) the use of Gato-walker for a fast carry, Eric goes with the bird; they do not separate. The standouts are the bird's:

- **Gato-walker covers ground.** Twice the distance of a horse in a day, on the right ground. Forest and rough country slow her; open road, hills, and the trade road's better stretches are what she was bred for.
- **Bulk is the limit.** A messenger; a single light party member; a small bundle. *Not* cargo. Eric will not make exceptions.
- **Eric is the rider, always.** Even if the party is paying for the carry, he is on the bird. A party member can sit behind him for short stretches if Gato-walker has agreed to it (which she rarely does on first meeting). The carry is *his*.

## At the workshop — for parties he has decided he can show

Off the barn, locked when he is away, open when he is there with a party he trusts.

Three things stand out on entry:

- **Plant cuttings and drying-racks.** A practitioner's workshop, not a herbalist's storefront. Things in stages of preparation; nothing for sale.
- **A small bound book on a shelf.** Notes in his hand on what he has observed: of plants, of weather, of Diederik (carefully — names abbreviated; never on the open page of a current entry).
- **A specific stool by the central bench.** The stool is *for his student*. He has not had one in years. The stool sits.

## Personal goals — early and later

His goals are quiet because he is still listening. The party's role is to give him things to weigh — not to push him to a side.

### Early-game

**Carry a fast message on Gato-walker — for him, not for the party.** *He says (when the party has earned a casual standing — at the Inn, on the road, by visiting the farm honestly):* *"I have a letter to send south. The road traveller I would normally entrust it to is not heading the right way this season. If you are going past the third crossroads, would you carry it. I will pay carriage; the letter is sealed. Do not open it. The keeper at the other end will know what it is."*
- *Where:* the letter's destination — DM-fill on the specific recipient and the specific town, but the letter is going to a contact within or adjacent to the **druidic order** (carefully framed; the seal is not the order's openly, but a knowledgeable reader would identify it). Plausible drop-points: a posting-house south of Salmonswell, a quiet shrine-keeper at one of the order's small chapels, an itinerant druid reachable through a fixed receiving address.
- *What:* carry the sealed letter; deliver to the named keeper; bring back any reply. Do not open. Do not name the destination aloud. The carry is a test.
- *Reward:* Eric's standing rises. The corner table at the Inn becomes available to the party — he will sit with them when they arrive, not separately. A second carry follows when the next letter is ready. The order knows who carried, in a way that may matter later.
- *Complications:* the letter is a real working artefact in its way — not magical, but politically charged; if it is opened, the seal cannot be reformed cleanly and Eric will know within a day. The road may be watched (the order's other observers; the Allondan crown's quartermasters; the buyers seeking spirit-bound artefacts if the campaign has surfaced them); a clean carry is harder than the simple route would suggest.

**Let him take his measure of Diederik through the party's account.** *He says (after the first few visits, when he has decided the party is worth talking to honestly):* *"You have been at his tree. You have walked with him. Tell me how he seemed to you. Tell me what he is teaching, what he is refusing to teach, what his hands are doing when he thinks no one is watching. I am not asking you to inform on him. I am asking you to tell me what you saw, in your words. I will not write it down."*
- *Where:* the corner table at Bridget's Inn; or Eric's farm if the conversation is too long for the Inn; or on the road if the party is travelling with him.
- *What:* speak honestly about the party's experience of **Elder Diederik** — what he has shared, what he has refused to share, how he has held the town's spirit-shield (the party may not yet know this is what he is doing), how he has handled the overseas plan, the Rowan errand, Alita's grave. The party gets to choose how candid to be.
- *Reward:* Eric's read of Diederik shifts toward whatever the party has shown him. A party that gives him an honest, generous reading nudges Eric toward *Diederik is what the order should learn from, not what it should fear* — and the late-game opens that way. A party that gives him a frank but worried reading nudges him toward *the order's fears have substance* — a different late-game. The conversation is not a betrayal of Diederik unless the party makes it one; Eric will not write the report unless what he hears compels him to.
- *Complications:* Diederik may know the conversation is happening; whether he minds is a separate question (`doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md` may have him already aware of Eric's role and choosing not to act on it). If the party tells Eric something Diederik has explicitly held private, the trust between the party and Diederik bends; if the party tells Eric something *false* to manipulate his read, Eric reads it.

### Later-game

**Take a side, finally.** *Conditional — opens late, when the campaign has surfaced enough of Diederik's overseas plan, the order's silence, and the chaos-spirit pressure that Eric can no longer remain a pure observer.* *He says (in private, late hour, at the farm, with the workshop open):* *"I have been watching for years. I have not written the report. I am at a point where the not-writing is itself a position. I will tell you which way I am leaning, and I will tell you what I would do if you helped me, and I will tell you what I will not do. Tell me back what you would help with. We will see if there is a working line between what I would do and what you would help with."*
- *Where:* the farm workshop; possibly Diederik's tree if Eric has decided to come into the open with the elder; possibly the order's nearest chapter-house if Eric has decided to deliver his judgement formally.
- *What:* depending on the lean Eric has reached:
  - *If Eric concludes the order is wrong about Diederik:* he will deliver a quiet refusal to file his report, write to **Rowan** directly (Diederik's secret correspondent within the order), and lend his standing — his Gato-walker, his farm, his druidic skill — to whatever the party is helping Diederik do (the overseas plan, the saplings, the corridor working).
  - *If Eric concludes the order's caution about Diederik has substance:* he will file a careful report and ask the party to bring his findings to Diederik in person, so the elder hears them from the right voice. He will not act against Diederik on the order's behalf; he will let the elder respond.
  - *If Eric concludes the truth is more complicated than either side admits:* he becomes a third voice — neither the order's nor Diederik's — and the party has gained an irregular ally who will work *with* Diederik on the saplings and *with* the order on the legitimacy of the work. The most useful outcome; the hardest to earn.
- *Reward:* the party gains an active partner instead of an observer. Gato-walker becomes a campaign-scale messenger asset (the late-game runs from the farm's yard, not from Eric's pocket). Diederik gains a quiet bridge into the order he has not had in twenty years.
- *Complications:* the order's other observers exist (DM-fill on how many and where); Eric's choice of side puts a target on him from whichever direction he chose against; the wrong choice of timing — too early, too public — costs the choice its weight.

**Open an irregular line into the druidic order from outside.** *Conditional — opens if Eric has chosen the *third voice* path above and the campaign needs druidic engagement the order will not formally provide.* *He says (when the late campaign has surfaced a need — the saplings need ratification, the corridor needs druidic acknowledgement, a refugee community needs a circle established):* *"The order will not say yes if you ask formally. They will not say no either; they will defer until the question loses urgency. The way to get something done with them is irregular: a request that lands on the right desk by way of someone they cannot dismiss. I am not that someone yet. I am working on it. If you bring me names of practitioners who would receive the request — Rowan first, but not Rowan only — I will carry it through. The desk it lands on will respond. The response may not be the one you want. The response will be real."*
- *Where:* the farm; the order's small chapel-houses scattered across the region (DM-fill); Salmonswell as a likely meeting venue; possibly Luchebert if **Father Elliot**'s standing is needed.
- *What:* receive Eric's named practitioners; help him reach them; carry his framed request through; report back what each practitioner answered.
- *Reward:* an irregular line into the formal druidic order opens. Specific late-campaign asks become possible: ratification of Diederik's overseas saplings; druidic acknowledgement of the spirit-corridor (Brumal-resolution Path 2); a formal training-place for **Hank** if she ever wants one (and decides she does); a sanctioned circle at a refugee community.
- *Complications:* the order is not unified; what one practitioner agrees to, another may overturn; Eric's standing is finite, and each carry uses some of it; the order will eventually realise what Eric has been doing, and he will eventually have to answer for it.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about Gato-walker — *how I came by her*

> *"Gato-walker came to me when I was younger and willing to ride south for a season. A trader at Linar harbour had her chick from a coastal flock further south; the chick had outgrown the trader's coop and was eating his profit. I bought her and walked her north over six weeks, sleeping in the same paddock she did half the nights, learning what she was at the same pace she learned what I was. She lets me on without ceremony now. She lets nobody else on at all unless I have put my hand on her flank first. That is the agreement. I have not broken it; she has not broken it; I have not asked her to."*

### When asked about the strange birds — *what curiosity brought me here*

> *"My father was a fox-folk farmer with no curiosity at all about birds. I am the curiosity in the line. The strange ones started arriving when I was twenty — a sea-bird a fisherman gave me at Linar; a marsh-thing that walked off a swamp-trader's cart; a hill-bird that came down with a Brumal refugee group ten years ago and would not go back up. They are not for sale; they are not for show. They are what my house has accumulated. I keep them because someone has to and because I want to. The farm is what they have made me, as much as the other way around."*

### When asked about Elder Diederik — *the dance we are in*

*(Trust-gated. Once the conversation is honest enough.)*

> *"Diederik is at his tree. I am at my farm. The road between us is not long; we have not crossed it on each other's accounts. He knows I am here. I know he knows. I take a long way around his tree when I ride, not because the short way would be unpleasant but because the long way preserves the form we have. The form is one of us watches and the other is watched, and neither names the watching, and the watching is not done in malice. I have been at this for years. I have not yet been satisfied. I am also not yet displeased. The order would have wanted me to be one or the other by now. I am not in a hurry to oblige them."*

### When asked about the order — *what they sent me to do*

*(Deep trust. Quiet voice.)*

> *"They sent me to watch a man they had excommunicated. They wanted to know what he was doing with what he had learned overseas — whether the things he had brought back were a danger to the order, a resource, or a private grief that would die with him. I have watched. I have not written the report I was supposed to send each season. I send shorter notes that say *no significant change* and let the order draw their own conclusions. The order has not pressed me; they have other observers in other places, and a slow correspondent is not the worst kind. The day they press me is the day I have to choose. I have been preparing for that day for several years."*

### When asked about the letters at the farm — *what comes and goes*

> *"Letters arrive once or twice a season. The seals are not the order's; they are personal seals of practitioners I correspond with. The contents are not always order business; some of it is plant-craft, some of it is animal-handling, some of it is the kind of conversation a working druid has with another working druid that is not for the chapter-house's records. I reply on the day the letter arrives, when I can. The road takes my replies south. If you ever need to send a letter into the order without it going through a chapter-house, I can route it. The route is mine; the responsibility for what is in the letter is yours."*

### When asked what he wants — *the small list*

> *"To keep the farm. To watch the elder fairly. To write the report that is true, when I can finally say what *true* is. To teach a student the work, eventually, on terms the student and I can both stand. To die before Gato-walker does so that she does not have to learn a second rider. That is the list. It is short. It has not changed in some years."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — does not deflect; does not over-answer. The hands stop moving — the small hand-motions for creatures still themselves. He says less than the question asked for, and the rest of the answer waits for a second question that may not come.
- **When distraught** — goes to a creature. A bird in the yard, a cat at the Inn, Gato-walker on the road. The creature does not need to know he is distraught; the contact does the work the conversation cannot.
- **When happy** — a quiet warmth in the eyes; a small specific smile; *"That is well."* Will not announce it. Most often when a creature has done something that pleases him, when a letter has answered cleanly, or when he has finally heard a sentence about Diederik that lets him sleep more easily.
- **When the order's seal arrives** *(an internal mannerism a careful party will read)* — the hands set the letter down before he opens it. He looks at the seal for a beat. Then he opens it. The pause is the moment he reminds himself that he is his own man.

## What he can teach

For students he has watched carefully and decided to take:

- **Animal-wrangling.** Real craft — handling birds, reading their fear and their settling, what a strange creature's body language is doing before the strangeness shows. He teaches by sitting on the gate with the student and waiting.
- **Druidic practice.** Trust-gated, slow, real. Plant lore, weather-reading, the small workings that hold a piece of land in alignment. He does not teach as quickly as Diederik does, because he has more to lose if his cover cracks. A student who passes his read learns at depth.
- **Riding Gato-walker** — the practical skill, *for emergencies only*. The bird's agreement is not transferable; a student learns the *shape* of what a Dromo wants, not the bird herself.
- **Reading correspondence.** What a seal tells you; what a paper's weight tells you; what a hurried hand tells you. He has been at this for years; the skill is real.
- **Holding a position in two registers at once** — by example, not by lecture. A party member who has been in his presence over a season learns something about *carrying a cover honestly* that no other NPC teaches the same way.

## What he will not say

- **The names of the other observers the order has placed**, unless the campaign has made the question urgent. He knows several; he will not give them on first asking.
- **The exact contents of the reports he has not written**, in the reverse direction. The not-writing is the position; describing what he is *not* saying would be its own kind of saying.
- **What he would do if Diederik confronted him directly.** He has rehearsed; he has not committed. He would prefer the day not come.
- **The names of his strange-bird sources.** The sea-bird's fisherman; the marsh-thing's swamp-trader. Their privacy is part of the deal he made with them.
- **The third reason he has stayed at this farm specifically.** Two reasons are public — proximity to Diederik; the right ground for the flock. The third is private. (DM-fill, if needed: a person; a debt; a quiet promise.)

## DM notes

- **Play him as a thinker, not a spy.** A DM running Eric as a covert agent flattens him; a DM running him as a quiet farmer with one big secret runs the cover and misses the substance. The truth is *both* — he is a working druid in honest observation who has not yet committed to a side. The not-committing is his character.
- **The dance with Diederik is mutual and quiet.** Both files (`elder_diederik.md` and this one) treat the relationship as known to both parties without either acting on it. A DM running them at the same table can use the *long-way-around-the-tree* habit, the not-volunteered awareness, and the carefully maintained distance as the texture. **Do not collapse the dance prematurely.**
- **Gato-walker is a real campaign asset, used sparingly.** A Dromo carry is fast and irreplaceable for specific moments — an urgent message that must beat a horse's pace, a single-rider extraction, a long-distance scout. Eric does not lend her casually. *The carry is his.*
- **The early-game letter is a test.** The party that runs a clean carry opens Eric's standing; the party that opens the letter (or loses it, or hands it to the wrong reader) closes the carry-relationship in a way the file says is years to recover from. Make the test specific; do not let it pass on a casual roll.
- **The late-game three-leaning structure** (Eric concludes the order is wrong / right / more complicated) is the campaign-scale shape of his arc. The party's role is to give him things to weigh, not to push him. A DM tempted to have him pick a side on the party's instruction is mis-running him; a DM who lets the party's *behaviour* across visits inform his lean is running him correctly.
- **He is a quiet bridge between the order and outside.** If the campaign reaches a place where druidic acknowledgement is needed and the order will not formally give it, Eric is the irregular line. Use this once or twice; do not let it become a routine bypass.
- **Hank's cover and Eric's cover share the same shape.** Both are working druids carrying a public surface that does not name the practice. They have not acknowledged each other. If the campaign brings them into the same room, run the recognition slowly; both have reasons to be careful, and both will read each other before either speaks.
- **The strange-bird flock is a generative prop.** Whatever creature the campaign needs to surface — a courier-bird; a watchful crow; a marsh-creature whose presence reads on shamanic registers — Eric plausibly has one. *Use sparingly.* The flock is a real flock with real keeping; not a free-bag of plot-creatures.
