# Eric Randell

- **Role:** Seemingly a farmer interested in strange creatures — actually a druid sent to watch Elder Diederik
- **Species:** Fox
- **Location:** His own farm (plausibly near Elmsfield, close enough to keep an eye on Diederik); travels from there on Gato-walker
- **Status:** Alive
- **Companion:** Gato-walker — a Dromo/Gastornis transport bird
- **On the farm:** a flock of birds — Gato-walker among them, along with others (ordinary and strange)

## Summary

At first read, Eric is a **fox farmer with an unusual taste for strange creatures**. He keeps his own **farm** — plausibly near Elmsfield, close enough to keep incidental track of Diederik — and the farm has a **flock of birds** on it. Gato-walker, the Dromo he rides on long journeys, is one of them; the rest are a mix of ordinary poultry and unusual specimens he has accumulated over the years, the kind of collection a man with a real interest in strange creatures inevitably ends up with. The farm is how he feeds himself and how he explains his presence. That first read is *genuinely true*: the curiosity is real, the wrangling is real, the Gato-walker is real, the flock is real.

What the first read does *not* cover is that Eric is **also a druid**, working for the formal druidic order that excommunicated **Elder Diederik** years ago. His presence near Diederik is not a coincidence. He has been **sent to keep an eye on** the excommunicated druid — to watch what Diederik does with what he learned overseas, to observe whether Diederik is becoming a threat or a resource, and to report back. His cover as an animal wrangler is flexible enough to let him drift near Diederik's territory plausibly, and strange enough that nobody asks what else he might be.

But — and this is the part the order did not plan for — Eric is **his own man**. He is a thinking person who wants to **reach his own conclusions before doing anything rash**. He has not yet decided whether Diederik is what the order fears, what the order hopes, or something else entirely. He is *observing*, not denouncing. He has written no report he would be ashamed to defend. He may not write one at all. If he comes to the conclusion that the druidic order is wrong about Diederik — or about the overseas situation, or about the chaos-spirit crisis, or about whatever else he sees on the road — he will not act against his own judgement to satisfy them.

This makes him a **quiet, useful, ambiguous NPC**. A party working near Diederik may find Eric friendly, useful, and more knowledgeable than his cover suggests. A party working *against* Diederik may find him the same. What Eric does with either party depends on what he has concluded by then.

## Gato-walker (transport Gastornis/Dromo)

- Fast, strong over long distance.
- Low bulk capacity — goods, not cargo.
- Well suited to messengers, scouts, and light parties.
- Lets Eric move between locations in ways that keep the cover plausible (a wrangler with a rare mount has reasons to wander).

## What he can teach

Real druidic craft — plant lore, weather-reading, animal-handling that goes beyond what his cover suggests. He teaches to those he has taken the measure of, at the pace he is comfortable with. He does not teach as quickly or freely as **Diederik** does, because he has less to prove and more to lose if his cover cracks.

## Relationships

- **Elder Diederik** — his assigned target, though the relationship is slowly evolving into something more complicated than surveillance. Diederik may or may not know Eric is watching him; Eric has not decided whether to admit it.
- The **druidic order** — his sponsors; the body that excommunicated Diederik and sent Eric to watch the aftermath. Eric's loyalty to them is no longer automatic.
- A party travelling with him for transport reasons is also travelling with him for reasons Eric has not disclosed.
