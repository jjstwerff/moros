# The Skull Gatherers

- **Role:** A group (cult, order, or profession — see LORE.md) that collects the skulls of mages
- **Species:** Mixed
- **Location:** Itinerant; periodically pass through Scarlet Vale (among other places)
- **Status:** Active

## Summary

The Skull Gatherers are a standing presence in the setting (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` for the background). They collect mage skulls for purposes that belong to their own tradition. For the campaign, what matters is that they are **active, organised, and currently moving through the kingdoms** — Allondo tolerates them; they tolerate Allondo in return.

## The Scarlet Vale encounter

The party's first sight of them is likely to be **on arrival in Scarlet Vale**: a group of Skull Gatherers leaving the city just as the party is entering. Robes, packs, the quiet assurance of people on a specific job. They do not draw attention to themselves. They do not hide either.

**A casual walk-up will be ignored.** Not hostile — simply ignored. The Gatherers do not talk to strangers who address them in the street. They have business; they are leaving; they are not available.

**They are not unapproachable**, however. The route to a real conversation runs through something specific the party offers or knows:

- **A skull-related offering** — information about a mage death, the location of old mage-era remains, a demi-lich's whereabouts (Laurent's situation in particular is the kind of lead that would make them pause).
- **A proper address** — the right greeting in their tradition, worked out from lore, a knowledgeable NPC, or an item whose provenance they would recognise.
- **A vouch from someone they know** — a named Gatherer, a trusted priest, a mage of the right lineage.
- **Meeting them on their own terms** — at a campfire on the road, at a burial site, at one of the places their work takes them, rather than in the city street.

A party that invests in one of these paths gets an actual encounter.

## Campaign hooks

- **Laurent's collection.** A demi-lich is the kind of target the Gatherers exist for. Why haven't they collected him? A party that raises this with them will learn something about their scope, their limits, or their fear.
- **A missing scholar.** If the campaign needs a named figure to have been Gathered, the Gatherers' leaving Scarlet Vale can be that scholar's story — a mage the party has heard of, now reduced to what the Gatherers came here for.
- **An offering request.** They may eventually ask something of a party with standing — a skull, a location, a witness. Accepting or refusing shapes the relationship.

## Open questions

- Their doctrine — why skulls?
- Their hierarchy, leadership, and internal politics (DM-fill).
- Their stance on demi-liches in general and Laurent in particular.
- Whether they have connections to the shamans' long plan, to Luchebert, or to the overseas mage tradition.
