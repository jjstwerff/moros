# The Skull Gatherers

- **Role:** Itinerant order that collects the skulls of mages — by request when allowed, by quiet arrival when not — and keeps them at the order's necropolis west of the blasted lands. Older than written record on this continent. Possibly serving an ancient off-world being whose interest in this world is the limiting of *other*, more chaotic ancient beings (`doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Where the gatherers came from — what no one knows").
- **Species:** Mixed — a typical group of three to five gatherers walking out of any city is one human, one mouse-folk, one of the smaller animal-folk, and one or two of less-common lineage. The diversity is part of how the order moves through cities unannounced; nobody quite has a *picture* of a Skull Gatherer, which is as the order prefers.
- **Lives:** Itinerant. The order's home is **the necropolis — the city of the dead — west of the blasted lands, at the desert's edge.** Gatherer-groups move through Allondo, Brumal, the agricultural towns, and the harbours on routes the order's senior planners maintain. **Andras** is the named contact most parties earn (`doc/npcs/andras.md`).
- **First met:** **Leaving Scarlet Vale as the party is arriving.** Robes, packs, the quiet assurance of people on a specific job. They do not draw attention; they do not hide. The party that pays attention to who walks past on the road *out* has the start of a vouch when they meet **Andras** later.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** The order — collectors of mage-skulls, keepers of the necropolis, the late-campaign destination for **Laurent**'s last flight. The party's earned entry is **Andras**.
- **Standouts:** *Plain dark robes, well-made, neither monastic nor mage-formal* — a register the party will not have seen before. **Packs that hold both practical road gear and the order's tools** — a small pry-bar, a folded cloth for wrapping, a bound book with a clasp. **A quiet assurance** that reads neither as authority nor as humility — *as people on a specific job whose schedule does not depend on the room*.
- **Speech tag:** Brief, formal, exact — they do not waste words. **A casual walk-up will be ignored** (not hostile — *unavailable*). A proper address opens a real conversation. The doctrine they speak in is fragmentary and the senior the gatherer, the more of it they hold.
- **Quests:** Early — *earn the vouch through an offering they recognise — most often Andras's dungeon (`doc/npcs/andras.md`).* Late — *carry the order's resting-place offer to **Laurent** at his last flight, as the destination he is choosing.* See §"The order's goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *They do not interrupt for strangers; do not interrupt them for nothing.* Stopping a gatherer-group on the street to ask casual questions costs nothing and gains nothing. **Threatening or harming a gatherer is a category mistake** — the order does not retaliate by violence, but doors close across the continent in ways the party will not see until they walk into them. **Laurent is fraught** — they cannot collect what is holding station, and they wait.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/skull_gatherers.md`.*

## What the party meets

The party reaches Scarlet Vale by the trade road, comes through the gate, and as they enter — **a group of three to five Skull Gatherers is leaving**. Robes, packs, walking at a pace that does not change for them. The group passes the party in the street with the same attention the party would give to any other group passing the other way: noticed, registered, not engaged.

A casual walk-up to introduce the party will be **ignored**. The lead gatherer's eyes pass over them and continue. *They have business; they are leaving; they are not available.*

Outside the wall, a few hours later, the group splits. **Most continue west toward the necropolis**, on the long road that skirts the blasted lands. **One — Andras — turns north** when his trail goes that way (`doc/npcs/andras.md`). A party that *watched* who walked out of Scarlet Vale, and who *split off where*, has a piece of vouch they did not know they were collecting.

If the party meets a gatherer-group again — at a campsite, at a burial site, at the threshold of a recent death — and addresses them properly (see §"What earns a real conversation"), the second encounter does not look like the first.

## What earns a real conversation

A gatherer-group does not respond to casual address. The four ways into a real conversation are:

- **A proper greeting in the order's tradition.** Worked out from lore (`doc/claude/LORE.md`), from a knowledgeable NPC (Rhianna, Diederik, an older priest at Luchebert), or from an item whose provenance a gatherer would recognise. The greeting is short, formal, and in a register the party should not improvise.
- **A vouch from someone they know.** **Andras** is the canonical name. A named gatherer the party has met by another route also works.
- **A skull-related offering.** Information about a mage death they have not heard about; the location of mage-era remains on a route the order has not yet covered; a demi-lich's whereabouts (Laurent's situation in particular is the kind of lead that gives them pause).
- **Meeting them on their own terms.** A campfire on the road; a burial site they are working at; one of the places their work takes them — rather than the city street where they are passing through.

A party that invests in one of these paths gets an actual encounter. Two of them — a vouch *plus* an offering — moves the relationship past first encounter into something the order will continue.

## How a Gatherer reads at a glance

- **Plain dark robes**, well-made, neither monastic nor mage-formal. The cut is the same across all gatherers; the cloth varies with where the wearer was last fitted. **The robes are working clothes**, not ceremonial.
- **Packs that hold the work.** Practical road gear in the outer pockets; the order's tools deeper in — a small pry-bar, a folded cloth wrapping, a bound book with a clasp, a vial of something the party will not be told about, a small carved stone the gatherer touches when thinking.
- **Quiet assurance.** Reads neither as authority nor as humility. They are *on the job*. Their schedule does not depend on the room. A party reading body language sees this within a minute of any encounter.
- **Mixed species in any given group.** A typical five is one human, one mouse-folk, one of the smaller animal-folk (badger, raccoon, otter), and one or two from less-common lineages — a fox, a boar, a bird-folk. The order accepts members on conduct, not species; the visible diversity is part of how the order moves through cities without being remembered as *a type*.

## Visible patterns

- **Travel in groups of three to five.** Lone gatherers exist; lone gatherers are unusual and usually senior. Andras is one of the few the party will likely meet alone (`doc/npcs/andras.md`).
- **Move at the pace the work permits.** Do not hurry; do not linger. A gatherer-group crossing a town square clears it in the time the work needs and not faster.
- **Camp at burial sites and ruins as a matter of course.** Locals who have seen this know they will be respectful neighbours and clean campers. Towns near old battlefields know to set out water for them when they arrive.
- **At a death-site, work in silence.** One gatherer reads doctrine fragments aloud; the others work. The party that observes from a respectful distance is allowed to; the party that interrupts is not.
- **Do not eat at townspeople's tables.** Carry their own food. The reasons are doctrinal and not explained. A party that *insists* on hosting a meal gets a polite refusal and a small loss of standing.

## At a campsite on the road — the second-encounter scene

The simplest place to actually talk to gatherers is a campsite. They keep one when their day's route ends short of a town, or when they are *waiting* at the threshold of a death-site for the right hour to begin work. The party stumbling onto a gatherer-camp on a known road is the cleanest entry the file offers.

- **A small fire, banked low.** They do not need a large one; the work does not require it.
- **Packs lined up at the edge of the firelight**, opened only at need. The order's tools are visible on the inside of the closest packs; a careful party reads them at a glance and does not handle them.
- **One gatherer keeping watch**, the others doing whatever the work needs at this hour — reading, copying notes, sharpening a tool, tending a small wrapped object on a folded cloth. The watch-gatherer will see the party approach; if the party hails properly, the camp opens.

## At a death-site — observing the work

If the party reaches a death-site where gatherers are working — a recent mage death the order has come for, a battlefield the order is sweeping, an ancient tomb the order has been authorised to enter — the work itself is what stands out:

- **Silence.** The work is done in silence except for the doctrine-reader.
- **The reader.** One gatherer reads aloud in a register the party will not recognise — not Allondan, not Brumalan, not the overseas trade-tongue. The cadence is steady; the words are short. *This is the order's working language*; the party may pick up the rhythm but not the meaning.
- **The wrapping.** When the skull is lifted, it is wrapped in the folded cloth on a specific pattern, set into the inner pack, and the doctrine ends. The whole sequence takes minutes, not hours.

A party that watches respectfully and asks honestly afterwards what they saw will get a real answer to the question — usually short, doctrinally fragmentary, but *real*.

## At the necropolis — the city of the dead (DM-fill)

**West of the blasted lands, at the desert's edge.** The order's home. Most parties will not reach it; some will, by Andras's hand or by a long campaign-arc that earns the journey.

The file does not yet describe the necropolis as a place. When the campaign reaches it, the place file (`doc/places/` — currently absent) can carry the standouts; this NPC file flags only the role:

- **It is the destination of the resting-place offer for Laurent** if the party brokers it. Andras's offer is verbatim: *"we will hold your station, in our city, with the others, when you choose to set it down"* (`doc/npcs/andras.md`). The necropolis is *the city, with the others*.
- **It is also the order's archive** — the place where the doctrine is held in full, where the senior gatherers spend their long stretches between expeditions, and where the records of mage-deaths reach back to a register the kingdoms have lost.
- **It is the only place a serious student of the order's craft can complete training**; junior gatherers do their seasons on the road and return to the necropolis in turn.

## What a Gatherer will tell — and when

| Topic | When they'll talk |
|---|---|
| Their immediate work — the death they are at, the route they are on | Once a vouch is in place. Plainly. |
| The order's general purpose — collecting mage-skulls, keeping the necropolis | Yes, in fragments that vary by seniority. Junior gatherers know what they do; senior gatherers know more of *why*. |
| The doctrine itself — full text | **Never publicly.** Doctrine is held in pieces; the full pattern is at the necropolis. |
| The founder — who or what | **Never specifically.** The order itself does not have it as one piece. *"The work was begun. We continue it."* |
| Their travel and timing | Yes, in general. Specifics are not for casual conversation. |
| Demi-liches in general | Yes; with care. They distinguish demi-liches who hold station from those who drift. |
| **Laurent specifically** | Yes — and the conversation changes. He is *known* to the order; *waited on*; not approached without invitation. |
| The names of senior gatherers | Briefly. Andras's name is the one the party most likely earns. |
| The off-world being | **Senior gatherers only**, and only on doctrinal terms. Junior gatherers are taught the work without the why. |
| Members' personal histories before joining | Not a topic. Members leave their old name at the necropolis when they take vows. (Andras gives his name — that is unusual and meaningful.) |

## Doctrine fragments — what they say

When the relevant trigger appears in conversation, these are what a Gatherer will offer. They are *fragments* of the order's larger doctrine; the senior the speaker, the more they say. Junior gatherers stop where the doctrine has stopped them. None of these is the full pattern.

### When asked what skulls hold — *the keeping*

> *"A mage's skull holds what the mage's working held. It does not become inert when the body dies. The drift, if not gathered, finds another shape — a household, a workshop, a child of the line — and confuses it for a generation. We keep the skull. The keeping is steady. The drift is stopped."*

### When asked why the order collects — *the limiting*

> *"There are older beings in the world than the kingdoms remember. Some of them are downstream of what the chaos spirits became. The order's work is the limiting of those older beings — the steady gathering, the steady keeping, the slow reduction of what they have to act through. The work is generations. The order is older than the kingdoms. We continue."*

### When asked about the founder — *what no one knows*

> *(Slight pause. Even a senior gatherer hesitates.)* *"The work was begun. The pattern was given. Who began it, the order does not say plainly — perhaps no one alive in the order knows. The doctrine speaks of *the first keeper*, not of a name. I have read the doctrine in pieces. I have not read the name. I think there is no name to read."*

### When asked about a specific death they have come for — *the work*

> *"This one drifted. We came for the keeping. The family knew us and asked. Or the family did not know us and we came anyway because the drift had already begun. We will be done by — soon. The keeping is fast once the working is read."*

*(The order's stance on consent varies by century; it does not lie about it. A party asking gets the honest answer for that case.)*

### When asked about the necropolis — *our city*

> *"West of the blasted lands. At the desert's edge. The road there is long. We hold our stations there with the keepings of those who came before us. **It is a city.** The dead live alongside us in the way the doctrine permits. If you are ever invited, you will be."*

### When asked about demi-liches in general — *the holding*

> *"A demi-lich is a mage who has chosen to hold. We do not collect what is holding station — we cannot, by the order's own working, and we would not even if we could. The demi-lich is doing the keeping the order would otherwise do, in their own way, on their own terms. We wait. If they ever set their station down, we are the place that receives it."*

### When asked about Laurent — *the partner of the work* (gated past initial standing)

> *"Laurent's wreck is east of the Lake of Tears. He has held his station for centuries. We have come close to his hall. We have left. He is not a target; he is a colleague the order has not had the formal language for. **Andras** carries the offer the order makes him: a resting place, in our city, with the others, when he chooses to set it down. The offer is open. The offer is patient. The offer is real."*

### When asked about pre-rift mage sites the order has noted — *the route-log* (gated past initial standing)

> *"The order's routes pass many places we do not enter. We log what we pass. A grain-mill on the trade road's skirt, in the broken lands — pre-rift, agricultural-side cutting, a bound earth-spirit still running the stones. We have not entered. The mill is one of the cleaner sites we keep noted; the spirit was bound gently and we leave gentle bindings alone. A press-house ridge a day-and-a-half south of it; a fallen castle further west called the Half-Tower; a buried laboratory in the desert near a sphinx-watched ridge — all in the route-log. The order does not gather at these places; we *count* them. If you are going to one, the order would receive a careful report."* (Cross-ref: the **Grain-mill** at `doc/places/grain_mill.md`; the **Press-house ridge** at `doc/places/press_house.md`; the **Anchor** at `doc/places/anchor.md`.)

## The order's goals — early and later

The order does not have *quests* in the personal-NPC sense; it has a generations-long working that the party can intersect. Two specific moments make that working actionable for a party in the campaign's frame.

- **Early-game — earn the vouch through an offering the order recognises.** *What a gatherer says* (when the party has reached the threshold by a proper greeting, a vouch, an offering, or an on-their-own-terms encounter): *"You have addressed us as you should have. We have time. Tell us what you bring. If it serves the work, we will hear it. If not, we will hear it anyway, briefly, and then we go. Be honest. We do not waste honesty."* — *Where:* the **road out of Scarlet Vale** for the noticing of who walks past; **a campsite or threshold** for the proper second encounter; the **active dungeon Andras carries** — the badger's family crypt where the stolen golden crown's spirit is reanimating bones (`doc/npcs/andras.md`); the long road west toward the **necropolis** if the work the party brings runs that far. — *What:* address them properly; bring an honest offering — most often by working the **Andras dungeon** in one of its four resolution paths and reporting plainly to the order what was done; carry information the order has not yet collected (a mage death by name; a demi-lich's whereabouts; a recovered keeping-tool); accept the order's terms (a share of the work, not of the find; the family's permission honoured if it was given). — *Reward:* **a vouch the party can use across the order**. Andras's standing extends; gatherer-groups they meet on later roads receive them on terms. Doctrinal fragments they have already heard add up faster. **The late-game offer to Laurent becomes available to broker.** The order's *long road west* opens, in time, as a route the party can travel safely. — *Complications:* the order does not credit *easy* offerings; a party that brings rumour rather than substance gets a polite hearing and no vouch. **Interrupting the order's work to bring an offering** is the wrong shape — wait until the work is done. The Andras dungeon has four resolution paths and the order reads which one was chosen; *a destructive resolution* of that dungeon does not close the vouch route, but it slows it. **Promises made to a gatherer are kept across the continent** — the order's records reach further than a party expects.

- **Later-game — carry the order's resting-place offer to Laurent at his last flight.** *Conditional — opens once Andras has formally extended the offer to the party as carriers (his own late-game; `doc/npcs/andras.md`) and Laurent's last flight is a real possibility (`doc/npcs/laurent.md` late-game; the six-shackle question with **Gerhald**, `doc/npcs/hermit_shaman.md`).* *What a gatherer says* (in private, only Andras for most parties; rarely a senior gatherer at the necropolis): *"The offer is open. We will not approach him to make it. You will. Carry it verbatim. The exact words matter — they are doctrine, not courtesy. Bring back his answer in the same register. If he accepts, the city will receive him; we will go to meet the flight at the agreed point. If he refuses, we will continue to wait. Do not soften either answer."* — *Where:* **Laurent's audience hall** at the wreck of his flying city, with the necklace in hand (`doc/npcs/laurent.md`, `doc/npcs/willow.md`); the **Lake of Tears towers** as the six shackles are cleared in turn; **Gerhald's hill tower** for the consent on the sixth (`doc/npcs/hermit_shaman.md`); the **agreed meeting point** on the last flight's route — most likely the necropolis itself if Laurent accepts, or an escort point on the road west if the order is bringing the receiving party. **Andras** as the carrier-of-the-offer the party works with throughout (`doc/npcs/andras.md`). — *What:* carry the offer to Laurent verbatim, with the necklace bringing Felicia's presence into the room (`doc/npcs/felicia.md` late-game *carry the necklace to Laurent at his parting*); listen for his answer; bring it back to Andras unsoftened; if accepted, broker the meeting-point and timing for the receiving; if refused, carry that back too with the same care. **The party are the conversation between Laurent and the order; they are not the parties to it.** — *Reward:* **one of the campaign's possible climaxes lands as it should** — the pre-rift world's last living voice ends with company, with Felicia present through the necklace, with the order receiving the keeping at the agreed point. **The necropolis receives Laurent**; the offer that has been open for centuries closes. The party becomes the only living people who walked the offer between its two sides; the order's standing with the party becomes permanent. — *Complications:* the order will not *push* Laurent. **Andras will not summon a senior gatherer to make the case for him**; the offer is *carried*, not negotiated. A party that softens either side's words breaks the working; the doctrine is exact. **If Gerhald withholds consent on the sixth shackle**, the cage does not fully open, the city does not lift, and the offer waits. **If Moth Hollow has been disturbed**, Felicia's necklace fails to condense at the audience hall and Laurent reads the room as armed; the offer does not land cleanly without her. The offer is patient — *but the party may not be*. The cost of the bittersweet is real (`doc/npcs/laurent.md` DM notes on the rain-buffer and the spirit-shield); a party that has thought through the destination understands what the order is offering and what it costs.

## Mannerisms — the order's collective conduct

- **When ignored** — they ignore. Casual walk-ups receive nothing. The lead gatherer's eyes pass over the speaker and continue. Not hostile; *unavailable*.
- **When properly addressed** — the lead gatherer stops. The group does not stop with them; the work continues. The lead gatherer answers briefly, weighs the offering, and either invites further conversation or completes a courteous close. **No warmth; real attention.**
- **When the order is at work** — silence. The doctrine-reader speaks; the others do not. A party that interrupts is not punished; they simply lose the work-window for that visit, and the order's read of them carries into the next encounter.
- **When the order is satisfied with work the party has done** — formal acknowledgment, no warmth, but their next encounter is different. The greeting comes in a register the party has earned; doctrinal fragments add up faster; the watch-gatherer at a campsite does not have to be hailed.
- **When the order is dissatisfied** — the same calm. The doors close across the continent in ways the party will not see immediately. The first signs are a gatherer-group passing without acknowledging the party at a city gate after they had been acknowledged before; later signs are Andras's own threshold growing harder to reach.

## If the party does something — what the order does

| If the party... | The order responds by... |
|---|---|
| Approaches casually in the street | Ignored. The lead gatherer's eyes pass over them. The group continues. |
| Approaches with a proper greeting | The lead gatherer stops; the group does not. Real conversation begins, briefly. |
| Brings a vouch (Andras's name, a senior gatherer's name) | The greeting opens further. The work-window of the encounter is real. |
| Brings an offering — a mage death, a relic, a demi-lich's whereabouts | Heard plainly. If it serves the work, the order takes it; standing rises. If not, the conversation closes politely. |
| Interrupts the order at a death-site | Not punished. Loses the work-window for that visit. The next encounter starts further back. |
| Insists on hosting a meal at a townsperson's table | Polite refusal. Small loss of standing. |
| Threatens or harms a gatherer | The order does not retaliate by violence. Doors close. **Andras's threshold becomes harder to reach.** Senior gatherers receive word at the necropolis; the party's name enters the records. |
| Brings news of Laurent | Attention sharpens. The lead gatherer asks specifics. The conversation lengthens. **The late-game offer becomes a thread the party can develop.** |
| Brings the resting-place offer back from Laurent — accepted | The order receives. Andras is the carrier; the meeting-point and timing are arranged through him. **The campaign's late-flight climax is now staged through the order.** |
| Brings the offer back from Laurent — refused | The order continues to wait. Standing held. *"We are patient."* |
| Asks to join the order | Considered slowly. **Membership requires leaving the old name at the necropolis** — that is doctrinal, not negotiable. The party-member who is serious is sent to a senior gatherer to begin the conversation; the conversation is years, not weeks. |
| Tries to follow a gatherer-group on the road | Permitted at distance for a day; if the party catches up to camp at the threshold of a death-site, they may be welcomed properly. **Tracking them in secret is recognised** and closes the route. |
| Asks to see the necropolis | *"You will see it when the order brings you there."* Not before. The road west is long, and the order does not casually invite. |

## What they cannot or will not say

- **The full doctrine.** Held at the necropolis. Junior gatherers know the work; senior gatherers know more of the why; the full pattern is not assembled in any one head outside the city.
- **The names of senior gatherers** beyond the few each junior knows. Names are leverage; the order does not hand them out.
- **The location or nature of the off-world being** that the order may serve. *"The doctrine speaks of an interest. We do the work."*
- **The full register of demi-liches.** They distinguish those who hold station from those who drift, but specifics are doctrinal and not for visitors.
- **What happened to gatherers who left the order.** A small number have. The order does not narrate it.
- **The journey's exact duration to the necropolis.** *"It is long. You will know when you have arrived."*

## What they can teach

For students the order recognises — and most students do not pass the threshold:

- **Rest-craft for the dead.** A piece of the keeping practice, taught only to those formally taken on by the order. Not transmittable in single sessions; not portable once the student leaves.
- **Reading a death-site.** A practical skill: what a recent mage-death has left behind, what a chaos-touched site looks like, where the drift is going if not gathered. Available to a party that has worked alongside a gatherer-group on the Andras dungeon or a similar offering. *Useful*; not full doctrine.
- **The conduct of the road.** How the order travels, camps, and observes. By example, not by lecture; a party that walks alongside a gatherer-group for a stretch picks the conduct up.
- **A specific doctrinal fragment**, occasionally, when the conversation has earned it. The fragment is real; it is not the pattern. A serious mage student who collects fragments over a campaign may reconstruct one or two pieces of pre-order practice the kingdoms have otherwise lost.

## DM notes

- **The order is a frame, not a faction.** It does not run plots; it carries on a working. The party can earn standing within it without joining; the standing is the *gate to Andras's late-game and Laurent's last-flight destination*.
- **Andras is the canonical entry.** Most parties will work the order through him. The order's first scene — leaving Scarlet Vale — is the breadcrumb; Andras is where the breadcrumb leads (`doc/npcs/andras.md`).
- **The Laurent offer is the order's late-game gift to the campaign.** Verbatim doctrine: *"we will hold your station, in our city, with the others, when you choose to set it down."* The offer has been open for centuries; the party broker its delivery, and Andras is their hand. Closing this thread closes Laurent's last-flight arc cleanly; refusing it leaves the offer open and waiting, which is consistent with the order's whole register.
- **The doctrine fragments are deliberately incomplete.** A DM running a senior gatherer can extend a fragment slightly; the order's register is fragmentary on principle, and the *full pattern is at the necropolis*. Do not assemble it at the table.
- **The order's neutrality is a real feature.** They do not take sides in the kingdoms' wars; they do their work across all of them. A party that tries to recruit them to a faction misreads the order. A party that respects the neutrality finds the order a constant.
- **The off-world being is a held secret.** The file flags it; junior gatherers do not know; senior gatherers carry pieces. A campaign that wants to surface this thread does so through a senior gatherer the party has earned access to over many sessions, not through Andras.
- **Membership requires leaving the old name at the necropolis.** This is doctrinal. A party-member seriously considering joining loses the old name and the old life as part of the entry. Andras's name is *known* — that is unusual and reflects either his particular role as the order's outward-facing contact or a doctrinal allowance the file does not yet specify. ## Origin — what no one knows

**Nobody really knows where the skull gatherers came from.** The order is older than written record on this continent and does not publish its origin. Fragments suggest the gatherers serve, or extend, an **ancient off-world being whose interest in this world is the limiting of *other*, more chaotic ancient beings** — the same older presences the chaos spirits are downstream of. Even within the order the doctrine is held in pieces; junior gatherers are taught the work, senior gatherers carry more of the why. Full treatment in `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Where the gatherers came from — what no one knows."
