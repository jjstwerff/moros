# John Bean

- **Role:** Boar folk (Tusk Clan) farmer; ruin-crawler; the accidental hero who ended the Overseas War
- **Species:** Boar folk (Tusk Clan)
- **Location:** The farm he was granted for his services, in Blackwood freehold (Allondo's agricultural town)
- **Status:** Alive; formally out of the fight

## Summary

A boar folk farmer and ruin-crawler of the Tusk Clan, from a lineage full of strong-willed ancestors. He lost an arm to an ancient machine inside a sealed ironwood-processing mill that **Felix** had found a way into — a low feeding-slot for whole trees, the only way in, and one of the only mills in the region capable of cutting ironwood at all. Inside was the machine that took his arm. **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** fitted him with an ironwood replacement. With the arm he is stronger than before: latent telekinesis, running through his lineage, manifests through it — short bursts directly, longer reach if he pulls the arm off.

He is not a hero in any clean sense. He is a stubborn man who survived things that should have killed him — and, by standing in the wrong place at the worst time, he inadvertently killed **Thorgal**, the overseas mage who had led the invasion. The killing was remote, and the mechanism was not his ironwood arm — he did not yet have it. When Thorgal's mind reached into John's head through the scrying mirror (built to carry spells both ways), it found not what it expected but the **arm John no longer had, still present as a mental projection** — a phantom limb shaped by habit and by forgetting that he had lost it. The probe recoiled from it. John, thinking it was his arm because he had again forgotten the loss, reached back along the connection with that same projection and **pulled**. The mirror carried the pull through. Thorgal fell, far away, among breaking glass. That accident ended the war on the homeland side too — **Raul**, the ox-tauren commander of the forward war band holding Hannes, lost his communication link in the same moment and fell back to regroup, and **Felix** slipped in during the confusion to free Hannes.

The ironwood prosthetic came *afterwards* — **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** fitted it to John's stump later, once the fighting had moved on. With the arm, John's latent telekinesis (which had briefly expressed itself through a phantom in the mirror scene) could now be channelled more reliably: short bursts directly, longer reach if he pulls the arm off.

Whether John fully understood then (or fully understands now) that Thorgal was acting out of need rather than conquest — that the war was a starving continent exporting its hungry — is another question, and not one John has been asked.

Asked, as payment for his service, only a farm and the right never to fight again. **King Hannes** agreed only after John promised that the king could enlist John's eldest son in his place. That bargain is now active — **Ron** is in training. Only **Irna** inherited the Bean lineage's strong will; Ron did not, which the crown has not yet fully reckoned with.

## Relationships

- Father of **Irna** and **Ron**.
- Tusk Clan of the Boar folk; descended from strong-willed ancestors. The Tusk Clan is small and dispersed across several nearby towns (see LORE.md §"Clans and their shape"); his clan-kin are scattered through Blackwood freehold and the surrounding region, linked by kinship and road-traffic rather than by living in the same place.
- Wartime companions: **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** (fitted his arm), **Felix** (raccoon scout).
- **King Hannes** paid him with a farm and holds the enlistment right to Ron.
- His ironwood arm is of the same rare material as the great tree of Elmsfield.
