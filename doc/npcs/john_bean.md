# John Bean

- **Role:** Boar folk farmer (Tusk Clan); ruin-crawler before the war; the accidental hero who ended it. Now formally out of the fight, by his own bargain.
- **Species:** Boar folk (Tusk Clan). Lineage carries latent telekinesis. He carries it more than most.
- **Lives:** The farm he was granted for his services — at **Blackwood freehold**, the agricultural town in central Allondo. **Ellie**, his wife, runs the household with him; their son **Ron** is in the crown's training and is what they both wake up worrying about; their daughter **Irna** is on her own road (currently with the party, if she's joined them) and what they ask the road for word of.
- **First met:** When the party finally rides out to Blackwood freehold to see him — usually because Irna has put him into their conversations enough times that they want to meet the man.

## What the party meets

The party rides up to the farm. It is a working freehold — fences kept, fields cropped, a barn that has been mended this season. A boar folk in farm clothes is doing whatever needed doing when they arrived: mending a fence, hauling feed, splitting wood. He sees them, sets down what he is holding, and walks over.

He does not greet them like a hero. He greets them like a farmer who has visitors. *"You're a way from anywhere. What brings you?"*

If Irna is with them, his face changes — he doesn't perform it, but the change is visible. *"Daughter."* He waits. He doesn't move to embrace her unless she does first. **Ellie** comes out of the house behind him a beat later — she has heard hooves and given him the first moment — and the embrace Irna does or doesn't get from her is on different terms.

If she's not with them, but they have news of her, he will hear it standing where he is, before going inside. **News of Ron, however, comes inside.** Ellie wants it heard at the kitchen table, and John does not argue.

## Personal goals — early and later

He is not going to fight again. That decision is final; the goals run alongside it, not against it. He and Ellie have two children in motion and two parents who cannot be: he by his oath, she because the household and the field will not run themselves. The party is the difference between knowing and not knowing.

- **Early-game — be Ron's parents at distance, while we cannot move.** *What the NPC says* (when the party comes to the farm with road-news, kitchen-table register, Ellie at his elbow): *"He's our worry. He has been since the day they took him. We cannot ride to Scarlet Vale; the bargain says I do not, and Ellie will not leave the farm with both children on the road. If you are going through the capital, find him. Tell us what he looks like. Tell us if he has someone with him in the yards. Tell us what he eats. We are not asking for a campaign of letters; we are asking for the small things people who love a son ask for. Bring back what you saw. We will hear it at this table."* — *Where:* the kitchen at the farm for the receiving; **Scarlet Vale**'s second training yard for the seeing (`doc/places/scarlet_vale.md` §"At the training yards"); **Petra** at the recruits' mess as the second touchpoint (`doc/npcs/ron_bean.md`). — *What:* on each capital visit, find Ron. Note the small things — uniform, weight, the colour of his hands, who he eats with, whether he laughs at Petra's joke this week. Carry the seeing back to the farm and report it plainly to **Ellie at the table**, with John listening. Carry their reply forward to Ron the next time. **Do not perform the news for them**; they want it flat. — *Reward:* John is a father at distance and knows it; Ellie is too. The line stays alive for both parents. The party becomes the household's hand in the kingdom — a standing they do not give lightly. — *Complications:* Ron will not write through the barracks scribe. He may also not want his parents to know specific things (the days the anger comes close); the party has to read what to carry and what to hold. **Ellie reads faces; she will know if a returning visit was fine because the visit was fine, or fine because the party softened it.** Soften it once and she stops trusting the channel. The mill-quest below cannot start until Ron is out of the uniform; this early-game quest is what the family runs while waiting.

- **Early-game — and bring word of Irna when you have it.** *What the NPC says* (after the Ron-update has been heard, at the same table, the second cup of whatever Ellie has poured): *"Our daughter is making her own road. We do not call her back. We did not call her into the road and we will not call her out of it. We do hear from her when we hear. If you are travelling with her — tell us how she is. Tell us if she is sleeping. Tell us if the cough has gone. Ellie wants to know what she is wearing; I want to know what she is thinking; we will both take whichever you are willing to say. The training I would once have wanted to give her, she does not want and would not take. That is hers to decide. The news is what we ask for."* — *Where:* the kitchen at the farm; wherever the party meets Irna on her own road (Elmsfield first, then on toward Luchebert); occasionally **Lady Rhianna** at Scarlet Vale, who arranged her exile and may have read Irna at a register the party has not (`doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md`). — *What:* carry word of Irna *to* the farm — health, mood, where the road has taken her, who she has trusted. Carry the parents' care *back* to her in their voice (Ellie's question about what she's wearing is real, not a performance — Irna will hear it as such). — *Reward:* Irna's parents stay in her life across the road; she is not asked to come back, only to be heard. The party is trusted by all four Beans. — *Complications:* Irna's pride may make small parental questions land hard; carry them with their plainness intact, which is what Ellie meant by sending them. The training-question, if it comes up, has been settled — *do not press it.* John will not raise it; Irna does not want it; Ellie has her own view and will not be cornered into it. The parents will hear *health and choice* gladly; *we are training your daughter* would land badly because it is not true.

- **Later-game — put the mill down, with Ron beside me.** *What the NPC says* (once Ron is discharged and home, at the gate of the farm or on a quiet evening at the field): *"There is one piece I left in the hills. Felix found the way in. I went through a door I do not remember coming back out of. Ron has never been there. Now that he is home, and now that he is the age to walk a road like that with me, I want to close it. Not as soldiers. As the people who are alive. Felix will guide. Rhianna will not come — that is her right, and I respect it. The party that has been my hand in the kingdom is welcome. We will go once. We will not go again."* — *Where:* the **old mill** in the hills (a dungeon: sealed mage-era cutting facility, cutter still partly working, the ground-level slot **Felix** found); the route through the broken lands' edge (`doc/npcs/wilder.md` for the regional context); the **fitting field** where Rhianna once worked on him, now overgrown, on the way out. — *What:* travel to the mill with John, Ron, Felix, and the party. Get inside through Felix's slot. Face whatever the mill still has working. Walk John back through the middle he does not remember. Come out together. — *Reward:* John closes the weight he has carried since the war; **Ron sees what his father survived** (and chooses what to do with the seeing); Felix's road-craft is honoured; the campaign closes a piece of the war's unfinished geography. — *Complications:* the mill is still partly working; the hazards are real. The middle of John's memory may surface in a way that costs him. Ron has not been there before and will be reading his father's silences for the first time. The mirror-camp — **Raul**'s old forward camp — is *not* John's to revisit; Felix and Rhianna can take a party there separately if the campaign calls for it (`doc/npcs/raul.md`).

## How he reads at a glance

- A boar folk in plain farm clothes, older around the eyes than his actual years. Weather-worn. Tusks worn down — he uses them less than younger boar folk.
- **The ironwood arm.** Dark wood, jointed, unmistakably not original. He uses it for the everyday — lifting, gripping, the work the farm asks for. Strangers' eyes go to it; he does not react to that.
- A **right (real) hand with scars** that don't match field work — older marks, from before the farm. He doesn't draw attention to them; they are simply there.
- **Long pauses before answering.** Not slow-witted; he is choosing what is worth saying. The pause is the answer most of the time.
- A walk that **favours the wood arm slightly** even though it shouldn't need to. The body is still adjusting, twenty years on.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **Always working.** If he is at the farm, he is doing something. Idle is not how he holds his time.
- **Quiet at meals.** **Ellie** sets the table; he eats; conversation is brief and practical. The household has settled into this pattern. *News of Ron lands here* — Ellie wants the news in the room where she is feeding people, not in a yard where she would have to carry a bowl back inside afterwards.
- **The wood arm goes through everything the right hand does** — not as a substitute, as a partner. It pours a drink. It carries a child. It mends a hinge. He does not draw a line.
- **Occasionally, when alone in a field**, he will *pull the wood arm off* and do something with the longer reach the lineage's telekinesis can stretch through it. The party catching him at this will see something nobody is supposed to see; he will set the arm back on without comment.

## At the farmhouse

A modest farmhouse, well-kept. Stone lower, timber upper. A kitchen that is clearly the household's centre — Ellie runs it, and the room reads as hers. A small front room with **John's chair** and **Ellie's chair** — both worn the way years of sitting in the same chair wear them.

- **Above the hearth, no trophy.** No memento of the war. No display. The farm is a farm, not a shrine.
- **In a corner of the front room, a small box.** Inside it, the few things he keeps from before — none of them weapons. None of them displayed. The party will not see inside; he does not show it.
- **The kitchen door opens to the back fields.** John leaves it open most of the year.

## At the fields

Where the party will most often find him during the day. Tilled rows, fences, the work of a freehold. He will keep working while they speak, unless they ask for a sit-down conversation; then he sets down whatever he is holding and gives them his attention.

The fields are also where he is alone enough to use the longer-reach telekinesis. A party who finds him at this without warning has stumbled on something private; how he handles it depends on how they handle it.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up. He does not embellish. The pauses are part of the telling.

### When asked about the mill — *what he remembers*

> *"Felix found the way in. Low slot at the ground. We had to fold ourselves to get through. I went first. I always went first. There was a machine inside, and it was still working enough to do what it had been built to do. I do not remember the moment. I remember waking up afterwards. I remember Felix's face. I remember Rhianna in the field, working on the arm. The middle is gone. That is true. I do not perform it for company."*

### When asked about Thorgal — *the mirror*

> *"There was a mirror in their commander's tent. The one their leader was using to give orders from across the sea. I was nowhere near it. He reached into my head through it. He found a missing arm I had forgotten was missing. He recoiled. I — also forgetting — reached back along that line with the same arm I no longer had. I pulled. The pull went through. He fell, far away. The mirror broke. I did not understand what I had done for a long time after."*

### When asked about the bargain with Hannes — *what he asked for*

> *"A farm. The right not to fight again. That was all I wanted. The king agreed on one condition — that he could enlist my eldest son in my place. I said yes. I have thought about that yes for nineteen years. I have not changed my mind about the trade. I have not made peace with it either."*

### When asked about Ron — *what the crown took*

> *(A long pause.)* *"He was not built for it. The lineage's will didn't run in him. The crown assumed it would; it didn't. He is in training. I have not seen him in two years. Irna will reach him before I will."*

### When asked about Irna — *what she did*

> *"My daughter has the lineage's will. More of it than I do, I think. She did what I would have done at twenty-two if I had been her. She paid for it harder than I would have. I did not stop her. I should not have needed to."*

### When asked about the Scarlet Vale version — *the song he won't sing*

> *"They tell it as a punch. It was not a punch. The arm came after. I do not correct the song. The song is not my problem. The song is also not the truth. If the truth matters to you, I have just told it. If the song matters to you, you can find a singer in any tavern and they will not tell you what I told you."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — the long pause gets longer. He looks at the speaker fully. He may glance at the wood arm. The answer, when it comes, is shorter than the question deserved, and final.
- **When distraught** — turns and looks at the field, or out the door, or at the box in the corner. Will not speak for some time. Does not refuse to be there; he is just not going to talk.
- **When happy** — a single short laugh, a nod. Reaches with the wood hand for whatever is near — pours a drink, claps a shoulder, sets a child down on a fence-rail. Does not say anything about it.

## What he can teach

- **Telekinesis through ironwood** — only to a Tusk Clan boar folk with the latent lineage gift. Will not pretend the gift can be transferred where it isn't.
- **Farming** — practical, freehold-scale. Will teach anyone who works alongside him for a season.
- **Brawl, Axes** — Tusk Clan basics. He still knows the work; he will teach what he knows to a serious student. He will not teach for hire and he will not teach for show.
- **Ruin-crawling** — the older trade. What an old machine sounds like before it kills you. How sealed places want to be entered. He doesn't advertise this skill; the party that has earned standing can ask, and he will say yes.
- **Plain speech** — by example. People who spend time with him say less and mean more. Not a power; a habit.

## What he will not say

- **His wife's name, or her own story.** *"Mine to talk about with my wife."*
- **What he said to Ron at parting.** Between father and son.
- **What he actually thinks of King Hannes.** He holds it. Will not perform it.
- **Whether he wishes the war had ended differently.** The pause is the answer.
- **What is in the small box in the corner of the front room.** Not for visitors.

## DM notes

- **Play him plain, not stoic.** John is not silent because he is performing strength; he is silent because he has decided what is worth saying. Whatever he says, he means; the pauses are the texture. A DM playing him too gruff misses the warmth he reaches for with the wood hand. A DM playing him as a wise old hero gets him completely wrong.
- **The arm is the visible motif.** The party will look at it. He uses it constantly without remarking. He pulls it off only in private (for longer-reach telekinesis); a party who sees this once should not see it again unless they have earned the trust.
- **The mirror-camp memory gap is real.** John is honest about it. If the party needs the camp's location, he points them at Felix or Rhianna and that is the end of his usefulness on that thread.
- **Irna's hot-blooded-under-restraint pattern is his too.** She inherited the will *and* the discipline from his side. He shows it differently — long pauses where she pauses, short refusals where she refuses, wood hand reaching for the practical instead of the dramatic. Treat them as the same temperament expressed at different ages.
- **He cost his son.** The bargain with Hannes is the live wound in him. He has not made peace with it, and the file should not pretend he has. A party who brings news of Ron, or who eventually helps Ron, is doing something John could not — and he will not forget that.
- **The farm at Blackwood freehold currently has him and Ellie in residence** (Ron is enlisted away; Irna is on the road). With Ellie now named, the household is two named NPCs; if Ron returns, the threshold is met for a separate place file. Until then, the farm is handled here.
- **He may visit Elmsfield once across the campaign**, if the party invites him for something specific (Irna's recovery, the Bean prophecy reveal, Diederik wanting to compare notes on ironwood). He does not make casual trips. When he comes, the trip means something.

## Role as spirit-shield (none)

John is not a mighty-spirit-adjacent presence. The Tusk Clan lineage is not a mage tradition; the telekinesis is a physical trait, not a working. The farm is held by ordinary means — neighbours, the freehold's loose protections, the broader Allondan agricultural buffer. If chaotic spirits ever pressed Blackwood directly, John would not be able to hold them off. He knows this. He has not advertised it.

## Relationships

- Father of **Irna** and **Ron**. Husband to **Ellie** — whom he does not name to strangers (the name is for his table, not theirs).
- Tusk Clan of the Boar folk; descended from strong-willed ancestors. The clan is small and dispersed across nearby towns (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Clans and their shape"); his clan-kin are scattered through Blackwood freehold and the surrounding region.
- Wartime companions: **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** (fitted his arm, kept him whole), **Felix** (raccoon scout; found the way into the mill).
- **King Hannes** paid him with a farm and holds the enlistment right to Ron. The bargain is active.
- His ironwood arm is of the same rare material as the great tree of Elmsfield — though he does not know this and would not particularly care if told. Diederik is the one who finds this connection meaningful.
