# Osric Vey

- **Role:** Pamphleteer-scholar imprisoned at **Clear Water Mine** for arguing in print that the **Overseas War**'s root cause was the overseas continent's famine, not conquest. **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**'s named extraction target — the *one she could not take* when she got Irna out (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"The NPC hook"). Once the party brings him out, a campaign-spanning ally with overseas-history expertise nobody else on this side of the rift can match.
- **Species:** Human. Tall, thin, ink-stained.
- **Lives:** *Currently:* the convict compound at Clear Water Mine, second-row cell. Coughing. *After extraction:* wherever Rhianna places him for recovery — likely **Luchebert Monastery** as the kingdom's quietest place to disappear (cross-ref `doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`), with **Father Elliot**'s consent. *Long-term:* DM-fill on his eventual settlement; possibly the necropolis (his work would interest the Skull Gatherers' archive); possibly Linar harbour's overseas-descended community where his pamphlet's framing might find sympathy.
- **First met:** At the **Clear Water Mine convict compound**, second row of cells, on the dungeon-arc Rhianna sends the party for. He is *there*. He recognises Rhianna's name if the party whispers it. He is smart enough not to break cover.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Imprisoned scholar-pamphleteer; Rhianna's extraction target; once out, a working historian whose pamphlet-line on the Overseas War's food-crisis origin is the cleanest civilian-side reading of the war the kingdoms have. **Pre-extraction**: a coughing prisoner the party must get out. **Post-extraction**: a campaign-spanning ally for any arc engaging the overseas continent.
- **Standouts:** *Tall, thin, ink-stained fingers*; **a cough that rattles** — Rhianna's exact description; the cough is real and getting worse. **Hands that have written for a living** — fingers stained at the writing pads, calluses on the inner middle finger from quill-work, never on the palms. **Eyes that catch a phrase before the speaker has finished it** — scholar's habit, persistent even when sick.
- **Speech tag:** Pre-extraction: *short, careful, the kind of careful a man learns when his words have already cost him a year underground.* Post-extraction (recovering): *measured paragraphs that turn into citations* — names, dates, archival references. He thinks in footnotes. *"What you are describing is the third manifestation of the food-crisis pattern. The first was at Steadington. The second was the trade-treaty I documented in pamphlet-three. This is the third."*
- **Quests:** Early — *get him out of Clear Water alive; deliver him to Rhianna; help him through the recovery weeks.* Late — *let him write again; carry his second pamphlet-line into the kingdoms; be his witnesses if a court ever wants the testimony.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *He will not perform innocence.* The pamphlet was real; the court called it sedition; he wrote what he believed and was imprisoned for it. He will not apologise for the pamphlet to a party that asks him to; he *will* apologise to specific people the pamphlet's circulation hurt (fishers' families at Linar harbour, soldiers' widows in Allondo) when given the chance. **The cough is real**; recovery is months, not weeks; *Healing* and Rhianna's care matter; *Druid* and the herbal at Luchebert can ease but not cure.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/osric_vey.md`.*

## What the party meets — pre-extraction

The party reaches the convict compound on **Rhianna's letter** (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"At the convict compound — finding Osric"). Trull the foreman walks them past the second row.

**Osric is there.** Tall, thin, *thinner than Rhianna's description* — the year has cost him; the cough has cost more. He is sitting on the cell's stone bench when the party passes; *his eyes catch the cell number on the door before any of theirs do* — he has been counting his own door for a year. He does not look up at the party directly. He listens.

If a party member whispers *"Rhianna sent us"* through the cell's grille — **his hands stop**. He has been folding a piece of cloth in a pattern that is not random; the pattern stops at exactly the place a code-fold is supposed to stop. *He recognises the name without acknowledging it.* He says *"I am Osric Vey, prisoner sixty-three. Your visit is appreciated."* — the line he says to every visitor, in the same tone — and *waits to see what the party does next*.

A party that handles this scene well — a quiet word; a token Rhianna gave them; a specific reference the warden's clerk would not catch — gets cooperation. A party that is loud or impatient gets nothing; Osric has been smart for a year about not breaking cover.

## What the party meets — post-extraction

**At Rhianna's residence in Scarlet Vale**, after the party has carried him out (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"At the path out — extraction"). She has the back room ready; he is on a low couch, propped on cushions, the cough quieter than it was at the mine because Rhianna has put him on a *Healing*-augmented draft (`doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Potion" Quality variant). He is reading a borrowed book; he sets it aside when the party is brought in.

**The first words after extraction are not *thank you*.** He says: *"You are the people who came. I owe you a debt I cannot yet pay; I am willing to pay it the day I can. What is your business at this hour, and would Rhianna want me to tell you the truth in front of you, or to wait until she has decided?"* — the scholar's instinct to *check the meta-question* before answering the question. Rhianna nods; he answers.

After that, the *thank you* comes — and it is plain, brief, and exact. *"You walked into a mine where I was being kept and walked back out with me. I will remember the walk. I will remember the people."* The smile is small; the cough is in the room; the gratitude is real.

## Personal goals — early and later

He has not been able to *want* much for a year. The early goal is survival; the late goal is the work he has been writing in his head while he could not write on paper.

- **Early-game — get me out of Clear Water alive; carry me to Rhianna.** *What the NPC says* (in the cell, with the right code-word from the party): *"I am ready. I have been ready for fourteen months. I cannot run; the cough will give me away within a hundred paces; I cannot ride straight on a hard road for more than two hours. **You will have to choose how to carry me, and the choice will be visible to the warden's clerk if you make it badly.** I am not asking you to die for the carry. I am asking you to think about the cough before you choose. Trull at the foreman's post is *not* on the warden's side; ask Rhianna. Take what I tell you with the understanding that the year underground has narrowed what I have read; I have been writing in my head; I have not been reading the world."* — *Where:* the Clear Water Mine convict compound for the extraction (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`); the road to Scarlet Vale for the carry (two days, with the cough); **Rhianna's residence at Scarlet Vale** for the delivery and the recovery weeks. — *What:* extract him through the route the dungeon supports — Trull's bribe, the warden's signed release after a Hand-or-Char manoeuvre, or a night extraction; carry him on the road with the cough's pace; deliver him to Rhianna at the residence; help during the first recovery weeks (specific tasks Rhianna will name — fetching herbs from Diederik, carrying letters Osric is not yet strong enough to carry himself, sitting with him so Rhianna can run her residence's other work). — *Reward:* **Rhianna's standing with the party rises substantially** — she has been carrying the guilt of leaving him behind; the party has lifted it. **Osric himself** becomes a campaign-spanning ally; he will write for the party (sealed letters; introductions to scholars elsewhere); he will think with the party (his footnote-mind catches connections in the campaign nobody else surfaces). The Clear Water arc closes cleanly. — *Complications:* the cough is *real* — *Healing*, *Druid*, and time are the answer; pushing the carry too hard worsens it. Trull will not bribe casually — Rhianna's instructions matter; a party that improvises the bribe loses Trull's quiet cooperation. **The warden Calder will eventually notice** Osric is gone — within days, sometimes within hours; the party that has not also recovered the document for Ron's case loses the cover Rhianna's letter provided. *Mella the chief engineer* (`doc/npcs/mella.md`) is not part of the political layer; she will not interfere with the extraction either way, but the warden's response under pressure includes pushing the convicts harder, and the wash-tables register the absence of Osric within the same week.

- **Later-game — let me write again; carry my second pamphlet-line.** *Conditional — opens once Osric has recovered enough to hold a quill steadily and to trust that writing again will not return him to a cell.* *What the NPC says* (at Rhianna's residence after the recovery, or wherever Rhianna has placed him; the cough quieter; the eyes catching phrases again): *"My first pamphlet-line cost me a year underground. I do not regret writing it. The court called it sedition; the court was wrong; the people who read it understood what I was saying about the food-crisis pattern. I have been writing the second line in my head for fourteen months. **It is about the same pattern at Brumal's southern desert and how Hannes's quartermasters are reading it the same way the overseas leadership read it before the war**. The pamphlet will be published; I cannot publish it under my own name now; I would like a network of carriers that gets it into the hands the first line missed. I would also like a witness if a court ever asks for the testimony — and I would like the witness to be honest people, not court-funded."* — *Where:* **Rhianna's residence** for the writing; **Nadine's merchant group** through Bridget for some of the carrier-network (`doc/npcs/nadine.md`); **Linar harbour** through the overseas-descended fisher-quarters (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md`) for the framing the first pamphlet missed (his original pamphlet did not name the fisher-quarters; the second will); **Luchebert** through Father Elliot for the careful court-side reading (`doc/npcs/father_elliot.md`); **the Skull Gatherers' necropolis** through Andras for the historical archive (`doc/npcs/andras.md`); possibly **Penelope at Cliffside** for the Brumal-side reading (`doc/npcs/princess_penelope.md`) — *Penelope is one of the few Brumal nobles who would read the pamphlet honestly*. — *What:* be the carriers Osric needs. Carry sealed copies to specific named contacts; carry replies back; sit with him as the work is being written; testify if a court ever calls (almost certainly not, but he plans for it). **Be honest with him** about what the carriers say in return — he wants the *response*, not just the *delivery*. — *Reward:* **the second pamphlet-line is published**. Court-side, the response is mixed; the king's reading-circle takes notice; **Hannes's war-frame may shift** if the pamphlet lands at the right hour (a quiet campaign-side reward — Hannes is not the man his predecessor was, and a serious civilian voice on the food-crisis pattern is something he will *read* even if he does not publicly act). **Linar harbour's overseas-descended community** receives the pamphlet's *acknowledgement of them by name* as one of the campaign's quietest standing-rises; Nadine carries the pamphlet on her circuit; Penelope reads it at Cliffside with serious attention. **Osric's standing with the party becomes a *working channel into civilian-historian readings* the kingdoms otherwise do not have access to.** — *Complications:* the second pamphlet-line risks his life again. A party that pushes for him to publish under his own name forces a choice he is not ready to make; *under a pseudonym* is the right answer but the network must hold the pseudonym; a careless carrier breaks the cover. **Calder is still warden at Clear Water** unless the campaign has moved him; the second pamphlet's circulation may bring back attention; Rhianna and Father Elliot will protect him as far as they can. **The court's reading-circle is not Hannes alone** — courtiers have other priorities; the pamphlet may be read against him as well as for him.

## How he reads at a glance — pre and post extraction

**Pre-extraction (in the cell):**
- *Tall, thin, ink-stained fingers* — Rhianna's description; accurate. The thinness is from convict rations, not from his original frame.
- **A cough that rattles** — heard from the cell's grille at five paces; deeper than ordinary cold; the mine's air has settled in his lungs.
- **Folding a piece of cloth in a pattern that is not random** — code-fold; he has been keeping his mind sharp by encoding the day's count into folds the warden's clerk does not read.
- **Eyes that catch a phrase before the speaker has finished it** — alert even when sick.

**Post-extraction (at Rhianna's residence):**
- The thinness is still there; recovery takes months.
- **The cough quieter, not gone**; *Healing*-augmented potion eases it; *Druid* and the Lethran-line herbs at Luchebert ease further.
- **A borrowed book always near his couch.** Rhianna keeps a small stack rotating — not heavy reading; histories he has not had access to in fourteen months; the catching-up is recovery for him too.
- **Hands that have written for a living** — visible again as the calluses re-firm and the ink-stains return when he begins to write.
- **A scholar's footnote-mind in plain conversation** — names, dates, citations; the way he thinks made legible.

## Visible patterns — pre and post

**Pre-extraction:**
- **Counts his own door.** Has done so since the first week. The count is in the code-fold; *Reading* recovers the days.
- **Listens at the second-row grille at the warden's morning announcements.** Catches phrases the announcer is not aware are being heard.
- **Refuses to break cover.** Has been smart for a year; will not be the one to spoil the extraction.

**Post-extraction:**
- **Reads in the morning; writes in the afternoon when he is well enough.** Rhianna's residence has a small writing-table set up for him.
- **Walks Rhianna's small garden once a day, slowly.** The walk is therapy; the garden's herbs are part of the recovery.
- **Sits with the party when they come, but does not stay long the first weeks.** The cough takes him out of conversations after fifteen or twenty minutes.
- **Begins to receive correspondence after the first month.** Letters under pseudonym; replies sealed for him by Rhianna.

## At Rhianna's residence — the recovery scene

A party that has carried him out finds him here for weeks or months. Rhianna keeps the back room for him; the door is closed when he is sleeping; opened when he is reading.

- **The low couch** is the room's centre. Cushions; a wool blanket; the book of the day on the side-table.
- **A small writing-table** at the window — pen, ink-pot, paper. Rhianna stocks it; Osric uses it more each week.
- **A jar of Diederik's herbs** on the bedside — *Druid*-prepared cough-easers; renewed by visits to Elmsfield Rhianna sends letters for.
- **The hour-glass on Rhianna's table in the receiving room** — visible through the back-room door; Osric watches it sometimes when the cough keeps him from reading. *"Rhianna's hour is the best hour I get."*

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the pamphlet — *what I wrote and why*

> *"The pamphlet was three sheets, printed in Linar harbour at a small press the harbour's overseas-descended fishers funded for me without putting their names on it. The argument was simple: the Overseas War was not started by people who wanted to conquer; it was started by people who could not feed themselves and could not find another answer. I named the food-crisis pattern as I read it from the trade records, the merchant-house ledgers at Linar, and three letters I had been allowed to read from a former prisoner of war. **The court called it sedition because it implicated Hannes's war-preparation reading by analogy.** I knew it would. I wrote it anyway. I do not regret writing it. The cell was the cost; the cost was real; I am still paying it; the writing was worth what it cost. The people who read the pamphlet understood what I was saying. The people who did not, would not have believed any version of the argument."*

### When asked about the cough — *what the mine did to me*

*(Pre-extraction or early post-extraction; he is honest about it.)*

> *"The mine's air settles. The wash-tables' dust gets through any cloth you tie over your face. I worked the wash-tables for eleven months; the foreman knew I was a scholar; he gave me the lighter shifts when he could; it was not enough. The cough is what fourteen months of mine-air does to a body that did not grow up in a mine. **It will ease**; it will not entirely go. I have made my peace with it. I would not wish a year on the wash-tables on the warden himself; I would wish him the cough only."*

### When asked about Rhianna's leaving him behind — *what I told her*

*(Trust real; quiet evening at the residence; the cough quieter.)*

> *"Rhianna came to the mine with crown standing for one extraction. She had not been told she could take a second. The clerk at the warden's office was watching the count; one was the limit. She came to my cell at the end of the visit and asked me what to do. I told her *take the girl*. The girl was Irna Bean; I did not know her name then, only that she was younger than me and that she had a family who had not been told what was happening. **I told Rhianna she would come back for me when she could.** I believed her then; I have believed her every day since; I was right to. She came back. The fourteen months I waited were honest weeks; I do not blame her for them; she was working on the path that brought your party. *The girl I told her to take has had her own year and she is alive; that was the right call.*"*

### When asked about Trull — *the foreman who helped where he could*

> *"Trull is not the warden's man, fully. He carries the warden's orders; he does not carry the warden's enthusiasm. He gave me the lighter shifts when he could. He looked the other way when one of the older convicts smuggled paper. He did not save me. He could not have; the warden's authority is real; the orders are real; Trull's room to manoeuvre was small. **He used the small room well**, and the convicts who survived the year owe him quiet credit for it. The party should know this if they ever come back to the mine on other business. He is not a friend; he is a man working in a wrong post; he handles the post better than most men in his place would."*

### When asked about the second pamphlet-line — *what I have been writing in my head*

*(Late-game; well enough to think about it aloud; the writing-table at the window.)*

> *"The second pamphlet-line is about the same food-crisis pattern at Brumal's southern desert. The desert is taking Brumal the way the overseas continent's failed agriculture took its people; Hannes's quartermasters are reading the situation the way the overseas leadership read theirs — not yet starvation; the precondition. **The point of the second pamphlet is to name the pattern early enough that this side of the rift can avoid the answer the other side reached.** It will not name Hannes; it will name the *pattern* and the *reading*; the reader will draw the line themselves. I have written it in my head; I will write it on paper across the next month if you and Rhianna will help me find the carriers. *I would like the second line to do what the first line did not — to reach the people who would have stopped the answer if they had been allowed to read the question.* Linar harbour's overseas-descended fishers should be named in the second line. The first line did not name them; that was a careful omission; in the second I am willing to be less careful. The fishers will know their names being there is the apology I owe them."*

### When asked about the kingdom — *what I think a writer can do*

> *"A writer can name a pattern. A writer cannot, alone, prevent the answer the pattern points at. But the *reader* who finds the named pattern at the right hour is the person who can prevent the answer; the writer's job is to put the named pattern where that reader will find it. **The first pamphlet-line did its job for some readers and not for others.** The second will do its job for different readers and miss the first set. I am not building a movement; I am writing pamphlets. The reader is the agent; the writer is the messenger. The cell taught me this distinction harder than I had previously read it."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — sets whatever he is reading or writing down. Looks the speaker in the face fully. Coughs once, sharply (the body's tic). Answers in measured paragraphs. Does not raise his voice; he learned not to in the cell.
- **When distraught** — folds whatever cloth is to hand into the code-fold. Will not write while distraught; the writing waits. The folding is the *processing* — a *Reading* observer reads it as Osric's hands working through what his head is sorting.
- **When happy** — a quiet smile that he does not perform. Cites a specific passage from a book the speaker would recognise if they have read it. *"You will have read the line about — yes."* The cough does not interrupt happiness as quickly as it interrupts other states.

## What he will not say

- **The names of the prisoners at Clear Water**, beyond what he has been given consent to share. He keeps the list in his head; he asks before naming.
- **The fishers at Linar harbour who funded the first pamphlet's printing.** Their cover is still in force; he will not break it even now.
- **The exact text of the second pamphlet** before it is finished and Rhianna has read it; he is careful with drafts.
- **What Trull's deeper political position is.** He suspects more than he knows; he will not speculate aloud.
- **Whether he will write a third pamphlet-line.** He has not decided; the second is the work he is committed to.

## What he can teach

- **Overseas-history reading** — the food-crisis pattern; the war's actual causes; the leadership-side material his pamphlet drew on. Open to any serious student; he prefers students who have read his first pamphlet first.
- **Pamphleteering as discipline** — argument-construction; evidence-handling; pseudonym-craft for political writers in a kingdom that imprisons the careless. Trust-gated; he will not teach a hot-headed student who would get themselves arrested in the first season.
- **Reading court records** — what the warden's vault held; what Scarlet Vale's archives hold; what *not* to look for first because the eye that goes there first is the eye the clerks watch. Practical for any campaign that needs records-work done.
- **Sitting with a sick body** — by example, not by lecture. A party member who has spent time at his bedside in the recovery weeks learns something about *waiting through illness with respect* that no other NPC offers.

## DM notes

- **The pre-extraction Osric is *the dungeon's emotional weight*.** A DM running Clear Water (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`) should treat his cell as a scene, not a checkpoint. The party that hears the cough through the grille and sees the code-fold cloth in his hands is meeting a person, not a quest objective.
- **The post-extraction Osric is a campaign-spanning ally** — quiet, honest, scholar-shaped. A DM running him well delivers the recovery weeks across multiple sessions; he is *not yet the late-game ally* in the first weeks; the trust is built across visits.
- **The cough is real.** Do not let it disappear at convenient moments. *Healing*, *Druid*, and time are the answer; the recovery is *months*, not a single rest-scene. The cough's persistence is *the cost the cell extracted*; honouring it gives the recovery its weight.
- **The second pamphlet-line is a campaign-scale arc.** A DM running it well makes the writing visible (he writes at Rhianna's table over weeks; the party brings him books; the carriers are real campaign work). The publication is a real moment that *changes the kingdom's reading-circle conversation*.
- **He will not be a fighter.** Players who try to make him one misread him. The signature move is *write a sealed letter that arrives where it needs to* — not *swing a sword*. Honour this.
- **The Linar harbour overseas-descended community thread** is *the apology Osric owes them*. The first pamphlet's careful omission is the wrong he is correcting. A DM running the Linar arc (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md`) can let the second pamphlet's circulation arrive at the fisher-quarters as one of the campaign's quietest standing-rises.
- **The food-crisis pattern is the spine** Osric reads through. A DM running the campaign's food-and-famine threads — Brumal's collapse, Hannes's quartermasters, the southern desert's advance, the war-preparation — has Osric's *named pattern* as a tool the party can reach for. He will read the campaign's events through his pattern and tell the party what he sees.
- **He pairs cleanly with Rhianna.** Almost every Osric scene runs through her. A DM should let this be — *Rhianna is the gate; Osric is what is inside the gate*. The party that has earned Rhianna has earned Osric as a downstream consequence.
