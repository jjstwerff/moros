# Ron Bean

- **Role:** Younger son of John Bean; reluctant enlistee; subject of a disputed proclamation; future heir to the Bean farm
- **Species:** Boar folk (Tusk Clan)
- **Location:** In soldiers' training under King Hannes
- **Status:** Alive; on a slow path out of uniform

## Summary

Younger brother of **Irna**, only son of **John Bean**. As tall as his father but without the soldier's build or the strong-willed lineage the country assumed he would inherit — Irna got it instead, which the crown's bargain did not account for. Enlisted in soldiers' training at **King Hannes**'s insistence, the price John accepted for his own right to stop fighting. He will most likely be dismissed in time and will inherit the farm his father bought with his arm.

**Father Elliot** of Luchebert Monastery issued a proclamation about a Bean child being a "valiant spirit." The original prophecy is about **Irna** — as a potential spirit-shield, the kind of being chaotic spirits back off from. But whoever translated the prophecy into the proclamation read "valiant spirit" as *martial* imagery — a warrior, a protector with military might — and the natural conclusion was that it must be about a Bean son destined for battle. Ron was enlisted on that reading. It is not what the prophecy meant.

**Lady Rhianna Linthrope** believes the proclamation as issued is trouble and has written to Elliot asking him to correct it. The proclamation has already had consequences — including, by a chain of them, Irna's attack on Didrich and her subsequent exile.

The quiet tragedy of Ron is that everyone expected more of him than he is, and he knows it. The further, harder tragedy is that the expectations were never about him to begin with.

### Unhappy in the city, and closed about it

Ron is **deeply unhappy in Scarlet vale**. He is in training he was never suited to, wearing a uniform he did not choose, on the strength of a proclamation that named him by mistake. He has been there long enough to understand something harder than the training itself: his father's old promise required this *because his father had already given him away years before Ron was old enough to have a voice in it*. He feels **betrayed** — not by any one person cleanly, but by the whole shape of his family's decisions made in his name without him.

If the party seeks him out and tries to talk to him about his family, **he will not**. Polite refusals at best; cold ones more often. Not because he has forgotten them — he has not — but because he cannot hear about them from strangers without the anger coming up, and the anger is the one thing he cannot afford to let show in a military yard.

**The only person who can still reach him is Irna.** Her story, told by her or carried to him by someone who is plainly hearing it from her, is the one channel he has not closed. She is the sister he knew before all of this. She is also, in attacking Didrich, the one member of his family who at least *did something* with their shared pain where he has only had to swallow his. If the party ever brings him Irna's story — in her voice or close to it — he will listen. That is the door.

### What he does know how to do

Ron's training has not been wasted in one specific sense: he has **proper defensive military grounding**. Shield work, holding a line, reading a threat from a guard position, small-group discipline. He is not a duellist and he will not be; he is a competent defensive soldier, which is what the training produces when it does not take deeper. If at some point he leaves the uniform — dismissed, released, or choosing to walk — and **joins a travelling party**, he can **teach a thing or two**. Basic guard work, watch discipline, the bones of surviving an ambush. Not the craft of a master-at-arms like his brother Corven would become; the craft of a soldier whose training *functioned* without giving him a soldier's soul. Useful on the road, given the time to pass it on.

## Relationships

- Son of **John Bean**; younger brother of **Irna Bean**. Tusk Clan of the Boar folk — a small dispersed clan (see LORE.md §"Clans and their shape"); his clan-kin are scattered, and those kin are watching his enlistment with interest, not pride.
- Enlisted in training under **King Hannes** as part of John's payment.
- Named subject of **Father Elliot**'s "valiant spirit" proclamation, though the underlying prophecy appears to be about **Irna**, not him. The proclamation's consequences are reshaping his family's life on a misreading.
- Eventual heir to the Bean farm in Blackwood freehold.
