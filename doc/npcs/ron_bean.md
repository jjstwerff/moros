# Ron Bean

- **Role:** Younger son of John Bean; reluctant enlistee in Scarlet Vale's training yards; subject of a mistranslated prophecy that was never about him; future heir to the Bean farm.
- **Species:** Boar folk (Tusk Clan).
- **Lives:** **Scarlet Vale**, in the soldiers' training barracks. Sleeps in a shared bunk-room; trains in the second yard; eats at the recruits' mess. A party looking for him finds him there at predictable hours. His *real* home is the Bean farm in Blackwood freehold, where he has not been for more than a season.
- **First met:** At Scarlet Vale, usually — the party visiting the training yards (see `doc/places/scarlet_vale.md` §"At the training yards") can find him at drill, at mess, or at the yard's edge when he is on break. The capital has many soldiers; Ron is the one who does not look like one.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Reluctant enlistee, subject of a misread proclamation, future heir to the Bean farm; the campaign's quietest open wound at court.
- **Standouts:** *Taller than the rest of the recruits, slightly off the beat*; **small tusks** (Tusk-clan marking, plain); a **uniform that does not fit at the shoulder**; eyes that go to the yard's edge when family is named.
- **Speech tag:** Polite, closed, short. *"Ron Bean. Recruit. What do you need."* Opens only when Irna's voice is in the room. The small tusks click once when the topic goes deep — a tell he does not know he has.
- **Quests:** Early — *carry the bridge between him and his sister Irna while the proclamation question runs.* Late — *come home to Blackwood after discharge — Irna at the old tree, the conversation with John, the work of years.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *The door is Irna; nothing else.* A party that pushes past his refusals on family or court politics loses what Ron is for. Do not rescue him too easily — the correction is **Elliot's** to make; bypass moves cost Ron something. He is a defensive soldier, not a duellist.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/ron_bean.md`.*

## What the party meets

The party walks the second training yard at Scarlet Vale. Ranks of recruits are drilling — shield drills, formation drills, long-rain-drills. At the back of one rank, **taller than the rest, slightly off the beat, a boar-folk with small tusks and a uniform that does not fit him at the shoulder**.

The drill-master barks; Ron corrects. He is **not bad at the work** — his shield-position is clean, his line-holding is steady — but nothing in his body suggests *soldier*. He is finishing the drill because the drill is this hour.

When the hour ends and the ranks break, he takes water at the side of the yard. A party that approaches him gets a polite, short answer to a polite, short question. *"Ron Bean. Recruit. What do you need."* He is not rude. He is *closed*.

If the party mentions his family without context, the conversation ends. If the party mentions Irna — and they are plainly carrying her story — **the door opens**, slowly.

## Personal goals — early and later

He is closed on every other topic. The two threads beneath the closure are simple and inflexible. He does not phrase them as quests; he answers when the party has already done the part he could not ask for.

- **Early-game — carry the bridge between me and my sister while this thing runs.** *What the NPC says* (when the party has plainly come from Irna and is using her voice, on a break at the yard's edge): *"You have been with her. Tell me what she said. In her words if you can. I cannot write to her through the scribe; the letters get read. I will give you a line back. You will say it to her in my voice when you see her again. I will not ask you what you saw at Luchebert; tell me what she chose to send. Once a fortnight is enough. I will know you are coming because Petra will know."* — *Where:* the second training yard at Scarlet Vale (`doc/places/scarlet_vale.md` §"At the training yards"); the recruits' mess for **Petra** as the second touchpoint; wherever **Irna** is on her road (Elmsfield first, then on toward Luchebert if she reaches it). — *What:* carry Ron's short verbal reply to Irna in his own voice, not summarised; carry hers back the same way; pass small intelligence Ron cannot ask after himself (what **Steven** has done since the attack, whether **Father Elliot** has written the letter only he can write, what **King Hannes** is willing to release on); use Petra as the messenger when direct visits would draw the drill-master. — *Reward:* Ron's closure opens; the small-tusks-click does not turn into something the yard would punish; the party becomes the only line Ron trusts in both directions; both siblings learn the other is real and asking. — *Complications:* Ron will not write through the barracks scribe (letters get read), so the party is his hand or there is no line. Repeated visits become a pattern the drill-master notices; vary the cover. **Court politics talk is refused at the yard** — *"I don't speak about that here"* — so any intelligence-carrying happens away from drill. If the party tries to skip the gate by *delivering* Irna to him at the yard, Ron stops them: the yard is not where she enters his life again. That is the farm.

- **Early-game — push the letter Elliot has not written, on Ron's behalf and without telling him.** *What the NPC says:* nothing. **Ron does not ask the party to do this.** He has decided — explicitly, hard — not to make his case the kingdom's case while he is still in the uniform. The quest is an *open door*: the party reads the situation and acts without being told. — *Where:* **Luchebert monastery** for **Father Elliot** (the only person whose word can correct the proclamation — `doc/npcs/father_elliot.md`); **Brother Darrel**'s upper path above the maze garden where Darrel argues this with Elliot every season (`doc/npcs/brother_darrel.md`); **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** in Scarlet Vale, who has already written to Elliot and signed her full name to the letter (`doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md`); **Irna in person at Luchebert** as the second half of Rhianna's argument. — *What:* amplify the existing pressure on Elliot — bring Irna to him alive (the inflection point per `doc/npcs/father_elliot.md`); carry Darrel's argument with you as evidence rather than rumour; deliver Rhianna's letter alongside her presence if she will travel. The act Elliot must perform is one short corrected proclamation in his own hand. — *Reward:* once Elliot writes, **Hannes releases Ron within a season, formally** (`doc/npcs/king_hannes.md`). The bargain closes. Ron goes home. Irna's case clears at the same hour. — *Complications:* the correction is *Elliot's to make.* A party that tries to force Elliot's hand, go to Hannes directly, or stage a prison-break costs Ron something — the bargain is legal and Hannes is publicly committed to it during a war preparation. Elliot's silence is itself the wound; it cannot be cracked with leverage, only with the right weight in the room (Irna alive; Darrel beside him; Rhianna's letter open on the desk). **Ron must not learn the party did this on his behalf until it is done** — the kingdom-case framing is what he has been protecting himself against; a premature mention turns the gift into an intrusion.

- **Later-game — come home to Blackwood after discharge, and let the farm do the work.** *What the NPC says* (on the road north, in the carriage that carries his discharge papers, or at the gate of the farm itself): *"This is the farm. The neighbours have been working a strip of it for me. I will thank them and take it back. My sister, if she is alive, is who I will look for next. My father is in the barn, probably. I am not going to forgive him today. I am going to work the land beside him until one of us decides. You can stay as long as you want. The work is here for whoever wants to pick up a tool."* — *Where:* **Blackwood freehold** — the farmhouse and outbuildings; **Ellie**'s kitchen as the household's centre; the old tree where John's mother (Ron and Irna's grandmother) is buried; the strip the neighbours have been tending; the nearest **Tusk-clan village** for whatever marriage and clan-restoration looks like; possibly the **old mill** with John and **Felix** who knows the way in. — *What:* be the witnesses, not the architects. Arrange the timing so **Irna** is at the farm when Ron arrives (or arrives shortly after) — the campaign's first patient and its first companion meeting her brother on the land they were both born on. Give the siblings privacy at the old tree; give Ron and John privacy when they begin working the field together; offer to ride alongside on a clan-village visit when invited. If the **old mill** opens as a late dungeon, the party's role is escort and support — Ron has never been inside. — *Reward:* the farm becomes a return-point for the party; Ron is invited to travel with the party on his terms (defensive soldier-craft, watch discipline, farm-sense); both Bean siblings have a place to rest; **John Bean** closes a weight he has carried for fifteen years. — *Complications:* Ron does not forgive easily and will not perform forgiveness for an audience. The conversation between him and John is *theirs*; a party that tries to mediate it forces a wrong shape. **Irna's road south is much longer than Ron's discharge process** — coordinating their arrival is real work, not a montage. The clan-restoration is Ron's to step into; the party is supportive, not central, and may be politely outside parts of it. If Ron decides to stay at the farm and not travel with the party, that is a complete ending, not a failure.

## How he reads at a glance

- **Tall, like his father, without the soldier's build.** He has his father's height and none of his frame. Shoulders that did not fill out the way the drill-master expected; a walk that is farm-walk, not march. The uniform fits him badly, and he knows.
- **Small tusks.** Tusk-clan boar-folk marking — nothing grand, nothing showy. Clan-kin can read the specific set of them at a glance; strangers see teeth.
- **Eyes that look *elsewhere*** when family is mentioned. He will hold eye contact on drill topics; on family, his gaze goes to the yard's edge, the training post, the sky.
- **Shield-callused hands, clean knuckles.** The training has taken at a level — he knows how to hold a line — without taking deeper. His hands have done the work; they have not become a soldier's hands.
- **Uniform care below average.** Recruits are graded on this; Ron does the minimum. Not rebellion. Not-caring-about-the-uniform.

## Visible patterns

- **On the drill rota.** The party can find him at dawn drill, mid-morning shield work, afternoon formation, evening inspection. The schedule is printed at the barracks gate.
- **At the yard's edge on break.** Alone. A waterskin. He does not mix with the other recruits more than required.
- **Mess at the recruits' table.** He eats with his rank. He does not sit at the drill-masters' corner; he does not sit at the officer-adjacents' corner.
- **Writes home rarely.** The barracks scribe knows him: short letters, mostly *to his parents at the farm* — Ellie reads them aloud at the table, his father listens. Nothing to Irna — he does not know where Irna is.
- **Does not go into the city on leave.** Scarlet Vale holds little for him. A rare day off finds him in the barracks yard reading a borrowed book, or at the training post rehearsing the week's form.
- **A joke about the drill-master** he tells only to one specific other recruit — a lanky ox-folk named Petra — who laughs every time. The joke is their running gag; neither would tell the drill-master to his face. A party that spends a shift observing may catch the moment.

## At the training yard — the main scene

Where the party reliably finds him. The yard is open; the drill-master is busy; the recruits are focused on their work.

- **He will speak to a visitor on break or after drill.** Polite. Short. The drill-master will notice if Ron is talking at length during hours; the party should pitch conversations to the breaks.
- **Court-politics talk is refused immediately.** *"I don't speak about that here."* Not because he doesn't think about it; because survival.
- **Work-talk lands cleanly.** If the party asks about shield work, about holding a line, about reading a threat from a guard position, Ron answers competently. This is ground he knows and does not mind standing on.
- **Irna-talk is the door.** If the party carries Irna's story in her voice or close to it, his eyes come back from the yard's edge. He listens. He asks questions. He holds the moment.

## At the recruits' mess — over food

A quieter version of the same scene. The mess is loud; Ron's table is less so.

- **Petra, the ox-folk recruit, is here.** Ron's only close tie in the yards. A party that speaks respectfully to Petra buys a small amount of additional openness from Ron.
- **Meals are short.** Ron eats, returns to work. He will not linger for a long conversation.
- **Drinks are rare.** He does not drink in the city. His father's example is part of this; his own inclination is the other part.

## At the Bean farm, on rare leave — the real scene

When he has leave long enough to reach Blackwood freehold (not often; the kingdom is not generous with training leave), he goes home. A party that arranges their visit on a leave-window catches him there.

- **At the farm, he is different.** Shoulders drop. The gait becomes the farm-walk it was always meant to be. He works the land without being told.
- **He speaks to his father.** Not easily. But plainly. John Bean does not apologise and Ron does not forgive; they work the land together. See `doc/npcs/john_bean.md`.
- **He will not speak of the yards here.** The farm and the barracks are separated in his head. A party that crosses the wall by mentioning Scarlet Vale at the farm is *intruding*, and he will say so.

## What he will tell — and when

| Topic | When he'll speak |
|---|---|
| His name, his rank, his current drill | Immediately. |
| Shield work, guard work, line-holding | Freely. It is what he knows. |
| His clan heritage (Tusk Clan; small dispersed) | Freely, once asked. |
| His family | **Not to strangers.** Polite refusals; cold if pressed. |
| Irna — his sister | **Only if the party is carrying her story.** Then, slowly, warmly. |
| His father's bargain with the crown | Will not speak of. This is the subject under the other subjects. |
| The proclamation, and what it has done to him | Carefully. The anger is close to the surface; he does not let it out in the yards. |
| Court politics, Hannes, Corven, Rhianna | **Not here. Not yet.** Safety first. |
| His time at the farm | Freely, and with a different voice. |
| What he will do when he is dismissed | Specifically. He has a plan. |

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up. Shorter than most, because Ron is closed. A party that earns a story has done something right.

### When asked about his drill — *what they have me doing*

*(Workmanlike; no pride, no grievance.)*

> *"Shield drills morning and afternoon. Formation in the middle. Guard-reading at the evening post. It is not bad work. I am not bad at it. I am not built for it the way the yard expects — everyone here expected a Bean to be smaller and fiercer; my sister has that part. I have what I have. The form is clean. That is all they can fairly ask."*

### When asked about Irna, by the party carrying her story — *before all this*

*(Eyes come back from the yard's edge.)*

> *"She is two years older than me. She beat me at everything until we both realised she was stronger than I was going to be and then she stopped. Not out of kindness — out of knowing. She used to catch chickens bare-handed in the dust. She used to read to me at night when our father was out. She wrote a letter when the mine took her; I did not see it. She is the part of our family I know how to love without qualifications. I do not know where she is. If you know, tell me."*

### When asked about the farm — *where I am from*

> *"Blackwood freehold. Ironwood on the ridges, old stumps in the pasture where the mages cut the males once. My father worked it with one arm for fifteen years. My mother Ellie still has every fence in her head before he does. I know every line fence too. I know the old tree under which our grandmother — my father's mother, the one he will not name — is buried. When they let me go, I will go back there and I will work it until I die. That is my plan. It is the only part of this that makes sense."*

### When asked about the proclamation — *what was said about me*

*(The small tusks click once; he does not notice. The anger is close.)*

> *"A letter came out of Luchebert. Someone read *valiant spirit* and saw a soldier. They named me. They enlisted me on it. My sister is the one the letter was about, and the people who know this cannot or will not say so. I stand in a yard because an abbot did not correct a sentence. I wake up most mornings angry about it. I do not say so in the yards. You asked. I have told you."*

### When asked what he will do after — *when they let me go*

> *"Home. The farm. There will be a field the neighbours have been tending; I will thank them and take it back. If my sister is alive, I will learn where she is and I will find her. I will marry a woman from the nearest clan-village if one will have me; I will not be famous. I will not be in uniform. I will not be in a city. That is the plan. It is all I want."*

### When asked about his father, at the farm — *what sits between us*

*(At the farm. Not in the yards.)*

> *"He gave me to the king before I was old enough to know what giving meant. He had a reason; I understand the reason; I do not forgive it yet. We work the land together when I come home. We do not talk about it much. He is waiting for me to forgive him without asking. I may. I may not. The land does not care, and the work still has to be done."*

### When a fellow recruit is being mocked and Ron steps in — *defending*

*(A rare moment. Short. Stated in the yard.)*

> *"Enough. He's holding the line. Leave him."*

*(That is all he says. The mocking stops. Ron goes back to his post. The moment is complete.)*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — shoulders drop; sentences get shorter; eye contact goes to the yard's edge or the training post. The party will read this as withdrawal. It is — but not rejection. He cannot afford to let the anger show in a military yard.
- **When distraught** (family mentioned, Irna's attack on Steven brought up, the proclamation raised) — **the small tusks click once**, a tiny sound, a tell he does not know he has. A party paying attention catches it.
- **When happy** (rare in Scarlet Vale, regular at the farm) — the farm-walk comes back. The gait slows; the shoulders rise. A specific dry laugh when the joke about the drill-master lands with Petra. He does not announce happiness; it shows in the body.

## If the party does something — what he does

| If the party... | What he does |
|---|---|
| Approaches politely on break | Speaks briefly. Polite. Short answers. |
| Asks about family without context | Ends the conversation. Walks back to the drill post. |
| Carries Irna's story in her voice or close to it | **The door opens.** Ron listens. He asks questions. He holds the moment. This is the one channel he has not closed. |
| Tells him Irna is alive | Stops whatever he is doing. Eye contact directly, for the first time. *"Where."* The answer matters. |
| Tells him Irna is dead | Goes very still. Asks once. If true, absorbs it silently and goes back to drill. The grief comes later, at the farm, alone. |
| Brings word from his father | Takes the letter or message; reads it in private; responds through the barracks scribe the next day. No drama. |

| Offers him a weapon | *"I have one issued. What I need is the uniform off, not a different blade."* |
| Offers him advice about the farm | Listens. He knows his father's land better than advice-givers do; he is polite. Specific practical advice on something he does not know (new seed, a new tool) is welcome. |
| Invites him to join the party after discharge | Takes it seriously. Does not commit on the spot. *"If they let me go, and my sister is alive, we will see."* |
| Mocks or degrades a recruit near him | He intervenes short and firmly. (See story above.) |
| Threatens his family | The shield-callused hands come up; the closed face does not change. He is trained. He will not strike first; he will not yield ground either. |

| Mentions Corven | *"The crown prince. I have seen him once. He looked away."* That is all. He does not volunteer more. |
| Mentions the king directly | *"The king holds my family's bargain. I do my drill. I do not discuss the king in the yard."* |

## What he will not say

- **Court politics.** Not in the yards. Not to strangers. Possibly not even after discharge.
- **His private anger at his father.** The farm knows; the yards do not; the party learns it only if earned.
- **Names of trainees who treated him cruelly.** He will not inform on anyone. Not moral; survival.
- **His full feelings about Irna attacking Steven.** He has not landed, and he will not perform a landing for anyone.
- **His plans in detail until dismissal is near.** He keeps them private. Saying them in the yard makes them unlikely.

## What he can teach

Only after discharge, and only if he joins a party.

- **Defensive soldier craft.** Shield work. Holding a line. Reading a threat from a guard position. Small-group discipline. **Genuine and valuable.** The training took at this level even though it did not take at depth.
- **Watch discipline.** How to stand a watch without missing what matters; how to rotate a watch across a party; how to handle the bored hours.
- **The bones of surviving an ambush.** Specific, practical, not the craft of a master-at-arms but the craft of a competent soldier.
- **Boar-folk farming sense** (from the land side). A party on a long stretch between towns can eat better with Ron than without him.

## DM notes

- **The door is Irna.** Every other conversation with Ron is polite and short. When the party is carrying Irna's story, his closure opens. A DM who plays this gate correctly gets one of the campaign's most emotionally available scenes. A DM who lets the party bypass the gate (by pushing past his refusals, for example) loses what Ron is *for*.
- **Do not rescue him too easily.** The arc is that he will be dismissed in time. A party that tries to shortcut this — by going to Hannes, by forcing Elliot to write the letter, by staging a prison-break — finds friction at every step. The correction is *Elliot's to make*. If the party gets Elliot to write the letter, Ron's release follows within a season. If the party tries to force a kingdom's hand, they cost Ron something.
- **He is not a trained warrior yet and will not become one.** The training produces a defensive soldier, not a duellist. Do not let him out-fight the party's combatants.
- **The farm scenes are different from the yard scenes.** Let the DM play Ron differently in the two places. He is a different creature at home. A party that sees both halves understands him properly.
- **Petra is small-scope on purpose.** An ox-folk recruit, no deeper backstory required. She makes Ron's yard-life bearable. The DM can develop her if the campaign wants; otherwise she is a walking hint that Ron is not alone.
- **The small-tusks-click is the tell.** Use it sparingly. Once per scene at most. When it lands, the party knows the topic went deep.
- **He joins the party post-discharge only if the campaign earns it.** A Ron travelling with the party is a valuable companion — defensive work, watch discipline, farm-sense — but only after dismissal. Do not have him desert; the story is that the kingdom lets him go because the proclamation was corrected.
- **His disagreement with Steven is unsettled.** If the party can arrange a conversation between them — Steven quietly, Ron on leave — it is one of the campaign's more powerful private-scenes. Neither knows how to start it. The party can.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Only son of John Bean and Ellie**, his wife. Both parents alive; both at the farm. Irna is two years older. **The old tree on the farm marks the grave of John's mother** (Ron and Irna's grandmother) — the figure John will not name. Ron has accepted the silence; he and Irna have not yet talked under that tree together.
- **Enlisted at Hannes's insistence** when Elliot's *valiant spirit* proclamation was read as martial and applied to a Bean son. John Bean, wanting to stop being a soldier himself, accepted the bargain.
- **Tusk Clan** — small, dispersed boar-folk clan (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Clans and their shape"). Clan-kin are scattered and watching his enlistment *with interest, not pride.*
- **Will be dismissed when the proclamation is corrected.** Elliot is the one who can do it; he has not. Darrel is pushing him.
- **After discharge, inherits the farm.** If Irna is alive, he will find her. If not, he will grieve at home.
- **Defensive soldier training took** at a functional level. Not deeply — he never became a soldier in his soul — but his hands know the work.
