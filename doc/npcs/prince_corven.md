# Prince Corven

- **Role:** Older son of King Hannes and Queen Fienna; Crown Prince of Allondo; martial heir
- **Species:** Human (of the Allondan royal line)
- **Location:** Scarlet vale (capital of Allondo)
- **Status:** Alive; outwardly the heir his father wants; inwardly coming apart

## Summary

The elder prince. Raised to Hannes's preferred mould: trained in arms, in command bearing, in the assumption that war is coming and Allondo must not look weak. The hardliners at court like him. His younger brother **Didrich** idolises him and has taken the humiliation of Irna's attack as a personal stain on the family honour he now expects Corven to answer. By outward signs, Corven is exactly the king his father wishes he had been himself.

The weight of that martial training is sharpened by the court's quiet prejudice about his mother. **Queen Fienna** is not of royal blood — she is from an important house of World's End but not a royal one — and the court has never fully accepted her. Corven carries *half* a royal bloodline from his father and half a coastal merchant-house lineage from his mother, and he has been *pushed very hard* by Hannes to be martial precisely because that is the easiest way for him to look like a Real Prince to a court that counts his half-heritage against him. Corven is aware of this. He does not talk about it.

What his father does not know is that Corven is **in love with Penelope**, a royal lady of Brumal. How they met, when, and through whom is open — a pre-war exchange, a neutral meeting at Salmonswell, a shared tutor travelling between courts, any of these fit — but the love is real, and it is current. She is in correspondence with him. He has seen her. He has kept all of it hidden.

This is the fact that is breaking him. His training, his father's pride, Didrich's hero-worship, the whole court's expectation of him are organised around leading a war against a country whose royal court contains the person he loves. Every hour of his martial preparation is practice for hurting her. He does not yet know what to do with that. He is visibly a promising prince to anyone who does not look too closely; to anyone who does, he is a young man who has started to sleep badly and who has stopped laughing at things that used to work on him.

## The night rides — what the court sees

Corven is **often away from Scarlet Vale**, and not always as his father's envoy. The court has noticed a pattern. Some of his departures are formal diplomatic errands on Hannes's behalf; the rest are not. On the unofficial trips he dresses down — **"unseemly travel gear"** a courtier would sniff at, plain enough that nobody reads him as a prince at a glance — takes a **fast horse**, and rides off into the night. He leaves quickly, explains nothing, and returns a few days later without a story worth telling.

The court **knows about this**. No one stops him; no one asks him to his face. But the gossip runs: courtiers speculate about a mistress in the countryside, an illegitimate child, a hidden drinking problem, a secret knightly order. The real answer — that he is riding to Brumal to meet **Princess Penelope** — is not among the common guesses, because the court's mental map of his options does not include *the enemy-in-waiting's royal house*. That hypothesis is too uncomfortable to have risen to the surface.

For a party present at court, the pattern is **directly observable**. They see him leave, hear about his having left, or notice his absence being handled with a practised non-answer. Following that thread — asking who he was, where he went, whether anyone has seen his route — is the player-accessible entry into the Corven/Penelope arc and the larger Brumal-pressure thread it sits inside.

## Relationships

- Elder son of **King Hannes** and **Queen Fienna**; Crown Prince of Allondo.
- Older brother of **Didrich** — who idolises him and expects him to lead the family's martial response to the Bean affair and the coming Brumal pressure. Didrich has no idea what Corven is actually carrying.
- Nephew of **Father Elliot** (abbot of Luchebert, former Crown Prince). Whether Corven has spoken to Elliot about Penelope, or whether Elliot has simply *noticed* in the way a retired prince notices his successor faltering, is a live question. Either would be in character.
- In love with **Princess Penelope of Brumal**. Secret, current, active correspondence.
- Quietly noticed by **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** — it is hard to hide personal turmoil from a Mouse clan priestess who reads courts for a living. Whether she has acted on the observation is open.

## What he can teach

Corven can teach **leadership** and **swordsmanship** at a high level — he has been drilled in both from a young age, the concentrated output of Hannes's Path 1 training and the expectations of the court. Command bearing, tactical reading, the mechanics of holding a fight, the harder craft of holding people. He is genuinely good at these, better than his self-doubting moods would suggest.

He will not teach them to just anyone. **Trust is the gate.** A party that has earned Corven's trust — met him without the court watching, kept his confidences, been in his corner when he needed one — can learn from him in earnest. Short of that, he will be a prince with a competent manner and a polite refusal. This is the one teaching relationship in the royal household that the party can access at all, and it is earned, not bought.

## Role in the campaign

Corven is the Hannes family's hidden fault line. So long as Brumal's war is theoretical, he can carry it. When it becomes real — when Allondo mobilises, or Brumal pushes across the border under desert pressure, or the coming-war thread demands a decision — he will not be able to.

Possible arcs (DM picks):

- **The public-love reveal.** Corven brings Penelope to Allondo openly, forces a diplomatic crisis, and by sheer willingness to be the scandal defuses the war. High-risk, high-reward.
- **The Elliot echo.** Corven renounces the crown rather than fight. Second abdication in two generations, Didrich becomes Allondo's king in the worst crisis of his lifetime, the Hannes line bends at exactly the wrong moment.
- **The collaborator arc.** Corven and Penelope work in parallel from each side to prevent or shorten the war, quietly, without either kingdom acknowledging their relationship. The party could discover this and become conspirators.
- **The breakdown.** Corven cannot square it and falters publicly at some ceremonial moment. The court loses confidence. Hannes has to choose between protecting his son and protecting his succession.

## Open questions

- How did Corven and Penelope meet?
- How long has the correspondence been running?
- Does Fienna know? (Plausibly yes — mothers and political intelligence overlap.) Does Hannes? (Plausibly no, unless he has begun to suspect and cannot face asking.)
- What does Penelope's position in the Brumal royal house look like, and how free is she to act on her own?
- Is the love reciprocal in full weight, or one-sided in either direction?
