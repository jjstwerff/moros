# Prince Corven

- **Role:** Crown Prince of Allondo. Older son of Hannes and Fienna. Martial heir by training; secretly in love with a Brumal royal; visibly coming apart to anyone who looks.
- **Species:** Human (the Allondan royal line). Half-royal by father, half-coastal-merchant-house by mother — and the court counts the halves.
- **Lives:** **Scarlet Vale**, the capital. Court-side rooms in the palace's east wing. *Often not there* — the night rides are a visible pattern. (Place file: `doc/places/scarlet_vale.md`.)
- **First met:** At court, in the public register. Or — if the party is watching for it — **at the gate in travel gear on a fast horse**, leaving after dark. The second reading of him is the truer one.

## What the party meets

The party is at a court banquet or a formal reception in Scarlet Vale. **The Crown Prince is present.** Tall, upright, dressed correctly, trained — every movement says *heir*. He greets the delegation, he drinks a measured glass, he speaks precisely, he smiles at the right beats. A stranger watching him sees the prince his father wanted.

He will speak with the party for a few minutes if they are introduced. His attention is polished; the questions he asks are sharp and correct. The interaction is pleasant and does not linger. He has something else on his mind and he is not going to say what.

Later that evening — if the party is near the palace gate for any reason — **they will see him leave.** Plain travel clothes. A fast horse. Alone. He passes the gate with a short word to the watchman the watchman does not volunteer to anyone.

A party reading the two scenes together is reading the truer Corven.

## How he reads at a glance

- **Tall, trained, impeccably carried at court.** The product of Hannes's martial priority. A duellist's shoulders; a commander's bearing; a prince's jaw. He has worked for all of it.
- **Eyes that go *elsewhere* at banquets.** The polite glance around the room pauses a beat too long on nothing in particular. A party paying attention reads this as *he is somewhere else*; the court reads it as distracted-by-war.
- **Hand on the hilt — trained, not hungry.** He rests his hand there the way a swordsman does from habit. The fingers do not grip. An *Axes* or *Sword* specialist reads the distinction at a glance.
- **Two wardrobes.** Court-formal, perfectly maintained. **Plain travel, kept ready.** A courtier who has served long enough has seen the travel gear come and go.
- **Did not laugh at what would have worked a year ago.** Old friends notice. His smile lands at the right beats; the laugh does not come without being summoned.
- **Sleeps badly.** There are signs — the eyes, the pace at dawn drills where he is thirty seconds slower than he should be. Trainers who know him note it and do not mention it.

## Visible patterns

- **At court when duty calls, not when duty does not.** He attends ceremonies, counsels, banquets. He does not linger. He is the first to excuse himself courteously when the evening is past its point.
- **Training at dawn in the first yard.** Where the recruits (including Ron Bean, see `doc/npcs/ron_bean.md`) drill in the second yard, Corven takes the first yard alone or with the master-at-arms. Form work. Sword. Staff for balance. A party who rises early can watch from a distance.
- **Night rides on no announced schedule.** Irregular. The pattern across a season is *frequent enough that the court has noticed*. Unannounced leavings; two- or three-day absences; returns without stories.
- **A specific servant who knows.** Corven's personal attendant — a young human woman named **Mira** — knows where the travel gear is kept and that he will not answer questions about it. She is loyal. She is not a secret-keeper by training; the secret is a secret because nobody has pressed her for it yet.
- **The uncle Elliot's name comes up.** Corven occasionally mentions Elliot at unusual moments — over drinks with a friend, in passing when Hannes is not in the room. A courtier watching for it will notice.
- **A specific look toward his mother at public events.** When Fienna is slighted by a courtier — which happens — Corven's jaw sets. He does not intervene. Fienna would not want him to. The set-of-jaw is the tell.

## At the court banquet — the public face

The scene the party gets first. Long table; the king and queen at the head; princes at their places. Corven performs flawlessly.

- **Polished, remote, correct.** The register is crown-prince. Answers are courteous and short. He will not be drawn into personal conversation here.
- **He asks good questions.** Observant — about the road, about news the party has brought, about the kingdoms. A party who brings genuine information will see his attention sharpen.
- **He will not discuss Brumal in this register.** Too close to the thing under the thing. *"The border matter is the king's."*
- **He will be gone soon.** The first excuse that works, he takes.

## At the training yard — at dawn

Where the prince does his real work, in public where anyone who rises early can see.

- **Form work alone or with the master-at-arms.** Sword; staff as balance work; shield drills he knows from youth. The quality is high.
- **He will spar a respectful visitor.** A party member with genuine skill who asks correctly may get a single match. He will not go hard; neither will he go easy. The match tells both of them where they stand.
- **He does not talk here.** The yard is for the work. Conversation happens after, at the well-house where he cleans up.
- **He notices Ron.** The boar-folk recruit in the second yard. *"The Bean boy."* He has a view; he has not spoken it aloud.

## At the gate, leaving at night — the second scene

If the party is watching — or if they are at the gate for their own business — they see him leave.

- **Plain travel clothes.** The kind that make a courtier sniff. A thick cloak, sturdy boots, a sword that is plain and functional.
- **A fast horse.** One he keeps at the palace stables specifically for this — the stablemaster knows, and does not ask.
- **A short word to the watchman.** Some conventional phrase; nothing revealing. The watchman returns the word and lets him through. He has done this many times.
- **He does not look back.** A prince riding to a mistress looks back at his palace. A prince riding to a diplomat does not. A prince riding to the person he loves does not look back either; the past pulls harder than the present would.

## On the ride, if followed — what the party discovers

A party that chooses to follow — at distance, carefully — discovers the route. Not immediately; the ride takes him a day and a half. His destination is **a border crossing into Brumal**, where he meets Penelope at a small inn or an old ruin, or (most often) at a *meeting place they have established and neither court knows*. See `doc/npcs/princess_penelope.md`.

- **He is not being reckless.** The route is good; the covering is careful; the contacts at both ends are loyal. He has had a year to refine it.
- **If the party is caught following** — by Corven, by either court's agents — the situation becomes delicate. *Not* violence; *not* formal diplomatic crisis; **a conversation Corven has been waiting for**.

## At the meeting with Penelope — if the party reaches it

Rare. Earned. A DM who wants to run this scene gates it behind trust-building across many court visits.

- **He is a different man.** The polished crown-prince is gone. What the party sees is a young man, intelligent, exhausted, laughing — a short specific laugh he has not made at court in months.
- **Penelope is his equal.** See her file. The meeting is not a prince visiting a mistress; it is two royals of opposite kingdoms planning-or-hoping-or-grieving-together.
- **He will ask the party's help** if they arrive honestly. The question in his mouth has been waiting: *can you carry something for us that neither court can know?* The answer will shape the Brumal-war arc.

## What he will tell — and when

| Topic | When he'll speak |
|---|---|
| His training, his command bearing, his martial craft | Freely. It is what he is publicly. |
| The kingdoms, the news, the political questions | At court, in court-register. |
| His mother Fienna, respectfully | Freely, with a warmth the court does not see. |
| His father Hannes | Carefully. The register is *filial respect*. |
| His brother Steven | With a specific tenderness. Complicated — he loves him; he does not trust him not to pressure him into the war. |
| His uncle Elliot | Quietly. Has met him privately at least once since taking on the crown-prince role; declines to say what they discussed. |
| **Penelope** | **Never at court. Only in private, only with earned trust, only once the night rides have been surfaced honestly.** |
| The night rides, at court | Deflected. A courteous non-answer. *"Crown business takes me to places I do not discuss."* |
| The night rides, in private to the right party | **Honestly.** With the necklace of his reasons laid out plainly. |
| The coming war | At court: the king's concern. In private, to earned trust: his private horror at what he is being asked to lead. |

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about his training — *what my father made of me*

*(Court register, lightly. He has answered this before.)*

> *"I started on the sword at eight. Command work at twelve. The master-at-arms was chosen by my father personally. I was drilled for what my father thought I would need — which was, and is, a general's craft. I am good at it; I am good because I worked at it. A Real Prince is what I was trained to be. A good prince — that I am still working at. The two are not the same, though most of the court does not notice."*

### When asked about his mother — *why I hold my jaw when they slight her*

*(Private. Warmth unguarded.)*

> *"My mother is from a house that *makes things*. My father's house inherits them. At court that is forgotten in *every direction except the direction that slights her*. She has borne it without asking me to fight it. I hold my jaw because she will not permit me to intervene; she does not want a scene. I am working on the voice I will eventually need to defend her in a register she can accept. My father is working on the same thing. Neither of us has found it. She has waited longer than she should have had to."*

### When asked about the first meeting with Penelope — *how it started*

*(Private, with earned trust. Rare.)*

> *"She and I met at a meeting neither court recorded — a shared tutor travelling between us when we were fifteen, or a neutral conversation at Salmonswell, or at a court function the chroniclers did not think worth naming. I have not asked her to agree with me on which it was; we have different memories and the difference is ours. The year I understood I loved her was the year I realised I would be asked to lead a war against her country. I have been hiding it since. I am tired. She is tired. We are not as careful as we used to be. You have found me out because the cover is thinner than it was."*

### When asked about his brother Steven — *what he expects of me*

> *"Steven wants me to answer the Bean woman's attack on him. I will not. Not because he does not matter — he matters more than he knows — but because the answer he wants is a duel, and a duel is the opposite of what our family needs. I have told him so. He thinks I am pretending a dignity I do not feel. He does not know what I am actually carrying."*

### When asked about his uncle Elliot — *the one conversation we have had*

*(Quietly. Private.)*

> *"I visited him at Luchebert once, early in my appointment as Crown Prince. He walked me through the maze garden without asking me why I was there. We reached the cloister door and he said *I will not tell you what to do; I will say what I did, and you will decide what that has to do with you.* He told me about his renunciation. I went back to Scarlet Vale and tried to become the prince my father wanted. I think, now, that Elliot was telling me both things — how to renounce, and how to stay — and leaving the choice with me. I do not know which I will choose. I think he does not either."*

### When asked about the night rides — *what they are for*

*(Private, with earned trust. He has been waiting to tell someone.)*

> *"I am riding to Brumal. Not as an envoy. Penelope and I meet at a place neither court knows. We talk; we plan; we fail to plan; we grieve a little; we ride back. I am the Crown Prince of a kingdom preparing for a war with her kingdom. Every lesson my father has paid a master-at-arms to teach me is a lesson in how to hurt her. I do not know what to do with that. I am telling you because I think you can help. Will you help."*

### When asked about the coming war — *what I cannot say at court*

> *"The king is preparing; the king is right to prepare. The kingdom cannot look unprepared; I understand the logic; I am part of the logic. What I cannot say in the chamber — what I am saying to you — is that a war with Brumal is a war I will not survive as a prince. I may survive it as a man. I will not survive it as what my court needs the Crown Prince to be. Something will break. I would prefer it broke *before* the war than during it. If you can help break it cleanly, help."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed at court** — *smoother*, not rougher. More formal. A warmer smile. The crown-prince mode tightens. A party that has seen him privately reads this as *armour up*.
- **When pressed in private** — honest. A pause, a hand to the temple, then a genuine answer. He has been waiting to be pressed honestly by someone he trusts.
- **When distraught** — hand to the hilt, without gripping. Remembering. A tell the master-at-arms would name; a court-watcher would not.
- **When happy** — a specific tilt of the head, quick laugh, brighter eyes. Most often when Penelope is mentioned or in the room. A party who sees this look twice knows what they are looking at.

## If the party does something — what he does

| If the party... | What he does |
|---|---|
| Meets him at a banquet and chats correctly | Correct answers. Polite. Forgettable. He moves on. |
| Brings genuine road news or intelligence to court | Attention sharpens. He will speak with them at length if it is interesting. |
| Asks about Brumal at court | Deflects to *"the king's concern."* Not his register. |
| Watches him leave at night | He does not see them, ordinarily. A careful party is not noticed. A careless one is. |
| Follows him — well-hidden, respectful | Reaches a meeting place or a border inn. The next decision is the party's: approach, observe, or withdraw. |
| Follows him — spotted, handled badly | **Scene shifts.** He has been expecting someone to find him. If the party are trustworthy people, he breaks his cover with them voluntarily. If they are not, the situation is awkward but not violent — he rides on; the party has a fact they cannot do much with. |
| Observes and withdraws | Earns a *specific trust*. Corven notices the restraint. On a later night he may seek them out. |
| Approaches him privately and says they know | The moment of his life he has been waiting for. *"I would like you to sit."* Honest conversation follows. |
| Promises not to tell Hannes | He asks quietly: *"Do you keep promises."* And watches them answer. The promise kept is the foundation of everything further. |
| Reveals Penelope's relationship to the court | **Catastrophe.** Corven's public denial is immediate. The cost to the kingdom and to him is real. He will not forgive. |
| Offers to carry messages between Scarlet Vale and Brumal | Accepted with gratitude, once trust is earned. He will not use the party as carriers casually — only as the stakes rise and he runs out of safer channels. |
| Offers to help prevent the war | **The conversation he has been waiting for.** He will tell them what he and Penelope have considered; what they have ruled out; what they might yet try. The party joins the conspiracy. |
| Offers to support a public-love reveal (see DM notes §"Possible arcs") | A long conversation. He wants this. He is also terrified of it. He will not commit without Penelope's consent. |
| Offers to back him in a renunciation (the Elliot echo) | A complicated conversation. His uncle's path is known to him. He is not sure it is his. |
| Asks him to teach | **Trust-gated.** A party that has earned his trust can learn leadership and swordsmanship from him. Not before. |
| Tells him his mother is being slighted | The jaw sets. He thanks them; he does not go and do anything about it in the moment. He talks to Fienna later, in private. |
| Brings word of Steven | Careful attention. He loves his brother; he does not know how to close the distance the war is opening. |
| Challenges him to a duel | He accepts, bemused. He will not hurt a challenger badly. He will not hesitate if they are a real threat. |
| Tries to blackmail him | He refuses to be blackmailed. *"I will lose either way. Pick your loss."* He means it. |

## What he will not say

- **Penelope's name at court.** Not under any circumstance. The court must not associate the night rides with her.
- **The meeting place's location.** Not to anyone who has not become a direct conspirator.
- **His own doubts about the war, in public.** The court must not know the Crown Prince is considering disobedience.
- **What he thinks of renunciation, out loud.** The conversation with Elliot stays private.
- **That his mother has noticed.** Fienna almost certainly knows. He will not confirm it.
- **What his father suspects.** He has decided not to know what Hannes suspects; the decision is part of how he still sleeps at night.

## What he can teach

**Trust-gated.** After earned standing through at least one conspiracy-level act — carrying a message, covering a ride, keeping a confidence tested in practice.

- **Leadership.** Command bearing; reading a formation; keeping a group together through fear. Trained in him since childhood; genuine craft; transmittable.
- **Swordsmanship.** High-level. He is better than he lets on. A party member with the aptitude can learn specific forms from him across sessions.
- **How to hold two lives at once.** Not a formal teaching; a by-example lesson. A party member who travels with him through a crisis learns something about managing competing allegiances that no book carries.
- **The politics of a court that counts half-heritages.** His view, earned from his mother's example. Useful for any character whose own position has a half-acknowledged dimension.

## DM notes

- **Two scenes, same prince.** The file's spine is the contrast between court-register Corven and private Corven. A DM who only plays the court version misses him; a DM who only plays the private version wastes the cover.
- **Gate Penelope.** The secret is the fulcrum of the whole arc. Do not let parties reach it cheaply. The sequence should run: notice the night rides → speculate (mistress? illness? knightly order?) → follow carefully → confront privately → confidence earned → eventual reveal. Skipping steps cheapens the arc.
- **The four possible arcs, all legitimate.** From the previous historical version, preserved and available:
  - **Public-love reveal.** Corven brings Penelope to Allondo openly; forces a diplomatic crisis; by willingness to be the scandal, defuses the war. High-risk, high-reward.
  - **Elliot echo.** Corven renounces. Steven becomes Allondo's king in the worst crisis of his lifetime. A second abdication in two generations; the Hannes line bends at exactly the wrong moment.
  - **Collaborator arc.** Corven and Penelope work in parallel from each side to prevent or shorten the war, quietly, without either kingdom acknowledging their relationship. The party as conspirators.
  - **Breakdown.** Corven cannot square it and falters at some ceremonial moment. The court loses confidence. Hannes must choose between protecting his son and protecting his succession.
- **Rhianna has noticed.** She has not acted. A party asking her about Corven gets a careful reply — she confirms he is carrying something, declines to specify. If the party has helped surface the truth, Rhianna may partner with them quietly.
- **Mira the attendant is a quiet access point.** She is not a conspirator. A party approaching her correctly — not bribery, not threat, *compassion for her position* — can learn small facts that confirm the pattern. Push her badly and she closes.
- **Steven is collateral.** Corven loves his brother; the gap between them is the gap between a prince who knows and a prince who does not. A DM can run Steven as the one person Corven most hates lying to — worse than Hannes, because Steven trusts Corven completely.
- **Elliot knows something.** Whether Corven has told him, or whether he has inferred in the way a retired prince reads his successor, is a DM decision. Either works. If the DM wants Elliot to act — writing the letter that corrects the Bean proclamation, pressing Hannes, intervening in the Brumal crisis — the nephew's situation can be the lever.
- **Do not run him as the tragic-young-prince stereotype.** Corven is not a doomed figure; he is a young man choosing under bad conditions. A DM who plays him as capable, tired, and *deciding* plays him correctly. A DM who plays him as unable-to-act plays a weaker version of him and loses the arc's stakes.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Crown Prince of Allondo** since his father's accession. Trained for it from childhood; shaped for it by Hannes's martial priority.
- **Met Penelope** at an unspecified pre-war encounter. Correspondence has been running for years; in-person meetings via the night rides for at least the last year.
- **Visited Elliot at Luchebert once** as a newly-appointed Crown Prince. The conversation in the maze garden is the one piece of counsel he holds privately from both his parents.
- **Has not acted.** He has carried. The arcs above are what happens when he stops carrying.
- **Mira the attendant** is a young human woman, loyal, not a conspirator — she keeps the travel gear and asks no questions because he has not answered any. A DM needing a named minor NPC for scenes with Corven can use her.
