# Lady Rhianna Linthrope

- **Role:** Priestess and crafter of the Mouse clan; wartime medic; political fixer
- **Species:** White mouse (animal-person), of the Mouse clan
- **Location:** Scarlet vale, capital of Allondo (court society); was in the field during the Overseas War
- **Status:** Alive

## Summary

A small white mouse who gives the impression of having seen most things and been impressed by few of them. She served with **John Bean** during the Overseas War — she is the one who attached an ironwood piece, salvaged from a walking statue of old mage work, to John's stump after his arm was taken by an ancient machine in a collapsed tower. That wartime bond is why she still speaks for the Beans inside Allondo's court.

Her wartime story included losing her own home. Her residence was in one of the towns the overseas force had occupied; when Crown Prince Elliot's offensive advanced toward that town in the war's cleanup phase, **Felix** judged the situation and pulled her out under urgency. She did not stay to see what the liberation looked like. She fled ahead of her own side's army, because in the fog of war being the right person in the wrong place is still not safe, and Felix knew it. She has not returned.

She was close enough to the events of that war to understand what John did not. John killed **Thorgal** — remotely, through the scrying mirror Thorgal was using to direct his forward commander **Raul**; it was that same breaking of the mirror that let **Felix** slip in to free Hannes. Rhianna knows (or has pieced together since) that Thorgal's war was about food, not conquest. The overseas continent could not feed itself; Thorgal shipped the surplus demi-humans here as soldiers because he had no better answer. John ended the war by killing him, and the food problem that drove it is still there. Rhianna has not told John any of this. She watches the coming next-war pressure with that knowledge in the background.

Today she is based in Allondo as a priestess and crafter of the Mouse clan. She intervened for **Irna** after the attack on Didrich — stormed into the royal castle, and **King Hannes** could not say no to her. She had been present for whatever shameful thing happened to Hannes in the war, and both of them know it; that shared knowledge is the lever she pulled. She got Irna out of a forced mining assignment and arranged exile to **Luchebert Monastery** instead — and in choosing that specific destination, she was not just saving Irna from the mine. She suspects (though may not yet be able to prove) that the "valiant spirit" prophecy was misread: that the prophecy was about Irna as a potential *spirit-shield*, not about Ron as a warrior. Luchebert is where Irna could discover what she actually is, under Elliot's care and near Felicia's shrine. She also wrote **Father Elliot** directly, signed her full name, and asked him to *correct the trouble he caused*.

Stern with her friends, practical in her kindness, and better connected than her size suggests.

### Iron on the outside, exacting on the inside

From the outside, Rhianna reads as a **spirited iron lady** — sharp-tongued, upright, unintimidated by rank, difficult to move and difficult to lie to. That is not an act; it is what decades of surviving a war and keeping a court together actually look like. Most people who deal with her deal with that surface first.

Underneath it she is **willing to teach**. Medicine, crafting in the Mouse-clan tradition, court-reading, spirit-adjacent priestly practice — she has real depth in each, and she will pass what she knows on. But not to anyone who wanders up and asks. **She teaches the worthy**, and what worthiness means to her is her own judgement: evidence of honest effort, evidence that the skill will be used for something she can respect, evidence that the student will not embarrass what she has spent her life being good at. The iron outside is part of the gate. A student who cannot get past it is not a student she wanted.

For a party that earns standing with her — through sustained good work, through not treating her kindness as a transaction, through showing up in ways that cost them — she becomes one of the most capable mentors in the homelands. For a party that does not, she remains sharp and polite and quietly unavailable.

Her letter to Elliot asking him to *correct the trouble he caused* is, concretely, a request that he **break his retirement from Allondan politics** long enough to publicly reattribute the prophecy. Rhianna knows Elliot will not want to. She is asking anyway, and she has sent Irna in person as the argument he cannot answer with silence.

## Role as spirit-shield (Scarlet vale)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a serious priestess of the Mouse clan reads to chaotic spirits as a mighty-spirit-adjacent presence. Scarlet vale, as the capital of Allondo, carries the same problem every major city on this continent carries: most of its genuine spirit-readers have gone to the countryside, and the urban cover is thin. Rhianna is one of the few remaining practitioners who still lives at court. The capital's resilience against chaotic-spirit pressure is partly on her — more than she or the king would say aloud. If she leaves Scarlet vale for any reason, the city's cover thins further; this is not in the league of her formal court duties, but it is real.

## Relationships

- Wartime companion of **John Bean** and **Felix**; fitted John's ironwood arm.
- Correspondent with **Father Elliot**.
- Arranged **Irna Bean**'s release from mining and exile to Luchebert Monastery.
- A figure of standing in the **Mouse clan** of Allondo. Per LORE.md §"Clans and their shape," demi-human clans are small and dispersed; Rhianna's standing in the Mouse clan represents a *network* of mouse folk distributed across Scarlet vale, the agricultural towns, and the villages whose mouse folk look to her — not a local constituency. Her political reach at court follows from that network, not the other way round.
