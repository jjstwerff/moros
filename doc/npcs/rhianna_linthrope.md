# Lady Rhianna Linthrope

- **Role:** Mouse-clan priestess and crafter; wartime medic; political fixer at Allondo's court; the one who got Irna out and wrote the letter Elliot has not yet answered.
- **Species:** White mouse (animal-person), of the Mouse clan.
- **Lives:** Her own residence in **Scarlet Vale** (see `doc/places/scarlet_vale.md` §"At Rhianna Linthrope's residence"). Does not return to Rakeville, where her old life was.
- **First met:** When the party reaches Scarlet Vale, or earlier through letters and reputation. Most parties hear about her before they meet her — Irna mentions her, John mentions her, Elliot's correspondence mentions her — and arrive curious.

## What the party meets

The party reaches Rhianna's residence — by invitation, by letter from a credible source (Elliot, Diederik, Irna), or by walking in with a credible introduction. They are received in a quiet room with two chairs and a small table.

A white mouse — small in physical scale — looks up from whatever she was doing. She does not stand for them. She gestures them to the other chair. *"Sit down. Tell me who sent you and what for. I have an hour."*

That is her opening every time. The hour is not a polite fiction; she runs her day in pieces, and the party gets the piece she has set aside for them. If they need more, they earn another hour next visit.

She does not waste the hour on courtesy that does not move the conversation. By the time it ends, she will know what the party wants, what they are willing to pay for it, and whether she trusts them. She will tell them whether she will help, and how, and on what condition.

## Personal goals — early and later

She runs her day in pieces and she runs her goals the same way. The early one is a single specific letter. The later one is the work of as long as the kingdom continues to need her.

- **Early-game — finish the Bean correction.** She wrote to **Father Elliot**; she sent **Irna** as the second half of the argument; she got Irna out of Clear Water on the leverage she has used twice and will not name. The piece still missing is **Elliot's letter correcting the proclamation**. *Where this goes:* through **Brother Darrel** at Luchebert, who has been pushing Elliot from inside the monastery for the same reason; through Irna's actual arrival at Luchebert, with the party as escort and as credible witnesses to what was done to her at Clear Water; through **King Hannes**, who has decided privately that he will release Ron the day the correction arrives. Rhianna does not need to be in the room when it is signed. She needs the party to *carry* the moment to the people who can produce it. A party that brings back a corrected letter from Elliot has done what years of her own letters could not.
- **Later-game — hold the kingdom's quiet shields steady through the Brumal crisis, and prepare the next ones.** Allondo's shape is held together by people who do not advertise the work — **Elder Diederik** at Elmsfield, **Felicia/Willow** at Luchebert's vale, **Wilder** on the western edge, herself in Scarlet Vale, the **Mouse-clan network** spread across all of it. The Brumal collapse will press on every one. *Where this goes:* coordinating the Mouse-clan response to refugee streams (**Hank**'s farm; **Ingram** at Salmonswell; the agricultural towns); reading whether **Willow** has chosen Irna as her successor at Felicia's shrine and what training is needed; backing Diederik's overseas plan when it requires court-side standing; sitting with **Hannes** in the hour before each hard decision so the king does not make it in worse company; recognising and protecting the *next* generation of spirit-shields wherever they emerge. She will not lead a faction; she will work piece by piece. A party in her standing becomes the carriers of the pieces she cannot run herself, and the eyes she does not have outside Scarlet Vale.

## How she reads at a glance

- A **small white mouse**, pale even for a mouse-folk. Sits straight. Stands when she chooses, not when convention asks.
- A **worn medical satchel** within reach — even at court, even when she is not expecting a patient. She has not put it down in twenty years.
- **Eyes that have seen too much to be impressed** by anyone in the room. She looks people in the face fully; she does not flinch.
- Mouse-clan **dress and cut** — well-made, not flashy. The fabric is good; the lines are plain. Anyone who reads court will read it; anyone who doesn't will see only that she looks like herself.
- **The hour-glass on her table.** A small one. She turns it when the conversation begins, not as theatre — as practice.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **Receives in pieces** — her day is divided into hour-or-so segments and she runs each one with the same focus. The party gets one segment; sustained relationships get more.
- **Writes constantly** — letters to the Bean family, to Elliot, to clan members across the network, to people she has not seen in years. The Mouse-clan postal habit runs through her hands.
- **Sees patients without ceremony** — at the residence, at the palace if the crown asks, in the trade districts when she is passing. The medical satchel is not for show.
- **Goes to court only when she has reason** — she does not haunt the palace. Her standing comes from being summoned, not from being available.

## At her residence

A quiet household in Scarlet Vale, small enough that a single hand keeps it. The receiving room is where she does most of her work with visitors. (For the residence as a place inside the wider city, see `doc/places/scarlet_vale.md` §"At Rhianna Linthrope's residence.")

Three things stand out in the receiving room itself:

- **Two chairs and a small table** — no audience seating, no display. The room is built for conversations she intends to be heard cleanly.
- **A small hour-glass** on the table. Measures the visit. She turns it without comment.
- **A wall of pigeon-holes** behind her chair — letters in progress, letters waiting to be answered, letters she has been told to forget about and has not. The Mouse-clan correspondence runs through this wall.

| The Bean family — John, Irna, Ron | Freely. She is invested. She has thoughts. |

| Teaching | Yes — to the worthy. Worthiness is her judgement. |
| The court's gossip | She has views. She rarely shares them. |

## Stories she tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about John's arm — *the walking statue*

> *"John lost the arm in the mill. By the time he reached the field where I was working, he had lost too much blood to wait for a careful solution. I had been salvaging from a walking statue of old mage work — ironwood plating with metal joints — that the field-camp had brought in for a different purpose. I shaped a piece of it to his stump and bound it. The piece carried the geometry the lineage's telekinesis needed; the gift in his line found it. He was stronger after than before. The story Scarlet Vale tells about an *immeasurably strong arm* is wrong. The arm is a channel. The strength is his."*

### When asked about leaving Rakeville — *Felix's call*

> *"I had a house in Rakeville. I had a practice there. The overseas force occupied the town. When Crown Prince Elliot's offensive turned to liberate it, Felix came to my door and told me to leave in the next hour. I argued for ten minutes. He won by walking out the door without me. I caught up. The town liberated itself without me. I have not been back. He was right, and I have not forgiven the country that made him right."*

### When asked about storming the castle for Irna — *what Hannes could not say no to*

> *"I came to court without an appointment. I went to the king. I told him what had been done to John Bean's daughter on his orders, in plain language, in front of his attendants. He could not say no to me, because of something I know about him from the war. I am not telling you what it is. I asked for Irna's release and her exile to Luchebert. He gave me both, in the moment, so that he would not have to give me more. I did not push for more. I left him standing."*

### When asked about the letter to Elliot — *what she wrote*

> *"I wrote to Father Elliot — I signed my full name on it — and asked him to correct the trouble he caused. The proclamation is his to fix. I told him so. I sent Irna in person as the second half of the argument. I expect Elliot to break his retirement long enough to write the letter. Whether he will, I do not know. He is a careful man. So am I. We are both, in our different ways, used to disappointing people who are waiting on us."*

### When asked about the prophecy — *what she suspects* (trust-gated)

> *"The prophecy was misread. It was about Irna, as a potential spirit-shield — not about Ron as a warrior. The interpreters wanted *valiant spirit* to mean *warrior*; it doesn't. I have not yet been able to prove it to anyone who matters. I sent her to Luchebert because that is the place where she could discover what she actually is, under Elliot's care, near Felicia's shrine. I have hopes. I have not committed them to writing yet."*

### When asked about teaching — *who she takes*

> *"I teach the worthy. What worthiness means is whatever I judge it to be. Honest effort. Evidence the work will be used for something I can respect. Evidence the student will not embarrass what I have spent my life being good at. The iron outside is part of the gate; a student who cannot get past it is not a student I wanted. People I have taught include — and I am not naming them — every successful figure in this kingdom you would expect to find under a teacher's hand. None of them advertise it. None of them are required to. I am proud of them quietly."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — does not soften. Looks the speaker in the eye more fully. Makes shorter sentences. The hour-glass continues to run; she does not pause it.
- **When distraught** — works. Goes to a patient, a letter, a piece of crafting at her bench. Will not sit. Does not refuse to be approached, but conversations during this time get the briefest possible answers.
- **When happy** — rare and specific. **A small tilt of her head. A single phrase she only uses then: *"There. That's right."*** A compliment offered to her that she actually accepts (instead of redirecting) is the surest sign.

## If the party does something — what she does

| If the party... | She responds by... |
|---|---|
| Asks for medical help | Provides it. Does not charge. The work is not transactional. |
| Asks for teaching, vaguely | Polite refusal. *"I'd need a clearer reason."* Tries the gate again only on later visits. |
| Asks for teaching with a specific honest reason | Considers. May yes; may delay. The gate is judgement, not formula. |
| Brings news of Irna | Stops everything. Asks them to tell her again, slowly. Will rearrange her week to act on it. |
| Brings news of Ron | Listens carefully. Does not perform feeling; she will write a letter that night. |
| Asks about the leverage she holds over Hannes | *"I am not telling you what it is."* Will not return to the topic; will not be drawn out. |
| Asks for the mirror camp's location, vaguely | *"I do not have anything useful for you on that."* Closes the topic. |
| Asks for the mirror camp's location, specifically and informed (naming Raul, the war's collapse, why they need the mirror) | Considers. May give the location with conditions. See `doc/npcs/thorgal.md`. |
| Tries to use her standing as leverage at court without consulting her | She finds out within hours and is *unimpressed*. The party's access through her closes for some time. |
| Lies to her | She knows, usually before they have finished. Does not call it out; the trust simply does not advance. The party may not realise this is what is happening for several visits. |
| Threatens her | Does not engage. Calls the household hand, who shows them out. The next attempt to reach her closes through the front door. Whether it closes through other doors depends on what they did next. |
| Brings her a piece of clan business — a Mouse folk in distress, a clan letter, a name she should know about | Sets aside the hour she had planned. The clan network gets her time without negotiation. |
| Asks her to publicly oppose Hannes | She will not. *"I am not a faction. I am one person with leverage I have used and would use again, privately. I will not lead a movement against him. That is not who I am."* |

## What she can teach

For students who pass the gate (see *the worthy* story):

- **Medicine** — practical and learned. From battlefield triage to long-term treatment. The most accessible of her teachings.
- **Crafting in the Mouse-clan tradition** — small-precision work, mechanism, fitted joinery. The same hands that fitted John's arm.
- **Court-reading** — how a room is arranged before anyone speaks; how a silence does the work of a sentence; whose absence matters more than whose presence. Rare and earned.
- **Spirit-adjacent priestly practice** — how a great spirit's reach is felt; how a shrine is tended without being asked. Less developed than Willow's but real. She will defer to Willow on the deepest levels of this.

She is not a mage and does not teach mage-craft.

## What she will not say

- **What she knows about King Hannes from the war.** This is the leverage; she has not used it more than necessary; she will not weaken it by speaking it.
- **The names in her Mouse-clan network**, beyond a few. Names are leverage. Leverage is not for casual conversation.
- **Whether she has personally been touched by Felicia's presence**, or whether her priestly practice runs only through study and discipline. Private.
- **What she actually thinks of King Hannes as a person**, separate from her professional dealings with him. She has views; she will not share them with strangers.
- **Where, exactly, in Rakeville her old house was.** The town is a closed page; she does not open it.

## DM notes

- **Play her sharp, not cold.** The iron outside is real; the warmth underneath is real too. A DM playing her aloof misses the practice of the work; a DM playing her warm misses the gate. The interest is the *combination* — a woman who will charge nothing for a child's broken arm and refuse to teach the same child's parents her craft because their reasons are not good enough.
- **The hour-glass is a generative motif.** Every visit has a clock. The DM should run her conversations with this in mind — she does not over-stay, she does not let the party over-stay, and the next conversation has to be earned, not assumed.
- **The leverage over Hannes is the campaign's quiet lever.** She has used it twice — once to get Irna out, possibly once before for something the file does not yet specify. A party that learns *what* she knows is in dangerous territory; she will not tell them. The leverage's existence is enough for them to work with.
- **She is one of the capital's last spirit-shields.** Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," she reads as mighty-spirit-adjacent and holds part of Scarlet Vale's chaotic-spirit cover. If she ever leaves the capital, the cover thins fast and the city does not realise it. This is a campaign-scale fact: anything that drives Rhianna out of Scarlet Vale is a quiet disaster.
- **She is the relational hub for the Bean arc.** John (fitted his arm), Irna (got her out, sent her to Luchebert), Ron (writing letters about), Father Elliot (the proclamation correction), Felix (lifelong debt). A party that wants to act on the Bean situation usually goes through her at some point.
- **Her residence does not yet meet the 3+ NPCs threshold for a separate place file** — she is the only named resident; household staff are unnamed. The receiving room is described above; the broader city context is in `doc/places/scarlet_vale.md`.
- Lore cross-references: Mouse-clan structure in `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Clans and their shape"; the spirit-ecology mechanism in LORE.md §"Spirit Ecology" and §"The protection is largely unseen"; the mill and John's arm in `doc/npcs/john_bean.md` and `doc/npcs/thorgal.md`; the Bean prophecy in `doc/npcs/father_elliot.md` and `doc/npcs/irna_bean.md`.

## Role as spirit-shield (Scarlet Vale)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a serious priestess of the Mouse clan reads to chaotic spirits as a mighty-spirit-adjacent presence. Scarlet Vale, as the capital, carries the same problem every major city on this continent carries: most of its genuine spirit-readers have moved to the countryside, and the urban cover is thin. **Rhianna is one of the few remaining practitioners who still lives at court.** The capital's resilience against chaotic-spirit pressure is partly on her — more than she or the king would say aloud. If she leaves Scarlet Vale for any reason, the city's cover thins further; this is not in the league of her formal court duties, but it is real, and she is not unaware of it.

## Relationships

- Wartime companion of **John Bean** and **Felix**. Fitted John's ironwood arm.
- Correspondent with **Father Elliot**. Has asked him to correct the proclamation about the Bean "valiant spirit." Expects an answer.
- Arranged **Irna Bean**'s release from forced mining and her exile to Luchebert. Suspects (privately) that the prophecy is about Irna as a potential spirit-shield, not Ron as a warrior.
- A figure of standing in the **Mouse clan** of Allondo. Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Clans and their shape," demi-human clans are small and dispersed; her standing represents a *network* of mouse folk distributed across Scarlet Vale, the agricultural towns, and the surrounding villages — not a local constituency. Her political reach at court follows from that network, not the other way around.
- Holds **leverage over King Hannes** from something she witnessed during the war. Has used it twice (at minimum) and would use it again if necessary; will not speak of what it is.
- Knows where **Raul's forward war-band camped** and where **Thorgal's haunted mirror** was left. A party that asks pointed, informed questions can draw it out of her; a vague approach cannot. See `doc/npcs/thorgal.md` §"Who knows the location."
