# Father Elliot

- **Role:** Abbot of Luchebert Monastery; tender of Felicia's shrine; source of the "valiant spirit" proclamation; former Crown Prince of Allondo
- **Species:** Human (the human royal line of Allondo — humans still tend to hold leadership in the kingdoms)
- **Location:** Luchebert Monastery, in a vale beyond Laurent's downed city
- **Status:** Alive

## Summary

Abbot of Luchebert Monastery. **Officially, the Abbey is dedicated to Saint Luchebert** — one of the first human priests of the white city, ridiculed by the mages, heard to by everyone else, who synthesised his teaching from an ancient holy book that had come through the portal (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Saint Luchebert"). The monastery's public identity runs through his tradition. The ties to Saint Luchebert are real but loose and mostly ceremonial — the tradition he founded was a tradition of *reaching, not grasping*, and it was always flexible enough to receive new presences. The rites that matter in the monastery today, the shrine that is actually tended, the drive that shapes the sheltering work done there, all run through **Felicia**, who is the monastery's *true* saint — received into a framework Saint Luchebert's own flexibility made room for. This is not stated openly. It is not secret either: anyone who spends enough time inside Luchebert and asks the right older members will eventually be told. The person most likely to tell them is **Willow**, an old rabbit-folk granny who has tended Felicia's shrine for decades.

He is also **the former Crown Prince of Allondo**. At the time of the Overseas War, Elliot held that title; his younger brother **Hannes** was not then heir and not yet king. Elliot **led the main offensive against the invaders** during the war — a Crown Prince in arms, fighting the war his kingdom had been forced into. His forces drove the enemy out of a set of occupied towns, town by town, after John's killing of Thorgal had already collapsed the overseas command. The opponents he fought were in shambles, cut off from their own lands, dying or surrendering in confusion. One of the towns he liberated was **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**'s residence — Rhianna had already fled under **Felix**'s urgency before Elliot's forces arrived, because any advancing army is dangerous and Felix's instincts said leave.

What broke him, and what eventually renounced him, was not the fighting itself. It was what he saw on the other side. As he pushed the offensive forward, he encountered the *misery among the invaders* — the starving demi-humans Thorgal had loaded onto ships because his continent could not feed them, now dying on a foreign shore in a war they had not chosen to wage. He was not prepared for that. He had come to win a war and had found himself killing people the other side had also been using up.

After the war, Elliot renounced the crown and entered monastic service at Luchebert. Hannes became king in his place. The result is that Allondo's current king is a man who did not expect the throne, and the royal line's most senior surviving brother runs a small monastery in a remote vale, tending the shrine of a great spirit and quietly sheltering the disappeared — some of whom are the very overseas demi-humans he cannot unsee.

That lineage matters for every political thread he sits on. He is **Hannes's older brother**, **Queen Fienna's brother-in-law**, and **Didrich's paternal uncle**. When Irna Bean attacked Didrich, she attacked Elliot's nephew. When Rhianna routed Irna to Luchebert for exile, she routed Elliot's niece-by-proxy into his personal care. When he issued a proclamation about a Bean child being a "valiant spirit" — one that misreads the underlying prophecy about Irna and got Ron enlisted — he was making a spiritual statement that his brother's kingdom then translated into a political one. Nothing Elliot does is only one thing.

A prophecy about a Bean "valiant spirit" — the prophecy is about **Irna**, her potential to become a spirit-shield — came out of his monastery. What he did *not* do was **direct how it was spread**. Elliot did not circulate the news himself; someone else did, the martial reading ("valiant spirit = warrior") took hold, and by the time it reached Allondo's court it had become a statement about a Bean son destined for battle. Ron was then enlisted on that reading.

Elliot **knows something went wrong**. He has known for a while. What has stopped him from correcting it is the decision he made after the war: **his role for Allondo is finished**. He stepped away from the crown and from its politics deliberately, and stepping back in — even to correct a misreading he should have directed more clearly — means reclaiming a role he does not intend to have again. So he has let the proclamation stand. It has cost Irna her freedom, Ron his life at the farm, and the Bean family its peace, and Elliot is aware of each of those costs and has chosen to live with the awareness rather than return to court to fix them.

**Lady Rhianna Linthrope** has written to him asking him to correct the trouble he caused, and sent **Irna Bean** to him as her messenger, though Irna has not yet arrived. Rhianna is asking, in effect, for Elliot to break his retirement. Whether Irna's arrival at Luchebert — as a living person, not a proclamation — is enough to get him to do so is one of the live questions of her path.

The monastery also shelters those who need to disappear for a while. Elliot does not ask every question. Since the Overseas War, some of the disappeared have been **stranded overseas demi-humans** — soldiers or their children who could not go back to a starving continent and could not stay visibly where they had been. The Luchebert shelter is, quietly, one of the few places in the homelands that has not treated those arrivals as enemies.

### The travelling years

Between renouncing the crown and settling at Luchebert, Elliot **travelled**. For how long exactly, and where exactly, even he can no longer say cleanly — he was **not in his right mind** during most of that stretch, and the places have blurred and re-ordered themselves in his memory in ways he has stopped trying to correct. Mountains, marshes, dry country, forested country, coast, inland, cold, hot. He went where his feet took him and kept moving. Some of it may have been as far as the fringe of the shifting lands. Some of it was probably much closer to home than he now remembers. The moral state he was in during those years was the moral state of a man who had led an offensive that he could not unsee and had not yet found somewhere to stop, so the travel was not *chosen* so much as *continued*.

What stayed with him is the **navigation**. The memories of terrain blurred; the skill of reading terrain did not. Wind, sun, watercourses, the angle of old stone, the way pine smells at different altitudes — these are things his body still reads automatically. He is one of the most capable navigators Luchebert holds, and arguably the most capable anywhere within reach. He does not advertise this. If asked cleanly and without pressure, he can teach it, and he can advise a party on terrain he has never seen by extrapolating from the terrain he *has* — the principles travel even where his memory of the places does not.

## Relationships

- **Older brother of King Hannes**; former Crown Prince of Allondo. Led the main offensive against the invaders during the Overseas War, saw the misery of the starving demi-humans Thorgal had sent as soldiers, and renounced the crown after the war on the strength of what he could not unsee.
- Parallel moral arc to **Brother Darrel** (the Beaver folk sergeant who walked away from the same war for similar reasons, at a different rank), who **lives at the monastery**. They know each other well — same response to the same war at opposite ends of the chain of command — and they disagree on one issue, steadily: Darrel thinks Elliot should write the letter that corrects the Bean proclamation, because Elliot is the only person who can. Elliot has not. The disagreement does not break the relationship; it is the texture of it. See `doc/npcs/brother_darrel.md` §"What he and Elliot disagree about".
- **Paternal uncle of Didrich** (and of the older heir).
- **Brother-in-law of Queen Fienna.**
- Correspondent with **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**.
- Expecting **Irna Bean** as an exile; she is lost and ill and has not yet arrived.
- Source of the prophecy about a Bean "valiant spirit" — the prophecy is about **Irna**, not Ron. He did not spread the news himself; someone else did, and the martial misreading took hold as it travelled. Elliot knows this has gone wrong and has **chosen not to correct it**, because doing so would mean breaking his retirement from Allondan politics.
- Oversees the Abbey that officially belongs to **Saint Luchebert** (the saint the monastery is named for) but is, in practice, **Felicia**'s; her actual shrine inside Luchebert is tended day-to-day by **Willow**, an old rabbit-folk granny whose quiet authority on the matter predates Elliot's arrival.
- Shelters others who wish to disappear, including **Brother Darrel**, who has lived at the monastery for years.

## Role as spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a priest tending the shrine of a great spirit reads to any passing chaotic spirit as a mighty-spirit-adjacent presence. Elliot knows Felicia personally through the shrine, which reinforces the reading. Luchebert's vale is therefore held by two overlapping layers of cover: Felicia as the great spirit she is, and Elliot as her serving priest. Elliot almost certainly understands he is part of that shield — given what he knows — though he is careful about saying so.
