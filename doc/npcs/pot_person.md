# The Pot-People (Buried Tower)

- **Role:** Two surviving iron puppets in the buried tower at the Lake of Tears. One small caretaker who keeps the workshop. One that *is* the tower's furnace.
- **Species:** Pot-people — iron puppets with bound spirits. Pot-shaped, not humanoid; a one-off experimental lineage of the mage who built this tower.
- **Lives:** Inside the buried tower, nearest of the six towers around the Lake of Tears. They are the place; the place is them.
- **First met:** Session 3, when the party descends the chimney into the workshop.

## What the party meets

The party reaches the buried tower under siege from the fungus and finds the chimney is the only way in. They descend it on the timing of the exhaust blasts — or fail to read the timing and pay for it. At the bottom the chimney empties into the open mouth of the central pot, and they step *out of* a being and into a workshop kept too neatly for a ruin.

A small pot sits on a workbench. It is not where pots usually go. A broom is leaning against it. A small chisel rests beside it. None of those tools arranged themselves.

This is the moment the party realises the tower is inhabited. What they do next decides whether the rest of the session is a friendship or a violation.

## How they read at a glance

**The Boy.**
- A knee-height pot of iron-fired clay, glazed dull. Stubby legs tucked under when still.
- **Holds tools when surprised** — broom, chisel, brush, dustpan — and forgets to set them down. This is how he is usually noticed.
- Moves in short, deliberate motions when not hiding. Never wastes a step.

**The Furnace.**
- A large central pot, twice a person's height, with **stubby iron legs partly buried in floor-ash**.
- **Breathes**. Exhaust comes in pulses; the air around it warms and cools on a slow rhythm.
- Glows faintly inside. Not fire; the spirit's presence.

## At the buried tower

Three standouts on entering the workshop after the chimney descent:

- **Fungal growth crawls the exterior** of the tower toward the lake, thickest where the chimney emerges. Inside, the workshop is *clean*. The contrast is sudden.
- **The mage's living quarters are preserved** as though their maker might be back tomorrow. Bedding still laid. Tools still racked. Dust controlled. Centuries of dust controlled.
- **The hum of the furnace at the centre** — a deep, slow warmth and a pulse of exhaust on a cycle the party can learn to read after one or two breaths.

The chimney is both the tower's only entrance and its lung. The workshop is the boy's life's work.

## How they communicate

The two pot-people communicate in different registers.

**The boy cannot speak.** A pot has no mouth. What he can do is **draw on the ground** — chalk, furnace-ash, a finger in dust. His drawings answer specific questions. Vague or hostile questions get small shapes, or silence; he thinks before committing ink to stone.

- A party that asks *which way to the workshop* gets a map on the floor.
- A party that asks *what happened to your maker* gets a sequence of images.
- A party that approaches him as a creature gets reluctant cooperation. A party that approaches him as a person gets a friend.

DMs should **describe drawings in the moment** rather than summarising them — what he chooses to draw, in what order, is more informative than any sentence he could have given.

**The furnace does not draw and does not move on party-scale time.** What it does is **breathe** — pulses of exhaust, pauses between, slow shifts in heat. A party that learns to read the rhythm can ask permission to descend, can wait for an answer, and will receive one — not in words, but in the breath pattern shifting to allow them through. Spirit-aware characters with **Shamanic** can speak to the bound spirit inside the furnace directly; this bypasses the breath channel and is the *only* way to have something like a real conversation in party-time. Even then the answers come slowly. A sentence from the furnace, on its own timescale, may take a season to arrive.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- The boy **sweeps** — the workshop is never not being maintained. Dust does not accumulate where he works.
- The boy **draws when asked, and only when asked**. He does not volunteer.
- The furnace **breathes on a cycle** — pulses of exhaust roughly every minute or two, longer pauses between, audible if the room is quiet.
- The furnace **never moves**. The party may visit many times before they realise it *can* move. It can.

## What the boy will draw — and when

Quick answers. The deeper topics (the mage, the furnace, Haven, what he wants) are full drawing-sequences — see §"Drawings he makes" below.

| If asked about... | What he draws |
|---|---|
| The way to a part of the tower | A small floor-plan in chalk, accurate. |
| The fungus outside | The fungal shapes, then a short line of breath-marks pushing them back. |
| The other towers around the lake | The towers as he has seen them from the chimney top — partial, accurate where he can see, blank where he cannot. |
| Strangers he has just met | A small pot with eyes. He is drawing the party. They are now in his house. |

## Drawings he makes (his version of stories)

The DM should *narrate the drawing* as it happens — what he draws, in what order, where he hesitates, what he erases.

### When asked what happened to his maker — *the door closing*

He crouches. Begins to draw. *The mage at his desk. The mage at the door. The door closing. A line of dust accumulating across the floor. The dust line lengthening over years. A small pot at the centre of the lengthening dust, sweeping.* He stops drawing when the line reaches the edge of the floor. He looks up at the party.

### When asked about the furnace — *family*

He draws two pots. The big one in the centre. The small one beside it. He circles them both with one continuous line. Underlines the circle twice. Sets the chalk down.

### When asked about Haven, or others like him — *the rumour he half-believes*

He hesitates a long time. Then draws *a horizon, a long road, a settlement of pot-shapes — but the shapes are wrong, taller, more humanoid. A question mark beside them. A small pot at the near end of the road, tools in hand, looking.* He has heard of Haven. He does not believe it. He wants to.

### When the party asks if he is afraid of strangers — *what hiding looks like*

He demonstrates rather than draws. Sits very still on the workbench. Pulls his legs under. Does not move. Waits for the party to look away. (The party may not look away — and *that* he has not figured out a response to yet.)

### When asked about the workshop he keeps — *the year cycle*

Four small pictures in a row: *the workshop in spring (cleaning), in summer (a slow week, mending a tool), in autumn (the fungus retreating), in winter (the chimney working hard).* An arrow from winter back to spring. He has been doing this for centuries.

### When asked what he wants — *the drawing he has never finished*

He puts down the chalk. He goes to the far wall of the workshop where there is a drawing he has made before, and erased, and made again, many times. The current version is faint. *The workshop with the door open, and a small pot standing in the doorway, looking out.* He has never drawn what is *outside* the door. He looks at the party and then at the drawing and waits.

## Mannerisms

**The boy.**

- **When pressed** — does not move at all. Becomes a pot. The party may wait minutes before he relaxes. Rushing resets the count.
- **When distraught** — draws over old drawings, scrubbing them away with his sleeve before redrawing. The same image appears and disappears repeatedly. Does not look at the party while doing this.
- **When happy** — draws something the party can keep. Hands them the chalk. Watches as they leave with it.

**The furnace.**

- **When pressed** — the breath rhythm gets *shorter*. Exhausts come closer together. The room warms. Not a threat exactly — the furnace becoming alert. A party who notices and eases off receives a slow return to the previous rhythm.
- **When distraught** — long, slow exhalation. Then nothing for a long time. The party may believe it has gone dormant. It has not. It is grieving on furnace-time.
- **When happy** — the breath steadies into a longer, deeper cycle. The room is warmer than it was. The boy notices first; he sweeps closer to the furnace when this happens.

## If the party does something — what happens

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Descends the chimney without timing the breath | Catches a full exhaust blast. Endu damage. The furnace is not pleased. |
| Descends the chimney respectfully, waiting between breaths | The exhaust pauses long enough. The furnace has agreed. |
| Picks up the boy or moves him | He becomes a pot. Will not move for hours. The session's trust resets to zero. |
| Notices the chisel/broom and points it out gently (without grabbing) | Wins the first beat of trust. The boy slowly *uncurls*. |
| Cleans alongside him in the workshop | He watches them while they work. After they leave he draws them at work. |
| Takes or damages anything in the mage's preserved quarters | He draws a single line through his drawing of the maker. He does not look at the party again that visit. |
| Treats the furnace as a machine — strikes it, levers a vent, demands a path | The breath rhythm shortens. The party gets a warning. If they continue, the *next* exhaust catches them on purpose. |
| Treats the furnace as a person — speaks to it, waits for it, thanks it | The furnace remembers. Next visit is easier. |
| Suggests the boy should leave with them | A long, long pause. He goes to the wall-drawing of the open door. Erases part of it. Redraws. The conversation is now open. He will not commit on first asking. |
| Suggests the boy should leave *and* names somewhere he would be safer (Haven) | He listens completely. Draws a question mark, then a Haven-shape, then his own pot beside it. He is considering it. |
| Threatens either of them | The furnace closes its exhaust upward and stops breathing for the duration of the threat. The room gets cold. The party realises that was the only way out. |

## Cross-NPC takes — drawings and breath

This serves the **cross-NPC quick takes** role for non-verbal NPCs: the medium changes (drawings for the boy, breath/heat for the furnace), the function is the same — *what each pot-person thinks of the people the party may ask after.* Short on purpose; they have met almost no one, and the goals.md cap-of-about-ten works in both directions. For the Mage, Haven, and each other, the boy's stance *is* one of his drawings (see §"Drawings he makes").

| Person | Boy's stance (drawn) | Furnace's stance (felt) |
|---|---|---|
| **The Mage** (their maker) | — see *the door closing* | Long, slow exhalation. Memory at a timescale the party cannot share. |
| **Each other** | — see *family* | Warmer for a moment. The boy is family. |
| **Haven** (rumoured) | — see *the rumour he half-believes* | A long pause. Has not heard the name in a very long time. Then steadies. It is thinking. |
| **The party — first arrival** | A small pot with eyes (himself), watching. | The chimney descent *was* the test. They passed or didn't. |
| **The party — after trust** | Drawings of them at work in the workshop. | Steady warmth. The breath includes them in the rhythm. |
| **The Hermit Shaman** (across the lake) | A tower across water; he has seen it from the chimney top, never met the shaman. Curious. | A slow attentiveness when the topic comes up. It has felt the shaman's tower across the centuries; whether they have communicated is a DM call. |
| **The fungus** (the visitor outside) | A spreading shape, then a single line crossed through it. He does not draw it twice. | Exhausts come closer together. The room warms by a measurable amount. The fungus is the one outside thing the furnace responds to physically. |
| **Tod / the forbidden woods** (if mentioned) | A small pot at one edge of the floor; an indistinct warped shape at the other; chalk-line of distance between. He has not seen it. He has heard chaos through the larger spirit network the furnace touches. | Steady. Does not want it nearer. |

## What they can teach

For students patient enough to learn from beings that do not speak in their rhythm:

- **Mechanism work** (boy) — repair, maintenance, the small precision of keeping old things working. Teaches by demonstration, slowly, at his own pace.
- **Patience with non-verbal beings** (boy) — informally, by being one. Players who spend time with him learn to read communication that isn't speech.
- **Reading breath patterns / spirit rhythms** (furnace; *Shamanic* only) — how to listen to what doesn't speak in words. Difficult; rare.
- **Heat-craft and forge basics** (furnace; eventually, only after deep trust) — the furnace knows how it was made, and how *more like it* could be made. This is **campaign-scale knowledge**. The third option alongside the Last Flight and the Shamans' Plan — restoring the furnace to fuller operation — opens here.

## What they cannot or will not communicate

The four items below are **honest ignorance** — things they genuinely do not know, or cannot represent in their medium.

- **The boy's name**, if he has one, is not something he can draw. He has no symbol for himself except the small-pot figure he draws. If the party gives him a name, he will accept it.
- **What is inside the workshop's locked drawer.** He maintains it. He does not open it. The mage closed it; he has not.
- **The reason the mage left.** He does not know. His drawings are observation, not explanation.
- **The furnace's name.** It may not have one. It may not need one. The boy's symbol for it is *the big pot.* That may be enough.

## What each carries — and will not share

Distinct from the honest-ignorance list above: each pot-person carries one thing they *do* know and have chosen not to put into their medium. The party does not need to find these; they shape behaviour whether the party does or not.

- **The boy is forgetting his maker's face.** In a corner of the workshop the boy never sweeps near, there is a faint ghost of a drawing he has made and erased many hundreds of times — the maker's face from memory, redrawn each time it has slipped a little further. Each pass is more abstract, more *shape* than person. He knows he is forgetting. He has not shown the corner. The dust on the floor in front of it is the only place in the workshop where his footprints overlap their own tracks; a careful party will see it before they understand it. To name it aloud would oblige him to say *I am losing him* — and that is a sentence he has not built a drawing for.
- **The furnace knows the chimney can be sealed from the inside.** It has known this for centuries. A long, deliberate exhalation upward, held closed, would end the breath cycle and let it go dormant. The seal is structural; it would not be undoable from outside. The furnace has considered this many times — when the maker did not return; when the fungus first reached the lake; whenever the boy has been still for a season. It has not done so because the boy is still here. **If the boy walks out with the party, the furnace's silent question becomes *what now*.** The answer, on furnace-time, may be sealing. The party that takes the boy may be the party that takes the boy *and* the breath.

## DM notes

- **Stories goal adapted: drawings replace verbal stories.** The pattern transfers to other non-verbal NPCs (Haven's puppets, certain spirits, mute beings).
- **Voice samples adapted: breath patterns and visual signs** for the furnace. Speech is not the only voice. Transferable pattern for spirit-NPCs generally.
- **Cross-NPC takes naturally short here.** The boy has met almost no one; the furnace fewer still. The "about 10" cap on cross-NPC takes works in both directions — sometimes the right answer is *four*.
- **They are the key to the first shackle.** The boy walking out with the party — *genuinely*, after earned trust — is what makes the furnace stand up and follow. The buried tower then leaves the ring around the lake. This is the relational mechanism for the first of the six tower-shackles. See `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"Sessions 5+ → The Other Towers" and the *last flight* thread.
- **Haven is the obvious destination** if the party commits to relocating the boy. He has heard of it; he does not believe it; he wants to. See `doc/places/haven.md`.
- **The chimney descent transfers as scenario pattern** — *negotiation with a being mistaken for an object*. Other NPCs in the setting fit this shape (Tod in a fox boy's body; the Storm Mage mistaken for a hazard; Laurent mistaken for a ruin). The pot-people are the gentle introduction.

## Role as spirit-shield (partial)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," both pot-people have bound spirits and have been in place for centuries. Their combined presence at the buried tower reinforces the Lake-of-Tears region's already dense spirit-shield against chaotic-spirit migration. The Lake-of-Tears ring (Felicia, Laurent, the Hermit Shaman, the boy, the furnace) is the densest concentration of mighty-spirit-readers on the continent. *This is two of them, not one.*

If the boy walks out and the furnace follows, that shield thins by two. The cost of the first shackle is paid here as much as anywhere.

## Open questions

- Do the boy and the furnace *communicate*? On what timescale? A sentence from the furnace might take a season to arrive.
- Does the furnace have its own name, or does it use no word for itself at all?
- Would restoring the furnace to fuller operation allow it to make *more* pot-people? That would be a campaign-scale consequence.

## Relationships

- Both are creations of the same unnamed mage who ran the buried tower's experimental programme. Creator long gone (left, died, or recalled overseas; the boy does not know which).
- Both are kin (in construct-type) to **Haven**'s humanoid ironwood puppets, but *not of* them. They are the last remaining branch of a side-project Haven's mainstream does not fully recognise. See `doc/places/haven.md`.
- The **Hermit Shaman** at another of the Lake-of-Tears towers is the most likely outside party to know about them, given proximity. Whether they have communicated across the lake in all these years is a live DM question.
