# Irna Bean

- **Role:** Daughter of John Bean. Exile bound for Luchebert Monastery. The campaign's first patient, then its first travelling companion.
- **Species:** Boar folk (Tusk Clan), female, 22 years old.
- **Lives:** *Currently* — wherever Diederik is keeping her alive. *Once recovered* — wherever the party is. She has no fixed home. The farm at Blackwood is her father's; her own life is in motion.
- **First met:** Session 1, slumped against a post at Elmsfield's gate, her grey-muzzled dog standing guard.

## What the party meets — Phase 1: at the gate

Session 1's opening obstacle. Irna is feverish, half-blind, **crystalline fungal growths on her skin and over her eyes**, breathing laboured. She **cannot speak**. Her cloak is pulled tight. She is slumped against a post and the only thing keeping locals off her is **a large grey-muzzled dog** that has already driven off two of them.

Whatever she came here to do, she is not doing it now. The party are bystanders who can choose to act. **[Farmer Joseph](farmer_joseph.md)** is the first voice raised — loud, afraid, sling in hand. **[Egbert](egbert.md)** reads Irna from a fence-line past the gate — she cannot approach because the **dog would detect her as a predator** through her concealment — and the read reaches the party indirectly (a note from Hank, or Hank in the square, or a party member glimpsing her at the fence): *no contagion signature, crystal almost scent-free, carrying something rather than spreading something*. The dog **notices her anyway** — hackles up, attention shifting to something out past the gate. **[Elder Diederik](elder_diederik.md)** arrives last and takes charge of her care, adding the full diagnosis (Lake of Tears fungus, not airborne, not contact-spread). Before that full arrival, the party may calm the dog, examine her, and discover the letter she carries to Father Elliot. She will not consent or refuse — she is past that.

This phase is short. She is unconscious or near-unconscious for most of it. The dog speaks for her; Egbert's nose (at distance) speaks for the *not-sickness-you-think*; Diederik speaks for the cure path.

## What the party meets — Phase 2: once recovered

When Diederik's cure takes (Session 4, give or take), Irna recovers her speech, her sight, and her feet. She wakes deliberate, hungry, and immediately impatient to be on the road again — the letter for Father Elliot is still in her pocket, and Luchebert is still where she was going.

She is **a different NPC after recovery**. Where the party met an unconscious patient, they now meet a **22-year-old Boar folk brawler with a strong will and a clear destination**. She will accept the party's company on the road to Luchebert — she has earned the right to ask, and they have earned the right to be asked. From this point on, she is **a travelling companion**, not a charge.

## Personal goals — early and later

She has an early-adult sense of justice: sharp, impatient, more willing than her elders to use the upheavals around her as pry-bars rather than wait them out. The two threads scale years apart, and each pulls a specific cast of people and places into reach.

- **Early-game — find out what actually went wrong with her family.** Her mother cried in the nights for a year before they came for Ron. Hannes's iron-demand at **Clear Water** was war preparation; she watched the orders come down to the pit. Steven has never explained the proclamation to her face. *Where this goes:* back into **Clear Water mine** for the orders book she did not get to read; to the **old mill** where her father lost his arm and **Felix** found the way in (Felix has the door-knowledge; her father has the rest); to a real conversation with **Steven** when his apology arrives (see `steven.md`); to **Lady Rhianna** on how the proclamation was framed; to **Father Elliot** on the prophecy that was actually about her, not Ron. She has the questions. She does not have the pieces. A party that runs these threads with her is a party she trusts at depth — and every answer feeds the later goal.
- **Later-game — reunite the Beans and clear their names.** Get **Ron** out from under the crown's misread of the prophecy — which means **Father Elliot** publicly correcting it, **Felicia** at the shrine confirming it, and the court hearing it. Give her **mother** the explanation. Strip the Scarlet Vale myth off her **father** and let what he actually did (Thorgal through the mirror, not a punch) stand. She is willing to ride the campaign's wider troubles toward this — Brumal's refugees, **Hannes**'s war if it comes, an audience at court — when the door is there. The **shackle** comes off when this is done, or when the king explains it out loud, whichever happens first.

## How she reads at a glance

- A young Boar folk — broader-shouldered than her age suggests. **Tusks visible** when she speaks; she does not hide them.
- **Hands that have done both farm work and pick work** — calloused in two different places. *In Phase 1* the fungus has crusted her knuckles; *in Phase 2* the marks are fading but never quite gone.
- A **scar from the mines** somewhere visible — shoulder, forearm; not advertised, not hidden.
- **An iron shackle on her ankle**, engraved with **the king of Allondo's crest**. The mine-mark from Clear Water. She wears it openly, **as defiance** — a deliberate sign that says *look what they have done to me*. It was never struck off when she was released, and she has chosen not to have it struck off since. The shackle clinks against rock and hard floors when she walks. A party will usually hear it before they see it on a patient day.
- **The dog at her side, always.** Grey-muzzled, large, fiercely protective, unfriendly to strangers. He is part of how she reads. (See *The dog*, below.)
- A direct gaze. Does not look away first.
- **Hot-blooded under restraint.** She feels things quickly — anger, grief, loyalty — and most of the time she keeps the feeling on a leash she has practised. Her *unrestrained* episodes are rare and remembered (she attacked Steven, once, on the worst morning of her life). The default Irna is *capable of* fighting and *choosing not to* — which is not the same as not feeling the urge.

## The dog

Irna's dog is present in every scene. He has no name the party hears — she calls him *boy* or nothing. He is grey-muzzled, large enough to be a real obstacle, and he has the temperament of an animal that has been through a long road with one person and intends to finish it with her.

**He is part of the reaction surface, not just décor:**

- He stands between Irna and anyone who approaches.
- He will let the party near her *only after Irna does* — and once she does, he treats them as part of her.
- He notices when Irna is distraught before the party does. He presses against her leg.
- He eats whatever the party shares with him without taking food from anyone's hand who has not earned it.
- He will fight if Irna fights. He will not start a fight she has not.

A party that earns the dog's tolerance has earned it twice over. A party that does not is one they cannot have a quiet conversation with her around.

## At her cot — Phase 1

The party may visit her where she is recovering, in Diederik's treehouse or a quiet room nearby. Three things stand out:

- **The fungus is visible on her skin** — crystalline, retreating once the cure begins, never quite gone in the visible Phase-1 scenes. Her eyes are filmed.
- **The iron shackle on her ankle**, visible below the cloak. The king of Allondo's crest is worked into the band. A party that recognises a mine-mark recognises it immediately.
- **The dog is on the floor at the foot of the cot.** He has not eaten properly since they arrived. He will not leave.
- **The letter to Father Elliot** is folded in her cloak pocket, sealed in Rhianna's wax. Visible to anyone who looks for it (calming the dog is harder than finding the letter).

She does not speak in this phase. The DM should resist filling the silence; the *not speaking* is part of what the scene is doing.

## On the road with her — Phase 2

Most of Irna's scenes happen here — campfires, walking the trail, sleeping in inns, sitting on a wagon's back-board. She is not a guest. She carries her own pack. She does her share of the watch. She picks fights with party members who annoy her in ways she finds productive, and lets them off when they relent.

She is **most herself at the campfire**. The road wears down most travellers; it loosens her. The stories come out at night.

Three things mark the experience of travelling with her:

- **The dog** is the constant — wherever she sleeps, he sleeps. Wherever she walks, he walks. He gets in everyone's way until he learns who is family.
- She **carries her axe across her shoulder, not at her belt**. Farm habit; she'd rather have it ready than tidy.
- She **stops at every farm they pass** for ten minutes, no longer. Talks to whoever is working. Asks one or two questions about the soil, the weather, the year. Walks on. She does not explain why she does this.

## Stories she tells

When the relevant topic comes up and she trusts the listener.

### When the topic is her father, and somebody has just repeated the Scarlet Vale myth — *what actually happened*

> *"They say my father killed the overseas leader by punching him with an immeasurably strong arm. Both halves are wrong, and I want to take a torch to every singer who tells it that way. I'm not going to. The arm is ironwood — Rhianna fitted it after the mill took the original — and what makes it work is that it channels a telekinesis that runs in our line. It isn't extraordinary on its own. And he didn't punch anyone. He killed Thorgal through a scrying mirror, with the **phantom** of the arm he didn't yet have. The mirror reached into his head and found a missing arm that was still in his head, and he reached back. That's the story. The capital can't sit with it. It needs heroes. My father gets none of his actual life back because of it. I —"* *(She doesn't finish that one.)*

### When the topic is the mill — *the door Felix found*

> *"Felix found the way in. The mill was sealed; everyone had given up. He spotted a low slot at ground level — where whole ironwood trees used to feed in toward the cutters, when the mill was running. Father went first. Father always went first. The cutting machine was still inside, and it was still working enough. They came out one short. Father has not said exactly what happened in there. I don't think he remembers all of it. Felix and I have not had our conversation about it yet. He was kind to me when I was a child; he can be kind to me now and we can still have it. When I'm ready."*

### When the topic is attacking Steven — *the morning they came*

> *"They came to take Ron. Two knights and the second prince. I knew, the way you know, that they were the same people who had broken my mother's nights and my father's days for years. The day before, I would have stood there. I had been standing for months — for the way my mother cried, for my father's face at the table, for what I knew was coming. That morning I didn't. I flung myself at him. I did not know what I thought I could do. I did not stop them. I hurt him enough to turn it into a political incident, which is how I got the mine. I do not regret hitting him. I regret not finding a better way. I had been finding better ways for so long, and on the one morning when it counted —"* *(She breathes out.)* *"I didn't."*

### When the topic is Clear Water — *what the iron told her*

> *"The mines were running harder than they had told us they would. The grades they wanted, the pace they kept, the bodies they used — somebody needed iron in a hurry. I figured it out from the orders that came down to the pit. Hannes is preparing for a second war. The court hasn't said so. The mines knew before the court would say so. The iron I dug went into a war chest. The same war chest that owns my brother. I think about that every time I hear a hammer ring. I'm not going to do anything about it tonight."*

### When the topic is her mother — *the nights she heard her crying* (trust-gated)

> *"In the last year before they came for Ron, my mother cried in the night. Never loudly. I asked, in the morning. She would say the wrong thing and change the subject — the kind of wrong thing that told me not to ask again. I didn't. I listened instead, for months. I wanted to break things and find out who had done this to her. I didn't do that either. I never got an explanation. I think she heard me listening, eventually. We never spoke about it. Some things we just both knew."*

### When the topic is Luchebert, or what she's heading toward — *the path she lost*

> *"I was looking for a small vale that lets through to the monastery, past the downed city in the hills. I couldn't find the entrance. I'd been told the route badly, or I read it badly. I ended up by a lake instead of in the vale, and the lake was sick with something I didn't recognise. The dog brought me to Elmsfield. He found the road. I didn't."*

### When the topic is the shackle — *why she still wears it*

> *"It's a mine-mark. The king's crest is on it. The smiths make them with a ring on the side — not because we were ever chained down, but because we **could** be. That's what the ring is for. It's the threat. You're crown property, you work where the crown puts you, and the day you stop working they bind you to the wall. I never had a chain on. I didn't need one; the shackle was enough. I wear it now so people see it. So they ask. So I get to tell them — to anyone who will listen — what was done to me, and to the people I love, under the king's crest. Any smith can strike it off in an hour. I haven't asked one to. When the king explains it out loud, or when I decide I have made the point, it comes off. Not before."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — sets her feet. Hands open at her sides — not fists yet; that comes one step further. Voice gets shorter. The dog comes closer.
- **When distraught** — goes silent. Looks past the speaker. The dog presses against her leg before the party notices anything is wrong. She'll sit down where she is and not move until she chooses to.
- **When happy** — laughs from the chest, broad and unguarded. Slaps her thigh once. The dog wags. She'll tease whoever made her laugh; the teasing is affectionate and may shade into a free brawl-lesson.

## If the party does something — what she does

| If the party... | She responds by... |
|---|---|
| Doubts the Scarlet Vale myth about John | Lights up. Tells the truth in detail. Will return to the topic, unprompted, on the next campfire. |
| Repeats the Scarlet Vale myth approvingly | Visibly cringes. *"That isn't what happened."* Will correct if pressed; will change the subject if not. |
| Threatens her father, her brother, or **Ellie** | The fight starts. She does not warn first. The dog joins. |
| Tries to keep her out of a fight to "protect her" | Gets in anyway. Lets them know afterwards that it was patronising and she does not need it. |
| Asks her to brawl-teach them seriously | Says yes; the lesson begins immediately, without warning. They will end up on the ground. They will not forget. |
| Tries to leave her behind at Luchebert without seeing her arc through | Does not stop them. Looks at them in a way they remember. |
| Brings her information about Ron | Stops everything. Asks them to tell her again, slowly. Will plan around it. |
| Brings her the chance to face Steven | Considers carefully. *"I'd like to."* Wants the meeting on her terms; will not ambush him. |
| Hurts her dog | The party member who did it is no longer welcome around her. Recovery from this is long, and is by their work, not hers. |
| Suggests she should let go of her grievance with the crown | *"You're new here."* Stops talking about it. Does not bring it up again with that party member. |
| Refuses to share food with the dog | She notices. The dog notices. The party member is now eating alone. |
| Offers to find a smith to strike the shackle off | Thanks them. Declines for now. *"When the king has explained it to me, I'll be ready."* May accept later if the right moment arrives. |
| Tries to remove the shackle without her consent (hauling her to a smith, cutting it off in her sleep) | She stops them firmly. *"That's mine now. I decide when it comes off."* A party member who pushes this breaks standing with her badly. |
| Recognises the king's crest on the shackle | She notices who noticed. If asked, she tells the *why she still wears it* story. If not asked, she files the fact that the party can read what they are looking at. |

## What she can teach

For students who are willing to learn the way she learned — by doing, with bruises:

- **Brawl** — by *free lesson*. Tease her too much and the lesson begins immediately, without warning. Mass, will, low stance, where guard drops. The lesson lands; the student remembers.
- **Axes** — the farm version, not the soldier version. Practical, strong, not theatrical.
- **Charge** — the Tusk Clan forward attack. Specific to boar-folk physiology, but the principles transfer.
- **Digging** — faster and more efficient than most. Mine-trained.
- **Smell-tracking** — Tusk Clan nose. What to read into and what to dismiss.
- **Reading whether someone is being straight** — by smell, by stance, by the small pauses. She picked this up at Clear Water. It has saved her since.

## What she will not say

- **The exact shape of her mother's nights** beyond *"she cried."* Some of what she heard she has not yet shared with anyone, including herself.
- **What she would do to Hannes if she were in a room with him alone.** She has the answer. She will not say it aloud.
- **The exact terms of her father's old promise to Hannes.** She does not know them; she suspects more than she can prove and will not speculate.
- **How frightened she was in the mines.** The Tusk Clan does not display fear easily; she does not display it at all. Asking does not help.

## DM notes

- Irna is a **companion NPC**, not a destination — the *defined-home* goal adapts here to **wherever the party is** once she's recovered. Her Phase-1 location is her cot at Diederik's; from Phase 2 onward she is in motion with the party.
- **Phase 1 is non-verbal.** She cannot speak until the cure takes. The dog speaks for her in this phase. The party's actions toward her while she is silent are remembered later.
- The **dog is a recurring physical motif**, present in every scene. He functions as part of her reaction surface (see *The dog* and *If the party does something*). Treat him the way Felix's tail-rings work — always-present, generative, with his own minor agency.
- **Play her hot-blooded but restrained.** Irna feels everything intensely — quick to anger, quick to loyalty, quick to grief. The discipline is hers; it is not the absence of feeling. Most provocations get her layered response (*sets her feet, voice shorter, dog closer*) before they get an actual fight; the layers are visible if a party watches for them. The Steven attack was an exception, not a baseline. A DM playing her purely calm misses her register; a DM playing her as a brawler at every provocation misses her judgment. The interest is in the gap between *what she would like to do* and *what she chooses to do*, scene by scene.
- The **shackle is a second recurring physical motif**, audible as well as visible — it clinks when she walks. It is a **mine-mark from Clear Water** — a plain iron band engraved with the king of Allondo's crest, with a ring on the side. The ring is there as a **threat, not an active restraint**: most convicts worked unchained, because the mine itself was the prison and the shackle was the standing warning — *you are crown property, and you can be bound here any moment we decide*. Irna was never actually chained. There is nothing magical about it — any competent smith could strike it off in an hour. **She keeps it on as defiance**: a deliberate public sign that says *look what they have done to me*. People see it; people ask; she tells; the story spreads. Every time she is seen in a new place, the crown is on trial in front of her without anyone in the crown being there to defend it. The shackle comes off when the king explains it out loud — or when Irna decides she has made the point.
- She is **the only path to Ron Bean** (see `doc/npcs/ron_bean.md`). The Bean family arc opens through her voice.
- She is **the actual subject of the prophecy** that produced the misread "valiant spirit" proclamation. She does not yet know. Father Elliot is one of the few people who could tell her; Felicia at the shrine is another. Her arrival at Luchebert is the inflection point for that arc.
- Her **stop-at-every-farm habit** (Phase 2) is left intentionally unexplained in-file — it is a chance for a DM to invent a small reason at the table that fits the moment, or to leave it as a tic the party never fully understands.
- Lore cross-references: the prophecy and proclamation in `doc/npcs/father_elliot.md` and `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"Irna Bean"; her father's actual story in `doc/npcs/john_bean.md` and `doc/npcs/thorgal.md`; the iron-demand context in `doc/npcs/king_hannes.md`; her potential as a spirit-shield in `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology."

## Role as a potential spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a true spirit-shield is a being that reads to chaotic spirits as mighty enough to make them back off — a mage, shaman, druid, priest, great spirit, or comparable. **The prophecy that produced the "valiant spirit" proclamation is about Irna, not Ron.** She is the one with the potential, very likely through the combination of her Tusk Clan lineage's strong will and training she has not yet received.

The interpreters misread it. *"Valiant spirit"* was translated into martial imagery — a warrior, a protector, military might — and the conclusion drawn was that the prophecy concerned a Bean *son* destined for battle. Ron was enlisted on that reading. It is not what the prophecy meant.

Irna's forced exile to Luchebert, arranged by Rhianna after the Steven attack, is — by accident or providence — delivering her to the place where her actual potential could be recognised and trained: Felicia's shrine, Father Elliot's order, and the hill country whose quiet spiritual ecology is the densest concentration on the continent. Whether Elliot knows this about her, whether Rhianna suspects it, and whether Irna herself can survive long enough to find out, are all live questions.

- Daughter of **John Bean**; older sister of **Ron**. Tusk Clan of the Boar folk — small dispersed clan, kin across several nearby towns (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Clans and their shape").

Steven has since, privately, gone looking for her to apologise once rumours of her disappearance reached Scarlet Vale (see `doc/npcs/steven.md` §"Seeking Irna out"). - Expected at **Luchebert** by **Father Elliot**.
- Carrying Rhianna's **letter** to Elliot about the misread proclamation concerning Ron.
- Accompanied by a **loyal grey-muzzled dog** (see *The dog* above).
