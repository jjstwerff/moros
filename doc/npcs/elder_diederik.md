# Elder Diederik

- **Role:** Elder of Elmsfield; exiled druid; the man who knows the tree
- **Species:** Owl
- **Location:** Elmsfield (originally from Steadington, in Brumal) — lives in his own **tree house** in the town
- **Status:** Alive; excommunicated from the druidic order; ecological refugee

## Summary

The current leader of Elmsfield. Elmsfield is not part of Allondo — the Great Swamp separates the town from the kingdom — so "elder" here is a real political role, not a ceremonial one. The town runs on his authority and the informal consent of its residents; there is no crown, no writ, no royal court reaching across the swamp. Diederik is what passes for government.

Owl, druid-trained, and excommunicated from his order — the offence was an ill-advised journey across the ocean. He flew there with a friend and came back by merchant ship, and the druids did not forgive him either the going or the returning.

He is also a refugee, whether anyone calls him that or not. He comes from **Steadington**, the village in Brumal that has since been overtaken by the advancing desert. He saw the ecological collapse of his own homeland from the inside before it finished, and he carries the knowledge of what it looks like when a country begins to die. That, as much as the ironwood, is why he is in Elmsfield now: the tree brought him, and Brumal pushed him.

### What he saw overseas

His "ill-advised journey" across the ocean — the one his druidic order excommunicated him for — took him to the overseas continent itself. He went with a friend, saw what almost no one on this side of the sea has seen, and came back. He knows first-hand what the overseas mages deal with: **lands never properly cultivated, a tradition cut off from druidic partnership, and a population that could not feed itself.** He understands, from having been there, why the Overseas War was an act of hunger rather than conquest — and why the continent's mages (including Thorgal, before his death) felt cornered into it. He also knows why the druids hate them, because he was raised in the tradition that does the hating, and he has thought hard about whether the hatred was right.

He also went overseas for a specific second reason, one almost no one here knows about: to confirm that the overseas continent still has its **male ironwood trees** (see `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Ironwood itself"). It does. This continent has only females — the ancient mages felled every male ironwood here for their puppet work, and the surviving females, Elmsfield's great tree among them, cannot bear fruit without overseas pollen. Diederik saw the male trees with his own eyes. The journey was reconnaissance. That finding has shaped everything he has done since.

### His proposal — if the party asks

If the party comes to Diederik asking how ironwood could actually be *saved*, he has an answer worked out. It is not elegant. It requires an expedition that most people on either continent would call impossible. It runs roughly like this:

1. **Go overseas** and find young male ironwood sprouts — not full trees, which are too large to move and resist uprooting besides; sprouts are hard enough.
2. **Dig the sprouts up carefully.** Even young ironwood resists tools; this is the harder part of the work than the travel itself.
3. **Bring the sprouts back** to this continent alive — water, soil, whatever it takes to keep them rooted through the crossing.
4. **Plant them close to Elmsfield's great female tree.** Close enough for the prevailing winds to eventually carry pollen across the gap. A grove just outside the town would do.
5. **Wait two years.** The sprouts need to settle, survive, and mature enough to produce pollen.
6. **A storm will do the rest.** When the mature males release pollen and the wind is right, a storm will blow it toward the female, and the fertilisation happens without anyone having to hand-carry it.

The two-year wait is what makes this a *setting-scale* project rather than a one-session fix. The *expedition* is what makes it a late-campaign undertaking. And the fruiting, when it finally comes, will be the Elmsfield crisis the party has already been warned about (heavy fruit, noxious gases, the Inn closes for a season — see `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Fruiting is not easy"). Diederik has thought all of that through. He will go through with it anyway, because the alternative is the slow end of ironwood on this continent.

If the party volunteers to help, Diederik is the man who knows what needs to be done — and he is old enough now that someone younger doing the sprout-digging while he advises would be genuinely useful.

### Why Elmsfield specifically

His settling in Elmsfield reads, from outside, as "he came here because the tree is ironwood." That is true, but incomplete. He came here because the tree is a **sterile female ironwood**, one of the last of its kind on this continent, and he chose to stand next to it while the question of whether cross-pollination with overseas could ever be arranged remained unanswered. If ironwood is to survive as a living material rather than a depleting stock of ancient timber, the Elmsfield tree and its overseas counterparts must eventually meet. Diederik is, in his way, the guardian of that possibility.

None of this makes him a friend of the overseas mages. But he is, uniquely, a druid who *could* in principle be reached — by someone overseas looking for a reconciliation, or by someone here trying to engineer one. He is the single likeliest hinge the campaign has for a non-military resolution to the coming food-crisis war. He is also the single druid most visible to anyone from overseas who might come looking.

He has basic knowledge of ironwood, and the ironwood tree at the heart of Elmsfield is a substantial part of why he came here. The next continent has more trees like that; this one has very few. He stays because of the tree, and because the border town is where Brumal's later refugees will arrive.

His background runs through two formed disciplines, **druidic and ascetic**. He was raised a farmer; he trained as a druid, deeply (druid is his advanced specialisation); and he has practised **ascetic** discipline alongside it — the kind of quiet contemplative work that keeps a druid's mind legible to itself when the rest of life gets noisy. He is also physically capable: **claw** work of his folk (owls) and **clan** working (a bound connection to a broader spirit-community he has not entirely renounced despite the excommunication). He walks with a staff.

He means to help anyone he can with what he knows — of ironwood, of natural medicine, of old workings, of what a dying land feels like — but too many people in the region are still too resentful from the last war for him to reach them freely. In Session 1 of the campaign he is the group's first contact: he has the standing to calm a panicked crowd, the druidic training to recognise that **Irna**'s affliction is fungal (not contagious), and the knowledge to compound her cure if the group can bring him the source material.

### The tree house

Diederik lives in **his own tree house** in Elmsfield. It is not the great ironwood at the town's centre — that one belongs to Bridget's Inn and to the town's shared memory, and is on a different scale entirely — but it is still a structure worth looking at. Dwarfed by the main tree, not diminished by the comparison: a druid's dwelling, grown and shaped rather than merely built, reaching up through a substantial living tree with rooms shaped into the structure rather than cut into it. It is a sight to behold in its own right, and it is also an implicit statement — a druid-crafted home, made the way his tradition makes things, standing quietly in the same town as an ironwood-era relic his tradition could not make today and does not fully understand. Two generations of tree-craft, side by side, with a substantial difference in scale between them.

Any visit to Diederik happens here. Climbing up to meet him is itself a small scene.

## Role as spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a druid of genuine standing reads to chaotic spirits as a mighty-spirit-adjacent presence, and those spirits will back off from the area that druid holds. Diederik is that druid for Elmsfield. He is why the town, sitting on the eastern frontier with the blasted lands and the forbidden woods within reach, is a stable place to live. His excommunication is a *political* status within the druidic order, not a metaphysical one — the chaotic spirits around Elmsfield do not care what his old order formally says about him; they respond to what he actually is.

Elmsfield's inhabitants have no idea this is the work he is doing for them. If Diederik died, left, or was driven out, the town would not immediately fall — but within a year or two the quiet protection would fail, and the chaotic-spirit pressure from the nearby hazards would begin to show. The ironwood tree may provide some secondary cover; Diederik on his own is doing most of the work.

## Relationships

- Ex-member of the druidic order; excommunicated.
- Travelled to the next continent once, with a friend, illicitly.
- Stays in Elmsfield for the tree — the same ironwood that **Bridget**'s Inn is built inside, and the same material as **John Bean**'s arm.
- Keeps **Irna Bean** alive while the party seeks her cure.
