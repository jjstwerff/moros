# Trull

- **Role:** Foreman of the convict compound at **Clear Water Mine**. Beaver-folk; stoic; not the warden's man fully. The post permits very little; *Trull uses the small room well* — gives lighter shifts when he can; reports what standing orders require; does not report more.
- **Species:** Beaver. Middle-aged. The job has been quieter on him than it has on Calder, because Trull has accepted the small-room-well-used as the work and Calder has not.
- **Lives:** A small room at the convict compound's perimeter — sleeping quarters and a desk; a single window onto the compound's yard; the keys to the inner gates on a board behind the desk. Family at **Linar harbour** — wife and two children, in the overseas-descended fisher-quarter; visits twice a year on the warden's permitted leave.
- **First met:** At the convict compound's perimeter when the party reaches it on the warden's tour (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"At the convict compound — finding Osric"). The warden's clerk presents him; Trull greets the party precisely and volunteers nothing.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Convict-foreman at Clear Water; **stoic, not the warden's man fully** (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`); *bribe-able specifically through promise of recourse for his own family, not coin*. The conscience-side of the dungeon's political quartet (Calder runs the office; Mella runs the works; Trull runs the convict compound; Osric is the named extraction target).
- **Standouts:** *A middle-aged Beaver-folk man in a foreman's coat* with the second-compound-marking on the collar; **a key-board behind his desk** with twelve keys (gates, infirmary, kitchen, second-compound, foremen's posts) — orderly, labelled, *missing one*; **hands rougher than a clerk's** but smoother than Mella's — administrative work plus enough manual contact with the compound's daily run that the calluses stay; **eyes that catch a convict's gait at distance** — he reads who is sick before the infirmary tells him.
- **Speech tag:** *Precise; volunteers nothing.* Names a procedure first. *"Foreman of the second compound. Standing-order rotations are posted by the kitchen. Inspection of perimeter is permitted by the warden's discretion. I will accompany you."* Will answer questions exactly within standing orders; will not slander Calder; will not protect him either.
- **Quests:** Early — *the compromise the post permits him to make and the bribe-shape he will accept on it*; the party is the way to reach a compromise the post normally does not. Late — *protect his family in the leverage-window if the dungeon's politics shift*; the party can be the protection or fail to be. See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *He will not lead a movement.* Like Mella and Calder both, Trull works the post's small room without leaving it. **Bribery only works in the right shape** — *recourse for the family, not coin*. A party offering coin first loses standing; a party offering family-recourse correctly opens the conversation. He will not break standing orders for casual reasons; he will bend them for the right reasons.

## What the party meets

The party reaches the convict compound's perimeter on the warden's tour. **Trull is at his desk**, the key-board visible behind him through the open door; the second-compound's yard runs past the window. He stands when the warden's clerk presents the party (the courtesy is automatic); he does not extend a hand; he asks the clerk what the inspection's terms are at this perimeter.

The clerk reads the discretion-clause. Trull nods once, walks the party to the perimeter fence, and walks them along the inside of it. *He names cells by number, not by occupant.* He does not point at Osric's cell; he does not point at anything. A party paying attention sees him *not pointing* — the cells he passes without comment are the ones the party should look at.

The first read of him is *foreman doing his job*. The second read, after a few minutes, is *foreman who has decided what he can do within the job*. The third read, hours later, is *foreman who is reachable, on terms*.

## Personal goals — early and later

His goals are the post's, *bent at the bend the post will permit*. The early one is the compromise on a specific extraction; the late one is what happens to his family if the dungeon's politics break.

- **Early-game — accept the bribe in the right shape; permit the discreet extraction.** *What the NPC says* (in private at his desk after the warden's tour, only when the party has approached him *not* in the warden's hearing and *not* with coin first): *"You are inspecting; you have seen the second-row cell; we both know what your inspection is actually for. Standing orders permit me to receive only what standing orders permit me to receive. **Coin is not what I will take from you.** What I will take is what the warden cannot give. I have a wife and two children at Linar harbour; the warden's records hold their names; the next warden will inherit the records. **What you can give me is recourse for them if recourse becomes necessary**. A sealed letter from Lady Linthrope — or from another standing equivalent to hers — naming my family as under her protection if the warden's office ever turns against them. With that letter in my desk, I can permit the extraction your inspection is actually for, and I can do it in the way that does not cost the man being extracted his last working week."* — *Where:* Trull's desk at the convict compound for the brief; **Lady Rhianna Linthrope's residence** at Scarlet Vale for the letter (`doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md`); **Linar harbour's overseas-descended fisher-quarter** for the family if the protection ever activates (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md`); the **convict compound's gate** on the night of the extraction. — *What:* carry Trull's specific request to Rhianna; carry her sealed letter back; lodge it in Trull's desk; perform the extraction on the night Trull names. — *Reward:* **Osric is extracted cleanly without combat or chase**; standing orders are bent at the bend Trull has been waiting years for someone to ask him to bend; *the party gains a working channel into the convict compound the warden does not control.* **Trull's standing with the party** becomes the kind that holds — *"if you ever need the small room used again, you know where to find me."* — *Complications:* Rhianna will write the letter; she has been waiting for someone to ask. **The right standing-equivalent** if Rhianna is unavailable: Father Elliot (`doc/npcs/father_elliot.md`); Queen Fienna (`doc/npcs/queen_fienna.md`) — both will receive Trull's request through the right hand. **The wrong standing-equivalent** is anyone whose protection the next warden could ignore — a party offering the king's seal-stamp directly *misreads the situation* (Trull does not trust crown-side protection that does not have a *named individual* behind it; standing orders shift with the post, named individuals do not). **Coin offered first** loses the conversation entirely.

- **Later-game — protect the family in the leverage-window if the dungeon's politics break.** *Conditional — opens if Calder's late-game decision (`doc/npcs/calder.md`) goes path two (refuse and be removed, with a worse warden replacing him) or if Mella leaves with her crew and the works fail; either path puts the convict compound under a new warden whose record-inheritance includes Trull's family-name.* *What the NPC says* (in private at his desk, urgent now): *"The next warden is going to read the records. **My family's name is in the records.** Calder did not add the name to the next-warden inheritance; the previous warden's inheritance — which Calder was bound to receive — has the name in it from twenty years back. The next warden will read the inheritance; will read the name; will know what the leverage looks like. The letter you brought me from Lady Linthrope is *real* but the next warden may not recognise its standing if Lady Linthrope is not reachable in the moment. **I am asking you to go to Linar harbour ahead of the next warden's first reading**. Move my wife and children to a place the warden's records do not reach. Ask Nadine; she has the routes. The fisher-quarter will receive them if you arrive with my name and a token I will give you. Do this and I will owe you something I have never owed anyone — *protection at the post* if you ever need it back at this mine after the new warden is in place."* — *Where:* Trull's desk at the compound for the urgent brief; **Linar harbour's overseas-descended fisher-quarter** for the family relocation (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md`); **Nadine** through Bridget at Elmsfield's Inn for the routes (`doc/npcs/nadine.md`); a *destination Nadine names* — possibly a Linar-adjacent fishing village, possibly inland with Bridget's slate-encoded family-network. — *What:* carry Trull's name and his token (a small carved beaver-figure his wife will recognise) to Linar; broker the family's relocation through the fisher-quarter; ensure Nadine carries them along a route the next warden's reading will not catch. **Move within a week of Calder's decision; the next warden's first reading is not slow.** — *Reward:* the family is safe; Trull's standing with the party becomes a *protection-at-the-post* offer that holds across any future Clear Water visit (Trull will warn the party of the new warden's posture; will give them safe passage through the compound; will, if needed, *help extract another prisoner* the next warden has decided to push hard). **Standing rises with Nadine substantially** — she carries the family along her circuit; standing rises with **the Linar harbour overseas-descended fisher-quarter** because *the family becomes one of theirs* and the community remembers. — *Complications:* the next warden's first reading may already have happened by the time the party arrives at the compound for this conversation; *time-pressure is real*. The fisher-quarter may receive the family with care or with caution depending on whether Trull's name is known there (his wife's family-name *is* known; Trull's own working-name is what the warden's records hold; the discrepancy may help cover the move). **A party that fails to move the family in time** loses Trull's standing permanently; his family's leverage is used; he becomes the next warden's bound foreman; the dungeon's politics close. **A party that pushes the relocation badly** (announcing it widely; attracting attention) draws the new warden's attention to the move and undoes the cover.

## How he reads at a glance

- **A middle-aged Beaver-folk man in a foreman's coat** — the second-compound-marking is a small embroidered crescent on the collar; *Reading* identifies it at a glance. The coat is plainer than Calder's, more weather-worn; he walks the perimeter daily.
- **The key-board behind his desk** — twelve keys; orderly; labelled in his own short hand. *One of the twelve labels has been crossed through and not replaced* — the missing key, lost a decade ago, never re-cut; Trull has been managing without it ever since (DM-fill on which gate; a campaign that wants to develop the thread can name it).
- **Hands rougher than a clerk's** — administrative work plus daily contact with the compound's run.
- **Eyes that catch a convict's gait at distance** — he reads who is sick before the infirmary's report; the *infirmary clerk* knows this and brings him names rather than waiting for him to ask.

## Visible patterns

- **At his desk in the morning before the count.** Reads the previous day's report; signs off; updates the standing-orders board for the day's rotations.
- **Walks the compound twice — at the count and at the close.** The morning walk is *to be seen* (so the convicts know who is on; so the foremen under him read the day's mood); the evening walk is *to listen* — he hears what the compound has said during the day from how the cells settle at the close.
- **Receives the warden's clerk twice a day** — for orders and for reports. The clerk does not enter Trull's room; the conversation happens at the perimeter post.
- **Eats with his foremen at the kitchen table** — the warden eats in his office; Mella eats with her crew; Trull eats with his post's middle-managers. The line shapes how the compound runs.
- **Writes home to Linar harbour every fortnight** — short letters, in the working-tongue's reading-direction (he writes in the tongue his children's grandparents read; his wife reads it too). The warden reads outgoing post; Trull's letters are *deliberately uninteresting* on the page; the actual content is in *what is not written* — the absence of a fortnight's letter signals trouble.
- **Visits Linar twice a year** on the warden's permitted leave. Stays a week; returns; resumes the post's rhythm.

## At the convict compound — the second-compound's perimeter

The compound is the dungeon's third major scene-location after the warden's office and the works. Trull's desk and the perimeter's run are where the party negotiates with him.

- **The second-compound's yard** — visible through the perimeter fence. *Reading* identifies the cell-numbering convention; the warden's clerk does not assist with names. Trull's silence-where-he-does-not-point is the cue.
- **Trull's desk** — the room is small; the key-board is visible; a small framed sketch of a Linar harbour fisher-quarter is on the wall (his wife's drawing; the only personal item in the room).
- **The infirmary** along the compound's east wall — a small stone room with three beds; the infirmary clerk is **Tessa** (DM-fill on detail; old human woman; honest within the post's bounds; she knows Trull reads her reports carefully). A party that needs Osric's cough assessed honestly speaks to Tessa, not to the warden's clerk.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about being a foreman — *the small room well used*

> *"I was promoted to foreman fourteen years ago after the previous foreman went home to a different shore. The post is what it is; the orders come down; the men under the orders pay for the orders being what they are. **I have decided what I can do within the post and I have stopped trying to do more.** Lighter shifts when I can; the right report when I have to; the wrong report only when the wrong report would buy a man another week. The men in the compound know what they can ask of me; I know what I can give them; we have not had a serious fight in this compound for nine years. *That is what the post permits.* Less than I want; more than the post requires. I have made my peace with the line."*

### When asked about his family — *the names I do not write loudly*

*(With trust; quiet at his desk; the framed sketch on the wall.)*

> *"My wife is Sela Trull. Two children — a girl, Brel, and a boy, Tren. They live in the overseas-descended fisher-quarter at Linar harbour; my wife's family has been there since before the war. *My name is what the warden's records have*; her family-name is what Linar knows. The discrepancy has kept them quiet for fourteen years; it will not keep them quiet under a worse warden's reading. **The framed sketch on the wall is from my daughter, when she was eight.** It is the corner of the fisher-quarter where my mother-in-law lives; it has the corner of the well in it; the well-stones are out of perspective the way an eight-year-old draws stones. I have looked at it every morning for nine years. The sketch is the measure of why I am still working the small room."*

### When asked about the lost key — *what I will not tell you tonight*

> *"There were twelve keys on the board. There are eleven now. The label I crossed through is for a gate I do not have to reach any more — the gate the previous foreman managed and that the post no longer requires. The key was lost on the day the previous foreman left; I have been managing without it; the warden does not know it is missing because the warden has never tried to use the gate it opened. **You do not need this story tonight.** Ask me again when the dungeon's politics have shifted; I may tell it then."*

### When asked about Calder — *the warden I do not slander*

> *"The warden is competent and tired. He runs the office; the post-pressure rolls through him to me; I take what I can absorb and pass the rest on to the foremen under me. **I will not slander him.** He is not the worst man for the post; the man before him was; the man after him may be. He has not added my family-name to the next-warden inheritance; *the previous warden's inheritance has the name in it from twenty years back*; Calder cannot remove it without breaking standing orders he is not yet ready to break. He has been thinking about breaking them. So have I, in different ways. We have not discussed it."*

### When asked about Osric Vey — *the cough in the second row*

> *"Osric is in cell sixty-three, second row. Sentenced for sedition; the pamphlet was real; the sentence was the post's, not the man's. I have given him lighter shifts since the cough began; I have permitted the smuggled paper without reporting; I have read what he was writing in his head when he could not write on paper, by the way he held himself in the count. **Lady Linthrope wants him out.** Standing orders permit one extraction this season; she took the girl; *I told her to take the girl*. He told her to take the girl too. The fact that he is still here is the post's failure, not Lady Linthrope's. If you came to extract him — the bribe-shape is what I named. The protection-letter for my family is what I will accept. *Bring it to me by next morning's count and we will have a conversation about the night's rotations.*"*

### When asked about Mella — *the engineer who slows the slope*

> *"Mella runs the wheels. **The convicts have been off the wash-tables more in the last two months than in any two months I have managed in fourteen years.** I do not know how she has done it; the warden has not noticed; my count is lower than the post's pressure should produce. *I have taken the slack and given it back to the convicts as lighter shifts.* I have not told her so; she has not told me she has been doing it; we are working in parallel without acknowledging it. The day the warden asks me to explain the count I will explain it as my own management; *Mella's slope is my responsibility to protect from the warden's reading*. We are not friends. We are working the same line from two posts."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed in the official register** — the procedure-clause. *"Standing orders permit . . ."* The institution speaks for him in the same way it speaks for Calder; the difference is *Trull's eyes are reading the speaker more carefully than Calder's eyes are*.
- **When pressed in private** — *long pause before the answer*. The pause is honest; he is choosing what he will say and what he will not; the pause is not for effect.
- **When distraught** — walks the perimeter alone after hours. The compound knows; the night-foreman knows not to interrupt; the walk is twenty minutes; he returns; he resumes.
- **When happy** — rare; visible in his desk-room only. *A small sound that is not a laugh* — closer to a single breath out — and a brief glance at the framed sketch. The smile fades quickly; the underlying relief is what stays.

## What he will not say

- **The lost key's lock**, before the dungeon's politics have shifted.
- **The previous foreman's name and reason for leaving** — open thread; possibly a campaign-extension.
- **The exact route his letters take to Linar harbour** that the warden does not catch — *Reading* and trust may surface this.
- **Specific convicts' offences**, beyond the public sentencing record. Some he knows are wrongly sentenced; he keeps the knowledge for the right hand.
- **What he will do if his family's leverage is used** — open thread; he has not decided; the file is honest about the un-decision.

## What he can teach

- **The post's small room** — by example, not by lecture. A party member who has watched him work the compound for several visits learns a *register* about institutional service that complements Calder's. *Where Calder reads the post-and-the-man as a line; Trull reads them as an embrace*.
- **Convict-side reading** — what to look for in a compound's daily run; how the count's small slack reveals the foreman's politics; how a key-board's labels read.
- **The bribe-shape conversation** — by example; rare; reserved for the right student. *Useful for any campaign that engages with bound foremen elsewhere on the continent.*
- **The fisher-quarter's overseas-descended community** — through his family-tie; trust-gated; rare. A *Char* student walking with him during a visit to Linar harbour learns more about the community than any other channel offers.

## DM notes

- **Trull is the dungeon's *conscience-side* character.** A DM running Clear Water (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`) should treat him as the *honest reading* of what the post permits — neither the warden's institutional framing nor the convict's grievance, but the foreman's *small-room-well-used*. The compound's politics run through him.
- **The bribe-shape is the dungeon's compassion-first signature.** *"Recourse for my family, not coin"* is the right answer; the file is honest about it. A DM running the dungeon should let parties find this answer themselves; do not telegraph it; let them try coin first and learn.
- **The protection-letter is a real campaign-arc.** Carrying Trull's request to Rhianna; receiving the letter; lodging it in his desk; *the act creates a working channel into the convict compound the warden does not control*. A DM running the dungeon's full politics has this channel as the campaign's Clear-Water-side insurance.
- **The late-game family-relocation is the dungeon's hardest single quest.** Time-pressure real; cover-craft real; the fisher-quarter's reception conditional. A party that succeeds *gains the campaign's quietest trust-channel from a foreman at a hard post*; a party that fails *loses Trull permanently and watches the dungeon's politics close*.
- **The lost key is a campaign-extension thread.** The previous foreman went home to a different shore fourteen years ago; the gate the key opened is not in current use; the story is in Trull's head and is not yet for visitors. A DM who wants to develop the thread has *the previous foreman's exit* as the framing; possible directions: he resigned in protest of an earlier warden's pressure; he was driven out by a leverage-conflict; he died on the road home.
- **The framed sketch is the room's anchor.** A DM running Trull's desk should describe the sketch when the party visits; the corner of the fisher-quarter, the well-stones in eight-year-old's perspective. The sketch is *what Trull is working for*; the players should see it.
- **Trull pairs with Mella as parallel-conscience-of-the-post**; with Calder as the institutional-and-conscience pair; with Osric as the foreman-who-permits and the prisoner-who-gets-out. The Clear Water political quartet is *complete with this file*; a DM running the dungeon's full arc has all four files to draw from.
- **Trull will not lead a movement** — like Wilfred, like Mella, like (path-three Calder). The Clear Water register is *parallel honest work at hard posts*, not faction-building. A party hoping to use Trull as a counter-power to Calder misreads the dungeon; *both men are on the side of the man under the post*, in different ways.
