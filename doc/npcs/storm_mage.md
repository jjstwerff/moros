# The Storm Mage

- **Role:** The Lightning Tower's original owner; survivor of both the tower's seizing and a chaos-spirit encounter; the lever that could, in principle, break the Lightning Tower's shackle
- **Species:** Human (presumed); possibly preserved beyond a normal lifespan by his own workings or by chaos-warping, unknown
- **Location:** Living somewhere in the hills — the party will need to look for him
- **Status:** Alive; **of unknown age**; **mind substantially gone**

## Summary

A storm-mage who lived in the Lightning Tower before the old-order mages seized the six towers and moved them to ring the Lake of Tears. He survived that taking and left the tower; he has been living in the hills ever since, the length of "ever since" measured in generations that nobody, including him, can properly count. **Nobody knows his age.** He cannot tell you himself, because he cannot tell you most things.

Something happened to his mind. Either the slow erosion of centuries alone, or an **encounter with a chaos spirit** that warped his thinking beyond repair (see LORE.md §"Why the chaos" and §"highly influenced original spirits" — his condition is consistent with a mage whose mind has been pulled out of true by prolonged chaos-spirit proximity), or some combination of both. He is not dangerous in any ordinary sense. He is simply not *there* the way a lucid person is there. He speaks in fragments. He reacts to weather. He remembers things in an order that is not quite chronological. He is still recognisably himself on some days and not on others.

He is **the Lightning Tower's lever** — or the closest thing to one. The pot-boy's lever worked because he could *walk out* and the tower followed. The Storm Mage cannot walk out as an act of will; he already walked out, long ago, and his connection to the tower persists from the hills where he lives. What moves the Lightning Tower, if anything does, is *him*: restored, reconnected, or simply brought back into contact with the tower in some state that the tower recognises. That is the part the party has to work out.

## What the party can do

Several possible arcs, none simple:

- **Find him.** He lives in the hills; he is not signposted. A local guide (Corné Dunham, someone at Luchebert, or the Hermit Shaman) might know his approximate territory. He moves; he does not stay in one cave.
- **Reach him.** Establishing communication with a broken-minded storm-mage is a patience scene, not an interrogation. A druid or shaman can probably do more useful work than a diplomat can. Rhianna, if ever consulted, would know what questions *not* to ask.
- **Understand what broke him.** If it was a specific chaos spirit (possibly one that the forbidden woods' resident, Tod's full self, has some distant kinship to), knowing that may inform how much healing is possible. If it was the centuries themselves, healing is not the right frame.
- **Restore what can be restored.** Felicia's shrine at Luchebert (with Willow's participation) is one of the few places on the continent where a mind warped by chaos-spirit contact might be gently restored by degrees. Months or years of work. Not a session.
- **Or simply bring him to his tower.** If his conscious will cannot be the lever, his *presence* at the tower in some coherent enough form may be. This is ethically fraught — he cannot give meaningful consent — and the party will have to decide what they owe him against what they owe the larger project.

## Relationships

- The **Lightning Tower**'s original owner and its presumed lever. See `doc/places/geography.md` and CAMPAIGN.md §"The Other Towers."
- A broken witness to the **taking of the towers** centuries ago. If he can be helped to speak coherently, he is one of the only living sources on what actually happened at that event, who the old-order mages were by name, and what was done to his own tower.
- Parallels with **Narissa** (early mage, spirit-fused, long-lived, diary still consulted) and with **Laurent** (survivor of an era nobody else lived through). He is a quieter, smaller instance of the same pattern — a person the centuries left behind in a shape they can no longer clean up.
