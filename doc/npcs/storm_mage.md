# The Storm Mage

- **Role:** The Lightning Tower's original owner; survivor of both the tower's seizing and a chaos-spirit encounter; the lever that could, in principle, break the Lightning Tower's shackle
- **Species:** Human (presumed); possibly preserved beyond a normal lifespan by his own workings or by chaos-warping, unknown
- **Location:** Living somewhere in the hills — the party will need to look for him
- **Status:** Alive; **of unknown age**; **mind substantially gone**

## Summary

A storm-mage who lived in the Lightning Tower before the old-order mages seized the six towers and moved them to ring the Lake of Tears. He survived that taking and left the tower; he has been living in the hills ever since, the length of "ever since" measured in generations that nobody, including him, can properly count. **Nobody knows his age.** He cannot tell you himself, because he cannot tell you most things.

Something happened to his mind. Either the slow erosion of centuries alone, or an **encounter with a chaos spirit** that warped his thinking beyond repair (see LORE.md §"Why the chaos" and §"highly influenced original spirits" — his condition is consistent with a mage whose mind has been pulled out of true by prolonged chaos-spirit proximity), or some combination of both. He is not dangerous in any ordinary sense. He is simply not *there* the way a lucid person is there. He speaks in fragments. He reacts to weather. He remembers things in an order that is not quite chronological. He is still recognisably himself on some days and not on others.

He is **the Lightning Tower's lever** — or the closest thing to one. The pot-boy's lever worked because he could *walk out* and the tower followed. The Storm Mage cannot walk out as an act of will; he already walked out, long ago, and his connection to the tower persists from the hills where he lives. What moves the Lightning Tower, if anything does, is *him*: restored, reconnected, or simply brought back into contact with the tower in some state that the tower recognises. That is the part the party has to work out.

## He comes into towns

The Storm Mage is **not unknown**. He is almost endlessly old, but he still needs to eat — and every few months, or less predictably, he **walks into a town** to trade for food. Usually a smaller settlement in the hills or along the valley edge, sometimes a larger one if his route takes him there.

**Nobody can make much out of his words.** His sentences are fragments and misorderings and stretches of silence. But the towns have figured out he is harmless and that he is *owed* respect — they know who he was, or at least what a practitioner of his age once was — and his visits are handled gently. Shopkeepers listen. Bakers hand him bread. Someone sits with him on a step for a while if he seems to want that.

**He pays in baubles.** Pieces of glass sometimes, of the kind any village child could have. Sometimes a genuinely valuable object — an old mage-worked trinket, a fragment of crystallised working, something that would fetch real coin in a city. He does not seem to distinguish between the two. The towns take what he offers without pressing him on value.

This makes him **findable**. A party that asks around — anywhere in the valley, up to the hill villages, possibly as far as Luchebert or Elmsfield — will eventually find a local who says *yes, the old storm mage comes through now and again.* Intercepting him on a supply walk, or simply **waiting in a town he is known to visit**, is more reliable than searching the hills. The traditional routes still work (Corné, Luchebert's older members, or Gerhald can give approximate hill territory between visits) — but the town-visit path is the gentler one, and it does not require a wilderness hunt.

## What the party can do

Several possible arcs, none simple:

- **Find him.** The easiest way is to **intercept him in a town he's known to visit** (see §"He comes into towns" above) — supplies bring him out of the hills periodically, and locals across the valley recognise him. If a party wants to search for him between visits, Corné Dunham, Luchebert's older members, or Gerhald can give approximate hill territory — but he moves and the search is real work.
- **Reach him.** Establishing communication with a broken-minded storm-mage is a patience scene, not an interrogation. A druid or shaman can probably do more useful work than a diplomat can. Rhianna, if ever consulted, would know what questions *not* to ask.
- **Understand what broke him.** If it was a specific chaos spirit (possibly one that the forbidden woods' resident, Tod's full self, has some distant kinship to), knowing that may inform how much healing is possible. If it was the centuries themselves, healing is not the right frame.
- **Restore what can be restored.** Felicia's shrine at Luchebert (with Willow's participation) is one of the few places on the continent where a mind warped by chaos-spirit contact might be gently restored by degrees. Months or years of work. Not a session.
- **Or simply bring him to his tower.** If his conscious will cannot be the lever, his *presence* at the tower in some coherent enough form may be. This is ethically fraught — he cannot give meaningful consent — and the party will have to decide what they owe him against what they owe the larger project.

## When he enters his tower

If the party gets the Storm Mage to the **Lightning Tower**, something unexpected happens. The moment he crosses into the tower's working space he has **a bout of clarity** — not a slow return of lucidity, but a sudden one, the way a mage's mind *snaps into* a working it knows intimately. He whirls around the tower pulling levers, adjusting workings, opening settings idle for centuries. The party will recognise almost none of it; the point is that he does, and he is fast, and he is *not asking them*.

**The tower comes to life.** The lightning arcs steady. The levitation increases. The tower **lifts higher into the air and begins to travel north**.

It does not last. The flight is short. As the tower reaches the **fringes of the forbidden woods** (see `doc/places/forbidden_woods.md`), the chaos spirit's influence **undoes the working** — the same mechanism that once undid the storm mage himself. The levitation fails. The tower **crashes** into the fringes of the woods.

After the crash the party can follow. The tower is a wreck now, broken open, workings vented. The **Storm Mage is wandering around it** — alive, mostly intact, and **even more confused than before**. His clarity is gone the moment the working was. He is poking the tower from every side, muttering, not placing why he is here or what this object is. The second chaos-spirit encounter has pushed his mind further out of reach than the first did.

## Consequences

This scene resolves and complicates several threads at once:

- **The Lightning Tower's shackle breaks.** The tower has left the ring; one more shackle is off whatever freed-elemental climax the party is working toward.
- **The Lightning Tower is now at the fringes of the forbidden woods.** A crashed mage-era tower at the edge of Tod's territory is a concrete destination — salvageable, explorable, dangerous. It is also the party's **in-world introduction to the forbidden woods as a place worth engaging with**, not just a backdrop.
- **The Storm Mage is in worse shape.** Whatever the party was planning for him afterwards (resettling him, handing him to Luchebert, finding his name) now happens in a harder register. He is in pain; his pain is not legible to him.
- **Tod may notice.** A crashed mage-working at the forbidden woods' edge is the kind of event Tod could wander out to investigate. Whether he arrives during the party's visit, after they leave, or not at all, is a DM beat.

The ethical weight of the scene is real. The party brought a broken-minded mage to his old tower; they got a shackle broken, a wreck, and a man more broken than before. Whether that trade was worth it is part of what Gerhald will want to know when the party reaches him (see `doc/npcs/hermit_shaman.md` §"He wants specifics before he cooperates").

## Relationships

- The **Lightning Tower**'s original owner and its presumed lever. See `doc/places/geography.md` and CAMPAIGN.md §"The Other Towers."
- A broken witness to the **taking of the towers** centuries ago. If he can be helped to speak coherently, he is one of the only living sources on what actually happened at that event, who the old-order mages were by name, and what was done to his own tower.
- Parallels with **Narissa** (early mage, spirit-fused, long-lived, diary still consulted) and with **Laurent** (survivor of an era nobody else lived through). He is a quieter, smaller instance of the same pattern — a person the centuries left behind in a shape they can no longer clean up.
