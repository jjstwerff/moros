# Master Darius

- **Role:** Badger forge-master of World Edge; the quietly fair employer of his factory floor; the material spine of the Leatherman railroad project; the man whose letter means something in three or four places he can name.
- **Species:** Badger. Animal-person; the forge smell is so old in his fur it is part of him.
- **Lives:** **World Edge**, the mining city on the northern coast (volcanic country, World's End region — see `doc/places/geography.md` §"The northern coast (World's End)"). His forge district is the working core of the city; he sleeps in a small house attached to the main forge, eats at the long table the apprentices share, walks to work in less than a minute.
- **First met:** At the forge during working hours, leather apron on, hammer or callipers in hand. Or — through **Remi**'s letter of recommendation if the party has met Remi first (`doc/npcs/remi.md`). Or — through **Queen Fienna**'s standing if the party is carrying a Leatherman letter from Scarlet Vale (`doc/npcs/queen_fienna.md`).

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** World Edge's badger forge-master; quietly fair; the railroad's material spine; the letter that opens doors.
- **Standouts:** *Forge smell so old it is part of his skin.* **Full leather apron, never off during work hours.** Hands forge-stained but precise — the hands of a master who has not lost the eye. Reads ground first, faces second.
- **Speech tag:** Short sentences, plain words. The characteristic *"But why?"* — quietly, the apron moves slightly as he breathes, before he commits to act. Will not perform indignation; will not lead a moral crusade; hands the party concrete help and asks them to use it.
- **Quests:** Early — *carry Remi's letter to one of the three or four places where it means something; deliver a specific commission only his forge can make.* Late — *open the forge to the Brumal-resolution effort (Path 4 trade routes; Spiritual path's material support); meet Queen Fienna for the first time, when the campaign brings them into the same room.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *He stood quietly when the rat expulsion happened.* He gave Remi a letter and asked *"But why?"* and went back to the forge. The party that expects him to lead a crusade against the mayor finds him at his work, doing what his hands can do — not what they want him to do.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/master_darius.md`.*

## What the party meets

The party reaches World Edge — by the inland trade road, by Leatherman letter, by following Remi's path back, or by some other route the campaign opens. The forge district is where the city's heat lives; even a casual visitor can find Darius's forge from three streets away by the smell and the sound.

He is at the anvil, or at the long worktable beyond the cooling vats, or in the small office where the orders are kept. **Full leather apron, sleeves rolled to the elbows, the forge smell as deeply part of him as the apron is.** He looks up when they come in, takes them in without hurry, and finishes whatever motion he was on before he greets them.

If they have come on Remi's letter, his face does not perform recognition; he simply reads the letter, hands it back, and asks what they need. If they have come on a Leatherman letter, the same. If they have come without a letter, he asks them what brought them to his forge specifically — and listens to the answer before he decides what kind of conversation this will be.

## How he reads at a glance

- **Badger in a full leather apron.** Wide-shouldered, heavy in the chest, mid-life by badger reckoning. The apron has been patched twice along the lower edge where sparks have cost it; the patches are clean work, his own.
- **Forge smell.** Old, deep, mostly iron and char with a base of the specific dust that forge-rooms carry. The smell does not leave him when he walks home.
- **Hands forge-stained but precise.** Black under the nails; calluses where calluses go; the precision of a master who has not lost the eye. He picks up small pieces with the same care he picks up large ones.
- **Reads ground first, faces second.** A field-commander habit applied to a forge floor — he has watched the spacing of the apprentices, the heat of the furnaces, the state of the cooling vats before he registers what kind of visitor has arrived.
- **A small notebook in the apron's chest pocket.** Specifications, customer notes, the standing orders. Not for show; he uses it.

## Visible patterns

- **At the forge from before sunrise.** The first hammer-strikes of the morning are his. Apprentices arrive an hour later.
- **Walks the floor every two hours.** Through every room — the main forge, the casting hall, the cooling vats, the office. The walk is a check; nothing on the floor changes without his eye on it within the day.
- **Eats with the apprentices at the long table.** Same food, same hour, same table. Has done so since he was a senior apprentice himself.
- **Reads Leatherman drawings on Saturday mornings.** Sealed packets arrive from Scarlet Vale once or twice a season; he reads them before the floor opens, marks them, files them in the office cabinet only he opens.
- **Goes to the council hearings he is asked to attend.** Does not seek council seats; does not refuse summons. Speaks short when called on; the council learns to bring questions Darius has answers to and not the other way around.

## At the forge — the main hall

The working heart of his district. Three things stand out on entering:

- **The heat.** Even far from the furnaces. The walls hold it; the air is dry and tastes of metal. A visitor unused to forge-heat learns within minutes; an experienced one greets it as familiar.
- **The order.** Tools racked in their places, materials sorted by shape and by alloy, finished work on shelves with their dockets above them. **The order is his work** — he laid it down when he took the forge, and he has been holding it for two decades.
- **The apprentices working without watching the door.** A forge floor where apprentices look up at every visitor is a forge floor whose master is not present in the way Darius is present. His apprentices work; the door is his to read.

## At the office — where the standing orders are

A small room off the main hall, glassed on one wall, the cabinet behind his chair holding the standing orders. The party reaches it only after the floor scene has concluded — Darius does not bring strangers to his cabinet on first meeting.

- **The standing orders.** Filed by customer; the **Leatherman house** is the largest single section. **Railroad components** in their own folder — rails, joinings, bearings, pump-housings. The specifications get more demanding each year.
- **The wall map.** A map of the northern coast with his customers marked — Rosepond, the smaller mining villages, the merchant houses inland, three or four places marked further south whose names the party may recognise (Bridget's badger-clan kin among them).
- **A small chest.** Locked. He does not open it for visitors. (DM-fill: it holds a piece of overseas mage-work he was once asked to repair and has kept while he decides whether the right repair has come to him; or his retired hammer; or his accounting since he took the forge. Open option.)

## Personal goals — early and later

His goals run quiet under the work. He will not lead a movement; he will hand the party tools and ask them to use them.

### Early-game

**Carry Remi's letter to one of the three or four places it means something.** *He says (when the party arrives with Remi's letter, or having met Remi on the road south):* *"My name on the letter will mean something in three or four places I can think of. The river settlements south are one. A forge-master in Salmonswell who owes me a season of materials is another. A merchant house at Linar harbour I have done good work for is a third. Tell me which way the rat is heading. I will tell you which letter does the most for him."*
- *Where:* one of the named places — most often **Salmonswell** (Ingram's orphanage adjacent; the Linar–Salmonswell road north), **Linar harbour** (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md`), or a river settlement south of the forbidden woods (DM-fill on which).
- *What:* carry the letter; vouch for Remi by what the party has seen of him; stay long enough to see Remi placed.
- *Reward:* Remi has work and a place; Darius's standing for life; a forge-master's introductions in the named place become available to the party afterwards.
- *Complications:* the place may have changed since Darius's last visit; Remi's family may have arrived first and the welcome may have been used up; the wrong forge-master may resent the imposition.

**Deliver a specific commission only his forge can make.** *He says (when the party has standing and a courier need that fits — a piece of metalwork the campaign wants for somewhere specific):* *"The piece you are describing — yes, I can make it. It is two weeks of work. The carriage south is the part I cannot manage. If you carry it, I will charge you for the metal and the time and not for the carriage. The customer will not know it was you who brought it. That is part of the price."*
- *Where:* the forge for the order; the destination DM-fill — most often a railroad-related delivery (rail joints, pump fittings, an engine component) to the southern survey teams; sometimes a custom piece for a named NPC (a fitted ironwood limb for **Irna** if **Lady Rhianna** asks; a forge-tool for Hank's farm; a piece for the **Bockthicket silversmith** working with **Penelope**).
- *What:* receive the wrapped piece; carry it without losing it or breaking it; deliver to the right hand at the right place; bring back receipt and any return-message.
- *Reward:* a working customer relationship for Darius the party has facilitated; the customer's standing is now also accessible through Darius's name; the party has carried something the railroad or the Brumal-resolution effort or the Bean-arc needed.
- *Complications:* the carriage is the hard part — the piece is heavy or fragile or both; the route may be watched; the wrong customer may try to redirect the delivery.

### Later-game

**Open the forge for the Brumal-resolution effort.** *He says (when the campaign has committed to one of the resolution paths and the material support is needed):* *"You are working on something that requires more than one forge-master can supply alone, and you have come to me first because the Leatherman house is in it and my forges are the spine of theirs. Tell me what you need. Tell me the timetable. I will tell you what I can do and what I will need to subcontract. The materials will not be a problem. The political cover will be."*
- *Where:* the forge; the Leatherman town-house in Scarlet Vale if the conversation needs the queen's quiet weight; the survey-camps along the Brumal southern border if the work is corridor-related (`doc/claude/BRUMAL_RESOLUTIONS.md` Path 2).
- *What:* commission and deliver components — rail joints if the railroad is the channel, fittings if the spiritual corridor needs metal anchors, custom items the path requires. Coordinate the political cover (**Queen Fienna** runs the back-channel on the Allondan side; Darius runs the work-channel on the World Edge side).
- *Reward:* the resolution path's material requirements are met. Darius's standing in the Leatherman house rises with each successful run; **Queen Fienna**'s late-game advances directly.
- *Complications:* the work is volume; one forge cannot supply everything; subcontractors may not all be discreet; the World Edge mayor may notice the increased Leatherman traffic and ask questions Darius does not want to answer.

**Meet Queen Fienna for the first time.** *Conditional — opens when the campaign brings them into the same room. Neither has sought it; both have been working in parallel for years.* *He says (when the meeting is being arranged through the party):* *"I have not met the queen. I have read her house's drawings; she has read my forge's deliveries. We have known each other through paper for fifteen years. If she comes here, I will receive her at the long table with the apprentices, because that is where I receive everyone. If I am asked to come south to meet her, I will. The choice is hers. Tell her so."*
- *Where:* most likely the forge if Fienna travels to World Edge; the Leatherman town-house or her receiving rooms in Scarlet Vale if Darius travels south. Either is unprecedented.
- *What:* arrange the meeting; carry word both ways; be present without making it a court matter.
- *Reward:* a relationship that has lived on paper for fifteen years becomes a relationship that has lived in a room. The Leatherman channel deepens; both NPCs gain something they have been quietly waiting for.
- *Complications:* the World Edge mayor is not party to the Leatherman politics and will read the meeting as a Crown move; Fienna's court will read a queen visiting a forge district as out-of-form; the meeting must be timed and framed to land cleanly.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the day Remi was expelled — *what I asked, what I gave, what I did not do*

> *"The bull had Remi by the tail when I came in. He told me what was happening; he told me whose order it was. I asked him *but why*. He answered. The answer was a policy answer; the policy was the mayor's; the work I had given Remi was not part of the answer. I gave Remi a letter. The letter said he is capable and honest and that my name on it would mean something in three or four places I could think of. I did not stop the expulsion. I did not call a council hearing. I did not refuse to accept the order. I went back to the forge and I worked the rest of the day and the day after and the day after that. I think about it every time I see a young rat in the river settlements south. I do not pretend the letter was enough. I also do not pretend I was going to stop a bull and a mayor by standing at my forge door. The shape of what I did is what I had."*

### When asked about the Leatherman railroad — *what we are building, slowly*

> *"The Leatherman house has been ordering railroad components from this forge for fifteen years. They send drawings; we make pieces; the pieces ship south. The near-term plan is Raft city. The long-term plan is Fata morgana and Gap city through the desert. I do not know whether either plan will land in my lifetime. I am not the planner. I am the forge that the plan runs through. The drawings get more demanding each year, which tells me the planners are still serious. I am still serious about meeting the demand. We will see who is right."*

### When asked about Queen Fienna — *what we have between us, on paper*

> *"I have not met her. I have read her house's drawings since she was eight; she has read my forge's deliveries since she was fifteen. The things her family signs off on cross her hand before they cross mine. The things I ship south cross hers when they arrive. We have known each other through paper for fifteen years. There is a meeting coming, eventually. Whichever direction it travels — she comes here, I go south — I will receive it the same way I receive everyone. The apron stays on."*

### When asked about the mayor — *what I think and what I do not say*

> *"The mayor is the mayor. He runs the city in the form the city has. The expulsion was his order; the order was lawful; the order was wrong. I have not said the last word aloud at council. I have said the first two. The men who would have liked me to say the third would also have liked me to lead a movement, and I am a forge-master, and a movement is not what my hands do. I keep the work. I keep my apprentices employed and fed. I write letters when I can. The day I am asked to move on the mayor in a way that uses my name and my standing — I will think about it. I have thought about it. I do not yet know."*

### When asked about the apprentices — *who I take, and why*

> *"I take apprentices who can work and who do not need to be entertained. The work is the work; the heat is the heat; the years are real. The apprentice who comes here looking for romance leaves quickly. The apprentice who comes here because their hands have been telling them something stays. I take apprentices from any species, any clan, any background — the bull at the door has been the same bull for a decade and he does not get to read who I take. He gets to be polite and bring them in. I have lost two apprentices to the expulsion order. I have not been asked to replace them yet. I will, when the expulsion eases."*

### When asked about old work — *the piece in the chest* (trust-gated)

> *"I have a piece of overseas mage-work in that chest. I was asked to repair it once, before the war, by a customer whose name I will not give you. The repair the customer wanted was the wrong repair. I declined the work. The customer did not come back. The piece has been with me ever since. I keep it because the right repair may come to me eventually, and because I do not yet trust where it would go if I sent it on. If you ever bring me reason to open the chest, I will open it. Not before."*

### When asked about pre-rift forge correspondence — *the styles I have read about and not seen* (trust-gated; party brings the topic)

> *"My grandfather kept letters from a forge correspondent in the broken lands — pre-rift, from a working-master at a grain-mill on the trade road's skirt. The grandfather's grandfather had trained that working-master, or near enough. The letters describe a stone-cutter sister-tradition to my own — agricultural-side cutting, large blanks for working-pieces. I have not seen the cutter; I would not go into the broken lands. If you do, and you come back with a piece — even a chip from one of the blanks — I will read what I can from the metal. The same letters mention a vineyard family on a ridge whose press-house was tied to the same commission line; a Pair of practitioners commissioning blanks across both sites. I do not know the Pair's name; my grandfather's letters did not. If you find their work and bring me a piece, I will know what I am reading."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — sets down whatever is in his hand, deliberately, on the workbench. *Then* answers. The pause is the answer; the words are courtesy. Does not raise his voice.
- **When distraught** — goes to the furnace. Stands at the heat, hand on the brick, until he has chosen what to say. The apprentices know not to follow him.
- **When happy** — a short specific nod. *"Yes."* Goes back to the work. The smile is in the eyes for a second, no more. A piece resolved at the anvil; an apprentice's first clean weld; a letter answered well.
- **When the *"But why?"* fires** — the apron moves slightly as he breathes; the hand goes to the hammer or the callipers, not to grip them but to touch them; the question follows. He does not raise his voice when he asks it.

## What he can teach

For students who can work and who do not need to be entertained:

- **Forge-master craft.** Years, not months. Iron, steel, the difference between cast and wrought, the heat of each, the hammer of each. Real work.
- **Reading metal.** What an alloy looks like at temperature; what a flaw sounds like; what a finished piece *should* feel like in the hand. Specific and slow.
- **Running a working district.** How the floor stays in order; how the apprentices stay employed; how the orders stay on time. Useful for any party member who will eventually run anything.
- **Letter-writing in his register.** Short, plain, true. Not a glamorous skill. The party member who learns it from him will never write a bad letter again.
- **Knowing when not to lead.** By example, not by lecture. The party watching how he stood when Remi was taken — and how the work continued — learns something about *the shape of what one person can do* that a speech could not teach.

## What he will not say

- **The other forge-masters' names**, beyond the ones he is willing to vouch for in a letter. Reputations are leverage; he does not give them on first asking.
- **What is in the locked chest**, until the right reason arrives.
- **What he thinks of the mayor's full record**, as opposed to the expulsion order specifically. The mayor will be judged by the city; Darius will not write the verdict.
- **What he was offered by the customer of the chest**, before he declined the wrong repair. Names; sums; the shape of the offer. Private.

## DM notes

- **Play him short, not stoic.** Darius is not silent because he is performing strength; he is short because he has decided what is worth saying. The *"But why?"* is the load-bearing line; it is the question he asks when injustice is happening, and he asks it quietly. A DM who plays him as a moralist over-reads the character; a DM who plays him as cold misses the warmth.
- **The forge is the scene.** Most conversations with Darius happen with him at work — at the anvil, at the worktable, walking the floor. The work continues while he speaks. A DM who stops the work to deliver a speech misses the texture; a DM who lets the work continue runs him correctly.
- **The letter has weight.** A Darius-letter to one of his three or four places is a real-in-world introduction. The recipient will receive the bearer with the standing Darius has built. *Use this; do not overuse it.* Three or four named places is the cap; the campaign should not let the letter turn into a universal pass.
- **He will not lead movements.** A DM who lets the party push him into a public stance against the mayor flattens him. The frame of the character is *what one craftsman does, with the hands he has, in the city he is in*. The party can be the movement; Darius will support the work, write the letters, ship the iron. He will not stand at the front.
- **The Fienna meeting is a real beat.** Two people who have known each other through paper for fifteen years sitting in a room is a campaign-grade scene. Earn it. Do not let it happen casually.
- **The locked chest is a DM-fill door.** Use it for whatever the campaign needs — overseas mage-work, retired tools, a personal piece. Keep it closed until the right reason arrives.
- **The forge is the railroad's spine.** If the campaign reaches the railroad project at scale, every piece of rail that gets laid passed through Darius's floor at some point. He is the material reality of a plan most NPCs only discuss.
