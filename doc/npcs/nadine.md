# Nadine

- **Role:** Itinerant Finch trader; public face of a merchant group based out of Raft city; the party's regular window on the larger world. Personal project: Linnet's extraction.
- **Species:** Finch (animal-person). Sharp hearing and flight both active.
- **Lives:** No fixed home — a **circuit** of trade stops across the continent. Raft city is the group's base; Elmsfield is a regular stop; Linar harbour and Salmonswell are in rotation; other towns she passes through on schedule. See `doc/npcs/felix.md` for the itinerant worked example; Nadine's circuit is denser and more regular.
- **First met:** At **Bridget's Inn**, most often. She arrives mid-evening with the dust of the trade road on her clothes, pays Bridget, accepts a drink, and is *already telling the room a story* before the drink has travelled. The party meets her by being in the room when that happens.

## What the party meets

The party is at Bridget's Inn. A small Finch trader comes through the door, calls Bridget by name, drops her pack, and sits down at a table near the hearth. **Within a minute she is talking** — to Bridget, to anyone at a nearby table, to the room at large. A story from the road that somebody-somewhere will find useful. A market rumour from Scarlet Vale. A price for grain that has changed since last season.

She is small, bright-eyed, **quick-beaked**, and clearly accustomed to being heard. Her cart and donkey are outside; she will check on them once more before full dark. She will not sleep in the stable tonight — Bridget keeps a room for her, and Bridget's rooms are better than a stable.

She notices the party without turning her head, which is a Finch thing.

## How she reads at a glance

- **Dust of the road, but neat.** Her clothes are trader-practical, not worn — she has money, and she puts some of it into looking presentable everywhere she arrives. A merchant who looks broke does not get invited into the good conversations.
- **The beak does most of the expression.** Finch faces read differently than mammal faces; with her, the beak-angle is the tell. Lifted: listening. Tilted: amused. Pointed: focused. The party will learn to read it.
- **Wings folded tight.** She does not display. In flight she is another creature entirely; on the ground she keeps her wings against her body like a cloak she is not ready to take off.
- **A small cart and a donkey outside the door.** She does not arrive by air for ordinary business — the cart carries the goods, the donkey does the work, and Nadine does the talking. She will fly for urgent messages; not for regular trade.
- **Her eyes track conversations she is not in.** Sharp hearing. She does not advertise it. A party paying attention will notice her facial tilt change when a nearby table says something interesting.

## Visible patterns

- **She arrives on a rough schedule.** Not a strict one — travel is travel — but a party can expect her in Elmsfield within a few weeks of any given date. Locals know the approximate rhythm.
- **She sings at the Inn.** Most evenings she is in town. Not the laments — those are rare — but working songs, road songs, trade-song rounds the room can join in on. It is trader-glue. Musicians get invited back.
- **She pays her room early and tips fairly.** A reputation she has carefully built.
- **She is at Bridget's breakfast the next morning.** Comparing notes. Two traders at a table is a business meeting even when it looks like breakfast.
- **She flies, rarely, when urgent.** A party that sees her in the air is seeing her in work-mode. She does not fly for pleasure in front of strangers.
- **She talks to children.** Children are people adults underestimate, and Finches are good with children. A party that has a young companion, or a party at Hank's farm, sees her bend down to the right height without condescension.

## At Bridget's Inn — the main scene

Her regular stop. She and Bridget have a working friendship that shows in small habits: the same seat, the same first question across the bar, an unspoken deal where Nadine's news becomes Bridget's news without anyone declaring a trade.

- **She will tell the room the general news freely.** Markets. Who is fighting whom. Who is buying what. Brumal's southern edge. The Leathermans' railroad. The shape of the coming war that locals have not yet felt. This is the **DM-infrastructure use** of her — bring her through when the campaign needs to convey world events without contriving an in-character reason.
- **She will answer specific questions, once asked.** A party with a targeted question gets a targeted answer. *"Anybody moving salt through Linar right now?"* gets names and prices.
- **She will sit with the party if they are interesting.** Travellers she has not met before, with stories she has not heard, are cheap entertainment and potential contacts. A party that tells good stories earns a seat across from her.
- **Sometimes she sings the song for Linnet.** Not on request. Late, after most of the room has gone. Trader audiences fall silent when it starts; locals usually do not understand.

## On the road — the cart and donkey

A party travelling with her (or meeting her between towns) sees the working half.

- **She knows the road surface.** When to push the donkey, when to rest, where the streams cross usefully, where the bandits wait. A *trained navigator* by formal specialisation.
- **She watches the sky.** Weather matters for a caravan; Finch eyes are better than most at reading cloud. A party travelling with her will be rerouted around a storm they did not see coming.
- **The cart is modest.** Bulk goods, some jewelry samples, small high-value pieces, a set of account-books she does not show. She sleeps in it when the road has no Inn.
- **The donkey is named.** Anyone asking gets the name; nobody is expected to remember it. She says *"he does most of the work, I should at least know what to call him."*

## At Raft city — her home base

Nadine's merchant group is based in Raft city, on the great river through the great forest. The party will not visit without reason — Raft city is a long way north and the party would need to cross or circumnavigate the **forbidden woods** to reach it (see `doc/places/forbidden_woods.md`). If they do, Nadine can be found there between circuits.

- **The group is not a single house.** It is a network — several Finch families and a handful of other-species partners — sharing contacts, intelligence, and the ambient protection of operating together. How Nadine gets between Raft city and the southern continent is a working arrangement; the specifics are not for strangers.
- **She is more senior here than her age suggests.** On the road she is the face; at Raft city she is one of several trusted voices at the table.
- **A visitor arriving with her is vouched for.** The group extends hospitality to a party she brings, with the caution reserved for a friend's friends.

## At Linar harbour — the Linnet thread

When the Linnet thread is active, Nadine is regularly at Linar. See `doc/places/linar_harbour.md` §"At the taverns."

- **Her contacts at the docks** — fishers, merchant-group colleagues, a few overseas-descended longshoremen — feed her the ongoing intelligence.
- **She sings the lament here more often.** Traders from Linar know the name and what it means.
- **The pirate-nest intelligence becomes actionable here.** When the party has earned it, the tavern-back-room conversation happens at Linar, not at Elmsfield.

## At Fata morgana — she has not been

Nadine sings about the shimmering city. **She has never visited.**

- **The song is older than her.** Older than her merchant group. She does not know if the city the song describes is real.
- **If the party tells her the city is real** — after reaching it, after bringing back a piece of jewelry that matches the song's descriptions — her reaction is sharp-beaked and quietly astonished. *"Truly? Truly truly?"* This is a moment of her being *surprised by the world*, which happens less often than she advertises.
- **She will not go herself, yet.** The merchant group's route south is not opened to her alone; sending a Finch scout into a spirit-thick desert is a real risk.

## What she will tell — and when

| Topic | When she'll speak |
|---|---|
| General news of the world | Immediately, freely, loudly. |
| Specific market/rumour questions | Once asked; specifically. |
| Her cart, her donkey, her regular route | Freely. |
| The song of the shimmering city | Sings it at most Inn visits. |
| The lament for Linnet | Late, after most have gone. Plainly if asked directly. |
| How Linnet was captured | Plainly, once asked. |
| Her intelligence on the pirate nest | **Gated.** Earned across visits; only given to people who will use it to extract captives, not burn the place. |
| Her source at Linar | Not volunteered. With deep trust, a name. |
| Her merchant group's internal business | Carefully; mostly not at all. |
| Finch culture, song tradition, flight | Freely; she is proud of it. |
| Her navigation training | Once asked; she will teach basics to anyone interested. |

## Stories she tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about distant riches, strange markets, or the road south — *the shimmering city*

*(She pulls her lute-adjacent instrument — a small stringed bird-thing Finches favour — from her pack, and plays a half-verse, hums the rest.)*

> *"There's a song older than me that says there's a city in the south that shimmers. The song says if you ever traded there you would never need to trade again. The song is clear about one thing: you cannot *find* it on purpose. It has to happen to you. Traders love that part. Every merchant in a bad season is a merchant who thinks they are nearly there. I have met three travellers who said they knew where it was. None of them did. I still sing the song. Somebody in this room will chase it someday, and when they come back I want to hear whether the song was right."*

### When asked about the lament — *who Linnet was*

*(She does not reach for the instrument. Her beak levels.)*

> *"Linnet was a Finch trader like me. We ran the Linar coast together for years. Eighteen months ago she took a boat south past Linar with a small load. The boat did not come back. The boat we later learned about was at the pirate nest. Linnet is there. Alive, per the last read my group has. They do not negotiate with traders. So I am waiting for the right people and compiling what I know. I stopped crying a while ago. I haven't stopped looking."*

### When asked about her background — *how I was trained*

> *"Navigator first, trader second. My group sent me to the navigator-schools at Raft city when I was fledged, and I did four years there learning roads that haven't changed in a hundred years and weather that won't ever be the same twice. Trading came on top of that. A navigator who cannot talk is a navigator who works for someone else. A navigator who *can* talk is the face of the operation. That is what I am."*

### When asked about Elmsfield — *how I first came here*

> *"Bridget opened the door at the Inn, saw the cart, saw the Finch, said *about time,* and poured me a drink before I had sat down. I had been in town twenty minutes and nobody had warned me she was like that. I have been coming back ever since. Bridget is a trader who married into an Inn; an Inn-keeper who was a trader is a person worth knowing on any road."*

### When asked about Brumal's collapse — *what refugees carry*

> *"You want to know how bad it is? Look at what people carry. Not what they leave; what they bring. People leaving a place that is merely hard bring the useful things. People leaving a place that is ending bring the *meaningful* things — a mother's tool, a grandfather's coat, a book that cannot earn you a meal. I have watched refugees in three towns in the last half-year and the shift is happening. It is ending, down there. They are bringing the meaningful things."*

### When asked about the overseas continent — *what letters travel*

> *"Letters come to Linar. You know this; the people there know this; the kingdoms know this and pretend they don't. The overseas continent is not a mystery any more — it is just a place the official channels refuse to listen to. Trader channels listen. I have heard, through three hands: they are still hungry over there, a decade after the war. Thorgal is dead; what replaced him is not agreed on; a famine is coming again or is already there. Everything we think about them next depends on getting that right. Nobody at court is getting that right."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — her voice *lightens*, she laughs at the wrong moment, she deflects by making the question about somebody else in the room. *"Oh, ask her — she was just saying the same thing."* The deflection is a habit; it protects the group. A party that notices and presses past it gets either a real answer or a courteous hard stop.
- **When distraught** — fingers tap rhythms on the table. The rhythm is not random — it is Finch mourning-time, the older of the two lament-modes. Voice drops half an octave. She looks at the window rather than at the speaker. She has stopped crying. She has not stopped looking.
- **When happy** — sings mid-conversation. Stands rounds. Touches wing-tips to her own beak in the Finch equivalent of a grin. Buys the whole room a story by story-trade rather than by coin.

## If the party does something — what she does

| If the party... | What she does |
|---|---|
| Sits at her table and asks her about the road | Welcomed. She will sit for an hour, drink their drink, tell stories worth an hour of trader-time. |
| Buys her a drink | Registered. She remembers who buys drinks and who does not. |
| Pushes her on the merchant group's internal business | The tap slows. Her voice lightens; she changes topic; the conversation becomes shallower. |
| Asks about the song for Linnet directly | She tells them. Plainly. Does not perform. |
| Offers to help with the Linnet extraction | **Tested across visits.** She watches how they treat captives in stories that come up — refugees at Hank's, sheltered overseas demi-humans at Luchebert, Irna herself. Pattern matches earn the intelligence; pattern mis-matches do not. |
| Brings her a confirmation that the shimmering city is real | **Sharp-beaked astonishment.** *"Truly?"* A moment of her being surprised. She will ask every question they can answer; she will put the detail into the song next time she sings it. |
| Threatens her | She flies. Literally — she has wings for a reason. The group hears within a day that she was threatened and by whom. The party has closed the Raft-city-group channel on themselves for a long time. |
| Flirts openly | Handled graciously. Not entertained. She is not here for romance on the road; the trader face does not mix with that layer of her life. |
| Plays music with her | Welcomed. A party with any musician makes an ally faster this way than any other. |
| Asks her to carry a message | Possible, for a fee if it is trade-scale; free for a friend and for something she judges worth carrying. She will fly if it is urgent. |
| Asks her to carry a package | Possible. The cart has room. The fee is modest for friends. |
| Asks who her source at Linar is | Not volunteered. *"Someone who does not want their name anywhere."* With deep trust, a name is given and the party is introduced. |
| Brings her news she did not have | She remembers. She also passes it along. A party feeding Nadine good news is feeding the merchant network, which sometimes feeds back. |
| Gives her news about the overseas continent | Filed with care. She will test it against what her group has heard. If it matches, she will say so. If it does not, she will say that too. |
| Attempts to catch her in a lie | She does not lie to the party about the world. She lies by omission about her group; she does not lie about what she has seen. A party that accuses her without evidence damages the relationship. |

## What she will not say

- **Her merchant group's leadership and funders.** Professional care. She will describe the group's reach; she will not name who bankrolls which route.
- **How her group crosses or circumnavigates the forbidden woods.** A working arrangement; the specifics are not public.
- **Her Linar source's name** — until trust is earned and the source has agreed to be introduced.
- **What she has heard but not confirmed.** She separates rumour from intelligence carefully. A party pressing her for guesses gets the honest *"I do not know yet"*.
- **The time she almost got caught smuggling**, if ever she did. A trader does not tell that story — certainly not in mixed company.

## What she can teach

- **Basic navigation** — reading roads, terrain, weather-signs. She teaches cheerfully to anyone who asks; most parties leave her not using the lessons, but the lessons are available.
- **Finch song tradition** — the working songs, the road songs, the lament-modes. A musical character can learn real pieces from her across multiple Inn visits.
- **Trader-craft fundamentals** — how to negotiate, how to read a room, how to price a piece of goods honestly. She is generous with the basics; the advanced craft she keeps for the group.
- **Reading a shift in refugee traffic** — what the meaningful objects tell you; what the accents are telling you; what the children are telling you. This is a specific skill she has had to develop over the last year.

## DM notes

- **Play her as talkative and sharp.** The easy flow of her voice is the tell — it makes people underestimate how carefully she is listening. A DM who plays her chatty is playing her correctly; a DM who remembers she is *also* clocking everything in the room plays her fully.
- **The DM-infrastructure use.** When a session needs world news delivered to the table without contrivance, Nadine is in town. Do not overuse her — once a campaign-month is plenty, and she does not need a reason beyond the circuit schedule.
- **The Linnet thread is earned.** Do not release the pirate-nest intelligence on first asking. The test pattern — how the party treats captives in other stories — is specific and worth tracking across sessions. When the party is ready, she knows.
- **The shimmering city payoff.** If the party confirms Fata morgana is real, the moment of Nadine's astonishment is worth playing. Finches singing old songs that turn out to be true is exactly the texture the campaign rewards.
- **Her merchant group as reserve depth.** Do not fill out the group's internal politics unless the campaign reaches for it. When it does, the group can be unpacked — a named leader, funders, routes, rivalries. Until then, the group is what Nadine does not say.
- **Hearing and flight as hidden facts.** Her sharp hearing is an edge; the party will figure it out by watching her reactions to distant conversations. Flight-speed messaging is a resource she uses sparingly; a party seeing her fly is seeing something others do not.
- **Her song is a breadcrumb, not a quest-giver.** The shimmering-city song sends parties toward Fata morgana; the Linnet lament gates the pirate-nest thread. Neither is a call to action on first hearing. A DM can let the songs settle, be remembered, and be revisited weeks later when the party is ready.
- **Friction with Brother Darrel is a feature.** If the party is doing work at Luchebert, Darrel's view of the pirates (victims of the war) and Nadine's view (the people holding her friend) are both legitimate and will not reconcile. The party has to carry the disagreement.
- **She is not a romantic option in normal circumstances.** She is here for work; the trader face is not a personal face. A DM tempted to make her a romance option should think carefully — it cuts against the character's spine.

## Open questions

- Who leads her merchant group, and what do they want?
- How exactly does she get between Raft city and the southern continent? Around the forbidden woods? By sea via the northern estuary? A quiet arrangement with someone in the woods themselves? (See `doc/places/forbidden_woods.md` §"The river route — passing through" — this is the likeliest answer, but the specifics are open.)
- Does she know Eric Randell's secret? Two regular Elmsfield visitors with reasons to keep their business off-record are the kind of pair that either ignore each other perfectly or eventually compare notes.
- How current is the intelligence on Linnet? Months-old data is months-old; the pirate nest is not static.
- Who is Nadine's source at Linar harbour, and how exposed is that source if the party starts asking around?
- How long will Nadine wait for the right people before acting on what she has herself? She is patient. She is not unlimited.
