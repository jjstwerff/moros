# Felix

- **Role:** Scout, opportunist, instigator. John Bean's wartime companion. The man who found the opening, freed the king, and is still moving.
- **Species:** Raccoon. Wears rings on his tail.
- **Lives:** Nowhere in particular. Moves through a circuit of regional fairs (see *Where to find him*). Winters somewhere near Linar harbour, with a Raccoon clan-kin he will not name.
- **First met:** At a fair. Whichever the party next passes through, or one soon after.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** John Bean's wartime scout; the man who found the slot at the mill, freed Hannes after the mirror broke, and pulled Rhianna out before Elliot's army arrived. Now an itinerant fair-circuit operator with a tingling feeling for precious things and a habit of walking out of rooms before he commits to anything.
- **Standouts:** *A raccoon with rings on his tail* — tarnished silver, mismatched, possibly ten or possibly six (the count changes between meetings and he will not explain why). **Eyes that catch every movement at the periphery**, looking past the conversation he is in. **Voice carries a smile** even when the rest of him is serious.
- **Speech tag:** Fast, casual, slightly transactional. Smiles wider when pressed; *"You don't actually want to do this"* when threatened. Has told his stories before — they come out the same way each time.
- **Quests:** Early — *bring him the next find: a place he cannot yet reach (the Storm Mage's cottage, Raul's mirror camp, the graveless paths for Andras's crown). The fee is a share; the existence of one is not negotiable.* Late — *bring him to the mill, finally, with John and Ron. He will not arrive on his own, ever.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *Will not commit to long arrangements.* He helps with the next thing; the thing after that is a separate conversation. Bribery accepts, threats are deflected by walking out (*"You don't actually want to do this"*); steal from him and a fake ring shows up in the thief's pocket with a meaningless note while he is already gone. Following him fails. He gives a real ring to whoever makes him laugh — that is the channel that opens.

## What the party meets

The party walks into a fair — market day, harvest festival, town gathering — and Felix is there. He is **already aware of them** before they spot him: he is mid-conversation with someone else, but his eyes have caught the new arrivals at the edge of the crowd. Within a minute he has broken off whatever he was doing and is making his way over, smiling like he knows them, even if he does not.

His introduction is fast, casual, and slightly transactional. He asks where they are from, how long they're staying, what brought them to this fair specifically. He does not appear to be questioning them. He is questioning them. By the time the conversation ends he has cost them nothing and learned everything.

If they're useful to him, he will turn up again. If they're not, they will not see him at this fair after today.

## Personal goals — early and later

He does not name his goals out loud. He carries one as a habit and one as a wound. A party that has earned standing reads them anyway, and the new specific work happens through the same hand: bring him the *next thing* and he will commit to it; bring him the *one thing he cannot bring himself to face* and he will, eventually, let you take him to it.

- **Early-game — bring him the next find.** *What the NPC says* (at a fair, when the party brings him a sealed place or an unfindable contact and the tail rings clatter): *"Tell me more. Where is it. What's around it. What's been tried. I'm not promising. I'm thinking. I'll think faster if there's something concrete on the table — bread, a name, a thing you noticed nobody else noticed. There's a share at the end of this. That part isn't negotiable. The shape of the share is. We'll talk about it when we know what we found."* — *Where:* whichever fair Felix is currently working — Elmsfield, Salmonswell, hill-town markets, harvest festivals; from the fair to wherever the party's *find* is. **Three named openings the campaign already has waiting**: the **Storm Mage's cottage** in the hills, which he can find faster than any other living tracker (`doc/npcs/storm_mage.md`); **Raul's forward camp and Thorgal's mirror**, where he knows the way and will draw it *in air, not on paper*, for a party that has earned it (`doc/npcs/raul.md`, `doc/npcs/thorgal.md`); the **graveless paths** through the broken-lands' edge a party transporting **Andras**'s stolen crown will need (`doc/npcs/andras.md`). Adjacent: **Corven's courier route** between Scarlet Vale and Brumal — Felix is one of the carriers Corven can recruit, on terms (`doc/npcs/prince_corven.md`). — *What:* bring him the place; describe what is sealed and why; agree on the shape of the share before the work begins; do the work *with* him (he is opening a slot for them, not delivering them to the prize); split the find on the agreed shape afterwards. — *Reward:* the place opens. The mirror-camp's location is given, in air, when the standing is real. The cottage is found in a single guided walk. The crown's wake is dampened on routes Andras's order has not yet mapped. Felix's standing with the party deepens — and a ring comes off his tail next time he laughs at one of them. — *Complications:* he will not commit to a long arrangement, only the next thing; a party that wants him on retainer is asking the wrong question. **Naming the share too late** — after the find — costs trust; the shape of it is on the table before, not after. **Threatening or following him** closes the channel; bribing him gets a smile and the same information he would have given for nothing. The find may cost something he warned about and they ignored — he does not stop them.

- **Later-game — bring him to the mill, with John and Ron.** *Conditional — opens when **John Bean**'s late-game mill quest activates (`doc/npcs/john_bean.md` — "Put the mill down, with Ron beside me"), once Ron is discharged and home at Blackwood freehold.* *What the NPC says* (in private, at a fair the party has tracked him to, after the party has news that Ron is home and John is ready to walk back to the mill): *"I haven't been near it since the day John came out short. He doesn't press. I don't visit. I have been carrying that mill on my back for nineteen years like it weighed nothing. It does not weigh nothing. If you are the people walking him back in — and you are, because no one else would — I'll come. I'll find the route again. I won't go through the slot first this time; he should. Tell me when. Don't tell me twice. I'll be there."* — *Where:* Felix's current fair for the conversation; the **Bean farm at Blackwood freehold** for the regrouping with John, Ron, and Ellie; the route through the **broken lands' edge** that Felix still knows (with **Wilder**'s stretch as regional context — `doc/npcs/wilder.md`); the **old mill** itself (a sealed mage-era cutting facility, the cutter still partly working, the ground-level slot Felix found, the hazards real). — *What:* be the people who finally bring Felix to the farm — he will not arrive on his own, ever. Travel to the mill with John, Ron, Felix, and the party. Get inside through Felix's slot, but **do not let Felix go through first this time**; that is John's beat. Walk John through the middle he does not remember. Come out together. — *Reward:* the wound that has been sitting since the war stops sitting. **Felix's late-game settles** — he does not expect the visit to fix him; he expects it will let him stop dragging the mill behind him. **John's late-game closes** in the same scene. **Ron sees what his father survived.** Felix's standing with the party becomes the kind that opens his winter fallback near Linar harbour to them, eventually, if the campaign reaches that long. — *Complications:* the mill is still partly working; the hazards are real. **Felix should not lead the entry**; if the party stages it as it was nineteen years ago, Felix will go first by reflex, and the wound deepens rather than closes. The middle of John's memory may surface in a way that costs him; Felix will recognise the cost and *will not narrate it for him*. **Asking Felix to come back to the farm afterwards** is not the offer to extend — he will not stay. *"I helped him put it down. That doesn't mean I move in."*

## How he reads at a glance

- **A raccoon with rings on his tail** — tarnished silver, mismatched, possibly ten of them, possibly six. The count changes between meetings and he will not explain why.
- **Eyes catch every movement at the periphery** — never quite still. Looking past the conversation he is in.
- **Voice carries a smile** even when the rest of him is serious. Sounds glad to see whoever he is talking to. Sometimes he is.
- **Light on his feet for someone who is constantly in motion** — does not appear to hurry. Manages to be wherever he needs to be anyway.

## Where to find him — the fair circuit

Felix has no fixed home, and a party looking for him by address will not find one. What he has is a **circuit of regional fairs** — market days, harvest festivals, town gatherings — that he moves through on a rough seasonal pattern. A party that wants to find him **attends fairs**: if not the next one they pass through, then one soon after.

When the party arrives at a fair where Felix is present, three things stand out:

- **He has already noticed them** before they spotted him. He will be mid-conversation with someone else, but his attention is on the crowd, not the conversation.
- **The rings on his tail catch the light** at angles a tail does not normally make. The tarnished silver moves with him through the crowd; once the party knows what to look for, they can spot him from across the field.
- **He breaks off and greets them like old friends**, whether they have met or not. Within thirty seconds he knows what they are carrying and what they want.

His **off-circuit fallback** is somewhere near **Linar harbour**, with a Raccoon clan-kin he will not name. He winters there if the season is bad. The party may stumble onto this if they go looking; he will not lead them to it.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **Always at the edge of a crowd**, not in the middle of it. Better view from there.
- **Talks with everyone, commits to no one.** Sells nothing he hasn't already bought once for less.
- **Eats fast** — fairs feed him; he eats whatever's nearest while talking.
- **Moves on between days.** A second day at the same fair is rare. By the third he is already at the next one.
- **Disappears when he doesn't want to be seen.** The party will look for him at a fair and not find him; later they will learn he was there the whole time.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up and he chooses to answer.

### When asked about the mill — *the slot*

> *"There were three doors I couldn't get through. There was one slot at ground level you'd never call a door. Whole ironwood trees used to feed in that way, back when the mill was running. I told John — look, that gap is wider than us; we can be wider than us if we breathe out. He went first. He always went first. I would not have known what to do inside. He would not have known how to *get* inside. We were a useful pair."*

### When asked about freeing Hannes — *the gate, when nobody was looking*

> *"The mirror went dark. The war-band's commander started shouting. For about twenty minutes nobody was looking at the king's tent. I was looking. I went in. I came out. The crown has never thanked me for it; I think the crown does not entirely want to admit they had a king to be untaken. I'm not bitter. I'm telling you the story."*

### When asked about Lady Rhianna — *getting her out before the army arrived*

> *"Crown Prince's army was coming to liberate her town. Liberation is dangerous if you happen to be standing in the way. I told her — pack a bag, pack a small one, we leave in the next hour. She argued for ten minutes. I won the argument by walking out the door. She caught up. Her town liberated itself without her. She has not been back."*

### When asked about his "feeling" — *the tingling*

> *(He shrugs.)* *"I get a feeling. Don't ask me to describe it. It's right about where the precious thing is and it tells me nothing about what it costs. The mill cost an arm. The king cost nothing. The next one might cost the rest of me. I keep going to fairs anyway."*

### When asked why he doesn't settle — *what cities are good for*

> *"A city is good for a week. Then everyone in the city has had a turn at me and I've had a turn at them, and the next interesting thing is somewhere I haven't been. I tried staying. It didn't take."*

### When asked about Raul — *the hills he won't take you to*

> *(Long pause. He stops moving. He is not smiling.)* *"There's a place in the hills between the great swamp and Allondo. Raul's people settled there after the war ended. They're still there. Thorgal's mirror is somewhere in that camp; nobody picked it up afterwards. I know the way in. I will not draw it for you. If you really need to go, I will think about it."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — voice gets *more* cheerful, not less. Smile widens. Says things that sound like answers and are not. Hands go into his pockets.
- **When distraught** — quiet. Will not speak. Touches the rings on his tail one by one, in order. Will leave the conversation if he can.
- **When happy** — laughs in short bursts. **Pulls a ring off his tail and gives it to whoever made him laugh.** He has been doing this for years and has not run out of rings; nobody has worked out where they come from.

| If the party... | He responds by... |

| Tries to bribe him for information | Accepts the bribe. Tells them what they would have learned for free anyway. Doesn't think this is dishonest. |
| Tries to threaten him for information | Goes very still. *"You don't actually want to do this."* If they push, he is gone by morning and nobody at the fair has seen him. |
| Brings him a worthwhile target — a place with something valuable that needs an opening found | His tail rings clatter. *"Tell me more."* Will help, for a share. *That* there is a share is not negotiable; the shape of it is. |
| Mentions John Bean by name | Pauses. *"How is he?"* Means it. Will cut off whatever else he was doing if the party has news of John. |

## What he can teach

For students he judges have the head for it — and the patience to learn from someone who keeps moving:

- **Stealth** — practical, not theatrical. The kind that gets you past one person who isn't paying attention, not the kind that gets you past an alert guard.
- **Reading a crowd** — picking out the person worth talking to, the person about to leave, the person carrying more than they should.
- **Finding openings** — looking for the slot, the vent, the way nobody else thought of. Cannot teach the *tingling feeling*; can teach the habit of looking for what is not a door.
- **Improvisation under pressure** — the trade he survived the war on.

## What he will not say

- **Where his winter fallback is.** If the party finds it, fair play. He will not tell them.
- **Who his Raccoon clan-kin are.** Names are leverage; he does not give leverage.
- **How his "tingling feeling" actually works.** He does not know himself, and does not want to look at it too closely.
- **What he is actually carrying.** The rings on his tail are decoys for the question. Whatever he actually values is somewhere else.

## DM notes

- Felix is the **mirror breadcrumb** in person (see `doc/npcs/thorgal.md` §"The chain, not the direct ask"). He is one of five who know where Raul's war-band settled after the war and where Thorgal's haunted mirror was left. He will mention the location in casual conversation if the topic comes up — but only after the party has earned enough standing that he trusts them not to walk into Raul's people unprepared.
- The **rings on his tail** are a recurring physical asset. He gives one to whoever makes him laugh. Over a campaign the party may accumulate several. They are mostly tarnished silver and mismatched; some are genuine trade tokens, some are not. *He does not say where they come from.*
- Felix has **no fixed home**, which adapts the goals' defined-home requirement to his **fair circuit**. The party can reliably find him; they cannot find him on demand. If the campaign needs him on a specific date, schedule a fair.
- He cost John his arm at the mill (see *the slot* story). John has never held it against him. Felix has never forgiven himself for it cleanly. This is the secret-level pressure under his cheerfulness.
- Lore cross-references: the mill and John's arm in `doc/npcs/john_bean.md`; Hannes's capture and release in `doc/npcs/king_hannes.md`; the mirror chain in `doc/npcs/thorgal.md`; the war background in `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"The Overseas War."

- Wartime companion of **John Bean** and **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**.
- The one who **found the opening at the ironwood mill** where John lost his arm.
