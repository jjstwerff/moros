# Felix

- **Role:** Scout, opportunist, and instigator; John Bean's wartime companion
- **Species:** Raccoon (animal-person)
- **Location:** Current whereabouts unrecorded
- **Status:** Alive

## Summary

Quick-thinking to a fault — described as already three steps ahead of whatever conversation he is in. He wears rings on his tail. Where John second-guesses himself, Felix is the one who pushes back: he reminded John, after the arm, that one-armed John was still a much better fighter than Felix himself, and therefore worth keeping around.

He is also the reason a great deal of the trouble happened in the first place. Felix was the instigator of most of the scrapes he, John, and **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** fell into during the Overseas War — he had an eye for openings that a sensible person would have left alone. The one that mattered most: an ironwood-processing mill that the group could not approach by any door, until Felix pointed out the low slot where whole trees fed into the cutting mechanism. They went in that way. Inside was the ancient machine that took John's arm. That mill was one of the very few in the region capable of cutting ironwood at all, which is why anyone wanted to get into it.

Felix was simply being his usual self. He had **no specific knowledge of the mill** — no conspiracy, no hidden agenda, not malicious in any direction. What he had was a **tingling feeling that something precious was there**. The feeling was real, and it was correct: there *was* something precious inside, and it has shaped the world since (the machine was one of the very few capable of cutting ironwood; John's arm came from the same mill's material, in a sense). Felix was right about the importance. He was wrong about the specifics. He did not know what was inside, did not know what it would cost, and did not foresee that his friend would walk out missing an arm. His instincts point him at worthy targets; they do not tell him the shape of the price.

This is the texture of Felix as an asset and as a liability: he finds the openings that matter. He cannot see what matters about them until someone pays.

## Relationships

- Wartime companion of **John Bean** and **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**.
- The one who found the opening at the ironwood mill where John lost his arm.
- **The one who actually freed King Hannes.** When John's ironwood-arm killing of **Thorgal** broke the scrying mirror and **Raul**'s forward war band fell back in confusion, Felix was the one who slipped in during the retreat and extracted Hannes. John's act ended the war; Felix's timing ended Hannes's captivity. The crown has never properly acknowledged this, in part because it has never properly acknowledged the capture at all.
- **Pulled Lady Rhianna out of her own home** ahead of Crown Prince Elliot's liberating advance. Felix read the fog of war correctly: advancing armies are dangerous to whoever is in front of them, regardless of allegiance. Rhianna still does not live in that town.
