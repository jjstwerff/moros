# Farmer Joseph

- **Role:** Raccoon farmer in Elmsfield; post-war refugee; recently land-rich and cash-poor; the town's loud voice of post-war fear
- **Species:** Raccoon
- **Location:** Elmsfield
- **Status:** Alive; stretched thin
- **Specialisation:** Sling

## Summary

A Raccoon farmer who arrived in Elmsfield **as a refugee** after the last war — one of many whose life the war rearranged. He carries a sling and knows how to use it, a habit from wherever he was before this; what he was before Elmsfield is open and matters less for him personally than for how the town's changing make-up reads to its older residents.

He **recently bought a plot of land from Hank**. The purchase was a considered decision — he needed the extra land for **better crop rotation**, the kind of medium-term agricultural thinking that distinguishes a farmer with an eye on the next decade from one farming season to season. But the plot cost him **his entire savings**. He is now land-rich and cash-poor, and that leaves him *a bit angry* — not at Hank, who gave him a fair price and genuinely needed to sell, but at the situation: he spent the whole buffer he had spent years building, and there is nothing left between him and a bad year. The extra land will pay off, given time. He just has to get there.

### The fear at the gate

Joseph also carries something heavier than the land stretch. He watched people die of **cholera** in the aftermath of the war — neighbours, people he knew. The post-war overcrowding (occupation armies, refugee flows, stranded demi-human soldiers with nowhere to return to) left Elmsfield pressed beyond what the town had been built for, and sickness followed. Joseph lost people to it. He did not forget.

That is why, when a stranger collapses at Elmsfield's gate covered in crystalline growths, **Joseph is the first voice raised in fear** of contagion. He is loud about it. His fear is not irrational — he saw what an outbreak did to his neighbours — but it risks turning the town against helping **Irna Bean** before anyone understands what is wrong with her. Session 1 opens with this: Joseph is the first antagonist the party meets, and he is not a villain. He is a farmer with specific trauma, standing at the gate of the town that has finally let him belong, refusing to watch it break again.

The stretched-thin financial state makes it worse. A man whose margin is gone is a man with less room for generosity toward strangers. His mood is not quite bitterness and not quite anxiety. It is the sum of *having just finished settling* and *having watched the last settlement disaster* — the combination that makes "no more" feel like reasonable policy.

Addressing him correctly in Session 1 — acknowledging that his concern is the town's safety rather than Irna herself — is how the opening scene moves forward. Ignoring him lets the fear spread. Overriding him creates a resentment that will colour the whole of his interactions with the party afterwards.

### Role in the campaign

- **Session 1's opening obstacle.** Not a monster; a man with a specific, earned fear.
- **A legible voice against more refugees.** As the Brumal crisis starts pushing people toward Elmsfield, Joseph's exhaustion + trauma + stretched finances combine into the town's most vocal "no more" voice. Not cruel. Tired. Already stretched.
- **A possible ally once turned.** A party that understands him — that respects what he saw, helps with his farm when the chance arises, or simply demonstrates that the strangers they bring are not what he fears — can shift him. He is not a permanent antagonist. He is a man waiting for evidence that this time will not be like the last time.

## Relationships

- Bought a plot from **Hank** — one of the transactions that has helped the farm on Elmsfield's edge expand into an orphanage of sorts. He does not blame her, but the transaction hollowed him out, and that will colour his stance on anything that asks more of him in the short term — including, likely, new waves of refugees arriving at Hank's farm.
- Part of Elmsfield's post-war settler community, with the specific edge of someone who *just* finished settling and whose margin is now zero.
- Lost neighbours or family to the post-war cholera outbreak; his fear at Irna's arrival comes from that loss, not from generic xenophobia.
