# Willow

- **Role:** Old rabbit-folk granny of Luchebert. Tender of Felicia's shrine. Keeper of the Abbey's quiet truth, and its gatekeeper.
- **Species:** Rabbit folk. Very old.
- **Lives:** A small room in the central building, near the kitchen. Walks down to **the cave chapel below the gate** — Felicia's shrine — at dawn and at dusk, every day. The chapel is her real home; the room is where she sleeps.
- **First met:** Either at the cave chapel as the party passes it on entry (if she is there at dawn or dusk), or in the maze garden's herb plots, or intercepting them later when they try to approach the chapel uninvited.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Felicia Vintala's hands at Luchebert; the granny-line's current holder; gatekeeper of the necklace; the third layer of the hill country's spirit-shield ring after Felicia and Father Elliot.
- **Standouts:** *A small old rabbit-folk in earth-coloured wool, never hurrying.* **Always with flowers** — a tray, a basket, a bundle in her apron, one in her hand. **Hands stained with soil and stem-sap**; fingertips darker than the rest of her fur. **A small knife at her belt** for cutting flower-stems — and a knife. Steady gaze, looks *at*, not through.
- **Speech tag:** Level, unhurried, gatekeeper-firm. The phrases recur: *"Do not defile my life's work."* *"Sit, then."* *"Not yet named. I'll know her when I meet her."* Calls younger people *child* once they have her trust.
- **Quests:** Early — *earn the necklace, on my terms, for Laurent; bring it back when you have used it elsewhere.* Late — *bring me the candidate I can name as my successor; help me hold the southern corridor if Path 2 opens.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *Asking too soon closes the topic for that visit.* The necklace cannot be taken; theft from the shrine triggers the **draining curse**, gradual and hard to undo. She does not warn. She does not fight; she stands still and the curse is ready.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/willow.md`.*

## What the party meets

The party arrives at Luchebert via the hill route. They are walked through the maze garden, taken into the cloister, settled. At some point they ask after the chapel that the path passed on the way up — or they try to walk back down to it themselves — and a small rabbit-folk granny is suddenly between them and the path.

She is not imposing. She does not raise her voice. She looks at the party for a long moment, entirely calm, and says, in the same level tone she uses for everything:

> *"Do not defile my life's work."*

That is her greeting. She means it. The party is not going down to the chapel until she has decided they may. How they respond to this — with respect, with dismissal, with the common visitor's *"we're just looking"* — is the first beat of their relationship with her, and she remembers it.

## Personal goals — early and later

Her goals are kept the way she keeps the shrine — quietly, daily, without ceremony. The early one is the necklace; the later ones are the line and, if it opens, the corridor.

- **Early-game — earn the necklace, on my terms, for Laurent.** *What the NPC says* (after the trust gate has held — the party has visited more than once, has not asked too soon, has handled small shrine-tasks cleanly, and has come to her with a specific need): *"The piece on the shrine is not a sentiment. It **is** a working. It can condense Felicia's presence near someone she cannot otherwise reach. You will want it for Laurent; that is why parties ask after it. I can lend it, if I decide to. I will want it back. I will want to know what you said to him when you used it."* — *Where:* the **cave chapel below the gate** (`doc/places/felicia_shrine.md`) — the loan is given here, in front of the shrine, with both hands; the **wreck of Laurent's flying city** in the hills near the Lake of Tears (`doc/npcs/laurent.md`, `doc/places/laurents_city.md`) — the audience hall, where the condensation fires and the open conversation becomes possible; back to the cave chapel for the return. — *What:* take the necklace from her hand, in front of the shrine, with the conditions stated aloud; carry it through the wreck to Laurent's audience hall; let the condensation fire; complete the conversation; bring the necklace back; sit with Willow in the chapel and **tell her what was said to Laurent while it was used**. — *Reward:* the campaign's clearest window onto the pre-rift world opens (Laurent's downfall narration; the six-shackle mechanism; the partner-of-the-work register Felicia is in with him). Willow's standing toward the late-game line deepens. The party becomes one of the few hands she has trusted with what is, for her, the most load-bearing object she keeps. — *Complications:* a party that **forgets** to come back and tell her what was said — even if the necklace itself is returned — gets one polite reminder and then a closed door. Lying about the conversation is worse; she will know. **The necklace cannot be loaned for cleverness or curiosity** — only for a specific need that has already been heard at the shrine. Asking on a first visit closes the topic for the rest of the visit; asking with a half-honest reason closes it for several visits. **If Moth Hollow has been disturbed in the campaign, the loan does not happen** until restitution is complete — Felicia's channels are closed and Willow will not pretend otherwise.

- **Early-game — bring the necklace back when you have used it elsewhere, and tell me where it went.** *What the NPC says* (after the Laurent loan has run cleanly and the party brings news of an adjacent need — Andras's stolen crown wake, an Eric Randell ghost in a relic crypt, Storm Mage restoration at the shrine): *"You have brought it back twice now. Sit, then. The same conditions: the piece returns; the work is told to me at this table. I am not lending it for collection. I am lending it because the work is the same work, and Felicia attends it. Tell me which it is this time. Tell me what is at stake."* — *Where:* the cave chapel for the asking and the return; **the long road west** with **Andras** if the stolen crown is the work (`doc/npcs/andras.md`); the **relic crypt** if **Eric Randell**'s ghost is the work (`doc/npcs/eric_randell.md`); the **shrine itself** if **Storm Mage / Ornalis** is being brought into coherence (`doc/npcs/storm_mage.md`). — *What:* ask separately each time, with the work named plainly. Carry the necklace, do the work the loan was given for, return it on the agreed day, and give the account at the table. Do not chain loans without the return between them. — *Reward:* a working channel between Willow and the party that holds across the campaign. Each adjacent loan deepens both parties' read of what Felicia attends and what she does not. Standing rises; the late-game line becomes more available. — *Complications:* she will refuse loans that are *not* the same work. A request to use the necklace as a generic spirit-tool gets *"That is not what it is for; that is what other workings are for; the answer is no."* She does not negotiate; she has already thought about it. **Each loan carries the same return-and-tell condition** — a party that begins to treat the loans as routine loses the channel. **The Storm Mage restoration is the highest-stakes loan** — if Ornalis comes back into coherence and gives the names of the old-order mages who broke him, the conversation belongs to Willow's table first, and to Wilder's notebook second.

- **Later-game — bring me the candidate I can name as my successor.** *What the NPC says* (only when the party has been a steady presence at the shrine across the campaign, and only when the topic of Willow's age comes up — she does not raise it, but she will not deflect it either): *"Not yet named. I'll know her when I meet her. The granny before me knew me when she met me; that is how it is done. There are candidates in the world. I am not talking about them. If you bring one to me, I will know in the first hour whether she is the one. I will not tell her so on the first day. I will sit her down, give her a flower, and start the slow work."* — *Where:* the **cave chapel below the gate** for the meeting that decides; the **maze garden's herb plots** for the second day's sitting if Willow says *yes*; **Luchebert as a whole** for the years that follow if the candidate consents to the work. The candidate herself — most likely **Irna Bean** if she reaches Luchebert and is recognised as the true prophecy's subject (`doc/npcs/irna_bean.md` §"Role as a potential spirit-shield"); a younger rabbit-folk apprentice if the line continues that way; another tradition's candidate if the campaign opens it. — *What:* bring the candidate to the chapel, on her own terms, with no pressure. Let Willow do the looking. **Carry the candidate's actual life with you** — Irna's recovery, her bargain with her exile, her own trust in the party — because Willow will read it through the candidate's bearing. If Willow accepts, broker the slow handover work that is years, not weeks. — *Reward:* the line continues. The shrine's continuous human response to Felicia's drive is not interrupted. The hill country's quietest piece of safety persists past Willow's life. **Felicia's late-game name-the-next-granny quest closes here through Willow's hands** (`doc/npcs/felicia.md`). — *Complications:* **the candidate must consent**, knowing what the work is. Willow will not name a candidate who has been pushed there. Irna in particular cannot be conscripted — she is on her own road, and the work she is doing for her family must clear before the shrine can ask her anything. If Willow looks and says *not this one,* she means it; pushing back loses standing across the campaign. **A wrong granny breaks the line**; the next attempt is much harder.

- **Later-game — help hold the southern spirit-corridor, if Path 2 opens.** *Conditional — opens if the campaign runs the **Brumal-resolution spiritual path** (`doc/claude/BRUMAL_RESOLUTIONS.md` §"Path 2: Spiritual") and Felicia's drive extends along the southern border to hold the desert back.* *What the NPC says* (in private, in the chapel, when the party has brought back news that the readers along the southern line are being asked to do work they cannot do alone): *"I will not move from the shrine. Felicia's centre is here. What I can do — what I have done before, in pieces — is train the women and men who will hold the corridor where it needs holding. Bring them to me one at a time. I will give them what I can in the months I have. They will go where they are needed. I will stay where I am needed."* — *Where:* the **cave chapel** for the training — Willow does not travel; the corridor's named points along the southern edge — **Wilder**'s hilltop on the western edge (`doc/npcs/wilder.md`); the **druidic circles** Diederik can introduce; **Fata morgana**'s mighty-spirit-readers on the southern reach (`doc/places/fata_morgana.md`); **Penelope**'s late-game refugee work which seeds the corridor on the Brumal side (`doc/npcs/princess_penelope.md`). — *What:* identify the candidates the corridor needs — younger practitioners with the temperament for shrine-and-channel work — and bring them to Luchebert, one at a time, on Willow's pace; carry her teaching outward as each candidate completes their months at her side; coordinate between her and Wilder so neither is asked to hold a piece the other should be holding. — *Reward:* the corridor stands as a multi-NPC working with Willow at one of its anchor points. The desert's chaos pressure does not press north. Felicia's drive extends, in the way her drive does, *along the work* itself; **Felicia's late-game spirit-corridor quest closes here** through Willow's training. — *Complications:* Willow's hands are *tiring*. She will say so once and not raise it again. The training-of-others is a real cost on top of her existing shrine work; if the campaign does not also resolve the successor question, the corridor's training arm collapses with Willow when she goes. **The corridor is a multi-NPC working** — one collapsing piece (Wilder lost; an unrepaired Moth Hollow violation; Diederik unable to introduce the druids fast enough) collapses the rest. **Willow does not direct the corridor; she only trains for it.** A party that wants strategic command of the work is asking the wrong person.

## How she reads at a glance

- An old rabbit-folk in earth-coloured wool. Small — rabbit-folk small — and never hurrying.
- **Always has flowers with her.** A tray, a basket, a bundle in her apron; one in her hand. Fresh ones. She does not put them down easily.
- **Hands stained** with soil and the sap of cut stems. Fingertips darker than the rest of her fur.
- **Steady gaze.** She looks *at* the visitor, not through them. Does not look away first.
- **A small knife at her belt** for cutting flower-stems. It is also a knife.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **Walks down to the cave chapel at dawn and at dusk**, with flowers and candles. She comes back up. She does not invite company.
- **At the herb plots in the maze garden** through much of the morning — choosing stems for the day's shrine, talking to whoever else is tending. The chapel's flowers come from here.
- **At her flower-work table inside the chapel** between the dawn and dusk visits, when the day allows.
- **Sits with Father Elliot at the chapel** some evenings, after the dusk visit. They do not talk. They are companionable in the silence.

## At Felicia's shrine

The cave chapel below the monastery's gate, set into the hillside on the path back down toward the hills. Visitors pass it on entry without necessarily noticing it. Willow walks down twice a day. (For the chapel as a *place* — the cave, the spring, the candles on rock-shelves, the standouts on entry — see `doc/places/felicia_shrine.md`.)

What the party finds here when Willow lets them in:

- **The shrine at the back of the cave**, set on a low natural ledge. Fresh flowers, candles, a few small preserved objects that mean something only to someone who has been here long enough. The flowers last longer than they should. The party walking up to it will understand, without being told, that *this is what the monastery is for.*
- **Willow's flower-work table just inside the entrance** — old wood, worn smooth by decades of her hands. A small knife, trimmings in a pile, a shallow bowl of water from the chapel spring. The shrine's maintenance is visible. It is not a museum piece; it is a living practice.
- **The quiet, and the cold.** Caves are colder than the air outside. This one is colder than even that, for reasons the party may not yet recognise. A party with any spirit-awareness (*Shamanic*, priestly practice) will feel the difference: Felicia's presence concentrates here.

**The necklace is on the shrine.** A party may not notice it on a first visit. Willow notices who notices.

## Stories she tells

When the relevant topic comes up and she has decided to answer.

### When asked about Saint Luchebert — *the first priest*

> *"A good first priest. Ridiculed by the mages, heard to by everyone else. He had a holy book from the other side of the portal — old, carried through by someone who thought to bring it — and he made up his own version of what it said. That is what he **was**: a keeper of ancient knowledge from the world the first humans came from. He died long before the rift. His tradition lasted because people liked being listened to, and because what he taught was flexible enough to receive what came later. The library Elliot works in is the same tradition — the same old books, still being copied out before they fall apart. I am what came after. He would be pleased."*

### When asked about Felicia — *the shape of my days* (trust-gated)

> *"She is not a person to describe. She is the shape of my days. I am the shape of hers. I have felt her answer. I have watched her arrangements move through the hill country for as long as I have been old enough to watch. She was fear for the people she could not save, and she still is. I am one of the ones she did save, in the sense of a great spirit saving someone — she found me a shrine, and a work, and a line of grannies to hand it to. I tend her. She tends me. That is all."*

### When asked about the grannies before her — *the continuity*

> *"Names you do not need. Work you can feel in the shrine's continuity. They were here; they did this; they handed it to me; I will hand it to the next. Elliot's tradition goes on paper. Mine goes through hands. Both are true."*

### When asked about Father Elliot's arrival — *he came knowing*

> *"He came into my work. I did not teach him what the shrine is; he arrived knowing. What I gave him was continuity — the names of the flowers that go on the shrine, the days that matter, the small rites that were here before he was. He has been worthy. I did not defer to his princely past. He did not interrupt my shrine work. That is the agreement."*

### When asked about the necklace — *what it does* (trust-gated)

> *"The piece on the shrine is not a sentiment. It **is** a working. It can condense Felicia's presence near someone she cannot otherwise reach. You will want it for Laurent; that is why parties ask after it. I can lend it, if I decide to. I will want it back. I will want to know what you said to him when you used it."*

### When asked about her successor — *I'll know her when I meet her*

> *"Not yet named. I'll know her when I meet her. The granny before me knew me when she met me; that is how it is done. There are candidates in the world. I am not talking about them."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — does not answer faster. Keeps working — the flowers move between her hands at the same unhurried pace. May not look up. The answer, when it comes, is *"No."* The gatekeeper's no, quiet and absolute.
- **When distraught** — sits down. Puts the flowers in her lap. Does not speak until her hands are still. The stillness may take a long time.
- **When happy** — smiles with her whole face. **Gives the speaker a flower from her tray.** Says *"Sit, then."* Means sit *here, with me.*

## If the party does something — what she does

| If the party... | She responds by... |
|---|---|
| Tries to walk down to the cave chapel without invitation | Intercepts on the path or at the chapel entrance. *"Do not defile my life's work."* They go no further until she permits. |
| Asks her directly about Felicia on first meeting | *"You are asking too soon."* Does not elaborate. The topic is closed for that visit. |
| **Steals the necklace or anything else from the shrine** | **Curses them.** Draining curse — gradual immobilisation over hours then days. No warning given; no reversal without her or a comparably capable practitioner. See §"The draining curse" below. |
| Brings her fresh-cut flowers (sincerely, not as a bribe) | Notices. Accepts. May let them sit with her while she works. This is the start of the long path to trust. |
| Offers to help with shrine maintenance | Yes, carefully. Hands them a small task. Watches how they do it. The task is a test; she knows. |
| Lies to her about their reasons for being at the monastery | She knows. Says nothing. The conversation is over; the path to trust is reset. |
| Brings Irna Bean to her | Looks at Irna for a long time. *"Sit with me, child."* The relationship begins. She will not explain why, yet. |
| Asks about her successor | *"Not yet named. I'll know her when I meet her."* Will not speculate. (The party may not realise she has *just started wondering* about Irna.) |
| Threatens her or the shrine | Does not fight. Does not run. Puts down her flowers and stands. The curse is ready. The party realises this by her stillness, and may or may not back off in time. |
| Offers her sword-arm protection for the shrine | Declines. *"I have what I need. Thank you."* Means it. |
| Returns the necklace after using it as agreed | Accepts it back with both hands. Asks what they said to Laurent. Listens. Does not tell them what she thinks. |

## The draining curse

Willow's working, deployed against shrine-thieves:

- **Trigger:** theft from Felicia's shrine. Any item, any amount, any intent. Intent does not matter.
- **Effect:** the thief slows, gradually, over hours and then days. They grow heavy. Their movements take effort, then more effort, then impossible effort. Eventually they cannot move at all.
- **Lethality:** not lethal on its own. The immobility eventually kills if not reversed, because the cursed body cannot feed itself.
- **Reversal:** Willow can undo it. A comparably capable practitioner can undo it with patient work. Nothing else. A party racing to find a reversal before their friend stops moving entirely is the usual shape of the crisis.

She does not warn. She does not perform it. The party will not know they have been cursed until the slowing begins.

## What she can teach

- **Flower-craft** — what to put on a shrine, when to replace it, what each flower means in the granny tradition. Slow. Requires patience.
- **Patience at a shrine** — not a power; a practice. Students learn it by sitting with her, not by being taught.
- **Reading whether a great spirit is near** — by small signs, temperature, the way flowers last. Rare; she teaches this only to those genuinely tending a shrine.
- **Granny witch-craft** — only to a student she has chosen as successor. This is not a course; it is the work itself, handed over across years.

## What she will not say

- **The names of the grannies before her.** Their work continued through her; their names are not a matter of public record.
- **Whether she has chosen her successor.** She watches. She does not yet commit.
- **The mechanism of the draining curse.** If asked, she does not answer. Not because it is a secret, exactly — because explaining it to the wrong person is its own defeat.
- **Exactly how Felicia speaks to her.** The spirit is a companion; the private language between them is private.

## DM notes

- The **Felicia-truth reveal** gates through Willow. Sustained, earned standing across multiple visits is the only path. A party that tries to shortcut with clever deductions or a single probing question will not get it.
- The **necklace** is on her shrine. She knows what it is and what it does. Taking it without her consent triggers the draining curse; earning her trust produces a loan, possibly a gift. The loan is conditional on returning it and *telling her what the user said to Laurent.*
- **The curse is campaign-significant.** Gradual, immobilising, hard to undo without her. Do not deploy lightly; the consequence is serious and the race to find a reversal is its own arc.
- **Her successor is the next arc's question.** Irna Bean is the obvious candidate if she reaches Luchebert and is recognised as the true prophecy's subject. Willow will not say so openly; she may be the first to see it. See `doc/npcs/irna_bean.md` §"Role as a potential spirit-shield."
- The **speech pattern** — *"Do not defile my life's work," "Sit, then," "Not yet named. I'll know her when I meet her,"* calling younger people *child* — is hers. A DM running her should reuse these phrases verbatim across visits; repetition is part of her presence.

## Role as spirit-shield (partial)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," the Luchebert vale is held by overlapping mighty-spirit presence: Felicia herself as the great spirit, and Father Elliot as her serving priest. **Willow adds a third layer** — she is not a priest in the formal sense, but she is Felicia's daily tender and the continuity of the shrine's work for decades. Chaotic spirits reading the vale find it densely covered; Willow is part of what makes that coverage redundant.

If she dies without a named successor, the shield thins. The shrine would still be Felicia's; the day-to-day tending would falter. This is part of what makes her successor question campaign-significant.

## Open questions

- Who came before Willow, and how far back does the line go?
- Does Willow have a chosen successor-in-training? If not, *who will the next one be?* This is a live campaign-adjacent question: whoever replaces her inherits a channel to Felicia. Irna, if she reaches Luchebert and is recognised as the true prophecy's subject, is an obvious candidate. That is probably not an accident.

## Relationships

- Tends **Felicia's** shrine at Luchebert; the continuous human vessel through which Felicia's drive has expressed itself in ritual for decades.
- Older than **Father Elliot**'s tenure at the monastery. He came into her continuity, not the other way around. Mutual respect; she does not defer to his princely past, and he does not interrupt her shrine work.
- Gives flowers unasked to **Brother Darrel**.
- Correspondent (indirectly) with **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** — Rhianna's letters arrive at Elliot's desk; Willow sees them.
- Part of the continuous line of older grannies (rabbit-folk and otherwise) who have handed the true saint's care from one to the next. Her own successor is not yet named.
