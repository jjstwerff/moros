# Willow

- **Role:** Old rabbit-folk granny of Luchebert Monastery; tender of Felicia's shrine; keeper of the Abbey's quiet truth
- **Species:** Rabbit folk
- **Location:** Luchebert Monastery, in a vale beyond Laurent's downed city
- **Status:** Alive; very old; not going anywhere

## Summary

Willow is the old rabbit-folk granny who has, for decades, **tended Felicia's shrine** inside Luchebert Monastery. The Abbey is officially dedicated to **Saint Luchebert** — the saint whose name the monastery and its vale carry. Those ties are loose and mostly ceremonial. The rites that matter, the shrine that is actually kept, the devotion that shapes the monastery's sheltering work — all of that runs through Felicia, and day-to-day it runs through Willow.

She predates **Father Elliot**'s arrival at the monastery. When Elliot came, she was already there, already tending, already knowing. She did not teach him Felicia's identity; he arrived knowing. What she gave him was the *continuity* — the patient accumulation of small rites, the names of flowers that go on the shrine, the days that matter, the quiet way the order's elders have been handing the true saint's care from one granny to the next long before any prince came to ask where to put his grief.

She **does not trust outsiders.** She actively keeps strangers away from the shrine. Approaches by unknown parties get polite non-answers at best; pushy approaches get a rebuke that makes clear she sees what the shrine is as *her life's work* and has no intention of letting it be defiled by people who have not earned standing with her. *"Do not defile my life's work"* is an actual phrasing she has used, and she has meant it each time.

This is not reflexive suspicion of travellers; it is centuries of care accumulated in one place, handed to her by the grannies before her, and she will not risk it on strangers' curiosity. Time alone does not earn access. Sustained, careful, demonstrated respect for what the monastery is and what she is tending — across visits, not through a single conversation — does. The party must *prove* to her that they can be trusted to hear the truth about the Abbey without treating it as a piece of information to spread. Until then, she keeps them at the door.

If that trust is earned — genuinely earned, not bargained for — she will sit with them at her shrine and tell them what they are standing in front of. The reveal is a reward for patience *and* integrity, not for mere patience.

Asked about Saint Luchebert himself, Willow has an answer, told with the affection one reserves for a long-dead figure whose work still gives one's own life shape: Luchebert was one of the first human priests in the white city, ridiculed by the mages and heard to by everyone else. He had found an ancient holy book from the other side of the portal, read it, and made up his own version of the religion it described. He died long before the rift. The tradition he started persisted because people liked being listened to, and because his framework was flexible enough that it could *receive* a great spirit later, when the time came. That later reception is Willow's own work.

## Role in the campaign

Willow is the **revelation character** for the Felicia truth at Luchebert. In the campaign's opening the monastery will appear to be dedicated to whoever the official saint is. Only through sustained, earned engagement with its people — not clever deductions, not one probing question, but *time* — does the party eventually meet Willow, sit with her, and learn that the angel of Luchebert is the ghost of a woman who died at Laurent's crash and who stayed.

She is also the living example of **how Felicia's drive (fear for the people she tried to save) has been sustained in ritual form for generations**. Without Willow and the grannies before her, the drive would have had no regular human vessel to reach; with them, it has had one continuous voice in the hill country for centuries. If she dies without a successor at the shrine, that line breaks — and Felicia becomes harder to reach, even as her presence remains in the arrangements she laid.

## Relationships

- Tends **Felicia's** shrine at Luchebert; by implication a channel through which Felicia's drive has expressed itself in ritual for decades.
- Older than **Father Elliot**'s tenure at the monastery; he came into her continuity, not the other way round. Mutual respect. She does not defer to his princely past; he does not interrupt her shrine work.
- Part of the continuous line of older grannies (rabbit-folk and otherwise, presumably) who have handed the true saint's care from one to the next. Her own successor is not yet named.

## Open questions

- Who came before Willow, and how far back does the line go?
- Does Willow have a chosen successor-in-training? If not, *who will the next one be?* This is a live campaign-adjacent question: whoever replaces Willow inherits a channel to Felicia. Irna, if she reaches Luchebert and is recognised as the true prophecy's subject, is an obvious candidate. That is probably not an accident.
