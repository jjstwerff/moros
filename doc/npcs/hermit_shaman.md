# Gerhald (the Hermit Shaman)

- **Role:** Otter shaman; current owner and inhabitant of the hill tower at the Lake of Tears; holder of the consent-veto on the last shackle
- **Species:** Otter (animal-person)
- **Location:** The hill tower above the Lake of Tears — the only one of the six towers in the ring that was *always there*, not moved into place
- **Status:** Alive; reclusive; knowledgeable; not sharing

## Summary

An old otter shaman who has lived, quietly, in the hill tower above the Lake of Tears for longer than he will say. He is **not** the tower's original mage — he came to it later, took possession of it, and has held it since. The difference matters, because the tower is dangerous: it was built as a **blood tower** whose first mage fed it by slaughtering an animal every day, or he himself would have been drained. Gerhald found a gentler solution. He bound a **special spirit that mimics an animal** — close enough to the tower's blood-working that it satisfies the feed, without anything actually dying. The curse is broken. The tower still runs.

He does not share how. He does not share the tower's other secrets either. The blood-tower tradition is not something he wants back in the world, and a party that comes to him asking will get careful answers, or no answers, depending on how they asked.

## The anchor tower

The five *other* towers in the ring were seized and moved by the old-order mages to form the cage that brought Laurent's flying city down. Gerhald's tower was not. It stood on this hill before any of that happened, and the old-order mages placed their five stolen towers around it because the hill made a natural anchor. That makes his tower the **sixth point of the cage** and, functionally, its anchor.

It also makes Gerhald's shackle different from the other five. The other towers leave the ring by their own levers — the pot-boy walks out with the furnace following; the volcano tower lifts on the fire elemental's energy; the sinking tower floats down-river; the darkness tower moves by the mosaic's mechanic; the Storm Mage is brought back within reach of the Lightning Tower. All five are *movement* levers. Gerhald's tower does not move; it has always been here. His shackle opens only by his **consent**. Even if the party breaks all five of the other shackles, the last one stays shut unless Gerhald agrees to lift it.

That makes him the single most important NPC for the last-flight climax. His agreement is the final gate, and he is not a man who agrees easily.

## What he sees from the hill

Gerhald's hill gives him the best natural vantage of the other towers in the ring. Decades of sitting on his own doorstep have made him familiar with the **pattern of the other towers' behaviour** from the outside, even though he has never entered any of them.

The tower he sees most is **the sinking tower** (see `doc/npcs/sinking_tower.md`), the closest to his own. He does not know its *specifics* — he has never been inside, never clung to it during a descent, does not know about the air-filled lower half or the bound owner-spirit. But he has observed one thing consistently: **the tower sinks below every night**, and as it goes down it gives off a **visible glow on the water**, the glow reaching the lakebed when the tower meets its counterpart.

He will tell the party this if asked. He will not speculate further than he has observed. The words *reaches its counterpart* are his; he takes them to mean the tower has a paired half below the waterline, but he has never confirmed it. For a party puzzling over how to engage with the sinking tower, his observation is a **hint without a solution** — they learn there is something below, that it glows at night, that the descent is regular. They still have to work out for themselves that clinging to the tower through the sink is the way in.

## Role in the campaign

Negotiation-first, patience-heavy, knowledge-guarded. By the time the party reaches Gerhald they will already have worked on the other five shackles; his view of them is shaped entirely by how they have handled those. A party that treated the pot-boy with care, approached the fire elemental with respect rather than force, earned the bound spirit's trust in the sinking tower, and did not shortcut the darkness puzzle will find Gerhald at least willing to *listen*. A party that took the dangerous options — enraging the volcano elemental, forcing the Storm Mage into contact with his tower without meaningful consent — will find him harder to reach.

He is not unreachable for such a party either. He has lived long enough to know that people's choices are not their whole shape. But the bar is higher, and he will want to understand *why* the choices were made before agreeing to something this large.

## He wants specifics before he cooperates

Conduct-on-the-ring is the first gate, not the last. **Gerhald will not agree to lift his shackle on the strength of good behaviour alone.** Before he cooperates, he wants the party to come to him with *specifics*:

- **What do you propose to do with the freed elemental?** If the plan is *let the last flight happen and take Laurent's city somewhere*, where, and why *that* destination? The overseas continent, the shifting lands, the blasted lands for the shamans' plan — each answer has different weight, and he wants the reasoning, not the slogan.
- **What about the rain-buffer?** The Lake of Tears holds back the desert for the inland area; freeing the elemental dismantles that buffer. Has the party thought about it? Do they have a plan for the region — Elmsfield, the hill country, Felicia's arrangements — once the buffer is gone? *They don't have to have a solution*, but they have to have *thought about it*. A party that arrives not having considered the cost has not earned his agreement, regardless of how well they treated the other towers.
- **Who else is on board?** Is this a party acting alone, or are they carrying anyone else's weight? A shaman's endorsement (Wilder would count), Laurent's agreement on the destination, Willow's blessing, the pot-boy and the furnace's willingness to travel — any of these matter. He will not spend his agreement on a solo run.

The purpose is not interrogation. It is **making sure the party has done the thinking**. Gerhald has lived long enough to know that good intentions followed by improvisation are how large workings go wrong. He will not hand over the last shackle to someone who has not yet decided what to do with it.

A party that arrives having thought it through — with destinations, contingencies for the rain-buffer, other mighty-spirit-readers on board — finds Gerhald ready to listen in earnest. A party that arrives with "*we are here to free the elemental*" and no answers to the next question finds him patient but immovable.

## Role as spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," an old shaman is precisely the kind of being chaotic spirits back off from — a practitioner whose lifelong work has been spirit-negotiation, and who therefore reads as mighty-adjacent. Gerhald is almost certainly holding chaotic-spirit pressure out of his hill and possibly out of a broader stretch of the Lake-of-Tears ring. That protection is layered on top of Laurent (in the hills), Felicia (at Luchebert), and the pot-people (at the buried tower). The lake region's unusual stability is not an accident; it is the densest concentration of mighty-spirit-readers anywhere near the kingdoms, and Gerhald is one of them.

## Relationships

- Current owner of the hill tower above the Lake of Tears. He took possession of it after the original blood-tower mage was gone (died, left, or was removed — the history is Gerhald's to tell, or not).
- Bound to the **animal-mimic spirit** that now feeds the tower in place of the daily sacrifice.
- A natural potential ally to the **pot-people** of the buried tower, to **Laurent** in the wreck, and to anyone else the old-order faction wronged — though his tower was not one of the wronged ones, so his grievance is different in shape.
- Holds a view at a distance of the party's actions at the other five towers, which becomes his basis for the consent decision.

## Open questions

- The animal-mimic spirit — its provenance, its name, its relationship to Gerhald. If he ever releases it (through death, choice, or accident), does the blood-tower curse resume?
- What Gerhald wants that would move him to consent, beyond the party's general conduct.
- Whether he knows more about the fungus and the bound air elemental than he has shared — he almost certainly does.
- How long he has actually been here.
