# Gerhald (the Hermit Shaman)

- **Role:** Otter shaman; current inhabitant of the hill tower at the Lake of Tears; **holder of the consent-veto on the last shackle** — without his agreement, the cage around the bound air elemental does not come fully apart.
- **Species:** Otter (animal-person). Old. Water-folk by species, hill-dweller by choice.
- **Lives:** The hill tower above the Lake of Tears — the only one of the six towers in the ring that was *always there*, not seized and moved by the old-order mages. He is not the tower's original mage; he took possession later. The history is his to tell, or not.
- **First met:** When the party climbs the hill to his tower — almost always late in the campaign, after they have engaged with the other five towers. He does not come down.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Otter shaman; sole holder of the hill tower (the only one in the ring that was *always there*); **consent-veto on the last shackle**; one of the densest layers of the lake region's spirit-shield ring (alongside Laurent, Felicia, the pot-people).
- **Standouts:** *An old otter, sleek-fur grey-streaked at the muzzle and tail*, in plain shaman's wool. **A small stone on a string** turning slowly between his fingers — always; slows when he listens, stops when he is distraught. **A water basin at his feet** he touches when thinking. **Does not stand for visitors** — long-post habit, watching door and lake at once.
- **Speech tag:** Slow, short, no ceremony. Opens identically: *"You've been to the others. Sit down. Tell me how it went."* Listens to the report in order — pot-boy and furnace, volcano, darkness, sinking, Lightning Tower / Ornalis. *"Yes."* *"Good."* are his strongest signs of approval.
- **Quests:** Early — *give him the report on the other five towers, and bring the three specifics he requires before consent on the sixth becomes real (rain-buffer, destination, who else is on board).* Late — *broker his meeting with Wilder; help solve the post's succession; stay clear of the animal-mimic feed.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *Reclusive but not hostile.* He does not soften for charm; he softens for *work well done elsewhere* and *specifics thought through*. Asking him to describe the **animal-mimic feed** is a clean refusal — do not return to it. Threatening triggers the bound spirit's reaction (water rises in the basin, stone turns faster, hill does not permit them to stay), not him. **Will not commit consent on the spot** — he sleeps on it.

## What the party meets

The party climbs the hill. The tower is old, plain, lower than the other five around the lake — a structure that belongs to this ground rather than standing over it. The door is unlocked. The party pushes it open.

An old otter looks up from where he is sitting — a low chair, a water basin at his feet, a small stone on a string turning slowly between his fingers. He does not stand.

> *"You've been to the others. Sit down. Tell me how it went."*

That is his opening. It is not an invitation to boast. He will listen carefully and in order — pot-boy and furnace first, volcano, darkness, sinking, Lightning Tower / Ornalis. He wants to hear *how* each went, not just *that* the shackle came off. What the party says, and how they say it, is most of the conversation.

## Personal goals — early and later

He has held one post for longer than is reasonable. The two questions still on his desk are the consent on the sixth shackle (an early-game gate the campaign's last-flight climax depends on) and the post's succession (deferred until the consent is answered).

- **Early-game — give me the report on the other five, and bring me the three specifics for the sixth.** *What the NPC says* (when the party first reaches his door): *"You've been to the others. Sit down. Tell me how it went. Pot-boy and furnace first. Then volcano, darkness, sinking, Lightning Tower. I will listen in order. After that I want three things from you, and not before. The rain-buffer the lake gives you against the desert — what happens to it when the elemental is freed. The destination of the last flight — where the city is going and why that destination earns the pledge. And who else is on board — Wilder's endorsement, Laurent's agreement, Willow's blessing. Bring all three. Reasoned. In one conversation. I will not commit on the spot. I will sleep on it. Come back the next morning."* — *Where:* the **hill tower** above the Lake of Tears for the report and the specifics conversation; **Laurent's audience hall** for his agreement (`doc/npcs/laurent.md`) — Laurent does not consent to a destination that does not earn the pledge's weight; **Wilder's hilltop** at the western edge for the endorsement (`doc/npcs/wilder.md`); **Felicia's shrine at Luchebert** with **Willow** for the blessing (`doc/npcs/willow.md`, `doc/npcs/felicia.md`); a **conversation with Hannes** or his quartermasters about what the rain-buffer's loss does to the southern frontier (`doc/places/geography.md` §"The Lake as rain-buffer"); the **other five towers** to actually finish the work being reported (`doc/npcs/pot_person.md`, `doc/places/volcano_tower.md`, `doc/places/darkness_tower.md`, `doc/places/sinking_tower.md`, `doc/npcs/storm_mage.md`). — *What:* clear the five towers in their levers, not by force; come to the hill with the work done; give the report in order, with no boasting; bring the three specifics in one conversation, each reasoned and named with the people whose endorsement it required. Sleep elsewhere overnight. Return the next morning for his answer. — *Reward:* **his consent on the last shackle**. With it the cage around the bound air elemental fully opens at the moment the party chooses; the **last flight** becomes possible (`doc/npcs/laurent.md` late-game; `doc/npcs/felicia.md` *carry the necklace to Laurent at his parting*). Without it the cage does not fully come apart, regardless of the other five. Standing earned with the lake region's tightest old spirit-reader. — *Complications:* a party arriving with destructive solutions at the other five towers (enraged elementals, forced Ornalis into contact, looted shrines) is not turned away; he is patient and wants to *understand why*. The bar is higher; the conversation lengthens by visits. **Bringing the three specifics half-formed loses the morning** — he does not finish the conversation he has not yet started. Attempting to open the last shackle without his consent fails; he knows they tried, and the consent conversation afterwards is harder. **A party that surfaces the three specifics with the wrong people** (a courtier hearing the rain-buffer question; a wrong druid hearing Willow's name) costs the work standing it cannot recover from cheaply.

- **Later-game — broker my meeting with Wilder, and help solve the succession.** *Conditional — opens once the consent question is answered (in either direction; even a refused consent leaves the deferred questions on his desk).* *What the NPC says* (in the working room, water basin at his feet, after the consent conversation has run its course): *"Two pieces left. The first: I have not met **Wilder**. We should have. We haven't. We are on the same chaos-spirit axis at opposite edges of what can be held; if we met we would recognise each other in the first minute. You have been to him. You have been to me. You can carry the question. Where, when, what register. He is not coming down off his hill any more easily than I am off mine. The second is harder. I have not trained a successor. The post needs a holder; if it is empty for long, the blood-tower curse resumes — and I have not put the workings of it into the world to teach. The succession is a real problem. I have thought about it. I have not solved it."* — *Where:* **Wilder's hilltop** at the western edge for the carry (`doc/npcs/wilder.md` late-game *broker my meeting with Gerhald*); a **neutral venue** if neither will travel — **Diederik's ironwood** at Elmsfield, **Felicia's shrine** if Willow agrees and the meeting needs the great-spirit register, or the hill tower itself if Wilder is willing to climb; the **shamans' loose network** for a candidate to take the post (DM-fill on the wider network's reach); the hill tower for the slow handover work that is years, not weeks, if a successor is found. — *What:* carry Gerhald's question to Wilder verbatim (no editorial), carry Wilder's answer back the same way; broker the venue if both agree to meet; do not stage-manage what they say to each other; for the succession, surface to Gerhald the candidates the shamans' network produces, or — if a party member has earned deep standing and is willing to take a long-post — bring that conversation as well. — *Reward:* two old shamans on the same axis in a room together for the first time in either's life. What they decide together — about coordination, about the **shamans' long plan**'s pace, about whether the **last flight** undoing the cage is part of the plan or a separate climax — reshapes the campaign's late shape. **A successor named** keeps the lake region's spirit-shield ring intact past Gerhald's life; the blood-tower curse does not resume. — *Complications:* both shamans have reasons of their own for not having moved (see each file); the meeting cannot be forced. **Asking Gerhald to describe the animal-mimic feed**, even in service of the succession, gets a clean refusal — *"some workings should not come back."* He will teach the *principles* of negotiating with bound spirits; he will not teach the working that runs his tower. A party that wants to *solve* the blood-tower problem cleanly is going to find the problem does not have a clean solution; the file is honest about this. **Removing Gerhald from the tower threatens the same outcome as letting the spirit go untended** — succession is the only safe path forward.

## How he reads at a glance

- An **old otter-folk** — medium build, sleek fur streaked grey at the muzzle and tail. Not showy. Plain shaman's wool.
- **A small stone on a string**, turning slowly between his fingers. Always. The stone is a working-aid; a spirit-aware party will feel a working in it they cannot quite read.
- **Eyes used to watching water and hill both.** Moves between the two without effort.
- **Does not stand** for visitors. Not rudeness; a long-post habit. Stays where he can watch the door and the lake at once.
- **A water basin at his feet**, always. The otter-folk need for water is real, but the basin is also part of his working. He touches the water when he is thinking.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **Watches the other towers from his door at dawn and dusk.** The daily perimeter is the ring of five, from his vantage. He has done this for longer than he will say.
- **Feeds the animal-mimic spirit each morning, quietly.** The ritual is small, indoors, and he does not explain it. A party that observes without interrupting may catch enough to understand it is a feed without being able to describe the working.
- **Does not leave the tower for long.** Not at all if the weather is against him. If he is gone, the animal-mimic spirit is unfed, and the blood-tower curse pulls closer to resuming.
- **Works the stone** through his fingers constantly. The motion slows when he is listening closely; stops altogether when he is distraught.

## At the hill tower

A plain stone tower, lower than the other five around the lake. It looks like it belongs to the ground rather than standing over it — which it does, because it is the only tower in the ring that has always been here. The party reaches it by climbing the hill above the lake's eastern shore.

Three things stand out on entering:

- **The interior is a working room, not a home.** A chair, a basin, a table. No bed visible (he sleeps elsewhere in the tower); no display; no personal effects a party would recognise as sentimental. The room is kept the way a craftsman keeps a workshop.
- **Water runs through a shallow runnel along one wall**, fed from somewhere inside the hill and drained out somewhere below. The tower has running water older than the kingdoms. This is part of why an otter took the post.
- **The window above his chair looks out on the five other towers.** A party standing there gets the same view Gerhald gets every day — the ring visible as a ring, the lake at its centre, the fungus visible where it has grown thick.

The tower's *other* secrets — where the blood-tower working lives, where the animal-mimic spirit sleeps, how the feed is performed — are not in this room. They are elsewhere in the tower and Gerhald does not show them.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the old-order mages' taking of the five towers — *what he watched from here*

> *"I was here when they brought the five in. It was the work of a season — slow, deliberate, the sort of moving that takes five mages and their puppets and their animal-teams, and the five towers coming one at a time from wherever they were. They placed the ring around my hill because the hill was the anchor. They did not speak to me. I did not offer to speak to them. They finished. They went. Within the month the fungus began at the lake edge. I have watched it since."*

### When asked about the sinking tower — *what he has seen* (the hint)

> *"The closest of the five to me is the one that sinks. Every night, without exception, it goes below the water. I have watched this for decades. As it goes, there is a **glow on the water** — visible from my door — that reaches the lakebed when the tower meets its counterpart. I use those words — *its counterpart* — because they match what I have observed. I have never been inside the tower. I have never confirmed what is below. I have only noted that there is *something there*, and that the descent is regular, and that the glow is the same colour every time. The rest is yours to work out."*

### When asked about the blood-tower history — *carefully*

> *"The tower was a blood tower. Its first mage fed it by slaughtering an animal every day, or the tower would have drained him instead. That is the shape of the working. I found a gentler method. I will not describe it to you. I will say only that it is holding, and that if I am gone from this tower for long, it does not hold. That is as much as I will put into the world."*

### When asked about his own tradition — *briefly*

> *"I am a shaman. Otter-folk, water-adjacent. My tradition is shamanic the way most shamans' traditions are shamanic — practice, patience, apprenticeship. I trained with people I will not name to you. I have not taken a student here. The post does not permit it. That is part of why I consider its eventual succession a real problem."*

### When asked what he wants after the last flight — *his thinking*

> *"If the cage around the elemental comes off and the flying city takes its last journey, the ring around my hill is gone. What I hold ceases to be a post against that cage. The tower is still the blood tower; it still needs feeding; the question of whether I hold it or someone else does becomes a question I can actually entertain. Until then, I am not going to think about what comes after. Finish the first question first. Come back with the answer."*

### When asked who his closest neighbour is — *the pot-people*

> *"There is a small puppet in the buried tower to my west. And the tower itself — the furnace — is also one. Two beings, very old, very quiet. I have not spoken to either. I have *noticed* them, over the years, as one notices another craftsman on the next hill. If the boy walks out and the furnace follows, I will know. I will be glad in my own way."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — slows down. Works the stone on the string more deliberately. The silences lengthen. He does not fill them.
- **When distraught** — goes to the water basin, touches the water, does not speak. The stone stops turning. A party should wait through this; he comes back.
- **When at ease** — a small head-tilt, a direct look, a single word. *"Yes."* *"Good."* The stone turns steadily. This is his strongest sign of approval; it means the conversation may continue.

## What he can teach

For students who have earned standing — usually through sustained work in the lake region, not through a single visit:

- **Shamanic practice** — at depth. Water-shaman register specifically; he is otter-folk and the craft has that grain.
- **Negotiating with bound spirits** — his own working with the animal-mimic spirit is the most refined version of this on the continent. He will teach the *principles* without teaching the specific working that runs his tower.
- **Long-post patience** — the discipline of a practitioner who has held a single place for a lifetime. Informal; by example.
- **Lake-reading** — the specifics of the Lake of Tears as a working-site. Where the fungus is thickening, how the towers' outputs interact, what the air above the lake reveals in a particular light.
- **The shaman's long plan** — he knows it; he will discuss it. He defers to Wilder on the specifics, though, and will send a serious student on to him.

He will **not** teach the **blood-tower working**, the **animal-mimic feed**, or anything that would let another practitioner build or run a tower of this kind. *"Some workings should not come back. This one is one of them."*

## What he will not say

- **How the animal-mimic spirit works.** Closest-held secret. Not for teaching.
- **His age.** Irrelevant; he deflects.
- **What happened to the original blood-tower mage.** *"He was gone when I came."* He will not elaborate.
- **What he would do if forced from the tower.** He has thought about it. He will not put the answer into the world.
- **Whether he has a named successor in mind.** He does not. This is a real problem.

## DM notes

- **The consent-gate is the load-bearing mechanic.** Even if the party breaks all five other shackles, the cage around the air elemental does not fully open without Gerhald. The file's whole shape supports this: he will not agree on conduct alone; he requires the three *specifics* (destination, rain-buffer, who else is on board). A party that arrives thinking *"five out of six, the last is formality"* will find him patient and immovable.
- **Play him reclusive but not hostile.** He has lived alone for a long time; he is not lonely and not difficult. He listens carefully. He does not soften for charm. He softens for *work well done elsewhere* and for *specifics thought through*.
- **The stone on the string is a generative motif.** It turns constantly; it slows when he is listening; it stops when he is distraught. A DM can use the stone as the visible state-indicator without narrating emotion.
- **The sinking-tower hint is *a hint without a solution*.** He has seen the glow and the descent; he has not confirmed the lower half. The party has to work out the cling-and-ride mechanism for themselves. Do not let him say more than he has observed.
- **The blood-tower working is a permanent question.** Destroying the animal-mimic spirit resumes the curse. Removing Gerhald threatens the same outcome. A party that wants to *solve* the blood-tower problem cleanly is going to find the problem does not have a clean solution — and should not, because the DM note says: *"some workings should not come back."*
- **The succession is a real campaign thread.** Gerhald has not trained a successor. When he eventually cannot hold the post, the tower's mechanism fails, or a replacement shaman takes over. A party that has earned deep standing with him may be offered the conversation. A DM who wants a late-campaign thread here has it waiting.
- **He pairs with Wilder.** Both old shamans, both at hill posts, both holding chaos-pressure; neither has met the other. A party that has been to both will notice; both will notice in turn. If the campaign reaches the shamans' long plan in earnest, bringing Wilder and Gerhald face-to-face for the first time is a scene worth setting up.
- **The hill tower does not yet have a place file.** Single named NPC (plus the animal-mimic spirit, not a full NPC). Below the 3+ threshold. The scene material is in this file. If a successor takes the post and creates a household, that may change.

## Role as spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," an old shaman is precisely the kind of being chaotic spirits back off from — a practitioner whose lifelong work has been spirit-negotiation and who therefore reads as mighty-adjacent. Gerhald is almost certainly holding chaotic-spirit pressure out of his hill and a broader stretch of the Lake-of-Tears ring. That protection is layered on top of **Laurent** (in the hills east), **Felicia** (at Luchebert), and the **pot-people** (at the buried tower). The lake region's unusual stability is not an accident; it is the densest concentration of mighty-spirit-readers anywhere near the kingdoms, and Gerhald is one of them.

Losing him thins that cover. The campaign should account for this consequence alongside the rain-buffer one whenever the last-flight option is finalised.

## Relationships

- Current owner of the hill tower above the Lake of Tears. Took possession after the original blood-tower mage was gone. How is his to tell, or not.
- Bound to the **animal-mimic spirit** that now feeds the tower in place of the daily sacrifice. The arrangement is ongoing and discreet.
- A fellow old shaman of **Wilder** at the blasted lands' edge — though of different species and very different post. Neither has met; both would recognise the other in the first minute.
- Closest neighbour to the **pot-people** of the buried tower; watches them from the hill without ever having spoken.
- Holds a view at a distance of the party's actions at the other five towers, which becomes the basis of the consent decision.

## Open questions

- The **animal-mimic spirit** — its provenance, its name, its relationship to him. If he ever releases it (through death, choice, or accident), does the blood-tower curse resume? (He implies yes.)
- What Gerhald wants that would move him to consent, beyond the party's conduct and the three specifics. *Is there a fourth specific he hasn't named?*
- What he knows about the fungus and the bound air elemental that he has not yet shared — he almost certainly knows more than he has said.
- How long he has actually been here.
- Whether the succession problem is solvable with any party-candidate, or whether it requires the shaman network to produce one.
