# The Hermit Shaman

- **Role:** Inhabitant of one of the towers at the Lake of Tears; possible original owner, or heir to one
- **Species:** (open)
- **Location:** One of the five intact towers around the Lake of Tears
- **Status:** Alive; reclusive

## Summary

An old shaman living, quietly, in one of the five towers around the Lake of Tears that is not currently buried or burning. They understand the towers, the bound air elemental, and the faction of old-order mages who seized the towers centuries ago better than anyone the party has met so far — because the tower is theirs, or was theirs, or their lineage's, before it was taken and moved to this lake. That wound has shaped them, and they have been patient about it.

Whether they share any of this depends entirely on how the party approaches. They are a Sessions 5+ thread, not an early-session encounter. By the time the party meets them they will already have formed a view of how the group handled the buried tower — and, not incidentally, the pot-person inside it.

## Role in the campaign

Negotiation-first. They are a source of understanding — of the towers, the elemental, the faction that took them, and the long memory of the wrong done — not a combatant. Their willingness to help is a referendum on how the party has conducted itself so far, and possibly an alliance offer: their grievance and Laurent's are the same grievance, at opposite ends of the same crime.

### They are the key to another shackle

Each of the six towers around the Lake of Tears is a shackle on the bound air elemental, and each shackle breaks by *moving its tower out of the ring* through that tower's own relational lever. The buried tower's lever is the pot-boy (the furnace follows him). The Hermit Shaman's tower almost certainly has a lever shaped like **them** — their presence is what holds their tower to the ring, because their tower was taken from their lineage and they are still tending it, and a tower with no one tending it is one the elemental's binding cannot keep anchored in the same way.

The Hermit Shaman choosing to leave — with the party, for somewhere else — is plausibly how their tower's shackle breaks. That is a real decision for them, not a given. They may choose to stay. Their choice is the party's second lesson, after the pot-boy, in how the cage comes apart.

## Role as spirit-shield

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," an old shaman is precisely the kind of being chaotic spirits back off from — a practitioner whose lifelong work has been spirit-negotiation, and who therefore reads as mighty-adjacent. The Hermit Shaman is almost certainly holding chaotic-spirit pressure out of whichever tower they inhabit, and possibly out of a broader stretch of the Lake-of-Tears ring. That protection is layered on top of Laurent (in the hills) and Felicia (at Luchebert). The lake region's unusual stability is not an accident; it is the densest concentration of mighty-spirit-readers anywhere near the kingdoms, and the Hermit Shaman is one of them.

## Relationships

- Lives in one of the five intact towers around the Lake of Tears.
- Almost certainly one of the surviving former owners (or their heir / student / bound servant) of the six towers taken by the old-order mages.
- A natural potential ally to the **pot-person** of the buried tower, to **Laurent** in the wreck, and to anyone else the old-order faction wronged.
- Holds an unformed view, at a distance, of the party's actions at the buried tower.
