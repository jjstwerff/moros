# Narissa

- **Role:** **The First Mage.** A 24-year-old colonist who became the first confirmed mage after an involuntary fusion with a water-and-earth spirit pulled her under a lake (`doc/claude/LORE.md` §"The First Mage"). Her diary became the foundational text of all magical knowledge that followed and sparked the Age of Wild Magic.
- **Species:** Human (in life); long-lived past the human norm by the spirit's continued presence; lived into her hundreds.
- **Location:** Historical — the early settlements; her defining event happened on a lake that has not been mapped since (DM-fill on which; possibly an early-fed precursor of the Lake of Tears). Her cloak's last documented appearance was *somewhere in the early forests*, trailing water across dry ground; current location open.
- **Status:** Deceased centuries ago. **Her traces persist** — the diary, the cloak (with the spirit lingering inside), and the entire mage-tradition the diary set in motion.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Deceased-with-traces; the foundational figure of the world's mage practice. The party encounters her through *what survives of her work* — the diary, the cloak (if found), and every spirit-binding any mage in the campaign has done. **She is not a person to meet; she is a tradition to read.**
- **Standouts:** *No body, no presence at scale.* What stands out is **the cloak when found** — plain wool of an old cut; **trails water across dry ground when worn**; the spirit inside the wool is *quiet but present*; a *Shaman* feels it within ten paces. **The diary**, in any of its surviving copies — old leather binding, hand-script that shifts subtly across decades as Narissa aged; **the writing slows and steadies in the later years**, then breaks off mid-sentence on the page she was writing the day she died.
- **Speech tag:** Through the diary, in the voice of a young woman who chose to keep working quietly *because she understood she would not be welcomed*. The voice ages across the volume; the early entries are tentative, the middle entries are practical, the late entries are *patient with the cost of long sleeplessness*. Through the cloak's spirit, in fragments only a *Shaman* can read — *not words, currents.*
- **Quests:** Early — *find a copy of the diary and study; even a fragment opens a piece of pre-rift mage practice.* Late — *find the cloak and decide what to do with the spirit inside; nobody alive knows the right answer; the campaign's hardest mage-tradition decision.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *She cannot be reached.* Attempts to summon, channel, or condense her *fail* — the spirit she carried has left her register and exists only in the cloak now. **A party that tries to *replicate* her involuntary fusion** by recreating the lake-conditions of her event is repeating the Age of Wild Magic's catastrophes; the file is honest about the danger.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/narissa.md`.*

## What the party meets — the ways she becomes present

Narissa is not a person a party walks up to. She is *the foundation under what every mage in the world is doing*. What the party meets depends on which trace they find.

- **Through the diary**, in any of its surviving copies. The original may be at Luchebert's library (locked alongside the Saint's Original; cross-ref `doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md` §"At the central building"); copies have circulated through mage-tradition households across centuries. Reading the diary at length is the campaign's most direct mage-craft education that does not require a teacher.
- **Through the cloak**, if found. The cloak's last documented sighting was in the early forests; a party that follows the rumour-chain across centuries may eventually locate it. **The spirit inside is quiet but present**; a *Shaman* working with the cloak finds something old, water-and-earth-aspected, *patient with the long wait*. The spirit will not bond again — it lived with Narissa too closely; her cadence is in it; it does not want a second human.
- **Through any spirit-binding ritual a party witnesses or performs**. Mage initiation (`doc/claude/LORE.md` §"The Mage Tradition") is *the working that emerged after the Age of Wild Magic's failures*; every binding the party sees is a downstream consequence of Narissa's diary having existed. *She is in the room every time a mage is initiated.*
- **Through Felicia**, if asked carefully. Felicia Vintala worked iron-and-shadow threads in pre-rift days (`doc/npcs/felicia.md` §"Background"); her tradition built on the foundation Narissa laid. *Felicia would know a great deal about Narissa as a tradition; her drive does not extend to discussing Narissa as a person, and the diary's most personal pages are not what Felicia attends to.*

## Personal goals — early and later

A deceased figure does not have *goals* the way a living NPC does. The framing here describes **what the campaign can do with her traces** — what the party can find, what they can learn, what they can decide.

- **Early-game — find a copy of the diary and study it.** *What the trace says* (in the diary's voice, opened at any of its readable pages): *"I felt it press itself into me during those minutes below the surface. I understood without being able to say how that what now lives in me will not be welcomed if I name it. I am writing this down because I will tell no one. The plants take root where I pass. I am leaving before they can ask how. Perhaps later, with more time, with a careful enough hand, the work I am doing in secret will be readable to whoever finds this."* — *Where:* **Luchebert's library** is the most likely venue (`doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`; the book may be alongside the Saint's Original or in a separate locked case); **Lady Rhianna**'s pigeon-holes hold a partial *transcribed copy* she inherited from her teacher (`doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md`); **Laurent's wreck** holds an old-court reference copy in the audience hall's preserved shelves (`doc/npcs/laurent.md`); **a wandering mage student** the party may meet in the kingdoms may have a fragment in their satchel. — *What:* earn access to a copy (gating: Father Elliot for Luchebert; Rhianna's hour-glass for the transcribed copy; Laurent's necklace path for the wreck's copy; *Char* for the wandering student); read at length (a winter's reading earns a real piece of mage-tradition history; longer earns deeper). — *Reward:* the party gains *foundational mage-tradition lore* that no NPC will narrate to them. A serious mage student who reads the diary across visits *understands what every binding-ritual since has been built from*; their own mage-craft (or their bonded mage's) deepens. **A *Shaman* reading the diary** has a different revelation — Narissa's fusion was *voluntary on the spirit's side*, the model the shaman traditions have studied as the gentle-binding signature underneath the kingdoms' forced-binding work. Cross-references the Lethran Pair's gentle-binding signature (`doc/places/anchor.md`, `doc/places/grain_mill.md`) — *the Pair's tradition descends from Narissa's, not from the Age of Wild Magic's*. — *Complications:* the diary does not explain how to *replicate* her fusion. *That* is the lesson of the Age of Wild Magic — many tried; many died. A party that reads the diary and then attempts to recreate the lake-conditions for one of their members is repeating the catastrophes the diary itself warned about. **The file is honest**: the wild-magic experiments killed *many* and turned the survivors into *something unstable, dangerous to themselves and everyone nearby*. Do not let parties walk into this without the canonical danger reading clearly.

- **Later-game — find the cloak and decide what to do with the spirit inside.** *Conditional — opens only if the party has read the diary across visits and has earned the standing to follow the cloak's rumour-chain.* *What the trace says* (in the spirit's register, through *Shaman* working with the cloak): *not words, currents. A water-and-earth pulse that has been alone for centuries; that recognises the diary's cadence in the reader's hands; that does not want to bond again but recognises the asker.* — *Where:* the **rumour-chain across the kingdoms' early forests** — Diederik may carry the oldest fragment (`doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md`); Wilder's notebook may have an entry (`doc/npcs/wilder.md`); Felicia's cave chapel holds a *secondary reference* in Willow's keeping (`doc/npcs/willow.md`). The cloak itself is *somewhere in deep forest country* (DM-fill region — possibly the forbidden woods' fringe before the rift made it forbidden; possibly the early forests around what is now Brumal; possibly carried away by the spirit itself across centuries). — *What:* follow the rumour-chain; locate the cloak; bring it (carefully, respectfully) to a *Shaman* of standing (Wilder, Gerhald, Willow, or a mage student with deep diary-reading) for the decision. — *The decision:* (a) **Let the spirit return to its element.** The cloak is unmade; the spirit goes to water-and-earth, free for the first time in centuries; the wool is buried with appropriate rite. *The world loses a unique trace*; what remains is the diary. (b) **Keep the cloak as a working-tool.** The spirit stays inside; the cloak passes to a serious mage student or to a shaman tradition that will treat it well; *it will not bond, but it can be consulted*. (c) **Attempt a new bond.** Almost certainly fails — the spirit does not want a second human — *but the attempt is what the Age of Wild Magic was made of.* A party that pushes for this against the spirit's clear refusal is repeating history. — *Reward:* one of the campaign's most *historically meaningful* moments lands. The decision is recorded (Felicia's drive registers it; Wilder's notebook records it; the necropolis's archive notes it if the party brings the news). Whatever path the party chooses, *they have done something the world has been waiting on for centuries.* — *Complications:* the cloak's spirit will not be coerced. A party that pushes too hard reads the spirit's refusal as a *physical* response (the cloak resists movement; water trails reverse direction; a *Shaman* working without consent fails). **The decision affects every mage in the campaign downstream** — option (a) ends a foundational trace; option (b) preserves it for future study; option (c) repeats the wild-magic disaster. **No path is clean.**

## How she reads at a glance — the traces

Narissa has no presence at scale. What the party encounters is the trace they have found.

- **The diary** — old leather-bound, hand-script that shifts subtly across decades. Early entries are *young and tentative*; the writing settles in the middle years into a *practical clarity*; late entries are *patient with sleeplessness*; the final entry breaks off mid-sentence (*the spirit had taught her how to recognise its currents but never how to live on its rest*).
- **The cloak** — plain wool of an old cut; an outsider would call it homely. **Trails water across dry ground when worn or carried**; *visible to a casual observer as wet patches that reform when wiped*. A *Shaman* feels the spirit within ten paces; a mage feels it within five; an ordinary observer sees only that the cloak does not stay dry.
- **The spirit's register, when consulted through the cloak by *Shaman*** — water-and-earth, deep-old, *patient*. Speaks in currents not words. Recognises the diary's cadence in a reader's hands. Will not bond again.
- **In the world at large** — every spirit-binding ritual ever performed on the continent is downstream of her. *She is everywhere mage-craft is and nowhere personally.*

## Visible patterns — what her traces do across the world

What survives Narissa expresses itself across the campaign without anyone announcing it:

- **Mage initiation** (`doc/claude/LORE.md` §"The Mage Tradition") is the discipline that emerged from the Age of Wild Magic's failures. Every initiated mage the party meets carries her diary's downstream consequence in their own bound spirit. *None of them will say "Narissa" when asked; most will not name her unprompted.*
- **The shamans' practice** of negotiating with spirits (rather than capturing them) traces back to Narissa's voluntary fusion — *the model the shamans have studied as the goal that the kingdoms abandoned*. Wilder and Gerhald both know this; neither speaks of it casually.
- **The Lethran Pair's gentle-binding signature** (`doc/places/anchor.md`, `doc/places/grain_mill.md`, `doc/places/tidehold.md`) descends from Narissa's tradition not the wild-magic tradition. A party that has read the diary at Luchebert's library reads the Pair's working with one extra layer of recognition.
- **Wherever the cloak has passed**, *plants take root in places where they had not been growing*. Centuries-old farmsteads sometimes carry stories of a *traveler in a homely cloak* whose passage left fields fertile that summer; the stories are mostly forgotten and occasionally surface in a village's elder-memory.

## What the diary will tell — and when

The diary's content is gated by access (above) and by the reader's patience. A serious reader gets a tier of information per session of careful study.

| Tier of reading | What the diary gives |
|---|---|
| First reading (a fortnight) | Narissa's account of the fusion; what she felt and did in the months after; her decision to tell no one. |
| Sustained reading (a winter) | The voluntary register of her fusion (*the spirit's choice*, not hers); a serious mage student's foundation. |
| Deep reading across years (returning visits) | Her account of the spirit's *cadence* — what it knew that she did not; how it taught her to read currents; the impossibility of replicating it. |
| The final pages | The breaking-off; what she was working on the day she died; the cloak's instructions (*"keep it where the spirit can come and go; do not give it to a living mage; do not put it in a sealed box; let it find what it finds"*). |

## What the cloak will tell — and when (through *Shaman*)

The cloak's spirit speaks only through *Shaman* working in the right register. A summary of what comes through across visits:

- **First contact** (any *Shaman*): a *current* through the working room. Recognition that someone is paying attention. Not a fact; a presence.
- **Sustained contact** (the same *Shaman*, multiple visits): the spirit's preference — *to come and go from the cloak, not to be sealed inside it*. A shaman who treats the cloak as *open* gets more contact than one who treats it as *contained*.
- **Deep contact** (a *Shaman* of real standing, with the diary read): fragments of *what the spirit knew before Narissa* — pre-human water-and-earth practice; ancient elemental cadences; the way the world's spirits worked before the kingdoms existed.
- **The decision-conversation** (only at the late-game cloak-decision moment): the spirit's preference for option (a) — *return to element*. Stated as a current, not a demand.

## Mannerisms — what the traces do

- **The diary's handwriting changes across decades**. A reader who has spent enough time with it can date a passage to within a few years by the script alone.
- **The cloak's water-trail forms in specific patterns** — a *Shaman* reads the spirit's mood from the shape (steady drips when calm; spreading puddles when curious; reverse-flow when threatened).
- **A working mage standing too close to the cloak feels their bound spirit *unsettled*** — Narissa's spirit recognises *forced binding* and disapproves silently.
- **A shaman standing too close feels at home**. The cloak's register is the same register shamans work in.

The cloak-spirit, through *Shaman*, gives readings on relevant figures across the world. Sparse, fragmentary, pulse-shaped:

| Figure | What the spirit communicates |
|---|---|
| **Felicia Vintala** | A current of recognition. Felicia's tradition built on Narissa's; the spirit *knows* the line. |
| **The Lethran Pair** | Recognition through *gentle-binding* register. The Pair's signature is the spirit's own register at human scale. |
| **The mage tradition (the kingdoms')** | A pulse of distance. *"They captured what should have been asked."* |
| **The shaman tradition** | A current of warmth. *"They asked. Some still ask."* |
| **The chaos spirits** | *Trouble.* The cloak's spirit knows them as kin-of-a-kind (older beings; downstream); does not engage. |
| **Laurent** | Recognition through the *holding-station* register. The spirit knows what Laurent is; respects it; will not approach. |
| **The Skull Gatherers** | A current of *necessary-work-not-mine*. The spirit understands the order's role; would accept being held in the necropolis if option (b) is chosen and the order will receive. |

## What the traces will not say

- **Narissa as a person past what the diary records.** The diary is the surviving voice; the cloak's spirit knew her cadence but does not narrate her life.
- **How to replicate the fusion.** The diary describes; it does not teach. The Age of Wild Magic is the warning.
- **Where Narissa is buried.** She lived in her hundreds; her death was quiet; the burial was unmarked or has been lost. Open thread.
- **The spirit's name (if it has one).** The spirit communicates in currents, not names. *Reading* and *Shaman* sustained study may, over years, produce a name the spirit *recognises* if asked correctly; the file does not commit.

## What the traces can teach

- **Pre-rift mage-tradition history** — through the diary. A serious mage student reads it across a winter and gains *foundational tradition-lore* that no living teacher will give them.
- **The voluntary-binding register** — through the cloak's spirit, with a *Shaman* and the diary read. Rare; deep; usable in the shaman traditions' working but not in the kingdoms' mage practice.
- **Ancient elemental cadences** — through deep cloak-contact across years. Reserved for parties that have committed to the trace.
- **What the world's spirits were like before the kingdoms** — through the cloak's deep memory. Atmospheric, not mechanical; informs *Shaman* working at depth.

## DM notes

- **Narissa is the campaign's *foundation*, not an encounter.** A DM running her well delivers her through *what survives* — the diary at Luchebert, the cloak in the forests, every mage-binding the party witnesses being downstream of her work. *She is in the room when a mage is initiated; she is not in the room as a character.*
- **The diary is gateable real reward.** A serious mage student reading across a winter earns mage-tradition history that no living NPC will narrate. Run the gating honestly — Luchebert's case is locked; Rhianna will read with the party in pieces; Laurent's wreck holds a court-copy gated by the necklace path.
- **The cloak is *the* late-game-mage decision.** Three honest endings — return to element, keep as working-tool, attempt new bond. Do not pre-load a correct answer. The campaign's record will reflect the party's choice; *whichever they choose, the world has been waiting centuries for the decision*.
- **The Age of Wild Magic's danger is real.** A party that decides to recreate Narissa's fusion experiments inherits the lesson the wild-magic centuries taught at terrible cost. The DM should not soften this; many died trying; the survivors were unstable; the kingdoms abandoned the voluntary register because the costs were unsupportable. The shamans kept the practice in different form. *The party is not the first to think they can do better.*
- **The Vintala-line connection through Felicia is real but soft.** Felicia's tradition descends from Narissa's foundation; *every* mage tradition does. The Pair's gentle-binding signature is the strongest cross-reference (Pre-Rift Pair adventure: `doc/claude/PRE_RIFT_PAIR.md`). A party that has done the Pair-arc before reading Narissa's diary has the *historical depth* of what the Pair were continuing; a party that has not still gets a beautiful piece of pre-rift mage-craft.
- **The cloak's spirit will not bond again.** A DM should be firm on this. The diary's final-pages instruction (*do not give it to a living mage*) is the spirit's request through Narissa; honouring it is the *correct* DM register.
- **Run the diary's voice slowly.** When a party reads at the table, deliver one or two short fragments per session of in-character reading. The voice ages across the volume; let the players hear the writing settle and break off.
- Lore cross-references: the First Mage account in `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"The First Mage" (the foundational text); the Age of Wild Magic in §"The Age of Wild Magic"; the mage tradition in §"The Mage Tradition"; the Vintala line through `doc/npcs/felicia.md`; the gentle-binding cross-references at `doc/places/anchor.md`, `doc/places/grain_mill.md`, `doc/places/tidehold.md`; Luchebert's library in `doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`; the necropolis's archive in `doc/npcs/skull_gatherers.md`.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script. Distilled from `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"The First Mage."*

- **24 years old at the fusion.** Colonist. Out on a lake checking seeded fish stocks when a whirlpool opened beneath her.
- **Pulled under, washed ashore an hour later.** Spirit had pressed itself into her during those minutes; she understood it would not be welcomed if named.
- **Travelled between settlements quietly, leaving plants taking root.** Was always gone before anyone could thank her.
- **Lived past a hundred.** The fusion extended her life; the spirit's continuous restless presence in her sleep made the long years uncomfortable.
- **Wrote the diary across decades.** The volume is the foundational text of all mage-tradition learning that followed.
- **Died with the spirit lingering in her cloak.** The cloak persisted in the early forests, trailing water across dry ground; current location open.

## Open questions

- **Where the cloak is now.** Open. A campaign that wants to use the late-game decision must place it; a campaign that does not, leaves it open.
- **The spirit's name.** Open. May surface across years of *Shaman* contact; the file does not commit.
- **Where Narissa is buried.** Open. May never be found.
- **Whether the diary's late pages can be fully copied.** Open; the working that protects them is the diary's own and is not described in any other surviving source.
- **What the Lethran Pair would have said about Narissa**, given the chance. The Pair's correspondence at the Anchor mentions her *in the working-tongue's reading-direction*, never by name in plain script. Open thread.
