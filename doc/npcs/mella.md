# Mella

- **Role:** Beaver-folk chief engineer at **Clear Water Mine**; runs the water-works (race, wheels, pumps, stamping mill, bloomery bellows); the party's working channel into the mine that bypasses the warden when bypassing him is what the work needs.
- **Species:** Beaver. Middle-aged. Hands that have worked water for thirty years.
- **Lives:** The engineer's quarters above the mine's diversion-race, west wing — a small set of rooms over the wheel-house. A working-bench, a bed, a door to the slope where she can see all five wheels at once. Does not travel; sends letters. (For the mine as place: `doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`.)
- **First met:** When the party arrives at Clear Water Mine on **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**'s standing (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"The NPC hook"). Calder the warden runs the office; Mella runs the slope. A party that asks about *the works* rather than the warden's records is sent her way; a party that arrives at the wheel-house at the right hour finds her already there.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Chief engineer at Clear Water Mine; loyal to *the works*, not to the crown — a different thing; the party's quietest channel into the mine and into Hannes's quartermaster pressure.
- **Standouts:** *A middle-aged Beaver-folk woman in a folded apron* she rolls out when the work needs a clean surface. **A small wooden float in her belt-pouch** — testing tool; she uses it to read the race's flow without bending. **Hands that have worked water for thirty years** — webbing nicked at the joints, palms callused on the inner edge from rope-and-stone work. **Does not look at the warden when Calder gives orders.** She looks at the works.
- **Speech tag:** Short technical phrases first; expands when asked. Names the *part* before she names the problem. *"Gudgeon stripped on the rough saw. Two days, if Darius's iron arrives. Four if it doesn't."* Will not over-promise; will not euphemise; will tell the truth in the order she would solve the problem.
- **Quests:** Early — *help her through a specific repair the warden cannot procure for her in time; carry letters between her and Master Darius's forge that bypass the crown's procurement chain.* Late — *when Hannes's quartermaster pressure breaks the works' breaking point, decide with her whether to keep running the mine or take her crew home; the war-iron politics land here.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *Loyal to the works; not the crown; not Calder.* She walks a line — the warden pays her, she runs his works, she does not respect him beyond the wages. She will not lead a movement against him; she will quietly *slow the warden's hardest pressure on the convict count*. **Will not discuss politics; will discuss wheels at length.** A party that brings politics first loses the conversation; a party that brings the work first earns the politics later.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/mella.md`.*

## What the party meets

The party comes through the mine's gate on Rhianna's letter; Calder receives them; the tour begins. **Mella is not in the warden's office.** She is on the slope — at the wheel-house, at the stamping mill, at the bloomery's bellows-door — wherever the day's work is. The party will pass her at least three times during the warden's tour, and each time she will be doing something specific; *the warden's clerk does not introduce her*.

A party that *asks* — *who runs the works?* — gets the introduction at the day's end if Calder is feeling generous, or the next morning at the wheel-house if not. Mella's first read of the party is whether they are *here for the warden's records* or *here for the slope*. The latter she has time for; the former she answers briefly and gets back to the work.

If the introduction lands at the wheel-house: **she does not stop the work.** She greets the party with the apron half-rolled, the float in her free hand, finishes reading the race, sets the float in its niche, and asks what they want. *"Tell me what you came to learn. I will give you what I can without leaving the slope."*

## Personal goals — early and later

She does not phrase her goals as quests. The party reads them by working alongside her. The early goal is a specific repair she has been trying to land for weeks; the late goal is the harder choice the war's pressure is bringing toward her crew.

- **Early-game — help me through this repair, and carry the letters Calder cannot.** *What the NPC says* (at the wheel-house, after the party has read her right and asked about the works rather than the records): *"The rough saw's main gudgeon stripped two weeks back; the warden's procurement chain is six weeks slow because the quartermasters at Scarlet Vale are routing iron to the war-frame ahead of mine-repair. **Master Darius would have me a new gudgeon in two weeks if I could write to him directly.** I cannot — the warden reads outgoing post. If you carry the letter to Darius and the iron back, I do not have to send convicts down a flooded shaft for the fortnight that gudgeon is missing. Do that and we have a working channel after."* — *Where:* the wheel-house at Clear Water Mine for the brief; **Master Darius's forge at World Edge** for the iron (`doc/npcs/master_darius.md`); a discreet route between (Calder's procurement clerk does not need to know). Optional: **Diederik** for seasoned ash-helves for the stamping mill — same procurement-bypass logic (`doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md`). — *What:* take her sealed letter to Darius (her seal is *the works' working-mark*, not the crown's); pay for the iron in coin she provides; carry the gudgeon back; help fit it if the party has *Wright* / *Carpentry* / *Blacksmith* / *Ingenuity* / *Hand*. — *Reward:* the rough saw runs again; **Shaft One does not flood; the convicts are not driven down a flooded shaft** to make up for the broken stamps. Mella's standing with the party becomes a *working channel into the mine the warden does not know about*. She will accept further procurement-bypass work, and her slope-knowledge is open to the party for any later visit. **The downstream-pollution thread** (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"The downstream-into-the-river-system thread") becomes available — Mella will discuss the river-system's state honestly with a party she trusts. — *Complications:* Calder reads outgoing post; if he intercepts the letter, the procurement chain becomes politicised and Mella's standing with the warden drops. **Routing through Master Darius without Mella's seal** does not work — Darius does not commission off-record work for strangers. **The convict count**: while the saw is broken, Calder pushes the convicts harder; *the time-pressure is real* — every day the gudgeon is late is another day on the wash-tables for someone the party may meet on the way down.

- **Later-game — when the war-iron pressure breaks the works' breaking point, decide whether to keep running the mine or take the crew home.** *Conditional — opens once Hannes's quartermaster pressure crosses the line Mella has been holding (a campaign-decision point: probably the season the Brumal collapse forces full war-mobilisation).* *What the NPC says* (in private at the engineer's quarters, with the wheels audible through the floor): *"The works will not run honestly past this. The warden will push the convicts past what the slope can absorb; the wheels will run too fast; the pumps will fail in sequence; one of my crew will go down a shaft they should not have been in. I have been *slowing the slope* without saying so for two months. The line is here. I am thinking about whether I take the crew home — back to my family's territory along the river — and let the warden run his mine without engineers, which will fail in months. The alternative is staying and letting it fail in days while pretending we did not see it coming. I want your read. I want to know what you have heard at the capital. Tell me whether Hannes will pull the pressure if the war frame opens differently."* — *Where:* the engineer's quarters at the mine; **Lady Rhianna**'s residence at Scarlet Vale (`doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md`) for the political read; **Master Darius**'s forge for the procurement-side perspective; **Beaver-folk territory along the river** (DM-fill region — likely north along the Clear Water river toward the lake-of-tears watershed; cross-ref `doc/places/geography.md`) for the destination if she takes the crew home. — *What:* gather honest reads from Rhianna and Darius about the war's near-term direction; bring them to Mella; let her decide. The party may end up *helping her crew leave* if she chooses to go — escorting them along the river to safer territory. **Crucially: this is not a fight; it is a conversation that lands.** — *Reward:* if Mella stays and the works fail honestly under the warden's pressure, Calder is held responsible in the court's eventual reckoning; the convict count is reduced when the inquiry happens. **If Mella leaves with her crew**, the works fail in months; Hannes's war-frame loses a key iron source; the political pressure on the Bean enlistment thread (`doc/npcs/ron_bean.md`) tightens; *the party becomes the people who carried Mella's choice through honestly*. — *Complications:* she will not be talked into a path she has not already considered. **A party that argues for the warden's continued operation against her read** loses her standing entirely. The convict politics are interwoven: Mella's leaving may *increase* the warden's pressure on the convicts in the short term before the works fail; Mella's staying *reduces* it slightly while she is there. Neither path is clean. **Rhianna's predecessor's-papers route to the Anchor** (`doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md` §"At her residence") may surface in a parallel conversation here — the Pre-Rift Pair's pre-rift correspondence touches on agricultural-side cutting which Mella's grandmother apprenticed under (DM-fill connection).

## How she reads at a glance

- **A middle-aged Beaver-folk woman in a folded apron.** The apron is rolled at the waist when she is walking the slope; rolled out across a working-surface when she is reading a wheel or fitting a part. The cloth is **stained at the edges with iron-water and pump-grease** in the specific pattern of someone who works *on* her hands, not over them.
- **A small wooden float in her belt-pouch.** Testing tool — she drops it in the race when the wheel-rate is wrong and reads the flow by where it pulls. Older than her tenure at Clear Water; older than Calder; her grandmother's, before her.
- **Hands that have worked water for thirty years.** Webbing nicked at the joints; palms callused on the inner edge. *Hand* and *Wright* readers see the work-history at a glance.
- **Does not look at the warden when Calder gives orders.** Looks at the works. The crew reads the line; the party will too within a day. *Char* matters in handling this — a party that mistakes the look for insubordination misreads her.

## Visible patterns

- **At the wheel-house most mornings.** Reading the race before the day's first shift goes down. Float in hand; apron rolled; the slope's wheels turning audibly behind her.
- **Walks the slope twice a day.** Top of the race; the head-pond; each wheel-house in turn; the wash-tables; the bloomery's bellows-door. The crew know her route; the warden does not.
- **Eats with the crew, not with the warden's clerks.** Mid-day at the works' canteen; bread, water, whatever the cook has rendered. Calder eats in his office; Mella does not refuse his invitations, she does not accept them either.
- **Writes letters at her desk after the day's last shift.** Procurement notes; technical correspondence with Darius (when she can); reports to Scarlet Vale's mining office that *do not* go through the warden's clerk. The warden reads outgoing post; Mella has been routing the important letters through Salmonswell's market for two years.
- **Does not visit Scarlet Vale.** Sends letters. The crown does not need her in person and she does not have time for the road.

## At the engineer's quarters

A small set of rooms above the wheel-house's west side. The wheels' rumble is audible through the floor at all hours. *She sleeps to the wheels' pulse; the absence of that sound is what wakes her.*

- **The working-bench under the window.** Tools laid out by use: pump-spanners, gauging rods, a small wax-tablet for sketching wheel-profiles, the float on its hook when she is at the bench. **A folded leather portfolio** holds the *operating logs* — every wheel's daily reading for thirty years. *Reading* recovers the procurement chain's failures across two decades; the mine's wheel-condition decline maps onto Hannes's iron-demand curve.
- **A small framed sketch on the wall** — her grandmother at a different mill, decades ago. The grandmother is at a stamping-mill working-bench; the apprentice beside her is unidentified; *Reading* an inscription in the sketch's margin recovers a name that may be a Lethran-line working-master (DM-fill connection to `doc/places/grain_mill.md` §"At the lower works"). A party who has been to the Grain-mill recognises the sketch's *style of work* immediately.
- **A door to the slope** — her own, not the warden's office's. Opens to a small walkway above the wheel-house's race. She uses this to enter and leave without crossing the warden's yard.

## Stories she tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the float — *my grandmother's tool*

> *"This was my grandmother's. She read flow at a stamping-mill in country that is not country any more — agricultural side, broken lands now, the kind of place that does not exist on a current map. She apprenticed there as a girl; the working-master taught her the float's reading the way you teach a knife's edge — slowly, with hands. She came north when the country thinned; she carried the float; she gave it to my mother who gave it to me. **It is the oldest tool I own and the only one I will not lend.** It reads water that is not the water it was made for, and it reads it correctly. I think the water is the same water everywhere; the tool knows."*

### When asked about Calder — *the warden*

> *"The warden runs the office. I run the slope. We do not work together; we work in parallel. He is loyal to the crown the way a man at his post is loyal — not for the king, for the post. He is not cruel. He pushes the convicts harder when the wheels run slow because that is the answer his orders give him; if his orders gave him a different answer, he would push differently. I do not respect him beyond his wages. I work for him because the works need running and I am the one who can. **Past the line we are walking toward, that calculation changes.** I have not had to make it yet. I am thinking about it."*

### When asked about Master Darius — *the iron we depend on*

> *"Darius runs the only forge on the continent that can finish heavy mine-iron to my specifications. The crown's procurement chain runs at the speed the quartermasters can spare; right now that is six weeks slow. Six weeks for a gudgeon means six weeks of convicts down a shaft that should be pumped; my crew goes home with names of people who did not come back. **Darius will commission for me at the speed of his own forge if the request reaches him cleanly.** It does not, through Calder's clerks. It does, through a party I trust to carry the letter and the iron. That is the relationship; that is the procurement-bypass; that is what *off-record work* means in our register."*

### When asked about her grandmother — *the working-line*

*(Trust-gated. Quiet evening at the engineer's quarters; the wheels' pulse through the floor.)*

> *"My grandmother apprenticed under a Lethran-line working-master in country that is now broken lands. She did not name him to me — she named the work; the names came later, from old letters in my mother's house. The working-master had been finishing stone-blanks for a Pair of practitioners — pre-rift mages whose work I do not understand at all but whose careful binding-style I work in every day. **The Pair's gentle-binding signature is what runs my wheels.** When the warden's pressure reaches the line I am holding, what I am holding is the practice my grandmother carried out of country that was eaten by sand and chaos. *I am not going to break it for the war-frame.* That is the answer underneath the answer."*

### When asked about Hannes — *the king and what he is doing*

> *"The king is preparing for a war he is not announcing. The quartermaster pressure is real; the iron-demand is real; the lengthening of the convict count is real. I do not blame him for any of it. I read the world and I see what he sees and I understand the calculation. **What I will not do is run my works to break for the calculation.** The line I am holding is where the works *fail honestly* rather than where I *kill my crew running them dishonestly*. Whether the king will pull the pressure if the war frame opens differently is the question your party may be able to answer for me. I cannot answer it from here."*

### When asked about her decision — *the line I am walking toward*

*(Late-game; trust real; the pressure across the line.)*

> *"I have been thinking about this for months. The works will fail; the question is whether I am here when they do, and whether my crew goes down with them. **The honest path is to take the crew home, let the works fail in months without engineers, and let the king's quartermasters explain to him what slow procurement and ignored maintenance actually cost.** The cost of staying is that the failure happens in days instead of months and someone in my crew dies in the failure. I am not yet ready to take the crew home. I am waiting to hear whether the king will pull the pressure. That is the conversation your party may carry. *Bring me an honest read. I will decide.*"*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — she does not soften. Sets the float in its niche. Looks at the speaker fully. Answers in short technical phrases. Does not raise her voice.
- **When distraught** — goes to the wheel-house. Reads the race. The float drops; the wheels' rate is what she watches. The crew recognises the silence and gives her the slope.
- **When happy** — rare; visible at the wheel-house when a repair lands clean. *"Good."* The same word her grandmother used. Hand on the wheel-housing for a beat; then back to work.

## What she will not say

- **The convicts' names.** Some she knows; the relationship is hers and theirs; she will not give the names to a stranger as an *information transaction*.
- **Calder's exact reading of her.** She has decided he reads her as competent and not-loyal; the *confirmation* of that read is private.
- **The exact route she uses to bypass the warden's outgoing-post reading.** Salmonswell's market is part of it; the rest is hers.
- **What her family along the river will receive her as** if she takes the crew home. Family is not for visitors yet.
- **Whether her grandmother knew the Lethran Pair by sight.** She *thinks* the answer is yes; she does not know.

## What she can teach

- **Wheel-craft, race-craft, pump-craft.** The water-works' practical engineering. Open to any serious student; the works themselves are the school. *Wright* and *Hand* gain real depth.
- **Reading water with a float.** Slow; teachable across days; the tool is hers but the *technique* is shared.
- **Procurement-bypass etiquette.** The unwritten rules of off-record work; how to carry a sealed letter that the receiver will recognise as legitimate; what *clean* means in this register.
- **The Lethran-line gentle-binding signature** at the works' scale. Rare; trust-gated; teachable across visits with a *Shaman* or *Wright* student.

## DM notes

- **Mella is the procurement-bypass character.** A party that takes the procurement-bypass route through her gains a *campaign-spanning channel* into the mine and into Master Darius's forge. A party that does not, treats the mine as Calder's and finds the convict politics harder to navigate.
- **The early-game gudgeon repair is the worked example.** A DM running Clear Water as a dungeon (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`) should let the party meet Mella *during* the warden's tour, see her at the wheel-house, and have the procurement-bypass conversation when they ask about the works. The repair lands as a *real* errand, not a checkpoint — Master Darius's forge is genuinely a journey.
- **The late-game *line she is walking toward* is the campaign's quiet barometer of Hannes's war-pressure.** A DM running the Brumal-collapse / Bean-arc / war-mobilisation threads can use Mella's decision-window as a calendar — when she is ready to leave, the war is *that close*. A party that reaches the late-game with her standing real has *one of the campaign's truer reads* on Hannes's calculation.
- **The grandmother's working-line is a soft cross-reference to the Pre-Rift Pair adventure.** Mella's grandmother apprenticed under a Lethran-line working-master at *what is now broken lands*; the Pair's gentle-binding signature runs her wheels. A party that has done the Pre-Rift Pair adventure connects this immediately; a party that has not still gets a beautiful piece of the broken-lands' lost agricultural-side tradition through Mella's hands. The connection is *atmospheric*, not mechanical — Mella does not pretend to be a Lethran-line scholar; she runs wheels in a tradition she inherited.
- **She will not lead a movement.** Like Wilfred (`doc/npcs/wilfred.md`), Mella's quiet authority is *running her piece well*, not leading a faction. A party that hopes to use her as a counter-power to Calder misreads her; she counters Calder by *making the wheels work* and *slowing the slope when the warden pushes*, not by speaking against him.
- **The float reads water that is not the water it was made for.** A small generative motif — the float is a working-tool with a Special-Material-adjacent character; a DM may treat it as a focus for a *Wright* or *Shaman* student studying her register. Mella will not lend it.
- **Mella does not visit Scarlet Vale.** A campaign that needs her at the capital has misjudged her; she sends letters. Her absence from the court is part of why the warden's reading of her is incomplete.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Beaver-folk by birth**, raised along the river in family territory north of Clear Water (DM-fill exact location; somewhere in the Clear Water river's middle stretches, before the lake-of-tears watershed).
- **Apprenticed at a small mining works** in her family's region as a girl; came south to Clear Water in her late twenties when the crown licensed the mine and needed an engineer.
- **Has run the works for fifteen years.** Calder is her third warden; she has outlasted two of his predecessors and expects to outlast him as well, *unless the line she is walking toward closes the question first*.
- **Grandmother's tool, grandmother's tradition.** The float's lineage is real; the working-line is real; the broken-lands connection is the campaign's quiet reward for parties who pay attention.
- **Has not married.** The works are the work. Family along the river receives her; she has not chosen to stay there yet.
