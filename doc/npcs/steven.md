# Steven of Allondo

- **Role:** Second son of King Hannes and Queen Fienna; nephew of Father Elliot. A young noble with a quiet herbalist's pull and a debt to Irna Bean he intends to pay in person.
- **Species:** Human (Allondan royal line, Leatherman on his mother's side).
- **Lives:** **Scarlet Vale**, the royal palace. The court-side rooms of the residential wing are his on paper; in practice he is more often found at **the herb-corner of the outer courtyard** he claimed for his own work, or at **the small drying-room** above it where his bunches and notebooks live. (Place file: `doc/places/scarlet_vale.md`.)
- **First met:** Either at a court reception, where he is performing a role that does not fit him; or — if the rumours about Irna have already reached the capital — on the road, in plain travel clothes, with a single attendant, asking quietly after a Boar-folk woman who is missing.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Second son of Hannes and Fienna; herbalist-prince in private; debt to Irna he intends to pay in person; possible heir if the Elliot-echo fires.
- **Standouts:** *Faintly green-stained at the fingertips* (he has stopped noticing). **A small leather-bound book in his coat pocket** — half notebook, half pressed-leaf collection; the hand goes to it rather than to a sword-hilt. **Watches doors for exits**; checks his mother's posture before he registers the rest of a room.
- **Speech tag:** Court courtesy *applied, not yet inherited* — exact and slightly held by a young man who would rather be elsewhere. *"Not the throne. Not the sword. The plants."* Will say *"I do not know"* aloud in private — a thing the court has trained him not to say.
- **Quests:** Early — *go to Irna privately and apologise; carry his question to Diederik for proper herbalist teaching.* Late (conditional on Corven's arc) — *stand in for his brother if the Elliot-echo fires; open a quiet druidic line from the crown.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *Gentle, not weak.* A party (or DM) reading him as soft misses him — he has been refused by three druids and kept working, decided to apologise to the Boar-folk woman who broke his collar, and is preparing to inherit a kingdom he does not want, if he must.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/steven.md`.*

## What the party meets

A young human, slight by Allondan-court standards, in palace dress that has been cut correctly and fitted twice and still sits on him a beat awkwardly — the body has not fully grown into it. His **fingertips are faintly green-stained**, a tell he no longer thinks about, the colour of a herbalist who crushed leaves an hour ago. He carries **a small leather-bound book** in a coat pocket; if the conversation pauses he does not put a hand to a sword-hilt the way his brother does — he puts it to the book.

He greets the party with the courtesy he was taught. The courtesy is exact and slightly held — he has not internalised it yet, the way Corven has. A party paying attention sees the trained prince and, just under it, a young man who would rather be elsewhere. If the party is at court, he is performing. If the party catches him in the courtyard or on the road, the performance drops by half and the curiosity comes through.

## Personal goals — early and later

He has been doing his own work in the corners of his life — the herbs, the druid letters that did not land, the quiet protectiveness of his mother. The early goals are debts he has decided to pay; the later goals are the shape his life takes if Corven's situation breaks the way it might.

- **Early-game — go to Irna Bean privately and apologise.** *What the NPC says* (when he reaches the party — most often in Elmsfield or on the road south, after rumours have reached Scarlet Vale that Irna never arrived at Luchebert): *"My name is Steven. You are travelling with a Boar-folk woman called Irna Bean. I am the boy she struck at her father's farm. I have not come on crown business; my father does not know I am here. I would like to speak with her, if she will speak with me. I will wait if she will not. If she wants me to leave, I will leave."* — *Where:* wherever the party is travelling with Irna — Elmsfield first, then any town along the road south, ultimately Luchebert if she reaches it; *not* a court setting. — *What:* mediate, or step out of the way of, a private conversation between Steven and Irna. Steven wants to say what he has come to say; Irna's answer is hers to decide. The party's job is to let it happen, or to end it, depending on what the moment needs. — *Reward:* a young prince who owes the party a favour in a register the court does not know about. That favour can later become an unofficial crown line, a druidic introduction, a read on Corven's crisis, or a quiet ally inside the palace. Irna may also gain something — closure, confirmation, a name attached to a face she had only as a uniform — depending on her response. — *Complications:* Steven has slipped his guard. If the meeting is observed by a courtier or a knight loyal to Hannes, the situation becomes a court matter and Hannes will hear of it within the week. Irna may refuse outright, deck him, or demand things he is not free to give (a public apology from the crown; the cancellation of the proclamation; her own freedom). If she has not yet recovered enough to think clearly, the conversation may come at the wrong week. Steven will accept any of these answers. The party may not.

- **Early-game — get a herbalist to teach him properly.** *What the NPC says* (when he learns the party has been to Elmsfield, or that they know Elder Diederik): *"The druids will not take me. I have asked three of them; the answer is always the same in different words. There is a fourth I have not asked — the owl elder at Elmsfield, who is not in the formal order any more. Word reaches the capital that he is willing to teach the people who reach him on their own terms. I cannot ride into Elmsfield as the second son of Allondo and ask. I can ask him through someone he already trusts. Will you carry the question for me, plainly. I am not asking him to teach me as a prince; I am asking him to teach me as someone whose hands have stained green for years and have learned everything they know wrong."* — *Where:* Steven's drying-room or the herb-corner for the ask; **Elmsfield**, specifically Diederik's tree (`doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md`); the carrying back of Diederik's answer to Scarlet Vale or wherever Steven is. — *What:* deliver Steven's question to Diederik — verbatim, without dressing it up — and bring back whatever answer Diederik gives. If Diederik agrees in any form, broker the first meeting on Diederik's terms, not Steven's. — *Reward:* Steven becomes a formal student, in the irregular sense Diederik teaches in. The party has linked the crown to the only working druid in the region, in a way the formal order has spent centuries refusing. Standing with both. Standing also with Hank, who is the second-druid-in-reserve and will recognise the move (`doc/npcs/hank.md`). — *Complications:* Diederik may refuse. He has reasons; the formal order's stance on noble houses is not a stance he entirely disagrees with. If he refuses, Steven accepts the refusal but the door does not shut — *"come back if you change your mind, on terms I can stand."* If Diederik agrees, the agreement must be kept private; if Hannes's court learns the second prince is studying with an excommunicated druid, the political price rises sharply. Steven will not pretend the price is small.

- **Later-game — stand in for his brother, if Corven steps out of the line.** *Conditional — opens if the campaign runs the **Elliot-echo** arc (`doc/npcs/prince_corven.md` §"Possible arcs") and Corven renounces.* *What the NPC says* (in private, after the renunciation has happened or is imminent — to a party that has earned the conversation): *"I am not the prince my brother was. I was not built for it; I was not trained for it; I do not want it. I will take it if he sets it down, because the alternative is a kingdom with no head in the worst week it has had. I am not asking you to talk him out of it; I have tried; he is right. I am asking you to stand near me when I take the throne, because the men who would rather see a regent than a herbalist-prince will move within a fortnight. Tell me what you will and will not do."* — *Where:* the audience hall at Scarlet Vale for the formal weeks; the queen's wing for the actual decisions; the field if Brumal moves before the succession settles. — *What:* be at hand during the succession — visibly, as Steven's chosen advisors-of-record — and back the difficult acts: the appointment of Fienna as co-regent in fact if not in title; the public meeting with Father Elliot that the court will resist; a quiet line opened to Penelope at Cliffside through the courier route Corven used. — *Reward:* a kingdom whose second prince inherited it on the condition his friends helped him survive the inheriting. The campaign's late shape is partly the party's. — *Complications:* the men who counted Corven as *the* military prince will not count Steven the same way; they will look for a regent, a council, a marriage that delivers a stronger consort, or a coup in the worst case. The Brumal collapse does not pause for Allondo's succession; the war Corven was being prepared for arrives at Steven's feet whether he is ready or not. Steven knows he is not ready. He is going to do it anyway.

- **Later-game — open a quiet druidic line from the crown.** *Conditional — opens if Steven becomes king, or if he remains a second prince long enough to grow into court influence.* *What the NPC says* (in private, with earned trust, after he has been studying with Diederik — or with Hank, if Hank has been brought into it — for at least a season): *"The druidic order has refused noble houses for centuries because noble houses ate the druidic order before. I understand the precedent. The precedent is going to fail us. The Brumal collapse, the chaos-spirit pressure, the spirit-shield over the capital reading thinner every season — none of these wait politely for the order to keep its centuries. I am willing to be the first crown-side voice the order does not have to fight. I will not bind them; I will not staff them with my men; I will fund the work they would do anyway and ask only that they let me sit at the back of the room when they meet. Carry that to whichever druid I have not yet been able to reach myself."* — *Where:* the order's loose meeting points — wherever Diederik or Hank can introduce; the queen's wing at Scarlet Vale where the funding decisions get made. — *What:* carry the offer; carry the refusals back without softening them; broker the few druids willing to talk; help Steven write a charter that does not lie about what it is. — *Reward:* a new institutional shape between crown and order that has not existed since before the Rift. A campaign-spanning shift. — *Complications:* the order is not one body; some druids will refuse on principle; some will accept and the others will distrust them for it. The court will read the work as Steven's grab for soft power and try to redirect the funding toward a court-controlled "shrine office." Hannes, if still alive, will support the work cautiously; Corven, if still in any role, will support it warmly. Penelope, if the courier line is still open, may have the decisive seat — Brumal's mighty-spirit-readers and Fata morgana's inner circle have things to say about how the work should be shaped.

## How he reads at a glance

- **Faintly green-stained at the fingertips.** From crushing leaves into paper. He stops noticing it; the court has stopped commenting on it. A party member with herbalist training reads it at a glance and knows what he has been doing.
- **Carries a small leather-bound book.** Half notebook, half pressed-leaf collection. Sits in his coat pocket when he is at court, in his hand when he is not.
- **Court bearing learned, not yet inherited.** The shoulders are correct; the gait is correct; the hold is *applied* in a way Corven's is not. A courtier reads this as *young*; Steven reads it the same way and is not pleased about it.
- **Watches doors.** Not for threats — for exits. The herb-corner habit. He knows which rooms he wants to leave from before he enters them.
- **Looks at his mother in a specific way.** Not a glance — a check. He registers Fienna's posture before he registers the rest of the room. A party watching the royal family for half an evening will pick this up.

## Visible patterns

- **Spends most mornings in the herb-corner.** He has cleared a back-corner of the outer courtyard for himself — a small bed of useful plants, a stone bench, a cane frame for drying. The gardeners have learned to leave it alone. Court business reaches him there if it must.
- **Drill in the second yard, under duress.** Master-at-arms gives him basic sword and staff. He does the work; he does not love it. The trainers know not to push him on form the way Corven was pushed.
- **Reads in the small palace library after sundown.** Not court histories. Older volumes — herbals, post-Rift survivals, what little the library has on druidic practice. The librarian has noticed which shelves he frequents and has not mentioned it to anyone.
- **Walks the queen's wing once a day.** A specific stop in his mother's receiving rooms, brief, often without speaking. He goes to see her face. Fienna knows. She does not call attention to it.
- **Asks travellers about the south.** When the court hosts merchants or messengers from Brumal-side, Steven finds reasons to be in the room. He has been collecting weather-and-plant notes the court would not recognise as intelligence.

## At the herb-corner of the outer courtyard

The corner is his by long quiet usage; the courtyard is the one off the residential wing, low-walled, sun-pocketed. The standouts on entry:

- **The bed.** Twelve, fifteen feet of cleared earth against the south wall. Useful plants — coltsfoot, yarrow, mint, comfrey, the harder-to-grow ones he has been trying. **Labels in his own hand**, on small slate tags, in a script the court calligraphers do not use.
- **The drying frame.** A low wooden cane frame against the stone wall, **bunches of herbs hung head-down in rows**, faded by sun and air. The frame is plain and exactly the right height for his reach.
- **A second small bench.** Beside the main one. He keeps it there for a tutor he has not yet been allowed. A party member who joins him on it earns a small private signal.

## At the drying-room — above the courtyard

The room is half a stair up from the herb-corner — originally a servants' linen-room, now Steven's. Locked; the key is on his belt.

- **The notebooks.** A small shelf of leather-bound volumes, dated, in his hand. Drawings of leaves; notes on what each plant did when he took it; failed experiments. The volumes most courtiers would not understand at all.
- **The hanging bunches.** What the courtyard frame could not finish in winter — dried complete here, in a dry-cold he has worked out for himself.
- **A single empty chair against the inner wall.** A reading chair he set up for company that has not arrived. Diederik would fit in it. So would Hank. He does not name the absence.

## On the road, when he goes to find Irna

If the campaign reaches the rumours-about-Irna trigger and Steven sets out, the party meets him in plain travel clothes — sturdy boots, a thick cloak, a sword that is functional and not pretty. One trusted attendant, sometimes none. The road is hard for him; he does it anyway. Standouts in this register:

- **Travel gear that hides his rank but not entirely.** The tailoring is too good for a merchant's son. The boots are too new. A road-wise observer reads him in three seconds; a friendly one decides not to.
- **Carries the book and the herb roll on his belt** — the second item is a small bundle of the most useful herbs in waxed cloth, kept for travel. He has used it twice on the road already.
- **Asks for Irna by description, not by name** — because he is not certain what name she is travelling under and he does not want to stamp her with the one he met.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the first druid he approached — *what she said*

> *"I was fifteen. I had been collecting herbs for two years and I had decided I needed someone who knew the work to tell me what I was getting wrong. I had heard there was a druid two days east of the capital, an old badger woman who took on students from time to time. I rode out under a court errand I had invented. I found her at a stream, doing what I would now recognise as listening to it. She let me sit. She let me show her my notebook. She turned the pages slowly, and at the end she closed it and said *whose son are you*. I told her. She handed back the book. She said *I can teach a child of any house except a noble one; the order learned that lesson and we keep it; do not ride out here again*. She said it without unkindness. I rode home. I have read the notebook back since. The work in it was not bad. It just was not anyone's work but mine."*

### When asked about the second and the third — *what they said differently*

> *"The second druid was polite for two hours and answered nothing. The third was a young man who said he would teach me, and named a fee, and named another fee, and named a third — until I understood he was running a small extraction at my expense and would teach me whatever the fees suggested I had paid for. I left. I was angrier at him than at the first one. The first one was honest about why she would not. The third one was not."*

### When asked about the day at the Bean farm — *what I remember of it*

> *"My father gave me the errand. He said I was old enough. I rode out with two of his knights. I did not know the family. I had been told the older brother was enlisted on the strength of my uncle's proclamation; I had not read the proclamation closely. I knocked at their gate. The father came out — John Bean — and looked at me, and looked at the knights, and said nothing for a long moment. The mother came to the door. The boy came out. The boy went with the knights. I was on the path to the road when the daughter struck me. I do not remember the blow well; I remember the ground. I do not remember her face clearly. I remember her hand. Her hand was the hand of a person who had decided. I have thought about that since."*

### When asked about hearing Irna was missing — *what made me get on a horse*

> *"The rumour reached us on a market day. A trader said the woman who attacked the prince had not arrived at the monastery; she had been seen ill in Elmsfield; she was missing. The court did nothing. I went to my drying-room and I sat for an hour. I had the thought I had been resisting for months, which was that the blow had not been about me at all, and that the right person to know that was her, and the right person to say it was me. My father would have stopped me from going if I had asked. I did not ask. I told the master-at-arms I was riding to the country to look at a tutor; I left at dawn. I am here. I am hoping she will hear me."*

### When asked about his mother — *why I watch her at court*

> *"My mother is from a house that *makes* things. The court treats that the way it treats anything it cannot itself do. She bears the room; she does not ask me to defend her; my brother does not defend her either, by her instruction. I check her face when I come into a room because that is what I have, while I am young. When I am older I will have a voice she will accept, and I will use it. For now I check her face."*

### When asked about Corven — *what he is to me*

> *"My older brother. I idolise him; I know I am not supposed to use the word at my age and I am using it anyway. He is what my father wanted, and he made it look like a choice he had also wanted. I have noticed in the last year that the look on him at banquets is wrong. I do not know what is wrong. He has not told me. I am not asking him because I think he will tell me when he is ready, and because I am afraid that if I ask and he does not tell me, the not-telling will become the shape of us. So I am waiting. I do not love the waiting."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed at court** — a small straightening of the back, a pause, a courteous correct answer that does not contain the truth. The hand goes to the book in his pocket and stays there. A party that has seen him in the courtyard reads the gesture; the court reads nothing.
- **When pressed in private** — the held quality drops. He looks at his hands. Replies after a beat. Will say *"I do not know"* aloud — a thing the court has trained him not to say. Trust him in the moment after.
- **When distraught** — goes to whatever plant is in reach. A leaf in the corner of the courtyard, a bunch on the drying frame, a sprig in his pocket if he is on the road. Holds it. Smells it. Does not speak until he has chosen what to say. The motion is a habit older than he is aware of.
- **When happy** — a quick wide smile that he tries to put away too fast. The first second of it is unguarded. Most often when a herb has done what he hoped, when his mother has laughed, or when a stranger has spoken to him as a person rather than as a prince.

## What he will not say

- **The exact contents of his notebook** — he will share specific entries; he will not loan the volume. The notebook is the shape of his thinking.
- **What he intends to do if he becomes king** — the work he has been doing in his head about the druidic line is private until he can do it.
- **That his herbalism is more than a hobby** — at court, publicly. The court must continue to think it is a young man's passing interest. He will not lie if pressed; he will deflect.
- **That he is afraid of his brother's silences** — he holds this only with people who have already named it for him.
- **The route he took to find Irna** — to court audiences, ever. He had a "tutor errand"; the court does not need more than that.
- **Whatever Father Elliot has asked him** — the questions are between them.

## What he can teach

- **Practical herbalism** — what he has worked out on his own. A real body of knowledge for someone whose hands are willing. He will share entries from his notebook with a serious student.
- **Court survival as the wrong-shaped son** — by example, not by lecture. A party member from a family that did not want them earns something specific by walking the palace beside him for a week.
- **Reading a room for exits** — taught quickly, used often. The herb-corner habit. He maps a room before he enters it; a student picks the trick up in two visits.
- **What a refusal sounds like when it is honest** — by example, from the three druids he has approached. A party member learning to ask for help benefits from his account.

## DM notes

- **The apology arc is a character moment, not a quest in the loot sense.** It pays in standing, not in items. The party that handles it well earns the favour; the party that handles it badly loses Steven for the rest of the campaign. Do not run it as a checkpoint.
- **Diederik's answer is not predetermined.** A DM running the herbalism quest can have Diederik refuse politely, accept on hard terms, or — if the campaign has built the trust — accept warmly. Each outcome is a different campaign shape. Hank can be the second-druid-in-reserve if Diederik refuses (`doc/npcs/hank.md`).
- **He is the brother who does not know.** The Corven/Penelope reveal will hit Steven harder than anyone in the household. A DM should *not* let the party reveal it cheaply; gate it behind earned trust on Corven's side first. The conversation is one of the campaign's hardest scenes, by design.
- **The Elliot-echo arc puts the throne on him.** If Corven renounces, Steven becomes king under conditions he is not ready for. The campaign's late shape can run through his court — the queen's wing as the working centre, Father Elliot brought back into visibility, a quiet line to Penelope at Cliffside through what was Corven's courier route. A DM running this arc should make Steven's *not-readiness* a feature, not a bug.
- **The druidic-line quest is a campaign-spanning shift if it lands.** It rewrites the relationship between crown and order. If the DM wants the campaign's late chapters to be about institutional change rather than war, Steven is the lever. If the campaign stays at the war scale, Steven remains a quiet ally and the long arc waits.
- **Do not run him as soft.** He is gentle, not weak. He has been refused by three druids and has kept working; he has decided to apologise to a Boar-folk woman who broke his collar; he is preparing to inherit a kingdom he does not want if he must. A DM playing him as fragile plays a weaker version of him and loses the arc's stakes.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Second son of King Hannes and Queen Fienna**, born after the Overseas War. Raised at Scarlet Vale.
- **Drawn to herbalism since adolescence.** Self-taught from books in the palace library and from his own field collection. Has approached three druids; refused by all three for the same structural reason — the order's stance on noble houses.
- **Sent on the enlistment errand to the Bean farm.** Did the errand correctly. Was struck by Irna Bean on the way out; the blow was non-lethal; the political consequences fell on her, not him.
- **Privately rides to find her** when rumours reach Scarlet Vale that she never arrived at Luchebert. Wants to apologise. Does it without his father's knowledge.
- **Possible mentor:** Elder Diederik in Elmsfield, if reached through the right channels.
- **Possible heir:** if the Elliot-echo arc activates, Steven inherits the throne under crisis conditions.
