# Calder

- **Role:** Warden of **Clear Water Mine**. Crown-appointed; runs the office, the records vault, the convict count, and the procurement chain. Mella runs the works; Trull runs the convict compound; *Calder runs everything between* — and the crown holds him to account for every part.
- **Species:** Human. Middle-aged. The job has aged him further than the years.
- **Lives:** **The warden's office at Clear Water Mine** (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`) — a working-room with the records vault behind it, sleeping quarters above. Visits Scarlet Vale once a season for the quartermasters' briefing; otherwise does not leave the mine.
- **First met:** When the party arrives at Clear Water Mine on **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**'s standing letter (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"The NPC hook"). The clerk at the gate walks them to him; he receives them correctly; the tour begins.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Crown-appointed warden of Clear Water Mine; *competent, tired, not cruel in the normal sense, loyal to the crown in the way that means he will not help the party beyond what his standing orders require* (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`). The man Mella works under and Trull works for; the man Hannes's quartermasters press; the man the convict count's pressure rolls back down on first.
- **Standouts:** *A middle-aged human in a crown-issued coat that has been re-stitched at the cuffs three times*; **a key-ring with seven keys at his belt** (records vault, gate, office, his sleeping quarters, the warden-only sealed cabinet, the seal-press for crown-marked outgoing post, and one key whose lock he has not opened in years); **eyes that read a room before he speaks** but *do not catch the same things Osric's catch* — Calder reads for *threat*, not for phrase. **Drinks tea three times a day**, in the same chipped mug, at the same hours.
- **Speech tag:** *Polite official register.* Names the procedure first. *"Standing orders permit your inspection of ironwork quality and mine-working conditions. The records vault is not within your inspection's terms. I will walk the tour with my clerk."* Will deflect direct questions about specific convicts; will answer technical questions about the works precisely; will not slander Mella but will not vouch for her either.
- **Quests:** Early — *the party is the inspection he has to host; how he handles them shapes how the dungeon's politics unfold for the rest of the visit.* Late — *when Hannes's pressure breaks his line, he must choose between the post and the men he runs; either path costs him.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *Loyal to the post, not to the king and not to the man.* He will not help the party beyond standing orders; he will not actively harm them either; *he is the institution they are negotiating with, not an antagonist*. **The mine is *normal*, not evil** (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` DM notes); a party expecting a dungeon-of-cruelty misreads him; a party meeting him as a *tired man at a hard post* gets the dungeon's actual register.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/calder.md`.*

## What the party meets

The party comes through the gate on Rhianna's letter. The clerk takes their horses to the stables, walks them through the office's outer corridor, and presents them at the warden's door. **Calder is at his desk** — papers in two stacks (one outgoing, one incoming), the chipped mug at his right hand, the seven-key ring on a hook beside the desk where he can see it. He stands when the party is announced (the courtesy is automatic; the standing is brief).

His introduction is *the official register*. He greets the party by Rhianna's letter's framing (*"Inspectors from the capital, on Lady Linthrope's standing"*), confirms what the inspection covers, and names the next steps. *"My clerk will accompany you on the tour. The works are running; the convict count is current; the records vault is closed to the inspection's terms but is open for any specific question my clerk cannot answer."* The line is rehearsed; he says it three or four times a year to crown-side visitors; it is not warm.

A party that responds in the official register gets the official tour. A party that responds *outside* the register — by asking about him personally, about the convicts by name, about Mella by way of the works — gets *Calder thinking* about what kind of inspection this actually is. He does not show the thinking; the clerk standing at his shoulder does not see it. *He has been a warden for twenty years; he reads the visitors who are more than they say they are.*

He will not help. He will not hinder. **He will be exactly as helpful as standing orders require, no more.** That register is the dungeon's negotiation surface.

## Personal goals — early and later

His goals run on the post's clock, not on his own. The early one is *make this inspection close cleanly*; the late one is the harder choice the war-pressure is bringing toward him.

- **Early-game — host this inspection cleanly; close the file.** *What the NPC says* (at the warden's desk, on the party's arrival): *"Inspectors from the capital, on Lady Linthrope's standing — welcome. The tour begins with my clerk; we will visit the three shafts in working order; the records vault is closed to inspection's terms; the convict compound is on the warden's discretion and the warden's discretion permits a perimeter walk only. If you have questions outside the inspection's frame, address them to me directly and I will answer what I can within standing orders. I assume you will not be staying past three days; the inspection's seal must be lodged at Scarlet Vale within the fortnight."* — *Where:* the warden's office for the brief; the **three shafts** for the tour (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"At the three shafts"); the **convict compound's perimeter** for the discretionary view; the records vault is closed but exists. — *What:* receive the inspection in the official register; tour with the clerk at the party's shoulder; sign the inspection's seal at the end and lodge a correct report at the capital. — *Reward:* the inspection closes cleanly. **Calder's read of the party** sets at *this party handles the official register correctly*; if they need to come back for legitimate further work, the receiving line is shorter. **He does not become an ally** — he remains the post's man — but he does not become an obstacle either. — *Complications:* the inspection is the *cover* (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"The NPC hook" — Rhianna's standing letter buys entry as inspectors). The party that uses the cover *correctly* gets the tour; the party that breaks cover (by asking about Osric directly; by demanding records-vault access; by forcing the convict-compound interior) loses the cover and Calder responds *by the standing orders' protocol*. **He is not unsympathetic** — he reads what is happening; he simply has nothing to offer that standing orders do not permit. **Forcing his hand** invokes the formal-violation register; he does not retaliate; he files a report and the matter goes to Scarlet Vale's mining office. The party's standing with the crown drops one step.

- **Later-game — when Hannes's pressure breaks the line, choose between the post and the men he runs.** *Conditional — opens once the war-iron pressure has crossed the line that Mella has been holding (`doc/npcs/mella.md` late-game). Calder is the *parallel decision*: Mella from the engineer's side, Calder from the warden's side.* *What the NPC says* (in private at his office, late at night, with the chipped mug between them; only after the party has earned a *second-register* relationship with him through prior inspections, conversations with Mella, possibly the Osric extraction having gone cleanly): *"I have been a warden for twenty years. I have buried convicts I should not have buried; I have signed orders I have not been proud of; I have run a mine the crown has wanted run hard. **The pressure now is past what the post can absorb honestly.** I have one of three answers ahead of me. I sign the next pressure-order and another convict goes down a flooded shaft. I refuse the order and the crown removes me — the next warden will be worse, and I am not the man who hands a worse man the seven keys without a fight. Or I write the report Lady Linthrope has been waiting twenty years to read — what the post actually does to the men in it, what the convicts actually cost, what the procurement chain has been hiding — and I send the report and resign. The third path costs me; the crown will not love me; my pension is the crown's to give. **Tell me what you have seen at the capital. I am asking honestly. The post is not the king. The man is not the post. I am trying to read the line."* — *Where:* the warden's office for the conversation (the chipped mug; the late hour); **Lady Rhianna**'s residence at Scarlet Vale for the report's filing if Calder chooses path three (`doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md` — *she has been waiting twenty years*); **the mine** for whatever happens in the months after, depending on his choice. — *What:* bring him an honest read from the capital — Rhianna's view; the king's reading-circle posture; the war-frame's near-term shape. *Do not push him toward a particular answer*; he has been thinking about this for years; the input is the gift, the choice is his. — *Reward:* whichever path he chooses, **the campaign's quietest political moment lands**. Path one (sign and continue): Calder breaks under the post's pressure; Mella's late-game becomes a referendum on whether to leave; the convict compound's grim register persists. Path two (refuse and be removed): the next warden is worse, but Calder *does not hand over the keys* without a fight; the campaign records the refusal. Path three (the report, then resign): **Rhianna's report-cycle gains the document she has been waiting twenty years for**; the king's reading-circle has to engage; the political ground shifts a degree. **Standing rises with Rhianna substantially** if path three is chosen; standing rises with Mella *quietly* (she has been holding the slope and never said so to him; Calder writing the report acknowledges her hold). — *Complications:* he is *not* the kingdom's hero. Path three costs him his pension and possibly his standing for the rest of his life; he is willing to pay; *the file is honest about the cost*. **The party that pushes for path three when he is leaning toward path two**, or vice versa, loses the conversation; he reads being pushed and closes. **A party that has not earned the second-register relationship** (through honest inspection conduct, through working with Mella respectfully, through handling Osric well) does not get this conversation at all; he files them as *crown-side visitors* and the late-game closes to them.

## How he reads at a glance

- **A middle-aged human in a crown-issued coat.** The cuffs have been re-stitched three times; *Reading* identifies the original tailoring as Scarlet-Vale-quartermaster's standard issue. He has not requisitioned a new coat in twelve years.
- **Seven keys at his belt** — *Reading* identifies six purposes (vault, gate, office, quarters, sealed cabinet, seal-press); the seventh is to a lock no one in the mine recalls Calder having opened. *Open thread*; could be his predecessor's private cabinet, could be a sealed convict cell from a previous era, could be his own inheritance from a prior post (DM-fill).
- **The chipped mug** at his right hand — gift from a former convict who did not survive the year; he knows that and uses the mug anyway. He has never explained the chip to a visitor.
- **Eyes that read a room before he speaks** — but read for *threat-and-procedure*, not for the phrase Osric's eyes catch. He sees what crown-side training trained him to see; he has narrowed the rest.

## Visible patterns

- **At his desk most of the day.** Two stacks; the chipped mug; the seven-key ring on its hook. The clerk in the outer office handles foot-traffic; Calder handles the desk.
- **Walks the slope once a day, briefly.** Not to inspect — to *show himself*. The convicts see him; the foremen see him; Mella does not look up. The walk is twenty minutes; he returns to the desk.
- **Tea three times a day** in the same chipped mug; the kitchen knows the schedule.
- **Eats in his office.** Has been doing so for years; the warden's table is a courtesy he does not invite the engineers' crew to share.
- **Reads outgoing post.** All of it. Mella has known for two years; Calder knows that Mella knows; *neither has said so to the other*. The mutual unspoken is part of how the mine has continued to function.
- **Visits Scarlet Vale once a season** for the quartermasters' briefing. Returns with a stack of orders; reads them at his desk that evening; signs and dates the next morning.

## At the warden's office — the inspection scene

Where the dungeon's negotiation begins.

- **The desk and the chipped mug.** Standing-orders register; courteous; rehearsed.
- **The clerk in the outer office** — DM-fill name; loyal to the post by training; not by enthusiasm. *Reading* identifies the clerk as *not a hardliner*; he can be addressed separately if the party knows where to find him.
- **The records vault behind the office** — third shelf from the left, the *special cases* folder, the sealed document Rhianna sent the party for (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md` §"At the vault"). Calder leaves the office for meals three times a day; the access window is real; the party that takes it has the document.
- **The seven-key ring on its hook** — Calder watches it without seeming to. A party member who has *Hand* and a clean approach can lift one of the seven during a single distraction; the lift is risky and Calder will notice the absence within an hour. The records-vault key is not the one to lift first; the *records vault* key is also at the seal-press for outgoing-post-marking, kept loose on the desk during business hours.
- **The chipped mug** is on the desk during the inspection; a party member who looks at it directly notices the chip is shaped like a small fingerprint; Calder may, if asked plainly and at a quiet moment in the second-register conversation, name the convict who chipped it.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about being a warden for twenty years — *what the post does*

> *"I came to Clear Water as deputy at twenty-six. Made warden at thirty-one. The post does what the post does; it does not ask the man to like it. I have buried convicts I should not have buried; I have signed pressure-orders the crown wanted signed and the men in my care paid for; I have written letters to families that the truth would have helped more than the lie I sent. **The post and the man are not the same thing.** Most of my predecessors lost the distinction; I have kept it; I am not proud of every decision I have made under the post's pressure but I am still able to look at the chipped mug. The day I cannot — that is the day I write the report and resign. I have been thinking about that day for two years."*

### When asked about the chipped mug — *the man who gave it to me*

*(Second-register hour; trust real; quiet.)*

> *"The mug was given to me by a convict named Aelin Resshen — Beaver-folk, sentenced for a crime I do not believe he committed; six years on the wash-tables; the year I was promoted to warden. He chipped it on his last shift in his second year; the chip is from where his thumb sat against the rim. He gave me the mug six months later, when he was dying in the infirmary; he said it would remind me which side of the desk I was on. I have used the mug every day for nineteen years. The chip is shaped like his thumbprint. I have not told anyone the story before tonight. *He was right.*"*

### When asked about Mella — *the engineer who has held the slope*

> *"Mella runs the wheels. She has run them better than the crown's procurement chain has supported them; she has held the slope past what the warden's pressure should have permitted; she has been *slowing the slope* for at least two months and I have been pretending not to notice because the pretence is the only working compromise the post permits me. She does not look at me when I give orders. She is right not to. I do not respect the post that requires me to give those orders; I do respect her for not pretending she does either. **We work in parallel.** It is the most honest working relationship I have at this mine."*

### When asked about Trull — *the foreman in the second compound*

> *"Trull does what he can in a post that permits very little. He gives lighter shifts when he can; he reports what the standing orders require; he does not report more than that. I do not press him for more. The understanding is unspoken; *he reads me as a warden who is not the worst man for the post*; I read him as a foreman who is not the worst man for his post; we have been working at the line of what the post permits for years. He has a family in Linar harbour; the family is the leverage anyone with serious intent could use against him; the leverage has not been used in my tenure and I have not added Trull's family-name to the records the next warden would inherit."*

### When asked about Hannes — *the king and the pressure*

> *"The king is not the king his predecessor was. The pressure rolls down from his quartermasters; the quartermasters are reading the war-frame and the iron-demand is what the war-frame requires. **I have not blamed Hannes for the pressure**; I have blamed the post for absorbing it badly. The line I am walking toward is not a line against Hannes; it is a line against *what the post requires of the men in it under this rate of pressure*. If the king reads the report I am thinking about writing, he may slow the pressure; if he does not, the post will continue to break the men in it; either way, I have made my reading."*

### When asked about Lady Rhianna — *what her letters carry*

> *"Lady Linthrope's standing at court is not what most courtiers' is. She has been writing to me — and to my predecessor before me — about the convict count for two decades. She has never demanded; she has documented. *She has been waiting for a warden who would write what she could not.* I am thinking about being that warden. **Her hour-glass is honest.** I have respected her without ever meeting her in person. If you carry word back to her — she will know how to read this conversation. Tell her the chipped mug is still on the desk. She will know what the line means."*

### When asked about the seventh key — *what the lock holds* (refused-with-context)

> *"That is a private matter. **You will not earn the answer through asking.** I will tell you what I will say: the lock is not on a convict cell; the key is not crown-issued; what is in the lock is mine. If you have read what I have said about the post and the man — *the seventh key is on the side of the man*. That is enough."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed in the official register** — the courteous response. *"Standing orders permit . . ."* — the rehearsed clause. He does not visibly tighten; the institution speaks for him.
- **When pressed in the second register** — the chipped mug rotates a quarter-turn in his hand before he answers. The pause is small; the rotation is the tell. The answer comes after; honest within the bounds of what he will say.
- **When distraught** — visits the records vault alone after hours. Re-files specific folders that are already in order; the act is meditative. Does not write while distraught.
- **When happy** — rare; visible only to a party at the second-register hour. *A small dry chuckle; the chipped mug raised an inch.* The smile fades fast.

## What he will not say

- **The chipped mug's chip-shape** to a stranger; Aelin Resshen's name only in the second register.
- **The seventh key's lock.** *"On the side of the man"* is the most he says.
- **Trull's family-name and location**, beyond the general fact. The protection is real; he has not added it to records.
- **Mella's grandmother's working-line** — he does not know it; if he learns, he will not name it.
- **Specific convicts' names**, beyond what standing orders require he share.
- **What he would write in the report**, before he has decided to write it.
- **His own family**, if any. Open thread.

## What he can teach

- **Crown-side institutional reading** — how a post absorbs pressure; how standing orders compound; how a man under twenty years of post-pressure thinks. Trust-gated; rare.
- **The post's procedures** — how warden-side operations actually run. Useful for any campaign that engages with crown-side dungeon politics elsewhere.
- **The line between the post and the man** — by example, not by lecture. A party member who has watched him at the desk for several visits learns a *register* about institutional service that no other NPC carries.

## DM notes

- **Calder is the dungeon's *negotiation surface*, not its antagonist.** A DM running Clear Water (`doc/places/clear_water_mine.md`) should treat him as *the institution the party negotiates with*. He is competent; he is tired; he is not cruel; he is bound by standing orders. The friction is *with the orders*, not with him.
- **The official-register / second-register distinction is the dungeon's politics.** First-visit, official-register; the party that earns the second register through honest conduct (not bribes; honest engagement) gets the second-register conversations across visits. The chipped mug's story and the seventh-key acknowledgement are second-register beats.
- **The late-game *line he is walking toward* is the parallel to Mella's.** Both characters are deciding whether to break under the war-pressure; both are honest about it; *neither will be talked into a path they have not already considered*. A DM running both threads in parallel has the campaign's quietest political arc — *the warden and the engineer, deciding separately whether the post can hold honestly*.
- **Path three (the report) is the campaign's documented quiet-good.** If Calder writes the report and resigns, the campaign records the document at Rhianna's residence; the king's reading-circle has to engage; *the political ground shifts a degree*. This is not the kingdom's hero-moment; it is *one man writing what twenty years let him see*.
- **The seventh-key lock is an open thread.** A DM who wants to develop it has *the side of the man* as the framing. Possible: a private cabinet at the office holding personal letters from his own family (open who they are); a sealed record from a previous post; an inheritance from a former mentor. Do not over-resolve; the *not-knowing* is part of the character.
- **Calder will not be a friend.** A party that ends the dungeon expecting to have Calder as an ally has misread him. **He will be the warden who closed the inspection cleanly**, and at the second-register hour he will be the man who told them the story of the chipped mug. That is enough.
- **He pairs with Mella as parallel-decisions** and with Trull as the post-and-conscience pair. The Clear Water political quartet (Calder + Mella + Trull + Osric) is now complete; a DM running the dungeon's full political arc has all four files to draw from.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Came to Clear Water as deputy at twenty-six.** Promoted to warden at thirty-one. Twenty years in the warden's chair as the campaign opens.
- **No documented family.** Open thread. The seventh key may relate.
- **Has corresponded with Lady Linthrope across his tenure** — letters from her about the convict count; documentation rather than demand. She has been waiting twenty years for the warden who would write the report.
- **The chipped mug** was given by Aelin Resshen (Beaver-folk; six years' sentence; died in the infirmary the year of Calder's promotion). Calder uses it daily; the chip is shaped like Aelin's thumbprint.
- **Has buried more convicts than he is comfortable with.** The list is in his head; he keeps it.
- **Is thinking about writing the report.** Has been for two years. The party's input is the gift, not the trigger.

- **Calder's family.** Open. May or may not exist.
