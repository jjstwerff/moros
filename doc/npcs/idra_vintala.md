# Idra Vintala

- **Role:** Pre-rift Vintala-line apprentice; **the painter** whose signed work survives at four sites across the campaign. Same line as **Felicia Vintala** (`doc/npcs/felicia.md`); a friend of the **Lethran Pair** (`doc/claude/PRE_RIFT_PAIR.md`).
- **Species:** Human (in life). Vintala-line. Apprentice rather than full practitioner — she chose the painter's path within the line rather than the working-mage's.
- **Location:** Historical — pre-rift. Her studio was at the white city; she travelled to the broken-lands and desert sites to paint at the Pair's commissions; her family-arms panel at the **Lethran dwelling** (`doc/places/lethran_dwelling.md`) was her gift, not a commission. **Her body is somewhere in the white city's ruin** (DM-fill on which room; she was at the white city when the rift came and did not survive).
- **Status:** Deceased at the rift. **Her work persists** — four signed pieces across three sites are the campaign's most consistent surviving pre-rift visual record.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Deceased Vintala-line painter; the *consistent signature* across the Pre-Rift Pair adventure's surviving art. The party that has been to the Generator, the Anchor, and the Lethran dwelling has met her work four times before they meet her name.
- **Standouts:** *No body, no presence at scale.* What stands out is **the recurring signature** — a small Vintala-line working-mark in the corner of each painting; a *fourth-figure-in-the-corner* convention she used when she was the painter signing as a friend rather than as a commissioned hand. Her brush-style is distinctive: **stylised figures with hands rendered carefully** (the Vintala line worked threads; she painted hands that *would have known how to work them*).
- **Speech tag:** Through her paintings. The Lethran dwelling's family-arms panel reads as her *voice* most clearly — the fourth figure in the corner is her, signing the gift, looking out at whoever finds the panel. *"I painted what was here. I painted it carefully. I was a friend."*
- **Quests:** Early — *cross-reference the signatures across all four sites; recognise the painter as a recurring figure; learn her name.* Late — *find what survives of her at the white city; if anything personal of hers remains, return it to the Vintala-line keeping at Luchebert or to Felicia's shrine.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *She is not a person to meet.* What can be done is to *recognise her work as work* — to see the friend behind the gift, the apprentice behind the commission. Players who pass over the murals as set-dressing miss her entirely; players who read the signatures meet her four times.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/idra_vintala.md`.*

## What the party meets — the four signed works

Idra is recognisable only through what she painted. The party meets her through cross-referencing the signatures across four pieces. Each piece carries her working-mark; sustained reading reveals the same hand.

- **The Generator's south-wall mural** (`doc/places/generator.md` §"At the working chamber"). The system at full power. Idra signed her work *in the leftmost figure's robe-hem* — her standard convention for a commissioned piece. *Reading* identifies the Vintala-line working-mark.
- **The Anchor's family portrait at the bench** (`doc/places/anchor.md` §"At the working chamber"). The Pair, the child, a desert-fox at their feet. Same hand. Signature in the same convention; **cross-checking with the Generator confirms the same painter**.
- **The Anchor's west-wall finished-system painting** (same file). Companion piece to the Generator's. Same hand again.
- **The Lethran dwelling family-arms panel above the kitchen door** (`doc/places/lethran_dwelling.md` §"At the kitchen"). **The fourth figure in the corner is Idra herself, signing the gift.** This is the only piece where she is *visibly in the work*; *Reading* identifies her as the painter, looking out at the viewer with a small, unguarded expression.

## Personal goals — early and later

A deceased painter does not have *goals* the way a living NPC does. The framing here describes **what the campaign can do with her work** — what the party can recognise, what they can carry forward.

- **Early-game — recognise the recurring signature; cross-reference the four works; learn her name.** *What the work says* (across sites; cumulative): *"The same hand painted at the Generator, the Anchor, and the dwelling. Three commissions and one gift. The painter knew the Pair as friends. The painter signed a fourth-figure-in-the-corner only at the dwelling — only at the family's home."* — *Where:* the **Generator's south wall**; the **Anchor's bench portrait** and **west-wall finished-system painting**; the **Lethran dwelling's family-arms panel**. — *What:* read the signatures at each site; cross-check the Vintala-line working-mark; the second-time signature recognition lands at any second site; the third confirms the painter; the fourth (the dwelling) reveals the friendship register through the corner-self-portrait. **Felicia, with the necklace at her shrine** (`doc/npcs/felicia.md`), can name the painter — *Idra Vintala* — if the party brings the cross-referenced sketches and asks who painted in this hand. — *Reward:* the campaign gains a *named pre-rift minor figure* whose work the party has been seeing all along. The painter becomes *a person the party met four times before they knew her name*. The *kindness-as-practice* register the Pair worked in is now legible across one more practitioner; the Vintala-line tradition gains a second face in the campaign (Felicia + Idra). — *Complications:* the party may pass over the signatures as ornament. A DM should *foreground* the working-mark on the second visit (Anchor) — *the players are reading the same hand; they are meeting a person*. Felicia will name Idra only with the cross-referenced evidence in hand; she does not volunteer the name unprompted, because *she does not always remember the names of her line at this remove*; bringing the sketches *helps her remember*.

- **Later-game — find what survives of her at the white city; if anything is recoverable, return it to her line.** *Conditional — opens once the party has named Idra and earned the standing to reach the white city's centre (which they likely have through the Generator-arc).* *What the trace says* (in the Generator's working chamber, after a careful *Druid* or *Shaman* reading: a sense of *another room nearby that the chaos has not erased*): *Idra was at the white city when the rift came. Her studio was three rooms down the corridor from the Pair's working chamber. The chaos has wrong-built much of the city's centre, but the Vintala-line preservation working at her studio held — narrowly — and what is in the room is what she was working on the morning of the rift.* — *Where:* **the white city's centre**, three rooms down from the Generator's working chamber (DM-fill on the exact corridor); the **studio** itself, narrowly preserved by a Vintala-line preservation working that has been failing slowly for centuries. — *What:* find the corridor (after settling or downing the chaos almost-construct so the working chamber is safe to leave from); read the studio; carry away what survives — *one final unfinished painting* on the easel (the Pair, the child, and a fourth figure who is not Idra — a sketch where Idra was painting *a fifth person* into a family piece for the first time); a folder of preliminary sketches for paintings that were never made; a small bound book of *Idra's own working-tongue notes on her painting craft*. — *Reward:* the campaign gains *Idra as a person, not just a signature*. **Returning the unfinished painting to Felicia's shrine** (`doc/npcs/willow.md`; cross-ref `doc/npcs/felicia.md` for the Vintala-line keeping) closes a Vintala-line thread Felicia has been *drift-aware of without knowing why* for centuries; her drive registers the closure. **Returning the working-tongue book to the Luchebert library** (`doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`) gives Father Elliot a piece of pre-rift practice the library does not yet hold; standing rises with him substantially. — *Complications:* the studio's preservation working has been failing; the room's contents are *fragile* — *Hand* matters; a careless hand damages the unfinished painting. The fifth figure in the unfinished painting is not named; *Reading* the Pair's correspondence at the Anchor and the Lethran dwelling cross-references against the sketch may identify the figure (DM-fill — possibly the Pair's child as an older self, suggesting Idra was painting *a future the family did not reach*; possibly a colleague at Fata morgana; possibly the painter's own younger sister). **Open thread**: the fifth-figure question is the campaign's quietest mystery and may be left unresolved.

## How she reads at a glance — the four signatures

Idra has no presence at scale. What the party encounters is the working-mark, and the cumulative reading of four works.

- **The Vintala-line working-mark** — a small geometric form, a circle within a triangle within a square, the lines crossing at specific points the line uses as a signature-grid. Identical across all four works.
- **The hand convention** — *figures' hands rendered carefully*; the Vintala line worked threads, and Idra painted hands as if they would *know how to work them*. A *Reading* check at any of her works identifies the hand convention by the second site.
- **The fourth-figure-in-the-corner** — used only at the Lethran dwelling. The Vintala-line painter's convention for *signing as a friend rather than as a commissioned hand*; appears in canon sources as a known mark for *gift-paintings to households the painter knew personally*.
- **The brush-style** — soft edges; preserved colour palette in her surviving work (warm browns, blue-greens, faded reds — the rift took the colours she was using on the morning of the failure; the studio's last canvas may show a colour that does not appear in any of her finished works).

## Visible patterns — what her work does across the campaign

- **A recurring signature across three sites of the Pair adventure.** A party reading systematically meets her four times before they meet her name.
- **The hands in her paintings *anchor*** — a *Druid* or *Shaman* studying the figures' hands at her commissions feels something *settle* (the Vintala line worked threads; her painted hands are *tradition-honest*).
- **The studio's preservation working** holds at the white city, narrowly. A party that has been to the Generator may *feel* the nearby room without identifying it; the working's fading is *audible to a Shaman* as a slow leak in the working-tongue's register.
- **Felicia recognises the hand** in the necklace's condensation, if shown a sketch — *not as memory in the personal sense; as the line's pattern*. The recognition is partial and offered carefully.

## What the work will tell — and when

The work's content is gated by reading and cross-referencing.

| Tier of reading | What the work gives |
|---|---|
| First sighting of a signed piece | Recognition of a *signature*; nothing more if not cross-referenced. |
| Second sighting at a different site | *Same hand* — recognition of a recurring painter. |
| Cross-referenced reading of all four | The fourth-figure convention; the gift-painting register; the painter as a *friend*. |
| Felicia's identification (with cross-referenced sketches) | The name *Idra Vintala*; her relation to Felicia's line; her presence at the white city when the rift came. |
| The studio's recovery (late-game) | Idra as a *person*, not just a signature — her own working-tongue notes; the unfinished painting; the question of the fifth figure. |

## Mannerisms — what the work does

- **The fourth-figure convention persists only at gift-paintings.** A party reads this as the painter's *friendship-signature*. The dwelling is the only known site she signed as a friend.
- **The hands in her paintings get more careful as her work matures.** A *Reading* check across the four pieces dates them — the dwelling panel is the latest; the Generator mural the earliest.
- **The colour palette warms over her career.** Early work uses cooler tones; the dwelling panel is warm. *The rift took the colours she was using on the morning it came.*
- **The Vintala working-mark stays identical across all four pieces.** The line's signature does not change with the apprentice's maturation.

## What the work will not say

- **Idra's age, full background, family of origin** beyond *Vintala-line*. The line is what the work declares; the rest is open.
- **Where her body lies.** The white-city centre is the working assumption; the specific room is open.
- **The fifth figure's identity.** Open thread; possibly resolvable through cross-reference, possibly not.
- **Idra's relationship status, family-side, partners.** Open. The campaign may leave it un-narrated.

## What the work can teach

- **Vintala-line painting craft** — through study of the four pieces and (late-game) the working-tongue book at the studio. A serious painter or *Hand* student earns real practice.
- **Reading a painted figure's *hand-convention*** — the Vintala-line discipline. Useful at any other Vintala-line surviving work the campaign opens.
- **The friendship-register signature** — the fourth-figure convention as it appears at the Lethran dwelling. A campaign-spanning visual literacy.
- **Pre-rift colour palette** — through pigment study at the studio. Atmospheric; not portable.

## DM notes

- **Idra is the campaign's most quietly recurring named minor figure.** The party meets her *four times* through her paintings before they meet her name. A DM running her well *foregrounds the signatures on each visit* — describes the working-mark in passing at the Generator, makes the same-hand recognition click at the Anchor, lands the fourth-figure self-portrait at the dwelling, and lets the players bring sketches to Felicia for the naming.
- **The naming is the early-game payoff.** *Felicia names Idra Vintala* is the moment; the players have been reading the painter as a stranger, and the great spirit gives them the name. Run it carefully; the recognition matters more than the name itself.
- **The studio is the late-game reward.** Three rooms down from the Generator; preserved narrowly by a Vintala-line working; recoverable by a careful party. *The unfinished painting is the campaign's quietest open mystery* — who is the fifth figure? A DM may resolve it (the child as an older self; a Fata-morgana colleague; a younger sister) or leave it open. Both are honest.
- **Idra is a friend, not a romance.** The fourth-figure convention is *friendship-register*. Do not romanticise the painter's relation to the Pair; *she signed the dwelling panel as a friend*. The line matters.
- **The Vintala-line connection ties to every Vintala canon piece.** Felicia is the great spirit; Idra is her line's apprentice-painter; Penelope's old tutor at Fata morgana may have been Vintala-adjacent (DM-fill). The line is *small, dispersed, and recognisable* — a campaign that has read the Vintala thread carefully recognises the working-mark across multiple figures' work.
- **The studio's preservation working is fragile.** A DM running the white-city late-game should let the room *be* fragile — the players' careful handling matters; carelessness costs the colour-of-the-rift detail and other small textures.
- **Do not over-reveal.** Idra is *minor*; she is *quiet*; she is *one apprentice in a line of many*. A campaign that builds her into a major figure changes the register. The pleasure of her file is in the *recognition*, not in the *expansion*.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Vintala-line apprentice**, junior to Felicia within the line. Chose painting over working-mage practice — within the Vintala line this was a recognised path; the line was small enough to accommodate one painter per generation.
- **Studio at the white city** for her active years; travelled to the broken-lands and desert sites for the Pair's commissions.
- **A friend of the Lethran Pair.** The dwelling panel is her gift; the Anchor's family portrait is a commission with the friendship visible underneath.
- **Painted Felicia once in life** — a small sketch beside her studio bench. Sentimental.
- **At the white city when the rift came.** Did not survive. Studio narrowly preserved by a Vintala-line working she had set against an unrelated risk; the working has been failing for centuries; the room is reachable now but will not be in another generation.
- **Body location open** (DM-fill); probably in an adjacent room.
- **The fifth figure in the unfinished painting is open** (DM-fill); the campaign may resolve or not.
