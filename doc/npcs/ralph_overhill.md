# Ralph Overhill

- **Role:** Young camp follower and animal handler; Elmsfield recruit for travelling parties that need someone to watch the mounts
- **Species:** Badger
- **Location:** Elmsfield (available to meet by asking around town)
- **Status:** Alive; young, unattached, willing to go

## Summary

Young badger who loves horses and knows his way around draft and riding animals generally. He is who the party ends up with when they hit the **travel dilemma**: if they want to be free to go exploring off the road, to split into smaller groups, or to leave camp to pursue something — their animals need someone to look after them. An unattended horse tied outside a ruin, a donkey left with the packs in a dell, a mule waiting at the edge of a forbidden zone: each is a problem that needs solving *by a person* if the party wants to move without losing their mounts.

Ralph is that person. Asking around Elmsfield turns him up pretty quickly — he is not hard to find, and he is willing. He loves the work. He asks fair pay and is satisfied when it comes.

## What he can and cannot do

- **Can:** look after horses, donkeys, mules, pack animals of most species with competence. Break and tend camp in the party's absence. Keep the fire going. Read the mood of an animal that is getting nervous before most travellers would notice.
- **Can:** **flee with the animals** if pressured. This is his signature move. If a camp comes under a threat bigger than he can face, he does not stay to fight; he gets the mounts moving and goes. The party may come back to an empty campsite and a trail leading elsewhere — but their horses are alive, Ralph is alive, and their gear is where he could carry it. That is his value: *preservation under pressure*, not defence.
- **Cannot:** **defend a camp against a real threat.** He is young, he is a badger, and he has no military training. If the party expects him to hold a position against an actual attack they have misunderstood what they hired. Sent alone against combatants, he will die. Sent alone against a bear, he will also die. He knows this.

## Role in the campaign

Ralph is the **logistical solution** to one of the setting's recurring frictions. Moros rewards getting off the road — into Laurent's wreck, into the forbidden woods, into the buried tower, into the blasted lands — and getting off the road usually means leaving the pack animals somewhere. The party can leave them tied up and lose them to whatever comes along; or they can bring Ralph. The second option costs them fair pay; the first costs them their transport.

He is also a **quiet campaign fixture**. Once hired, he is around. He does not initiate plot, but he witnesses things the party does not — what visited the camp while they were away, which travellers passed through, what the horses heard in the dark — and a DM who wants to pass information to the party at third hand has him available. He is not a mystery and he is not a villain. He is the young man looking after the horses, and he is worth his pay.
