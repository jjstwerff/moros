# The Volcano Tower

- **Role:** One of the six towers ringing the Lake of Tears; cage-shackle of the bound air elemental; houses a trapped fire elemental
- **Location:** Around the Lake of Tears — one of the five seized towers moved into the ring by the old-order mages
- **Status:** Active; occupied by a large bound fire elemental

## Summary

Shaped from the outside like a small volcano — stone cone, wide rim, open top — with flames visible on the inside and heat shimmer rising constantly. At the centre of that cone is a **large bound fire elemental**, trapped in the tower's enclosure as part of the tower's original workings. The elemental **lashes out at anyone trying to climb the rim**, throwing heat and fire at any approach from above.

The tower was one of the five seized-and-moved towers; its original mage is long gone (killed in the taking, fled, or faded into history). The fire elemental has been here as long as the ring has, and has been angry about it for most of that time.

## The two levers

The Volcano Tower has **two ways to break its shackle**, one relational and one violent.

### Befriend the elemental (relational)

Bring the elemental **things that burn**. Wood, oil, pitch, anything it can consume. Do this enough, and do it in a way that treats the elemental as a being rather than a disposal chute, and it warms to the party. Once it is fed and willing, it **produces enough energy to let the tower float** — the whole cone lifts, slightly, above the ground. From there it can follow the group out of the ring.

This is the intended path and the one consistent with the other relational levers (the pot-boy, the Storm Mage, the sinking tower's bound owner, Gerhald's consent). It costs the party effort and attention; it gains a mobile tower and, potentially, a new companion spirit.

### Enrage the elemental (violent)

Push the elemental until it breaks out. The tower's enclosure is strong but not infinite, and with enough provocation the fire elemental will **burst the containment**. When it does, it lashes out at everything present — the party, anyone nearby, the surrounding terrain. Eventually, having vented, it **leaves for the mountain range** to the east.

The shackle is broken either way. The cost is higher: the tower itself is ruined, the party is in immediate danger from an enraged bound elemental finally free, and whichever settlements or creatures lie in the elemental's path out are not safe either. This is the **dangerous option**. DMs running this path should make the cost concrete and remembered — Gerhald, in particular, will weigh this heavily when the party arrives at his door for the final shackle.

## Role as spirit-shield (partial)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a large bound fire elemental is another mighty-spirit-reader presence around the Lake of Tears, layered on top of Felicia, Laurent, Gerhald, and the pot-people. Its presence is part of what keeps chaotic-spirit pressure out of the Lake region. Breaking its shackle by the relational route keeps the elemental alive and its shielding contribution at least partially available, even as the tower moves. Breaking it by the violent route ends the elemental's contribution to the region's cover entirely.

## Relationships

- Original mage is unknown/long-gone; the tower was seized in the taking of the ring.
- The **fire elemental** is the tower's main resident and the lever for the shackle — a being, not a furnace.
- A potential ally to **Laurent** (whose city the ring was built to bring down) and to the other wronged tower-owners and -inhabitants, once freed.
