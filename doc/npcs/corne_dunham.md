# Corné Dunham

- **Role:** Local guide; Elmsfield's valley-and-hills man
- **Species:** Badger
- **Location:** Elmsfield and the surrounding valley
- **Status:** Alive

## Summary

Elmsfield badger; knows the valley — the paths, the seasons, the passable and impassable ways through the hills. The practical kind of knowledge the rest of **Felicia**'s settled region runs on without having to think about it. Part of Elmsfield's dispersed **Badger clan** presence (see `doc/npcs/bridget.md` for others).

## The hill route to Luchebert

Of the handful of people on the Allondo side of the Great Swamp who know how to reach **Luchebert Monastery**, Corné is the most accessible for a party based in Elmsfield. He has walked the **inland route over the hills** himself — the path that leaves the river behind, climbs across ridges, and descends into the vale from above — which is the approach **Irna Bean could not find from the water**. The river-route that Irna tried is the one that *does not* work: the vale's river side is cliffs a traveller cannot climb, and the obvious valley-mouth is overgrown enough to hide itself from anyone who expected the path to be on the riverbank.

Corné will describe the hill route plainly if asked, and will guide a party along it for a day or two if the request is reasonable and the timing is right. He is the party's **in-world answer** to the gap CAMPAIGN.md §"The Monastery Road" leaves open.

## Clear of the fallen city

Corné has **always tried to stay clear of Laurent's downed city**. He has never been inside. His hill route to Luchebert actively routes *around* the wreck rather than past it, and he does not treat the avoidance as optional. When pressed about why, he gives a hill-man's answer — old stones, bad feelings, things that are not meant to be walked through — and he is **genuinely superstitious** about the hills around the wreck. Not performatively superstitious, the way some tavern-tellers are; the way of a man who grew up on stories that turned out to be true.

The stories behind his avoidance are concrete. **Over the years, people from Elmsfield and the surrounding valley have gone into those hills treasure-hunting — and some of them never came back.** The wreck of Laurent's city is known, in vague local terms, as a place where old mage-era riches must still be; and everyone who decides to find out confirms the fear a little more by disappearing. Corné can name a few of them himself if the party asks. The specific mechanism is not something he has reasoned out (and see `doc/places/laurents_city.md` for what actually kills intruders there) — he only knows that the pattern is stable, long, and not random, and that *that* is the kind of thing you take seriously when you are a valley guide who intends to grow old.

This matters two ways for the party:

- **As a guide to Luchebert, he is reliable.** The route he knows avoids the wreck, which for most parties is a feature rather than a limitation.
- **As a guide *to* the wreck, he is not available.** If the party wants Corné to lead them *into* Laurent's city, they will need a different local — or go without one. He will not walk the wreck with them. He will, however, point them at its general direction from the outside; he just will not step across the threshold.

## Role in the campaign

The **in-world pointer to the Luchebert route** that CAMPAIGN.md §"The Monastery Road" flags as a necessary next step. A party that has saved Irna and knows she needs to reach Luchebert, and who asks around Elmsfield, will be directed to Corné by Diederik, Bridget, or anyone else in town who knows the valley well enough to know who *does* know. He is the answer to "how do we actually get there."

Possibly also a secondary pointer for the **Storm Mage** in the hills, whom CAMPAIGN.md names Corné as a potential source for — though Corné's personal knowledge of that figure is open (see below).

## Relationships

- Part of Elmsfield's dispersed **Badger clan** (see LORE.md §"Clans and their shape"; others in town include **Bridget**, **Wilfred**, possibly **Ralph Overhill**).
- Known to **Elder Diederik** as the town's practical valley-man; Diederik will refer the party to him for route questions.
- Has had no direct dealings with **Laurent** or anyone inside the downed city, by choice.

## Open questions

- Would he reconsider if the party asked him to accompany them to the wreck's *outer* ruins rather than inside?
- Does he know anything about the **Storm Mage** living in the hills, whom CAMPAIGN.md lists him as a possible source for?
