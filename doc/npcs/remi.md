# Remi

- **Role:** Rat factory worker, expelled from World Edge with the rest of his kin
- **Species:** Rat folk
- **Location:** Expelled from World Edge; travelling south toward the river settlements his uncle had mentioned
- **Status:** Alive, displaced
- **Carrying:** Master Darius's letter of recommendation

## Summary

A young rat who worked the forge factories of World Edge doing what nobody else would — crawling into dark spaces, fixing machinery by touch alone, working hours that never showed up on the books. Honest worker, well-regarded by **Master Darius**. When the mayor of World Edge ordered all rat-kind out of the city, Remi went with them. The justification given to him as he was hauled out by the bull was that even if he personally was honest, most of his kin were not, and no rat could be trusted above family loyalty.

Remi caught himself weighing years of unrecorded labour against the covering he had done for dishonest relatives, and he was not sure which weighed heavier. He left with a letter of recommendation from Darius (which might mean something in three or four places the badger could name) and a destination he only half-trusted — his uncle had mentioned river settlements to the south where the work was different.

## Relationships

- Worked for **Master Darius**; left World Edge with the letter of recommendation Darius handed him at the factory door.
- Family ties to other rats in World Edge, some involved in petty crime (part of what his expulsion was officially about, whether fairly or not).
- Heard of the southern river settlements through his uncle.

## Note on the name

This character was originally named Egbert in `doc/stories/world_edge_expulsion.md` — the same name as Hank's wolf-folk partner at Elmsfield. The name collision has been resolved: the World Edge rat is Remi, and the Elmsfield wolf is Egbert. Two distinct characters.
