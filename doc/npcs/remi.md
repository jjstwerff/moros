# Remi

- **Role:** Rat folk worker expelled from World Edge with the rest of his kin; carrier of Master Darius's letter; the honest outlier his city decided was no different from his cousins.
- **Species:** Rat folk. Young; small for his age even by rat standards; quick hands and quicker eyes.
- **Lives:** *In transit*, when the party first meets him — on the road south with his family, expelled from World Edge under the mayor's order. Eventually settles at one of the **river settlements south** his uncle had mentioned (DM-fill on which; see *Where he is going*). Until then his home is wherever the family stops for the night.
- **First met:** On the road between World Edge and the river settlements, with the rest of the displaced rat-folk; or at the river settlement he reaches, if the campaign opens after he has arrived; or — earlier — at the factory door in World Edge if the party is in the city the day of the expulsion (see `doc/stories/world_edge_expulsion.md`).

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Expelled rat-folk worker; Darius's letter in his coat; on the road south to the river settlements; the honest one the policy did not separate out.
- **Standouts:** *Small frame, even for a young rat — fits where others cannot.* **Fingertips dark with old factory grease** that has not come out. **Carries Darius's letter folded in his coat**, warm from the apron when he received it; he touches the pocket through the cloth without thinking.
- **Speech tag:** Plain, careful. *"I didn't do nothing"* — the protest he made at the factory; he says it less now. The voice of a young man who is weighing things he had not weighed before. Will not perform indignation. Will not perform shame either.
- **Quests:** Early — *carry his family safely south to a river settlement; deliver Darius's letter to the named keeper; find the work his uncle described.* Late — *build a life the expulsion did not get to define; pull at least one cousin out of the loyalty-trap that cost the family their city.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *He covered for dishonest cousins. He is not sure which weighed more — the years of unrecorded work or the covering.* The party that asks him to perform innocence gets a level look; the party that asks him to perform guilt gets the same. He is doing the weighing himself, in his own time.

## What the party meets

The party is on the road south of World Edge — the inland trade road from the volcanic coast toward the river country and the forbidden-woods fringe — when they overtake (or are overtaken by) a slow procession of rat-folk on carts. **Families with bundles.** Children who do not understand the pace they are being asked to keep. **An old rat woman on a cart that holds everything she owns**, watching the road they have left without a face the party can read.

Remi is somewhere in this. Slim, young, working a cart-axle that has not been right since the bull's men loaded the family's belongings without care. **A coat with the letter in the inner pocket** — he touches it through the cloth when he is thinking, the way a soldier checks his sword-belt at a halt.

If the party stops to help — with the axle, with the food, with the road south — Remi does not perform gratitude. He says thank you plainly, accepts what is given, and asks what the party knows about the road ahead. If they have come from World Edge, he asks one question: *"Has anyone been told to follow us?"* The answer matters. The bull at the door said the family was at the start of their journey out; Remi is not sure the start is also the only stretch.

## How he reads at a glance

- **Small frame, even for a young rat.** Fits into spaces an adult does not reach. Carries himself a little smaller than he is — a habit from the factory floor, not yet shed.
- **Fingertips dark with old factory grease.** Years of crawling into machines by touch alone. The colour will go eventually; the eye for what a machine is doing will not.
- **The coat.** Plain, dark, worn at the elbows. **The inner pocket has Darius's letter in it.** He does not show it casually; he touches it through the cloth when he is unsettled.
- **Eyes that read a stranger before reading a friend.** Not paranoia — habit. The factory taught him which men coming through a door meant the work was about to change.
- **A small bundle of tools at his belt.** A worn knife, a thin file, a length of wire. The tools of someone who has fixed things in the dark.

## Visible patterns

- **Walks the cart's track at every halt.** Looks for stones, for ruts, for the wheel's wear. The family does not ask him to; he has appointed himself the cart's keeper, the way someone who knows how things break would.
- **Eats last.** The mother and the children first; the older relatives next; Remi when there is enough. Does not announce this.
- **Does not speak about the cousins.** When the family talks about who in the household did what — the cousin who took, the cousin who covered, the cousin who lied to the bull — Remi is at the cart, working, ears open. He does not contribute. He does not interrupt.
- **Touches the letter once a day.** Through the cloth, not taking it out. It is the part of his life that is not yet decided.
- **Watches the young fox he used to eat lunch with**, in his head, on the long evenings. (See §"What he will not say".)

## On the road south — the family-group scene

Where the party most reliably meets him. The procession is real and continuous; the family-group is fifteen, twenty, twenty-five rats and their belongings; the road is the inland road heading toward river country.

Three things stand out about the road-scene:

- **The pace is the children's pace.** The young children cannot keep an adult's marching speed; the family will not leave them; the procession crawls. **Remi reads this clearly** and accepts it; the party will hear him, more than once, telling another adult to slow down. *"They are not running. We are not running. We do not perform the mayor's pace for him."*
- **The carts are over-loaded.** Rats brought what they could; the carts are heavy; the wheels are not what they should be. Half the road's work is keeping the carts moving.
- **The family talks past Remi.** He is twenty-something, an adult by years, but the older relatives discuss the household in front of him as if he is younger. He does not correct them. He works the cart.

## At the river settlement — when he gets there

DM-fill on which settlement; the candidates are the **river towns south of the forbidden woods** along the great river's course (`doc/places/geography.md` §"The great river"). Likely a small-to-mid town with a working harbour, a fishery or two, light forge work, and the looser politics of a place far enough from any capital to do its own naming.

Three things stand out about the arrival scene:

- **The settlement was not waiting for them.** The locals know an expulsion happened up the coast; they did not know the families were on this road; the welcome is by negotiation, not by ceremony.
- **The letter does the work the family alone could not.** Remi finds the named keeper Darius pointed him to (DM-fill: a forge-master who owes Darius a season of materials, a merchant-house clerk, a fishery owner). The letter is read; the keeper looks at Remi, looks at the family behind him, asks one or two questions, decides. *"Yes."* The work begins.
- **Not everyone in the family is welcome.** The keeper's *yes* is for the work and for the workers who can do it; the cousins the policy was actually about are still cousins. Remi will spend the next year deciding which of them he covers for and which he does not.

## Stories he tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the expulsion — *what the bull said and what I did not say back*

> *"The bull said *most of you are not honest*. He said even an honest one would not put honest work above family. I said *I didn't do nothing*. He had me by the tail. The grip did not loosen. Master Darius came in then. He asked the bull *but why*. The bull answered him with policy; Master Darius did not answer back. He gave me a letter. The letter is in my coat. The bull walked me out of the factory. The other workers looked at the floor. A young fox at the cooling vats met my eye for a moment and looked away. We had eaten lunch together most days for two years. I do not blame him. I do not entirely forgive him either. I do not know which weighs more."*

### When asked about the cousins — *what I had been doing for them* (trust-gated)

> *"I had been covering. Not for the worst of them — they did not need my covering, they had their own. I covered for the small things. A late ledger; an inventory short; a tool that had walked. I told myself each time it was a small thing, that it was family, that it was what you did. I had not thought about what it looked like from the outside. The bull's answer made me think about it. Some of what he said was not wrong. Some of it was. I will not give you the names. I am still working out who I will cover for next time and who I will not."*

### When asked about Master Darius — *what he is, what the letter is*

> *"Master Darius is the forge-master. The letter says I am capable and honest and that someone reading it should give me a fair chance. He said his name on it would mean something in three or four places he could think of. He did not stop the bull. He did not lead anyone against the mayor. He gave me the letter and went back to the forge. I have thought about that. I think I am angry that he did not do more. I am also not sure I would have wanted him to do less. The letter is what I have. The letter is enough to start. Whether it is enough to land is what I am about to find out."*

### When asked about the young fox at the cooling vats — *what we did not say*

*(Trust-gated. Quiet voice.)*

> *"He was a fox. He was a friend. He looked at me, and then he looked at the floor. I had been about to wave. I do not know what I would have wanted him to do; the bull had me by the tail and the bull had a hand on his hammer. Whatever the fox did, the bull was going to do what the bull was going to do. The looking-at-the-floor saved nobody and cost nothing. Saved nobody and cost nothing is what I think about now when I think about him. I will not name him. He has his own life. The expulsion was not the only test he is going to be given."*

### When asked about the river settlements — *what my uncle said*

> *"My uncle has not been to them. He had heard about them through a cousin of his who had heard about them through a man at a market. The work is *different*. He could not say what *different* meant. The river country is not the volcanic country; the towns are smaller; the forge work is lighter; the fishing is real. Men there do not all work for one master. That is what *different* meant, I think. That is what I am about to find out."*

### When asked what he wants — *the small list*

> *"To work. To be paid for the work. To live somewhere I can be hauled out only by my own decisions. To send a letter back to my mother when she is settled, in a hand she can read. To know whether the cousins I covered for are worth covering for. That is the list. It is short. I have been told it is small enough that it might fit."*

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed** — small. Even smaller than usual. Looks at the speaker's hands rather than face. Answers shortly. The answer is rarely a defence; it is the fact, plain.
- **When distraught** — touches the letter through the coat. Does not take it out. The hand stays on the pocket until the wave has passed. Will not speak during this.
- **When happy** — quick, narrow smile that does not quite reach the eyes; ducks the head; goes back to whatever work was in his hands. Most often when a machine he is fixing comes back to working order, or when one of the children laughs at something the road has not yet beaten out of them.
- **When the bull's voice comes back to him** *(an internal mannerism a careful party will read)* — a slight set of the shoulders; a tightening of the hand on whatever tool he is holding; the eyes go to the road behind them. It passes. He does not name it.

| Brings him news that the young fox at the cooling vats has done something — good or bad — since the expulsion | Goes very still. Asks them to repeat it. Does not say what he is going to do with the news. |

| Threatens his family | Stands between them and the threat. He is small; he is also fast and has a knife. He will not strike first if he can avoid it. The bull at the factory door taught him what hands the size of small trees can do; he is not going to invite that on his own people. |
| Asks him to come back with them to fight World Edge's mayor | Refuses. *"That fight is not mine to lead. The mayor is the mayor. I am a rat with a letter."* |
| Shows him a hidden mechanism — a lock, a sealed crate, a working that needs reading | Cannot help being interested. The fingers are already moving. *"Show me."* Will work the problem with the party for an evening if the work is honest. |

## What he can teach

For students who can sit at a workbench in the dark:

- **Mechanism work in the dark.** What a machine is doing when you cannot see it. Learning by touch, by sound, by the temperature of a casing. He is unusually good at this.
- **Crawling into spaces.** Practical. The size of the space, the way to fit, the way to come out without leaving anything behind. Not dramatic; useful.
- **Reading a workplace before the workplace reads you.** Who watches; who does not; whose voice carries; whose order is policy and whose is something else. The factory taught him; he can teach it.
- **The discipline of weighing what you did.** Informal; by example. A party member who travels with him for a season and watches him refuse to perform either innocence or guilt learns something they cannot get anywhere else cleanly.

## What he will not say

- **The names of the cousins he covered for.** That decision is his to make.
- **The young fox's name.** *"He has his own life."*
- **What he thinks of his uncle's choice to send them south specifically** — the uncle made a guess; the guess may land; Remi is not yet ready to praise or blame the guess.
- **How frightened he was when the bull's hand closed on his tail.** The bull is a bull; the grip is the grip; he does not name the fear.
- **Whether he will ever go back to World Edge.** *"I have not decided."*

## DM notes

- **Play him small, not pitiful.** Remi is a young man weighing things, not a victim performing displacement. He is honest about what he did and did not do; he is not asking for the party to make the decision for him. A DM running him as a *poor expelled rat* misses the character; a DM running him as *a young man making real decisions about his own life* runs him correctly.
- **The letter is the door.** Darius's letter is the campaign-mechanic that closes the early-game arc — recovery of work, dignity, footing in a new town. A DM running the Darius quest delivers the party to Remi or Remi to the party; the closing of the chain is shared between Master Darius's file and this one.
- **The cousins are open DM territory.** The file does not name the cousins or specify which ones did what. A DM building out the rat-family extended household can fill this in to fit whatever the campaign wants — small petty crime, a more serious matter, or a misread the bull made on the family. The campaign's eventual position on the expulsion depends partly on what the cousins actually were.
- **The young fox is a thread.** The friend at the cooling vats is unnamed in the file; a DM who wants a return-to-World-Edge thread later can develop him — fox-folk worker who acted (or did not) after the expulsion, who Remi may meet again on his own terms. Up to the campaign.
- **The river-settlement keeper is the receiving end of Darius's letter.** DM-fill on the specific keeper. Make them recognisable as one of the *three or four* Darius can name; let the keeper's standing in the river settlement matter for how Remi lands.
- **Remi can be a recurring NPC.** A party that runs the Darius arc cleanly leaves Remi placed; later returns can find him at the river settlement, working, settled, ready to receive a follow-up letter or carry a small piece of work the campaign needs. He is not a one-scene character if the table wants more.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Rat folk worker** at one of World Edge's forge factories. Crawled into spaces no one else would; fixed machines by touch in the dark; worked unrecorded hours that never appeared on the floor's books.
- **Apprenticed under Master Darius** — taken on against the casual prejudice of the floor; trained; given Darius's letter at the factory door the day of the expulsion.
- **Mother, two brothers, an uncle, and a sprawl of cousins** in the expelled family-group. The uncle had heard of the river settlements south through a chain of three hearings; that is what is sending them on this road.
- **Covered for cousins on small things.** A late ledger; an inventory short; a tool that had walked. Was not the bull's primary target; was lumped in with the rest of the family by policy. Has not finished deciding which cousins are worth the covering and which are not.
- **Carries Darius's letter in his coat's inner pocket.** Touches it through the cloth, daily.
- **Does not yet know whether he will return to World Edge.** Will not decide before he has lived in the river settlement long enough to know whether *different work* meant something he wants.
