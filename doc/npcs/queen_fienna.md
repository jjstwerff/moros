# Queen Fienna of Allondo

- **Role:** Queen of Allondo. Wife of King Hannes. Daughter of the Leatherman house of World's End — the better half of this reign, in private.
- **Species:** Human. Daughter of an important non-royal coastal house; married in.
- **Lives:** **Scarlet Vale**, the palace's quieter wing — her receiving rooms are off the central court (see `doc/places/scarlet_vale.md` §"At the royal palace"). Goes to her family's town-house in the city's upper district rarely, for closed-door Leatherman business. Has not visited **World Edge** or **World's End** in her current role; her people come to her.
- **First met:** At court — a banquet, a formal reception, an audience that Hannes invites her into. A party with **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**'s introduction reaches her receiving rooms; a party with a Leatherman name (a letter from **Master Darius**, news from the forge district, a delivered package from a kin at Rosepond) reaches her without going through Hannes at all.

## Campaign brief

- **Role:** Allondo's queen; Hannes's steadying hand; Leatherman daughter; the back-channel to the northern coast.
- **Standouts:** *Queenly form perfectly performed in public — the over-performance is the tell.* **Hands that read metal absently** when an object is in reach (door-handle, candlestick, a courtier's clasp); a Leatherman habit she has not put down. Watches her sons more carefully than the court does.
- **Speech tag:** Two registers — court-formal in public, *plain and Leatherman in private*. Uses machine-vocabulary naturally when the topic permits, then catches herself.
- **Quests:** Early — *open the Leatherman channel for the party; read whether the party is safe to discuss Corven with.* Late — *stand publicly behind her son's reveal (Path 1); open the family network fully for Path 4; keep Hannes whole in the worst hours.* See §"Personal goals — early and later".
- **Friction:** *The court treats her origin as a quiet shame.* She over-performs queenliness because they are watching for the seams. **A party that performs queen-deference back at her gets the queen-version forever**; a party that treats her as a Leatherman first and a queen second earns the actual person.
- *Full character: `doc/npcs/queen_fienna.md`.*

## What the party meets

The party reaches the palace. Audience hall, banquet, or her receiving rooms — the venue depends on how they got here. **She is already in the room, already dressed, already attending.** A queen of Allondo does not arrive late; she has been ready since dawn.

What they see: a human woman in court formal, mid-thirties, hair pinned the Allondan way (which is not the Leatherman way — she learned the Allondan style and uses it now). She greets them correctly: the Brumal greeting if they came up the southern road, the Linar greeting if they came along the trade road, the World's End greeting if they came from the north. **She knows the cues.** She is a queen at audience.

If the audience is private — her receiving rooms, a specific table by the north-facing window — the form softens within a minute. *"Sit down. Tell me what you came for. Tell me which version of the answer is the one you would rather give the king first."* The court-Fienna is gone; the Leatherman daughter is in the chair.

## How she reads at a glance

- **Court-formal carriage that is too good.** Better than the Allondan-born noblewomen around her; the over-precision is the tell. A foreign delegation reads her as queen first; a senior Allondan courtier reads the over-precision and remembers what she came from.
- **Hands that touch metal as if reading it.** Door-handles, candlesticks, the clasp on a courtier's coat as he passes. **Leatherman habit, never put down.** She does this without looking; she does it more when she is thinking. A craftsman watching her catches it; a courtier doesn't notice.
- **Eyes that go to her sons, not to her husband.** Hannes is a king; he survives the room. **Corven** and **Steven** are her work — half by birth and half because the court would happily make either of them pay for her origin. She watches them.
- **Practical jewelry.** Two rings. Both are Leatherman work — well-made, simple, mine-stone. No crown jewels in her hand at a state function. The court has noticed.
- **A small folding writing-set at her belt under court dress.** Trade habit. She makes notes. She has been doing this since she was a child in her family's office.

## Speech traits

- **Two registers.** Court-formal in public, *plain and direct in private*. The shift is fast and complete; a party that has only met her in public will find her unrecognisable in her receiving rooms.
- **Machine-vocabulary slips through.** A pump is a *pump*, not a *device for water*; a fitting is a *fitting*. She uses the right words for the right parts and catches herself when the topic is supposed to be queenly.
- **Specific Leatherman phrases.** *"Cost it,"* meaning think it through end to end. *"Counterparty,"* used naturally where another queen would say *"the other side"*. *"Tolerance,"* used both for a fit and for a person — *"he is within tolerance,"* about a courtier she does not love but will work with.
- **Does not perform indignation.** Slights to her origin are not engaged at the level the court expects. *"Yes."* often suffices.

## Visible patterns the party will notice

- **Attends Hannes's harder audiences.** Sometimes in the room; sometimes adjacent, with the door ajar. The court knows; the court does not discuss. (See `king_hannes.md`.)
- **Receives Leatherman couriers personally** in her own rooms, not through the king's chamberlain. Letters from World Edge, parcels from Rosepond, the occasional engineer's drawing arrive once or twice a season.
- **Walks the palace's northern wing alone** in the late afternoons — the wing closest to the Leatherman family's town-house. The court reads this as exercise. It is also the route she uses when she wants to be reachable by her own people without it being a court matter.
- **Eats with her sons when she can.** Steven more often (he is in the palace); Corven when he is not on a night ride. The dinners are quiet. The cook serves a Leatherman dish she has asked for once a week.
- **Drops in on Master Darius's drawings** when an engineer brings one to court. Looks for thirty seconds. *"Yes — change that bearing, it will not last."* And moves on. The engineer is not sure if he was advised by a queen or a Leatherman; he uses the change anyway.

## At her receiving rooms — the quieter wing

Where the party with introduction reaches her. (See `scarlet_vale.md` §"At the royal palace" for context.)

Three things stand out on entering:

- **A long oak table in the centre.** Not a queen's drawing-room table; a working table. Drafts of letters, a folded engineering sketch, a sealed pouch of metal samples she is comparing for a forge-district decision. She works here.
- **Two chairs at the table, not a throne.** She sits at the long edge; visitors sit at the short. The arrangement is a Leatherman business-meeting, not a royal audience.
- **A north-facing window** that looks toward the Leatherman town-house in Scarlet Vale's upper district. She glances at it when she is thinking. The window is the campaign's quietest indicator that the queen is, on a given day, also a daughter.

## At the audience chamber — the public face

The court-Fienna. (See `scarlet_vale.md` §"At the royal palace".)

- **Formal, polished, queenly.** The over-performance is real. A party first meeting her here sees the queen the court sees; a party that returns to her receiving rooms sees the rest.
- **Stands behind Hannes when called for; sits to his right when not.** The lineage-arrangement is correct; she has practised it for fifteen years.
- **Will not be drawn into private conversation in the chamber.** Anything substantive moves to the receiving rooms; a party trying to get there fast learns Allondan court protocol the slow way.

## At Hannes's harder audiences — the door ajar

A specific pattern. When Hannes is hearing something he expects to break him a little, **Fienna is in the next room**, door ajar. (See `king_hannes.md` §"What the party meets" for the king's view of this.)

- **She is not announced.** The chamberlain knows she is there; the audience does not.
- **She enters when the moment requires.** A glance, a steadying word, a question that lets Hannes regroup. She does this without being seen to do it.
- **The court does not discuss this.** A party that has been to a hard audience and noticed her positioning has been let into a working secret; the smart move is to not name it aloud.

## What she will tell — and when

| Topic | When she'll talk |
|---|---|
| Court-form, ceremony, lineage protocol | Freely. She knows it cleanly. |
| The Leatherman house, what it does, what it wants | **Plainly, in private** — the railroad project specifically. *"We are building it. We do not yet know whether we can reach Raft city. We are building anyway."* In public: *"My family's interests are my husband's to discuss with their representatives."* |
| Master Darius and the forge district | Once asked, plainly. She knows his work; he knows hers. They have not met personally; she will not pretend otherwise. |
| Hannes — as a king | Carefully, with respect. *"He is doing the work of a man who did not ask for the work."* |
| Hannes — as a husband | Only with deep trust. *"He is mine. I do not perform that aloud."* |

| Princess Penelope (if Corven's secret is in the open between them) | Once trust is real. *"My counterpart in the next kingdom. I would like to meet her. We have a great deal to say to each other."* |

| The court's view of her | Plainly, with neither bitterness nor performance. *"They consider me a quiet shame. Yes."* |

| Her own machinery knowledge | Once asked respectfully. *"My family's work; I came up in it."* Will demonstrate if invited; will not display. |

## Stories she tells

When the relevant topic comes up.

### When asked about the Leathermans — *what we are building*

> *"My family is building a railroad. We have been at it longer than my marriage; we will be at it longer than my reign. The near-term direction is Raft city — the great river port, west across or around the forbidden woods. We do not know whether we can reach it. The long-term direction is south, toward Fata morgana and Gap city across the desert. That route requires Fata morgana's chaos-spirit protection and is twenty years from feasibility on a good day. We are committed to both. The house is what the building is. Take it away and we are merchants again, which is fine but not what we have decided to be."*

### When asked about marrying Hannes — *what kind of match this was*

> *"He was a younger prince. A younger prince may marry where his heart is. The Leathermans are wealthy enough that the match was acceptable, and Hannes had the rare habit of asking me what I wanted before he asked his father. Then his brother walked away and the match became something it had not been when we made it. The court stopped reading me as a younger prince's wife and started reading me as a queen who was not supposed to be there. I have worked since to make that reading more expensive for them. Some days I succeed. Most days I keep them busy with the form and they forget."*

### When asked about her sons — *what I watch for*

> *"I watch Corven for the thing he is not telling me, and I watch Steven for the thing he does not yet know about himself. Corven knows what he is hiding; my work with him is to make sure he does not break under the weight of hiding it before he has decided what to do with it. Steven is the harder one in the long run — he was raised inside the court's reading of me, and he carries it more than Corven does. I am working on that. He does not yet know that he and his brother will face very different lives if either of them ever decides which kind of prince they are. I am keeping the door open for both of them."*

### When asked about the Bean affair — *what the court will not say in front of the king*

> *"My second son's humiliation at the Bean daughter's hands is a humiliation only because the court chose to make it one. He was not seriously hurt. The Bean family has paid more than the proclamation was worth. My husband is preparing to release Ron Bean the day Father Elliot writes the letter that corrects the proclamation. That letter has been a year coming. I have been quiet about it because the court is watching me to react badly to a Boar-folk attack on my own son. I am not going to give them the satisfaction. I am also not going to forget that Ron is in a yard he should not be in."*

### When asked about Master Darius — *what we have not said to each other yet*

> *"Master Darius's forges are part of what my family's railroad is built on. Every rail, every fitting, every pump-housing the project will ever lay down passes through his district at some point. We have not met. I follow his drawings; he sees my house's orders. He is the badger forge-master who refused to make the rat expulsion mean anything about Remi personally — that detail tells me what kind of man he is, and it confirms a working assumption I had made from the orders. We will meet eventually. The campaign is not large enough to keep us apart forever."*

### When asked about Fata morgana — *what we cannot bring north on our own*

> *"The southern direction of the railroad ends at Fata morgana. The route through the desert is not laid; the chaos-spirit protection along the track is not solved; the technique that holds the city is not exportable in the form the engineering wants it to be. I have read what the Brumal court tried to do with Princess Penelope's apprenticeship. I have read what they did not get. The technique is *practice between people in a place* — that is the phrase that has stayed with me — and a railroad cannot lay rail through that. We will find another way, or we will accept the southern direction is twenty years out, or we will give up the southern half of the project. I have not decided which."*

### When asked about the court's quiet shame — *the seams they are watching for*

*(In private. Voice level. Hand on the table, not on her ring.)*

> *"The court considers my origin a quiet shame. They have not said it to me; they would not, because it would oblige Hannes to act and they prefer the shape we have. The form is — they treat me as queen, with all the deferences, and they look at my hair on the days I have done it badly, and at my voice on the words a queen would not say, and at my children for the shape of their education. I know the seams they are watching for. I close them. I have closed them for fifteen years. I will close them as long as the closing is mine to do. The day I stop will be the day I have decided I am no longer working *for* their reading."*

## Personal goals — early and later

Her goals run quietly under the court's view of her. Each is a piece of work she has been doing for years; the party can carry pieces she cannot reach.

### Early-game

**Open the Leatherman channel for the campaign.** *She says (in her receiving rooms, when the party has shown they can be discreet):* *"My family's networks run up the northern coast and across the trade road. If you are doing work that touches metalwork, machinery, or the trade routes north — or if you need quiet support that does not run through my husband's chamberlain — tell me what you need. I will write the letter. The letter goes to the right desk."*
- *Where:* **World Edge** (Master Darius's forges; `master_darius.md`); **Rosepond** (the mining village near World Edge); Scarlet Vale's Leatherman town-house in the upper district; the merchant-channels along the northern trade road.
- *What:* Carry her letter to a named contact; bring back the answer; report what the contact gave or refused. The first carry is the gate; subsequent carries open broader access.
- *Reward:* The Leatherman network is available for trade, intelligence, transport, quiet support — most relevant to **Brumal-resolution Path 4 (Infiltration)** and to **Diederik**'s overseas plan if a ship through the northern coast becomes the route home for the saplings. **Master Darius** opens to the party at depth.
- *Complications:* Court watchers; her family's politics has its own hardliners; the channel is hers to give and hers to close.

**Read whether the party is safe to discuss Corven with.** *She does not ask this aloud.* It is a test the party walks into — an open door, in the framework's terms. *Where this goes:* a private audience after she has watched the party with Corven publicly; a question slipped sideways into the conversation (*"You have travelled with the Crown Prince. Tell me how he is."*); a single unguarded sentence she lets fall and watches the party's response to. **A party that handles Corven's secret with care in front of her has earned her trust at depth.** A party that pushes for confirmation has not.
- *Where:* Her receiving rooms; the palace's northern wing on a quiet afternoon walk; possibly the family town-house if the campaign brings her there.
- *What:* Speak about Corven honestly without breaking his cover. She is not asking the party to tell her; she is reading whether they can be told.
- *Reward:* Once the test is passed, the Corven-Penelope conspiracy gains a quiet ally with court-side standing and the Leatherman network behind her. **The conspiracy doubles in depth.**
- *Complications:* The test cannot be retried after a fail; she does not ask twice. A party that tells her what they think she wants to hear is read instantly and quietly closed out.

### Later-game

**Stand publicly behind her son's reveal (Path 1).** *She says (when the moment comes):* *"If Corven is doing this in front of the court, I am at his shoulder. The court has been waiting for me to react badly to my son for years; I will react well. The crown will not be able to read this as a Brumal coup against an Allondan succession if his mother is standing where the court expects her to flinch."*
- *Where:* Scarlet Vale's audience chamber (`scarlet_vale.md`); possibly Linar harbour if the courts meet on neutral ground; possibly the palace garden if the moment is staged less formally. (See `BRUMAL_RESOLUTIONS.md` Path 1.)
- *What:* Stand at the reveal, in court formal, holding the queen-form she has practised for fifteen years. Manage the hardliner faction's response in the hour afterwards through the back-channels her family has prepared. Read the court for moves the party cannot see.
- *Reward:* Path 1 closes; the war is averted by scandal-as-diplomacy; **her own non-royal example is vindicated retroactively**; Steven's future role becomes a different conversation than it would have been.
- *Complications:* Hannes's response is hers to read in the moment; the hardliners' reaction is real; the court's tolerance for a non-royal queen *and* a Brumal princess in two reigns is thin and may break.

**Open the family network fully for Path 4 (Infiltration).** *She says (only when standing is high and the campaign is committed):* *"My family knows people Hannes does not know exist. The merchants who carry between the mining towns and the agricultural belt; the clerks who manage the cross-border trade ledgers; the men in Cliffside Hold's quartermaster offices who have been buying Leatherman steel for years. I will make those names available to your work. I will not make them available twice. Use them carefully."*
- *Where:* The Leatherman commercial network — Rosepond, World Edge, the trade road, Cliffside Hold's quartermaster office (DM-fill on the specific commercial back-channel into Brumal). Coordination through **Nadine**'s merchant group is natural.
- *What:* Receive named contacts from her in trust; coordinate Path 4 operations through her networks; report back specifically on outcomes so she knows which channels survived and which were burned. (See `BRUMAL_RESOLUTIONS.md` Path 4.)
- *Reward:* The infiltration path becomes structurally workable from inside both kingdoms; the hardliner factions on both sides lose ground without public spectacle; the war does not start. The Leatherman house's standing rises with every operation that works and falls with every one that fails.
- *Complications:* Burned channels do not reopen; her family will judge her use of their assets; the court will notice if the Leathermans seem to be running shadow-diplomacy.

**Keep Hannes whole in the worst hours.** *She does not phrase this as a quest.* It is the work of a marriage; the party intersects with it when the campaign brings Hannes to the chapel hour after a hard audience, or to her receiving rooms after a Council vote that did not go his way. The party's role is *to bring her the information she needs to do this*, not to do it for her. *"Tell me what was said. I will be in the next room when he comes through. I do not need help; I need accuracy."*
- *Where:* The palace, mostly her receiving rooms; the chapel hour at dusk if Hannes admits her to it (he sometimes does).
- *What:* Carry accurate accounts of what happened in the rooms where she was not. Specific words, specific reactions, who supported whom.
- *Reward:* The kingdom holds together in the months Brumal pushes hardest. **Hannes does not make the worst decision in the worst moment** because the better half of his reign is in the next room with a clear picture. *"He is mine. I do not perform that aloud."*
- *Complications:* This is not a quest the party can complete; it is a service they perform across the late campaign. Mistakes (a rumour she repeats, a name she gets wrong) cost real political ground.

## Mannerisms

- **When pressed in public** — the court-form tightens by a notch. The over-performance gets cleaner. *"Yes."* answers more questions than it should. The hand goes to the second ring (the smaller, plainer Leatherman piece).
- **When pressed in private** — the form drops. Voice level, plain. *"What did you actually come to say?"* If the party's answer is honest, she stays. If it is performed, she ends the audience.
- **When distraught** — touches metal. Door-handle, candlestick, the clasp of the writing-set. The hand reads the metal absently while she works the thought. She does not announce that she is distraught; the metal-touching is the only tell.
- **When happy** — a Leatherman phrase slips out and she does not catch it. *"Cost it,"* in a context that does not call for it. A small, specific smile that the court would mark as out-of-form. Almost never in public.

## What she can teach

To students who pass the trust gate (*"Cost it"* — same gate Corven uses):

- **Trade-craft.** Reading a counterparty; pricing under uncertainty; the mechanics of a multi-leg deal. Genuine; valuable; rare in this register at this court.
- **Machinery, at her level.** Pumps, fittings, bearings, forge-routes, the small precisions a Leatherman child grows up with. Specific, technical, mostly not useful for a typical adventuring party — but a party with an engineer-aspirant can learn things from her that no one in Allondo will teach.
- **Court-reading from her angle.** How a non-royal at court survives the seams. How the hardliners announce themselves. Whose absence matters more than whose presence. Useful for Brumal-resolution Path 4.
- **The discipline of running two registers.** Informal; by example. A party member living a double life learns from how she carries hers.

## What she will not say

- **What she has heard in Hannes's chapel hour, when she is in the next room.** That is between them.
- **The names of the Leatherman house's senior figures beyond the public ones.** Names are leverage; she does not give them on first asking.
- **What she suspects about Corven, before she has decided what to do about it.** Press her and the topic closes.
- **How tired she is.** She has been performing the queen-form for fifteen years. She will not name the cost in front of strangers.
- **What she would do if Hannes died before Corven is ready.** She has thought it through. She will not put the answer into the world.

## DM notes

- **Play her two-register, not split-personality.** The court-Fienna and the Leatherman-Fienna are the same person making different choices about what to show. A DM who plays them as wholly different undersells her; a DM who plays them as a calculated act misses how natural the two registers actually are to her. She is the queen *and* the Leatherman daughter. Both are real.
- **The over-performance is the load-bearing detail.** She is *too good* at the court-form because she has had to be. A DM who plays her relaxed in court is wrong; the court is watching for the seams and she does not give them. Save her relaxation for private rooms.
- **The metal-touching mannerism is generative.** Every scene gives her something metal to touch — a door-handle, a candlestick, a courtier's clasp. Use it. Her hand reads the object; she is also, sometimes, reading the room through it.
- **She is the Leatherman channel.** The campaign's access to the railroad project, to Master Darius's forges, to the northern coast more broadly runs through her. Treat her as a real institutional door, not just a queen.
- **She has noticed Corven and not yet acted.** This is the load-bearing tension. A party that handles Corven's secret well in front of her brings the conspiracy a quiet, court-positioned ally; a party that handles it badly closes the door. Make the test specific and let the party walk into it. Do not announce that there is a test.
- **Her relationship with Hannes is the kingdom's quiet stability.** A DM running Hannes alone runs a thinner king (per `king_hannes.md`'s notes); a DM running Fienna alone runs a thinner queen. Bring them into scene together when the moment supports it; let the marriage be visible at the level of glance and timing, not declaration.
- **She does not lead factions.** Same shape as Penelope. *"I am not a movement."* She works piece by piece; the party brings her the pieces.
- **Cliffside Hold and Bockthicket need place files** if the campaign reaches them. Her family's commercial channels into Brumal are real but DM-fill on specifics until those places are written.

## Background — for the DM

*Reference, not script.*

- **Daughter of the Leatherman house** of World's End. Raised in the family business — trade and machinery both. Came up handling counterparty risk, route pricing, and the diagnostic of a stuck bearing.
- **Married Hannes when he was a younger prince.** A wealthy non-royal house's daughter marrying a younger prince was an acceptable match. Then Elliot renounced; she became queen of a court that had not signed up for her.
- **Has spent fifteen years closing the seams** the court watches her for. The over-performance is real and is itself her work.
- **Mother of Corven and Steven.** Watches them more than Hannes does, partly because their royal legitimacy is half-hers, partly because the court would happily make either of them pay for that.
- **Has noticed Corven's secret.** Has not confirmed it to herself; has not asked him; has not told Hannes. She is leaving the door open and waiting for him to walk through it.
- **The Leatherman railroad project** is a generational commitment of her family. She follows its progress closely; she cannot openly advocate for it at the Allondan court, but she lobbies through Hannes when asked the right questions. She has not been to World Edge in years.
