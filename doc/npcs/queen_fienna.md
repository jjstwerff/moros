# Queen Fienna of Allondo

- **Role:** Queen of Allondo; wife of King Hannes; daughter of an important (non-royal) house of World's End
- **Species:** Human
- **Location:** Scarlet vale (capital of Allondo)
- **Status:** Alive

## Summary

Wife of **King Hannes**, co-ruler of the small kingdom. She is human, and she is from the **Leatherman house** of World's End — an important but **non-royal** family in the northern volcanic coastal region (forges, mines, merchant-industrial lineages). The Leathermans are **outwards-facing**: they are not content with World's End's isolation and are actively working on a **railroad project** to connect the forge cities of the north to other parts of the continent.

The project has **two intended directions**:

- **The short-term priority** is a line reaching toward **Raft city** — the trade city on the great river, west past the forbidden woods. Raft city is a real commercial target: a major river port, tied into the continent's largest river system, ideally positioned for goods exchange with the northern coast if only a rail line could reach it. The Leathermans *do not know whether they can actually reach it*. The forbidden woods sit between World's End and Raft city, and rail track through chaos-spirit territory is a problem nobody has solved. The route may have to circle the woods entirely, which is a much longer undertaking, or find a chaos-spirit-safe way through — neither is worked out.
- **The longer-term direction** runs **south**, aimed at **Fata morgana** and eventually **Gap city** — connecting the homelands to the independent southern polities across the chaos-infested desert. This route is harder: it requires Fata morgana's chaos-spirit protection techniques applied along the track, and it crosses the continental desert. It is a *later-campaign* ambition rather than a now project, but it is on the Leathermans' maps.

Both directions share the same hurdles — terrain, chaos-spirit interference along certain routes, cost, political approvals across polities that do not trust each other. It has not been built. It may never be. But the Leathermans are *committed*, and that commitment defines them as a house.

Notably, the Leathermans **do not know about Laurent's crashed flying city**. The wreck is outside their awareness; their railroad plans do not account for it, and a route toward Raft city that passed through or near those hills would surprise them as much as it might surprise anyone.

The Allondan royal family treats Fienna's origin as a **quiet shame**. Queens of Allondo are supposed to be of royal blood; Fienna is a Leatherman — moneyed, capable, northern-industrial, but not royal. She knows it, the court knows it, and nobody says it aloud.

She married Hannes at a time when he was a younger prince — when his brother **Elliot** was Crown Prince and Hannes was not expected to inherit. A younger prince marrying the daughter of a wealthy coastal house is an acceptable match. Then Elliot renounced the crown. Hannes became king. Fienna became queen of a kingdom whose court did not quite consider her one of their own. She has been carrying that ever since.

That origin shapes her in specific ways:

- She over-performs queenly form in public, because the court is watching her for the seams.
- She is far better connected to **World's End** than any previous Allondan queen. Her family's trade and political networks run up the northern coast; if Allondo ever needs to reach the forge cities or the mining towns, Fienna is the natural channel.
- She watches her sons more carefully than Hannes does. Their royal legitimacy is only half through her, and she knows it. She is not going to let either of them be hurt by the thing she carries.
- **As a Leatherman daughter, she is genuinely expert in trade and in machinery.** She was raised in the family's business — price and routing and counterparty risk on the trade side, and the mechanical understanding of forges, pumps, drills, rail prototypes and the repair of all of it on the engineering side. She learned this the way a child learns a family's work, by being around it from a young age. *The machinery part is not particularly useful in her current role.* A queen does not get asked to diagnose a stuck bearing. She has the knowledge anyway, and occasionally catches herself about to correct a craftsman who is explaining something to her wrongly, and stopping before she does.

She can teach what she knows, if the student has earned trust and does not come asking as a transaction. Same model as her son Corven's teaching gate, for the same reason — the court has taught her what happens to skills handed over on someone else's terms.

Her own politics, her own view of the Bean affair, and her own read of the coming Brumal war are consistent with this: she is practical, regionally-aware, and quietly wary of a court that would be relieved if she made a visible mistake.

## Relationships

- Married to **King Hannes** — who was a younger prince when they married, not the Crown Prince. The court has not quite forgiven her for becoming queen.
- Daughter of the **Leatherman house** of World's End. The family's networks extend through the northern coast — forge cities, mining towns, merchant lines — and she has kept those connections alive. If Allondo needs anything from the north (metalwork, information, quiet support), she is the channel. The Leathermans are currently invested in a long-term **railroad project** to connect World's End to the interior's cities; Fienna follows its progress closely even though she cannot openly advocate for it at the Allondan court.
- Mother of **Prince Corven** (Crown Prince; martial heir) and **Steven** (second son). Plausibly knows, or has strongly guessed, that Corven is in love with **Princess Penelope of Brumal** — mothers and political intelligence overlap, and Fienna has reason to watch her sons more closely than Hannes can. Whether she has told him, or decided not to, is a DM call.
- Sister-in-law of **Father Elliot** (former Crown Prince, now abbot of Luchebert). Whether she wrote to him about Corven — or about anything — is a DM call.
- Indirect connection to **Master Darius** (badger forge-master of World Edge). Fienna's house almost certainly does business with his forge district. Whether the two have spoken personally is open.
