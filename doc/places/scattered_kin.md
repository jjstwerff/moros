# The Scattered Kin — a blasted-lands dungeon-pattern

- **NPC-tied dungeon** — **tied to Haven**. Not a single site; a **pattern** of sites across the blasted lands. Each visit is a short dungeon-scene; the full arc is a campaign-scale fetch.
- **Type:** Retrieval — iron-puppet parts that have been *moved* by the chaos-pressed region, surfacing at different ruins across the blasted lands. Haven wants them home.
- **Region:** The blasted lands (see `doc/places/blasted_lands.md`). Specifically, ruins scattered across the region's interior — a half-day to two days between sites.
- **Officially:** Nothing. The parts are not on any map. The pattern of their migration is not known outside Haven and (slightly) the party.
- **In practice:** **A walking meditation on loss**, paced across many sessions. The party visits several ruins; each holds a part; each carries a small encounter; each adds to what Haven can reassemble. The climax is the *gathering site*, where the parts have been converging.

## The NPC hook — how Haven asks

**Haven asks if and only if the party has earned standing at the council stones.** The retrieval is not offered to a first-visit party. The request comes in a specific moment: after the party has passed the greeter's filter, after the workshop yards have opened to them, after one or two visits to the stones have happened and the council has taken their measure.

When Haven asks, it is in the form of a **long story told slowly**. One of the older puppets speaks. The council attends in silence. The story is this:

- Puppets have been *missing* from Haven's historical record — not ones that came to Haven and left, but ones that never arrived. Kin the original Haven-settlers knew of and waited for.
- Fragments of those missing have been **surfacing in the blasted lands** over centuries. Haven knows because travellers (rare ones — Wilder, a shaman once, a scholar Haven later refused to receive again) have described iron-puppet parts at specific ruins.
- The parts **do not stay where they surface**. They move. Slowly, across seasons, in a direction that reads as *purposeful*.
- Haven believes the parts are *going somewhere*. They do not know where.
- **They want the parts brought home.** Or, if the parts cannot be brought home — if the destination has already claimed them — they want *what the destination is* known.

The older puppet ends: *"We do not ask you to go. We are telling you what we know. If you travel the blasted lands, you will pass these ruins. We will receive what you bring. We will be grateful beyond what we can say."*

**The party is not paid.** They are trusted.

## What the party meets — the first surfacing

The party's first encounter with a scattered kin is usually **not deliberate**. They are at Silvertrace (see `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Silvertrace") and they pull the wooden man's arm and find no body. Or they are at the Great Thresh and the iron elemental has a piece at its foot that does not belong to the machine. Or they are at Commons House and something is on the doorstep the cerberus does not know what to do with.

**The first part they find teaches them the pattern.** If they are observant, they will recognise the second and third without prompting.

## The seven known sites

Haven names (or the party discovers) seven ruins where parts surface. A DM running the full arc uses all seven; a lighter arc uses three or four. The parts *move*, so a DM can rearrange which site holds which part by the time the party arrives — the arm is at Silvertrace on the first visit, at the Great Thresh on a return, at Commons House on a third. The migration is slow but real.

### 1. Silvertrace — the arm (wooden/ironwood mixed)

See `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Silvertrace."

- **What is visible:** the arm and a piece of head above the swallowing ground.
- **What is readable:** the workshop-mark on the arm's inside, matching Haven's grade.
- **The retrieval scene:** the swallowing ground. A party that has crossed it safely (or a shaman who can call the arm out) gets the piece.
- **The clue:** the arm *was* deeper yesterday; the ground has *pushed it outward* in the direction of the next site.

### 2. The Great Thresh — the torso

See `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At the Great Thresh."

- **What is visible:** a partial torso, half-embedded in the threshing-floor, near where the iron elemental walks its circuit.
- **What is readable:** the torso's chest-plate has a small seal — a **specific workshop sigil** Haven will recognise as belonging to a specific pre-Rift maker.
- **The retrieval scene:** working around the iron elemental. A befriended elemental *helps*; an engaged elemental *ignores*; a provoked elemental *obstructs*.
- **The clue:** there is a layer of dust on the torso's lower half that does not match the threshing-floor's dust — the torso has been rolled here from elsewhere.

### 3. Commons House — the legs

See `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Commons House."

- **What is visible:** a pair of ironwood legs, laid carefully on the doorstep. The cerberus does not know what to do with them.
- **What is readable:** the legs are *arranged*, not fallen. Somebody — or something — placed them there.
- **The retrieval scene:** asking the cerberus's permission to take what it did not know it was guarding. A party that addresses it correctly is allowed; a party that simply takes the legs is met.
- **The clue:** the legs were placed *facing away* from the door, as if they were leaving the house. The direction they face is toward the next site.

### 4. A ruined vineyard press-house — the second arm

Named in no other file. A ruin the party reaches by following the direction the legs pointed.

- **What is visible:** a second arm, lodged in the press-house's crushing-beam.
- **What is readable:** the arm's hand is *gripping* the beam, as if it had reached to support itself as the press tried to fall.
- **The retrieval scene:** releasing the arm from the beam requires *Ingenuity* — the press's mechanism has tensioned wrong since the rift, and the beam could fall on whoever pulls.
- **The clue:** the press-house was being *used* when it was abandoned. Grape-stems are still in the pressing-cradle; someone was making the year's wine when they had to leave.

### 5. An abandoned communal kitchen — the head

A small ruin halfway between Commons House and the ruined press-house. A kitchen that served a labour-gang.

- **What is visible:** the head, on the kitchen's worktable. Eyes closed. No body. Not new; centuries old.
- **What is readable:** the head has been *placed* on the table. A cloth has been laid beside it, and a small pewter cup, as though someone was *sitting vigil*.
- **The retrieval scene:** entering the kitchen. A *warped helper-spirit* has taken the hearth (a hearth-dweller, scaled up — see CREATURES.md §"Encroachments"). The helper is attached to the head and will not let a party take it without resistance.
- **The clue:** the cup is fresh — filled with water within the last few days. The helper has been *drinking with* the head. Something is keeping a vigil.

### 6. A fallen castle's outer courtyard — the chest-plate

Further south, a smaller fallen castle than Laurent's (see `doc/places/laurents_city.md` for scale), crashed across a ridge. The party will find this one if they are pressing deeper into the blasted lands.

- **What is visible:** the puppet's chest-plate, propped against a broken wall in the outer courtyard.
- **What is readable:** the chest-plate has an *inscription* — a name, in old demi-human script. **This is the name of the puppet the parts belong to.** The name is DM-fill; suggested: a name that matches one Haven's council will recognise at once.
- **The retrieval scene:** the courtyard holds two chimeras' worth of nest (scaled down from the Half-Tower — these are blasted-lands chimeras, with a different shape from the desert's). Respect-distance or negotiate-pass.
- **The clue:** the inscription faces outward, toward the ridge. Whoever propped the chest-plate wanted it *read* by a traveller on the ridge above.

### 7. The gathering site — the puppet reassembled (or nearly)

Near the white city's outer ruins. **This is the climax.** The party, having gathered many parts, follows the pattern of direction-clues inward.

- **What they find:** a small chamber in the outer ruins, cleaned and kept. **The puppet is being reassembled here.** Parts the party is carrying fit into gaps in the reassembled body; parts already present show where the migration was headed.
- **Who is reassembling:** DM-choice, with three canonical options:
  - **The puppet itself.** Its core spirit has survived across all the parts. It has been calling its own body home, through the chaos's currents, piece by piece.
  - **Another puppet.** An elder iron-puppet, blasted-lands-bound rather than Haven-bound, who has been reassembling a kin across centuries because it is the only thing left to do.
  - **A shaman.** A reclusive practitioner — not Wilder, not a Luchebert priest; someone older, stranger — who has been doing this work as a *kindness*, or as a *research project*, and will meet the party here when they arrive.
- **The scene:** conversation. Not combat. The party hands over what they have; the reassembler takes it; the puppet is closer to whole than it has been since the rift.

## Treasure — what the NPCs want

- **For Haven:** every part returned home, OR the truth of what happens at the gathering site.
- **For the puppet being reassembled (if it is itself):** to *be whole again*. The party restoring its parts gives it back a self that has been in pieces for a thousand years.
- **For an elder puppet at the gathering site (if so):** recognition. A witness. Someone to remember that the work was done.
- **For a shaman (if so):** a conversation. A contact Haven did not know existed.
- **For the party:** a campaign-scale quiet good. They have done work nobody else has been doing. Haven knows. One day, a fellow-traveller somewhere else knows too.

## If the party does something — what happens

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Ignores the pattern | Parts continue migrating. The reassembly completes without them. Haven hears of it later. |
| Picks up the first part and carries it | They have started. They may notice the pattern; they may not. |
| Asks at Haven after two parts | Haven tells them the story (see §"The NPC hook"). The pattern becomes legible. |
| Returns parts to Haven one at a time | Received each time with gratitude. Haven's standing with them rises substantially. |
| Returns parts in a batch | Received with the same gratitude. The council gathers to see. |
| Follows the direction-clues inward | Reaches the gathering site. The DM's scene unfolds. |
| At the gathering site, hands over parts | The puppet is completed (or near-completed). Whichever option the DM chose plays out. |
| At the gathering site, takes the reassembled puppet back to Haven | A scene Haven has been waiting centuries for. A kin they thought lost comes home. The council stones open to the party permanently. |
| At the gathering site, leaves the reassembly there | A different scene. The puppet stays where it is. The reassembler stays with it. Haven receives the news rather than the kin. |
| At the gathering site, meets the shaman (if that option) | A new NPC enters the campaign. DM-fill: their name, their practice, their relationship to Wilder. |
| Destroys parts, sells them, or loses them | **Haven closes the council stones to them.** The trust was explicit; the failure is explicit. |
| Shares the migration-pattern with Wilder | He listens carefully. He may ask to come along on a later leg. |
| Shares it with the skull gatherers | Complicated. The gatherers collect mage-spirits; an iron-puppet's spirit is not their usual quarry, but they will attend. |

## DM notes

- **The arc is paced across sessions, not contained in one.** A party that is doing other work in the blasted lands (Silvertrace un-warping, the Great Thresh's community roster, Commons House's wall-panels) can integrate the scattered-kin retrieval naturally. Do not rush it.
- **Let the clues point.** Each site's direction-clue is the spine. A party that reads carefully finds the next site without Haven's help; a party that does not can ask Haven. Both are fine.
- **The gathering site's answer is the campaign's reveal.** The DM should pick one of the three options (the puppet itself, an elder puppet, a shaman) based on what serves the larger campaign. *The puppet itself* is the most emotionally direct. *An elder puppet* opens the *Haven has cousins they do not know of* thread. *A shaman* connects this arc to the shamans' long plan.
- **Haven's gratitude is quiet.** Do not turn the return into a celebration; Haven does not work that way. A council scene with the older puppets attending, a slow thanks, a standing-open of further trust — this is enough.
- **The puppet has a name.** The DM should settle on it early — it surfaces on the chest-plate (site 6) and Haven's council uses it at the return. A name with weight for Haven's history.
- **The parts' register is *patience*, not *urgency*.** They have been migrating for a thousand years. The party is not racing a clock. A DM should let the work feel *long* at the table.
- **Compassion-first throughout.** The puppet is being reassembled, by someone or something. The party's role is *helper*. Combat only arises when an encroachment or a creature has taken a part — and even then, respect-distance or negotiation usually works.
- **Not every site every campaign.** A light version runs three sites (Silvertrace, Great Thresh, Commons House) and the gathering site. A full version runs all seven. A DM can add or remove sites based on how many blasted-lands sessions the campaign wants to carry.
- **Returning a reassembled puppet to Haven is a set-piece.** Among the campaign's most emotionally available scenes. The council stones. The older puppets rising one at a time. The reassembled kin recognised. A DM who reaches this scene has earned the right to play it at length.

## Open questions

- **The puppet's name and pre-Rift history.** DM-fill as the chest-plate is recovered.
- **Who is reassembling.** DM-fill at the gathering-site scene.
- **Exact site locations.** DM-fill as the blasted lands are explored; the file names seven but leaves the geography flexible.
- **Whether other puppets are similarly scattered.** Probably yes — the file holds one puppet's arc; the region may have others. A DM can run a second scattered-kin arc with different NPC-tie (a specific pot-person's cousin; a puppet Diederik heard of from Rowan).
- **Whether the reassembly can fail.** It can. A party that destroys parts, or whose choices lead the gathering-site reassembler to disengage, leaves a puppet permanently unwhole. This is a real cost.
