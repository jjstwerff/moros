# Clear Water Mine

- **NPC-tied dungeon** — **tied to Lady Rhianna Linthrope** (with secondary connections to **Irna Bean** and the **Bean arc**).
- **Type:** Allondan crown-run iron mine and convict-labour camp. The mine that held Irna. Still operational.
- **Region:** Allondo's interior, in the foothills east of Scarlet Vale. Reachable by a two-day road from the capital.
- **Officially:** Crown-licensed. Iron goes to the army's smiths and quartermasters.
- **In practice:** **The mine the crown runs hard because it needs iron for a war it is not saying aloud.** Convict labour alongside paid miners. The warden answers to Scarlet Vale, not to the local magistrate.

## The NPC hook — how the party arrives here

The party reaches this dungeon through **Lady Rhianna Linthrope**. She arranged Irna's release from the mine and sent her toward Luchebert (see `doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md`). **There are two things she could not take when she took Irna**, and each of them is a reason she sends the party.

**The specific thing she could not take:** a **sealed document** from the warden's records that proves Irna's imprisonment was illegal on a technicality — specifically, that the paper authorising her to Clear Water named her *for the wrong crime*, on the wrong date, because the clerks in Scarlet Vale were rushed by Hannes's court. A clean document. Crown-signed. With it in hand, **Elliot has cleaner grounds to correct the proclamation** and release Ron; without it, the correction is harder.

**The person she could not take:** a **second prisoner** named **Osric Vey** — a scholar who was imprisoned at Clear Water for writing a pamphlet about the Overseas War's food-crisis origin (he argued the overseas were not conquerors but starving, which the court did not appreciate). Rhianna was allowed to extract one. She had to leave him. She has been carrying the guilt since.

**Rhianna asks the party** — over tea in her residence at Scarlet Vale, with her hour-glass on the table — to **retrieve both if they can**. If they can only retrieve one, she wants Osric. *"The document helps Ron. The man is a man."*

She gives them: a sealed letter to the warden that buys them entry as inspectors from the capital (she has genuine standing here); a description of Osric (tall, thin, ink-stained fingers, a cough that rattles); and a warning — *"Do not show the warden who you are until you have both."*

## What the party meets

The mine complex is a long low sprawl in the foothills. The party sees it from the road:

- **A palisade and gate** at the base of the hill, where the road meets the works.
- **Barracks and mess-halls** along the slope; administrative buildings at the crest.
- **Three shaft-heads** running into the hillside, each with its winch-house and ore-sorting yard.
- **A separate secure compound** at the far end — lower fence, inside fence, windowless building. The **convict barracks**.

A clerk at the gate checks their letter of passage, takes their horses to the stables, and walks them to the warden's office.

The warden is a **middle-aged human man named Calder** — competent, tired, not cruel in the normal sense, loyal to the crown in the way that means he will not help the party beyond what his standing orders require. Rhianna's letter says they inspect *ironwork quality and mine-working conditions*. They get a tour. They get access. They do not get straight answers.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts

- **The iron-smell.** Different from a forge's smell. Mined iron has a wet-rust quality; Clear Water's yards are thick with it. The smell reaches the road half a mile from the works.
- **The convict compound is visibly separate.** Not hidden. Fenced apart. The workers moving between shafts and barracks cross a specific line nobody else crosses. A party reading the compound from the outside sees a handful of faces at the barracks windows.
- **Rote violence.** Not dramatic; *routine*. A convict trips on the path and a guard cuffs him back into line without looking. A foreman at a shaft-head speaks to a miner the way he would to an ox. The mine runs on a low-level pressure of force that everyone here has stopped noticing.

## At the warden's office — the cover and the trap

The party's first scene inside. The warden receives them correctly, shows them the working-records, asks polite questions about their mandate. The scene has two purposes:

- **For the warden:** establish how seriously to take them. Rhianna's letter is good standing. It is not infinite.
- **For the party:** learn the mine's layout from the records, identify where Osric is held, and see the warden's records vault.
- **The document** is in the vault. Third shelf from the left, the *special cases* folder. The warden has not read it in years; he would not know to hide it. But the vault is kept.

## At the three shafts — the working mine

The party gets a tour through the shafts. The warden's clerk walks with them. Sees everything they see; remembers it.

- **Shaft One** is the main iron shaft. Deep, loud, well-lit, dozens of workers; the iron output the army needs is coming from here. A bent iron ore-cart ran over a worker's foot last week; he is in the infirmary.
- **Shaft Two** is shallower — a clay and soft-ore shaft the iron-workers also pull from when the main shaft is blocked. Lighter work. Healthier workers.
- **Shaft Three** is older and narrower. Closed on the tour; the clerk says *"stopped out."* A party paying attention notices **the rails are still oiled and the winch is still greased.** Something is being brought up from Shaft Three that is not on any manifest.

The Shaft Three secret is DM-fill: a black-iron seam the crown is quietly working; a pre-Rift deposit the warden is not meant to know about; a chamber with a mage-artefact the crown does not want officially logged. A party who investigates is into a second-order dungeon the file does not spell out — let it be.

## At the convict compound — finding Osric

A short walk from the main mess. The warden prefers to end the tour here but will walk the party past the fence on request.

- **The compound holds fifty-two prisoners** (current count; printed on the board at the clerk's desk). Almost all are minor political offenders, debtors, or people Hannes's clerks wrote up in a bad week.
- **The foreman is a beaver-folk man named Trull**, stoic and not entirely on the warden's side. He will answer questions precisely and volunteer nothing.
- **Osric's cell is in the second row.** He is there. Coughing. Thinner than Rhianna's description, which means the year has cost him. He recognises Rhianna's *name* if the party whispers it but says nothing aloud. **He is smart enough not to break cover.**
- **Getting him out** requires either: the warden's signed release (which needs the full document retrieval and Rhianna's standing being used in a second step), a bribe to Trull (plausible; Trull is loyal to the warden but not enthusiastically so), or a direct extraction (dangerous; guards on the path back).

## At the vault — retrieving the document

The records vault is inside the warden's office, behind a locked door the warden keeps the key to.

- **Access options**: the warden leaves the office for a meal three times a day; a party with lock-picking or *Ingenuity* can enter briefly; a party with a shaman can approach Trull separately for a distraction.
- **The vault is organised.** The *special cases* folder is third shelf from the left. The document bears Hannes's seal and is clearly a clerk's error. A party can copy it (slow) or take it (faster; more dangerous).
- **Alarm conditions**: if the warden returns mid-theft, the party must explain their presence in a locked vault. Rhianna's letter does not cover this. The play is to brazen it out or to leave quickly.

## At the path out — extraction

A party that has both Osric and the document must get out of the mine. The road to Scarlet Vale is two days and guarded in stretches.

- **The clerk who walked the tour will notice** if Osric is missing at morning count.
- **A night extraction** has a six-hour head start before the clerk compares counts.
- **The road back** has one crown-garrison post the party must pass; a waylay-waypoint with guards but no gate. A clever party may go cross-country; a bold one bluffs through.
- **If pursuit catches them**, the scene escalates into either a running fight or a fast diplomatic scene at the garrison post (an *officer who remembers Rhianna* can be bribed with political information).

## Treasure — what the NPC wants and what else is here

- **For Rhianna (primary):** the document + Osric. The document corrects the Bean proclamation problem cleanly; Osric is personal.
- **For Rhianna (secondary):** anything the party learns about Shaft Three. If there is an unofficial working there, she wants to know.
- **For the party:** iron ore of unusual quality, Trull's possible ongoing intelligence on the mine, a potential ally in Osric (a scholar with Overseas-War-origin expertise — the pamphlet that got him arrested is a useful working paper for any campaign engaging that thread).
- **For the wider Bean arc:** the document means Elliot's correction of the proclamation becomes easier. A party carrying it to Luchebert closes a loop.

## If the party does something — what happens

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Arrives with Rhianna's letter openly | Received correctly. Tour given. Access to the shafts. No access to the vault. |
| Asks the warden directly about Osric | Cover blown. The warden remembers Rhianna's letter and does not like the implication. Tour shortens. Party asked to leave the next morning. |
| Befriends the tour clerk | Small intelligence available. The clerk is not a traitor; he is lonely and talks. |
| Bribes Trull | Trull takes the bribe in specific form (a promise of recourse for his own family, not coin). A discreet extraction becomes possible. |
| Picks the vault lock at night | Possible. Risk of discovery. The document is findable in ten minutes if they know the shelf. |
| Copies rather than steals the document | Lower alarm. The warden may not notice for weeks. Rhianna can work from a copy; she prefers the original. |
| Steals the document | Faster. The warden notices within days. Pursuit begins. |
| Extracts Osric without the document | Rhianna accepts. *"The document helps Ron. The man is a man."* She does not blame them. |
| Extracts the document without Osric | Rhianna is **disappointed** but does not show it. She will fund a later attempt. |
| Investigates Shaft Three | Into a second-order dungeon. DM-fill. A party that presses without Rhianna's authority risks being treated as crown spies. |
| Returns with both | **Scene at Rhianna's residence.** She sits a long moment with Osric. Quiet. The hour-glass turns. She will do what she said she would do with the document. |
| Is pursued and returns wounded | Rhianna covers. She has favours she can call. The party's standing with her rises. |
| Kills a guard or a foreman | **Serious.** Rhianna does not approve. A party that kills at Clear Water carries a specific debt — the guard or foreman had family, and Rhianna will find them and pay what the crown will not. |

## DM notes

- **The mine is *normal*, not evil.** Calder the warden runs a working mine with convicts because the crown's orders say so. The foremen are tired men. Most of the miners are paid; most of the convicts are small offenders. A party coming here expecting a dungeon-of-cruelty finds instead a dungeon-of-routine, which is the register the scene should carry. Grim, not gothic.
- **The document is the practical thread.** It makes the Bean arc cleaner. A DM running the Bean arc without this dungeon has to write around the mechanism; with this dungeon, the mechanism is *physical* and the party earns the correction.
- **Osric is the emotional thread.** Rhianna's regret. A specific man the crown imprisoned for a pamphlet. A scholar who survives Clear Water becomes a potential NPC in later sessions — Luchebert would shelter him, Brumal refugees would listen to him, Nadine might carry his next pamphlet. The DM can use him further.
- **Shaft Three is DM-optional.** The file names it but does not fill it. If a campaign wants a deeper arc — Allondo secretly mining something unauthorised, a mage-artefact being extracted, pre-Rift ore coming up — Shaft Three is ready to open. If not, leave it sealed.
- **Rhianna's hour-glass as the closing image.** When the party returns, the tea, the hour-glass turning once while she reads the document, Osric sitting quietly nearby. *This* is the dungeon's emotional close — not a payout, a *return*.
- **Compassion-first compatible.** The dungeon can be done entirely without violence. Lock-picking, bluffing, bribing Trull, night extraction. The DM should reward a party that works this way.
- **Do not kill Osric on a bad roll.** The extraction risks him — he is sick; the road is hard — but the DM should not make his death a dice outcome. If the extraction fails, he is recaptured; if the extraction is brutal, he is wounded; if the party does the work carefully, he lives.
