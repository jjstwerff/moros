# The Press-house Ridge

- **Type:** Pre-rift vineyard hall on a ridge overlooking the broken lands' middle stretches; the press-house mentioned in the opening prose at `doc/places/blasted_lands.md`. Above ground: ruined hall with stone vats. Below ground: the Pair's regional supply-cache.
- **Region:** Broken lands, on a ridge along the migration line between the trade road's skirt and the white city's centre. A party tracking puppet-parts northward-to-centre passes within sight of it.
- **Officially:** Nobody's. Pre-rift vineyard community; gone with the rift. The press-house stands because vineyard-presses were built to last.
- **In practice:** **A working ruin** — the warped wine-spirit at the stone vats keeps the building partly stable; the sub-cellar holds the **Lethran Pair's regional supply-cache**, sealed mid-shipment when the rift came. The party tracking the migration through this ridge finds the *first concrete reference* to the Pair.
- **Status:** Stable as ruin. The wine-spirit is patient; the cache is intact under stone the chaos has not been able to cut through.

## What the party meets

The party crosses the broken-lands middle stretches (chaos-pressed, *reciprocity betrayed* register; cf. `doc/places/blasted_lands.md`) and reaches a ridge where a **large stone press-house** still stands open to the sky. Vineyard terraces step away from it down the south slope; no vines grow now, but the lines of the terraces are still readable in the grass.

The first thing the party hears is **a slow, regular drip from inside the press-house**. Not water. Something darker. The vats are still working at the slow speed the wine-spirit kept them at; what they press has not been grapes for a millennium.

A party that walks the terraces first reads what was here — *shared work, not homes* — before stepping inside.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The vineyard terraces.** Stepping rows down the south slope, recognisable as cultivation. **Empty, but not collapsed.** The community that worked them was many; the terraces are too long for one or two households. A party that has read the broken lands knows this register.
- **The stone vats inside the press-house.** Three large vats, basalt-cut, dark-stained around the rim. Empty by daylight; a slow viscous *something* drips into the central one continuously. The wine-spirit's residual work.
- **The Lethran seal beside the door.** Carved into a stone block to the right of the threshold — a circle bisected by two parallel lines, smaller than the seals at the Generator and Anchor (`doc/places/generator.md`, `doc/places/anchor.md`). *The Pair were here as guests, not residents.* The seal marks a stop, not a workplace.

## At the terraces — coming up the slope

Optional approach. A party that walks the terraces before entering the press-house reads the community's *shape*.

- **Stone markers at the rows' heads** carry community names. Pre-rift script. *Reading* names families that worked specific rows; some names match those at Commons House (`doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Commons House"). **The bottom of each marker carries a small carved community-glyph** — a clipped vine-leaf in a circle. The same glyph appears on Bridget's slate-encoded side (`doc/npcs/bridget.md` §"Personal goals"); a party that has seen both connects the family.
- **A faded wall-fresco above the lowest terrace's shrine-niche** — pre-rift; the *cultivation rite* the community performed at the year's first pressing. Three figures (a working-master, an elder, a singer) face the bound-spirit's niche; lines of grape-clusters arc between them. *Druid* identifies the rite as a community-binding form the kingdoms have lost; the bound-spirit pictured was the cultivation's local kindly spirit (the one the wine-spirit *was*, before the rift warped it).
- **A small shrine at the lowest terrace's foot** — a niche-stone with offerings still piled. *Coins, a carved grape-press model, a child's spoon.* The community offered to a small bound-spirit of the *cultivation*, not the press-house itself; the spirit warped with the rest of the broken lands and is now part of what the wine-spirit holds.
- **A wandering creature hazard:** the broken lands' encroachment-fauna patrol the open slopes. **Field-flock Jackalopes** (`doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Field-flock Jackalope") graze the terraces; *Druid* reads the field as warped; the jackalopes vanish-miss any attack. Ignore them and they ignore the party.

## At the press-house — the warped wine-spirit

The hall's main floor. The roof is gone; the stone walls hold; the vats stand. The wine-spirit *is* the building's continuation.

- **The vats** carry the slow drip continuously, day and night. *Smell* reads the contents as wine that has been turned past wine into something the body should not consume; *Druid* confirms the wine-spirit is warped (parallel register to Silvertrace, smaller in scale; cf. `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Silvertrace").
- **The wine-spirit can be addressed.** *Shaman* working at the vats finds it slow, sad, and *making the same product it always made* without understanding what changed. Address it correctly — name the community that worked here; offer something non-monetary at the central vat — and the spirit *thins its drip* for a short while, opening the floor's stone seal to the sub-cellar without combat.
- **Forcing the door** to the sub-cellar (without addressing the spirit) wakes the building's defensive working: the vats' contents *rise* over the rim, sloshing across the floor. *Endu* and *Speed* matter for getting clear; a target hit takes Char −1 from the sour burning and Will −1 from the wrongness.
- **The puppet-parts migration passes through here.** The party may see, in a corner of the press-house, **a scattering of small migration-fragments** — a hand from a wooden man, a strap from a working-puppet, a folded cloth from a community's laundry-line. The chaos has been *moving things across the ridge*; what the party finds in this corner has been waiting to be carried further north. Returning these to **Haven** (`doc/places/haven.md`) is a small good.
- **A single Stitchling** scavenges among the corner-fragments — a small chaos-spirit construct (`doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Stitchling") that has wandered down from the migration line and is *sorting* the fragments before the next leg of the journey. It is not aggressive unless cornered. *Shaman* can teach it small structure (Tod-mechanism); a settled stitchling sits beside the corner-fragments rather than carrying them, which *delays* the next migration-leg and gives Haven time to reclaim the pieces.

## At the sub-cellar — the Lethran supply-cache

Beneath the press-house, reached by a stone trapdoor in the floor. **Sealed by the wine-spirit's working** for a thousand years. A party that addressed the spirit cleanly enters by the spirit's permission; a party that forced the door enters with the contents-flood draining behind them.

- **A working-room the size of a small kitchen.** Stone, dry, intact. A wooden crate in the centre, sealed with the Lethran working-mark. **Inside the crate:**
  - A short stack of pre-rift mage tools the Pair had ordered for the Anchor's last fittings — a fine-edged scribing stylus (Tools Material variant); two sealed inkpots (still wet — the Pair-signature small kindness); a bundle of pre-cut Heartwood splints (Special Material per `doc/claude/ITEMS.md`; *the Pair were using Heartwood to sustain the Anchor's preservation working*).
  - **A sealed letter** addressed to *one of the Pair*, in the other Pair member's hand. Dated *six weeks before the rift*. The writer is at the Anchor; the recipient is at the Generator; the letter says *the work is taking longer than I thought; come south as soon as you can; we are running out of weeks.* **A margin note in a softer hand** adds *"give my regards to the family at the ridge — the child's drawings on my desk are the only company I have here."* This is the **first concrete spine reference** the party has found — they now know the Pair existed, that they were a *pair*, that the work was paired across two sites, **and that there is a family at *a ridge* somewhere with a child** (the Lethran dwelling, `doc/places/lethran_dwelling.md`).
  - A small carved stone bearing the Pair's seal in miniature — a *seal-token*, smaller than the Generator's stamp, useful as a recognition piece at any of the Pair's sites. Speeds passage at the Anchor's sphinx by one round of address.
- **A wall-niche** holds three corked bottles of the Pair's house wine — the *real* product the warped vats above used to produce. The bottles are sealed by the same working that holds the room. Drinking ceremoniously is *safe* and lifts spirits; a party that offers a bottle to the wine-spirit at the vats above on the way out *settles* the spirit a degree, slowing the warping. **Thinking of taking the bottles to Bridget at Elmsfield** (`doc/npcs/bridget.md`) — possible; she will pour one for the slate's encoded side.

## Lore the place contributes

The Press-house is the spine's first concrete delivery and the first lore-revelation a party gets at the Pair-sites:

- **The Pair existed and worked together** — the cache letter is the spine's *first concrete* moment, named in the audit as the smoking-gun lore-revelation. Before this, the Pair are an open question; after, they are *real people who corresponded*.
- **A pre-rift vineyard community** — the terrace markers, the lowest-terrace shrine, the wall-fresco of the cultivation rite. The Press-house reads as a *small functioning society* the rift erased; one of the broken lands' clearest pre-rift social pictures.
- **The bound-spirit-vs-warped-spirit contrast** — the wall-fresco shows the *original* cultivation spirit the community honoured; the wine-spirit at the vats now is *that spirit warped*. A party that has read both pictures the rift's *exact mechanism* on a small spirit at scale.
- **The community-binding rite** — *Druid* identifies a form the kingdoms have lost; a party with a Druid student carrying notes can later restore parts of the working at other warped sites (Silvertrace's stream, the Mill-shade at the Grain-mill encroachment-belt).
- **A family-name across two encoded ledgers** — the vine-leaf glyph on the terrace markers matches an entry on Bridget's slate. A party connecting both has reconstructed *one family's full pre-rift trajectory* from medallion-to-ridge.

## What's around the dungeon — the broken-lands canon at this ridge

The Press-house ridge sits inside the broken-lands' middle-stretches register. A party here is in country the file already populates:

- **The migration line passes through the ridge.** The puppet-parts the party tracked from Silvertrace are northward-bound *along this stretch*. A scattering of small fragments rests in the press-house's corner; the party finds matches to pieces seen earlier and pieces they will see at the Generator (`doc/places/generator.md`).
- **Silvertrace** (`doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Silvertrace") sits a day west; a party that approached the ridge from there will have crossed the swallowing-ground.
- **Commons House** (same file) sits a day-and-a-half north; the wall-panels were taken north *past* the press-house, and a careful tracker reads the migration trail leading away from the ridge in the direction of the white city.
- **Wandering creatures** of the broken lands (cf. §"Wandering creatures and environmental hazards"): warped helper-spirits at small wells and ovens between named ruins; a *Hippogriff (Blasted Lands)* may pass overhead but does not land.
- **Ruin-crawlers on the trade-road's skirt** (a half-day north) sometimes carry warnings about *the press-house at the ridge*. A party that listens hears that *a thing drips inside that should not* — accurate, partial, useful.
- **Haven** (`doc/places/haven.md`) is the destination for the small migration-fragments the party recovers. The party that has already met Haven knows what to do with them; the party that has not, finds Haven through this collection.

## Visible patterns

- **The drip never stops.** Audible from outside the door; readable as *the building's pulse*.
- **The vines are gone but the rows are still there.** A party walking the terraces feels the missing community.
- **The wall-niche at the lowest terrace is tended.** Wandering ruin-crawlers leave small offerings; the niche is never empty.
- **The seal-block beside the door is clean of moss.** Something protects the Pair's mark; not maintained, just resistant.
- **The sub-cellar's air does not carry the warping.** A party stepping in feels the change; *Druid* and *Shaman* both register it.

## DM notes

- **The press-house is the spine's first concrete delivery.** Before this, the puppet-parts migration is an *open question*; after the cache letter, the party knows the Pair existed and the work was paired. The DM should run the sub-cellar slowly, letting the players find the letter as the *real* moment.
- **The wine-spirit is the place's conscience.** Treating it well opens the cache; treating it badly costs nothing immediately but cuts off a campaign-long thread of slow recovery. A party that returns to the ridge late in the campaign with bottles of the Pair's wine settles a small thing the chaos has been wrong-building over.
- **The Heartwood splints connect the spine to the Anchor.** The Pair's choice of material at the receiver-end becomes an act of recognition for the party — *they were trying to keep this end alive*.
- **The migration-fragments at the corner are small but real.** Each is a piece of someone's life moved by the chaos. Returning them to Haven (or, occasionally, to a specific NPC who recognises the piece) is the broken-lands' relational-grief register made personal.
- **The Pair's house wine is intact.** The bottles are not items of mage-power; they are the Pair's *household*, surviving a thousand years because the working held. A party that drinks one ceremoniously at the campaign's end shares the Pair's table.
- Lore cross-references: the broken lands canon in `doc/places/blasted_lands.md`; Heartwood in `doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Heartwood"; the Tools Material in §"Tools"; the spine in `doc/claude/PRE_RIFT_PAIR.md`; the Anchor counterpart in `doc/places/anchor.md`; the Generator endpoint in `doc/places/generator.md`; Haven's recovery thread in `doc/places/haven.md`; Bridget's slate in `doc/npcs/bridget.md`; Field-flock Jackalope in `doc/claude/CREATURES.md`.

## Open questions

- **The community's specific name.** The terrace-markers carry surnames but not the village's collective name. A reader cross-referencing Commons House records may piece it together.
- **What the wine-spirit was *originally*.** A vineyard-binding has a register; this one's particular voice is open. A campaign that wants the spirit *fully restored* across long shamanic work may name them.
- **Whether the Pair returned to the ridge after the supply-shipment.** The letter is dated six weeks pre-rift; the rift came; the cache is sealed. Whether the Pair came south before the rift or remained at the Generator until the end is in the correspondence at the Anchor.
