# Felicia's Shrine — the Cave Chapel

- **Type:** Natural cave chapel set into the hillside on the path between the inland hill route and Luchebert Monastery's back gate.
- **Region:** Below Luchebert's maze-garden portal, on the descending path back into the hills.
- **Officially:** A side-shrine of Saint Luchebert's Abbey.
- **In practice:** The true devotional centre of the monastery — Felicia's, tended by **Willow**.
- **Status:** Lightly maintained, deeply tended; not advertised.

## What the party meets

Visitors coming up the hill route to the monastery pass the chapel **on their right**, just before they reach the back gate. The entrance is dim, half-grown over with creeper, and a worn wooden plank leans against the lintel. Most parties walk past it on entry without realising what it is — and most parties' second encounter with the chapel is being intercepted by Willow on a later attempt to come back down to it.

A party that *does* enter, with Willow's permission, steps off the main path through the low entrance and finds themselves inside a place that does not feel like a built room.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **It is a natural cave, not a built chapel.** The vault is irregular rock; the floor is rock; the entrance has been lightly enlarged but the rest is as it was. The air is **colder** than outside — felt before seen.
- **The shrine at the back, on a natural ledge.** Flowers, candles on rock-shelves the cave provided, a few small preserved objects only Willow understands. The party walking up to it understands without being told that *this* is what the monastery is actually for.
- **A spring at the back drips into a small pool.** The sound is constant — the chapel is **quiet but not silent**. Willow uses the water in her work.

## At the entrance — Willow's flower-work table

Just inside the entrance, where the natural light still reaches, sits an old wooden table worn smooth by decades of Willow's hands. A small knife. Trimmings in a pile. A shallow bowl of water from the spring. This is where she prepares each day's flowers.

When Willow is in the chapel — at dawn or dusk — this is usually where the party finds her. When she is not, the table is set and ready for her return.

## At the shrine — the back of the cave

The shrine sits on a low natural ledge at the back of the cave, where the rock would have invited it even before anyone arranged anything there.

What is on it:

- **Fresh flowers**, replaced daily. They last longer than they should.
- **Candles on the rock-shelves** above and around the shrine. Set where the rock invites them; firelight moves shadows in ways no built room would.
- **A few small preserved objects** that mean something only to someone who has been here long enough.
- **Felicia's necklace** — old, intricate, on the shrine itself. A party may not notice it on a first visit; **Willow notices who notices.** For what the necklace does — condense Felicia's presence near someone she cannot otherwise reach, e.g. Laurent — see `doc/npcs/willow.md` §"At Felicia's shrine" and `doc/places/laurents_city.md`.

## At the spring

A small natural seep at the back of the cave, dripping into a pool the size of a basin in the rock. The water is cold, slightly mineral.

Willow uses the water for her flower-work, for trimming candle wicks, for the small daily preparations. The pool is not a wishing well; it is a working spring.

A party that drinks from it tastes ordinary cold water. A *spirit-aware* party drinking from it may sense more — the chapel's concentration of Felicia is in the water as it is in the air. The DM decides what that means at the table.

## Visible patterns

- **The chapel is empty except at dawn and dusk**, when Willow is there.
- **Candles lit and trimmed twice a day** — once before each of her visits, once after.
- **Flowers change daily** — yesterday's removed, today's set.
- **The spring drips constantly.** Even in the deep night, the chapel is not silent.

## What you learn here, and when

| Topic | When the chapel gives it up |
|---|---|
| That this is Felicia's, not Saint Luchebert's | Once Willow has let the party in. The shrine itself makes it obvious. |
| The necklace, what it is and what it does | Only with earned trust. Willow gates this. |
| Felicia's presence — felt, not explained | Immediately on entering, for any spirit-aware character. |
| The spring's water, properties beyond ordinary | Open question. Spirit-aware drinkers may notice; Willow may or may not confirm. |
| The chapel's history — how long it has been a chapel, who tended it before Willow | Briefly, from Willow. *"Names you do not need."* |

## If the party does something — what happens

| If the party... | What the chapel does (or what Willow does about it) |
|---|---|
| Enters without invitation | Willow intervenes (see her file). The chapel itself is not booby-trapped; the curse is hers. |
| Steals from the shrine | **Willow's draining curse.** See `doc/npcs/willow.md` §"The draining curse." |
| Lights more candles than were lit | Willow notices on her next visit. She will not put them out; she may or may not approve. The chapel's candle-count is hers to keep. |
| Drinks from the spring | Ordinary cold water, slightly mineral. *Spirit-aware* characters may sense Felicia in it; the DM decides what that means. |
| Disturbs Willow's flower-work table | Re-set when Willow next arrives. She notices. |
| Sits quietly with the shrine for a long time | The chapel allows it. A party that does this may, over multiple visits, begin to feel something the regulars know. *Reaching* a great spirit in the church-tradition sense is precisely what Saint Luchebert's order is for; the chapel is where it happens. |
| Brings their own flowers to add | Willow accepts them if they are sincerely chosen — the same test as offering her flowers herself (see her file). |
| Brings the necklace **back** after using it as agreed | Replaces it on the shrine herself if Willow is not there; otherwise hands it to Willow. Willow asks what the user said to Laurent. |

## DM notes

- **This chapel is gated by Willow.** The rules of approach are hers, not the chapel's. A DM running scenes here should keep Willow's framework in mind — see `doc/npcs/willow.md` for the full reaction surface.
- **The chapel is part of Luchebert's spirit-shield stack.** A great spirit's tended shrine reads as mighty-spirit-adjacent territory to chaotic spirits. The cave is a small but real piece of the buffer that holds the surrounding hill country.
- **The cave is not a secret** — visitors arriving via the hill route pass it. What is gated is *entering* the cave, and *understanding* what it is. A party may walk past the chapel on every visit and never go in. Some do.
- **The necklace** is the chapel's most consequential object — see `doc/npcs/willow.md` §"Stories she tells" for the way Willow speaks about it, and `doc/places/laurents_city.md` for what it does at Laurent's wreck.
- **The spring's properties** are deliberately open. The DM may treat the water as ordinary, as faintly blessed (small bonuses to spirit-related actions for a brief duration), or as a setup for a later reveal. Pick whichever serves the campaign and stay consistent.
- Lore cross-references: Felicia's drive in `doc/npcs/felicia.md`; great-spirit reach in `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Great spirits do not want — they are driven"; the spirit-shield mechanism in LORE.md §"Spirit Ecology"; Laurent and the necklace in `doc/places/laurents_city.md`; the wider monastery in `doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`.
