# Salmonswell

- **Type:** Small independent city on an unnavigable river, built on salmon and salt.
- **Region:** Inland, well upriver — on a **tributary of the great river** (same watershed as Raft city's river; both reach the ocean at the northern estuary). The tributary itself is **not navigable**: rapids and hostile surrounding terrain make it impassable for travellers. Salmonswell is reached overland.
- **Officially:** Self-governing. Neither Allondo nor Brumal has claim here. A rotating council of merchant-houses sits at the Harbourmaster's Office.
- **In practice:** A working city whose independence is held together by **fair trade fees and careful neutrality**. Both kingdoms use it; neither kingdom owns it. Brumal's collapse is putting that balance under quiet strain.
- **Status:** Stable but stretched. Refugee numbers are growing. Fishing rights around the Upwelling are a live dispute. The harbourmaster's clerks have a lot to do.

## What the party meets

The party arrives overland. Salmonswell has no water approach — the tributary is rapids from here all the way north, and unsailable in the other direction past the first bend. Most parties come up the valley road from Allondo's interior, or down the main trade road from Linar harbour's direction. A few will have come *in* from Brumal — the refugee direction, which tells Salmonswell a lot about them before they have spoken.

They crest the valley rim and see the city below. **A working river-wharf on one bank**, the row of smokehouses stretching along it, the salt-yards behind, merchant-houses tight at the centre, fisher-dwellings climbing the valley sides. The river itself is visible: a slow stretch with a clear *swell* at its middle — the Upwelling, the reason the city exists.

The wind carries. **The smell of smoked salmon reaches them before the city does.**

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The Upwelling.** A freshwater spring rising in the river itself, producing a visible swell in an otherwise slow stretch. Even from the ridge the party can see it — a lift of water a boat's length across, carrying its own small current within the larger one. At the height of the salmon run, the water around it **boils with fish**.
- **The smell from Smokehouse Row.** The smokehouses run along the riverbank; their work is constant during fishing season and near-constant the rest of the year. A party smells the city a mile before they see it — oak smoke, salt, salmon. Once inside the city, the smell is part of the air.
- **The mixed crowd at the dock.** Old salmon-fishing families in working clothes; **Brumal refugees** with what they carried out of the desert's advance; the **harbourmaster's clerks** in tidy coats tallying fees. Three regions of accent, more languages than a town this size should hold. **Salmonswell is a small city that already belongs to more people than it was built for.**

## At the approach — coming in over the valley rim

The valley road runs along the eastern flank of the tributary and drops into Salmonswell from a saddle high on the ridge. The view from the saddle is how most travellers first see the city.

- **The valley walls are steep**, the river fast and white above and below the city's slow stretch. The city is *here* and not elsewhere because the Upwelling makes it possible to fish this water.
- **The road passes no gate**, no wall, and no guard-post. Salmonswell has never been garrisoned. A party walks in.
- **Trade wagons stand along the road's last stretch**, waiting their turn at the wharf. Drivers will tell any traveller how long the wait is and whether the clerks are fair this week.

## At the wharf — first contact

The working centre. Dock timbers, fish-crates stacked and cycled, the smell of wet rope and river-weed. The harbourmaster's clerks keep tables here.

- **Clerks at open-air desks** tally fees for goods going in or out. They are courteous; they are also the first people who will ask the party's business in Salmonswell. The answers go into a ledger.
- **Fishers unloading salmon** in season, or mending nets out of season. A party that stops to watch is often invited to help carry; helping is accepted as arrival-gift in the local custom and opens doors later.
- **Children selling small things** — smoked-fish skewers, handfuls of salt crystals, river-polished stones. A few of them are Brumal orphans the town is quietly feeding; they look a little thinner and wear clothes that travelled with them.
- **Information is cheap here.** Directions to the Harbourmaster's Office, directions to the road up to Linar, directions to *"the otter druid up the rapids"* — any wharf-hand or clerk will confirm what the party half-remembers.

## At Smokehouse Row — where the economy runs

The stretch of riverbank north of the wharf where the smokehouses stand. Squat stone-and-timber buildings, chimneys low, doors open to let handlers through. The smoke is oak — a specific cut the fishers' families have argued about for generations.

- **Work is continuous.** Salmon come in fresh at the wharf, are gutted at the shoreline, smoke in the houses for days, and leave packed in salted crates for the trade road. Each step has a family that has done it for a century.
- **Salt-yards behind.** Open flats where brine crystallises in pans and is swept up and barrelled. The salt trade is the quieter half of the economy; a salmon without salt is a wasted salmon.
- **The fish-knives are good.** A party that needs a working blade can buy one here at a price that does not recognise what the work is worth.
- **Brumal refugees are taking wage work** in the smokehouses where they can. The families that own the houses have been hiring or not hiring on a spectrum from generous to grudging; the town's politics are partly this question.

## At the Upwelling — the spring and the fishing rights

A short walk upriver from the wharf. A broad slow stretch where the spring's lift creates the *swell* the city is named for. In season, boats cluster in and around the swell with nets ready for the salmon that gather here before the final upstream run.

- **The water is visibly different at the swell.** Clearer; colder; rising slightly in the middle. In winter it does not freeze where the rest of the river does.
- **Fishing rights are the city's most contested property.** Boats closest to the Upwelling catch the most; the **right to place a boat there is inherited**, traded, and occasionally fought over in the Harbourmaster's Office. The clerks keep the register.
- **In the off-season the swell is quiet.** A party visiting outside the salmon run sees the spring as a pool-like eddy with the current sliding around it. Fishers may be at the edges mending nets.
- **A meeting here is a serious meeting.** When the council wants a conversation to be *weighted* — with an outside visitor, a delegation, a merchant-house rival — they sometimes hold it at the Upwelling rather than in the office. The place reminds everyone why the city exists.

## At the Harbourmaster's Office — the council's seat

A working building on the wharf's inland side, unimpressive in construction, well-used inside. A central room with a long table, a back room with the ledgers, a second floor where the rotating council meets.

- **The title is older than the function.** "Harbourmaster" dates from when this stretch of river was the furthest inland point proper boats could reach. The council inherited the name because nobody agreed on a replacement.
- **The council rotates.** A dozen merchant-houses share seats; one chairs at a time; the chair changes every season. Decisions are **slow and compromise-shaped**, which is part of why Salmonswell has not fallen to either kingdom.
- **The ledgers downstairs** hold every trade fee collected for the last century. A merchant tracing a family dispute can often settle it by an hour's reading. The clerks will help if asked courteously.
- **Outside delegations are received here** — Allondan envoys, Brumal envoys, traders from Linar, occasionally a druid or priest with a message. The council receives them all. The council commits to almost nothing.

## At the north gate — where the rapids begin

North of the city the tributary runs fast. A road leaves the northernmost edge of the settled area and follows the river on foot — not a proper trade road, just a walking path kept clear by locals and refugees who have found work upriver.

- **The path skirts the water's edge.** The rapids are audible before they are visible; by the first fall, conversation has to be shouted.
- **Three falls mark the route**. The first fall is a short walk from the city; locals sometimes picnic above it. The second is rougher terrain; only fishers and hunters go there. **Past the third fall, the path becomes wilderness.**
- **"The otter druid up the rapids."** Locals know her by reputation, not by name. Fishers have spotted her at the water's edge; refugees have heard rumours; nobody bothers her. A party with Diederik's errand can confirm direction here — *eastern bank, in a stand of rowans she planted around her cottage* — and press on past the third fall. See `doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md` §"The errand to Rowan."
- **The path from the city is a day's walk to the third fall.** Beyond it, another half-day to Rowan's place, if the party knows what they are looking for.

## At the orphanage — Ingram's house

One of the buildings on the valley side was once a fisher-family home, expanded twice, and is now the **orphanage**. A party who has a reason to come here will be directed up the slope by any wharf-hand. Otherwise it is easy to walk past.

- **A small front room with a desk.** Behind the desk sits **[Ingram](../npcs/ingram.md)** — a very old male Mouse, silver-furred, stooped, bright-eyed. A thin chain at his neck holds the key to a chest under the desk.
- **Dormitories behind** — simple bunks, mixed origins of children, the same absorbing logic the party may have already seen at Hank's farm in Elmsfield. Salmonswell's version is larger and less personal.
- **A ledger that runs back decades.** Ingram has kept every child who passed through by date, in a hand that has gone spidery with age. Two of the names in it are **Hanny** (rabbit-folk) and a wolf-folk girl brought in from the enemy side, dated just after the war.
- **The chest under the desk** held a Beaver-folk sergeant's bag of wartime wages, untouched, for years — until the day the two girls came of age and Ingram handed it to them with a description of the sergeant but not his name. That name has lived in Ingram alone since the day the bag was left. A party arriving with the bag again — and **Hank's sealed letter** — is the release Ingram has waited for. See `../npcs/ingram.md` for the full scene, and `../npcs/hank.md` and `../npcs/brother_darrel.md` for the quest chain.

## At the refugee quarters — the other half of the current town

Not one neighbourhood but **several**, spread through the fisher-dwellings on the valley sides. Guest-houses that were once family homes; rooms rented for low fees; whole buildings the council has taken over to keep people warm.

- **Brumal families in transition.** Some here for months, some for a year or more. Some have already found work in the smokehouses or the salt-yards; some are still waiting. The children are learning salmon-fishing from neighbours' children.
- **The council's quiet policy.** Salmonswell takes in as many as it can feed. The line is economic, not ideological; nobody is turned away for political reasons, but numbers beyond the smokehouses' absorbing capacity become a conversation at the Harbourmaster's Office.
- **A parallel to Hank's orphanage in Elmsfield.** The scale here is larger, less personal; the logic is similar. A party that has been to Hank's and then to Salmonswell will recognise the pattern.
- **News from Brumal arrives here first.** A party wanting current information about Brumal's collapse will get it from a refugee over a meal, not from the council. The stories are raw and recent.

## Visible patterns

- **Smoke always visible somewhere along the riverbank.** The Row does not stop.
- **Boats cluster at the Upwelling during the run.** The cluster thins outside the season but never empties entirely.
- **Brumal accents are increasingly common** in every setting. Six months ago there were few; now they are in the smokehouses, at the wharf, in the refugee quarters, and occasionally at the council's open sessions.
- **Clerks with ledgers everywhere.** The harbourmaster's office runs on documentation; every trade, every fee, every dispute is written down somewhere.
- **The council meets on a fixed day.** Every week. Public portions are open; anyone may sit at the back. Closed portions are closed.
- **Merchants from the Linar road pass through regularly.** Traders using Salmonswell as an inland hub will know the place well and have opinions about specific merchant-houses and specific smokehouse families.
- **Salmon on every table.** Fresh in season, smoked the rest of the year. A party eating in Salmonswell will eat salmon more than once.

| Fishing-rights politics at the Upwelling | Freely from any fisher; the council's view takes more patience. |

| Which smokehouse families are hiring refugees | Visible by watching; named at the wharf. |

| That the sergeant who left the bag was called **Darrel** | Only from Ingram, and only to a party carrying Hank's sealed letter. Not on the wharf. |

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Asks about "the otter druid up the rapids" | Directions given freely, with a caveat: past the third fall is wilderness, and she does not welcome casual visitors. Go if you must, not if you are curious. |
| Carries fish-crates at the wharf on arrival | Registered as *arrival-gift* by local custom. Doors open faster; the harbourmaster's clerks are warmer. |
| Asks to meet the Harbourmaster's council | Possible. The next open session is announced at the Office. A non-urgent party waits for it; an urgent party may get an expedited audience if their errand is credible. |
| Tries to buy salmon, salt, or smoked fish in quantity | Easy. Prices are posted; the clerks collect a small fee. The economy is built for exactly this. |
| Tries to buy *fishing rights* at the Upwelling | Effectively impossible. Rights are inherited inside merchant-house lines. A party that wants to involve itself would need to *marry in, serve a house for years*, or carry a favour worth more than fish. |
| Intervenes in a fishing-rights dispute | The council has seen many parties try. They receive the attempt politely. Intervention that respects the ledgers has a chance; intervention that ignores them is filed and ignored. |
| Brings news from Brumal | **Refugees gather to listen.** The council asks formally a day or two later. Recent news is currency here. |
| Brings specific news about the Brumal court | **Closed-session topic.** A party with Corven's or Penelope's confidence can bring this — and should think carefully about whether to. See `doc/npcs/prince_corven.md`. |
| Offers to fund a refugee guest-house | Accepted, gratefully. The council will want to know the source of the funds and will decline political conditions. |
| Tries to navigate upriver | Impossible. The rapids start within the city's northern edge. Any boat attempting it will be wrecked. The overland path is the only option. |
| Arrives badly under-equipped for the rapids walk | Locals will point this out; a refugee who made the walk in the other direction will often volunteer guidance. Nobody at Salmonswell profits from a party that goes unprepared. |
| Asks to meet *here* with someone from Allondo or Brumal | Salmonswell is the plausible neutral inland venue. The council will receive the request. Logistics can be arranged at the Harbourmaster's Office. |
| Causes a public disturbance | The clerks and fishers handle it together; no formal garrison, but the wharf crowd is not small. A party that escalates will be asked to leave the city and not return. |
| Arrives carrying Hank's bag and Hank's sealed letter | Directed up the slope to Ingram. Ingram opens the letter, speaks **Darrel's name** for the first time in decades, confirms the description, and points the party onward to **Luchebert Monastery**. Tells them plainly he will refuse the bag. |

## DM notes

- **The errand is the load-bearing visit for most parties.** Diederik sends them for Rowan's confirmation of the overseas ironwood plan. Salmonswell's role in that visit is to *point onward*: confirm direction, provide a hot meal, stock the walk up the rapids. Play the wharf scene for the directions and let them press on past the third fall.
- **Brumal-refugee pressure is visible from the first scene.** Let it set the tone. The city is generous but stretched. A DM who surfaces the pressure — a clerk hesitating over a ledger entry, a smokehouse family declining work, a meal shared too thin — turns Salmonswell into a barometer for the Brumal-collapse thread.
- **Neutral-meeting-ground use is the second function.** Any campaign arc that needs a discreet inter-kingdom conversation, a confidential meeting with Corven, or a private audience between parties who cannot meet at their own courts — Salmonswell is where. The council will host; the council will not take sides.
- **The Upwelling can be a focal scene.** A negotiation, a revelation, a confrontation — held *at the swell*, with the water lifting beneath the party, lands harder than the same scene indoors. The place is charged by what it is.
- **Rowan is not here.** Do not let the errand end at Salmonswell. The wilderness walk past the third fall is part of the scene — see `doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md` §"The errand to Rowan" for its weight. Salmonswell is the jumping-off, not the destination.
- **The council is a slow instrument.** A party that wants a decision *today* will not get one. A party that can wait, or that has a compelling enough urgent case, will be heard next session. This is a feature of how Salmonswell has stayed independent; do not let it feel like a frustration without reason.
- **Labour-trade custom at the wharf.** Carrying crates on arrival is *a thing locals do*. A party that does it unprompted signals something about themselves. A party told to do it and complying is still read as willing. A party that refuses is read as uninterested in Salmonswell's hospitality.
- **Ingram's house is a quest stop, not just an atmosphere stop.** The orphanage is the middle scene of the Darrel-coin chain (Elmsfield → Salmonswell → Luchebert). If the party arrives from Hank with the bag and the sealed letter, Ingram releases the sergeant's name — long held at Darrel's request — and sends them onward. Without the letter, the name stays held. See `../npcs/ingram.md` §"The story of Darrel's coin" for the full scene.
- **Bridget's trader history lands here.** If Bridget is with the party, her three years on the Linar-Salmonswell road will surface — named traders, specific families, specific cheats and honesties. See `doc/npcs/bridget.md` §"Her old road" for what she carries from this route.
- Lore cross-references: the errand to Rowan in `doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md`; the geography in `doc/places/geography.md` §"Salmonswell's tributary"; Bridget's trader-road history in `doc/npcs/bridget.md`; Corven/Penelope potentially meeting here in `doc/npcs/prince_corven.md`; Linar harbour at the other end of the trade road in `doc/places/linar_harbour.md`; Brumal collapse context in `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"Brumal's collapse."

## Open questions

- **Named merchant-houses on the rotating council.** Not yet set; DM-fill as the campaign requires.
- **The Harbourmaster themselves** — the current chair's name, background, and politics. Open for the DM to fill when the party's arc asks for it.
- **A named senior fisher** who knows the Upwelling and the rapids intimately — useful to give a face to the fishing-rights politics and to the rapids route. Not yet assigned.
- **Whether Salmonswell has ever *formally* refused either kingdom** on anything, or whether the careful neutrality has held without any public refusal on record.
- **The exact relationship between Salmonswell's tributary and the great-forest river** running through the forbidden woods. See `doc/places/geography.md` §"Open questions"; this affects whether a water route through the woods could, in principle, link here.
