# Salmonswell

- **Type:** Small city
- **Region:** Inland, well upriver — on a **tributary of the great river** (the same system Raft city's river belongs to; their waters share an ocean outlet at the northern estuary). The tributary itself is **not navigable**: rapids and hostile surrounding terrain make it impassable for travellers. Salmonswell is reached overland.
- **Political status:** Independent — neither Allondo nor Brumal
- **Economy:** Salmon fishing at the seasonal runs, smokehouses, salt trade, overland trade up and down the valley roads

## Summary

A small inland city set a long way up-river, where the water widens into a slow stretch fed by a natural spring — the "swell" for which it is named. Every season the salmon run up from the coast reach this stretch in huge numbers before the final stages of spawning further upstream, and the city grew around catching, preserving, and trading the fish.

Salmonswell is independent, like Linar harbour, and its independence is held together more by economics than by treaty. Neither Allondo nor Brumal has found it worth the trouble to absorb, and the city's habit of charging fair trade fees and quietly refusing to take sides has kept both willing to use it as a shared inland port. With Brumal's ecological collapse now pushing people toward the region, that balance is under strain.

The city is small — a working river-wharf, a row of smokehouses along the bank, the salt-yards behind them, a tight core of merchant-houses, and fisher-dwellings climbing the valley sides. Its people are mixed: old salmon-fishing families, river traders, and an increasing number of Brumal displaced who reached the valley overland.

## Key features

- **The Upwelling.** A freshwater spring rises in the river itself, producing a visible swell in an otherwise slow stretch. It is why the salmon gather here in such numbers. Fishing rights around the Upwelling are the city's most contested property.
- **Smokehouse Row.** The preserved-salmon industry is the city's economic spine. Salmonswell smoked salmon reaches as far as Allondo's interior and (before the collapse) into Brumal.
- **The Harbourmaster's Office.** The de facto seat of whatever passes for Salmonswell's government — a rotating council of merchant-houses that collects river-wharf fees, adjudicates fishing disputes, and takes care to displease neither kingdom decisively. ("Harbourmaster" is an old title from when this stretch of river was the furthest inland point proper boats could reach.)

## Campaign hooks

- **Brumal refugees by river.** Those fleeing overland from Brumal's desert advance can follow the valley up to Salmonswell. The council is quietly taking in as many as it can feed — a parallel, larger-scale version of Hank's orphanage in Elmsfield.
- **Fish as leverage.** In a region whose food supply is under pressure, whoever controls the Upwelling controls something very valuable. Tensions around the fishing rights could draw either kingdom in.
- **A neutral meeting ground.** When Allondo and Brumal need to talk — or when anyone wants to meet away from two royal courts — Salmonswell is the plausible inland venue.
- **The otter druid up the rapids.** Locals know — by reputation, not by name — that a lone druid lives north of the city along the unnavigable tributary, somewhere past the third major fall, in a stand of rowan trees on the eastern bank. Fishers have spotted her at the water's edge; refugees have heard rumours; nobody bothers her. Her name is **Rowan** (see `doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md` §"Rowan's letters" and §"The errand only the party can run"). A party arriving on Diederik's errand can confirm direction at the wharf or the smokehouses before pushing on.
