# Haven

- **Type:** Settlement of iron puppets
- **Region:** Beside the shifting lands to the west
- **Political status:** Independent — kept deliberately off most maps
- **Inhabitants:** Surviving iron puppets from the mage era, mostly humanoid wooden-bodied constructs with metal strip reinforcements

## Summary

Haven is where the **iron puppets** who survived the fall of the white city have gathered, slowly, over the centuries since. See `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"The Iron Puppets" for the full history.

The settlement sits **beside the shifting lands, to their west**. The shifting lands themselves are a barrier: no road runs cleanly from either kingdom into this area, and the approach requires either crossing the shifting lands (which reject structure and navigation) or circling around them along their western flank. Haven is therefore **hard to reach unless someone actively wants to find it** — which is exactly what its inhabitants prefer. Travellers who arrive here arrive on purpose.

## Inhabitants

Most of Haven's puppets are **humanoid**: roughly person-shaped constructs of ironwood reinforced with metal strips and plates. They were built for heavy labour and detail work at person-scale during the white city era, and most of them have been walking slowly through quiet days for close to a thousand years. They are spirit-bound. They have accumulated something close to personhood in that time — patient, deliberate, deeply attached to their own durability.

They are not hostile. They are cautious. The last people to deal with them in bulk treated them as tools, and they have been selectively invisible since.

The **two pot-people** in the buried tower at the Lake of Tears (see `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`) are *different* kinds of construct — an experimental side-programme's twin survivors: a small caretaker and the tower's own furnace. Both are pot-shaped, both spirit-bound, neither humanoid. Haven's inhabitants would recognise them as kin but not kind.

## Campaign hooks

- **The buyers currently acquiring spirit-bound artefacts** (per LORE.md §"The Shifting Lands") have not yet identified Haven's location. Haven intends to keep it that way. If the party earns Haven's trust, the party may become the *reason* that location stays hidden — or, if they betray it, the reason it does not.
- **The pot-person** could be brought here. That is a meaningful journey — for the pot-person, for the party, and for Haven's inhabitants who would meet a cousin they did not know existed.
- **The shifting lands** are a different arc, but Haven's western location puts the settlement adjacent to them. Any long arc that wants to engage with the shifting lands' origin (or with the black iron fortress rumoured to stand beyond them) has Haven as a possible base of operations.
- **The last flight** (Laurent's flying city, if it rises) could reach Haven easily where a ground expedition could not. That is a natural stop on an airborne journey.

## Open questions

- How many puppets remain at Haven? (Dozens? Hundreds? Small enough to know each other by name.)
- What do they actually *do* all day, at their glacial pace?
- Has anyone outside their own number visited and left still trusted?
- Is there a spokes-puppet — a Haven elder, so to speak — who would speak for the settlement to a party?
