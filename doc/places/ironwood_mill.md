# The Ironwood Mill

- **NPC-tied dungeon** — **tied to John Bean** (and reached through **Irna Bean** as the usual pointer).
- **Type:** Ancient ironwood-cutting mill, one of the very few buildings on the continent ever capable of working that material. Sealed from above; entered from a ground-level feed-slot. Chaos-touched by proximity to the blasted lands.
- **Region:** The edge of the blasted lands, past Wilder's hill, among the ruins visible in that broken country.
- **Officially:** Abandoned. The mill has not operated since before the Overseas War.
- **In practice:** John Bean's arm is still inside. So is the cutting machine that took it. So are the ironwood blanks no other mill on this continent can produce. So, now, is whatever has moved into the empty building in the years since.

## The NPC hook — how the party arrives here

The party does not come to the ironwood mill first. They come to **John Bean** first — on Irna's pointer after she joins the party, or by following the Scarlet Vale heroic-arm legend to its quieter source at Blackwood freehold. John tells them the story of his war, including the building where he lost the arm. **John himself does not know the building was an ironwood mill**; he knows only that Felix found a way in and that something inside ate his arm.

**Irna has pieced it together** from later reading and family conversation. She knows which broken country it is in, and roughly where past Wilder's hill. Once she is in the party, she can point. Sometimes she wants to go herself. Sometimes she wants to be told what was there.

Alternative triggers: **Felix** will describe the way in if asked specifically about the war's end — the low ground-level slot he found. **Rhianna** will confirm the mill's ironwood-cutting nature if asked by a character who has already pieced it together. **Wilder** knows the building's significance and will warn a party going in about what still lives there.

**What John actually wants:** nothing. He does not ask. *"Leave it where it is."* But a party that brings back the arm-remnant, or a piece of ironwood of the grade only this mill cut, or the story of what the building really was — gets one of the few scenes where John's long pauses open into something that might be thanks. Irna wants this for him even when he will not ask for it.

## What the party meets

The approach is through broken country. The ruins of the blasted lands' edge are visible: fallen castles in the distance, old vineyard terraces going to scrub, communal halls the sand has not yet reached. The mill sits in a shallow fold of ground that a party not guided would not notice.

**From outside, it looks like a single large stone building partly buried in its own hillside.** Roof intact. Walls intact. No obvious door — the upper stories sealed, possibly deliberately sealed, possibly the original ironwood-secured entrances have not yielded in centuries. **The only way in is the feed-slot at ground level**: a broad low opening at the building's base where whole trees once went in, horizontal, toward the cutters. A party has to crawl.

Felix's way in. John's way in. The party's, now.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts

- **Intact from outside in a way nothing else in the blasted-lands edge is.** The ironwood-reinforced walls have not crumbled. The roof has not fallen. A party approaches expecting a ruin and finds a *closed building*. This is what ironwood-construction does: it lasts.
- **The feed-slot.** Low, wide, a long horizontal opening at the base of the building. A party has to crawl in flat. The last yards before entry are blind. The smell that reaches the party first is *old oiled wood* and the smell of iron kept indoors for centuries.
- **Silence from inside.** No wind-noise, no water-noise, no creature-sound. The mill is not dead-silent; it is *attended-silent*. Something inside is awake and paying attention.

## At the feed-slot — the entry

- **The crawl is twenty paces.** Dark by halfway in. A *Fur* or *Hide* character takes the crawl better than a party-member in armour; armour scrapes the stone and the sound carries.
- **The slot opens into a receiving bay.** Broad, dim, ironwood rails still visible in the floor where trees once moved through.
- **Here is the first sign of the current inhabitants.** Tracks in the dust — old, specific, *made by something with a weight the party should read carefully*. If the party looks up, they will see where something roosts in the ceiling-beams at night.

## At the grinding-chamber — the cutter

The mill's heart. A wide chamber around a massive iron-and-ironwood cutting mechanism — a great set of jointed blades, a feeding cradle, a power-drive that used to be fed by a bound elemental. The **whole thing is still in place**.

- **The cutter is silent.** Not dismantled; not destroyed; not obviously disabled. Just not running.
- **Dried blood on the feeding cradle.** Old. Not much. A strip across the rail where a man's forearm was briefly in the wrong place. **John's arm.** A party who knows the story reads this immediately.
- **The arm itself is not in the cradle.** The cutter took it; the cutter did not keep it. It is *somewhere* in the chamber, or in the bay below where ironwood-dust and waste would have been collected. Finding it is possible with patience.
- **The cutter is still active in a specific sense.** It does not run without its bound elemental, and the elemental is *here* — stationary, quiet, waiting. A *Shamanic* character feels it immediately. It is a **bound iron elemental**, similar register to the Great Thresh's elemental (see `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At the Great Thresh"), but narrower — it was bound to cut, not to grind.
- **Approaching the cutter provokes the elemental.** Not into violence; into *attention*. The elemental tests whether the approacher is there to feed the cradle. If yes — if someone stands at the cradle, stillness suggesting a log ready to go through — the cutter begins to ready itself. This is how John lost his arm.

## At the storage bays — ironwood blanks

Beyond the grinding-chamber, several sealed bays hold **finished ironwood blanks**: cut, shaped, stacked. Pieces that no living mill on the continent can produce.

- **Two or three dozen blanks** of varying sizes. Each one is **extraordinarily valuable** — ironwood-craft material at a grade Bridget's Inn and John's arm represent the last surviving examples of.
- **Haven would recognise the grade.** Carrying a single blank to Haven is recognisable as coming from a mill the puppets remember.
- **The bays are preserved by mage-work still holding.** The air inside is dry; the wood has not drifted; the ironwood is stable.

## At the operator's quarters — the mage's notes

Off the grinding-chamber, up a short ironwood stair (still sound), is a small apartment. The mill's master operator lived here — a pre-Rift mage of the specific niche of elemental-binding-for-ironwood-work.

- **Preserved room.** Bed, table, tools, notes. The mage's clothes still on a hook. A working-desk with papers under a glass weight.
- **The notes are extraordinary.** **Ironwood-cutting technique** as a mage-working — how the bound iron elemental was set; how the feed rate was controlled; how the blades were tempered. A party with a *Mage* or *Ingenuity* specialist carrying these notes has material no living practitioner has.
- **A personal journal at the bed-side**, abandoned mid-sentence. The operator left in a hurry. The last entry is from **the week the White City fell**. The operator joined the evacuation and never returned. The mill sealed itself behind him, as it was designed to.
- **A seal-ring in the desk's drawer.** A *master-of-the-mill* ring, ironwood-and-iron. An artefact to return to Haven, or to keep as a token.

## At the power-core — the bound elemental

Under the grinding-chamber, a small chamber houses the iron elemental that drives the cutter. A party that descends finds:

- **The elemental visible as a hovering iron shape**, patient, aware. It has been here for a thousand years doing nothing because nothing was asked of it.
- **It can be addressed.** *Shamanic* or *Druid* work reaches it. It remembers the mage who set it. It remembers being told its work was *trees, not arms*.
- **It carries a small guilt about John's arm.** A party that brings it the fact of *who John was, what he did with his war* — the arm's afterlife as the thing that broke the scrying mirror — changes the elemental's register. It becomes *grateful* in a slow, iron way.
- **The elemental can be released or not.** Freeing it (with its consent) requires the seal-ring from the operator's quarters. Free, it leaves and seeks the blasted lands' other bound ironwork — potentially the Great Thresh's elemental, potentially others. Bound, it stays here, and the mill could in principle be **run again** by a party who wanted to make ironwood blanks.

## Creatures that have moved in

The mill is mostly empty. But something has been using the building since the war:

- **A small group of field-flock jackalopes** have nested in the storage bays. Pass-through threat; they vanish when approached.
- **A single warped helper-spirit** has taken the mill's cooling-race, a small stone channel that used to carry water through the grinding-chamber. The race is now a swallowing-line across the chamber floor. A party fighting near the cutter can step in it.
- **A displaced iron-puppet fragment** — an arm, specifically — rests in the waste-bay beneath the grinding-chamber. A piece of a Haven-kin from some other blasted-lands site that has migrated here. A party gathering the *scattered kin* (see `doc/places/scattered_kin.md`) picks up another piece.
- **At night, something roosts in the ceiling-beams.** A blasted-lands *hippogriff* sometimes comes to rest here. Not the encounter-kind; the observing-kind. If the party camps in the mill, they see it come at dusk and leave at dawn.

## Treasure — what the NPC wants

- **For John (unspoken):** the arm-remnant. He will not ask; Irna carries the want for him. Returning it lets John bury it.
- **For Irna:** confirmation that the building was an ironwood mill, and the story the operator's notes tell. She wants her father's war to be *legible*.
- **For Haven:** the seal-ring; the displaced iron-puppet fragment; news of the bound elemental's state.
- **For a mage-student in the party:** the operator's notes. Genuinely rare technique.
- **For the party:** the ironwood blanks. Practical and valuable.

## If the party does something — what happens

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Stands at the feeding cradle without understanding | The bound elemental wakes the cutter. Reflex-saves required; a character who does not clear the cradle in time loses an arm. This is how John lost his. |
| Addresses the bound elemental first | The elemental is *told* they are not logs. The cutter stays silent. |
| Takes ironwood blanks | Easy; allowed; the mill does not defend them. Their weight makes extraction a practical problem. |
| Reads the operator's notes | Valuable. A mage-student finds material the living world no longer holds. |
| Takes the seal-ring | Opens the release-option for the bound elemental. Haven will want it. |
| Returns the arm-remnant to John | **Quiet scene.** John takes it. Looks at it. Buries it behind the farm that night. Says *"thank you"* in his voice, which is rare. |
| Releases the bound elemental | It leaves slowly, gratefully. The mill becomes non-functional as a cutter. |
| Leaves the bound elemental in place with the seal-ring in hand | The mill could in principle be run again. Haven would be interested in the proposition. |
| Provokes the jackalopes | They scatter; the vanish-mechanic costs certainty; no combat resolves. |
| Steps into the swallowing cooling-race | Serious consequence. A party-member can be fully submerged. |
| Tells Wilder what they found | He listens. He will add the mill to his mental map of what the blasted lands still hold. May visit one day. |

## DM notes

- **John's arm is the emotional fulcrum.** The mill has many treasures; the one that matters is the arm. A DM should let the party find it — in the waste-bay, under ironwood-dust, small and dry and specific. A party that recovers it and returns it to John has done something real. A party that leaves it has still done something real; John does not require the return.
- **The cutter-reflex is the one trap.** Nobody else in the building is hostile by default. The trap is an old mechanism waking in response to a posture the elemental reads as *load ready*. A DM should let the party avoid this by *address*, not by a DEX check. Let them talk to the elemental.
- **The operator's notes are the campaign-scale reward.** A mage-student carrying them learns techniques the blasted-lands registers make otherwise unreachable. Do not overstate the value at the table; let it unfold across later sessions when the student applies what they read here.
- **Haven is the natural terminus.** If the party has standing at Haven, bringing them the seal-ring and the iron-puppet fragment earns real regard. Haven will want to know about the bound elemental and may discuss *reopening the mill* as a real possibility. That is a campaign-scale thread that sits beyond this dungeon.
- **Irna's role at the mill, if she is present.** She wants to see. She will not go into the waste-bay for the arm herself — she cannot face it. But she will *see the building* and understand it, and she will carry what she sees back to her father in a way the party cannot.
- **Do not pad the dungeon.** The mill is *small* on purpose — a 1-session site, possibly two if the party lingers with the operator's notes. It is a focused dungeon, not a sprawling one.
- **The jackalopes and the warped helper are ambient, not central.** Run them lightly. The mill's weight is in its history, not in its combats.
