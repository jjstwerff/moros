# The Forbidden Woods

- **Type:** Forested region inhabited by a large, slow chaos spirit. The woods *are* the spirit's body; Tod is its face.
- **Region:** North of Allondo, between the settled south and Raft city. A river from the great forest runs through them.
- **Officially:** No polity claims them. No polity wants them.
- **In practice:** Stable at the fringe, costly deeper in, dangerous at the heart. Passing through is possible along the river; staying is not.
- **Status:** Contained — for now — by a ring of mighty-spirit-readers (druidic circles, Luchebert's priests, Felicia's diffuse presence, possibly the ironwood tree). If the ring thins, the woods' pressure begins to spread.

## What the party meets

The party arrives from Elmsfield's direction (past the Lake of Tears hills), from Wilder's way (down along the blasted-lands fringe), or from the north (the Raft-city route). On reaching the last ridge before the woods, they see them.

**They are almost a forest.** Visible enough to recognise, wrong enough to notice. The tree line begins perhaps a hundred yards off; nothing in between. Beyond the line, the forest is visibly warped — branches at wrong angles, bark patterns that don't match local wood, a few trunks carrying two canopies at different heights.

If the party stops and waits, **a small figure emerges from the tree line and walks toward them.** A young fox boy, alone, unafraid. This is Tod. (See `doc/npcs/tod.md`.) He does not need to be found. He comes.

A party that does not stop can turn around and leave; the woods do not reach out. A party that stops, or crosses, has entered the woods' reach.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The tree line is warped.** Visible within seconds of sighting the woods. Bark patterns that are almost-but-not-quite local, branches at angles a tree would not choose, a few trees that carry **two canopies at different heights on the same trunk**. A party with wilderness knowledge reads it as unhealthy land; a *Druid* reads it as spirit-warped. The warping becomes more pronounced deeper in.
- **The silence.** Ordinary forest sound — birds, insects, wind — is absent, or is *wrong* in some way the ear cannot place. The silence is not dead. It is a different soundscape, run on rules this world does not normally support. A party that tries to describe what they hear will not agree.
- **The light bends slightly wrong.** Shadows fall from angles that do not match the sun. A patch of shade is a half-shade bluer than it should be. A spirit-aware character (*Shamanic*, *Druid*) feels this immediately; a casual party feels *something* they cannot name.

## At the tree line — the fringe

The only place most parties will stand with the woods. **Stable, safe-for-short-visits, and entry-by-invitation.** Crossing the edge does not damage anyone who turns around inside a few minutes; neither does standing on it for as long as a conversation.

- **The warped trees are visible at arm's reach.** A party can examine them. Close inspection is uncanny but not costly — the warping is static and does not reach back.
- **A small clearing lies just past the edge.** Large enough for a conversation. Nobody maintains it; the woods settled into it when Tod began meeting visitors here. This is where his scenes happen — see `doc/npcs/tod.md` §"At the edge of the forbidden woods."
- **Local animals avoid the fringe by a narrow margin.** A deer will graze ten yards outside the tree line and not cross. A party that watches for an hour will see the pattern.
- **Short visits have no cost.** A conversation with Tod, a walk along the edge to examine the warped trees, a careful spirit-study by a druid or shaman — all safe.

## Deeper in the woods — the cost of extended stay

The party can follow Tod further in if he invites them. **Beyond the fringe, cost begins.**

- **Workings stutter.** A mage casting inside the woods feels their spirit resist; a druid working feels the answer come slower. Shamans adapt faster than others; none ignore the drag.
- **Minds fray at the edges.** Not permanently unless the stay is very long, but noticeably — in the way a cold night leaves you shaky. The fraying compounds; a second deep visit lands on the erosion of the first.
- **Visible weirdness compounds.** Trees grow at angles no tree should. A stream runs uphill for a short stretch and then corrects. A stone has a footprint-shape that was not pressed by any foot the party can identify.
- **Time passes wrong.** A conversation that took fifteen minutes feels like an hour. An hour's walk feels like twenty minutes. Two timekeepers may disagree by a third.
- **Maps do not hold.** A sketch made an hour ago no longer matches the ground. The warping is gentle on brief passage; it defeats cartography.

DMs should **set a soft time-limit** on deeper visits. A scene can happen here. A second one should cost something the first one did not. A third should start to scare the party.

## Warped fauna — what lives at the edge

The woods carry a small native fauna that has *accommodated* the chaos rather than been broken by it (the same register as the swamp's spitting bush and creeping vines, not the chaos-construct register). Two creatures are reliable enough at the edge to mention by name; both are DM-fill on stat blocks at the table, but the shape is consistent:

- **A warped wolf-shadow** — a wolf-shape whose canopy-shadow is bluer than it should be, whose breath hangs in air it should not. It hunts the way a wolf hunts; it does not pack the way a wolf packs. *Hunter* reads its sign as wolf-near-but-wrong; *Druid* reads it as accommodated-not-warped, the same as the swamp's bushes — the wolf is local, the chaos is what it learned to live in. Stat as Wolf with Will +2 and the *Vanish-miss* mechanic from the Field-flock Jackalope replacing one Bite per scene. Not aggressive to a party that does not press deeper.
- **A two-canopy spirit** — not a creature exactly; the local tree-spirit of a single ironwood-cousin tree the warping has pulled out of true (the canopy at one height; a second canopy half-realised at another). It will speak through the rustle of leaves to a *Shaman* or *Druid* who sits beneath it for an hour. It carries fragments of pre-rift forest-memory; a careful conversation surfaces names of demi-human villages that vanished with the rift. Stat as Warped Helper-Spirit (`doc/claude/CREATURES.md`) with Char +1 and immobile by canon.

A party that has the patience for either does not need to fight; a party that does not, will lose to the woods before either creature finishes assessing them.

## At the Lightning Tower wreck — a fringe scene

If the party has returned the Storm Mage to his tower (see `doc/npcs/storm_mage.md` §"When he enters his tower"), the tower **flew north in his bout of clarity and crashed at the woods' fringes** when the working failed. The wreck is here now.

- **The crash-site straddles the tree line.** Parts rest on open ground; parts rest in warped trees. The wood around it is already beginning to *take* the metal — bark growing over fallen spars. A party that arrives within days of the crash sees it happening; a later party finds the tower half-absorbed.
- **The Storm Mage is still at the wreck.** Wandering around it, more confused than before. See his file for how he reads here versus at his old tower.
- **Materials are salvageable**, for a party willing to camp in the chaos spirit's shadow. Working salvage takes spirit-sensitive care; the workings resist being moved. **Stormweave fragments** are the practical find — a small handful of charged threads from the tower's lift-array, usable as a Special Material per `doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Stormweave"; the woods' chaos pressure complicates the recovery and a careless party leaves charge behind in the bark, which the trees then *carry*. *Druid* reads which fragments can come clean and which the woods have already taken.
- **Tod may wander out.** A tower crashing into his territory is new; he is curious. He will not demand anything. He will ask what it was.

This is the **in-world entry point to the forbidden woods thread** for most parties: they follow a tower they were responsible for flying here, and the woods are where it came down.

## The river route — passing through

The river running through the great forest continues north to Raft city. It is the practical way to pass *through* the forbidden woods without pressing deep into them — **the woods warp more slowly along running water**, and the current sets a pace that does not linger.

- **A boat can make the passage in a day or two.** Fraying occurs, but less than an overnight camp inside would cost.
- **The river does not answer the normal way.** Fish do not live in it here, or they do and cannot be caught. The water is clear. It feels too cold in summer and too warm in winter.
- **Tod may follow the bank.** For stretches of the route he appears on the shore, walking alongside, curious about travellers. He does not cross running water in either direction.
- **Raft city is the destination.** A party heading for Raft city routinely uses this route; no other is faster, and most overland attempts come back.

## Toward the heart — the undiffused spirit

Few parties have reason to reach the centre of the woods. For those who do — on a shaman-led mission, on a rite to lay the boy to rest, on a climactic confrontation — the deep interior is where **the larger spirit's mass is densest**.

- **The warping stops being weird and becomes structural.** Trees as a *species* stop behaving like trees. The canopy's relation to the ground loses coherence.
- **Tod himself becomes less distinct.** The deeper he goes with the party, the more the larger spirit moves through him. His pronouns drift to "we." His voice widens.
- **The spirit does not meet visitors here the way Tod does.** Its mass is inert; it cannot personify at this scale. What presents, if anything presents, is *the whole woods speaking at once* — a register the party should feel for half a sentence and then not again.
- **This is where the cost is real.** Mental, spiritual, material. The scene is earned, not walked into.

## Visible patterns

- **Tod meets visitors at the edge.** Reliably, within minutes of someone arriving. The woods are never *unattended*.
- **Animals avoid where Tod is standing, but not where he has just been.** The pattern is specific — they read him, not his footprint.
- **Magical workings stutter in a gradient.** Weakest at the ridge-above; moderate at the fringe; substantial deeper in. A mage can feel the distance-response as they approach.
- **Light and shadow are subtly wrong at the fringe; obviously wrong inside.** The gradient is the same as the working-stutter and travels with it.
- **The silence is not empty.** There is sound, but it is sound the visitor's ear does not know how to parse. A party that records it (a shaman's ear; a chant transcribed) finds the recording does not match what they remember hearing.
- **Seasons do not mark this forest.** Bark patterns, canopy density, ground cover all look the same in summer as in winter. The spirit holds a state the weather does not dislodge.
- **The reader-ring is invisible but present.** Druids, Luchebert's priests, Felicia's diffuse presence, and possibly the ironwood tree form the outer frame that keeps the spirit contained. A party aware of spirit-ecology can name this; others notice only that the woods *do not spread*.

## What you learn here, and when

| Topic | When the place gives it up |
|---|---|
| That the trees are warped | Immediately — visible from the last ridge before the tree line. |
| That something lives here | Within minutes — Tod arrives. |
| That the spirit is inertial and rarely moves | From Tod; from a shaman; or over time, as the party notices the woods do not spread. |
| That the spirit lashes out when provoked | From Tod's *larger voice* moment, or from provocation's consequences. |
| That a ring of mighty-spirit-readers holds the spirit in | From Wilder, from a druid, from explicit lore questions. Not visible locally. |
| That the Lightning Tower wreck is here | Immediately on approach, if the crash has occurred. |
| That teaching Tod reduces chaos slightly | From doing it; from Wilder or Willow noticing the region's reading softening later. |
| The boy's real name | **Not here.** That thread belongs to his home village; Tod does not know it. |
| Why the river is the sensible way through | From a trader; from trying; from any waterway-knowledgeable local. |

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Stands outside the tree line and watches | No cost. Tod may or may not come. After a while, they see the warping details for themselves. |
| Crosses the edge for a short visit | Safe. Tod appears. Short conversations cost nothing. |
| Follows Tod deeper for an extended scene | Workings stutter; minds feel slightly off after. A second scene compounds. A third is a real cost. |
| Camps at the edge overnight | No cost beyond ambient unease. The fringe is stable. |
| Camps deep in the woods overnight | Real cost — fraying compounds across hours. Recovery takes days after leaving. |
| Cuts or burns warped trees | **The woods push back.** Local warping intensifies; animals flee further; Tod becomes harder to look at. Sustained destruction invites the larger voice. |
| Performs a major working inside the woods | The spirit reads it as provocation. Slow to register; disproportionate when it does. |
| Attempts to bind or dispel the chaos spirit | **The larger voice arrives** through Tod (see `doc/npcs/tod.md`). One clear warning. A second attempt is met with mass-scale force. |
| Tries to map the woods | The map does not hold. A few scribbles may survive; distances and relations do not. |
| Takes the river through to Raft city | Slower than it would be if the woods were normal; faster than any overland attempt across the warping. Fraying occurs but less than an overnight camp would cost. |
| Investigates the Lightning Tower wreck | Accessible. The Storm Mage is present. Salvage is possible with care. Tod may come. |
| Performs druidic or priestly rites over days to lay the boy to rest | Slow, costly, not guaranteed. If it works, the body rests. The larger spirit does not die; it loses Tod as face. It may re-condense elsewhere later. |
| Teaches Tod patiently (a foot's landing, what breath is for) | **The chaos around him softens slightly.** The effect is real, cumulative across visits, and noticed by Wilder or Willow if they are consulted. |
| Brings Tod food or other offerings | He accepts. The body cannot process food. He is grateful anyway. |
| Brings a druid or shaman for observation | Safe at the fringe. The observer learns more about chaos-spirit ecology from a single visit here than from any other accessible source on the continent. |
| Asks Tod to leave the woods with them | He cannot. He is the shape of the woods; the woods are here. |

## DM notes

- **The place and the spirit are the same thing.** The forbidden woods are a body for a chaos spirit. Tod is its face. When the party interacts with the place, they are interacting with the spirit — even when Tod is not present. A tree they touch is part of him.
- **Three layers of visit.** *Fringe* — stable, safe, where Tod meets people. *Shallow* — deeper, cost compounds, workings stutter, minds feel off. *Deep* — rarely reached, real cost, the larger voice becomes possible, the rules stop working. Build the cost across visits; do not hammer on the first scene.
- **Pair with `doc/npcs/tod.md`.** The place file covers the woods; Tod's file covers the person-shape the spirit uses to talk. Conversations happen through him. The place does not speak on its own except once or twice per campaign, via the larger voice.
- **The reader-ring is structural and load-bearing.** Druids, Luchebert's priests, Felicia's diffuse presence, and possibly the ironwood tree hold the spirit in. A DM who plans to remove or thin any of those — through plot or through player choice — should know the forbidden woods' pressure begins to spread as a consequence. This is a real campaign-scale lever.
- **The river is a safe-enough route through.** Parties pushing to Raft city should not be blocked by the woods; the river works. It is not *comfortable*, and fraying occurs on a lesser scale even there, but it works.
- **The Lightning Tower wreck is an entry point.** If the campaign has engaged the Storm Mage and broken his shackle, the party lands here with a concrete reason — salvage, the Storm Mage, the tower's workings. The woods stop being backdrop and become destination.
- **The heart is not for first-time visitors.** If the party reaches the centre, they have earned it through sustained engagement. The scene there is climactic and costly. Do not run it on the first visit.
- **Teaching Tod is a quiet good the party can do.** It does not feel like questing. It is not dramatic. But it reduces chaos, cumulatively, in the area. Reward it through NPC recognition (Wilder, Willow) later.
- **Maps and the passage of time do not work normally.** Narrate this through sensation, not mechanics. A party that insists on a map finds theirs disagreeing with itself within hours. A party that tracks time through sun-position finds the sun does not move as expected. Do not roll for it; describe it.
- **Destroying the spirit sounds like a victory and is not one.** What lives in the woods now is settled, slow, and conversable. What would move in if it were gone is worse (see `doc/npcs/tod.md` §"Role as spirit-shield (anti-shield)").

## Open questions

- The fox boy's original name and village of origin — and whether his family is still looking for him.
- Whether the spirit *knows* it is wearing a dead boy, or whether it thinks this is simply what it is now.
- Whether the forbidden-woods spirit is related to the blasted-lands spirits, to Fata morgana's desert chaos, or is independent.
- Exactly *which* mighty-spirit-readers are holding the main spirit in place. Useful to know before any of them leave or fall.
- Whether "the forbidden woods" and the broader "great forest" are the same thing, or whether the woods are a specific warped section of a larger forest system.
- How Raft city's residents read the woods — whether they know of Tod, whether they have their own lore.
