# Fata Morgana

- **Type:** Big city — a surviving oasis city within the southern desert
- **Region:** Inside the southern desert / shifting lands
- **Political status:** Independent — neither Allondo nor Brumal
- **Defining feature:** Has developed unique protection against the chaotic spirits that have made the desert so hostile

## Summary

Fata morgana sits inside the southern desert, the one advancing slowly on Brumal from the south. That it still exists at all is the interesting thing about it. The desert has become much more hostile over time — not through drought or sand alone, but because **chaotic spirits from the blasted lands have migrated into it**. Any smaller settlement would have been consumed by now. Fata morgana is not smaller; it is a big city, and it has persisted because it has **developed unique protection against those chaos spirits**.

The protection is generations in the making. It is also **limited**: there are things it cannot do, threats it cannot answer, and places inside the city where it does not reach. Fata morgana's people know this; outsiders tend not to, until they find out.

The city is the natural negotiating table for anything to do with the desert, the chaos spirits within it, or any attempt to understand what is driving Brumal's collapse. Allondo, Brumal, and the overseas powers all have reasons to be interested in Fata morgana's methods. Fata morgana has reasons not to share them freely.

## Key features

- **The protection itself.** Chaos spirits back off from anything that reads as a mighty spirit — and they cannot tell the difference between actual spirits and powerful mages, shamans, druids, and priests. Fata morgana's defence is *generations of carefully arranged mighty-spirit-readers*: priests at the wells, shamans at the gates, mages at the walls, druids in the gardens. Every part of the city that matters reads, to a chaos spirit, as territory a mightier spirit already holds. The city survives because the chaos spirits refuse to cross. The protection's **limits** follow directly: it extends only as far as the arrangement can be staffed, it fails where a protector weakens or is lost, and it cannot reach outside the city walls into the desert itself. What Fata morgana protects is *what is inside its arrangement* — nothing more, nothing less.
- **The oasis.** Water in a desert is always the underlying story; Fata morgana's oasis is presumably what kept the city possible in the first place, before the chaos spirit problem appeared.
- **Trade position.** Fata morgana is on (or near) the far end of the southern trade road, past the blasted-lands detour. Goods, refugees, and information all reach it filtered.

## Famous jewelry craft

Fata morgana is known for its **jewelry craft** — a high-value trade whose full technique is tightly held. The Brumal royal court sent **Princess Penelope** to study it in her youth, hoping to import the craft home and uplift Brumal's raw metal trade into finished goods. That plan has not panned out. Whether the craft is too entangled with Fata morgana's chaos-spirit protection techniques to travel cleanly (jewelry and wards overlapping in practice), too dependent on trainers Fata morgana declines to export, or simply larger than a single princess-apprenticeship could carry, is open. What is established: Penelope went, learned what she could, came back, and the uplift did not arrive.

## Campaign hooks

- **Source of protection lore.** If the party or the kingdoms need to defend against chaotic spirits — in the desert, in the blasted lands, or elsewhere — Fata morgana is the place that already knows how. Access to that knowledge is the negotiation.
- **Jewelry and wards.** The famous craft may or may not be separable from the protection techniques. A party interested in one will inevitably have to consider the other.
- **A known foreign student.** **Princess Penelope of Brumal** studied here in her youth and can recommend the city (or warn against it) to anyone she chooses to tell. Her network from that time, if she still has one, is the softest channel a foreign party has into Fata morgana's inner circles.
- **Limited refuge.** Fata morgana's protection is limited. The city cannot simply absorb Brumal's refugees safely; there are constraints on what (and whom) it can protect.
- **Neutral ground.** Like Salmonswell and Linar harbour, Fata morgana is outside the two kingdoms. Unlike them, it is deep in spirit-infested territory, which changes the power balance at any meeting held there.

## Relationships

- **Gap city** — on the far southern side of the desert, also not part of either kingdom. Likely in some kind of contact with Fata morgana given their shared geography.
- **Brumal** — whose collapse is downstream of the chaos spirits Fata morgana has spent generations managing.
- **The blasted lands** — original source of the chaos spirits that the city's protection is built against.
