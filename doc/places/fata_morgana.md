# Fata Morgana

- **Type:** Oasis city within the southern desert. A working city, not a mirage — despite the name.
- **Region:** Deep inside the chaotic-spirit-infested desert, on (or near) the far end of the southern trade road past the blasted-lands detour. Neighbour to Gap city on the desert's far side.
- **Officially:** Independent. Neither Allondo nor Brumal has claim; no kingdom's reach extends this deep into spirit country.
- **In practice:** A **deployment of mighty-spirit-readers** in the shape of a city — priests at the wells, shamans at the gates, mages at the walls, druids in the gardens. The chaos spirits in the desert cannot cross the arrangement. The city has survived on that logic for generations.
- **Status:** Stable, limited, cautious. The protection extends only to what the arrangement can hold; outside the walls the desert starts again. Brumal's collapse is putting pressure on the limits.

## What the party meets

The party has crossed the desert to reach Fata morgana. Whichever road they came by — up from Gap city, down from the blasted-lands detour, through the desert the long way — the crossing was hard. **Chaos spirits thicken the air.** Workings stutter; minds fray; a day's walk feels like three. A party without a shaman or a strong mighty-presence takes real damage on the crossing. A party with one takes less.

Hours before the city resolves, **a shimmer appears on the horizon.** It looks like a heat-mirage — the exact phenomenon that gave the city its name — and a first-time visitor will spend most of a day unsure whether they are looking at water or at nothing. Then the walls resolve. Then the green inside the walls. Then the sound of people and wells and the ordinary noise of a working city.

At the gate, **a shaman is already watching the party**. She has been watching for the last several hours, since they became resolvable on the approach. Her assessment is not hostile. It is also not *yet* welcoming. It is careful, and the party will be assessed on how they carry themselves across the last hundred yards.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The shimmer.** The city reads as *almost not there* from the desert. Stone and palm and water, all at the edge of haze. Even inside the gates a traveller new to the heat will keep feeling the place is about to dissolve. **Fata morgana earns its name every day.** The shimmer is also the first line of defence — chaos spirits looking for structure to warp find the city's edge *indistinct*, and indistinct is harder to attack.
- **Mighty-spirit-readers at every civic point.** A priest kneels at the nearest well, tending the water at a pace the party will recognise as *ritual*, not casual. A shaman sits cross-legged at the gate arch. A druid is visibly hand-watering a row of date palms at the first street-corner. A mage stands on the walkway above the wall, looking outward. **Each post is staffed, each post is watched, and the city is laid out so that no part of it is more than a few paces from one of the readers.** The party will realise, within an hour, that the staffing is the city.
- **The jewelry.** Shop-fronts along the market street carry work in gold, silver, and inlay that even a casual traveller recognises as *finer than anywhere else*. Patterns the eye wants to follow even when it should be looking at something else. A closer look shows the same patterns — reduced, repeated — in the door-frames, the well-copings, the gate-stones. The craft and the wards are one practice; a visitor will learn this slowly.

## At the approach — crossing the desert

The last day into Fata morgana is the hardest stretch of desert the party has walked. The spirits are at their thickest here — they are *leaving* the city, bouncing off its readers, and the dispersion-field around the walls is where they concentrate before scattering.

- **A guide makes the difference.** A shaman or druid-companion on the party can steer by the spirits' patterns — using their hostility as a negative map of Fata morgana's influence. Without one, the party follows the shimmer and hopes.
- **Water runs out here fast.** A party that expected to ration for two more days finds they need to ration for three. The desert is not only sand.
- **Mirages of Fata morgana.** Ironically, *real* fata morganas of the city appear several times during the last approach — flat reflections of the actual city projected on the sand. Disorienting. A party will arrive at a mirage before they arrive at the city at least once.

## At the gates — first filtering

The gate is low, stone, unassuming. No garrison. A shaman sits beside it on a woven mat, within reach of a pot of water. One or two more readers are usually nearby — a priest walking past, a mage coming down off the wall.

- **The shaman reads the party first.** Not as interrogation — she is *feeling* who they are. A party carrying chaos-spirit residue (from the forbidden woods, the blasted lands, or the desert crossing) will be offered water and a seat before anything else; the residue bleeds off faster inside the shaman's radius.
- **Questions come after.** Who the party is, where they are coming from, what they want here. Names are asked. The shaman's memory is long; names she does not recognise go into a register that is checked later. **Penelope's name opens doors** if the party has honest standing with her.
- **The city admits freely.** Fata morgana does not turn travellers away at the gate. It receives them — and then sorts them by behaviour over the first day or two inside.
- **Weapons are not collected.** The city trusts its protection, not its guards. A party that behaves with any weapon is watched; a party that misbehaves is escorted out and not welcomed back.

## At the market — jewelry as craft and as ward

A single long street of open-fronted shops, each a family's workshop. Gold and silver worked into pendants, rings, torcs, small pieces the trade road carries out at high value.

- **Two levels of craft visible.** The outer-facing work — pretty, sellable, recognisable — fills the front of each shop. The back benches hold quieter pieces with **pattern-work that matches the door-frames and the well-copings**. That second level is the ward-work; it is not for sale in the ordinary sense.
- **Penelope's old apprenticeship is remembered.** A shop-keeper approached with her name responds warmly, if carefully. *"The princess. She was here for a year. She learned the outer work well. The inner work we could not teach her fully in the time she had."* A party with her confidence can reach further into the back benches on that reference.
- **Buying is transactional; learning is not.** A party can buy outer pieces for coin. A party wanting to *learn* the craft must commit the kind of years Penelope committed — or find a teacher willing to teach in a compressed form, which is rare.
- **Brumal's interest is known.** The shop-keepers know Brumal sent their princess, that the plan was uplift, and that the plan did not carry. They are not resentful about it; they note the outcome.

## At the wells — the water life of the city

Several wells stand at the central squares. Each has a priest or a priest-apprentice at it during daylight hours.

- **Tending is continuous.** The priest blesses the water, the well-copings, and the small offerings left at the rim. The work is unhurried. The rhythm is the city's heartbeat.
- **The water is drinkable by anyone.** The protection the priests provide is *spirit-ward*, not ritual-exclusion. A stranger with a cup can drink without asking.
- **Offerings at the rim** are small — a coin, a bead, a folded paper with a name, a fruit. These accumulate through the day and are cleared at sunset. A party will recognise the ritual pattern from their own cultures, adapted.
- **The wells are the protection made visible.** A shaman-tutor will point out that wells are natural spirit-attractors and that Fata morgana's priests are the first line holding them. Without the tending, the wells would pull chaos spirits into the city's heart. With the tending, the wells are *strong points*.

## At the gardens and walls — where the green and the outward watch are

Small gardens between the streets — date palms, fig trees, rows of low vegetables kept alive by careful irrigation and careful druid-attention.

- **Druids are visible at the plots**, hand-watering, feeling for plant health in ways that read as more than agriculture. A *Druid* character will recognise the practice immediately; a casual party will see a city that takes its food seriously.
- **The walls are walkable.** Mages stand watch along the parapet, looking outward across the desert. The watching is *spirit-sighting*, not lookout-for-enemies. A party invited up — a rare courtesy — sees the desert as the mages see it: spirits thick around the city, thinner further out, thickest at the blasted-lands horizon.
- **The walls are decorated with ward-patterns** matching the jewelry's inner work. A walking mage will point out a specific segment and explain, in broad terms, what it does. Specifics are not shared with strangers.

## At the inner circles — where knowledge-negotiation happens

Fata morgana's **inner practitioners** — the senior shamans, priests, mages, and druids who designed and maintain the arrangement — do not meet visitors casually. Reaching them is a matter of earned standing.

- **Not one building but a rotation.** The inner circle meets on a changing schedule, at different senior homes, to keep the city's defence diffused rather than centralised. A party reaching them is not reaching a council at a specific office; they are being invited to a specific evening, at a specific home, by a specific host.
- **Conversation here is slow and specific.** What does the party want? Why do they want it? What can they offer? The inner circle will answer direct questions with direct answers — but only after establishing the answers will not be misused.
- **Protection-knowledge is the most gated topic.** Teaching outsiders how the arrangement works is a real risk — if the technique travels poorly, or travels to the wrong people, Fata morgana has traded its safety for nothing. A party wanting to carry the knowledge home — for Elmsfield, for the hills around the lake, for Luchebert — is asking a big thing. The conversation will span multiple visits.
- **Penelope's old network runs through here.** Some of the inner practitioners taught her the outer craft. One or two were her inner-craft tutors and stopped when the apprenticeship ended. These ties are the cleanest soft-entry a party with her standing can use.

## At the edge — where the protection ends

Walk to the far side of the city and the ward-work ends at the wall. Beyond it, the desert. Within a dozen paces outward, the party can feel the difference: the air tightens, spirits thicken, the mind goes small.

- **A marked line at the wall base.** A band of stones, slightly different in colour, runs along the outer wall-foot. This is where the readers' combined radius terminates. Inside is protected; outside is not.
- **Visitors sometimes camp outside the wall.** The city cannot take them all in; a party may see a small camp of travellers the city has not yet admitted, or has declined to admit. These visitors are *in the desert*, under the desert's rules, despite being in sight of the city.
- **Refugees get the hardest version of this.** Brumal refugees arriving in large numbers cannot all be absorbed; the arrangement has a staffing limit. The city takes what it can and must refuse the rest — which refusals put real people in real desert-danger. A DM should let this land as the quiet tragedy it is.
- **The edge is where Brumal's collapse is visible** in a way it is not anywhere else on the continent. Travellers who reach here from the south are carrying stories the interior has not yet heard.

## Visible patterns

- **Staffing at every civic point.** Water, gate, garden, wall. Always someone reading the spirit-field.
- **Unhurried rituals.** Nothing in Fata morgana's daily practice is rushed. Ritual time is protection time; hurrying breaks it.
- **Jewelry and wards as one practice.** The eye that notices patterns will see them repeated everywhere — from pendants to door-frames to well-copings.
- **Shimmer day and night.** The city never quite resolves completely. Even a long-term resident will say *"the walls look different today"* and be half-right.
- **Travellers arriving shaken.** Almost everyone who reaches Fata morgana has come across the desert and is in some state of strain. The priests give water; the city is practised at this.
- **Closed-door conversations among inner practitioners.** A house with lamps burning late and visitors coming and going quietly is probably hosting an inner-circle evening.
- **The name-register.** The shaman at the gate keeps one; the priests at the wells keep a version; the inner practitioners compare notes on who has been here and what they were carrying.

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Arrives across the desert exhausted and chaos-touched | Received with water and a seat at the gate. The shaman's radius bleeds off the residue. No demands for hours. |
| Arrives carrying Penelope's name with honest standing | Specific contacts open — at the shops she studied in, in the inner circle where her tutors remember her. |
| Tries to buy outer jewelry | Trade is easy. Prices are high; quality is real. |
| Tries to buy inner ward-work jewelry without standing | Declined politely. *"This piece is not in the window."* |
| Asks a shop-keeper to teach the craft | Declined unless the party is committing years. Penelope's committed year is the reference case. |
| Asks how the protection works | Inner-circle topic. A casual ask gets a casual deflection. A serious, committed ask — with a clear reason — may open a multi-visit conversation. |
| Tries to perform a major working inside the city | The readers notice before the working finishes. A shaman will appear; a polite conversation follows; the working is stopped or guided. |
| Performs **chaos-reducing** work in the city (teaching, like with Tod) | Noticed with real interest. The practitioners may seek the party out to discuss. |
| Brings a large refugee group to the walls | **Painful refusal.** The city cannot absorb all. Some will be taken in; most will not. The party is asked to help the rest find their way to Gap city or to Salmonswell via the long route. |
| Arrives as a formal delegation from Allondo or Brumal | Received correctly. The inner circle rotates a host; the visit happens; nothing is promised, most is discussed. |
| Meets a Brumal envoy and an Allondan envoy here | Possible. The city hosts; the city does not broker. |
| Tries to map the arrangement (which reader is where, rotation schedules) | Noticed. A sustained attempt loses standing. The map the party would draw is already in the register kept by the inner circle. |
| Steals a ward-work piece | Recovered within the day. The party is escorted out of the city. The city's register records their names permanently. |
| Asks about the desert's chaos spirits' origin | Talked about. Pointed at the blasted lands. The details become specific with trust. |
| Asks about Gap city | Freely discussed. Directions given. Warnings about the crossing given too. |
| Asks to accompany a mage's wall-watch | Possible, once trust is earned. A single watch-turn teaches more about spirit-sighting than any book. |
| Stays in the city for weeks | Becomes known. Names register. By the end of a month, the inner circle knows the party well enough to speak candidly. |

## DM notes

- **Porous and limited in equal measure.** The city receives visitors, hears out requests, and commits to little. A party trying to *extract* knowledge or guarantees will hit Fata morgana's patience — which is deep but not infinite. A party trying to *earn* relationship will find the city genuinely engaged.
- **Penelope is the cleanest soft-entry.** If the campaign has opened the Corven/Penelope arc, her standing opens specific shop-floors and specific inner-circle homes. The DM can use her name as a tunnel into depth the party would otherwise need weeks to reach.
- **The staffing of the city is the protection.** Make it visible. Every scene should have at least one reader nearby — at a well, at a gate, at a garden, on a wall. A DM who omits the readers omits the city's main visual fact.
- **The edge is quiet tragedy.** The refugees refused at the walls, the camp of travellers outside, the understanding that the arrangement cannot scale — these are not villain-shaped facts. They are limits. Let the party sit with them.
- **Shimmer as a scene-tool.** The city's almost-not-there quality lets the DM narrate *physical* uncertainty — walls that look different in the morning, a shop-front that the party remembered elsewhere, a gate that feels further than it was. Use this sparingly and deliberately; it is part of Fata morgana's voice.
- **The Nadine breadcrumb lands through the song, not through geography.** Nadine's *shimmering-city* song (see `doc/npcs/nadine.md`) points the party here. When the party arrives and sees a shimmer that the song had described, the payoff is earned. Do not spell the connection out; let them notice.
- **The chaos-reducing work is the highest honor.** A party that, across the campaign, has taught Tod and earned standing at Fata morgana is doing the same work the city has done for generations. The inner circle will recognise kin-practice. This opens the deepest conversations the city can host.
- **No named NPCs assigned.** The file treats the city collectively. Named practitioners — a senior shaman, the gate-watcher, a specific jeweler-tutor, Penelope's old inner teacher — should be filled by the DM when the campaign needs them.
- **Gap city is a live neighbour.** Unwritten but mentioned. If the campaign reaches toward the desert's southern side, Fata morgana is the place that has been corresponding with Gap city for generations.

## Open questions

- **Named inner practitioners.** The senior shaman who gate-watched Penelope's arrival; her primary jewelry teacher; the priest-elder at the central well; the mage who walks the north wall. DM-fill as the campaign needs them.
- **The exact composition of the reader-rotation** — how many people the arrangement needs, how they are recruited, how apprenticeships work.
- **Gap city's relationship to Fata morgana** — formal alliance, informal correspondence, rival practice, or something else.
- **Whether the technique can, in principle, be taught and exported** — and whether Fata morgana has ever done so. The inner circle's answer is probably *"carefully, and not yet,"* but the specifics matter if the campaign opens the Brumal-collapse thread to an arc where the technique becomes a possible intervention.
- **How much of the protection depends on the specific site** (the oasis, the well-network) versus the practice (the readers' deployment) — and whether a sister-city could be built on the same principles elsewhere.
