# The Mirror Camp

- **NPC-tied dungeon** — **multi-tied**: Thorgal (the residue; wants bonding); Raul (wants delivery); Felix (points the way, on request); Rhianna (confirms under pointed questioning); Hannes (will never say).
- **Type:** An abandoned overseas-forces forward camp in the hills, where the Overseas War's pivot moment happened. The scrying-mirror Thorgal used to command Raul is still here, cracked but not destroyed. Thorgal's residue persists in it.
- **Region:** The hills between the Great Swamp and Allondo, north of the Lake of Tears, in a valley Allondo's current maps prefer to leave blank.
- **Officially:** Nothing. The camp is unmarked. The crown pretends it is not there.
- **In practice:** **The campaign's haunted-mirror location.** A mage-aspirant can bond with Thorgal's residue here and inherit overseas mage-craft in exchange for showing Thorgal the world he never got to set foot on. A non-mage party has other reasons to come (Raul's request; Felix's curiosity; Rhianna's unspoken unresolved business; the sheer weight of the site).

## The NPC hook — how the party arrives here

The site is gated by *knowledge*, not distance. Four paths lead to it; each is an NPC door.

### Raul — direct request

If the campaign has brought **Raul** in (see `doc/npcs/raul.md`), he wants the mirror. He actively seeks couriers. He pays well. He is willing to meet the party and brief them. A party that accepts the mirror-retrieval contract from Raul has the fastest route to the camp — **he knows where it is** and will describe the location specifically. *"I was its commander. I know where I left it."*

The tradeoff: Raul's purposes for the mirror are not the party's. A party that delivers the mirror to him closes a door (the haunted mirror goes *overseas*, potentially under the control of heirs of the old faction). A party that accepts the contract and *does not deliver* makes an enemy.

### Felix — quiet pointer

**Felix** knows where the camp is. He pulled Hannes out of it. He will **not volunteer** — it is not his information to give. A party that asks him at a fair, over a specific drink, with the right conversation, gets directions. *"I have been once. I do not go back. You will find what you find."*

### Rhianna — drawn out

**Rhianna** knows the location through back-channel work done years ago. She will not volunteer either. A party that has read the war's *fuller* history, has earned her standing, and asks *specifically* — *"Where was Hannes held?"* — gets a cautious confirmation and a rough location. She wants to know what the party does with the information.

### Hannes — will never

**Hannes** knows exactly where the camp is. He will **never say.** To speak of it is to admit the full shape of his capture. A party that raises the mirror with him in audience gets a signet-hand-flat and a close of topic. Do not use this path.

### The mage-aspirant

A party with a **serious mage student** in it has a fifth path: **study of pre-Rift and overseas mage-tradition leads here.** A mage reading deeply in Luchebert's library, or studying with Rhianna, or earning fragments from Laurent, eventually finds the mirror referenced in the margins of old notes. They want to see it. They want to *bond* with it. This is a character-level motivation that brings the party along.

## What the party meets

The approach is up a thin valley that Allondo's current road-network pretends does not exist. A cart cannot go; horses can to the treeline; then on foot. The last hour is up a wooded slope that ought to have a path and does not — **somebody has been unmaking the path since the war.**

The camp appears suddenly: a clearing among the trees, a quarter-mile across, that holds the remains of **a forward military camp abandoned in a single afternoon**.

- **Tents have collapsed into mouldered heaps.** Canvas rotted; poles still upright in a few places.
- **Cooking-fires gone to pits.** Kettles lying on their sides.
- **The forage-racks overgrown** with the specific vines that favour abandoned ground.
- **A command tent at the centre** — larger than the rest, its frame still standing. Inside it: the mirror.

The party will hear wind. They will hear nothing else. The camp's specific silence is not natural. **Thorgal's residue makes a small cone of quiet around the mirror.**

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts

- **The camp was abandoned mid-activity.** A cooking-pot on a pit. A pair of boots half-out of a tent. A weapon-rack with three blades still in it. Nothing was taken when Raul's force fell back; the retreat was that fast.
- **The command tent in the centre, still standing.** Canvas blackened where the mirror's light once shone. Wooden ribs intact. **The mirror is visible from the tent's open flap, upright, propped against the central pole.**
- **The specific silence.** A hundred paces out, a party hears forest. Inside the camp's radius, the forest sounds fade. At the tent, silence is *complete* — no wind, no distant bird, no breathing audible but one's own. The residue defines the zone.

## At the outer camp — the fall's frozen moment

The tents, the fires, the racks. What Raul's force left when the mirror went dark.

- **Search the tents.** Most hold nothing. One or two still have overseas-made items — a carved drinking-horn, a tobacco-pouch with leaves the party has not seen before, a letter in a script the party cannot read (overseas alphabet; a shaman or a scholar can eventually decode it; an emotional letter from a soldier's mother).
- **Bones at the forage-racks.** A few. Overseas soldiers who died of wounds they did not have time to report. Their gear is still with them.
- **A single horse-skeleton** at the picket-line, its reins still tied. The horse panicked in the retreat and was forgotten.
- **Raul's tent specifically**, on the east side, is identifiable by its size. Inside: a camp-bed; a chest the retreat did not unload; inside the chest, **a rolled map of the Allondan interior from the overseas-mage command's perspective**, with positions, routes, and Allondo's military estimates marked in an overseas hand. A rare piece.

## At the command tent — approaching the mirror

The centre of the camp. The tent-flap is pulled aside, the canvas blackened.

- **The mirror is upright, propped against the central pole.** About a man's height. Silver-backed. **Cracked** in a long spider-web pattern from a single impact at the centre — John Bean's reach-through, a thousand miles of mage-working undone by one farmer's phantom-arm. The crack has not propagated further. The mirror is still whole.
- **Thorgal's residue is here.** A *Shamanic* or *Mage* character feels it immediately: a consciousness, thinned, weary, *still present*, occupying the mirror's workings.
- **Approaching is physically easy.** The residue is not violent. It is *aware* and *waiting*. It has been waiting for centuries for the right visitor.
- **A party with a mage-aspirant triggers Thorgal's attention.** The residue *reads the student* and decides whether to engage.

## At the mirror — Thorgal speaks (if engaged)

A scene, not a combat. Thorgal's residue can be spoken with by any character with *Mage* or *Shamanic* training. The voice arrives in the party's heads — not loud, not intrusive, *proposing*.

What Thorgal will tell (from lore — see `doc/npcs/thorgal.md`):
- He was not a conqueror. He led a starving continent's emigration under arms.
- His faction was not unanimous. Those who opposed him could not offer an alternative.
- He does not know who succeeded him.
- He will bond with the right mage-aspirant. The bond gives the aspirant overseas mage-craft — *technique unavailable to this continent's current mage tradition* — in exchange for showing Thorgal the world he never reached.
- The bond is *real*. It is not possession. The aspirant remains themselves, *plus*.
- He cannot leave the mirror on his own. He can be carried with the mirror, or the bond can extract enough of him to travel with the aspirant.

**The offer is genuine and campaign-scale.** A DM running this scene should treat it with real weight.

## At the mirror — the other options

A party without a mage-aspirant still has choices.

- **Deliver the mirror to Raul.** Possible. Raul pays well. The mirror goes overseas. Campaign consequence: the overseas heirs of the old faction gain a voice of Thorgal again. Real cost.
- **Destroy the mirror.** Difficult. The residue protects the mirror from casual damage — attacks glance off. A **committed working**, specifically directed at the residue first and the glass second, can destroy the mirror. Thorgal ends; the overseas connection closes; the campaign loses a unique breadcrumb to overseas politics.
- **Leave the mirror in place.** The camp stays. The site becomes known to someone (Raul, a successor, a scholar). The party has seen and chosen not to act.
- **Carry the mirror somewhere safer.** Luchebert will reluctantly receive it. Rhianna will *reluctantly* receive it. Haven would accept it with cautious gratitude. Each destination shapes what happens next.

## Creatures — what has moved in

The camp has been empty for a decade. Things have arrived:

- **A pack of hill-wolves** dens in one of the collapsed tents. Not exceptional; they can be dealt with as ordinary wolves (see CREATURES.md). They do not enter the command tent — the silence-zone keeps them out.
- **An encroaching *encroachment-scale* creature** may have taken up residence in the overseer's quarters — DM-fill. A *hearth-dweller* in the captain's tent; an *inn-walker* in the command tent's margin; a *door-kept* at the camp's original gate-line. Pick one. The presence signals the camp's *leak* into encroachment-territory.
- **The mirror's silence-zone** keeps larger creatures away from the command tent. The party is safest there.

## Treasure — what the NPCs want (and what else)

- **For Raul:** the mirror. He will pay handsomely; the payment is overseas coin and overseas access.
- **For Felix:** nothing. He has no stake; he provided directions.
- **For Rhianna:** knowledge of what the party chose. She will not ask them to destroy it or deliver it; she will ask what they did and will respond accordingly.
- **For a mage-aspirant:** the bond. Campaign-scale character change.
- **For Luchebert:** if the mirror goes there, it becomes a sheltered artefact. Elliot will accept reluctantly; he has known about it since the war.
- **For the party:** Raul's map of the Allondan interior; the overseas drinking-horn; the soldier's letter (once translated, a piece of the Overseas War's human register).

## If the party does something — what happens

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Arrives as Raul's courier | Receives the route. Arrives efficiently. Raul's people may be watching. |
| Arrives from Felix's pointer | Solo route. No watchers. They have time. |
| Arrives from Rhianna's cautious confirmation | Rhianna's concern travels with them; she wants to know the result. |
| Arrives with a mage-aspirant | Thorgal's residue attends. The offer becomes available. |
| Approaches the mirror respectfully | No threat. The silence-zone receives them. Conversation with Thorgal is possible. |
| Attacks the mirror without a mage's preparation | Attacks glance. The residue defends. The party does not damage the mirror unless prepared. |
| Performs a working to destroy the residue | **Difficult scene.** Possible with effort. Campaign cost is real. |
| Carries the mirror to Raul | Delivered. Payment in overseas coin. Campaign-consequence: overseas faction gains a breadcrumb. |
| Carries the mirror to Rhianna | She takes it grimly. It goes into her private collection. The campaign loses a direct access-channel to Thorgal but keeps him reachable via her. |
| Carries the mirror to Luchebert | Elliot receives. The mirror sits sheltered; Thorgal stays reachable but gated by the abbey. |
| Carries the mirror to Haven | Accepted cautiously. Haven's view on bound-spirit artefacts is careful; they will treat it with respect and *store* it. |
| Bonds a mage-aspirant with Thorgal | **Campaign-scale character change.** The aspirant gains overseas mage-craft; the aspirant's perception changes; Thorgal accompanies them in a diminished form. |
| Fails to bond and leaves the mirror | Thorgal waits longer. The next visitor finds what the party found. |
| Shares the location publicly | The camp becomes destination. Scholars, skull-gatherers, faction-agents arrive. The site's integrity ends. |

## DM notes

- **Thorgal is not a villain in this scene.** Lore (see `doc/npcs/thorgal.md`) is explicit: he led a starving continent. The residue carries that honestly. A DM who plays him as the evil overseas-mage loses both the arc and the campaign's moral framing. Play him as the man he was — weary, reflective, aware his choices were bad and could not have been better.
- **The bond is a real campaign-scale offer.** If a player takes it, the consequences unfold across many sessions: new craft-techniques, new attention from the overseas continent, new vulnerability to overseas workings. The DM should run this as a genuine change, not a gimmick.
- **Raul's path is the *bad* default.** Not *evil* — *bad*. Delivering the mirror to Raul closes a useful campaign door. A DM who has a Raul-committed party should let them choose freely; the consequences follow.
- **The camp is small and focused.** One-session dungeon. The mirror is the scene; the outer camp is colour.
- **The silence-zone is a scene-tool.** Use it deliberately — when the party enters the zone, narrate the silence. When they approach the mirror, make it audible that *nothing else is*.
- **Hannes's attitude matters.** If Hannes ever learns the party went, his reaction is complicated — shame that the site exists, anger that it was visited, concern about what was found. Keep this in reserve for a later scene at court.
- **The site connects multiple NPCs cleanly.** Raul, Felix, Rhianna, Hannes, Thorgal, a mage-aspirant. The party's brokering of these NPCs' attitudes about the mirror is a scene worth running *before* they go.
