# The Tidehold

- **Type:** Pre-rift sea-mage's cliff-tower, half-claimed by the sea. Built into the cliff face where the coastal mountain range descends to ocean. Two of its four levels are walkable only at low tide; one only at extreme low tide once a season; the upper level reached by a cut stair from the cliff top that is older than anyone has been here to maintain.
- **Region:** **Eastern coast**, on the stretch where the coastal mountain range meets the sea **south of Linar harbour and north of the pirate nest** (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md` §"At the southern coast road"). Reachable from land by the cut stair (climb work; the trail is half-erased); from sea by a small boat in good weather at low tide; not reachable in most other conditions.
- **Officially:** Nobody's. Linar harbour does not claim it and would not enforce a claim if it did. The sea-mage who built it is centuries dead; no successor was named.
- **In practice:** **A working-tower the sea has been taking back**. The lower workings flood twice a day; the salt has eaten what it could eat; what survives, survives because it was made to. The Tidehold is the rare cliff-coast site where a pre-rift practitioner left enough behind that a careful party can still read what they were doing.
- **Status:** Stable as ruin. Tidal cycles are reliable; the cut stair is dangerous but predictable; the bound spirit at the well-room has been there for centuries and will be there for centuries more.

## What the party meets

The party reaches the Tidehold by one of three approaches: **down the cut stair from the cliff top** (Linar fishers know roughly where it begins; the trail through the heath is half-erased and a *Lookout* finds the head-stone); **by small boat at low tide** (the cliff face has a sea-cave entry that is door-shaped if you know where to look); or **at extreme low tide once a season** (a third approach opens at the very base — visible only when the king-tide pulls the water further than usual). The first approach is the safest in any tide; the second works for the lower levels; the third is the rare one that opens the drowned vault.

Whichever approach they take, the first thing the party meets is **the sound of the sea inside the stone**. The Tidehold is *carved* — corridors and rooms cut into the cliff itself, with the outer wall replaced by working-stone where the sea-side opens. The sound never stops. A party that camps inside hears it when they sleep.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The cut stair.** From the cliff top: a stone stair cut into the rock face, descending in switchbacks toward a flat platform two-thirds of the way down. **Older than visible work elsewhere on the coast.** Three of its treads are gone; one switchback's outer wall has fallen; a *Climber* with rope handles the gaps without trouble, an unprepared party loses time and possibly a person.
- **The salt-line on every wall.** Inside, every wall carries a horizontal band where the salt has stopped — *the high-tide mark from a thousand years of cycles*. Below the line: pitted, fragile, missing. Above the line: smooth, still working. The party reads the building's *flooded depth* at a glance; the band tells them which levels are walkable now and which are sea-floor at high tide.
- **The window onto open sea.** The upper room's seaward wall is open — not collapsed; *built that way*. Stone frame, clear sight to the horizon, the wind coming straight in. **The mage who built this watched the sea continuously.** A party standing at the window sees what they saw. The room is the place the party will remember.

## At the cliff top — finding the cut stair

The trail begins on the heath above the cliffs. Linar fishers know it; nobody walks it now.

- **The head-stone is a stub of cut rock** at the trail's start, easy to walk past. Carved with a small mark — a circle bisected by a vertical line, *the sea-mage's seal*. *Lookout* finds it; *Reading* names what the seal means if the party has read in Luchebert's library.
- **The trail is half-erased** by a generation of weather. *Hunter* reads it as a working-trail (laid by purpose, not animal-made); *Climber* reads the sequence of switchbacks as deliberately spaced for ropework that no longer exists.
- **The descent is real.** Three tread-gaps; one wall-fall. A party with a *Climber* and a length of rope handles each in turn; a party without takes the descent as a series of held-breath jumps. The platform two-thirds down is where the cut stair meets the upper room's outer door.

## At the upper room — the watching window

Reached by the cut stair. The party steps off the platform into a stone room that has held against the sea for centuries because the working in its frame is *still running*.

- **The window onto open sea**, framed in stone. *Always cold in summer; less cold than expected in winter*; the wind off the sea passes through and out again rather than swirling.
- **A long working bench** under the window — slate top, salt-crusted but intact. Tools laid out where the mage left them: a small **deepwater glass** lens (Special Material per `doc/claude/ITEMS.md`); a brass-and-bone tide-counter that still works; a sealed inkpot whose ink has not dried. A party with *Wright* or *Hand* reads the bench as a *measuring-station* — the mage was reading the sea, not commanding it.
- **A shelf of bound notebooks**. Pre-rift mage's hand. Older volumes are damp through; **two on the highest shelf are dry and readable** — the working-bench's residual heat-spell preserved them. Notes on tidal patterns, the binding of small water-spirits at the well-room below, observations on the deeper currents. **A serious mage student earns a real piece of pre-rift sea-craft from a winter's reading.**
- **A tide-pattern carving runs around the room's stone window-frame** — pre-rift; small pictograms following the working-tongue's reading direction. The carving names the *full tidal cycle* by sound — twelve tide-states, twelve cycle-names, the working's pulse. *Reading* recovers the reading-direction; a Mage student studying the notebooks at the bench can use the window-frame as a *reference key* the bench's notebooks point to but never reproduce in full.
- **A faded fresco above the bench** — the sea-mage at work. A robed figure stands at the bench facing the window; the sea outside is rendered in three layers (surface, mid-water, deep current); thread-lines arc from the figure's hand into each layer. The signature in the fresco's lower right is *not* a Vintala-line mark — the sea-mage was from a different tradition. **A *Reading* check identifies the signature as belonging to the Lyrentine line**, a coastal mage-house that does not appear in any other surviving canon. *The Tidehold is the only known site where a Lyrentine-line working still runs.*

The room is also the **only level safe to camp in.** A party using it as a base point for descents has the Tidehold's one stable working-space.

## At the tidal level — the well-room

A stair descends from the upper room to a level that floods twice a day. **At low tide:** ankle-deep at the entrance, dry to mid-shin in the inner rooms, a working **stone well at the centre** that is itself the room's purpose. **At high tide:** all of it under water; the stair is sealed by the sea against the upper level for six hours at a stretch.

- **The well-room's bound spirit** is a small **Water Elemental** (`doc/claude/CREATURES.md`) bound by the sea-mage to *read* the tide rather than to fight. It is not hostile to a respectful visitor at low tide; it surfaces in the well, reads the party in turn, and responds to a *Shaman* who knows its register. *Druid* does not work here — the spirit is not accommodated, it is bound.
- **The working at the well** is what kept the upper-room notebooks dry. A party that *removes the binding* (release the spirit; carry the well-stone away; break the stone deliberately) loses the residual heat-spell upstairs within days, and the notebooks rot. The binding is integral to the place.
- **A party caught here at the turning tide** has six hours sealed below. The room does not flood entirely — there is a high-shelf in the inner room with breath-room, but the wait is real and the cold is real. *Sleeper* and *Survivor* characters handle it; others lose stat points to the wait.
- **The first finds**: a sealed glass vial in the well-room's inner shelf — *one Refreshment potion, found-form per `doc/claude/ITEMS.md`*; a small carved **windbone-and-water clasp** (Bracelet Material variant for a buoyancy-aspected wearer); the sea-mage's smaller working tools on a shelf above the salt-line.

## At the sea-cave entry — the boat approach

Reached only by small boat at low tide. The cliff face has a vertical fissure that reads as natural until the boat is ten paces off; then the worked stone of the lower threshold becomes visible.

- **A *Boater* lands the boat cleanly** in the right window of the tide; an unskilled party scrapes the boat or misses the entry entirely. **The window is short** — about twenty minutes per low tide.
- **The threshold opens into the lower corridor**, which connects up to the well-room and (at extreme low tide) down to the drowned vault. A party arriving by boat reaches the well-room without using the cut stair; they also have a *boat tied at the threshold* that the next high tide will lift away if not retrieved.
- **A *Swimmer* can reach the threshold without the boat** in calm weather; a party member with the power has a route the rest of the party cannot follow.

## At the drowned vault — extreme low tide only

Once a season, the king-tide pulls the water further than usual. The base of the cliff exposes a third entry — a low arch that is sea-floor for the rest of the year. **The window is two hours, twice in the king-tide week.** A party that times it has a fully accessible vault; a party that mistimes is sealed in.

- **The vault holds the Tidehold's principal find.** A pre-rift **navigation chart of the eastern coast and the deeper currents**, sealed in a bone tube against the salt — usable by a serious sailor or a Mage student to read the sea the way the sea-mage read it. The chart is *the only one of its kind on this side of the rift*.
- **The chamber is small.** A working-circle on the floor, a stone bench, the bone-tube on a hook above the bench. The mage came here once a season for whatever the chart let them do.
- **Hazards**: the chamber's ceiling is low and the floor is slippery; the room itself is safe enough; the *getting back* is the danger. A party that lingers past the king-tide window is sealed in for **six months** until the next pull. The breath-room is real; the food is not.
- **A small chaos-warped sea-creature** (DM-fill — a coiled fragment of something the mage was studying when the rift hit) lives at the back of the vault; it is curious rather than hostile, but a careless party may startle it into a defensive strike. *Shaman* settles it; *Smell* reads the wrongness; *Druid* does not work here either.

## Lore the place contributes

The Tidehold is the campaign's only direct window onto the *Lyrentine line* and pre-rift sea-mage practice generally:

- **A pre-rift mage tradition the kingdoms have lost in full** — the Lyrentine line is named here and nowhere else in canon. A party reading the bench's notebooks and the window-frame tide-pattern reconstructs *the only working sea-mage practice still legible on the continent*. Master Darius's grandfather mentioned a *sea-mage's tower somewhere on the coast*; the Tidehold is where that fragment of correspondence lands.
- **The gentle-binding signature, broader than the Pair** — the Tidehold's well-spirit is bound in the same kindly register as the Lethran Pair's spirits at the Anchor and the Grain-mill, *but the binding-mark is different*. The lore-revelation: gentle-binding was a *cross-tradition pre-rift register* — the Pair did not invent it; they inherited a craft-virtue the kingdoms have forgotten was a virtue.
- **A navigation chart the rift took elsewhere** — the drowned vault's pre-rift chart of the eastern coast and deeper currents is the *only one of its kind on this side of the rift*. Cross-references the overseas-east connection that Thorgal's residue (`doc/npcs/thorgal.md`) carries from the other side.
- **A complete pre-rift tidal-cycle reading** — the window-frame carving names twelve tide-states by working-tongue. A *Boater* or *Swimmer* trained in this nomenclature reads coastal water differently for the rest of the campaign.
- **The sister-tower thread** — the notebooks reference a *colleague at the next headland north*. Open thread; if developed, a second Lyrentine-line site exists to find. The Tidehold is the *worked example* for any future cliff-coast dungeon.

## Visible patterns

- **The salt-line maps the building's flooded depth.** Walls below it are fragile; walls above it are working. A party that learns to read the line moves through the Tidehold safely; a party that does not loses time to wrong-room choices.
- **The sea is always audible.** Inside every room. A *Hearing*-aware character reads the *current* state of the tide from the sound's pattern.
- **The bound spirit at the well does not move.** It does not pursue. It does not surprise. The Tidehold's hazards are tidal, structural, and time-bound — not the spirit.
- **The cut stair's gaps do not change.** Once mapped, they are predictable. A second descent is faster than the first.
- **The king-tide is the calendar.** Linar fishers know when the king-tide pulls; a party can plan around it if they ask the right fisher.

## What you learn here, and when

| Topic | When the place gives it up |
|---|---|
| The mage was a *measurer*, not a commander | At the working bench; *Wright* reads it within an hour. |
| The notebooks survive on the highest shelf | On entering the upper room; the dry shelf is visible. |
| The well-room's spirit is bound, not free | At the well at low tide; *Shaman* reads it immediately. |
| The boat threshold exists | From a Linar fisher; or by *Lookout* from a boat at low tide. |
| The drowned vault exists | Only by reading the notebooks, by a Linar fisher's cousin who saw it once, or by *Shaman* asking the well-spirit politely. |
| The chart is in the vault | From the notebooks; or by reaching the vault. |
| The breath-room timing is real | By failing to leave on time, or by careful pre-planning. |

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Descends the cut stair carefully with rope | Reaches the upper room without loss. |
| Descends without rope | One stat-loss on the wall-fall switchback; possibly worse on the third tread-gap. |
| Camps in the upper room | Rests cleanly. The window-wind makes for thin sleep; *Sleeper* compensates. |
| Removes the working from the well | Notebooks rot upstairs in three nights; the upper room loses its heat-spell; the Tidehold begins to fail. |
| Asks the well-spirit a question respectfully (*Shaman*) | Receives an answer in the spirit's register — a tide-rhythm rather than words. *Shaman* translates. |
| Threatens the well-spirit | The spirit does not retaliate; the working it runs *fails* for that visit, and the high-tide seal is harsher. |
| Times the king-tide correctly and reaches the drowned vault | Recovers the navigation chart; can leave on the return tide. |
| Mistimes the king-tide | Sealed in the vault for six months. The breath-room holds; the food does not. *Survivor* matters; refreshment potion matters; the party that has neither does not all come out. |
| Brings the chart to a Mage tutor (Rhianna; the Storm Mage's cottage; Luchebert's library) | The chart is identified; the campaign gains a working overseas-east-coast navigation reference. |
| Brings the bone-and-brass tide-counter to a sailor at Linar | The sailor pays generously; the party has a friend at the harbour. |
| Reports the place to Linar fishers | They already know. They will not go. They are grateful the party went. |
| Reports the place to Master Darius at World Edge | He has heard of *a sea-mage's tower somewhere on the coast*; the party's confirmation closes a fragment of his old correspondence. |

## DM notes

- **The tidal mechanic is the dungeon.** The Tidehold is structurally a *time-pressure* dungeon, not a combat one. The fight is with the tide, not with creatures. A DM running it as a monster-arena loses what it is. *The party that learns the salt-line, the cut stair's gaps, and the king-tide window has solved the place.*
- **The well-spirit is the conscience of the dungeon.** It is bound, not hostile. A party that respects it is rewarded with cleaner workings; a party that breaks it loses the rest of the place. The spirit has no name; the sea-mage did not give it one. A party that asks it respectfully whether it has a name learns *no*, and may, over multiple visits, *give* it one.
- **The drowned vault's chart is the principal find.** A *real reward* per `doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Where items come from" — *the only one of its kind on this side of the rift*. A campaign that wants to open the overseas-east-coast naval thread has its in-world tool here. A campaign that does not still benefits from giving a serious sailor or mage a generation of useful reading.
- **Linar fishers know the place.** They will not approach it. They will help a party that asks — directions to the cliff-top trailhead, the king-tide schedule, an old fisher's cousin's account — but they will not go. The Tidehold is *known and avoided*, which is the right shape for a pre-rift sea-mage's tower.
- **The chaos-warped sea-creature in the vault is small.** Do not run it as a boss. It is a fragment, curious, easily startled — the register is *Tod-adjacent*, not *combat encounter*. A party that handles it well leaves with the chart and a working note about what the sea-mage was studying when the rift came.
- **Three approach routes is deliberate.** The cut stair, the boat threshold, the king-tide opening. A party with all three powers (*Climber*, *Boater*, *Swimmer*, *Survivor*) handles the place fully; a party with one or two runs it with effort; a party with none should not be down there.

## Open questions

- **The sea-mage's name.** Not in the surviving notebooks. May be on the principal cover of the Saint's Original at Luchebert (`doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`); may be in Rhianna's correspondence-files; may be lost.
- **What the drowned vault's working-circle was for.** The chart was the find; the *circle* was the work. A serious mage student reading the notebooks across visits may piece it together.
- **The chaos-warped fragment in the vault.** What was the sea-mage studying when the rift hit? DM-fill; the fragment is the trace.
- **Whether a second sea-mage's tower exists.** The notebooks mention *colleagues at the next headland north*. A campaign that wants a sister-dungeon has the hook.
