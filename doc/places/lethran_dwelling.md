# The Lethran Dwelling

- **Type:** Pre-rift family home of one of the **Lethran Pair** (see `doc/claude/PRE_RIFT_PAIR.md` for the arc; `doc/places/generator.md` and `doc/places/anchor.md` for the paired sites). Modest household, partly preserved by a small Pair-working that has been running for a thousand years. Atmospheric, not combat.
- **Region:** **Southern desert**, on a sand-ridge near the **Half-Tower** (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At the Half-Tower"). The Pair owned the holding in pre-rift days; sand has covered most of it; the household-rooms remain.
- **Officially:** Nobody's. The Pair's family line is gone (the child mentioned at the Anchor's entry-corridor is not accounted for); no successor was named. The desert has kept the house for a millennium.
- **In practice:** **A house the desert did not take.** The kitchen is exactly as it was on the morning of the rift; the supper is set on the table; the embroidery is in the chair; the child's toy is on the floor. The party walking in walks into a moment that did not finish.
- **Status:** Stable, preserved. The house-spirit running the working has held; the desert has tried to take the house and not been allowed.

## What the party meets

The party is on the sand-ridge between Half-Tower and the Anchor's location. **The roof of the Lethran dwelling rises from the sand** — half a roof; the rest is buried. A *Lookout* picks it out from the ridge; a *Hunter* reads sand-pattern that suggests *something has held against the drift here*. The sand around the house is shaped *wrong*: it falls naturally elsewhere; near the dwelling it seems to step *back* by a finger's width every winter.

The entry is a stone door at the buried side, found by digging the last metre of sand the wind has not been able to clear. **A small house-spirit holds the door from the inside.** The spirit is not hostile; it is watchful. A party addressing it gently (a *Char* or *Will* test, not a *Shaman* one — the spirit responds to *household manners*, not to working-craft) is admitted.

Past the door, **the inside is dry, lit by a small bound lamp in the kitchen, and exactly as the family left it.** This is the Pair's home. The Pair were here that morning. The Pair did not come back.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The supper on the table.** A loaf, a small pot of stew, three bowls (two for the Pair, one for the child), a clay jug of water. *Frozen in time by the working*; the bread is soft, the stew warm. **Eating is safe; it is also the Pair's hospitality, taken**. A party that does not eat may take what they need; a party that does eat *honours the table* and the house-spirit reads it. Standing earned.
- **The unfinished embroidery in the chair by the kitchen window.** Stretched on a wooden frame; the needle still in the cloth. The pattern is a flowering desert-vine — pre-rift, recognisable from the herbal at Luchebert (`doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md` §"An Old Herbal in a Hand No One Currently Speaks"). Two rows from completion. The party may complete it if they have the patience and the *Hand* skill; the house-spirit reads this as a kindness.
- **The child's toy on the kitchen floor.** A small carved wooden animal — a desert-fox shape, stylised, kindly. *Worn in the way a toy is worn by being loved.* The Pair's child played with it that morning. The party may take it and carry it; the house-spirit does not object. The toy is the dwelling's most affecting find.

## Approach — what the party sees on the way

The Lethran dwelling sits in the canonical southern desert. A party reaching it is in country `doc/places/southern_desert.md` already populates:

- **From the Half-Tower** (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At the Half-Tower"): a half-day's walk along the ridge. The chimera lineage and air elementals at the Half-Tower are *distant neighbours* — the Pair's working held the chimeras back from the dwelling; the chimeras *recognise* the working at distance and do not approach.
- **From Three Walls** (same file, §"At Three Walls"): a full day. The sand-ruin serpent matriarch's territory does not extend here; the *sphinx* at Three Walls knows of the dwelling and may direct a party with right standing toward it.
- **From the Anchor** (`doc/places/anchor.md`): two days through open desert. A party that has read the Anchor's entry-corridor script (which names the Pair's child) may seek the dwelling specifically; the path between is *more dangerous than the dwelling*.
- **Dust-dervishes** drift across the open stretches. The Pair's working slows them as they pass within sight of the dwelling — a small atmospheric detail visible to a *Shaman*.
- **A *Sphinx* may sit at the dwelling's outer ridge** at certain hours (DM choice); the same tradition that watches the Anchor and Three Walls. *Char* and *Will* test for permission to dig the door clear, if a sphinx is present.

## At the kitchen — the supper room

The first room past the door. **The kitchen is the household's centre**; the supper room is here.

- **The table** with the supper laid out as described above. *Three bowls; two adult, one child.*
- **A small bound lamp** on the wall — pre-rift mage-light, still working at low warmth. The Pair's working anchors here.
- **The kitchen door opens to a small enclosed garden** — *the desert outside has not reached the garden either*. A few pre-rift desert herbs grow here, fed by a working that pulls water from somewhere beneath the sand. *Druid* reads this as *the rare working that is still doing its job*; one or two of the herbs are not in any contemporary herbal — the Pair were keeping a small private collection.
- **A shelf above the hearth** holds the household's small library — practical books, a child's primer, a bound volume in one of the Pair's hand. **Inside the bound volume**: *the first letter of the chronological correspondence.* Dated *the morning the Pair separated to begin the laboratory work*; the writer is the household-resident, the recipient is the partner about to leave for the broken lands. The letter is *short, warm, and sets the scale of what they are trying to do.* The DM should hand the players this letter as the dwelling's principal document.
- **A small painted panel above the kitchen door** — pre-rift; the household's *family arms*, painted by the same Vintala-line apprentice whose murals run at the Generator and the Anchor. A vine-and-stone wreath surrounds three figures: the Pair holding the child between them. The *fourth figure* in the corner is unnamed; *Reading* identifies it by the apron-cut and Vintala-line work as **the painter herself, signing the gift**. The household's chosen painter was a friend.
- **A child's slate** lies on the kitchen-table's edge, propped against the bread-board. **Three drawings on it** — pre-rift, kept by the working: a desert-fox; the Pair holding hands; and a stylised *house with the sun coming over the ridge*. The drawings are crude in the way a young child's drawings are crude. *The third drawing — the house — is the dwelling itself, drawn from outside.* A party reading the slate has the Pair's life from inside the child's hand. **Carrying the slate is possible** *only* if the party also carries the child's toy from the kitchen floor; the working's preservation reads them as a set.

## At the working room — the small private workspace

A side-room off the kitchen. The Pair's *household* working-space — small, used for what they did at home rather than at the laboratories.

- **A working bench** under a shuttered window. Sealed inkpot (still wet); a bundle of half-used parchment; a stack of finished ones, each carrying small workings the Pair tested before scaling them up at the laboratories. *Reading* gathers a few small fragments of pre-rift mage-craft — *not* the system's documentation, but the *household-scale* practices the Pair lived by.
- **A small wax-cylinder recorder** on the bench — pre-rift, intact. The cylinder holds *a recording of the child laughing*. The DM may play it once, in the room, when the moment lands.
- **A child's writing-tablet** beside the bench — the child was learning to read, copying out simple words. Pre-rift script. The last word on the tablet is unfinished: *t-r-* and a curve.
- **A wooden case** against the inner wall holds *a small finished working-piece* — a stone-and-Heartwood pendant the Pair made for the child. *Power-enhancing jewel*-class (`doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Power-enhancing jewels") for a power the child was already carrying — DM-fill on which power. **A party-member who carries a child-scale gentleness in their workings** can wear the pendant and gain its boost; nobody else can.

## At the bedrooms — the family's last morning

Two small rooms, sharing a wall.

- **The Pair's bedroom** — bed unmade; a robe across a chair; a pair of working-shoes by the door, set out for the journey to the Generator. *The robe in the chair is the partner who stayed home — they had not yet dressed for the day*.
- **The child's bedroom** — bed made (the child got up first; they always did); a row of small carved animals on a shelf (siblings of the toy on the kitchen floor); a window covered with a curtain the child stitched themselves. The window opens to the garden.

The bedrooms are *the most affecting* rooms in the dwelling. A DM running this slowly should give the players time to read the rooms without finding mechanical objects — *the rooms are the find*.

## Lore the place contributes

The dwelling is the spine's *emotional preface* and the Pair's most personal lore-delivery:

- **The Pair were a family before they were practitioners** — the child's toy, the supper on the table, the embroidery, the family arms-panel. The dwelling proves the Pair lived a *normal pre-rift life* with neighbours, friends, a child learning to read.
- **The child existed and was loved** — the slate's three drawings, the writing-tablet, the recording, the carved animals on the bedroom shelf. This is the lore-revelation that the *third site / sixth blank* thread (Grain-mill) gestures at — the Pair were planning a place for *the child*. Whether the child survived the rift is the campaign's strongest open thread.
- **The Vintala-line apprentice was a friend** — the same painter who painted the Generator's south wall and the Anchor's planning pair *signed her gift* in the dwelling's family-arms panel. A party that has now seen four Vintala-line works (three murals + one panel) has the painter as a *campaign-spanning minor figure* — an apprentice colleague the rift took alongside the Pair.
- **The pre-rift desert ecosystem** — the garden's herbs include species not in any contemporary herbal; the embroidery's flowering desert-vine pattern names plants the rift erased. Cross-confirms Luchebert's Old Herbal and the Anchor's drying-shelf herbal. A *Druid* with cuttings has *the lost desert flora* in their hand.
- **The chimera lineage knew the Pair** — atmospheric lore, but real: the chimeras at the Half-Tower remember the family across centuries. A party carrying the toy or the seal stamp moves past Half-Tower's chimeras safely. The lore-revelation: pre-rift practitioners were on speaking terms with wild creature lineages.
- **The Pair's specific kindness as practice** — the household's small workings (the bound lamp; the preserved supper; the warm working that holds the desert at the threshold) are the Pair's signature register at *household scale*. A party that has read this and the gentle-binding spirits at the Anchor / Tidehold / Grain-mill carries an integrated picture of *who the Pair were as practitioners*.

## What's around the dwelling — the desert canon at this ridge

The Lethran dwelling is part of the desert's *archaeological grief* register — *someone's home, taken*; here, *the home that was not taken*. A party here is in country the desert canon already populates:

- **The Half-Tower** (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At the Half-Tower") sits half a day along the ridge. The Pair's correspondence at the Anchor mentions the Half-Tower's pre-rift owner as a colleague; a party that reads the Half-Tower's surviving inscriptions after this dwelling reads the Pair's signature beside the lord's.
- **The Anchor** (`doc/places/anchor.md`) is two days east. A party that visits the dwelling first carries the *first letter* of the chronological correspondence to the Anchor; the *last letter* there closes the arc.
- **Three Walls' sphinx** (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At Three Walls") may direct the party to the dwelling if they ask after the Pair specifically; the sphinx-tradition has been *watching this ridge as well as the Anchor's*.
- **Mage-dwellings** elsewhere in the desert (DM-fill per the `southern_desert.md` open slot) — the Lethran dwelling is *the worked example* of what a preserved pre-rift household looks like. A DM filling another mage-dwelling's interior may use this file as a template.
- **The chimera lineage at the Half-Tower** does not approach. The Pair's working *reads as kin* to the chimeras at distance; the family was on speaking terms with a wild creature lineage and the lineage has remembered for centuries.
- **The desert's chaos-pressure** does not reach the dwelling's interior. The party, used to crossing the chaos-pressure zone, feels its absence here as a quiet weight lifted.

## Visible patterns

- **The sand falls back from the house every winter.** Not maintained — *resisted*. A traveller seeing the ridge in spring sees the same finger's-width of clear stone the working cleared in autumn.
- **The bound lamp does not flicker.** Steady. Has been steady for a thousand years.
- **The supper does not spoil.** The bread does not stale; the stew does not go cold; the water does not slime in the jug.
- **The garden grows at its own pace.** The herbs within do not follow the desert's seasons; they follow the working's, which is the morning of the rift extended.
- **The child's recording plays once for each respectful party.** A second playing within the same visit fails; the cylinder *resets* between visits.
- **The chimera lineage does not approach.** A party seeing chimeras pass at distance reads the Pair's standing with the wild.

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Addresses the house-spirit at the door politely | Admitted. The visit is gentle. |
| Forces the door | The spirit does not retaliate; the lamp cools; the supper goes cold within an hour; the *recording does not play*. The visit lands as a violation. |
| Eats the supper at the table | Hospitality accepted; standing earned. The supper renews after the party leaves. |
| Reads the first letter and carries it | The chronological correspondence opens. The party knows the Pair as people before they meet them as practitioners. |
| Plays the child's recording | Once per visit, with respect. The room does not need the recording played twice. |
| Takes the child's toy | The house-spirit reads this; if the party is carrying it for *the child's memory* rather than as loot, the spirit accepts. The toy carries no mechanical bonus; it carries weight at the Anchor's heart and at any later Pair-site. |
| Takes the wooden-and-stone pendant | The pendant is given freely *if a party-member can wear it* (per the power-enhancing-jewel mechanic; the wearer must already carry the named power); otherwise it stays. The case opens at the right hand. |
| Tries to take the supper, the embroidery, the child's writing-tablet | The working refuses; the items cannot be removed without breaking the preservation. A party that *insists* breaks the room. |
| Completes the embroidery | A small kindness; the house-spirit's attention rises; standing earned. The completed embroidery cannot be removed but reads, on later visits, as *finished*. |
| Threatens the house-spirit | The spirit has no defence; it does not retaliate. The dwelling stops responding to the party for the rest of the campaign. The lamp cools; the next visitor finds an empty house. |
| Brings the Pair's seal stamp from the Generator | Recognition; the working room's bench *opens* a small concealed compartment holding a single working-piece the Pair were saving — DM-fill on what; appropriate to a household-scale Pair working. |
| Leaves the dwelling intact and walks out | The standing is real. *Felicia* and *Willow* both register the kindness if asked later. |

## DM notes

- **The dwelling is *not a combat scene*.** It is the spine's emotional preface. A DM running it as a treasure-hoard or as a creature encounter loses the arc's heart. The house-spirit can be addressed; nothing has to die.
- **Run it slowly.** Let the players walk the rooms without rushing them to objects. The supper, the embroidery, the toy, the writing-tablet, the recording, the bedrooms — each is a beat. Six or seven beats is enough; the dwelling is small.
- **The first letter is the spine's chronological starting point.** Hand it to the players in the kitchen, beside the table, with the supper between them. Give them time to read it before moving on.
- **The child's toy is the campaign's small carried thing.** A party that takes it will carry it through the Anchor and the Generator; reading the Pair's last letter at the Anchor with the toy in a pack is one of the campaign's quietest moments.
- **The pendant is gift-conditional.** The Pair made it for *their child*; the case opens for someone who can wear it as the child would have. A power-enhancing jewel mechanic means it sits inert otherwise. A party that does not have the right wearer leaves the case where it is.
- **The chimera lineage at the Half-Tower remembers the Pair.** A campaign that wants to develop this can have the chimeras *recognise* a party carrying the Pair's seal stamp or the toy; passage past the Half-Tower becomes safer for that party.
- **The third-site hint** (from the Grain-mill's cutter's-bench letter, `doc/places/grain_mill.md`) does not surface here. The dwelling is *home*, not *the third laboratory*. A campaign that develops the third-site thread does not place it here.
- **The dwelling's preservation is fragile across centuries.** A party that strips the rooms or violates the working accelerates the desert's reach by decades. A returning party finds the place subtly worse if they treated it badly the first time.

## Open questions

- **The Pair's child.** Where were they that morning? At the dwelling, asleep — the bed was made. *Were they at the dwelling when the rift came?* The room does not say. A campaign that wants to extend the spine has the strongest hook here.
- **The garden's herbs.** One or two are not in any contemporary herbal. *Druid* with sustained attention may identify them as plants the rift erased. A party that brings cuttings to Diederik or to Steven's drying-room (`doc/npcs/steven.md`) opens a small restoration thread.
- **The pendant's power-pairing.** Open which power it boosts. A campaign that has a party-member with a quiet child-scale working can match.
- **The recording**'s subject. The child's laughter, in a moment the Pair captured. *Why that day?* Open.
