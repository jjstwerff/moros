# Linar Harbour

- **Type:** Small independent harbour town
- **Region:** East coast, at the **southern end of the coastal mountain range** (which extends north from Linar all the way up to World's End); start of the main inland trade road, which crosses the range's southern terminus to reach Elmsfield and the interior
- **Political status:** Independent — neither Allondo nor Brumal
- **Demographics:** Mixed; **most of the working population (fishers and dock workers) are of overseas origin** — the settled half of the Overseas War's stranded soldiers and their families

## Summary

Linar harbour is a small independent harbour town on the east coast, at the point where the **main inland trade road** (running west/south through Elmsfield and on toward Allondo and the desert) meets the sea. The town is the overland trade's point of import. Goods off ships are transferred to caravans here; goods up from the interior come here to be loaded. That trade is the economic backbone of the town.

It is also **the first place to have fallen in the Overseas War**. The overseas invasion landed at Linar harbour, occupied it, and pushed inland from here. The occupation lasted longer here than anywhere else on this continent — Linar harbour was the last place to be liberated, not the first. When the war ended with Thorgal's death, most of the occupying demi-human soldiers did not go home. They could not; their continent could not feed them. **They stayed. They took up fishing. They learned the docks. They had children. A generation later, the working population of Linar harbour is largely descended from them.**

The town's independence is partly a practical consequence of its demographics. Neither Allondo nor Brumal fully trusts a harbour whose longshoremen are the children of the soldiers who captured it. Linar harbour, for its part, has grown into the ambiguity. It charges fair dock fees, takes no sides, and provides neither kingdom with grounds to annex it. It is the kind of town where an Allondan merchant and a Brumal courier might pass each other in the same tavern without remarking on it.

## Character of the town

- **Mixed demographics** run all the way through. Dock work, fishing boats, net-making, salt, the chandleries that supply the caravans — most of these crafts have overseas roots, and the town's material culture shows it in small ways: rigging styles, fish-preservation methods, the kinds of songs sung at the end of a long shift.
- **Locals** from before the war are a minority of the population now. Some of them resent it. Most have simply got on with their lives; the second and third generations have intermarried across the old war's line, and the distinction is softening even where it is remembered.
- **The old occupation story** is not talked about freely. Everyone knows who is descended from whom. Nobody raises it with strangers.
- **Overseas contact** is not formal (there is no diplomatic channel across the sea), but there *is* private correspondence. Fishers have cousins still overseas. Dock workers have letters that arrived on boats no one talks about. The town is the single most porous point on this continent to overseas news — quietly.

## Campaign hooks

- **Informal overseas channel.** If the party needs to learn something about overseas that no official source can tell them, Linar harbour is where they find someone whose grandmother is still writing to their cousin across the sea. Not reliable; not secret; just *there*.
- **The pirate parallel.** The two halves of the Overseas War's aftermath run side by side: the settlers in Linar harbour kept the peace, the other half became the pirate nest further south. The fishers of Linar harbour have strong feelings about the pirates — some of them are first cousins.
- **The war's remaining tension.** If Hannes (or anyone else) pushes a hard-line "overseas are the enemy" stance during the coming Brumal crisis, Linar harbour is where that stance breaks. The demography won't allow it.
- **Corven/Penelope.** If Corven and Penelope's relationship ever needs a neutral ground to meet in — a place where an overseas royal and an Allondan crown prince can sit down without either court's eyes on them — Linar harbour is plausibly it. The town already contains overseas-born people with complicated loyalties; another one wouldn't stand out.
