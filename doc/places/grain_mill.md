# The Pre-Rift Grain-mill

- **Type:** Pre-rift agricultural-side mill, the broken-lands counterpart to the ironwood mill (`doc/places/ironwood_mill.md`). Cuts and grinds **stone-blanks** for working-pieces in addition to grain. The smoking-gun regional dungeon for the Pre-Rift Pair adventure — the stone-blanks the Anchor's stone is cut from were finished here.
- **Region:** Broken lands, on the **trade road's skirt** at the northern edge — closer to the kingdoms than the press-house ridge or the white city's centre, but still inside the warped country. Visible from the road as a stone block-house with a cracked aqueduct above it.
- **Officially:** Nobody's. The community that worked it gone with the rift. The mill's bound earth-spirit kept the building partly stable; the chaos has not erased it.
- **In practice:** **A working ruin** — the millstones still turn at the bound earth-spirit's slow tempo; the cutter's working-room is cleaner than the warping should have left it. **Grain-shadow rats** graze the surrounding fields; *Druid* reads the field as warped.
- **Status:** Stable as ruin. The earth-spirit is patient; the cutter's working sleeps; the chaos has not been able to reach the lower stone.

## What the party meets

The party comes off the trade road at the broken-lands' northern skirt and walks down a worn path toward a square stone block-house. **A cracked aqueduct** runs above it, dry, the water-channel snapped a millennium back. The millstones inside still turn — slowly, regularly, at the earth-spirit's bound pace. **Nobody is feeding the stones grain** any more; what they grind is the residue of centuries of dust and the small fragments the spirit can pull from the ground beneath them.

A party that has walked the broken lands' middle stretches reaches the mill from the south; a party that has come from Elmsfield or Salmonswell along the trade road reaches it from the north (the road skirts the broken lands' edge at this stretch — `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At the trade road's skirt"). Either approach passes **field-stretches that read as wrong**.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The cracked aqueduct above the mill.** Stone arches stepping down from the ridge to the building. *Dry* — the water that drove the original wheel has been gone for a thousand years; the earth-spirit drives the stones now, grinding on without water. A party reading the structure understands what was cut from it.
- **The stones that turn without grain.** Through the open door, two great millstones rotate at the spirit's tempo. *Slow, deliberate, audible from twenty paces off.* The rhythm is the mill's pulse.
- **The grain-shadow rats grazing the field.** Hare-sized creatures that read as *too still* and *too aware* (per Field-flock Jackalope register, `doc/claude/CREATURES.md`; mill-specific variant — they graze the dust the millstones spill rather than what should be growing). They scatter from a person's approach; they vanish when struck. *Druid* reads the field as warped; the rats are part of what the chaos did to the cultivation here.

## Approach — what the party sees on the way

The Grain-mill sits in country canon already populates:

- **The trade road itself** (`doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At the trade road's skirt") — a party arriving from Elmsfield/Salmonswell passes scavengers and ruin-crawlers; some carry warnings about *the mill that grinds without grain*. Accurate, partial.
- **Field-stretches between the road and the mill** — old grain country, now warped. Stalks come up with husks that are not seed; the grain-shadow rats graze it patiently; a party crossing reads the *betrayed cultivation* register.
- **A small abandoned farmstead** on the path to the mill — three buildings, half-collapsed. *Door-kept* (`doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Door-kept") at the farmhouse threshold; *Hearth-dweller* in the main hearth; **a *Mill-shade* in the working-trough behind the barn** — wandering off-station from a different mill upstream, drawn to the broken Grain-mill's pulse but unable to reach it. Encroachments at small scale — the chaos has been *taking the periphery* even as it builds toward the centre. A party can pass without engaging if respectful; a *Shaman* who addresses the Mill-shade and names *the Grain-mill's working-master* can guide it home, settling one piece of the encroachment-belt for the rest of the campaign.
- **The press-house ridge** (`doc/places/press_house.md`) sits a day-and-a-half south. A party that has been there reads the Pair's seal at this mill more readily.
- **Wandering creatures**: *warped helper-spirits* at small wells along the path; possibly a *Stitchling* scavenging at the edge of the warped fields.

## At the field — the grain-shadow rats and the stone-blanks

Optional approach. A party that walks the field before entering the mill reads it.

- **The grain-shadow rats.** Field-flock-Jackalope-register; mill-variant. They graze the dust-spill from the mill, not grain. Vanish when struck; reappear at the field's edges by morning. *Druid* reads the wrongness; the rats are the field's *signal*, not a fight.
- **A scattering of pre-rift stone-blanks** at the field's edge, near a small stone-walled yard. *Pale stone, oblong, dressed but unfinished*; sized exactly to fit the **socket at the Generator's heart** (`doc/places/generator.md` §"At the heart") and to match the **Anchor stone's** dimensions in the desert (`doc/places/anchor.md` §"At the heart — the Anchor stone"). **The Pair's working-mark on each blank's underside.** A party that has been to either paired site recognises the connection immediately. *This is the smoking gun — the stone-blanks the Anchor stone was cut from were finished here.*
- **A small mage-cutter knife** in a stone box beside the blanks — a fine-edged cutting implement, pre-rift, sized for stone-blank work. Sister-piece to the **ironwood-cutting set** (`doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Tools" Masterwork). The cutter knife works on stone of this specific blank-type; useless for everything else.

## At the millstones — the bound earth-spirit

The mill's main floor. The two great millstones turn at the earth-spirit's slow pace; the dust they grind drifts down through gaps in the floorboards toward the lower works.

- **The earth-spirit can be addressed.** A *Shaman* working at the millstones finds it patient, grounded, *content with the work it has* — unlike the wine-spirit at the press-house, this one is not warped. The Pair's binding was *gentle*; the spirit has been doing what it agreed to for a thousand years and has not been wronged.
- **The earth-spirit knows about the stone-blanks.** Asked respectfully, it surfaces a memory: *the Pair came in person to commission the blanks; the work was done over weeks; the Pair carried the finished blanks south themselves.* The party hears this as a tactile sense more than as words. *Shaman* translates.
- **A side-room** off the milling floor holds the **old grain-ledger** — a bound book with community names, harvest weights, and (in a margin in different ink) **a list of the Pair's commissions** with dates. The ledger names *six blanks* commissioned across two months; only one became the Anchor stone, four were rejected for grain irregularity, and **one is unaccounted for** — neither at the Anchor nor at the Generator's heart. **Open question for the campaign**: where did the sixth blank go?

**Items here:** the old grain-ledger (community names; spine entries); the mage-cutter knife (working tool, fine-edged, stone-blank-specific); a small stone-blank chip a previous worker dropped (a *Heartwood-and-stone* test-piece — `doc/claude/ITEMS.md` cross-ref to Heartwood + a stone-blank Material variant the file invites a DM to define).

## At the lower works — the cutter's working-room

A stair descends from the milling floor to the lower works. **Cleaner than the warping should have left it** — the earth-spirit's bound stability protects the cutter's working-stone.

- **The cutter's working-room** holds the pre-rift stone-cutter — a working-driven blade-and-bench arrangement that shaped the blanks. *Now sleeping*; the earth-spirit could wake it but does not without a reason. *Wright* and *Hand* reading the cutter understand it as the Pair's pre-rift industrial scale piece — sister-tool to the ironwood-cutting set, scaled up.
- **A faded mural runs the cutter-room's back wall** — pre-rift. Two cutter-masters at work side by side: *the agricultural-side cutter on the left* (this room), *the ironwood-side cutter on the right* (the mill John Bean lost his arm in — `doc/places/ironwood_mill.md`). The mural depicts them as *paired traditions* — one working stone for stone-blanks, one working ironwood for the mage-arm-and-tool industry. Master Darius's grandfather's grandfather is signed in the right-hand figure's apron-hem. *This is the only surviving image showing the two cutter-traditions as the *same family of practice*.* A party that brings a sketch of this mural to Master Darius shifts a centuries-long question for him: *the agricultural cutter was not a sister; it was a sibling.*
- **The mill's lintel above the milling-floor door** carries a deeply-carved community record — three generations of the working-master's family, dates of the mill's major repairs, the names of the bound earth-spirit's *first three keepers*. Pre-rift script. The earth-spirit is content with parties who pause to read it; *Reading* recovers names that appear nowhere else and dates that anchor the broken-lands' agricultural century before the rift.
- **A wooden box on the cutter's bench** holds **two unused cutting-stones** the Pair specified for finishing the blanks. *Special Material* — the box is dry and the stones are intact; they would still cut clean. A party that recovers them carries materials no living craftsman has made for centuries.
- **A second box** beside it holds a **sealed letter** from one of the Pair to the mill's working-master, dated *eight weeks before the rift*. The letter thanks the working-master for the careful stone work and asks for the *sixth blank* to be held back for *a third site we have not yet named to anyone, including each other in writing*. **The third site is the Pair's third-laboratory hint** — open thread, unconfirmed, suggestive. The campaign can leave it open or develop it.

## Lore the place contributes

The Grain-mill is the spine's *materials and traditions* delivery — the lore-revelation that ties pre-rift mage-craft to *current craftsmen* the campaign already knows:

- **The Pair commissioned six blanks; only one became the Anchor stone** — the ledger and the cutter's-bench letter open the *third site / sixth blank* thread. The clearest extant evidence that the Pair were planning beyond the two laboratories.
- **Sister-tradition cutters across the rift** — the back-wall mural is the only surviving image showing the agricultural-side and ironwood-side cutters as *one family of practice*. Master Darius's lineage signed on the right-hand figure's apron. **This shifts a centuries-long question for Darius**: he had thought the two were sister traditions; the mural confirms they were *siblings*.
- **The bound earth-spirit's lineage** — the lintel-carving names the spirit's first three keepers, anchoring a continuity the kingdoms have lost. A *Shaman* student carrying these names back to Wilder or Gerhald earns a real conversation about the gentle-binding tradition's depth.
- **A pre-rift agricultural century by date** — the lintel's repair dates anchor a window of broken-lands history that other sites only gesture at. A party building a chronological picture of the rift gets *seventy years before* the rift here.
- **The encroachment-belt as *downstream* of pre-rift work** — the Mill-shade at the abandoned farmstead behind the mill comes from a *different* mill upstream, drawn to this one's pulse. A party that settles it learns the encroachments are *ghosts of working communities*, not invasive species. The lore-revelation: the encroachments register tells the story of the broken lands' agricultural century the chaos has been *pulling apart* for a thousand years.

## What's around the dungeon — the broken-lands canon at this skirt

The Grain-mill sits at the broken-lands' northern edge, where the trade road runs and the kingdoms still half-touch. The country around it is canonical:

- **The trade road** (`doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At the trade road's skirt") — scavengers, ruin-crawlers, occasionally a Skull Gatherer group passing through. Information is cheap on the road; the mill is locally known.
- **The encroachment-belt** along the road's southern skirt — small farmsteads and abandoned waystations holding *Hearth-dwellers, Door-kepts, Mill-shades*, and *Wrong-flocks* (`doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Encroachments"). The Grain-mill is *bigger and older* than these encroachments — pre-rift, not post-rift; the encroachments are downstream of the mill's broader region.
- **The Press-house ridge** sits a day-and-a-half south (`doc/places/press_house.md`). The Pair's seal connects the two sites; a party that has been to one will recognise the other.
- **The white city's outer ruins** sit two days south-east; the Generator a day past that. The migration line passes *around* the Grain-mill (the chaos's wrong-build cannot reach the lower works) but the field above is part of the warped country.
- **Haven** would receive any small migration-piece the party recovers from the field's edge.
- **Master Darius** at World Edge (`doc/npcs/master_darius.md`) recognises the cutter's design from his own forge tradition; a party that brings him the mage-cutter knife or one of the cutting-stones earns a real conversation about *what the pre-rift mage-foundries shared with the iron-foundries*. He has not seen the Grain-mill himself; he would know what he was reading.

## Visible patterns

- **The stones turn at the same slow tempo, day and night.** A *Hearing* character reads the mill's pulse from a distance.
- **The dust drifts down through the floor.** The lower works are protected by the dust-cushion the upper works have laid for centuries.
- **The grain-shadow rats appear at the field edges by morning.** Even after a hard scattering.
- **The aqueduct does not crack further.** Same crack for a thousand years; the earth-spirit holds the stone against weather.
- **The encroachment-belt holds the road's southern skirt.** A party crossing the field-stretches sees small encroachments at the edges; the mill is *the bigger sister* of those encroachments and the encroachments know it.

## DM notes

- **The Grain-mill is the spine's middle delivery.** Press-house gave the *first concrete* (the Pair existed); Grain-mill gives the *materials* (the stone-blanks were finished here). Generator delivers the *wrong-build's destination* and Anchor delivers the *Pair's resolution*. Run the Grain-mill before the white-city run for the cleanest spine pacing.
- **The earth-spirit is the *good* register.** Press-house's wine-spirit is warped; Silvertrace's stream-spirit is warped; Grain-mill's earth-spirit is *content*. The Pair's binding was kind and the spirit knows it. A party that has only met warped spirits at this point in the broken lands meets a *settled bound spirit* here for the first time. The contrast is the point.
- **The third-site hint is a real campaign thread.** The cutter's-bench letter mentions a *third site* not named anywhere else. A DM may leave it as a *quiet open thread* for the campaign to never resolve, or develop it (the Pair's child is named at the Anchor's entry-corridor — perhaps the third site is *the child's place* — open).
- **The mage-cutter knife is a working tool, not a weapon.** Sister-tradition to ironwood-cutting; the knife works on stone of the specific blank-type only. Master Darius would commission new ones if shown the design, but only for someone the Pair would have approved of.
- **The cutting-stones are an embedded reward.** Two pre-rift cutting stones in a sealed box is a real find — the kind of *real reward* the items canon prescribes. A party that has Master Darius as a contact can have one recut for their own use; a party without can carry them for an arc.
- **The grain-shadow rats are atmosphere, not encounter.** Like the Field-flock Jackalope's mill-variant — a *signal* the field is warped, not a fight. A DM running them as a fight loses the register.

## Open questions

- **The sixth blank.** Named in the ledger and the letter; unaccounted for at the Anchor and the Generator. Where did it go? The Pair were planning a third site; the blank may be there. Whoever — or whatever — has the blank is also a thread.
- **The third site itself.** Hinted, unconfirmed, named only in private correspondence the Pair did not write down explicitly. A campaign that wants to extend has the hook.
- **The cutter's working-master.** The letter is addressed to a named person (DM-fill); their fate is not in any document. A descendant may be alive; a regional ruin-crawler family at Linar harbour may know.
