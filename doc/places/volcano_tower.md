# The Volcano Tower

- **Type:** One of the six towers ringing the Lake of Tears. A shackle on the bound air elemental. Houses a large bound fire elemental.
- **Region:** Around the Lake of Tears, one of the five seized towers moved into the ring by the old-order mages. Stands on the ground; rises in a stone cone with an open top.
- **Officially:** Nobody's. The original mage is long gone.
- **In practice:** **Inhabited by a fire elemental who has been angry about it for centuries.** The tower is the elemental's cage; the shackle is the elemental's body.
- **Status:** Active. Heat visible from half a valley away. The elemental throws fire at anyone climbing the rim.

## What the party meets

The party approaches from the lakeshore or from the hills beyond. **They see the tower before they see the lake** — a stone cone rising maybe thirty feet, shaped exactly like a small volcano, with **flames visible through the open top and heat-shimmer rising constantly**. The stone at the rim is scorched black for a hand's breadth down; below that the tower is pale grey, an old mage-era construction.

There is no door. **The only entrance is the rim.** A party circling the base finds seamless stonework — the tower was built to hold what was inside from the inside.

As they approach the rim climb, a first wave of heat hits them. The elemental knows they are there.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The cone shape, and the heat.** From a distance, the tower looks like a small volcano with weather. From the base, it looks like a furnace with a chimney. The stone is scorched at the top, unblemished below — **the heat is contained upward, not outward**. A party can stand at the base unharmed. A party at the rim is being fired at.
- **Flames in the open top.** Look up and they are visible — not a diffuse glow, but a *being*, shifting shape, watching the rim from inside. A party spending a minute at the base can see the fire *turn* toward a movement outside the rim.
- **No door, no windows, no way in that is not the rim.** The tower was built to contain, not to house. A party looking for a normal entrance finds sealed stone. The rim is the engagement.

## At the base — the approach

The stone at the tower's foot is cold. Not cool — *cold*, more than ambient. The elemental's heat rises; the foundation pulls the opposite way.

- **A ring of ash around the tower.** Fine white ash, maybe a pace wide, circling the base. The remnants of fires the elemental has thrown over the rim over centuries — much of it burned itself to nothing.
- **Offering-stones, old.** A few flat stones set at intervals around the base, clearly placed by an earlier visitor (or many). Charred remnants on them — wood, rope, pitch. Someone, long ago, tried the relational approach. The stones are mostly cold; the offerings are mostly gone.
- **The stone is inscribed faintly.** Ward-work at the base that controls what the elemental can do at range. A *Mage* or *Shamanic* character will recognise it as containment-work and read the direction of the wards — they point up and in, not out.
- **Climbing the outside is possible.** Handholds are natural; the stone is rough; the tower's construction supports an ascent. A party can climb to within a few feet of the rim without engaging the elemental.

## At the rim — where the elemental throws fire

The rim is a wide circular lip, stone, scorched black. Standing on it is standing at the elemental's front door.

- **Heat as wall.** A party reaching the rim immediately feels the heat as a force pushing outward. Staying is costly.
- **Fire thrown at approach.** The elemental throws heat and flame at anything at the rim — not continuous, but in response to a climber reaching the lip. A prepared party with *Magic* (Water), *Druid* (working), or good cover can absorb one or two throws; a party staying long absorbs more.
- **Seeing the elemental clearly.** From the rim, the elemental is fully visible — a being of shifting flame at the cone's centre, shapes resolving and dissolving as it watches. It is *large*. A party expecting a manageable fire-sprite finds a creature the size of a small house, compressed into the cone.
- **The rim is the negotiation space.** A party wanting to engage the elemental does so from the rim — dropping things in, calling down, making offerings that reach the elemental itself rather than the base-stones.

## At the inside — what the elemental sees

A party that enters the tower (by being lowered, by a climbing working, or through the relational lever as trust develops) stands inside a cone of stone with a being of flame at its heart.

- **The interior walls are scorched smooth.** Centuries of fire have polished them. The stone here remembers nothing of the mage-era's workings — the elemental has burned the interior inscriptions away. What remains is stone shaped like a furnace.
- **The elemental does not speak in words.** It communicates through *heat patterns* — warmer when pleased, narrower when curious, sharper when angry. A party with *Shamanic* or *Druid* training can read this; a casual party will feel only the extremes.
- **It has a sense of time.** Centuries have not broken it. It is old as elementals reckon age, and it has held a specific grudge against the old-order mages that moved it here — a grudge the party can inherit as alliance if they approach it right.
- **The air is thin.** The elemental consumes oxygen; prolonged interior stay is physically taxing. A party lingering without protection will feel it.

## The two levers

The Volcano Tower's shackle breaks two ways. The DM should know which the party is pursuing and pace accordingly.

### Befriend the elemental — the relational path

Bring things that burn. Feed the being. Treat it as a being.

- **Offerings at the rim.** Wood, oil, pitch, dry grass, whatever is combustible. Drop them into the cone. The elemental consumes them; the heat briefly brightens; the throws stop for the duration.
- **Approach-at-pace.** Return repeatedly. Each visit brings a new offering. The elemental's reading of the party warms across visits.
- **Beings, not chutes.** A party that treats the elemental as a being — addressing it, acknowledging it, asking its name — earns faster trust than a party that dumps fuel and leaves. The elemental *notices* the difference.
- **Once fed and willing, the tower floats.** The elemental's energy, released through its cooperation, lifts the whole cone slightly off the ground. From that point the tower can **follow the party out of the ring**. The shackle breaks the moment the tower crosses the ring's boundary.
- **A new companion.** Post-lift, the elemental is a being the party has a relationship with. The tower-and-elemental can accompany the campaign as a mobile asset — a moving fire-source, a being the party can consult on fire-related workings, a creature that remembers centuries of mage-era history.

### Enrage the elemental — the violent path

Push the elemental until it breaks out. The containment is strong but not infinite.

- **Provocation.** A major working directed at it, sustained hostility, physical attempts at its prison, damage to the ward-stones at the base. Any of these push the elemental toward the breaking point.
- **The tower ruptures.** When the elemental bursts the containment, it **lashes out at everything nearby** — the party, the lakeshore, the surrounding terrain. This is not a manageable combat; it is a natural disaster in miniature.
- **The elemental leaves.** Having vented, it travels east **to the mountain range**. Whatever settlements, creatures, or ecologies lie in its path are not safe.
- **The tower is ruined.** The shackle is broken — but the tower is a pile of scorched stone. Nothing to lift, nothing to follow, no new companion.
- **The cost is permanent.** The elemental's departure removes its presence from the Lake region's spirit-shield cover. Whatever shielding contribution it made (see below) is gone.

## Role as spirit-shield (partial)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a large bound fire elemental is a mighty-spirit-reader in the region's cover. The Lake of Tears is shielded by a stack — Felicia, Laurent, Gerhald, the pot-people, the tower elementals — and the volcano contributes its piece.

- **Relational path keeps the contribution partly available.** The elemental alive and willing and travelling with the party is still a mighty presence; the region loses the fixed point but not the being.
- **Violent path removes the contribution entirely.** An elemental in the mountain range, enraged and lashing, is no longer a stable shield anywhere.

## Visible patterns

- **Heat-shimmer from the top constantly.** The tower is always working, whether or not the party is near.
- **Cold stone at the base.** The foundation pulls the opposite direction from the rim.
- **No traffic.** The lakeshore path curves well around the tower. Locals do not come here.
- **Offering-stones at the base.** Someone has tried the relational path before; the stones' charring is old.
- **The elemental watches the rim-line.** A party standing nearby will see the flames *orient* toward them without crossing the rim.
- **Storm weather goes around.** Rain and wind track around the tower's immediate vicinity. The heat evaporates the first and deflects the second.

## What you learn here, and when

| Topic | When the place gives it up |
|---|---|
| That the tower contains a fire elemental | Immediately — visible at the top. |
| That the only entrance is the rim | Within minutes of circling the base. |
| That the tower is a shackle on the air elemental | From Laurent (`doc/npcs/laurent.md`), from Gerhald (`doc/npcs/hermit_shaman.md`), or from the pot-people. Not from the tower itself. |
| That the elemental can be fed | From seeing the old offering-stones, or from trying. |
| That the elemental has a memory and a grudge | From interior time — reading its heat-patterns, or from a shamanic approach. |
| That there are two levers — relational and violent | From Laurent's narration; also self-evident to a party that tries one and sees what is possible. |
| That the violent path costs the region its shield-contribution | From Gerhald, from a druid, from explicit lore questions. The tower itself does not tell the party. |
| The elemental's name, if it has one | Inner-relationship-gated. An open question the campaign may answer. |

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Approaches the base without climbing | Safe. The elemental throws nothing at the base. |
| Inscribes or disturbs the ward-stones | The containment weakens. The elemental stirs. The tower becomes more dangerous immediately. |
| Climbs to the rim | Immediate heat. First approach: one fire-throw as warning. Staying: more. |
| Drops wood, oil, or pitch into the cone | The elemental consumes it. Heat brightens briefly. Throws pause. The elemental registers the giver. |
| Addresses the elemental by title (not name; *"being of flame,"* *"fire"*) | Heat-pattern responds. A being being addressed as a being. Foundations for trust. |
| Asks the elemental's name | Not given immediately. Earned across visits. When given, it is a key — the elemental can be called by a name it has chosen. |
| Returns with offerings across multiple visits | Trust builds. The elemental's throws stop for the party. Eventually it will cooperate. |
| Performs a *Magic* (Fire) working nearby | The elemental *feels kin*. Interested. A fire-working Mage has a potential advantage here. |
| Performs a *Magic* (Water) working nearby | The elemental reads it as threat. Heat spikes. The party should not sustain this near the tower. |
| Commits to the relational lever and brings enough fuel | **The tower lifts.** The elemental's cooperation floats the cone. From that point it can follow. The shackle breaks at the ring's boundary. A new companion joins the campaign. |
| Provokes the elemental sustained | The tower ruptures. **Violent path engaged.** Immediate danger to everyone present; the elemental lashes out; eventually it leaves eastward. |
| Attempts to *kill* the elemental outright | Not possible in any reasonable combat scope. A bound elemental of this size is beyond the party's scale unless the campaign has armed them for it specifically. |
| Asks it about the old-order mages | Answered, in heat-patterns. The elemental remembers the taking. A party that can read it gets a first-hand account of the ring's construction. |
| Asks it about Laurent | Answered, with a specific warmth the elemental rarely shows. The elemental was present when Laurent's city was lifted by its sibling air elemental. The memory is clear. |
| Brings the Storm Mage nearby | The elemental is uneasy. Two bound elementals in sight of each other is old politics. |

## DM notes

- **The two levers are the whole design.** Every scene here is implicitly asking which the party will commit to. The DM can let the party discover this gradually — Laurent's narration, Gerhald's warning, the base stones' evidence of prior attempts — or surface it explicitly at a key moment.
- **Relational is *intended*; violent is *possible*.** The campaign's geometry works best with a party that chooses the relational lever and gains a companion elemental. The violent path is a real option the DM should honour — the party may commit to it for story reasons, or stumble into it — but the cost is permanent and campaign-shaping.
- **Heat-pattern communication as craft.** A *Shamanic* character reading the elemental's heat-patterns is a scene the DM can lean into. Make it work; let the player feel they have *heard* the elemental, not just decoded it.
- **The name is a key.** When and if the elemental gives its name to a party, the campaign unlocks a companion relationship. The DM should pace this carefully — the name comes *after* trust, not as a reward for a single visit.
- **The violent-path disaster has scale.** Enraged elemental leaving for the mountain range is a regional event — settlements in its path, shepherds on the flanks, ecosystems in the foothills. The DM should narrate this as consequences the party will hear about across future sessions.
- **Gerhald weighs this on the last shackle.** A party that has broken the volcano via the violent path arrives at Gerhald with that history. He will weigh it. See `doc/npcs/hermit_shaman.md` §"He wants specifics before he cooperates."
- **The tower as base after relational success.** A floated, cooperative volcano tower with a companion fire elemental is a real asset for the last-flight arc. The DM can use it as a moving warm-point, a platform for specific workings, or a place the party can retreat to if the campaign goes hard.
- Lore cross-references: the six-shackle mechanism in `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"The last flight"; Laurent's narration of his downfall in `doc/npcs/laurent.md`; Gerhald's consent-gate on the last shackle in `doc/npcs/hermit_shaman.md`; spirit-shield ecology in `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology"; the pot-people who were tower-kin in `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`; the other shackle-towers in `doc/places/sinking_tower.md`, `doc/places/darkness_tower.md`, the Lightning Tower context in `doc/npcs/storm_mage.md`, and the buried tower in `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`.

## Open questions

- **Does the elemental have a name?** If so, it has not shared it with anyone the party has spoken to.
- **What did the elemental look like before it was bound?** Memory of its pre-binding shape may be reachable through sustained conversation.
- **Can the elemental's energy, while still contained, be channelled for something other than lifting the tower?** A *Mage* with standing might ask.
- **What does its migration eastward look like, under the violent-path?** Specific settlements in the mountain range, specific creatures. DM-fill based on how much the campaign wants to pay for that path.
- **What happens to a cooperative elemental *after* the last flight?** Does it stay with the party, return to the mountain range on its own terms, or find its own arrangement?
