# The Darkness Tower

- **Role:** One of the six towers ringing the Lake of Tears; cage-shackle of the bound air elemental; houses a suppressed light elemental and a mosaic puzzle
- **Location:** Around the Lake of Tears — one of the five seized towers moved into the ring by the old-order mages. Stands on the ground (unlike the floating Lightning Tower or the lakebound Sinking Tower)
- **Status:** Active; inhabited by a trapped light elemental whose radiance is being suppressed

## Summary

Stands on the ground, unassuming from the outside. The entrance is **pitch black** — not shadowed, not unlit, but actively dark in a way that ordinary light does not dispel. Inside, the tower is hard to see through: rooms are there, stairs are there, but sight fails within a pace or two of a torch. Worse, **all life seems to be drained from whoever enters** — Endu damage accumulating each scene, vitality quietly leaking out of the party the longer they stay.

The tower has **two floors** connected by several stairs leading up and down between them. The rooms on each floor are laid out in an arrangement the party has to feel their way through rather than see. In one of those rooms, a **light elemental** is trapped beneath a large **black basket**. The basket is what is suppressing both the light *and* the life — it bottles the elemental's natural radiance and, as a side effect of that suppression, drains the area around it.

## The two-step lever

The Darkness Tower's shackle does not break by moving the tower via relationship or violence. It breaks by **solving the tower's own puzzle**, and the light elemental is the first step of that puzzle, not the final answer.

### Step 1 — free the light elemental

Find the basket; lift it. The elemental's natural radiance returns. **The darkness effect ends**; the life-drain ends with it. The rooms become visible, and the party can actually *see* the tower they have been stumbling through.

### Step 2 — read the mosaic

With the darkness lifted, the walls become legible. They are lined throughout with **mosaic stones — some lighter, some darker** — in patterns that were invisible under the darkness. This mosaic is the tower's mechanic: decoded correctly, it shows the party **how to walk the tower out of the ring**. The specific form of the mechanic is a DM-fill design — a sequence of stones to press, a pattern to trace, rooms to walk through in a particular order, or some combination — but the principle is that the mosaic *tells the party what to do*, and the tower obeys.

Both steps are needed. Freeing the light elemental alone does not move the tower; the mosaic cannot be read without freeing the elemental first.

## Role as spirit-shield (partial)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a light elemental, even suppressed, is still a mighty-spirit-reader presence — the suppression does not fully remove it from the region's cover. Its release (Step 1 of the lever) probably strengthens the local cover further in the short term, and its eventual departure with the tower removes it. The Darkness Tower is therefore, paradoxically, a contributor to the region's shield even in its current distressed state.

## Relationships

- Original mage is unknown/long-gone; the tower was seized in the taking of the ring.
- The **trapped light elemental** is the tower's captive and the first step of its lever.
- The **black basket** is the suppression working; its provenance (who made it, whether it is itself an enchanted object or a mundane one used cleverly) is a DM-fill detail.

## Open questions

- What exactly does the mosaic instruct? The specific mechanic is open.
- Does the freed light elemental stay with the tower as it moves, or leave independently?
