# The Darkness Tower

- **Type:** One of the six towers ringing the Lake of Tears. A shackle on the bound air elemental. Houses a suppressed light elemental and a mosaic puzzle.
- **Region:** Around the Lake of Tears, one of the five seized towers moved into the ring by the old-order mages. Stands on the ground.
- **Officially:** Nobody's. The original mage is long gone.
- **In practice:** **A suppressed light elemental trapped in a room inside, and a mosaic on every wall that cannot be read until the light is freed.** The tower is a two-step puzzle in which the puzzle pieces are beings, not mechanisms.
- **Status:** Active. The tower's darkness reaches past the entrance and drains the life of anyone inside.

## What the party meets

The party reaches the tower on foot from the lakeshore. It is **unassuming from the outside** — a plain round tower of grey stone, two storeys, no windows, a single door at the base. Nothing scorched, nothing shimmering, nothing floating. A party expecting a dramatic outside reads it and wonders if they have the right tower.

The **entrance is pitch black**. Not shadowed, not unlit — *actively dark*. A torch held at the doorway illuminates the last foot or two of open air before the door, then stops. The darkness beyond the threshold does not dispel. Inside, from this angle, nothing is visible.

A party stepping through the door enters the dark, and the darkness closes behind them. And then they feel the draw — **life leaking out**, Endu accumulating down, the quiet sensation of being *drained*.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **An ordinary tower with an extraordinary door.** The exterior is plain; the door is a hole in the world. A party will stand at the threshold and understand they are looking at something that should not be. **Light does not cross the line.**
- **The life-drain on entry.** The moment the party steps inside, they feel the drain. It is not rapid; it is *constant*. Endu accumulating loss every scene, vitality quietly bleeding into whatever the darkness serves. A party that lingers without addressing the cause will be exhausted before they find what they came for.
- **Nothing visible inside.** Torch, *Magic* (Light), a hand held in front of a face — none of it works. Sight fails within a pace or two of any source. A party must **feel their way** through the tower. The layout is there; they just cannot see it.

## At the doorway — the threshold

The door is stone, heavy, propped open. The opening faces outward into sunlight; inside it is black.

- **Light crossing the threshold stops.** A torch beyond the line is a torch that goes out. A mage's *Light* working does not illuminate more than a hand's-width past the door.
- **Sound carries strangely.** A voice in the doorway sounds muffled to the person speaking; a voice deeper inside sounds closer than the body that makes it.
- **The draw starts at the threshold.** A party stepping in feels it immediately. Stepping back out feels like surfacing.
- **A decision point.** The DM should let the party hover here, test the line, understand what they are about to enter. Some parties will turn back; most will commit.

## At the ground floor — feeling the layout

Inside, blind. The party must navigate by touch, memory, and communication.

- **Rooms are real.** Stone walls, wooden doors, floor-shapes consistent with mage-era tower construction. Everything is *where it would be* in a normal tower; the party just cannot see it.
- **Several stairs.** Multiple staircases connect the two floors — not the usual single stair. The design is deliberate; the tower's layout is part of the puzzle. A party mapping by touch will find the redundancy confusing at first, logical on a second pass.
- **Life-drain accumulates.** Endu damage each scene. A party that stays long is paying the cost.
- **Workings stutter inside.** A mage casting finds the working slower, weaker, half-starved. The suppression bleeds magic as well as vitality.
- **The walls feel *patterned*.** A careful hand moving across a wall feels small stones of different textures — smooth and rough, alternating in patterns the hand cannot follow far enough to map. This is the mosaic. It is unreadable now.

## At the upper floor — more rooms, more stairs

A partial mirror of the ground floor, reached by any of the stairs.

- **Same blind navigation.** Same life-drain. Same feel of patterns under the hands.
- **A different number of rooms.** The upper floor has fewer rooms than the lower — the central space is larger. A party mapping carefully will notice the asymmetry.
- **The mosaic continues.** Up the walls, across the ceiling where the hand can reach it. The same pattern logic, extended.

## At the basket — the elemental's prison

In one of the rooms on one of the floors — not signposted, not obvious; the party must find it — sits a **large black basket**, knee-high, stone-heavy. Nothing about it suggests what it holds; a party stumbling across it in the dark may not immediately realise it is significant. A *Shamanic* or *Druid* character will feel the spirit beneath the stone as they approach.

- **The basket suppresses both the light and the life.** The elemental's natural radiance is bottled inside; as a side-effect of that suppression, the room — and by extension the whole tower — is drained of vitality.
- **Lifting the basket is possible.** It is heavy but not fixed to the floor. A strength check with both hands; a leveraging tool; two or three party members together. The weight is physical, not magical.
- **What holds the basket in place is the mage-work at its rim.** A party that examines it with *Shamanic* or *Mage* craft sees the binding. Cutting it, disrupting it, or simply lifting the whole basket releases the elemental.

## The two-step lever

The Darkness Tower's shackle does not break by relationship or violence. It breaks by **solving the tower's own puzzle**, and the light elemental is the first step, not the final answer.

### Step 1 — free the light elemental

- **Lift the basket.**
- **The elemental emerges.** A being of steady pale light, small — the size of a large lantern-flame, not a human. It *unfurls*. The room brightens. Within seconds, the whole tower brightens. Within a minute, the darkness is gone and the life-drain with it.
- **The elemental does not leave immediately.** It is grateful. It lingers in the tower for a time; a party present at the release can address it and be heard. It may follow them out of the tower for a while; it may choose to stay near the rooms it has inhabited for centuries.

### Step 2 — read the mosaic

- **With light restored, the walls become legible.** The mosaic the party felt under their hands is now visible — stones of lighter and darker shades, laid in patterns across every wall and much of the ceiling. The design was made to be read in *light*; in darkness it was invisible by design.
- **The mosaic is the mechanic.** Decoded correctly, it tells the party **how to walk the tower out of the ring**. The specific form is a DM-fill design — a sequence of stones to press, a pattern to trace on a particular wall, rooms to walk through in a particular order, or some combination. The principle: *the mosaic says what to do, and the tower obeys.*
- **The mosaic takes time to read.** A party that reads it in an hour has rushed. A party that spends a day with it gets the full pattern. A party with *Ingenuity*, *Druid* (for natural patterns), or *Mage* (for ward-structure) reads faster.
- **Acting on the mosaic breaks the shackle.** Follow the instruction and the tower *moves* — walks, in some sense, or rotates, or simply shifts off its foundations. The shackle breaks when the tower crosses out of the ring.

Both steps are required. Freeing the elemental alone does not move the tower; the mosaic cannot be read without freeing the elemental first.

## Hidden in the darkness tower

The original mage hid pieces here before the seizing (see `doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Where items come from"). The cache is **shadow-aspected**, in style with the tower:

- **A folded shadowsilk wrap** — small, dense, weighs nothing; cancels the wearer's reflected light for a scene; one of the pieces the seizing mages walked past.
- **A veilthread cord** — Material: misdirection-aspected; concealable in a sleeve or a hem; a Boost to Stealth in lit rooms.
- **A blackened glass scrying piece** — broken; reads only on its broken plane; once per scene shows the asker a glimpse of a place they have never been if the question is honest.

Resolving Step 1 gently and reading the mosaic before disturbing the room uncovers the cache. Forcing Step 2 first scatters it.

## Role as spirit-shield (partial)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a light elemental, even suppressed, is a mighty-spirit-reader presence. Its suppression does not fully remove it from the region's cover. Its release (Step 1) probably **strengthens the local cover** further in the short term, and its eventual departure with the tower removes it.

The Darkness Tower is therefore, paradoxically, **a contributor to the region's shield even in its current distressed state**. A party that frees the elemental without yet breaking Step 2's shackle has a mobile-ish bright spirit in the area for as long as the elemental chooses to stay.

## Visible patterns

- **Exterior unremarkable.** No shimmer, no heat, no glow. Just a plain tower with a dark door.
- **Darkness holds the threshold.** Constant; permanent; not weather-dependent.
- **Life-drain in the interior.** Everyone inside is losing vitality slowly, measurably.
- **Sound inside carries closer than distance should allow.** Whispers reach further than they ought to.
- **Stairs more numerous than the construction logic requires.** The tower is built for the mosaic-walk, not for ordinary circulation.
- **The mosaic is tactile under the hand.** The patterning is there to feel even when it cannot be seen.
- **Once lit, the tower reads as old mage-work.** Clean construction, spirit-binding at the basket's rim, ward-patterns in the floor stones.

## What you learn here, and when

| Topic | When the place gives it up |
|---|---|
| That the entrance darkness is not ordinary | At the threshold. |
| That life is draining inside | Within a scene of entry. |
| That the tower's rooms are navigable by touch | First exploration attempt. |
| That the walls carry a tactile pattern | A careful hand-sweep of any wall. |
| That something — a basket — contains the cause | On finding it; or with *Shamanic*/*Druid* sensing. |
| That a light elemental is inside the basket | On lifting it; or with spirit-sensing. |
| That the mosaic is the shackle's lever | Only after the elemental is freed and the walls are visible. |
| How to decode the mosaic | Work. A careful party solves it in a day; an inspired party faster. *Ingenuity*, *Druid*, *Mage* speed the work. |
| Why the tower was set up this way | Not from the place; from Laurent's narration or from a surviving record. The old-order mages designed the shackle to be solved, not forced. |

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Stands at the threshold and tests the line | Light stops. A hand pushed through feels cold. Voices dampen. They learn without entering. |
| Steps inside | The darkness closes behind them. Life-drain begins. Sight fails. Navigation is by touch. |
| Lights torches, casts *Light*, holds lanterns | No effect past the threshold. Inside, nothing works. |
| Navigates carefully by touch and memory | Maps the ground floor in a scene; the upper floor in another. Finds rooms by patience. |
| Feels the walls | Discovers the tactile pattern. Unreadable in the dark. |
| Finds the basket | *Shamanic* or *Druid* sensing locates it faster. A party without sensing stumbles onto it eventually. |
| Examines the basket with *Mage* or *Shamanic* craft | Reads the binding. Understands that lifting the basket will release what is under it — without damage. |
| Lifts the basket | **Step 1 complete.** The elemental emerges. Light floods the tower. Darkness lifts. Life-drain ends. |
| Talks to the light elemental on release | It listens. It has been suppressed for centuries and is grateful to be freed. It may linger in the tower for the party's next visit or choose to depart with the tower when Step 2 activates. |
| Reads the mosaic quickly and acts | **Step 2 complete if the reading is correct.** The tower moves. The shackle breaks when the tower crosses out of the ring. |
| Reads the mosaic incorrectly | The tower does not respond. Try again. The mosaic tolerates multiple readings — it does not punish errors. |
| Tries to break the mosaic (chisel, hammer) | **Damages the lever.** The tower becomes less responsive. A party that destroys significant mosaic cannot complete Step 2 and has to find another way out of the shackle — probably impossible without rebuilding the mosaic, which they cannot. |
| Tries to move the basket instead of lifting it | Difficult (heavy) and pointless. Moving does not break the suppression; lifting does. |
| Tries to destroy the basket | Possible, and *breaks* Step 1 cleanly (the elemental emerges anyway). The basket as an object is then gone; the room is brighter than it would have been with just the lift. No disadvantage for the tower's Step 2. |
| Tries to leave before lifting the basket | Possible. Step back out the threshold and the life-drain ends. The tower is unchanged. |
| Stays inside without lifting the basket, studying the tactile mosaic | They learn the tower's layout and the mosaic's extent. They cannot decode it in the dark. A long stay costs real vitality. |
| Brings the freed light elemental out into the lake region | The elemental brightens the hills around the lake for as long as it chooses to stay near. A party that has freed it and treated it as a being has a new minor ally. |

## DM notes

- **The puzzle is the point.** A party that treats the Darkness Tower as a combat scene or a cursed ruin misses the whole mechanic. The DM should make the tactile pattern *felt* early, even before the party understands what it is. The moment the light returns and the walls become visible is the payoff scene.
- **The two steps are both necessary and both rewarded.** Freeing the elemental is a good act in itself — a being trapped and suppressed, released. The mosaic is the shackle-break. The design rewards both as distinct accomplishments.
- **The mosaic's specific mechanic is DM-fill.** A sequence of stones pressed; a pattern walked through the rooms; a path the party's feet trace on particular tiles. Pick what fits the campaign's puzzle-taste. What matters is that *the mosaic tells the tower what to do*, and the tower obeys once the right instruction is given.
- **Do not rush the dark phase.** A party fumbling by touch is not wasting time — they are learning the tower's geometry. A DM who skips this lightly loses the contrast when light returns.
- **Life-drain as pressure, not punishment.** The Endu damage should make the party feel the weight of the place without pushing them to a TPK. If they run out of time, they can leave, recover, and return. The tower does not lock them in.
- **The light elemental as a minor ally post-release.** A small pale being, steady, grateful. Not a major companion like the volcano's fire elemental could be; a quieter presence that can illuminate dark places elsewhere in the campaign if invited. A DM who wants to use it can let the elemental follow the party for a session; a DM who doesn't can have it stay near the tower or ascend into the sky.
- **The "contributor to the shield" note matters.** Step 1 strengthens the local cover briefly; Step 2 removes the tower and potentially the elemental. A party weighing the last-flight cost should hear from Gerhald or a druid that the Darkness Tower's shield-contribution is particular — a suppressed light elemental is still a presence, and freeing it briefly *helps* the region before Step 2 takes it away.
- **No hostile inhabitants.** The darkness itself is the antagonist. Once dealt with, the tower is gentle. The elemental is not hostile; the mosaic is not a trap; the old mage is long gone. The party is mostly in dialogue with a structure.

## Open questions

- **Why was the light elemental trapped here** — by the old-order mages at the ring's construction, by the tower's original mage before that, or by something earlier still? The file does not commit.
- **What exactly does the mosaic instruct?** Specific mechanic is open; DM-fills at the table.
- **Does the freed light elemental stay with the tower as it moves, or leave independently?** Either is defensible.
- **Is there a way to leave the basket suppressing an elemental as it sits, but remove the shackle some other way?** Probably not — the design makes the two steps dependent — but a party with enough standing at an adjacent tower or with enough mage-craft might argue for a third path.
- **Can the mosaic be *learned* — as a working — by a mage who studies it?** The question matters if the campaign opens toward transferable pre-Rift technique.
