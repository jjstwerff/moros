# The Anchor

- **Type:** Pre-rift mage laboratory in pre-desert agricultural country, **buried under sand and intact**. The *anchor* end of the **Lethran Pair**'s paired chaos-management system (see `doc/claude/PRE_RIFT_PAIR.md` for the arc; `doc/places/generator.md` for the broken-lands counterpart). Sphinx-protected.
- **Region:** **Southern desert**, on a sand-ridge in the country between Three Walls and Half-Tower. Reachable only by sphinx-mediation or by reading the broken-lands documentation that names the location.
- **Officially:** Nobody's. Lost from human knowledge for a thousand years. The sphinxes who watch the ridge consider themselves the only standing custodians.
- **In practice:** **Quiet, dry, preserved.** The desert's spiritual register does not carry the warping that drives the broken-lands wrong-build; the chaos has not reached here. The Pair finished this end and *hid* it before the rift; the burial was their own work. The room has been waiting for someone with the address.
- **Status:** Stable; preserved by a small water-spirit working the Pair left bound at the room's centre.

## What the party meets

The party is on the sand-ridge their broken-lands documentation named, walking toward an entrance the documentation said *would not be obvious*. It is not. **The sphinx finds them.**

A *(named or unnamed; DM-fill)* sphinx of the same tradition that watches Three Walls comes out from a sandstone outcrop's shadow and stands across the party's path. **It has been waiting.** The party that has handled Three Walls properly (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At Three Walls") earns the right address; the party that has not faces a closed door. The sphinx's questions are rooted in ancient world lore: *what was the work the rift interrupted, and what should the kingdoms have learned from the interruption?*; *the Pair who built this place tried to spare the world something — what was the something, and why would the sparing have been worth their lives?* (See `doc/claude/LORE.md` for the deep lore; honest *"I do not know"* is always a true answer; pretending to know closes the door.)

Past the sphinx, a sandstone arch hides the entry-corridor's mouth. Inside: cool air, dry stone, the Pair's working-mark over the threshold. The desert has been keeping the place.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The dryness.** No salt, no rot, no sand inside. The Pair's preservation working *holds the desert out at the threshold*. A party stepping in feels the temperature shift and the air go still.
- **The Lethran seal carved into the inner archway.** The same circle-and-two-lines as at the Generator and on the Press-house letter (see `doc/places/press_house.md`) — *but here it is intact*. A party that has carried the seal stamp from the Generator (`doc/places/generator.md` §"At the heart — Level 3") matches it across.
- **The quiet.** A Generator-veteran feels the absence of the fitting-sound the way the ear feels a song stopping. The Anchor is *what the broken-lands wrong-build was supposed to be reaching*. It has been at peace.

## Approach — what the party sees on the way

The Anchor sits in canonical desert country. The journey to it crosses ground the desert canon already populates:

- **From Lastwater** (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At Lastwater"): the keeper points the party toward the sphinx-watched ridge. Two days' walk through the chaos-pressure zone; *Survivor* and *Smell* matter for water and contamination. The old desert nomad at Lastwater (same file) knows the sphinx's outcrop from earlier crossings and can describe its shape without naming what is inside.
- **From a wandering shaman the party may meet earlier in the campaign** (DM-fill — possibly one of **Wilder**'s loose-network correspondents, or a desert-roaming shaman who passes through Linar harbour twice a year). The shaman has stood at the sphinx's ridge once and *was not admitted* — they did not pass the questions. They carry the rumor at the casual level; they will name the ridge to a respectful party and warn that *the questions are about the world before the rift, not the asker's life*.
- **From Rhianna's predecessor's papers** (`doc/npcs/rhianna_linthrope.md` §"At her residence" — locked drawer below the pigeon-holes). A folded letter from a Lethran descendant at Fata morgana names the ridge directly. **A serious party that earns access to the drawer learns the Anchor by name without entering the broken-lands Generator first.**
- **From Penelope at Fata morgana**, with trust (`doc/npcs/princess_penelope.md` §"If the party does something"). Her old tutor was the Pair's last correspondent; the inner-circle remembers; a Penelope-introduced visit to the right inner-circle home opens a third route into the Anchor.
- **From Three Walls**: the matriarch's clutch sits on the route the party will likely take. A party that has handled Three Walls well crosses *with permission*; a party that has not faces the matriarch's territorial sweep along the way. The sphinxes at Three Walls and the Anchor talk *across the desert in ways not well-understood* (per the desert canon); a token from Three Walls' sphinx is the cleanest entry to the Anchor's.
- **Past Half-Tower**: optional. The Pair owned a holding near the Half-Tower's ridge in pre-rift days; the party that visits **the Lethran dwelling** (`doc/places/lethran_dwelling.md`) en route reads the family's life before the laboratory delivers them as the work.
- **Dust-dervishes** drift across the open stretches between the named sites (full entry at `doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Dust-dervish"). A party with a small offering passes safely; a party without may be *carried* by one. **One specific dervish has been observed, by Three Walls' sphinx, drifting around the Anchor's outcrop at certain hours** — it carries fragments of old Lethran dust the working has shed across centuries; a party that asks it correctly through *Shaman* or *Wind* power may receive a piece of *that dust* as a small Special-Material-adjacent token (a Pair-signature dust pinch; useful at any preservation-working they later attempt).
- **Mage-dwellings** dot the desert (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At a mage-dwelling"). At least one — perhaps the Lethran dwelling — sits between the party's likely approach and the Anchor.

## At the entry corridor — the threshold

A short cut-stone corridor descending into the ridge. The Pair's preservation working runs at the threshold itself, marked by an inlaid line of dry pale stone that the party crosses. **Everything past the line is preserved as the Pair left it.**

- **A small carved bench in a niche** beside the corridor — for the sphinx to sit, by canonical agreement. The bench is empty when the party passes; warm when the sphinx has just been there.
- **A water-jar in another niche**, clay, holding clear cold water. The water has not gone bad in a millennium. The Pair's small kindness to whoever might come back. *Drinking is safe;* the jar refills overnight if respectfully replaced.
- **Pre-rift script along the wall** in the working-tongue. *Reading* picks up names — the Pair's family names; their working-marks; *and a third name* that does not appear elsewhere in the spine, **a child of the Pair**, named here because the Pair worked in this corridor with the child present once.

## At the working chamber — the receiver

The Anchor's main room. Matched in scale and orientation to the Generator's working chamber, but *clean*. The chaos has not been here; the desert does not carry that warping; the Pair's preservation working has held everything still.

- **A working bench against the south wall.** Tools laid out where the Pair left them. A pair of fine-edged scribing tools (one for each of the Pair); a sealed inkpot whose ink is still wet; a stack of clean parchment. **A small framed painting** sits at the bench's edge — a *family portrait*: the Pair, the child between them, a desert-fox curled at their feet. The painter is the same Vintala-line apprentice whose mural runs the Generator's south wall (`doc/places/generator.md`); cross-checking the signatures confirms the same hand. *This is the only known image of the Pair as people.*
- **The chamber's west wall carries a finished-system painting** — companion piece to the Generator's mural, but here the system is shown *running clean*: the chaos-pressure represented as small dark birds being routed gently through the thread-lines back to where they belong. The Pair's working-marks identify the painter (Vintala-line, again) and the *intended ending* of the work. **A *Reading* check against the Generator's mural reveals the two paintings are *the same scene, before and after*** — the Generator shows the system at full power; the Anchor shows the chaos *managed*. The Pair commissioned both as a planning aid; the second was their dream of the work succeeding.
- **The Pair's *receiver* notebooks** stack on the bench's shelf — fully readable, the dry desert and the working preserving them perfectly. A serious mage student earns *a year's reading* from these alone; the receiver-side of the system is the more documented half.
- **The Pair's correspondence** — a wooden box on a smaller bench, holding the letters between the Pair across the months the system was being built. **The first letter is at the Lethran dwelling**; **the middle letters are at the Generator's working chamber** (in a wax cylinder the Memory Shade can hand over); **the last letter is in this box**. The DM should hand the last letter to the party only at the room's heart, not at the door.
- **A small water-spirit** runs the room's preservation working, surfacing in a stone basin at the chamber's centre. The spirit is bound — not free — but the binding is *gentle* (the Pair's signature register; cf. the well-spirit at the Tidehold, `doc/places/tidehold.md`). A *Shaman* address in the right register confirms the spirit is content; speaking with it gathers a few words about the Pair as people.

**Items here:** receiver notebooks (year's reading); fine-edged scribing tools (Tools Material variant); a sealed inkpot (still working — a small Pair-made oddity that may be useful as a Special-Material adjacent crafting component); the correspondence (the heart of the adventure; chronological closure); a small drying-shelf herbal naming pre-rift desert plants (cross-ref `doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md` §"An Old Herbal in a Hand No One Currently Speaks").

## At the heart — the Anchor stone

A short corridor from the working chamber descends to the heart room. The room is small: a working circle on the floor, a stone fitting at the centre, a single stone bench.

- **The Anchor stone** sits in its fitting at the working circle's heart. Carved with the Pair's working-mark; smaller than the broken-lands socket suggested but exactly matching its dimensions. **Special Material** classification: cousin to **Hearthstone** (`doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Hearthstone") with pre-rift signature; a DM may treat it as a Hearthstone variant or as a one-off Pair-stone. *The Anchor stone is the campaign-defining find.*
- **The last letter of the correspondence** rests on the stone bench, weighted by a small carved stone the Pair shared. The letter is dated *the morning of the rift*. It is short. It is the adventure's emotional resolution.
- **A second sealed jar** beside the bench holds two **refreshment potions in pre-rift working-bottles** — the Pair's small provisioning for whoever would come back. Find-form per `doc/claude/ITEMS.md`.
- **The room's preservation does not extend to large objects leaving.** The Anchor stone *can* be lifted; the working maintains the room around the stone's absence for hours but not for days. A party that takes the stone and leaves the room sealed behind them gives the working time to settle. A party that strips the room throws the working off, and the desert will reach in within a season.

## Lore the place contributes

The Anchor is the spine's *resolution* on the lore side; what the Generator delivered as *the chaos's wrong-attempt*, the Anchor delivers as *the Pair's intended completion*:

- **The Pair as people, not just practitioners** — the family portrait, the chronological correspondence's last letter, the bench's domestic intimacy (still-wet ink, pair of scribing tools, clean parchment). The Pair *finished here* as a household, not just as a working partnership.
- **The system's intended success** — the west-wall painting is the *only* surviving image of the Lethran tradition working as the Pair imagined it. A party that has seen both murals (Generator + Anchor) carries the planning-pair forward.
- **Pre-rift desert society** — the Pair lived in canonical desert country with neighbours (Half-Tower's lord; the colleague at Fata morgana). The Pair's correspondence and the receiver-notebooks describe a *normal pre-rift social life* in country that is now sand.
- **A pre-rift desert herbal** — the Pair's drying-shelf herbal names plants no longer growing, cross-confirming the Old Herbal at Luchebert (`doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`). A serious party reading both *fills in pieces of the lost desert ecosystem* the Pair were planning to protect.
- **The Pair's child** — named at the entry-corridor; pictured in the family portrait; mentioned in the cylinder letter at the Generator. **Three independent references confirm the child was real;** the campaign open thread of *what happened to the child* is the most personal lore-question the spine raises.
- **What the rift took** — the last letter is the campaign's *cleanest reading* of what the world lost. Not the white city's fall as Laurent describes it; not the war as Thorgal's residue offers it; the Pair's letter is *the small loss at the household scale* — a child's birthday missed, a working unfinished, a partner not yet home. That register lives only here.
- **The gentle-binding tradition's signature** — the Anchor's water-spirit, the Tidehold's well-spirit, and the Grain-mill's earth-spirit are *the same hand*. A party that has met all three reads the Pair's *kindness as practice* across three sites.

## What's around the dungeon — the desert canon at this register

The Anchor is part of the desert's *archaeological grief* register, not its hostile-creature register. A party at the Anchor sees, on approach and on departure, the canonical desert texture:

- **The sphinx-tradition** (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At Three Walls"; the sphinxes communicate across the desert) is the place's living watch. A party that respects sphinxes throughout the campaign earns the Anchor's; a party that does not, does not.
- **Sand-ruin serpent matriarchs** at the desert's mid-distance ruins (Three Walls and others). The Anchor is *outside* their territory — the sphinx's presence keeps them off — but the matriarchs are part of the same desert.
- **Dust-dervishes** on the open stretches. Movement-creatures, not hostile, can carry messages or items if asked properly.
- **The Lethran dwelling** (`doc/places/lethran_dwelling.md`) is two days' walk away. A party that visits before the Anchor reads the family's last morning before the laboratory delivers the Pair as practitioners.
- **Fata morgana** sits further south (`doc/places/fata_morgana.md`). The Pair's correspondence mentions Fata morgana once — a colleague there the Pair was writing to; the colleague died before the rift and was not replaced. A party with Penelope's standing at Fata morgana can ask after the Pair there.
- **The Skull Gatherers' necropolis** sits to the west of the blasted lands at the desert's edge (`doc/npcs/skull_gatherers.md`). The order knows the Anchor exists in the way the order knows most pre-rift sites exist; they have not approached. *Andras* would receive an Anchor stone offered in a carry path with the order's full attention.

## Visible patterns

- **The sphinx is always nearby.** Not always visible. A party that returns to the Anchor a second time finds the sphinx's awareness has grown.
- **The water in the entry-corridor jar refills overnight.** Always. A party that empties it sees no fill if they do not respect the bench-and-jar arrangement.
- **The bench's ink does not dry.** A pre-rift kindness. *Reading* from notebooks at the bench is faster than copying out elsewhere.
- **The room is cool in summer; less cold than expected in winter.** Desert temperature does not reach inside. The preservation working's character.
- **The desert outside continues.** Sand drifts at the entry-corridor's outer mouth; the sphinx watches from the same outcrop each day. The Anchor is held; the country around it is not.

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Addresses the sphinx properly with Three Walls' standing | Granted the way in. The sphinx may walk with them to the threshold. |
| Addresses the sphinx without standing | The sphinx asks the question; the party's answer earns or denies entry. *Char* and *Will* matter. |
| Cuts the sphinx down | The Anchor is not entered. The sphinx-tradition closes against the party across the desert; Three Walls' sphinx leaves and does not return. The campaign has lost something it cannot recover. |
| Drinks from the entry-corridor jar | Safe. The party that carries water away and returns with full skins finds the jar still respects them. |
| Sits on the corridor bench | Polite. The sphinx may join. |
| Reads the receiver notebooks at the bench | A year's reading; mage-craft fragments useful for restoration work. *Reading* and *Char* matter. |
| Engages the water-spirit through *Shaman* | The spirit is content; words about the Pair as people; standing earned. |
| Threatens the water-spirit | The preservation working fails for the visit; the room cools; the ink dries; the party leaves with what they can carry but the place loses its character for the rest of the campaign. |
| Reads the last letter at the heart | The adventure's emotional resolution lands. The Pair finished this end. The Pair did not finish in time to use it. |
| Lifts the Anchor stone | The working settles around the absence; the party has the stone. The room remains preserved for some hours. |
| Strips the room | Working fails; desert reaches in within a season. The Anchor is *gone* by the next season's check.|
| Brings the Anchor stone to the Generator's heart (completion) | The system completes. See `doc/claude/PRE_RIFT_PAIR.md` §"Three honest endings". |
| Brings the Anchor stone to a third party (carry) | Wilder, Gerhald, Luchebert, or the Skull Gatherers via Andras receive. The decision waits. |
| Uses the Anchor stone as catalyst for destruction | Both ends can be unmade with the stone's help. The chaos in the broken lands stops *seeking* a shape. |

## DM notes

- **The register is *quiet, not victory*.** The Anchor is the adventure's emotional resolution; the heart is a small room; the find is a stone and a letter. A DM who plays this room as a treasure-hoard or as a boss-arena loses what the Pair were doing. *They were sparing the world something. They did not finish in time.*
- **The water-spirit is the conscience.** The Pair's care for the room's preservation tells the party who they were as practitioners. A party that respects it carries that respect into the choice at the end.
- **The sphinx is the gate AND the keeper.** A party with an existing relationship with sphinxes (Three Walls handled well) walks past it. A party without, earns the entry by handling the sphinx now. *Char* and *Will* test through honest answers about the visit.
- **The chronological correspondence is structured to land in three reads:** the first at the Lethran dwelling (the Pair as people, not yet at the work); the middle at the Generator's bench through the Memory Shade (the Pair at the work, alarmed by the rate of the chaos's approach); the last at the Anchor's heart (the Pair on the morning of the rift, knowing the Anchor end is finished but the Generator end will go without backup). The DM running this in order gets the players to the last letter as a real beat.
- **The Anchor stone's classification is open.** Hearthstone-adjacent works mechanically; a one-off Pair-stone variant works narratively. A DM who wants the stone to behave only-in-this-system should treat it as the Pair's signature; a DM who wants it usable for other workings should treat it as Hearthstone and add a *Pair's signature* boost to its existing Effect/Weapon/Armor/Accessory lines.
- **Do not pre-load the choice.** Complete, destroy, carry — all three are honest. The Anchor's room favours none. The Pair's last letter does not say what the survivors should do; it says what the writer was hoping for and accepting that the hoping might not be enough.

## Open questions

- **The Pair's child.** Named in the entry-corridor script; not in the notebooks. Did the child survive the rift? Was there a third Lethran site the child was at? A campaign that wants to extend can open this thread.
- **The colleague at Fata morgana.** The Pair's correspondence names them once; nothing else. A party with Penelope's standing can search; the trail may have something or nothing.
- **The Pair's *seal-keeper* tradition.** Their seal stamp at the Generator's heart has been recognised at every Pair site so far. Where else is it recognised? Old-order mage households elsewhere? Possibly.
