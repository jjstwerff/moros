# Laurent's Downed City

- **Type:** Ruined flying city (mage-era)
- **Region:** In the hills near the Lake of Tears, east of the lake
- **Political status:** Abandoned; no living claim; its dead lord still resident as a demi-lich
- **Inhabitants:** **Laurent** (demi-lich; see `doc/npcs/laurent.md`); dormant wooden alcove puppets throughout the structure

## Summary

The wreck of **Laurent's flying city** — the refuge-castle that grew into a city over the years it carried survivors after the Rift, until it was brought down by the old-order mages' tower-ring at the Lake of Tears (see `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` and `doc/npcs/laurent.md` for the history). It fell in the hills east of the lake and has been sitting there ever since.

It is an enormous ruin by any scale a homeland traveller knows. Mage-era construction — quarters and halls and colonnaded streets — folded together at the angles the fall put them at, with galleries and towers tumbled into each other and the whole mass come to rest on the hillsides in a shape no architect intended. Parts are intact enough to walk through. Parts are open to the sky. None of it is safe in the way a lived-in town is safe.

**Local hill-folk avoid the wreck on principle**, and their avoidance has folk-memory behind it: over the years, valley people who went into the hills treasure-hunting have disappeared, and the pattern has been stable long enough to become a story parents tell. The specific mechanism is the alcove puppets and Laurent's fire (below); the valley does not know that in those terms, only that the hills around the wreck take people and do not give them back. See `doc/npcs/corne_dunham.md` §"Clear of the fallen city."

Laurent himself is still here, bound to the wreck as a demi-lich. So is his standing grievance against the faction that brought him down. And so is a quieter resource he has been holding in reserve for centuries: his **alcove puppets**.

## The alcove puppets

Set into many of the city's walls are alcoves, and set into those alcoves are **wooden puppets** — humanoid constructs, kin in construct-type to Haven's humanoid population and to the buried tower's pot-people (see `doc/places/haven.md` and `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`). They are **physically complete but not activated**: bodies ready, spirit-slots never filled. This was the step that was pending when the city fell. The puppets have stood in their alcoves since, waiting for an awakening that never came by the usual route.

### Laurent animates them remotely

When Laurent detects people moving through his city, the nearest alcove puppets **gradually come to life with his will behind them**. He does not awaken them the way the original workings would have (binding a spirit into each one); he *puppets* them remotely, demi-lich-to-construct, using each body as an extension of his own awareness and reach. The waking is not instant — it takes time for him to bring each one online — but once moving, they pursue whatever Laurent has decided about the intruders.

### They were not made for combat

The alcove puppets were intended for labour and detail work, the same purposes as Haven's humanoid population. They are awkward fighters, telegraphed, slow to adjust. A competent party will not be surprised by their attacks.

### They are almost impossible to damage

Their construction is too robust for ordinary weapons. Blades glance off. Blunt strikes jar joints without breaking them. Fire chars the wood surface without penetrating. The construct design absorbs damage in ways the party cannot out-hit in any reasonable time. **The only reliable way to stop one is to throw it off the city** — over a broken edge, into a fallen gallery's drop, off a tower's open side, wherever the terrain offers a down. A puppet in free fall and then at rest far below is a puppet Laurent can no longer usefully animate from here.

This makes the wreck's combat axis **verticality, not damage**. The fallen city is full of broken edges; the party's question in every room is not *how do we kill this* but *where is the nearest drop, and can we make the puppet go over it*. DMs preparing encounters here should design the terrain with that question in mind.

### The gradual awakening

Because Laurent has to bring each puppet online in sequence, an initial intrusion meets one or two. The longer the party stays, the more appear. A party that moves quickly and purposefully through the city may outrun the awakening wave. A party that lingers will meet a steadily rising count, eventually more than any edge can clear.

## Laurent himself — fire and the necklace

Beyond the alcove puppets, **Laurent can act on the party directly**. His demi-lich workings let him throw fire at intruders when they do things that displease him — and the most reliable trigger is **looting**. Handling the city's contents with respect (looking, learning, leaving alone) keeps the fire away; pocketing objects invites it. The fire is not full-scale incineration — he still uses it with the restraint of the man he was, not the rage of an undead — but it hurts, it carries, and it announces his disapproval without ambiguity. A party that takes the hint and puts things back will typically see the fire stop.

### Felicia's necklace — calming Laurent

The best way to **calm** Laurent once he is roused is to bring him **Felicia's old intricate necklace** — a piece of jewellery that was hers in life and still carries enough of her working that her presence can condense through it. When the necklace is brought within Laurent's sensing range, **Felicia senses him through it** and *appears* — a localised condensation of the great spirit at the necklace, in a form close enough to Felicia-as-she-was that Laurent will recognise her. This is the specific trigger of the "condensation" mechanic from `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Speaking with a great spirit directly," invoked here by an object-and-proximity condition rather than a formal ritual.

For Laurent, her appearance is the one thing his demi-lich condition does not have an answer to. The grievance he has been nursing for centuries cannot survive her actually being in the room; the drive of hers that still runs everything she does — fear for the people she tried to save — is the drive that made them allies in life and has not changed. In her presence the fire stops, the alcove puppets still, and he listens.

**Where the necklace is:** on **Felicia's shrine at Luchebert Monastery**, tended by **Willow** (see `doc/npcs/willow.md`). Willow has kept it there for decades and does not advertise its presence; she will not hand it over to anyone who has not earned standing with her. The party's access to the necklace is therefore a **test of earned trust**, not a find-and-take. Two paths are open: the **earned path** — sustained, careful engagement with Willow until she decides the party can be trusted to use the necklace for Felicia's purpose and return it — and the **theft path**, which is real but expensive: Willow is a capable witch, and she will curse any thief with a **draining curse** that slows the victim until they cannot move (see `doc/npcs/willow.md` §"Willow as witch — when the shrine is crossed"). Finding the necklace is not the problem. Earning the right to carry it is.

## Felicia's room — the observational hint

Somewhere in the city is **Felicia's preserved room**. She worked alongside Laurent in the years the flying city carried refugees, and the room was hers. Laurent has kept it — swept, intact, her things still in it — in the same way the pot-boy keeps the buried tower's mage's quarters: a tended space whose occupant is gone but whose tender cannot yet accept they are gone.

It is a **hint for players who observe well**. Not signposted, not brought up by Laurent. A party that sweeps through the city on the way to a confrontation will miss it. A party that looks carefully at what Laurent has preserved can infer: *Felicia mattered to him; if she mattered this much, reaching her might reach him; the place she can still be reached is Luchebert, the shrine, Willow.* That inference chain is the **observational path** to the necklace mechanism. Willow's trust (see `doc/npcs/willow.md` §"Closest to Felicia") is the **relational path** to the same answer. Laurent's own narration of his downfall (see `doc/npcs/laurent.md` §"What he will tell") mentions Felicia's evacuation work as a third, overlapping pointer.

**Contents of the room are DM-fill**; the texture should support inference without delivering the answer bluntly. Suggestions: her working-table with intricate pattern-work matching the "intricate" of her necklace; personal effects that read as lived-in rather than ceremonial; a window overlooking what was once sky; maybe a sketch or note in her hand. No plaque reading "the necklace calms Laurent." The point is the *inference*, not the gift.

## Campaign hooks

- **Finding Laurent.** He is somewhere inside, and reaching him is a session in itself. The alcove puppets are the obstacle rather than the goal.
- **Understanding Laurent.** He was a man who chose to help everyone. What he is now, and what he remembers of that choice, is the negotiation the party is eventually here for. Reaching him is also the **primary in-world moment at which the six-shackle mechanism becomes legible**: he narrates his downfall freely (though agitatedly) to a party that gets to him. See `doc/npcs/laurent.md` §"What he will tell."
- **The last flight.** If the air elemental at the Lake of Tears is freed (see `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"The last flight"), this city rises for one final journey. Preparing the city for flight — clearing what needs clearing, provisioning what needs provisioning, reaching an arrangement with Laurent about where it goes and who rides — is that arc's concrete work.
- **The alcove puppets as resource.** If the party reaches an accord with Laurent, the alcove puppets stop being hazards and become *available*. Dozens or hundreds of remotely-animated bodies under a willing demi-lich's control is a serious piece on the board for any larger plan — including the Shamans' long plan to close the portal, which would benefit from durable workers at the blasted lands' centre.
- **Kin to Haven.** The puppets' construct-type is the same as Haven's humanoid population. A party that has earned standing at Haven has a potential bridge here; Haven's inhabitants would recognise the alcove puppets as kin-they-never-met, and would have views on whether Laurent's remote-puppeting of bodies never spirit-bound is itself acceptable.

## Open questions

- How many alcove puppets does the city hold in total? Hundreds is plausible; the exact count is DM-fill.
- Does Laurent's remote-puppeting degrade with distance within the city, or can he run any of them from any point?
- Can a puppet thrown off the city be recovered and reactivated, or does the fall destroy something central?
- Is there a way to awaken one *properly* — with its own bound spirit — rather than using it as Laurent's extension? Would Laurent allow it?
