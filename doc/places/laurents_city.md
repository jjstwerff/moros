# Laurent's Downed City

- **Type:** The wreck of a mage-era flying city, fallen into the hills.
- **Region:** The hills east of the Lake of Tears, between the lake and Luchebert Monastery.
- **Officially:** Unclaimed ruin. The valley folk call it *the fallen city* and do not go in.
- **In practice:** Tended at the top, abandoned at the bottom. Laurent is still resident as a demi-lich on the upper deck; the wall-alcoves of the upper deck still hold his wooden puppets; the lower decks have gone to green.
- **Status:** Dangerous and under-mapped. Hill-folk lose people here on a pattern stable enough to be a story parents tell.

## What the party meets

The party crosses the hills from Corné's route — or by their own way; Corné will not lead anyone into the wreck (see `doc/npcs/corne_dunham.md` §"Clear of the fallen city"). The last rise is bare. The wreck appears at once, below them, impossible to mistake for anything else: **a city fallen on a hillside, slightly off-level where it came to rest.**

They walk in around the edge. **Every intact door, window, and gate they find is sealed or fused.** The city warded itself during the fall, and the warding held on the intact faces. The only way in is the one the fall itself opened — **a ragged hole punched through the side of the castle low down, where it first struck the hills.**

Through the hole, they enter the **lower decks**. There is no other route up.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The tilt.** The whole city rests slightly off-level where it came down. Floors pull the foot half a step off true; standing water slides to one wall; a rolled object does not stay put. **Walking is constant small correction.** The fatigue accumulates across a session.
- **The hole in the side.** Huge, ragged, the fall's own doing. The rock of the hillside is still locked in its broken edges. Whatever passed through it is long weathered off; the stone has had centuries to forget. A first-reading party sees a hole and nothing more.
- **The green.** Plants have taken the lower decks. Vines up every wall, moss on the floors, creeping growth threaded through shapes that resolve, on a second look, into **iron cages**. The party steps into a tangle, not a ruin. Looking up, the growth **climbs a certain distance and then stops** — a hard line several metres up, above which the wreck is bare stone. The line is a fact before it is an explanation.

## At the approach — descending in

The bare rise above the wreck is the *last normal ground*. Below it the hill-grass gives way to tumbled mage-era stone, pitched at the angle the city settled at. Every tower-stump that still stands leans a degree or two.

- **Every door is sealed.** A party walking the outside finds no intact opening that admits them. The warding held on the intact faces.
- **The hole is the only way in.** Low on one flank, facing the hillside the city struck.
- **No wind inside where there should be wind.** Once across the lip of the hole, the weather stops reaching. Something holds the air within.

## At the hole — the only way in

The hole is both a passage and a scene. Large enough that a crowd under duress could have passed through in steady flow; not large enough that anyone could have missed it. It sits in the lower decks, and the lower decks have grown over — so the hole itself is fringed with vines, edged with moss, **the hillside rock still locked in its rim**.

Time has done its work on the evacuation. **There are no clear signs left** of what passed through here. The stone is smooth from centuries of weather rather than from the press of bodies. A first-reading party sees only that something made this hole a very long time ago. What actually happened here reveals itself only inside (see §"At the lower decks — reading the rubble").

## At the lower decks — the green tangle

The hole opens onto the city's holds. Iron cages bolted to the floors; ceiling-chains where some held. They are sized for small people.

The green has had centuries.

- **Vines root in the cage-bars themselves**, climbing the iron as if it were trellis. Moss layers the floors between the ranks. Creepers loop through ceiling-chains and hang in curtains across the hold's far end. The party walks an **overgrown lattice** rather than an open floor; the original geometry has to be inferred.
- **Stairs and ramps lead out of the holds toward the middle decks.** Other routes are sealed or crushed by the landing. **The party is funnelled upward whether they want to be or not.**
- **Laurent does not attack here.** His fire does not reach the lower decks; his puppets cannot descend. The lower decks are *his* frontier too — he has not come down here for centuries. The wreck has done the rest.

### Reading the rubble — skill-gated

On a walk-through, the lower decks read as *cages + overgrowth + ruin*. A first-visit party that does not look carefully can walk out with exactly that impression, and it will **colour every scene above** — they will conclude **they have walked into a slaver's lair**, and will meet Laurent with that frame in hand.

With real observation — *Hunter*, *Druid*, *Ingenuity*, *Relaxed*, or a patient skill-check — the rubble tells a second story:

- **Cot-frames in spaces that were not cage-spaces.** Makeshift wooden frames, rotted to skeletons, in the *gaps* between cage-ranks — in corridors, in storerooms, in whatever open floor the holds had. **Refugees built these; they are not original fittings.** And they are *not in the cages*. Even under evacuation pressure, nobody slept in a cage. The cages stayed taboo even to desperate people.
- **Food-storage traces.** Barrel-rings in the floor dust where staves have long rotted; grain residue compacted into corners; brackets bolted into walls to hold sacks and crates that are long gone. The holds were used to **stockpile food for a population** — hundreds of people, for a journey of weeks.
- **Taken together**: the lower decks housed refugees at the city's last inhabitation. With the original cages present but deliberately avoided. A moral mark left by the evacuation itself.

A party that reads this has found the city's **real history** from the structure alone. Laurent will confirm it when asked (see `doc/npcs/laurent.md` §"When asked how he came to own the castle") — but their reading is already complete before he speaks.

A party that *doesn't* read it carries the slaver's-lair frame into the rest of the wreck. **This is legitimate, not a failure.** The correction is real; it arrives from Laurent's own account, from Felicia's room, or from a return visit with fresh eyes. Misreading on first contact is a valid state and the DM should let it hold until one of those paths delivers the correction.

## At the middle decks — where the plants stop

Climbing the stairs out of the holds, the party crosses the **plant-line** — a hard boundary perhaps a deck and a half above the hole. Below, green. Above, stone. The line is not cut; it is a **tideline**. Something above the line has kept the plants from climbing further, and has been doing so for a very long time.

This is the **first visible edge of Laurent's reach**. He does not actively cull the green; he simply *is* up here, and his preservation holds. Where he stopped caring, the plants established. Where his care begins, they did not.

Beyond the plant-line, the middle decks begin:

- **Rooms partly renovated, partly raw.** Walls freshly plastered next to walls still bearing the original cage-fittings. Doors cut where cages used to stand. A kitchen expanded out of what was once a holding pen. The retrofit is **unmistakable once noticed** — a change of trade, mid-structure.
- **Unfinished.** Several rooms are in mid-conversion. Tools left against the walls. A beam half-installed. **Laurent had not had time.** The work stopped when the White City fell.
- **The stairs continue up.** The middle decks are a traversal zone more than a destination. A first-visit party will likely pass through without lingering. Lingering is rewarded: this is where the party first feels **Laurent, the man**, rather than the demi-lich — someone who was trying.

## At the upper deck — alcoves and the first waking

Emerging onto the upper deck, the **character of the wreck changes again**. Higher ceilings. Stone dressed with inlay. Mage-era colonnades that somehow held through the fall. **No plants at all.**

And, set into the walls, the **alcoves** begin.

- **Wooden humanoid puppets** — kin in construct-type to Haven's population and the buried tower's pot-people (see `doc/places/haven.md` and `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`). Physically complete, never spirit-bound. Hundreds of them on this deck; the party passes dozens without registering that every one is looking vaguely ahead.
- **The first alcove does not wake immediately.** The party crosses several halls first. Then a head tracks, slowly. Then a puppet steps down. **Laurent has to bring each one online;** initial intrusion meets one or two. The longer the party stays, the more appear.
- **The puppets were not made for combat.** Built for labour and detail work. Awkward fighters, telegraphed, slow to adjust.
- **They are almost impossible to damage.** Blades glance off. Blunt strikes jar joints without breaking them. Fire chars the wood without penetrating.
- **The reliable way to stop one is the nearest edge.** See §"At the broken edges." A puppet at rest far below is a puppet Laurent can no longer usefully animate from here.

Two things a party realises on this deck that change how they read the whole wreck: **the puppets are the *owners'* population**, and **the owners lived up here**.

## At the kept corridors — the tended core

Past the first puppet engagements, the party crosses a threshold they did not mark. The tilt is still there, but **dust on the floors stops accumulating**. Cloth on walls does not fray. Wood that should have split has not. Someone maintains this.

The **plant-line far below** and the **dust-line here** are the two visible boundaries of Laurent's active reach — one passive, one kept. Plants don't root where his workings hold; dust doesn't settle where he attends.

- **The kept corridors lead toward the audience hall.** Quickest route in; also the route Laurent is most attentive to.
- **The puppets here are more alert.** Thresholds lower. A puppet here wakes earlier than one on the periphery of the upper deck.
- **The party will feel watched.** Not in a specific room — continuously.

## At Felicia's room — the observational hint

Somewhere off the kept corridors, a small room is **preserved completely**. Unmarked; not signposted; not brought up by Laurent. A party sweeping through will miss it. A party that looks carefully — opening the right panel, turning into a side passage on the way to the audience hall — finds it.

- **A working-table** with intricate pattern-work that matches the word *intricate* in any description of Felicia's necklace.
- **Personal effects** — lived-in rather than ceremonial. A shawl; a tool she used; a cup. Not museum pieces; hers.
- **A window that once overlooked sky.** Now overlooks the hillside the city fell onto. The sill is dust-free.
- **Maybe a sketch or a note** in her hand, DM-fill. Nothing says *the necklace calms Laurent*.

The point of the room is **inference, not gift**. A party that *observes* this room can reach the chain on their own: Felicia mattered to him. If she mattered this much, reaching her might reach him. The place she can still be reached is Luchebert, the shrine, Willow.

This is the **observational path** to the necklace mechanism. See also: Willow's trust (the **relational path**, `doc/npcs/willow.md` §"Closest to Felicia") and Laurent's own narration of his downfall (the **narrated path**, `doc/npcs/laurent.md` §"What he will tell"). Three overlapping pointers; any one suffices.

## At the audience hall — Laurent's seat

The room at the centre of the kept corridors. Laurent is seated at the back. Beyond this, the room is his scene, not the place's: see **`doc/npcs/laurent.md`** §§"At the audience hall," "What he will tell," "Stories he tells," for what happens here.

What the *place* contributes: the chair was the chair of the city at its height, preserved without aging; Felicia's empty seat waits to his right; the alcoves around the walls hold puppets that stay still while the welcome holds. If the welcome breaks, the hall's alcoves are the first to animate.

## At the broken edges — verticality as a mechanic

The wreck's most load-bearing terrain feature is its drops. A DM should know where they are before running a scene.

- **Sheared galleries** — the colonnaded streets broke at the fall; many end in open air over the hillside. The drop ranges from survivable-with-damage to fatal.
- **Open tower sides** — where a tower tumbled, one or two faces are now open; the internal rooms are balconies.
- **Stair-cuts** — grand staircases that terminate mid-flight.
- **The outer edge** — walking out on the downhill side leads to a long drop onto the hill below, not a gentle slope.

**A puppet thrown off any of these stays down.** A party member who falls takes the same damage the puppet would have taken, and does not have Laurent's workings to recover.

## Hidden in the wreck — what survived the looters

Anything obvious is gone (see `doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Where items come from"). What survives now survives because Laurent preserved it deliberately — Felicia's room, the audience hall, his own kept pieces — **or because it was hidden well enough that the looters never found it**. The hidden caches are pre-rift mage-aristocratic in register and live in places the puppets do not patrol:

- **A pre-rift mage-traveller's pack** — between-deck cache; the *found-piece* Masterwork form of Backpack (`doc/claude/ITEMS.md` §"Backpack"). Not at any market; nobody alive can build a new one.
- **One or two refreshment potions** — preserved by their bottles' workings; centuries on the shelf and still good for a single dose each. The bottles are themselves the late-game recipe for an alchemist who can read the working.
- **A small piece of pre-rift court-blade or a wartime master-piece** — Sword's *found-piece* Masterwork form: easier to wield and carry, *not* more deadly. Hidden in a structural void Laurent does not visit.
- **A piece of Felicia's life-practice** — set aside in her preserved room before the fall, in a small drawer Laurent has not opened since. Reaching this requires standing earned through the necklace path; he will tell some of what it is and reserve the rest.
- **Inside the half-replaced lower decks** — small mage-craft fragments in the gap between the original trafficker's frame and Laurent's intended replacement; the renovation was halted mid-work and the gap is its own hiding place.

A party that has earned the necklace path can ask Laurent about the wreck's contents directly; he will tell some, reserve some. A party opening caches without his standing risks the puppets — or worse, the fire.

## Visible patterns

- **The puppets do not move while the party is welcome.** The moment the welcome ends, alcoves begin to animate — not all at once; in a rising wave.
- **The alcoves are an upper-deck feature.** The middle and lower decks have none. The owners built their puppet-servants to work the halls the owners lived in.
- **The plant-line and the dust-line map Laurent's reach.** Plants below the first line; bare stone above it. Dust settling below the second; controlled above it. A party walking the wreck end-to-end crosses both.
- **No wind in the kept corridors.** The outer wreck admits weather; the inner does not. Something holds the air.
- **Nothing rots the way outdoor ruin rots above the plant-line.** Below it, time has done its full work.
- **The valley is not audible from inside.** The sounds of the hillside stop reaching once past the hole.
- **The smell of Laurent's fire lingers** in certain halls on the upper deck — the ones where prior intruders were burned. A scent that is not quite woodsmoke.

## If the party does something — what the place does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Walks the exterior looking for another way in | Finds none. Every warded door holds; only the hole admits. |
| Examines the lower-deck rubble with real skill (*Hunter*/*Druid*/*Ingenuity*/*Relaxed*, or a patient check) | Reads the cots and food-storage traces. Understands the evacuation-shelter layer. Sees the whole place differently from that point forward. |
| Walks the lower decks without looking carefully | Comes away with *cages + overgrowth = slaver's lair*. The misreading is legitimate and correctable later. |
| Cuts or burns the plants on the lower decks | Possible, but the growth is centuries deep. Meaningful clearance is session-scale work. Laurent does not respond — the green is not his. |
| Crosses the plant-line climbing up | Feels the change of air. No further plants above. |
| Lingers in the middle decks reading the retrofit | Understands Laurent-as-remediator. The pledge on the upper deck lands harder when they reach him. |
| Loots anything above the plant-line | **Fire.** Laurent sees every hall on the upper deck. Putting the object back typically stops it. |
| Loots from the lower decks | Laurent does not respond. The lower decks are not his. |
| Damages the wreck's preserved areas (especially Felicia's room) | Fire, plus full puppet activation across the city. Escalates fast. |
| Moves quickly and purposefully toward the audience hall | May outrun the awakening wave. Reaches the hall with only one or two puppets engaged. |
| Lingers on the upper deck | Meets a steadily rising count of puppets. Eventually more than any single edge can clear. |
| Throws an animated puppet off an edge | Works. Stays down. Laurent notices but does not hold it against them. |
| Tries to fight puppets at range | Marginally effective. Wood absorbs; joints shrug. Use the edges. |
| Carries **Felicia's necklace** into the wreck | **Puppets nearest the party still as the party approaches.** Laurent senses her proximity long before they reach the audience hall. The wreck becomes passable. See `doc/npcs/laurent.md` for the conversation that follows. |
| Approaches respectfully, handling nothing | Fire holds off. Puppets wake slowly. The party reaches the audience hall alive with the option to negotiate. |
| Examines Felicia's room without touching anything | The observational hint lands. The party can infer the necklace chain on their own. |
| Opens, cracks, or salvages a cage | Laurent does not respond. *"Those are not mine"* if asked. The iron is serviceable; the welds are old but holding. A cage used to restrain a puppet is a party *edge* that does not require a drop. |
| Asks Laurent what the cages were for | He tells the story. See `doc/npcs/laurent.md` §"When asked how he came to own the castle." The mood while he tells it is colder than usual, and steadier than usual. |
| Asks about the partly-renovated middle decks | He confirms the retrofit was his, and unfinished. Does not dwell. *"Time ran out. I had hoped to finish before anyone needed the holds."* |
| Brings a **Haven**-standing companion into the wreck | The Haven companion recognises the alcove puppets as *kin they never met*. Their view on Laurent's remote-puppeting of bodies-never-spirit-bound is its own conversation; it changes what the Haven-Laurent arc becomes. |
| Tries to bind, dispel, or destroy Laurent from within the wreck | Full city activates. Every alcove puppet animates at once. The fight is lost unless the party has prepared across many sessions. See `doc/npcs/laurent.md`. |

## DM notes

- **The misreading is part of the design.** A first-visit party without the observation skill reads the lower decks as a slaver's lair and may carry that frame all the way to Laurent. **This is legitimate, not a failure.** The corrective reading exists (skill, Felicia's room, Laurent's own narration, a return visit) — let the first-reading frame hold until one of those paths delivers the correction. Do not hand the refuge-layer for free.
- **The place is session-scale, not scene-scale.** A first visit sees a fraction. Plan for return. First visits end with Laurent reached (or not) and the scale legible; second visits dig into Felicia's room, the alcove puppets as *resource* rather than hazard, and the last-flight option.
- **Verticality is the load-bearing combat mechanic.** Before running a fight, know where the nearest drop is. If no drop is available, the fight is impossible on the upper deck; adjust the geometry. The party should leave knowing that *edges matter* in a way no other location has taught them.
- **Laurent is the security system of the upper deck, not the lower.** Treat every upper-deck hall as observed. No covert looting above the plant-line. The lower decks are outside his reach — a party needing to regroup, talk privately, or recover from an engagement can do so down in the green.
- **The plant-line and the dust-line map Laurent's reach.** Narrate them as real transitions, not flavour. Crossing the plant-line upward is a physical threshold the party should *feel*. It is also a tactical marker: above it, Laurent sees; below it, he does not.
- **Felicia's room is an observational hint.** Do not signpost it; do not bring it up. A party that finds it has earned the inference. A party that does not find it has the Willow path and the Laurent-narration path still open.
- **Not all alcoves wake.** The wave is sequential and local. The DM controls the count — a small number for a first-visit sampler, a rising tide for a party that stalled, full activation only for direct provocation.
- **The alcove puppets as resource, post-accord.** If the party reaches an accord with Laurent, the puppets stop being hazards and become *available*. Dozens or hundreds of bodies under a willing demi-lich's control is a serious piece on the board — including for the Shamans' long plan.
- **The cages as a party resource.** Iron sized to hold small people is also iron sized to restrain an alcove puppet with limited leverage. Creative parties will notice.
- **Kin to Haven.** A party that has earned standing at Haven has a bridge here. Haven's inhabitants would recognise the alcove puppets as *kin-they-never-met* and would have views on whether Laurent's remote-puppeting of bodies never spirit-bound is itself acceptable. That conversation is a campaign-scale thread in its own right.
- **The last flight is this place's climax.** If the six shackles at the Lake of Tears are broken (see `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"The last flight"), this wreck rises for one final journey. The concrete work of preparing the city to fly — clearing what needs clearing, provisioning, reaching an arrangement with Laurent about where it goes — is a whole arc rooted here.
- **The passive spirit-shield.** Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a demi-lich reads as a mighty spirit; chaotic spirits back off from the hills around the wreck. Destroying or releasing Laurent removes that shield. The region pays. A campaign that ends with the last flight should account for this in its final pages.

## Open questions

- **Who were the prior owners?** The group Laurent bought the castle from — who ran the continent's demi-human trade until legal trouble broke them. Names, fate after the trouble, whether any descendants still carry the grudge: DM-fill.
- How many alcove puppets does the wreck hold in total? Hundreds is plausible; exact count is DM-fill.
- Does Laurent's remote-puppeting degrade with distance within the wreck, or can he run any of them from any point?
- Can a puppet thrown off the wreck be recovered and reactivated, or does the fall destroy something central?
- Is there a way to awaken one *properly* — with its own bound spirit — rather than using it as Laurent's extension? Would Laurent allow it?
