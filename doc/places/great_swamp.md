# The Great Swamp

- **Type:** Wide wetland region surrounding the Lake of Tears. Marshland, bogs, reedbeds, slow water, fungal infestation thickening toward the lake at its centre.
- **Region:** South of Elmsfield; surrounds the Lake of Tears (see `doc/places/laurents_city.md` for the hills east; the six towers ring the lake within the swamp).
- **Officially:** Nobody's. Not claimed, not mapped, not policed.
- **In practice:** **The barrier between Allondo and the wilderness at its heart.** Crossable, but *hostile to stay*. The party passes through the swamp on their way south from Elmsfield (Session 2); they do not settle in it. Anyone who tries pays.
- **Status:** Stable — and stable here means *stably dangerous*. The fungus does not weaken. The insects do not migrate. The water stays unsafe.

## What the party meets

The party comes south from Elmsfield by following the small river. For the first hour or two the terrain is ordinary hill-country. Then the ground changes. Water stands where it should drain. The grass gives way to reeds. The trees lean wrong — roots half-drowned, trunks dark with moisture, lichen where lichen should not be.

Within the first half-day, **the three threats announce themselves in sequence**:

1. **The insect-sound.** Before they see anything else, the party hears *the hum*. Not individual midges; a *continuous low drone* that rises and falls across the marsh's breathing. By midday the party is batting at clouds of blood-gnats; by dusk, the swarm is a wall.
2. **The water moves wrong.** A log that was at the edge of a channel drifts across it; a ripple runs counter to the current. Something watches the party from the mud that they cannot identify. They lose a boot; they save a hand.
3. **The fungal colour.** As they approach the lake's basin, crystalline growths begin to appear on trees, stones, the standing water. Thin at first; thickening toward the centre. The air takes on the specific metallic quality that poisoned Irna.

**Elder Diederik warned them.** Most parties do not fully believe the warning until they are half a day in.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The insect-drone.** Continuous; omnidirectional; rising to a roar at dusk and dawn. Not quiet any hour of the day. A party conversing normally raises their voices within minutes; by evening they are shouting.
- **The water that is not water.** Channels the party's eyes register as *wet* but whose surface does not quite move like water should. Too thick. Too slow. Too *aware*. A stick dropped in sinks at the wrong angle.
- **The fungal colour, intensifying south.** Thin on entry — a patch of crystalline growth on a fallen log; a thread of white moss that should be green. By the time the party is within sight of the Lake of Tears, the fungus is *everywhere* and the air has gone metallic.

## At the swamp's edge — coming down from Elmsfield

The transition from hill-country to marsh is not sharp; it grades. A party walking the Elmsfield road south for two hours is still on dry land; at three hours, they are squelching; at four, they are on actual footing that sinks under their weight.

- **The river narrows and spreads.** The small watercourse from Elmsfield becomes a braided channel, then a maze. Following it south is still possible — the water leads to the lake — but navigation requires attention.
- **The path dies.** There is no maintained track. Hunters from Elmsfield know a few high-ground routes for specific game seasons; none of them lead where the party is going.
- **Ground-cover shifts.** Reeds replace grass. Standing water replaces mud. The trees that stand here are **alder, willow, and something darker** that Diederik would name if he were with the party; on their own they see *wrong-coloured bark*.
- **Animal sign thins.** A party used to reading forest-sign sees less — fewer tracks, fewer droppings, fewer bird-calls. The creatures that do live here are *not the animals the party recognises*.

## At the marshland — the crossing

The main body of the swamp. A half-day's walk on good footing; longer on bad. The party does not run this stretch — they pick their way.

- **High-ground islands** are scattered across the marsh. Reachable; usable for brief stops. **Not safe to camp on** — the water's predators know them as the only dry ground and circle at night.
- **The reedbeds** rise higher than a person's head in places. A party wading through cannot see each other; communication is by voice, and voice draws the swarm. Parties often link up at the wrists with cord.
- **Sudden drops.** The mud is not uniform. A party crossing a clearly-shallow stretch can suddenly find the bottom gone beneath them. A *Swimmer* or *Hunter* character reads these; others do not.
- **Game trails that are not game trails.** Paths in the reeds that read as deer-track are often **mud-lurker runs**. Taking one saves walking and raises risk.

## At the bogs — the worst ground

Patches within the marsh where the bottom is peat and the water stands stagnant. The party should avoid these; sometimes they cannot.

- **Visible surface is deceptive.** A pool that looks like a puddle may be four paces deep. A mat of moss may be floating over nothing at all.
- **Peat-rot smell.** Distinct from the marsh's ordinary wet-earth; sharper; reads as *dead thing close*.
- **Preserves what falls in.** Ironic and grim. A party that loses gear here does not necessarily lose it permanently — the bog holds items for generations. A party that loses a *person* will not get them back quickly.
- **The fungus likes the bogs.** Concentrated growth — crystalline threads across the moss, films on the water. A party inhaling a deep breath near a bog edge takes fungus into the lungs.

## At the fungus-zone — approaching the lake

The southernmost stretch before the lake basin opens. **The fungus is thickest here.**

- **Crystalline growths on every surface.** Trees, stones, the water itself. A party in this zone without protection begins accumulating Endu damage each scene — the same process that afflicted Irna.
- **The air is metallic.** Prolonged breathing tastes wrong. A face-covering of damp cloth helps; a priest-blessed kerchief helps more; a *Druid* working can hold the fungal spores back for a scene or two.
- **The trees are not green.** Some are dying; some have died; some have *accommodated* the fungus and are now *differently alive* — not spirit-warped the way the forbidden woods' trees are, but drained, pale, half-hollow.
- **Animal sign vanishes.** Nothing lives in this stretch except the fungus and whatever can eat it.

## At the six towers — within the swamp

The Lake of Tears sits at the swamp's centre, and the **six towers ring it within the marsh**. A party that has reached the fungus-zone is within sight of them. For the towers individually, see `doc/places/laurents_city.md` (and the wreck east of the lake), `doc/places/volcano_tower.md`, `doc/places/darkness_tower.md`, `doc/places/sinking_tower.md`, and the buried tower in `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`, and the Lightning Tower context in `doc/npcs/storm_mage.md`.

The swamp-approach to any of these is its own short scene. Crossing the last stretch of fungal marsh to reach a tower is not crossing neutral terrain.

## Why this is a bad camping spot

**Camp here is hostile.** A DM should know why before a party tries.

- **Insects at night.** The blood-gnats swarm harder after sunset. Without smoke, repellent-herbs, or *Druid* intervention, a camp takes accumulated bite-damage across the night. Sleep is interrupted. Rest is shallow. The Moros camp-scenario outcome (full recovery of stat reductions) becomes *partial*.
- **Water-predators hunt at night.** Mud-lurkers are nocturnal. A camp on the swamp's high-ground islands is *where they come*. A watch is non-negotiable. A party without a watcher who can see in the dark (*Night*, *Lookout*) loses a sleeper.
- **Fungus breathes in a sleeping lung faster than a walking one.** A party that sleeps in the fungus-zone without protection takes Endu damage overnight they would not take walking through in daytime. Irna's poisoning worsens in sleep.
- **Dreams come wrong.** The fungus-adjacent air triggers specific dreams — of dying trees, of the fungus growing on sleepers' own skin. Not prophetic; *ambient*. A party waking from them is shaken.
- **The fire is a lure.** A signal visible across the reeds to anything watching. A party that builds a proper fire calls in what they do not want to meet.

## Why this is a bad foraging spot

**Foraging here gives little, and what it gives is often wrong.**

- **The visible plants are mostly inedible.** Reed-starch is a possibility but the reeds in this swamp are fungus-adjacent; a *Druid* must read each clump, and most are contaminated.
- **Water is rarely drinkable.** Standing water holds insect-eggs; running water holds fungal spores; the few clean springs are high-ground islands the predators hunt.
- **Frogs carry the fungus.** A rot-frog eaten for meat transmits the poisoning. *Smell* detects this; untrained parties do not.
- **Fish from the lake are not safe.** The Lake of Tears' fish have been fungal-touched for centuries. Catching them is easy; eating them is how Endu leaks out of a party across days.
- **Game is scarce.** Most wild animals avoid the swamp. The few that do live here are either fungal-contaminated or they *are* the predators the party is trying to avoid.

**A *Druid* or *Hunter* can find enough clean food for a day's forage with real effort.** A party without either struggles. The blasted lands and the desert are worse still (see `doc/claude/SURVIVAL.md`).

## Visible patterns

- **Insect-drone continuous.** Loudest at dawn and dusk; quieter at noon.
- **Predator circling after dark.** A watcher sees the reeds move on a calm night.
- **The fungus intensifies southward.** The gradient is the party's map.
- **Trees die a specific way.** Fungus-accommodated trees are pale, half-hollow, still standing — and can be read by a druid as a regional indicator of how long the infestation has held.
- **High-ground islands are fought-over.** The few dry patches are where predators den and where prey animals shelter briefly. Signs of both on every island.
- **Bogs smell sharper than marsh.** A party's nose distinguishes them at ten paces, with training.
- **The air goes metallic at a specific line** — the party will cross a subtle edge and suddenly taste it. Diederik would call this *the fungal front*.

## What you learn here, and when

| Topic | When the swamp gives it up |
|---|---|
| That the marsh is hostile | Within the first hour. |
| That the fungus is here | Within half a day, as the first crystalline growth appears. |
| That the water is not water | First time the log drifts wrong, usually day one. |
| That the insects are *this* many | First evening. |
| That mud-lurkers exist | When one takes a boot, a dog, or a party-member. |
| That the fungus intensifies southward | Over the crossing — the gradient becomes legible. |
| That the towers are within the swamp | On sight, once the fungus-zone is reached. |
| That the lake is at the centre | From the ridge approach on return, looking back. |

## If the party does something — what the swamp does

| If the party... | What happens |
|---|---|
| Walks in daylight, briskly, with purpose | Passes through. Insect-bites accumulate; a watchful party keeps them at tolerable. |
| Lingers | Insects escalate; predators close; fungus accumulates. Lingering is how the swamp kills. |
| Camps on a high-ground island | Watches required. Mud-lurkers circle. A party without *Night*, *Lookout*, *Hearing* loses the watch. |
| Camps deep in the fungus-zone | **Do not.** Overnight Endu damage; dreams; fungus in the lungs. A party that does this wakes diminished. |
| Builds a fire | Visibility lure. Calls in what they do not want. A small, shielded fire in a stone hollow is better than an open one. |
| Takes a game-trail in the reeds | Saves walking; raises predator risk. A *Hunter* reads the difference between animal and mud-lurker trails. |
| Swims a channel | Possible. Mud-lurker exposure is real. A *Swimmer* handles the water; no one is safe from what's in it. |
| Eats a frog | **Fungus-transmission.** Without a *Smell*-check or Druid-vetting, the party member takes poisoning. |
| Drinks swamp-water unboiled | Same mechanism. Fungal spores in the gut are the same disease as in the lungs. |
| Boils swamp-water thoroughly | Safer. Not completely safe; the fungus's spore-form survives some boiling. |
| Asks Diederik beforehand about the swamp | Gets specific advice. Which plants are edible. Which reeds are not. Where the fungal front is likely. |
| Performs a *Druid* working to read the region | Reads the gradient. Can identify specific clean springs. Can clean a clump of food-plant. The power's swamp-use. |
| Performs a *Shamanic* working | Local spirits are weakened by the fungus. A shaman senses the fungus's *origin* lies further south — at the Lake. |
| Carries Willow-blessed cloth | Fungal protection for a scene or two. Willow gives these sparingly; see her file. |
| Attempts to burn the fungus | Burns in localised patches; does not clear in meaningful quantity. The fungus regrows within days. |

## DM notes

- **The swamp is a *crossing*, not a destination.** Structure any swamp session as *getting through*, not *spending time in*. A party that lingers pays; a DM who structures a long in-swamp arc without reason punishes them.
- **The three threats compound.** Insects reduce watch-effectiveness → predators get closer → fungus thickens → rest is shallower → Endu drops. A party in poor condition is more vulnerable to all three. Make the compounding visible through short narration: *"By third watch the gnats have taken more Endu than the walk did."*
- **This is the campaign's worst camp biome *before* the blasted lands and desert.** See `doc/claude/SURVIVAL.md` for the biome-gradient. The swamp is the *introduction* to hostile-biome survival — a party learns here what a real camp-challenge looks like before they face worse.
- **Diederik is the pre-swamp advisor.** A party that asks him before crossing gets real preparation. A party that does not is learning by cost.
- **Willow's protective cloths are rare currency.** Do not give them out casually. Willow gates them through trust.
- **Irna's poisoning story is the emotional spine.** A party carrying Irna on the return is carrying someone whose condition is *what the fungus does*. The swamp makes that register concrete.
- **Cooperative setpieces live here.** A swamp camp with watch-rotations, wound-tending, fungus-protection, and insect-management is a scenario that *requires* cooperation. See `doc/claude/SURVIVAL.md` §"Surviving a swamp night" for the worked set-piece.
- **The fungal origin is the Lake's bound air elemental.** A party that fully understands the swamp understands it is *downstream of the cage*. Releasing or freeing the air elemental changes the swamp's register entirely. See `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"The last flight."
- Lore cross-references: the fungus and the air elemental in `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"The Lake of Tears"; the tower ring in the lake in the tower place files; Diederik's knowledge in `doc/npcs/elder_diederik.md`; Willow's blessed cloths in `doc/npcs/willow.md`; Irna's poisoning in `doc/npcs/irna_bean.md`; the swamp's creatures in `doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Swamp Fauna"; the survival mechanics in `doc/claude/SURVIVAL.md`.

## Open questions

- **The specific clean springs**. A DM can place one at a high-ground island if a party needs drinkable water; the file does not fix locations.
- **The number and territorial range of mud-lurkers.** Probably a handful across the swamp; DM-fill.
- **Whether the fungus is *reducible* by a working.** Short-term localised: yes. Campaign-scale: only by freeing the air elemental. DM can allow other approaches if a campaign earns them.
- **Whether Elmsfield's river is itself contaminated.** Probably not at source; becomes contaminated as it enters the swamp. Drinking-water rules at Elmsfield remain safe.
