# The Sinking Tower

- **Role:** One of the six towers ringing the Lake of Tears; cage-shackle of the bound air elemental; two-halved, with a bound owner-spirit in its lower half
- **Location:** Floating on the Lake of Tears itself — upper half visible above water, lower half at the lakebed when the tower descends
- **Status:** Active; its upper half responds defensively (by sinking) to anyone trying to climb it

## Summary

From the lakeshore, it looks like a single tower floating on the surface of the Lake of Tears. Closer observation shows it is buoyed rather than built on piling; the upper half rides on the water. **Any attempt to climb onto the tower causes it to sink** — straight down, out of reach, to the lakebed below. The sinking is not slow. It is a controlled descent that puts the climber out of the game if they let go, and out of sight entirely if they hold on.

**It also sinks of its own accord.** Every night, the tower descends to the lakebed as part of its own cycle — not triggered by anyone climbing — and rises again before dawn. During the descent it gives off a **visible glow on the water**, the glow persisting until the tower meets whatever it is settling onto at the bottom. From the lakeshore at night the descent is visible; from **Gerhald's hill** (the closest vantage on the ring), the nightly cycle is an unmistakable sight, and he will tell the party what he has seen if asked (see `doc/npcs/hermit_shaman.md` §"What he sees from the hill"). He does not know the specifics; he knows the pattern, and he knows the glow ends where the tower *reaches its counterpart*.

Holding on is the point. The sinking is not a defence but an **entry mechanism**. A brave player who **clings to the tower all the way down** discovers what the upper half is for: it is the antechamber. The real structure is the **lower half at the lakebed**, an air-filled sealed living space that was its original owner's home. The tower is two halves joined at the waterline, with the lived-in half underwater and reachable only through the sink.

## The owner-spirit

The original owner is dead. His **spirit is bound to the tower** and still occupies the lower half — specifically the living quarters — where he has remained since his death. He is not hostile in the ordinary sense, but he is *here*, aware, and waiting. The binding keeps him in place; the tower keeps him company.

He **wants something**. The specific content is a DM-fill detail, but it should be the shape of a **fetch quest**: a specific object he lost, a person he wants to hear from, a ritual he could not complete before he died, a belonging of his that a surviving heir now keeps elsewhere. Something the party can *bring him* through sustained work, not resolve in a single conversation.

## The lever

Bring the bound owner-spirit what he wants, and he will release the tower — but in a direction the other towers cannot match. He **floats the tower down the small river to the sea**. The upper half lifts, the lower half follows on the same buoyancy that has held the structure together, and the whole tower travels with the water current out of the lake, down the valley, and eventually into open ocean. The shackle breaks the moment the tower crosses out of the ring; the spirit goes with it on his own terms, heading wherever he is going.

This is a **unique exit direction** among the six shackles. The pot-boy walks out with the furnace following. The volcano tower lifts on fire-elemental energy. The darkness tower moves by mosaic mechanic. The Storm Mage is brought back to the Lightning Tower. Gerhald consents. The Sinking Tower *floats away by water*.

## Role as spirit-shield (partial)

Per `doc/claude/LORE.md` §"Spirit Ecology," a bound owner-spirit inhabiting his old home for generations reads to chaotic spirits as a mighty-adjacent presence. The Sinking Tower contributes, quietly, to the Lake region's unusual spirit-shield cover. When it leaves — by the river, to the sea — that contribution departs with it.

## Relationships

- Original owner — dead, bound, still present in the lower half. His name, species, and what he wants are all DM-fill.
- A potential ally to anyone the old-order faction wronged — his tower was one of the five seized and moved, and he has had a long time to think about that.

## Open questions

- What does the owner-spirit want? The fetch-quest content is open.
- Is the lower half's air-seal metaphysical or mechanical? A party thinking about salvaging the tower later may care.
- Does the spirit *leave* with the tower when it floats to sea, or stay bound even as the structure travels?
