# The Generator

- **Type:** Pre-rift mage laboratory at the **white city's centre**, in the broken lands. The *generator* end of the **Lethran Pair**'s paired chaos-management system (see `doc/claude/PRE_RIFT_PAIR.md` for the arc). Three levels cut into the ruin; the chaos has been *building toward* this place for centuries with whatever it could lay hands on.
- **Region:** Centre of the broken lands, inside the white-city ruins. Reachable only by pressing through the chaos-pressure that thickens toward the centre (`doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"The white city — the centre").
- **Officially:** Nobody's. Most ruin-crawlers do not reach this depth; those who do rarely return.
- **In practice:** **The migration's destination.** The puppet-parts that have been moving across the broken lands for centuries arrive *here*. The chaos is *wrong-building* what it thinks the Pair's system needed. The wrong-build is patient, sad, almost-coherent.
- **Status:** Active. The chaos has not stopped. The Generator is *not a ruin* — it is a working corpse with new parts grown over the old.

## What the party meets

The party crosses the white city's outer ruins (chaos-pressure thickening; *Druid* and *Shaman* both feel the weight) and reaches a pre-rift mage-tower whose lower three levels are cut into the ground. The doors at ground level are gone; the stairwells stand. **The first thing the party hears is the wrong-construction at work.** A slow, deliberate sound — not building, not breaking; *fitting*. Something is fitting parts to other parts down there, and the fitting has been going on for a long time.

The migration the party tracked across the broken lands ends here. The puppet-parts they saw at Silvertrace, Commons House, the Press-house ridge — those parts are *now in this building*, fitted into shapes the chaos thinks the system needs. None of those shapes are right. The chaos is patient.

## How it reads at a glance — the 2–3 standouts on entry

- **The fitting-sound.** A regular, slow tap-and-press from below the ground floor. Continuous. Audible from the entrance. *Hearing*-aware characters read its rhythm as deliberate, not random.
- **The Lethran seal carved over the threshold.** A circle bisected by two parallel lines (DM-fill on exact form), worn but unmistakable. The Pair's working-mark — the same mark on the supply-cache letter at the Press-house, on the Anchor stone in the desert (`doc/places/lethran_dwelling.md`), on small tools at the Grain-mill. The party who has been collecting the spine recognises it now.
- **The walls are paneled.** Wooden panels lining the ground-floor walls, more arriving across the centuries — the **moved Commons House panels** are *here*, set into the ground floor's surfaces by the chaos's wrong-build. A community's emptied records, *back together*, on the wrong wall, in a wrong order. The room reads as a *gathering* the visitor was not invited to.

## At the ground floor — the gathering hall

The pre-rift entry hall, now the chaos's *holding floor*. The fitting-sound is below; up here the moved panels and small migration-pieces are arrayed. The **reassembled puppet-guards** patrol this level on a fixed pattern (per the Skeletal Guard mechanic, `doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Animated Guardians" — adapted: the bound working sits at the chest-knot rather than the breastbone, since these are iron-puppet limbs not bones).

- **Three to five reassembled puppet-guards** walk the ground floor. *Shaman* can settle one at a time by addressing the chaos-impulse holding it; *Hand* with the right tools disables a chest-knot in a single careful action; *Reading* finds the migration-record-fragments on the panels and may identify a *quieting phrase* the Pair used in their notebooks. Combat works but each guard is the toughness of three Skeletal Guards joined.
- **The moved panels** are readable. *Reading* uncovers community names from Commons House; a returning party recognises specific names. Removing a panel for return to **Haven** is possible but breaks the chaos's wrong-build at that wall, *triggering an alert*.
- **A side-room** off the gathering hall holds smaller migration-pieces — fragments from the dozens of broken-lands ruins the file does not name (a wooden man's elbow from Silvertrace; a kettle from a fallen farmstead; an inscribed lintel from a vanished schoolhouse). A party that has been to a regional ruin will find a piece they know.

**Items found here:** moved Commons House panels (returnable to Haven; cross-ref `doc/places/haven.md`); a folded-steel knife abandoned by a previous ruin-crawler who never made it back (Knife with the *quality folded steel* Material — `doc/claude/ITEMS.md`); a fragment of a wooden man (Haven receives this gratefully).

## At the working chamber — Level 2

A stair descends from the gathering hall into the pre-rift work-space. The fitting-sound is louder here; the floor is gritty with the dust of centuries of slow construction.

- **The Lethran Pair's working bench** stands against one wall, salt-dusted by time but intact — the bench's working-stone preserved by the same kind of small heat-spell that runs at the Tidehold (`doc/places/tidehold.md`). On the bench: pre-rift tools; a sealed inkpot the chaos has not been able to open; **the Pair's third-volume working journal**, water-damaged at the edges but fully readable in the centre.
- **A pre-rift mural runs the length of the chamber's south wall** — faded but legible. The painting depicts the system *at full power* as the Pair imagined it: the Generator on the left, a stylised horizon in the centre, the Anchor on the right, with thread-lines arcing between them through what the painter rendered as *sky-and-ground simultaneously*. Three figures stand at the Generator (the painter's signature is in the leftmost figure's robe-hem); two stand at the Anchor (the Pair); a child sits between them, drawing on a slate. **A *Reading* check identifies the painter as a Vintala-line apprentice** — the same line as Felicia (`doc/npcs/felicia.md`); the mural was a gift to the Pair from a colleague at the white city. *The mural is the cleanest single image of the Pair's tradition extant on the continent.*
- **The wall opposite the bench carries old-order seizing-marks scrawled over earlier inscriptions** — the chaos's wrong-build has not effaced them; the chaos cannot read. *Reading* recovers names: three of the seizing mages who ringed the lake-of-tears (cross-ref `doc/npcs/storm_mage.md` for additional names; the Generator's wall extends Ornalis's correspondence by two unique signatures).
- **A Memory Shade** drifts in this chamber, holding fragments of the Pair's last hours before the rift (`doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Memory Shade"). The shade is not aggressive unless ignored; it tries to *show* the party what it carries — the Pair at the bench in the days before the working failed. A character who genuinely engages gains a fragment of the chronological correspondence (the *middle* letters; the first is at the dwelling, the last at the Anchor) and can redirect the shade peacefully.
- **A handful of Stitchlings** patrol the chamber's perimeter, scavenging any small piece they can fit. *Shaman* can teach a stitchling small structure (the Tod-mechanism); a settled stitchling will sit by the working bench rather than fight, occasionally fitting a wrong piece to a wrong other piece in a slow rhythm.
- **The chaos almost-construct** sleeps in the chamber's far corner. *It does not engage on first entry.* The party can study the bench, the journal, the Memory Shade, and the wrong-fittings *while* the construct rests. Disturbing it (loud working; threatening the shade; removing a wrong-fitting it has spent decades on) wakes it.

**Items found here:** the Pair's third-volume working journal (a winter's reading for a serious mage student; names the Anchor's location and the sphinx-tradition that watches it); the bench's tools (a fine-edged scribing tool — Tools Material variant); two refreshment potions in their pre-rift working-bottles (find-form per `ITEMS.md`); a pre-rift wax cylinder with a sealed letter from one of the Pair to the other (the *middle* of the chronological correspondence, dated three months before the rift). **The cylinder's letter closes with a household line** — *"the child's birthday is the second week of next month; I will be sorry to miss it; tell them the dust I have brought home will mean something one day"* — a passing reference to the family at the **Lethran dwelling** (`doc/places/lethran_dwelling.md`), naming a child the system documentation never mentions.

## The chaos almost-construct — the heart's guardian

A specific named entity; full stat block at `doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"The Almost-Construct." Patient, sad, coherent in a way most chaos constructs are not. **Most importantly:**

- **The construct is sad.** A *Shaman* working with the Memory Shade nearby can address the construct *as the work it has been trying to do* rather than as a hostile entity. The mechanism is the Tod-style teaching, scaled up by a thousand-fold of patience — but it works. A successful address ends the encounter without combat; the construct *stops fitting* and *settles*. The chaos's wrong-build *halts* across the broken lands at the same hour.
- **Combat is possible** and costly. The construct does not pursue beyond Level 2; a party that takes damage can withdraw to the bench room and rest if the Memory Shade has been settled. Killing it ends the wrong-build entirely; the chaos in the broken lands does not lessen but does *stop seeking*. Felicia and Wilder both notice within the season.
- **Forcing it** (binding, dispelling) is *worse than killing*. The construct's confused awareness scatters; nearby Stitchlings amplify; a chain reaction can set off the entire white-city centre's chaos pressure. Do not let parties force it without cost.

## At the heart — Level 3

A short corridor from the working chamber descends to the heart. The fitting-sound is *loudest* here — and *stops* if the construct above has been settled or downed. The heart is small.

- **The unfinished socket** sits at the room's centre. Stone-cut, working-marked, *empty*. The party sees the shape — exactly fitting a stone-blank of the type they may have seen at the Grain-mill (`doc/places/grain_mill.md`); exactly fitting the **Anchor stone** the Pair finished and hid at the desert site.
- **The system documentation** is here. A single bound volume in a sealed-stone niche — the Pair's *operating manual* for the system, written for whoever might restart it after the rift. The manual names the Anchor's location, the sphinx-tradition that watches it, and the working that completes the system. **Reading it is the adventure's hinge** — the party now knows where to go and what to do when they get there.
- **A small sealed jar** beside the manual contains a piece of Stormweave woven by one of the Pair (Special Material; `doc/claude/ITEMS.md`). Not the system's piece — a personal token, the Pair's small kindness to the future. It is given.
- **A second working-bench** in a side-alcove holds an old-order seal stamp (Medallion *(authority)* Material variant; the Pair's seal recognises a bearer at any of the era's surviving sites — the Press-house, the Grain-mill, the Lethran dwelling, the Anchor itself). A party that has the stamp moves through these places more easily.

## Lore the place contributes

A party that reaches the Generator and reads what it carries learns specific pieces of the world's deep lore that no other site delivers in full:

- **The Pair as practitioners** — names, working-tradition, Vintala-line connection (the south-wall mural; the Pair-tradition signed on the leftmost figure's hem). *Cross-references Felicia's pre-rift identity.*
- **The chaos's wrong-build pattern** — what the chaos thinks the system needs; the migration's *destination* and what it has been trying to make. The clearest single demonstration of the chaos as *confused, not malicious*.
- **The system's purpose** — the Pair's documentation at the heart names *what they were trying to spare the world from* and what *completion* would have done. The system was meant to *manage* chaos-pressure across the rift's outer pulses; it was not anti-rift, it was *post-rift* preparation.
- **Old-order seizing names** — the wall opposite the bench carries two new signatures that extend the Lightning-Tower correspondence at Ornalis's cottage. A campaign mapping the seizing faction has its third and fourth names here.
- **The Lethran Pair's child** — referenced in the wax-cylinder letter; cross-confirms the Anchor's entry-corridor script.
- **The chaos's *sadness*** — by encountering the Almost-Construct settled, a party reads chaos in a register most worlds never get: *the wrong-build is grief, not malice*. This is the campaign's most affecting lore-revelation about the chaos spirits' nature.

## Creatures by level

- **Ground floor:** 3–5 reassembled puppet-guards (Skeletal Guard mechanic, chest-knot variant); occasional Stitchling scout

## DM notes

- **The construct is the adventure's emotional weight, not a fight.** Run it as *something patient and sad that has been trying for a long time*. A DM who plays it as a boss-monster loses what the Lethran Pair's correspondence is for — the construct is the chaos's grief at the unfinished work.
- **The Memory Shade is the bridge.** Settled, it shows the party the Pair at the bench in their last days — readable as one or two short fragments per visit. A DM running this slowly across multiple visits gives the players the Pair as people before the Anchor delivers them as the heart.
- **The puppet-guards are *not* random encounters.** Each is a specific reassembly; *Reading* the chest-knot identifies *which migration piece this guard was built around* (a Wright fragment from the Press-house; a Commons-House clerk's sash; a child's puppet from Three Walls' sister-village). Killing one cleanly returns *that piece* to its origin community in spirit if not in fact.
- **The unfinished socket is the campaign's hinge.** A party that reaches Level 3 *can leave the socket alone*. The completion path requires bringing the Anchor stone back here. The destruction path requires the Anchor stone here as catalyst. The carry path uses the documentation but not the socket. Do not foreclose any of the three.
- **The chaos almost-construct settled stays settled** for the rest of the campaign unless something at scale wakes it again. A DM running the long campaign can use *the absence of the wrong-build* as the quiet barometer of a region beginning to recover.

## Open questions

- The construct's *original* purpose — what shape was the chaos trying to build, exactly? The Pair's notebooks describe the Generator's role as the *active* end; what the chaos has been building looks superficially right and is structurally wrong in a way the documentation makes legible only at the heart.
- Whether the construct, fully settled, can be *taught* to do something useful for the shamans' long plan. Wilder will have views.
- What the wooden man's full body, if assembled here, would have done. Haven returns the parts. The construct's wrong-build had different plans.
