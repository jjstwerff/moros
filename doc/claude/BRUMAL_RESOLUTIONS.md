# Brumal Resolutions — four paths and how the world influences them

Brumal's collapse is a campaign-scale tension thread: a neighbouring kingdom being eaten by desert, driven by chaos spirits migrated out of the blasted lands, with refugees reaching Allondo and a second war brewing on the border. (See `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"The Neighbour's Crisis" and §"Campaign Threads — Brumal's collapse" for context.)

**There is no single canonical resolution.** The thread resolves in any of **four distinct paths**, each fully valid and each with its own peak-register. The party's choices, standing, and accumulated resources determine which path — or combination of paths — they pursue.

Crucially, **what the party has achieved elsewhere in the campaign changes what is possible for Brumal**. Laurent's city risen for one last flight becomes an asset. The portal closed by the shamans' long plan removes the ecological driver entirely. Felicia's drive extending to Brumalians enables the spirit-corridor. Haven's trust earned grants access to puppet-labour. Thorgal's mirror bonded opens an overseas channel. **Moros's world-specifics are not backdrop — they are levers on the Brumal arc.**

This document lays out the four paths and catalogues the world-specific influencers for each.

## Design principles

- **Parallel, not exclusive.** The party can pursue multiple paths simultaneously or sequentially. Partial success on one compensates for setbacks on another.
- **Partial resolutions accepted.** A path that fails to close completely still contributes. The campaign does not require a clean sweep.
- **Paths combine in natural patterns.** Political + Spiritual integrates Brumal as a transformed kingdom with a shielded land. Political + Infiltration reshapes both courts without public spectacle. Military + any other limits the damage of war-fought. Spiritual + Infiltration rescues the right people and holds the land.
- **World-specifics accelerate, complicate, or transform each path.** What the party has done *elsewhere* determines what resources, threats, and opportunities they carry into Brumal's resolution. No path is complete without awareness of these levers.
- **Each path has a peak setpiece.** Designed per the coordination-roadmap principle: 5–8 tasks in shared space, different powers, group-required. See `doc/claude/COORDINATION_ROADMAP.md` §"The campaign's spine."
- **No path is the right one.** They differ in cost profile, not in legitimacy. A DM should let the party steer toward whichever register fits their character.

## Path 1 — Political resolution

**Register:** Negotiation, earned standing, public scandal as diplomatic lever. Courtroom scenes; garden walks; back-room tavern arrangements.

**Lead NPCs:** Prince Corven, Princess Penelope, King Hannes, Queen Fienna (quietly), Lady Rhianna Linthrope (as fixer), Father Elliot (as corrective standing), Didrich (whose reaction shapes the court).

**Key locations:** Scarlet Vale court (`doc/places/scarlet_vale.md`), Cliffside Hold (Brumal's capital — currently no place file), Linar harbour as neutral venue (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md`), Luchebert Monastery for Elliot's legitimacy (`doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`).

### Primary peak — the public acknowledgement

After the party has facilitated Corven and Penelope across sessions — carrying messages, covering night rides, escorting Penelope safely — the moment comes when the relationship is forced into view. Either Corven declares at Scarlet Vale's audience chamber; or the two meet at Linar harbour with both courts' retinues present; or Penelope is formally received at Hannes's court as a royal emissary and the deeper bond becomes undeniable.

**Task palette (6–7):**

| Task | Power/Stat | What it prevents |
|---|---|---|
| Present the principals correctly | *Politics* | The court dismissing it as scandal, not diplomacy |
| Vouch through Penelope's Brumal lineage | *Clan* (via Rhianna's standing) | The match being read as illegitimate across borders |
| Hold Hannes's composure at the reveal | *Relaxed* | A father's reaction tipping into a king's refusal |
| Read the hard-liner faction's response | Perc + *Hearing* | Missing the counter-move brewing in court |
| Offer terms (marriage-treaty, joint-kingdom, or novel structure) | *Ingenuity* | A diplomatic hole the courts cannot fill themselves |
| Protect Penelope during the crisis hours | *Shield* or *Portage* | Hardliners acting on her physically |
| Broker Didrich's reaction | *Clan* or *Politics* | Didrich fracturing from his brother |

**Closes:** The war-brewing thread. Brumal and Allondo linked by succession rather than separated by border.
**Opens:** Two-kingdom integration politics. Clan-loyalty tensions across the former border. Queen Fienna's non-royal example vindicated retroactively. Didrich's future role.
**Partial-failure shape:** Hannes accepts the relationship publicly but refuses formal union; the war is deferred, not closed; Brumal continues to collapse ecologically even as the border stays peaceful.

### World-specific influencers

- **Laurent's flying city, post last-flight trigger.** If the party has broken the six shackles and the city is rising, *it is a diplomatic fact the courts cannot ignore*. A flying city above Scarlet Vale at the moment of the reveal forces the conversation to *scale*. The court cannot dismiss what is visible from every window. Laurent himself, present or remembered, gives the scene gravitas — a pre-Rift prince whose own succession-renunciation is being quoted by Corven's uncle next door.
- **Thorgal's mirror, if recovered and sheltered.** The overseas faction's potential intervention in the Brumal-Allondo question becomes an offstage pressure the party can reveal or withhold. Brumal's own mage-tradition (if it maintains any) would register the mirror as a Scarlet Vale asset of overseas origin — complicating or strengthening the negotiation, depending on how it is presented.
- **Fata morgana jewelry as a marriage-token.** Penelope's apprenticeship there gives her access to **inner-work ward-jewelry** — pieces carrying genuine spirit-protection. A wedding-gift of such pieces makes the marriage materially significant: the union *delivers* ward-work into Allondo's royal line. The court reads this as a Brumal-Fata-morgana-Allondo triangle the hardliners cannot argue against.
- **A chaos-spirit incident at the politically critical moment.** If the party has witnessed desert-creep firsthand (Steadington; a chaos-eruption on the march) and can testify, the hardliners' military frame is publicly undermined. A visible manifestation during the audience — a fata-morgana vision drifting through Scarlet Vale's streets on the day of the reveal — closes the argument. The ecology is the threat; the war is Hannes's distraction.
- **Felicia's drive acknowledged.** If the party has brought Brumal refugees to Hank's farm, to Luchebert, and Felicia has been named as already protecting them (her rewrite explicitly extends her drive to new comers), her moral legitimacy *backs the marriage*. Willow at Luchebert, Elliot as abbot — both can be asked to witness the union formally. A marriage blessed by Felicia's own priesthood is harder for the hardliners to frame as foreign.
- **Haven's support, quietly.** If the party has earned Haven's trust and brought them news of their scattered kin, Haven can lend silent authority — overseas-descended figures testifying (through the Linar harbour settled-overseas community) that the Brumal-Allondo question cannot be resolved without the descendant-communities having a voice. Adds weight the courts lack the standing to dismiss.

## Path 2 — Spiritual resolution

**Register:** Shamanic, Druid, Religion, Mage. The spirit-shield ecology at work. A multi-faction ritual-deployment that halts the desert's advance by extending the protection-arrangement.

**Lead NPCs:** Felicia (through Willow; via necklace-condensation or ritual), Fata morgana's inner circle, Wilder, Rowan (druidic-order legitimacy), Penelope as Fata-morgana-trained bridge. Possibly the surviving bound fire elemental (if befriended) and the light elemental (if freed).

**Key locations:** Moth Hollow (condensation with Felicia; `doc/places/moth_hollow.md`), Fata morgana (inner circle negotiation; `doc/places/fata_morgana.md`), Brumal's southern border (the deployment site — specific stretch, DM-chosen), Luchebert as anchor (`doc/places/luchebert_monastery.md`), Elmsfield's ironwood tree as the northern pole of the arrangement.

### Primary peak — the spirit-corridor deployment

A multi-faction working establishes a shielded line across Brumal's southern border, analogous to Fata morgana's deployment but corridor-shaped rather than city-shaped. The working binds together: Felicia's drive (explicitly extending to Brumalians — her rewrite acknowledged this); Fata morgana's teaching delegation; Wilder's shamanic lineage; druidic-order acknowledgement (via Rowan); Willow as Felicia's hands; possibly named bound-spirits (fire elemental from the volcano tower; light elemental from the darkness tower) as anchor-points.

**Task palette (7–8):**

| Task | Power/Stat | What it prevents |
|---|---|---|
| Carry Felicia's consent through a condensation | *Shamanic* + the necklace near an anchor | The arrangement lacking her legitimating drive |
| Negotiate Fata morgana's teaching delegation | *Politics* + earned standing (+ Penelope's vouch) | Exported technique refused, delayed, or conditional |
| Secure druidic-order acknowledgement | *Politics* with Rowan + Diederik's seal | The order treating the work as illegitimate |
| Stage the reader-ring at the border | *Druid*, *Mage*, *Priest* — all staffed | The arrangement reading incomplete to chaos spirits |
| Stand vigil during the binding | *Relaxed* + *Religion* | Breaks in register during the working |
| Manage the physical site | *Shield* + *Portage* | Brumal opportunists or Allondo hardliners disrupting |
| Read the chaos-spirits' response | *Shamanic* | Missing the moment when the spirits test the line |
| Anchor named bound-spirits (if present) | Individual-elemental register (heat-patterns; light-register) | Breaking the bound-spirits' own trust |

**Closes:** The desert's advance — halted at a shielded line. Brumal's south preserved; collapse slows.
**Opens:** Who staffs the corridor across generations. Felicia's drive now formally reaches a wider "her people." Fata morgana's outward turn as a campaign-scale shift. The question of whether the portal-close (shamans' long plan) becomes redundant or complementary.
**Partial-failure shape:** The corridor works partially — buys Brumal a generation, not permanence. Refugees still flow; fewer. Fata morgana extracts more concessions than the party wanted to give. Some reader-stations unstaffed; weak points in the line.

### World-specific influencers

- **Felicia's necklace, carried from Willow's shrine.** The **condensation trigger** for the binding's central moment. Without it, the ritual's Felicia-anchor requires Moth Hollow as the venue — impractical at scale. With it, the binding can be held anywhere.
- **Fata morgana's protection-technique, formally taught.** The core mechanism. Without Fata morgana's consent, the spiritual path cannot complete — the technique is not reinventable on a campaign timeline. The negotiation for this consent is itself the earlier half of this path.
- **The shamans' long plan / portal-close.** **Complementary, not competing.** If the party is also pursuing the shamans' long plan, the portal-close provides root-cause resolution. The spirit-corridor handles the short-term (holding the line); the portal-close handles the long-term (removing the driver). A campaign doing both achieves *restoration*, not just *halt*.
- **Laurent's flying city as delivery vehicle.** If the last-flight is triggered, the city is the largest pre-Rift mage-work capable of carrying a reader-ring into place. A city-scale deployment along Brumal's southern border is possible *only* if the city flies. Bittersweet: the city's parting flight could be *this flight*, the corridor's establishment as Laurent's final journey.
- **The Elmsfield ironwood tree as a spirit-pole.** The great tree is part of Felicia's network (established in LORE). If used as a northern anchor for the corridor, the arrangement gains structural stability — but **pollination** of the tree (if Diederik's overseas-plan has succeeded) would complicate: fruiting-gases would periodically weaken the anchor. The party must time the deployment to tree-cycle.
- **Haven's puppets as permanent staff.** A reader-ring needs *ongoing* staffing. Haven's spirit-bound puppets, offered willingly (if the party has earned that level of trust), can serve as *indefinite* staff — beings who do not tire, do not age in the way humans do, and whose presence reads to chaos spirits as mighty-adjacent. This is the cleanest long-term staffing solution.
- **The pot-people at the buried tower.** Kin-by-construct-type to Haven's population. If the party has earned the pot-boy's trust and he (or the furnace) participate in the corridor working, their specific register is the buried-tower lineage's re-engagement with the world. Wilfred would consult.
- **Moth Hollow as the working's Felicia-anchor.** If the condensation is performed at the hollow directly — at the centre where she stays — the working is *more stable* than one done elsewhere with the necklace. The cost: the hollow is a small space and cannot accommodate the full delegation. A staging-ground is needed.
- **Tod as an inverse teacher.** Per his file, the party can *teach* Tod things about structure. If the party has done this work and reduced the forbidden-woods chaos-pressure measurably, the spirit-corridor has less *atmospheric* pressure to hold. Tod cannot leave the woods, but his reduced influence reduces the whole region's chaos-density.
- **The bound fire and light elementals (if befriended / freed).** If the volcano tower's elemental has been befriended, it can be offered as a *fire-anchor* in the corridor — a major point of mighty-spirit presence. If the darkness tower's light elemental is allied, it is the light-pole. Elementals formally consenting to anchor the corridor is a larger commitment than briefly helping; their presence elevates the whole arrangement.

## Path 3 — Military resolution

**Register:** Combat at scale. **Moros allows this but weighs it.** The compassion-first frame does not rule out war; it rules out *enjoying* war. The party's role in this path is not to win; it is to *fight it better* — civilians protected, specific excesses prevented, terms offered at the end.

**Lead NPCs:** King Hannes and his war council, Prince Corven as field-commander (even if the Political path is not pursued, he has the training Hannes requires), Didrich (who may push too hard), Brumal's desperate generals (DM-fill — a senior commander pushed into the offensive by circumstance; a noble house trying to hold Cliffside), Raul (if he has re-entered the campaign as an overseas lever), Brother Darrel at Luchebert (the prior-war conscience).

**Key locations:** The border defense-line (specific site, DM-chosen — likely a gap in the southern hills where the desert-creep has opened passable terrain); Cliffside Hold if invasion proceeds; a specific field-battle site; Hannes's war-room at Scarlet Vale; the Linar trade road (caravan-bandit pre-war flashpoints).

### Primary peak — the border engagement

Brumal pushes across the border under desperation-pressure; Allondo meets them. The party is present in a specific capacity — attached to Corven's command, at a critical hold-point, on the flank managing refugees, or embedded with Brumal trying to prevent excesses from their side. The scene is combat, but the tasks that matter are the ones *beyond* combat.

**Task palette (7–8):**

| Task | Power/Stat | What it prevents |
|---|---|---|
| Hold the line against the push | *Shield* + Might + Endu **dig in** | The line collapsing at a fragile point |
| Coordinate retreat for non-combatants | *Portage* + Char **strategy** | Civilian casualties in the fallback |
| Read the field | Perc + *Lookout* | Missing the commander's real move |
| Draw a key enemy commander into a negotiation window | *Scolding* + *Politics* | The general dying instead of being captured |
| Prevent war-crimes by either side | A specific watcher-party-member | Atrocity that the party carries forward |
| Offer terms to cornered units | *Politics* + *Relaxed* | Annihilation of a force that would have surrendered |
| Tend the wounded on both sides | Healer | The casualties compounding into generational grief |
| Manage the post-fight register | *Relaxed* + *Religion* | The aftermath fracturing into revenge |

**Closes:** The war. Brumal defeated at the border, or Brumal's push halted; a formal treaty; the territory question settled by arms.
**Opens:** Post-war reconstruction. Clan-divided loyalties across the former border. The grief of both sides. Darrel's specific attention (he walked away from the *last* war; this one's echoes find him at the monastery). Felicia's drive registering new dead.
**Partial-failure shape:** The war fought to a stalemate; neither kingdom wins; refugees multiply; the ecological driver continues unchecked. The party has shed blood and closed nothing.

**The compassion-first framing of this path:** The party's role is not to *win*; it is to *fight it better*. A party that fights Moros-style in this path is doing work that only fighters-with-conscience can do. The DM should reward that work visibly — a saved commander who later becomes a Brumal-Allondo liaison; a non-combatant family whose survival becomes a later refuge-arc; a prevented atrocity that Felicia specifically marks.

### World-specific influencers

- **Laurent's flying city as air support.** If the last-flight is available, the city provides reconnaissance and — potentially — direct intervention. **Morally heavy.** A flying city dropping alcove-puppets onto a battlefield is Laurent's ancient faction's tactics inverted. The party that uses it this way has *taken* something specific from the setting, and the DM should let the weight land. Laurent himself will have views on being used this way.
- **Haven's puppets as fortification-workers.** Silent, tireless, ideal for digging defensive lines, raising walls, repairing breaches overnight. Haven will agree *only* if the use is explicitly defensive and civilians are the primary beneficiaries. Offensive use is a betrayal; the trust collapses.
- **Ironwood weapons and armour.** If the mill has been restored (or ironwood blanks from it recovered), pre-Rift quality weapons become available to a select few. *Moros is cautious about rewarding this.* The weapons give a specific advantage; the advantage is weighed against the cost of using pre-Rift material for violence. Diederik would notice. Irna would notice.
- **Felicia's corridor (if also active).** If the Spiritual path has established a partial corridor, the corridor *protects civilians during the combat*. The battle happens in front of the line; refugees shelter behind it. A DM running this combination rewards the party's integrated work.
- **The volcano tower's fire elemental (if befriended).** An ally capable of extraordinary localised force. **Do not deploy casually.** The elemental's consent is specific; it will fight alongside a side it has been asked to help, but it distinguishes *defensive* use (protecting a specific group) from *aggressive* use (destroying an enemy force). Aggressive use breaks the friendship permanently.
- **Chaos-spirit eruptions during battle.** Unpredictable. A battle fought on ground the desert has just taken can be interrupted by a fata-morgana drift, a dust-dervish crossing the field, a chaos-pressure spike that makes both sides' workings fail. Moros's ecology is a *third combatant* neither side controls. The DM should narrate this as *the world refusing to be used for war*.
- **The Storm Mage's crashed Lightning Tower at the forbidden woods' fringe.** If the party has triggered his clarity-bout and the tower has crashed there, the crash-site is a secondary flashpoint. Tod may notice a nearby military force. The chaos-pressure from the forbidden woods reaches the border-corridor. Not an ally; a complicating factor.
- **Thorgal's mirror as reconnaissance.** If bonded by a mage-aspirant, the mirror offers overseas perspective — a faction-view Brumal and Allondo both lack. Bonding during a war is ethically freighted; the mirror-bond is not a military tool in the clean sense.
- **Skull Gatherers arriving on the battlefield.** If the party has had contact, the gatherers may appear after a significant engagement to collect mage-spirits drifting from the dead. Their presence is *disturbing to both sides*; their intentions are their own. A party that has earned their standing can ask them for specific information or favours.

## Path 4 — Infiltration resolution

**Register:** Stealth, Sly, Hearing, Adaptation, Night. Information as weapon. Specific people as leverage. The war *does not start* or collapses from within — no one admits why.

**Lead NPCs:** Nadine (her Finch merchant network — the backbone), Felix (scout who knows routes into Brumal), Penelope (if the party has earned her trust as a Brumal-side coordinator), Raul (if active; overseas lever), Bridget (trader-network intelligence from her old road-years), a new Brumal-side contact (DM-fill — a disaffected noble, a Brumalian intelligence officer, a turned general).

**Key locations:** Brumal's political underworld (cities, taverns, noble houses); Cliffside Hold's secondary entrances (the kitchens, the stables, the chapel); Linar harbour as staging-point (`doc/places/linar_harbour.md`); the trade road as the communication channel; Nadine's meeting-places across the continent.

### Primary peak — the Cliffside operation (or its equivalent)

The party moves unseen. The specific operation varies by campaign:

- **Extraction** — pull specific Brumal figures out before collapse reaches them: Penelope's family; disaffected generals who want to switch sides; hostage-held civilians.
- **Sabotage** — disrupt war-preparation without declaring war: fouling a stockpile; spreading information that reframes the border-push; blackmailing a hardliner at Hannes's court or Brumal's.
- **Intelligence-trade** — gather what both sides do not admit they know: Brumal's true food situation; Allondo's real military readiness; the overseas faction's position. Delivered at the right moments, this information bends the war-calculation.
- **A specific assassination** — canonically discouraged by Moros's register, not ruled out. A Brumal general who will *start* the war; a hardliner at Hannes's court manipulating him. Costly; morally weighted; leaves traces.

**Task palette (7–8):**

| Task | Power/Stat | What it prevents |
|---|---|---|
| Scout the target site | *Lookout* + *Travels* | Surprises during the operation |
| Cover approach | *Adaptation* + *Night* + *Scurry* | Detection before the move |
| Run the operation | *Sly* + Hand + specialist power | The operation failing in execution |
| Manage the asset | *Politics* (defector) or Healer (extraction) | Losing the asset after acquiring them |
| Extract cleanly | *Sprint* + boat-handling + falsified documentation | Trail back to the party |
| Carry the consequences | Nadine's network (background); Felix's routes (transport) | Traces surfacing later |
| Read the political pulse after | *Hearing* + *Politics* | Missing the reshuffle the operation caused |
| Protect the party's own cover | The party collectively | Any member being traced |

**Closes:** The war, quietly. No formal peace. The war simply *does not start* or collapses without anyone admitting why. Brumal's elite reshaped; Allondo's hardliners outmanoeuvred.
**Opens:** Nadine's network as a permanent campaign-scale asset. Whatever the party promised in exchange. The question of what was done in whose name. Later revelations (history sometimes catches up to covert work). The ethical residue the party carries.
**Partial-failure shape:** An operation blown. A death that did not have to happen. The target achieved but with witnesses. A compromised agent needing permanent sheltering at Luchebert or Haven.

### World-specific influencers

- **Thorgal's mirror as long-range communication.** If bonded by a party-mage, the mirror allows *real-time* communication between infiltrators separated across continents. An extraction team at Cliffside coordinates with a diversion team at Linar. **The mirror is not subtle** — workings-sensitive figures detect the bonding-activity. Use sparingly.
- **Fata morgana's ward-work jewelry.** Pieces carrying genuine spirit-concealment. A party-member wearing an inner-work piece is harder to detect by spirit-sensitive means. Not invisibility; *diminished spirit-signature*. Penelope has access through her apprenticeship; Nadine's network could acquire pieces at price.
- **Haven's puppets as indefatigable couriers.** A Haven-kin carrying a message across the continent does not tire, does not stop for sleep, does not speak to anyone on the way. Haven will agree *only* to missions they read as non-violent. Messengers, not agents.
- **Nadine's merchant network.** The backbone. Finch traders across Moros, with safe-houses, route-knowledge, and specific contacts at every major settlement. Nadine personally coordinates if the mission aligns with her Linnet arc or with the merchant group's interests. *Not every mission qualifies.*
- **Felix's war-scout knowledge.** Felix knows routes, names, and specific sympathisers from the last war. A party that has earned his trust can ask him for specific doors — former overseas soldiers in Linar willing to help; Allondo-side hill-folk who know the border passes; specific figures who owe him favours.
- **Pirate-nest intelligence** (from Nadine or directly). If the party has closed the Linnet extraction arc, the network that supplied the nest's intelligence is now available for broader infiltration. Operating inside Brumal without detection becomes much easier when the party controls a covert channel into the southern coast.
- **Chaos-pressure zones as concealment.** Moving through the forbidden woods' fringe or the blasted lands' edge hides the party from ordinary pursuit — few pursuers will follow. **Not safe** — the party takes the chaos-pressure themselves. A shaman in the party makes the crossing survivable; without one, the infiltration route itself becomes a cost.
- **Adaptation/Night powers via befriended creatures.** The hippogriff's quiet-company presence (if the party has it) provides aerial reconnaissance in a form no one is looking for. A dust-dervish carrying a note between ruins leaves no trail. A freed light elemental holding a dark passage open is a travel-mechanic only this party can deploy.
- **Laurent's alcove puppets, post-accord.** If the party has reached accord with Laurent, a small number of alcove-puppets can be deployed as *agents*. They are slow, but they do not sleep, do not panic, and can carry a recognisable face (mask and robe) to a target site. *Laurent must consent* to each deployment. He will not deploy them for offensive work.
- **Moth Hollow as a safe-house register.** A party-member injured in an operation can be brought to Moth Hollow and rested there; Felicia's attention *shelters* them. Willow is the gatekeeper. **Not a permanent refuge** — the hollow cannot be used as a base — but a recovery point after a rough extraction.

## How the paths combine

### Natural combinations

- **Political + Spiritual = the Integrationist combination.** Corven's marriage-alliance closes the political question; Felicia's corridor + Fata morgana's technique closes the ecological. Brumal survives as a kingdom; the desert is halted; refugees integrate under dual-kingdom structure. **The cleanest long-term shape.**
- **Political + Infiltration = the Statecraft combination.** Corven's public reveal plus Nadine's covert work reshaping both courts' hardliners. The public scandal is the visible move; the quiet reshuffle is the real one. Efficient; morally ambiguous.
- **Spiritual + Infiltration = the Quiet-Rescue combination.** Felicia's drive-extension plus specific extractions of key Brumalian figures for safekeeping at Luchebert. The land is held; the people who need saving individually are saved. *Nobody claims credit publicly.*
- **Military + any other = the Damage-Limitation combination.** The war is fought, *but*: Corven's succession-concern limits its scope; Felicia's corridor protects civilians; Nadine's extractions preserve key figures for post-war reconstruction. The fight happens; the cost is contained.
- **All four simultaneously = the Saturated resolution.** Rare. Requires a long campaign and a party willing to work at multiple registers. The most expensive path; the most complete.

### Combining with the shamans' long plan (portal-close)

The shamans' long plan is a *separate* campaign-climax that intersects the Brumal thread. Closing the portal does not immediately save Brumal — the desert's retreat is slow, years-long — but it removes the driver. A campaign that closes the portal *and* pursues any Brumal resolution is doing **restoration-scale** work: both the immediate kingdom and the long-term ecology.

### Combining with the last flight

Laurent's city rising for its one final journey is Brumal-adjacent in its possibilities:

- **As Political lever:** the flying city above Scarlet Vale at the moment of Corven's reveal.
- **As Spiritual delivery vehicle:** the city carrying the corridor-working into place.
- **As Military tool:** morally heavy; the city as air support (Laurent's consent required).
- **As Infiltration cover:** the city as an *impossible-to-intercept* transport for extraction ops.

The last flight is Laurent's parting. Using it for Brumal is a specific choice about what the parting *does*.

### Combining with other campaign threads

- **The scattered-kin arc (Haven).** A completed scattered-kin return strengthens Haven's willingness to contribute labour, couriers, or corridor-staff to Brumal resolution.
- **The pirate-nest extraction (Nadine's Linnet).** A completed Linnet arc gives Nadine's full network availability for Brumal work.
- **The Bean-arc resolution.** Ron released + Irna's prophecy correctly understood gives Rhianna leverage at Hannes's court that benefits Political or Infiltration paths.
- **Tod-teaching across visits.** Reduced chaos-pressure in the region; makes any desert-halting arrangement easier.
- **The Bridget's-hoard opening.** If the party has surfaced the wartime hoard inside the ironwood tree, Bridget has resources she can contribute — including funding Hank's farm during refugee absorption.

## Campaign structural implications

- **CAMPAIGN.md's Campaign Threads table** needs Brumal's collapse updated to reference all four paths.
- **Each path needs its peak setpiece written** when the campaign approaches it (Tier 2–3 in COORDINATION_ROADMAP.md).
- **The DM should track which paths are active.** A party juggling Political + Spiritual is doing a different campaign from one committed to Military alone. The session-planning implication is that *approach scenes* differ by path — what the party is doing *between* peaks changes.
- **Path-triggering scenes matter.** What commits the party to one path over another? A conversation with Corven opens Political; a condensation with Felicia opens Spiritual; Hannes's war-council invitation opens Military; a back-channel approach from Nadine opens Infiltration. These triggers are as important as the peaks.
- **Pre-conditions determine availability.** A party that has not earned Fata morgana's standing cannot pursue Spiritual; a party without Nadine's trust cannot pursue Infiltration; a party without Corven and Penelope established cannot pursue Political; a party without Hannes's war-council awareness cannot pursue Military. **Earn the path, then walk it.**

## Open questions

- **Cliffside Hold as a place file.** The Brumal capital is referenced throughout but has no dedicated file. Writing it would strengthen Political and Infiltration paths especially. DM-fill until a campaign reaches it.
- **A named Brumal-side contact.** The Infiltration path benefits from a specific named figure (a disaffected noble, a turned general). DM-fill — could be promoted to a full NPC file if the campaign approaches.
- **Penelope's agency.** Her file is historical-format (per the audit). A full scene-first rewrite would strengthen all four paths — she is a bridge NPC in each. Priority candidate.
- **Raul's role if he returns.** The overseas lever. An active Raul complicates all four paths.
- **The precise mechanism of exporting Fata morgana's technique.** Canon is cautious about whether it can travel cleanly. DM-fill the specifics; the Spiritual path requires at least a partial export.
