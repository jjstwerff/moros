# Situations

Short scene-seeds where the encounter is **the situation a creature is in**, not the bestiary entry. The mother-bear-with-cubs in Session 2 is the campaign's worked model: she is not a forest-monster; she is a mother with cubs in pain, and the party's response is shaped by *that*, not by her stats.

This file collects **six situations** and **four broker-scenes** — specific moments that a DM can drop into a session when the campaign needs a creature-encounter that carries emotional weight. Each is written as a set-piece: what the party sees, what the weight is, what the options are.

Creatures referenced here are in `doc/claude/CREATURES.md`; regions in `doc/places/`; the compassion-first register in `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"Tone."

## Situations

### 1. The chimera-mother with an injured cub

**Region:** Southern desert. Any sanded ruin where a chimera-lineage is resident (see `doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At the Half-Tower" as the established worked site, but usable elsewhere).

**What the party sees:** A chimera crouched low over a cub. The cub is still. One hind leg is clearly broken — bone visible, the angle wrong. The mother is hunched around him, tail-coil protective, wings half-raised. She will not leave. She cannot carry him safely with the leg the way it is.

She *watches* the party, not hunting. The threat-display is there, but there is no step forward. She is *defending without pursuing*. The cub breathes but does not move.

**Weight:** A mother with a child who cannot travel. The same register as the Session-2 bear, shifted to the chimera lineage the campaign has built out. The party who helps has real consequence with the region's chimeras; the party who fights has killed a mother over her injured child and the sphinxes will not speak to them again.

**Options:**
- **Druid — heal.** A working that sets the leg and closes the break. Costly; the mother must *allow* the approach, which takes patient offering first. Success earns real standing with the lineage.
- **Hunter — distract.** Draw the mother away for a few minutes; a second party-member sets the leg without her watching. Risky. If she senses the deception, the scene becomes combat.
- **Relaxed — wait.** Stay at distance for hours; offer food; do nothing. Some mothers heal cubs over days. The party's presence as *witness* without intervention can be the gift.
- **Pass — witness only.** Walk past with respect. Send a shaman later. Not all problems are the party's to solve.
- **Fight — ill-considered.** She loses. She takes party-members with her. The cub dies.

**After:** A healed cub grows. A chimera lineage that has been helped remembers the party's scent. A shaman who hears of this work reads the region's chimera-register differently thereafter.

### 2. The waiting puppet

**Region:** Blasted lands. A specific smaller ruin not yet named in a place file — a press-house, a communal-hall outbuilding, a field-shrine. DM places it.

**What the party sees:** A single iron puppet standing in an otherwise empty ruin. Not damaged. Not moving. *Standing*. The dust around its feet is undisturbed — it has been in this spot for a long time. Up close, the puppet's eye-slits track them.

It does not attack. It does not approach. It *waits*.

A *Shamanic* or *Druid* character feels what it is waiting for: the puppet is looking for **a specific kind of traveller**. DM-picks from these options (let one be true for the campaign):
- A traveller who has been to Haven.
- A traveller carrying a specific item (a seal-ring from the ironwood mill; a piece of another kin).
- A traveller who knows a specific name.
- A traveller with a specific power (Shamanic, Druid, Mage).

**Weight:** A being who has stood in one place for centuries because the message it carries is for someone it has not yet met. The party may *be* that someone. Or may not.

**Options:**
- **Approach and present.** A party member who fits the waited-for description earns the message. The puppet *opens* — speaks (if capable) or gestures — and delivers. The message is DM-fill: a location, a name, a warning.
- **Approach and do not fit.** The puppet resumes waiting. Does not attack. Does not speak. The party learns only that it is waiting and that they are not its audience.
- **Ask what it waits for.** A *Shamanic* character can ask. The puppet answers, in its register, with the shape of the waited-for traveller. The party can then choose whether to find that traveller and bring them.
- **Move the puppet.** Possible physically (it is heavy but not rooted) — but offensive. The puppet becomes hostile if moved; its wait is its purpose.

**After:** A puppet that delivers its message may walk away, or fall still for the final time, or begin a new journey. A puppet that was not met fits into the scattered-kin arc — see `doc/places/scattered_kin.md`.

### 3. The fire elemental deciding

**Region:** Wherever a befriended fire elemental has been travelling with the party (usually from the volcano tower, post-befriend; see `doc/places/volcano_tower.md`). At a chosen scene — a camp-fire under a clear sky, the foot of the mountain range, a specific view.

**What the party sees:** The elemental has been in its brazier for the last several sessions. This evening, without announcement, it *rises*. Not aggressively; not leaving. It *stands* in the fire — visible in full, taller than the party remembers it being. It is looking east.

A *Shamanic* character hears it first, in heat-patterns clearer than they have been: *"I have decided. I am going to the mountain range."*

**Weight:** A companion telling the party it is time. The decision is the elemental's; the party's role is to *witness* and to respond.

**Options:**
- **Accompany for a stretch.** A day or two toward the mountain. The elemental is warmer on the road than it has been. At the foothills, it turns and walks up alone. The party watches it go.
- **Release at the camp.** The elemental rises from the brazier, drifts east across the sky, goes. A parting in one scene.
- **Ask it to stay.** Possible. The elemental will stay if asked seriously. But *staying past its decision* weighs on it; within a few sessions, it dims visibly, and the party will see that asking was wrong.
- **Ask it to carry a message.** If the party has someone in the mountain range — DM-fill: a smith, a hermit, a distant settlement — the elemental can deliver before it settles. It does this gladly as a parting work.

**After:** The mountain range has a fire-spirit in it now. A party later reaching those mountains finds the elemental at a specific peak or forge. It recognises them. The reunion is its own scene.

### 4. The cerberus whose last kin has died

**Region:** Blasted lands. A specific second communal building somewhere past Commons House (DM picks). Smaller than the Commons House cerberus.

**What the party sees:** A *two-headed* cerberus at the door of a smaller communal hall. It is correct in posture, correct in attention, correct in duty — *except that it is only two-headed*. The third head is *gone*. There is no wound; the third head is simply not there and has not been for a while.

A careful look shows a fourth socket on the neck where a fourth head once was. This building had a *four-headed* cerberus, of a kind the records do not remember. It is down to two. One of the surviving heads is slower than the other.

**Weight:** A guardian *alone* in a way it cannot articulate. Its duty remains; its *kin* are gone. It does not know what to do with that gap because it cannot conceive of the gap as anything other than *the door I guard*.

**Options:**
- **Address it in its register.** It responds; it stands aside; passage is granted. But it does not ask for anything.
- **Ask about the missing heads.** The cerberus cannot answer. A *Shamanic* character senses the absence directly — and senses that the cerberus is *still listening for them*.
- **Bring it company.** Sit with it for a scene. The two remaining heads turn toward the party one at a time. This is a small grace the party can give.
- **Release it.** The hardest option. Speaking the truth — *you are alone; you are the last* — is the ending that allows the cerberus to discharge its duty. The scene is *parting*. The door becomes unguarded after, and what moves in is the next scene.
- **Fight it.** Possible; costly; a fight against a kin-less guardian that cannot run away. The party that kills it kills the last active person of a building no one else has named.

**After:** A cerberus given company becomes a regular of its hall again. A cerberus released fades slowly across a season; the hall empties. A cerberus unmet continues to guard, waiting.

### 5. The warped helper remembering

**Region:** Blasted lands. A spirit the party has been *un-warping* across sessions — Silvertrace (see `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Silvertrace") is the worked example, but any warped helper-spirit fits.

**What the party sees:** The spirit, accustomed now to the party's returns, responds *differently* on this visit. The swallowing-ground is smaller. The temple offerings, long stale, are fresher. The water has *come back* in a thin rivulet at the bed's edge. A *Shamanic* character senses the spirit is **confused**: it is remembering what it used to do, and that memory does not match what it has become.

It reaches — in its register — for the party. Not for help in the sense of rescue. For *confirmation*. *"Am I what I was?"*

**Weight:** A spirit halfway through un-warping. Across sessions, the party has done this work. This scene is the *recognition* — the moment the spirit names what it is becoming.

**Options:**
- **Confirm.** Name the spirit aloud (*"Silvertrace"*, or the DM's equivalent). Speak the spirit's old function. The spirit *accepts*; the un-warping continues; the stream comes back further next visit.
- **Perform a small ritual in its old register.** If the party has the offerings — a coin, a carved token, the specific ritual-spoon from the altar — a priest or shaman can complete a rite. The spirit's register stabilises.
- **Abandon at this stage.** If the party does not return in reasonable time, the spirit's confusion becomes acute and may regress. A partial un-warping is possible; it is more brittle than a full one.
- **Bring a descendant of the original community.** A party that has learned the village's names (at Three Walls, at the community records) can find living descendants elsewhere. Bringing one to the spirit completes the un-warping in a scene.

**After:** A spirit un-warped is *helpful again*. Wilder will notice the change in the regional reading; Willow will sense it at the shrine; the field the spirit served becomes growable again. The party has restored a small piece of the region.

### 6. The sphinx with news

**Region:** Southern desert. A sphinx the party has spoken with before (usually the pair at Three Walls; see `doc/places/southern_desert.md`). The party returns to the village ruin for their own reasons — a different retrieval, a pass-through, a shelter from storm.

**What the party sees:** The sphinx is already at the edge of the village on their approach. Not watching; *waiting*. When the party is in hearing-range, she speaks first, without preamble — which is the first time a sphinx has not been addressed first.

She has news. She has been holding it for the party's return.

**Weight:** The sphinx as *witness and archivist*. She has seen something in the party's absence — a descendant passing through; the serpent matriarch dying; a new traveller at a nearby ruin; a creeping pattern across the sand. She chose to carry it for them.

**Options:**
- **Listen and pay the riddle-cost.** The sphinx speaks news in her register. A question follows — a riddle the party must answer truthfully. Good answers receive the full news. Evasions receive the news in fragments.
- **Thank her and act.** A party that takes the news seriously and does something about it (carries it to Haven, goes to the new traveller, tends the serpent's clutch if the matriarch is dead) returns later to a warmer welcome.
- **Listen and do nothing.** The sphinx has delivered; she does not resent inaction. She will carry less next time.
- **Offer her a token.** A sphinx accepts *story-shaped* offerings — a verse, a sung line, a carved token. A party that gives properly makes her a contact the campaign can return to deliberately.

**After:** A sphinx who is being *used* as a news-channel responds with more news over time. She has been waiting for someone to come back and listen. The party, once established, can ask her what she has heard from the sand.

## Broker scenes

Scenes where the party mediates between two creatures or two creature-registers. Each short; each a *scene*, not a full arc.

### A. The cerberus and the upper-floor shrine (Commons House)

**Setup:** At Commons House (see `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Commons House"), the cerberus guards the door. On the upper floor, a smaller spirit attends the hall's shrine — the eating-place deity, the granny-figure the community's children were presented to. A candle that has burned for a millennium.

**The gap:** The cerberus does not know the shrine-spirit is still present. The shrine-spirit does not know the cerberus is still on duty. Both have been alone for a thousand years *in the same building*.

**The broker-move:** A party who has met both can tell each about the other. The DM should slow the scene.
- Telling the cerberus: *"The hall is not empty. There is a spirit on the upper floor. She has been tending the candle since the community left."* The cerberus's three heads turn to each other. It does not fully understand. It *acknowledges*.
- Telling the shrine-spirit: *"The door is still guarded. The cerberus has been at his post since the community left."* The spirit — warmly — says she had hoped so. She will now tend the candle for *two*.

**Result:** Neither can leave to meet the other — both are site-bound. But both know. The building's loneliness *lessens*. A party returning later finds the cerberus steadier, the shrine's candle brighter.

### B. Haven's puppets and the Great Thresh elemental

**Setup:** Haven's iron-puppet community (see `doc/places/haven.md`) and the Great Thresh's bound iron elemental (see `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At the Great Thresh") are **kin in construct-type but have never met**. Haven's puppets know of the threshing-works only as a place their ancestors fed grain to. The Great Thresh's elemental has memories of the community that fed the stones — but Haven was founded after the rift, and the elemental does not know this.

**The gap:** The elemental walks its circuit, grinding nothing, remembering a community that lives on in reconfigured form at Haven. Haven's puppets, told of the elemental, would recognise *the memory of their ancestors' work*.

**The broker-move:** The party can carry the elemental's remembered community-roster (assembled over visits — see `doc/places/blasted_lands.md`) to Haven. Haven's council reads the names. They match pre-Rift ancestry lists the puppets kept. *Haven's puppets are, in part, the descendants of the workers the elemental remembers.*

A party that carries this across both ways — tells the elemental that its community still exists (as Haven) and tells Haven that their ancestors' elemental is still walking — has brokered a scene neither side could reach on their own.

**Result:** Haven may send a delegation to the Great Thresh. The delegation would be the elemental's first contact with descendants of its community in a thousand years. The scene is quiet, slow, and one of the campaign's rarest forms of grace.

### C. The sphinxes and the desert chimeras

**Setup:** The sphinxes and the chimeras of the desert share the same ruins and do not share much else. Sphinxes are intelligent; chimeras are territorial-with-family; the sphinxes sometimes *vouch* for respectful travellers with the chimeras, and sometimes *don't*. The relationship is not hostile but is not coordinated.

**The gap:** When a chimera-lineage suffers — a mother with an injured cub (see situation 1); a nest threatened by a flock-mother running out of prey — the sphinxes know but cannot help directly. They lack hands and medicine. The chimeras know the sphinxes see but cannot ask for help in a register the sphinxes can process.

**The broker-move:** The party carries *between*. *"The sphinxes at Three Walls saw the mother's cub — they sent me to set the leg."* *"The chimera-mother at the Half-Tower is grateful — tell the sphinxes who asked."* The party becomes the hands-and-voice that pass between the two.

**Result:** Across a campaign, a party who brokers between sphinxes and chimeras regularly ends up with standing in *both* kin-groups. The sphinxes begin vouching for them *by default* with chimeras elsewhere; the chimeras recognise them even at ruins they have not visited. The desert opens to them further than to most travellers.

### D. The volcano tower's fire elemental and the forbidden woods' Tod

**Setup:** The volcano tower's fire elemental (post-befriend; see `doc/places/volcano_tower.md`) is a *bound elemental that has been freed to will*. Tod in the forbidden woods (see `doc/npcs/tod.md`) is a *chaos spirit approximating humanity*. They occupy adjacent positions on the spirit-ecology spectrum, and neither knows the other exists.

**The gap:** The elemental's memories of binding and freedom are exactly the thing Tod is trying to understand about himself. Tod's approximation-speech and inverse-teaching practice is exactly the kind of sustained relational work the elemental has never done. They are potentially *teachers to each other*.

**The broker-move:** The party, having travelled with the fire elemental (see CREATURE_COMPANIONS.md) and having visited Tod enough to know him, arranges a meeting. Neither will travel to the other's home directly — the elemental cannot cross into the forbidden woods without dimming; Tod cannot leave the woods. **The meeting is at the edge.**

A shaman (the party's own, or Wilder mediating) facilitates. The fire elemental speaks in heat-patterns; Tod answers in approximation-speech; the party translates between the two registers where needed. The conversation is awkward at first — neither has protocol. Across an afternoon it becomes substantive.

**Result:** Tod learns something about freedom that his previous interlocutors could not teach. The fire elemental learns something about approximation-without-binding. Both are *changed* — lightly, slowly, the way Moros rewards work.

Wilder, if he sees the scene, says only that it was *good to watch*. That is among his higher register of praise.

## Using this file

**The situations:** drop one into a session when the campaign needs a creature-scene with weight. They are portable — most can run at the DM's choice of location within the stated region.

**The broker scenes:** these are longer-arc rewards for parties that have engaged multiple threads. Use them when the party has standing in both sides of the brokered relationship.

**Not a checklist.** A campaign does not have to run all of these. Run the ones whose register fits the session's pacing. The others remain available.

**The register across all of them:** compassion-first; emotional weight that earns its place; creatures as *beings in specific contexts*, not encounters in a list. A DM who writes new situations following this pattern can extend the file indefinitely — the shape is the key, not the count.
