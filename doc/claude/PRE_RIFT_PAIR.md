# The Pre-Rift Pair — multi-session adventure plan

A 6–8 session adventure spanning the **broken lands** and the **southern desert**, structured around two big dungeons (a paired pre-rift laboratory) and the regional dungeons that connect them. The campaign-thread the puppet-parts migration has been gesturing at since `blasted_lands.md` finally answers; the desert's archaeological grief and the broken lands' relational grief are revealed as two halves of the *same* unfinished pre-rift work.

This plan is a *spine to work through*, not a finished script. Each beat is sketched at the level a DM can run from; the specific names, monsters, and exact mechanics are DM-fill where they aren't already in canon.

---

## The spine

A pre-rift mage-pair — call them **the Lethran Pair** (DM may rename) — built a paired chaos-management system in the decades before the rift. Two laboratories, paired as *generator* and *anchor*: one in what is now the broken lands (the working end, near the white city), one in what is now the southern desert (the anchor end, in pre-desert agricultural country). The system was **incomplete when the rift hit**. The generator end ran for some hours unsupported, then collapsed.

After the rift, the chaos spirits *felt* the unfinished generator structure and have been **trying to complete it** for centuries — with whatever they could lay hands on. The puppet-parts migration in the broken lands is the chaos *wrong-building* what it thinks the system needs. The Commons House wall-panels that were *taken* are part of the same impulse: the chaos is gathering community-traces and puppet-bodies into a shape that is the wrong shape because it has never seen the original.

The party's discovery: there *is* an original. The desert end. **Buried, unbroken, waiting**.

The party's choice — **three honest endings**, none clean:
- **Complete the system** with the desert half restored to the broken lands. Stabilises some chaos-pressure regionally; massive undertaking; ties to the **shamans' long plan** (Wilder, Gerhald) and to **Felicia**'s drive extending southward. The system was *meant* to be finished.
- **Destroy both halves** deliberately. The chaos stops *seeking* a shape; the broken lands' pressure does not lessen but does stop migrating. Quieter ending; loses what was once almost-built.
- **Carry the desert half to a third party** — Wilder's network, Gerhald, the Skull Gatherers, Luchebert. The party becomes the carrier, not the decider. The decision waits.

## The two big dungeons

### The Generator — at the white city's centre, broken lands

The pre-rift working laboratory of the Lethran Pair. **Three levels deep into the white city's outer ruin.** The chaos has been *building toward* this place for centuries; the Generator is a working corpse with new parts grown over the old.

- **Level 1: the outer hall.** Pre-rift architecture half-eaten by the chaos's wrong-construction. Reassembled puppet-parts walking the corridors as guards (Skeletal Guard mechanic adapted; the parts are not bones but iron-puppet limbs, so the *Hand* → breastbone-working flag becomes *Hand* → gathering-knot at the chest). The migration's destination is here; the moved Commons House panels are stacked along the walls.
- **Level 2: the working chamber.** The original pre-rift work-space, partly preserved. The Lethran Pair's notebooks (water-damaged, partly readable) explain the *generator* role; the ledger names the desert pair-site by location. **A bound chaos-construct**, built by the chaos itself across centuries from puppet-parts and community-records, is *here* — not a chaos elemental but a slow, sad, almost-coherent thing trying to be the missing half. *Shaman* can address it; combat is possible but heavy.
- **Level 3: the heart.** Where the system would have terminated. **Unfinished.** The *socket* for the missing piece is visible — a stone fitting designed to receive a specific working-stone the desert end was supposed to bring. The party finds the socket, the documentation, the address. **The chaos almost-construct is in the room below; a party that has settled it can study the socket safely. A party that hasn't is racing.**

**Find:** the Lethran Pair's working journal (third volume, the one that names the desert site); a piece of pre-rift mage-craft a serious mage student earns a real piece of practice from; **the location the desert end is buried at**.

### The Anchor — sister-site in the desert

A buried laboratory in pre-desert agricultural country, sanded over completely; **accessible only by Sphinx-mediation or by reading the broken-lands documentation**. The desert has *protected* it — the chaos cannot easily reach it across the sphinx-watched ruins.

- **The approach:** through a sanded ridge that hides the entrance. A **sphinx** sits above it, has been watching for centuries, and **knows what it is**. The party that addresses the sphinx properly (`doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At Three Walls" register) earns the way in.
- **The entry corridor:** small, dry, intact. Pre-rift architecture as the Pair built it. *No chaos* — the desert's spiritual register doesn't carry the warping that drives the broken-lands wrong-build.
- **The working chamber:** matched to the Generator's. The Pair's *receiver* notebooks (clean, dry, fully readable) explain the system's *anchor* role and what completion was supposed to do. **A small bound water-spirit** still runs the room's preservation working (parallels the Tidehold's well-spirit; cf. `doc/places/tidehold.md`). Respectful address through *Shaman* keeps the room intact.
- **The heart:** a small stone fitting holding the *original missing piece* — the **Anchor stone**. Carved with the Pair's working-mark. Functions as a Special Material — DM-fill on which existing material it most closely matches; *Hearthstone* or a new pre-rift variant fits. The room also holds the **Pair's correspondence** with each other across the months before the rift, which is the adventure's emotional heart — two practitioners writing letters about whether they will finish in time. They did not.

**Find:** the Anchor stone (the missing half); the correspondence (one of the campaign's most affecting documents); a party that completes the system has it; a party that destroys both halves uses the stone as part of the destruction.

## Regional dungeons along the way

Six smaller sites that connect the two big dungeons through the spine. Most are existing canonical places extended; two are new.

### In the broken lands

1. **Silvertrace** (existing — `blasted_lands.md`). The party's first encounter with the migration; the wooden man's parts surface here. Silvertrace's warped stream-spirit *has been seeing pieces pass through its catchment*. A *Shaman* asking respectfully gets a fragment of the migration's direction.
2. **Commons House** (existing). The emptied wall-panels are the second-clearest evidence of the chaos *gathering* community-traces. **The party that returns to Commons House later in the arc** finds the panels at the white city — closing the loop.
3. **The Press-house ridge** (new — recommended in §"Recommended scope" of the dungeons audit). A pre-rift vineyard hall on a ridge; warped wine-spirit at the stone vats. The Press-house's underground store-rooms hold *the Lethran Pair's regional supply-cache* — early-rift workings stored there for the laboratory's use, left mid-shipment. A party finds working-tools, a sealed letter to one of the Pair, and confirmation the Pair was *near here* as the rift came. (Plan to write `doc/places/press_house.md` if used.)
4. **The Grain-mill** (new — alternative regional site). Pre-rift agricultural-side counterpart to the ironwood mill. A bound earth-spirit at the millstones; the mill ground stone for the Pair's working-pieces. The party finds raw stone-blanks of the type the Anchor-stone is cut from, confirming the materials came from this side. (Plan to write `doc/places/grain_mill.md` if used.)

### Crossing — at the southern frontier

5. **Lastwater** (existing — `southern_desert.md`). Travel-rest before the desert proper. Local rumour about *the buried laboratory* the sand has hidden for a millennium; an old keeper at Lastwater knows the sphinx's territory and can point the party. The Sphinx has been waiting; Lastwater's keeper has been waiting for someone to ask the right question.

### In the desert

6. **Three Walls** (existing). Sand-ruin Serpent matriarch and the buried well-cache; a sphinx watches. **Three Walls' sphinx is the same sphinx-tradition that watches the Anchor.** A party that handled Three Walls well earns the *correct address* for the Anchor's sphinx; a party that handled it badly faces a closed door. (Cross-reference: `doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At Three Walls.")
7. **Half-Tower** (existing). Pre-rift fallen castle; air-elemental and chimera lineage. **The Lethran Pair owned a property near the Half-Tower's ridge** in pre-rift days — a small holding mentioned in their correspondence at the Anchor. A party that cross-references the correspondence with the Half-Tower's surviving inscriptions reads the Pair's signature.

### Optional, deeper

8. **A pre-rift mage-dwelling** (new — fills the existing `southern_desert.md` §"At a mage-dwelling — DM-fill location" gap). Could be **the surviving home of one of the Lethran Pair**, kept partly preserved by a Pair-working that still runs. The party finds personal things — a child's toy; an unfinished embroidery; the half-eaten supper the day the rift came — that the desert protected. *Char* and *Will* are the powers that matter here; this is a place the party feels rather than fights. (Plan to write `doc/places/lethran_dwelling.md` if used.)

## Session-by-session outline

**6–8 sessions, depending on pace.** Each beat is one session unless flagged otherwise.

### Session 1 — the migration becomes legible

- Party is invited (by **Wilder**, **Haven**, or a returning ruin-crawler) to look at a fresh sighting of moved puppet-parts.
- **Silvertrace** scene: the warped stream-spirit responds to *Shaman* questioning; party learns the parts are *gathering toward the white city*.
- **Hook:** Haven asks for the parts to come home; Wilder asks for the *direction* and the *destination*; the party has reasons of their own.

### Session 2 — the migration's middle

- Party tracks pieces across **Commons House** and a regional ruin. Encounter chaos-fauna at the road; reach the Press-house ridge or the Grain-mill.
- The regional ruin produces the *first concrete reference* to the Lethran Pair — a working-tool, a letter, a stone-blank.
- **Bridging research:** party returns briefly to **Luchebert library** or to **Rhianna**'s residence to identify what they found. *Reading* and *Char* matter here. Father Elliot recognises the Pair's seal from a fragment in the library; Rhianna confirms the line.

### Session 3 — the white city's outer ruins

- Party crosses into the white city's outer halls. Chaos-pressure thickens. Encounter a **wrong-construct** built from the migration's parts.
- Reaches the **Generator**, Level 1. The reassembled puppet-guards and the moved Commons House panels are *here*, finally located.

### Session 4 — the Generator (big dungeon, broken lands)

- **Levels 2 and 3.** The party enters the working chamber, reads the Lethran Pair's notebooks, decides how to handle the chaos almost-construct in the heart.
- Climax: the party stands at the unfinished socket. They have the address of the desert site.
- **Outcome:** the party leaves with the third-volume journal and the desert's location; the chaos almost-construct is settled, contained, or temporarily broken (campaign-decision).

### Session 5 — south, through the canon

- **Travel session** through the trade road and into the desert. Lastwater's old keeper points them at the sphinx-watched ridge. The party may stop at **Three Walls** along the way, where their handling of the matriarch and her sphinx earns or denies the *correct address* for the Anchor's sphinx.
- Optional: **the Lethran dwelling** — a place the party feels, not fights. They find the family's life. The Pair had children.

### Session 6 — the Anchor (big dungeon, desert)

- Party reaches the Anchor's sphinx. *Char* and *Will* test for entry.
- Inside: the Pair's correspondence; the Anchor stone; the system's full documentation. **No combat unless the party brings it** — the desert's quiet preserves the room. The water-spirit at the room's preservation working may need addressing through *Shaman* if the party plans to leave with anything material.

### Session 7 — the choosing

- Return journey. **Three threads to pull**:
  - **Wilder and Gerhald** can be brought into the decision through the shamans' long plan; *Shaman* matters here. The pair-system completion may align with the long plan's late-game.
  - **Felicia and Willow** can be brought in through the necklace path; if Path 2 (spiritual corridor) of the Brumal resolutions is open, the system may align with that too.
  - **Laurent** can be consulted through earned standing; he *recognises* the Pair's seal from his own pre-rift practice and has views.
- Party makes their choice — complete, destroy, or carry.

### Session 8 — the act

- Whichever path: the climactic scene. **Complete the system**: a sustained working at both ends with the Anchor stone in the socket; party defends the working from chaos-construct interference. **Destroy both halves**: a slow, costly working that uses the Anchor stone as catalyst; the destruction is mournful, not triumphant. **Carry**: an extended scene of handover at the third-party's location — Wilder's hilltop, Gerhald's tower, Luchebert's library, the Skull Gatherers' necropolis through Andras.

## Pacing and DM notes

- **The system is broken; do not pretend the party can fix everything.** Even the *complete* path leaves much un-fixed. The register is *the unfinished work was real, and the world has been wrong without it*.
- **The chaos almost-construct at the Generator's heart is the adventure's most dangerous combat.** Run it as a *Shaman*-able encounter that becomes a fight only if address fails or the party is impatient.
- **The desert side is a rest from the broken lands' grief.** Make the Anchor's interior *quiet* — the desert preserves; the chaos has not reached here. The find-pace is reading and feeling, not fighting.
- **The Pair's correspondence is the heart.** Two practitioners writing letters about whether they will finish in time. They did not. A DM running these letters slowly — one per visit; the last one read at the Anchor's heart — gives the players what the system was *for*.
- **The choice is not a vote.** The party debates and decides; whichever they choose, the world responds in scale. Do not pre-load a "correct" answer.
- **8 sessions is the long form. 6 is achievable** by combining session 5 with the Anchor's approach and folding session 7's research-choosing into session 8's act. A faster pacing loses the Lethran dwelling and one regional ruin; the Generator and Anchor remain.
