# Moros — DM Staging: NPC Introduction Order and Reveal Layers

## The core principle

**Moros has a dense cast** (34 named NPCs for the opening region). Introducing all of them at once would overwhelm the table. The setting is built to *prevent* that — if you let it work.

The principle is: **NPCs are introduced by role first, name second, depth third.** A first encounter meets a *function* (the fearful farmer at the gate, the old druid who calms the crowd). The *name* lands on a second mention, when the party has a reason to remember it. *Depth* — the NPC's secret, their teachable skill, their interior life — emerges only when the party has earned the relationship.

This maps cleanly onto Moros's explicit **layered places** design principle from CAMPAIGN.md. A location is not solvable in one session; neither is an NPC. Both reward return.

### The five rules

1. **Role before name.** "A loud farmer shouting at the gate" is what the party meets. "Farmer Joseph" is what they remember on a second scene.
2. **Spokesperson pattern.** In a crowd, one NPC speaks. The rest are present but unnamed.
3. **Bundled introductions.** Wilfred enters as *Bridget's husband who runs the farm*. No new mental slot.
4. **Offscreen until relevant.** Ron is not mentioned until Irna mentions him. Felicia's name is not offered until Willow offers it. Penelope does not exist in play until Corven's secret starts surfacing.
5. **Recurring wins over one-shot.** Three NPCs met four times each hold more weight than twelve NPCs met once.

## Session-by-session staging

Target: **no more than 4 new named NPCs per session**, peaking around 6 in the Luchebert monastery session. NPCs the party has already met can recur freely.

### Session 1 — Elmsfield Gate

**New NPCs introduced:** Irna Bean, her dog (unnamed), Joseph, Elder Diederik. Four.

**Already in scene but backgrounded:** Bridget and Wilfred (only surface; no depth) if the party sleeps at the Inn.

**Not yet mentioned by anyone:** everyone else in Elmsfield, every NPC outside Elmsfield.

### Session 2 — River South

**New NPCs:** None named. The mother bear and her cubs are *creatures*, not social NPCs.

Breathing room. The Session 1 cast consolidates in memory.

### Session 3 — The Buried Tower

**New NPCs:** The Pot-Boy; the Furnace. Two.

Both are kin, both introduced in the same scene. One mental slot.

### Session 4 — Return and Recovery

**New NPCs:** None in person. But **Irna names her relationships in conversation** — John Bean (father), Ron (brother), Lady Rhianna Linthrope (her advocate), Steven (the prince she attacked), Father Elliot (her expected destination). These are *names attached to a story she tells*, not present NPCs. Players hear them; they don't meet them.

### Session 5+ — Vale and Monastery

**New NPCs (spread across 2–3 sessions of monastery-arc play):**

- **Father Elliot** — first encounter; reveals the monastery's official saint (Luchebert) but *not* Felicia.
- **Brother Darrel** — if the user decided he's at Luchebert; possible second-visit reveal.
- Other monks (unnamed spokesperson-pattern).

**Layered reveal — only on earned return:**

- **Willow** — the Felicia truth-keeper. First visit: the party may see an old rabbit granny but not understand her role. Second or third visit, with demonstrated trustworthiness: she lets them near the shrine.

### Session 5+ — Laurent's Wreck

**New NPC:** Laurent, when the party chooses to enter the wreck.

### Session 5+ — Lightning Tower

**New NPC:** The Storm Mage, in the hills. First contact is difficult (he's broken); the "bond" can be earned over time.

### Session 5+ — Other Towers

**New NPC:** The Hermit Shaman, at their tower. Other towers introduce their own NPCs once characterised.

### Session 5+ — Elmsfield on return

The party's first visit to Elmsfield only met 4 named NPCs. A return visit (or two) naturally surfaces:

- **Hank** and **Egbert** — if the party takes interest in the orphanage on the town's edge.
- **Corné Dunham** — if they ask for a valley guide.
- **Ralph Overhill** — if they need someone to watch animals.
- **Eric Randell** — if he chooses to drop by with Gato-walker.
- **Nadine** — when the DM wants world news delivered.

**Two to three per return visit** is the natural cadence. Elmsfield gets richer each time the party comes back.

### Sessions 6–10 — The Royal Court at Scarlet Vale

When the campaign moves to Allondo's capital (for the Ron enlistment situation, for the Rhianna connection, for post-Bean-resolution political follow-up):

- **Lady Rhianna Linthrope** — introduced as Irna's advocate; the iron-lady surface is the first thing the party sees.
- **King Hannes** — only when the party is at court in a capacity that puts them in front of him.
- **Queen Fienna** — observing, usually silent in public.
- **Prince Corven** — visible before he is open.
- **Steven** — the prince Irna attacked; encounter is fraught.
- **Ron Bean** — in the training yard; closed off; only opens when Irna's story arrives.

**Layered reveal:**

- **Penelope** — not in Scarlet vale. Not mentioned. Enters the campaign only when Corven opens, which takes earned trust and real time. Late-campaign NPC.

### Sessions 8+ — World Edge (northern coast)

If the party goes north:

- **Master Darius** — at his forge district.
- **Remi** — if his wandering has taken him south on the trade roads and the party crosses him. Possibly met *before* Darius.

### Late campaign — The Portal and Beyond

- **Thorgal** — encountered only when the mirror is found (somewhere in a forest; DM places).
- **Raul** — only if the campaign returns to the overseas thread; DM-fill.

### Historical — never in person

- **Narissa** — through her diary, if found or referenced.
- **Saint Luchebert** — through Willow's telling.
- **Felicia** — only as a great spirit, through Willow's shrine-tending, potentially through a condensation ritual.

### Never named until relevant

- **Tod** — encountered in the forbidden woods. Given as the setting's gentle chaos-spirit introduction.

## Cards — dual purpose

The card system (see `doc/npcs/cards.md` and `doc/places/cards.md`) serves **two functions**:

- **Player-facing** — cards are issued to the party as earned standing. A card is a record of *relationship* (for NPCs) or *local standing* (for places). It travels with the party and reminds them of who/where they know.
- **DM-facing** — the full deck stays with the DM as a **rumour-reference toolkit**. When a rumour about an NPC or a place comes up at the table — a character drops a name, a stranger mentions a town — the DM pulls the relevant card instead of flipping through full files. The card carries the shape a stranger would know, which is exactly what the scene needs in that moment.

## Card issuance — when players earn them

Cards are not issued on *meeting* an NPC (or passing through a place). They are issued when the NPC or location would recognise the party as **someone specific** — not a stranger who did a thing, but a known quantity whose standing has shifted.

Concrete triggers:

- **The NPC tells the party something they would not tell a stranger.** (Willow mentioning Felicia's name. Corven's first oblique mention of Penelope. Egbert admitting she is a wolf.) This is the typical gate.
- **The NPC chooses to travel with the party, teach them, or shelter them.** An active mode of engagement.
- **The NPC has been aided or harmed in a specific, memorable way** — enough that they would remark on the party by name if asked.

When any of these triggers fires, the DM hands over the NPC's card. The party now has a *record of the relationship*, and can refer to it for what the NPC teaches, what they know, what they hide.

The number of cards a party has accumulated is the clearest measure of campaign depth: a party with 15 cards has meaningful standing across the setting; a party with 3 is still early.

## Pacing notes

- **Session 1 is the one exception** to the card-earning rule. Meeting the Session 1 NPCs well enough to know their names probably earns *two* cards (Irna, Diederik) by session's end. Joseph is a harder card — the party has to reconcile with him, not just shout him down.
- **The five rules above should be explicit to the DM** because the setting is *structurally* built to support them, but a DM under time pressure will default to introducing too much too soon. This doc exists to resist that.
- **New campaign directors reading this before running their first session** should aim for **no more than 3 card-earned relationships in the first two sessions combined**. The campaign earns its depth over time.

## How to use this document

1. Before each session, review the NPCs the party has card-earned and the NPCs they have met without cards.
2. Identify any *NPC whose card is ready to be earned this session* — typically one, occasionally two.
3. Identify any *NPC who should be introduced but not fully revealed* — keep them at first-contact level.
4. Keep offstage anyone who is not relevant to the session's arc.

The goal is that at the end of every session, the party feels they have *earned something* about the setting's people, without feeling like they just got introduced to a new wing of a dormitory.
