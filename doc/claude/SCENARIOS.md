# Moros — Scenarios

Each scenario presents a distinct type of situation the group must navigate together. Scenarios
are not exclusive — a travel scene may turn into combat, a market visit may become a negotiation.
The tension rules and challenges below apply whenever that scenario's conditions dominate.

For tension mechanics and card rules see [RULES.md — Core Mechanics](RULES.md#core-mechanics).
For which powers apply in which scenarios see [POWERS.md](POWERS.md).
For statistics and their scenario uses see [STATISTICS.md](STATISTICS.md).

---

## Combat

The group is trying to get the upper hand in a struggle with another group.

**Why tension exists:** Every card passed to a struggling ally, every shout of warning, every
split-second coordination draws attention and costs composure. Helping each other is what keeps
the group alive — but it is also what makes the fight increasingly chaotic and dangerous.

**When tension rises:** Reinforcements arrive or enemies regroup. A flanking threat appears from
an unexpected direction. A cornered enemy fights with nothing left to lose. The environment turns
hostile — fire spreads, a structure gives way, the crowd closes in.

**To ease tension:** Defeat or drive off enough enemies to change the odds. Use *Scolding* to
consolidate enemy attention onto one character, reducing simultaneous threats. Use *Inspire* to
let the group act more efficiently. Use *Blur* or *Sprint* to reposition and break out of a
losing situation.

**Challenges**

**Enemies hold prepared high ground or a fortified chokepoint.** Attacking into the position
costs extra actions and removes most defensive options.
- *Climber* or *Flight* — reach the high ground directly and collapse the advantage from above
- *Charge* — crash through the chokepoint before the defenders can react; the prone special
  disrupts their formation
- Perc **weak point** — identify a gap in the position before committing; an ally can exploit it
- *Sly* — feign retreat to draw defenders out of position and onto open ground

**The group is outnumbered and enemies are coordinating flanks.** Fighting normally means
someone is always undefended.
- *Scolding* — pull all enemy attention onto one character, collapsing the flanking pressure
- Char **strategy** *(Char ≥ 3)* — let every ally add another card, compensating for the
  numerical disadvantage with combined force
- *Balance* — erratic movement makes the character a hard target regardless of how many attack
- Shield **block** — protect an adjacent ally without raising Tension; the one form of aid that
  costs nothing on the tension track

**A group member is downed mid-fight and cannot continue.** Every round they lie there is a
round someone must spend protecting them or the fight worsens.
- *Blood* — borrow life energy from a nearby source to restore the downed character's ability
  to act
- Endu **second wind** *(Endu ≥ 4)* — the downed character discards their hand and draws three
  fresh cards; any Endu shown may be added to their recovery
- Char **rallying cry** *(Char ≥ 5)* — every ally recalls one card from their discard, buying
  the downed character time to recover
- *Religion* — bless the fallen character before the next blow lands

**Something must be protected during the fight — a person, an object, a door.** Splitting
attention between fighting and protecting divides the group's effectiveness.
- Shield **block** — cover the protected thing directly without spending a separate action
- *Scolding* — draw all aggression away from the thing being protected toward a single
  character who can absorb it
- *Jaws* or *Claw* — silence or grab anyone trying to reach the protected target
- *Sly* — convince enemies the thing they want is elsewhere, buying time without fighting for it

**The environment turns against the group — fire spreading, structure collapsing, crowd
closing in.** The fight now has a third participant that does not take damage.
- Speed **sprint** *(Speed ≥ 3)* — move to any position in the scene without spending an
  action; escape the environmental effect before it peaks
- *Nimble* — burst movement on all fours through terrain that slows everyone else
- *Fur* or *Hide* — absorb environmental damage that would stop a less protected character
- *Ingenuity* — improvise a way to use the environment against enemies instead of fleeing it

**An enemy uses fear, poison, charm, or a power that directly targets the mind.** Normal
combat actions do not address the effect; it must be countered directly.
- Will **steel the mind** *(Will ≥ 3)* — negate a fear, charm, or confusion effect on yourself
  or an adjacent ally immediately
- *Religion* — bless allies before the effect lands; the bless absorbs one mental attack
- *Blood* — purge a poison or draining effect by redirecting the life energy away
- *Relaxed* — calm presence is not immune, but it reduces the severity of panic spreading
  through the group

## Stealth

The group tries to avoid the attention of a powerful monster or patrol.

**Why tension exists:** The group must move and communicate — a whispered warning, a hand signal,
passing a route around an obstacle — and every one of those moments risks giving the group away.
The more players help each other navigate, the more noise and movement accumulates.

**When tension rises:** The monster or patrol grows alert and begins actively searching. Safe
windows of movement narrow. A character gets separated. A hiding spot becomes exposed or shared
by too many people.

**To ease tension:** Stop moving entirely and wait for the threat to settle. Use *Night*,
*Adaptation*, or *Scurry* to slip through without needing to coordinate. Use *Scolding* to
deliberately draw the monster toward one character who has a prepared escape, clearing the path
for the rest.

**Challenges**

**A patrol changes route unexpectedly and cuts across the group's planned path.** The safe
window the group was counting on has closed; moving now risks detection.
- *Hearing* — hear the patrol's new direction before it arrives; the group has time to adjust
- *Lookout* — spot the route change visually before the patrol rounds the corner
- *Smell* — detect the patrol's presence by scent before any sound or sight gives it away
- *Labyrinth* — instantly identify an alternate route through the space without hesitation

**A group member makes a sound at the wrong moment — a clatter, a sneeze, a misplaced foot.**
The threat has heard something; it is now alert but has not yet confirmed the group's position.
- Dex **action** — act fast before the threat investigates; reach cover before it turns around
- *Sly* — produce a convincing alternative explanation for the sound before the threat commits
- *Adaptation* — step further into shadow immediately; being partially seen is not being caught
- *Scurry* — burst to a new position silently before the threat reaches the source of the noise

**The only viable route passes through a lit area or open ground with no cover.** There is
nowhere to hide; moving means being visible.
- *Night* — see clearly and move in darkness others cannot use; create your own cover with shadow
- *Adaptation* — move through increasingly undetected regardless of the light level
- *Flight* — approach from above, where the patrol is not looking
- *Swimmer* — if water is present, approach via the one route that leaves no trail or sound

**An animal — a guard dog, a bird, a stabled horse — detects the group before any person does.**
Animals alert to threat differently than people and cannot be bluffed.
- *Druid* — communicate directly with the animal; a druid can calm a guard dog before it barks
- *Smell* — understand what the animal has detected and whether the group can move upwind of it
- *Relaxed* — calm presence does not arouse animal suspicion the way nervous movement does
- *Sly* — create a distraction that redirects the animal's attention before it alerts its handler

**A group member cannot move quietly and is slowing everyone's progress.** Their noise and
pace are the limiting factor; the group can only move as quietly as its noisiest member.
- *Portage* — carry the noisy equipment or the struggling character; silence the source directly
- *Scurry* or *Nimble* — the quieter members move ahead and clear the path so the noisy one
  needs to cover less distance
- *Ingenuity* — improvise padding, a muffling wrap, or a solution that silences the problem item
- *Scolding* — accept the role of deliberate distraction; draw the threat away so the group's
  noise no longer matters

**The hiding spot is compromised — discovered, too small, shared by too many, or about to be
searched.** The group must move under the worst possible conditions.
- *Adaptation* — slip past the searcher at the moment they look away; the power is made for
  exactly this
- *Scurry* — burst to a new position in the gap between one search sweep and the next
- *Night* — use darkness the searcher cannot penetrate to cross ground they believe is covered
- *Control* — compress or alter form to fit a space others cannot

## Negotiation

Finding common ground with another group that has different interests.

**Why tension exists:** Every party member who intervenes, adds an argument, or tries to tip the
balance shifts the emotional temperature of the room. Helping an ally make their point is useful
but reads as pressure to the other side.

**When tension rises:** The other party's willingness to engage closes off. Positions harden,
concessions are withdrawn, and someone reaches for leverage or a weapon. A third party with its
own interests appears and complicates the table.

**To ease tension:** Offer a genuine concession — give something before asking for something.
Use *Relaxed* to bring the room's temperature down. Use *Politics* to find the shared interest
that makes agreement feel safe again. Use *Musical* to shift the emotional tone before words do
more damage.

**Challenges**

**The other party does not recognise the group's authority, credentials, or right to negotiate.**
Without standing, every argument lands weaker than it should.
- *Clan* — invoke shared kin ties; being of the same people opens a door no credential can
- *Religion* — invoke divine or institutional authority that exists outside individual standing
- Char **strategy** *(Char ≥ 3)* — make the group's collective weight of voice undeniable
- *Sly* — construct a credible identity or framing that gives the group the standing it needs

**A cultural taboo or local custom has been violated — accidentally, before the group knew.**
The other party is now offended; continuing as if nothing happened will not work.
- *Relaxed* — radiate calm and genuine regret; the offense is acknowledged without escalating
- *Politics* — understand immediately what was violated and what the correct repair looks like
- *Clan* — invoke shared identity as proof that the violation was ignorance, not contempt
- *Musical* — shift the emotional temperature of the room before the offence hardens into a
  position

**A third party arrives with opposing interests and tries to derail or hijack the negotiation.**
The table now has three parties with different goals, and the group cannot control the room.
- *Politics* — identify the shared interest between all three parties and reframe the discussion
- *Sly* — redirect the third party's attention to a different grievance or opportunity
- Perc **weak point** — identify what the third party actually wants; use it as leverage
- *Hearing* — overhear the third party's private conversation before they enter the room

**The other party makes a demand the group cannot or will not meet.** Refusing outright collapses
the negotiation; accepting creates a different problem.
- *Sly* — reframe the refusal as a different kind of offer; redirect toward what can be given
- *Politics* — find the interest beneath the demand and offer something that satisfies it instead
- Hand **improvise tool** *(Hand ≥ 4)* — produce a tangible alternative on the spot that the
  other party did not know they could have
- *Ingenuity* — construct an arrangement from available parts that no one had considered

**Someone across the table is lying and the group needs to know what is actually true.** The
negotiation cannot proceed safely on false premises.
- Perc **called shot** *(Perc ≥ 4)* — declare before the action that the goal is to expose the
  lie; success reveals it
- *Hearing* — hear the hesitation, the breath change, the whisper to a colleague
- Will **steel the mind** *(Will ≥ 3)* — see through manipulation and charm as they are applied
- *Sly* — introduce a false claim and watch how the other party reacts; the reaction reveals
  what they actually know

**The atmosphere collapses and someone on the other side reaches for a weapon.** Talking is
no longer the immediate option; the group must de-escalate before violence begins.
- *Relaxed* — a calm presence in the moment of crisis slows the escalation before it completes
- Might **power through** *(Might ≥ 3)* — physical presence makes drawing the weapon visibly
  inadvisable without any strike being thrown
- *Scolding* — redirect the aggression toward a specific target who is prepared for it and away
  from the fragile negotiation
- *Musical* — a sudden shift in the room's emotional tone breaks the momentum of the escalation

## Exploration

Carefully exploring an unknown, potentially dangerous place in search of treasures.

**Why tension exists:** The group has to help each other past traps, unstable ground, and unseen
hazards. Each warning called out, each hand extended across a gap, each shared light source is an
act of aid that registers on the tension track.

**When tension rises:** Traps become more likely to trigger. The structure becomes less stable —
ceilings shift, floors crack. Guardians that were dormant begin to stir. Passages that were open
are now blocked. Light and resources start to run low at the worst possible moment.

**To ease tension:** Slow down and move carefully. Use *Perception* and *Lookout* to read the
space before entering it, reducing the need for reactive aid. Use *Handiness* to disarm traps
before they become problems. Use *Labyrinth* or *Travels* to navigate confidently so the group
doesn't second-guess every turn.

**Challenges**

**A passage is blocked — rubble from a collapse, a sealed door, flooded chambers.** The route
the group planned does not exist anymore.
- *Digging* — breach through earth, rubble, or packed stone directly
- Might — force a door or shift debris that is not too deeply buried
- Hand **improvise tool** *(Hand ≥ 4)* — construct a lever, wedge, or prying tool from what
  is at hand
- *Labyrinth* — immediately identify an alternate route through the structure without backtracking

**A trap is found — or triggered — unexpectedly.** Either it needs to be disarmed before the
group proceeds, or someone is already in trouble.
- Hand **disarm** — strip the trap's mechanism before it completes; the action requires Hand
  greater than the trap's effective Dex + Might equivalent
- *Lookout* — find the trigger, the mechanism, and the safe path through before stepping onto it
- Dex **feint** *(Dex ≥ 3)* — swap position at the last moment to avoid an already-triggered
  trap; requires acting before the trap resolves
- *Ingenuity* — use a found object to trigger the trap safely from a distance

**The group loses its bearing in complex, shifting, or deliberately confusing architecture.**
Every turn looks the same; retracing is not reliable.
- *Labyrinth* — navigate and read complex layouts confidently; the primary use of this power
- *Travels* — rapidly map and orient in a new space, reducing the layout to understood geography
- Hand **improvise tool** *(Hand ≥ 4)* — mark the path physically with chalk, torn cloth, or
  scratched stone so the group cannot loop without knowing it
- *Smell* — follow a scent trail — air movement, water, recent passage — to the exit or objective

**A dormant guardian is disturbed and becomes active.** It was not on the map because it was
not moving. Now it is.
- *Shamanic* — communicate with the guardian's animating spirit; some guardians have conditions
  under which they stand down
- *Scolding* — pull its attention onto one character while others continue past
- *Adaptation* or *Night* — slip past it before it has fully oriented to the intrusion
- *Labyrinth* — lead it into a dead end and let it guard an empty passage

**The light source fails — a torch burns out, oil runs dry, magic lapses.** The group is now
in complete darkness; most members cannot see at all.
- *Night* — see clearly where others are blind; guide the group through without a light source
- *Magic* *(light element)* — bend the light card's element to produce or sustain illumination
- *Ingenuity* — improvise a light source from available materials; fat, fungus, or polished
  metal reflecting a distant glow
- *Hearing* or *Smell* — navigate by non-visual sense until a light source can be restored

**An area of the structure is dangerously unstable and will not bear the group's weight or
movement.** Crossing normally will trigger a collapse.
- *Flight* — cross without touching the unstable surface at all
- *Climber* — find anchor points on walls or ceiling and traverse without floor contact
- Endu **dig in** *(Endu ≥ 3)* — hold position through the first collapse and cross on the
  settled debris
- *Nimble* — move fast enough across the unstable surface that it does not have time to give

## Travel

Hurriedly moving through unknown terrain.

**Why tension exists:** The group is moving fast across ground nobody knows well. Helping a
companion who slips, sharing knowledge of the route, keeping a struggling member on pace — all of
it adds up across the miles.

**When tension rises:** The terrain worsens — weather closes in, the path becomes harder to
follow, a river crossing turns treacherous. The group's pace slows at exactly the moment it
needs to speed up. An encounter on the road arrives while the group is already stretched thin.

**To ease tension:** Find a landmark or waypoint that gives everyone a clear bearing — remove
the uncertainty that forces constant coordination. Use *Travels* or *Navigation* to orient the
group so each person can move independently. Use *Speed* or *Nimble* to push through and reach
safer ground before conditions deteriorate further.

**Challenges**

**Severe weather closes in — rain, snow, wind, or heat — and the terrain becomes actively
dangerous.** Progress slows; the group's physical condition degrades with every hour.
- *Fur* — absorb cold, wet, and harsh weather without penalty to pace or actions
- Endu **dig in** *(Endu ≥ 3)* — ignore the next instance of environmental damage or
  complication from the weather
- *Camp* — identify a defensible shelter before conditions peak; stop before the storm worsens
- *Travels* — know the terrain well enough to find the route that weather affects least

**The path disappears or forks without a clear indication of which way is correct.** Following
the wrong branch costs time the group may not have.
- *Travels* — primary use; orient immediately in unfamiliar terrain and move efficiently
- *Smell* — follow water, smoke, animal trails, or human scent to the inhabited or correct path
- Navigation specialization — read stars, moss, water flow, and terrain slope for orientation
- *Lookout* — spot a landmark, a distant light, or a trail marker that resolves the uncertainty

**A river, flooded plain, or body of water blocks progress and cannot easily be crossed.**
The crossing is dangerous; gear may be lost; not everyone can make it the same way.
- *Swimmer* — cross open water without delay; carry a rope for others to follow
- *Flight* — cross without touching the water; can carry one other character at lower mastery
- *Ingenuity* — construct a raft, bridge, or flotation from available materials
- *Portage* — carry the group's gear and a struggling member across together

**A group member is injured and cannot maintain pace.** Everyone slows to match them or someone
must be left, which is not an option.
- Healer specialization — treat the wound properly so the character can move at full pace again
- *Blood* — restore the injured character's capability by redirecting life energy
- *Portage* — carry the injured character rather than slowing the whole group
- *Sleeper* — the injured character rests during a halt and recovers enough to continue

**The group is being followed and cannot shake the pursuit.** Trying to outrun it burns
resources; trying to hide risks being caught in the open.
- Speed **blur** *(Speed ≥ 5)* — move and immediately withdraw; break visual contact without
  committing to a direction
- *Night* — move in darkness the pursuit cannot penetrate; cross ground they cannot safely follow
- *Sly* — leave a false trail that sends the pursuit in the wrong direction
- *Nimble* — move through terrain that cannot support a large pursuing group

**Supplies run out before the destination is reached.** The group can continue but at cost to
condition; decisions about rationing create conflict.
- *Hunter* — catch food en route without breaking the march significantly
- *Scrounger* — find provisions in unlikely places along the road; a farmhouse, an abandoned
  camp, natural sources that others would miss
- *Druid* — ask nature directly for sustenance; forage along the route using the land's
  cooperation rather than searching independently
- Char **strategy** *(Char ≥ 3)* — organise rationing so everyone moves efficiently on less;
  remove the decisions that cause conflict

## Camp

Trying to get some rest in a makeshift camp after a long and stressful day.

**Why tension exists:** Rest requires trust and stillness, but the group is in an unknown place.
Setting watches, tending wounds, calming anxious companions — every act of care keeps people from
fully unwinding, and the accumulated strain of the day sits on the camp like weight.

**When tension rises:** Rest becomes shallow and ineffective — characters don't recover as much
as they should. A sound in the dark keeps everyone on edge. A nighttime threat appears before the
group has had a chance to recover. Morale frays and interpersonal tensions surface.

**To ease tension:** Establish a defensible perimeter using *Lookout* or *Hearing* so that not
everyone has to stay half-alert. Use *Camp* to find the best possible rest spot. Use *Relaxed*
to help companions actually unwind. Use *Sleeper* to recover regardless of conditions.

**Challenges**

**The chosen campsite is on a patrol route, inside claimed territory, or already occupied.**
Staying means being found; moving means losing what little rest time exists.
- *Camp* — primary use; find the genuinely safe rest spot before the group commits to a bad one
- *Lookout* — establish the perimeter situation before setting up; notice the danger early
- *Smell* or *Hearing* — detect that the site is used before anyone else realises
- *Labyrinth* — identify a defensible position within the larger space that reduces exposure

**There is not enough natural shelter and conditions are severe.** Without protection, the group
will not recover; rest on exposed ground in bad weather is barely better than none.
- *Ingenuity* — build shelter from available materials; the action makes something from nothing
- *Camp* — find the best shelter point in the area; a rock overhang, a dense stand of trees
- *Digging* — excavate a sheltered hollow quickly; earth holds heat and blocks wind
- *Fur* — sleep comfortably in exposed conditions regardless; the power's camp use

**The watch rotation leaves the whole group exhausted — every watcher has too short a rest.**
By morning everyone is diminished rather than recovered.
- *Sleeper* — the character recovers fully regardless of shortened rest; the rotation shrinks
- *Hearing* — a character with Hearing can maintain an effective watch passively from inside a
  tent, freeing others to sleep through
- *Lookout* — a single reliable watcher replaces multiple less effective ones; reduces rotation
- Dog companion (Druid bond) — the dog maintains the watch through the night without a
  rotation; characters can sleep fully

**Conflict within the group surfaces and sours the camp.** People who are tired and scared do
not always keep their frustrations to themselves; rest becomes conditional on resolution.
- *Relaxed* — bring the emotional temperature of the camp down before the conflict locks in
- *Musical* — shift the mood around the fire before words make positions permanent
- *Politics* — find the underlying interest behind the conflict and address it directly
- Char **rallying cry** *(Char ≥ 5)* — remind the group what they are doing together; reframe
  the conflict as a shared challenge rather than an internal one

**Something finds the camp in the night before rest is complete — a predator, a patrol, a
creature drawn by the fire.** The group must respond while impaired by incomplete rest.
- *Lookout* or *Hearing* — detect the approach before it reaches camp; the group acts first
- *Scolding* — draw the threat's attention to one prepared character; others finish resting
- *Shamanic* — bind protective spirits to the camp during the night; the binding detects and
  deters threats passively
- *Night* — see the threat in darkness that hides it from everyone else; act before it acts

**A wound, poison, or condition is not healing and is getting worse overnight.** Rest alone is
not enough; without treatment, the character will be worse by morning.
- Healer specialization — the only reliable way to treat serious wounds and conditions
- *Blood* — accelerate recovery by redirecting life energy toward the affected character
- *Druid* — ask nature for medicinal materials; the land around a camp often provides what the
  group did not think to carry
- *Religion* — bless the healing; the boost applies to both the character's recovery and the
  effectiveness of any treatment applied

## Market

Walking into town to gather information, buy supplies, and hopefully meet friends.

**Why tension exists:** Towns have their own politics and factions. Every favour asked, every
contact approached, every attempt to steer a companion through a tricky social situation adds
pressure to relationships that are not yet fully established.

**When tension rises:** Prices rise or goods dry up as the group's presence becomes known. A
faction grows suspicious of what the group wants and why. A contact becomes unavailable or cold.
A misunderstanding between group members and locals threatens to become an incident.

**To ease tension:** Build goodwill slowly — buy something before asking for something. Use
*Clan* to find kindred people already inclined to help. Use *Politics* to navigate the local
power structure without antagonising anyone. Use *Musical* or *Sly* to warm the room before
making the ask.

**Challenges**

**Needed goods are unavailable, controlled, or priced beyond reach.** The thing the group came
for is not accessible through normal channels.
- *Clan* — find kindred people who have access the group does not; kin open doors that money
  cannot
- *Sly* — locate the informal market that exists beneath the official one
- Jeweler specialization — appraise what the group does have accurately and trade up from there
- *Travels* — know from prior experience where in town this kind of thing actually moves

**A key contact is cold, unavailable, or actively avoiding the group.** The person who was
supposed to help is not cooperating.
- *Politics* — understand why before approaching; the reason shapes the remedy
- *Hearing* — overhear what the contact is telling others; find out what changed
- *Musical* — warm the atmosphere before making contact; a changed emotional temperature changes
  the response
- *Sly* — approach through a third party the contact does trust rather than directly

**The group is recognised — as outsiders, as wanted, or as affiliated with the wrong faction.**
The recognition is working against them before they have said anything.
- *Adaptation* — move through a crowd without drawing attention; become background
- *Relaxed* — a calm presence that belongs; not nervous, not hurrying, not conspicuous
- *Sly* — present a different identity or affiliation convincingly before the recognition
  hardens into a problem
- *Clan* — find kindred people whose recognition of the group works in the group's favour

**A rival is buying the same scarce resource and has a head start or better connections.** The
thing the group needs will be gone before they can acquire it through normal means.
- *Sly* — redirect the rival's attention toward a false opportunity elsewhere
- Char **strategy** *(Char ≥ 3)* — move faster and with more coordination than the rival can
  match; arrive first
- *Politics* — understand the rival's position well enough to offer the seller something the
  rival cannot
- *Travels* — know the town's layout and find the supplier the rival has not reached yet

**Goods on offer are not what they appear — counterfeit, stolen, unsafe, or misrepresented.**
Taking them at face value costs the group money and possibly safety.
- Perc **called shot** *(Perc ≥ 4)* — declare the intent to detect the deception before
  committing; success reveals it
- Jeweler specialization — appraise the goods accurately; forgeries and substitutions do not
  survive this
- *Smell* — detect rot, false preservation, or the wrong material beneath a convincing surface
- *Sly* — bluff the seller into revealing the flaw by pretending to already know it

**A local faction takes offence at the group's presence — their race, their employer, their
previous actions — and the market atmosphere sours.** Other contacts become cautious.
- *Politics* — navigate the local power structure without antagonising anyone further; find
  the faction whose goodwill counterbalances the hostile one
- *Relaxed* — a calm, unthreatening presence reduces the heat before it spreads to other
  contacts
- *Clan* — find kin who are neutral or allied with neither faction; their endorsement matters
- *Musical* — shift the room's mood before the faction's influence fully poisons it

## Forage

Desperately scrounging for food in a forest because supplies are getting scarce.

**Why tension exists:** Hunger sharpens fear. The group must spread out to cover ground, which
means helping each other from a distance — calling across the undergrowth, regrouping at a
sound, sharing finds. Every coordination costs composure in a place that rewards stillness.

**When tension rises:** Predators are drawn toward the noise and movement of a foraging group.
Dangerous animals are disturbed. The group becomes more scattered and harder to reassemble. The
weather closes in. What food was found turns out to be unsafe.

**To ease tension:** Find enough food to lift the immediate desperation — success at the core
task relieves the pressure to keep searching recklessly. Use *Hunter* or *Scrounger* to find
food efficiently so the group can stop and regroup. Use *Smell* or *Druid* to locate resources
without spreading out. Use *Camp* to find a safe clearing where the group can collect itself
before continuing.

**Challenges**

**The area has been stripped — by animals, a rival group, the wrong season, or previous
passage of the group itself.** What the group expected to find is not there.
- *Scrounger* — find food and supplies where others see nothing; the power is specifically for
  this situation
- *Smell* — follow the scent of what animals have been eating; their trails lead to what remains
- *Druid* — ask nature directly; the land knows what is in it even when the group cannot see it
- *Travels* — know the territory well enough to identify where productive ground actually is

**A predator is claiming the most productive foraging ground.** Approaching it means risking
a confrontation; avoiding it means working with what is left.
- *Hunter* — understand predator behaviour and patterns; move through the territory in windows
  the predator leaves open
- *Stealth* powers (*Adaptation*, *Night*, *Scurry*) — work the ground without alerting the
  predator to the group's presence
- *Druid* — communicate with the predator or the land around it; some predators will accept
  temporary coexistence
- *Scolding* — one character draws the predator away while the rest work the productive ground

**Edible plants and poisonous ones grow side by side and are difficult to distinguish safely.**
Eating the wrong thing costs the group more than finding nothing.
- *Druid* — nature cooperates in identifying what is safe; the power's forage use
- *Smell* — the difference between edible and toxic is often a smell; detect it before touching
- Healer or Druid specialization — the background knowledge to distinguish reliably
- *Ingenuity* — test small quantities by animal reaction, boiling, or other field methods before
  committing the group to a find

**The group must separate to cover enough ground but loses the ability to coordinate.** Someone
finds something useful; getting everyone there without noise raises Tension.
- *Hearing* — detect what others in the group have found without them calling out
- *Smell* — follow a pack member's trail to their location without noise
- Falcon or raven (Druid bond) — a bird can relay location information between separated group
  members without any sound from the ground
- *Nimble* — cover ground fast enough that separation is brief; the group is never far apart

**Conditions make foraging difficult — rain obscuring plant signs, cold slowing movement, night
falling early.** The group can still search but every action costs more effort.
- *Fur* — push through cold, thorns, and cold water without slowing; the condition does not
  impose a penalty
- *Night* — see clearly in darkness others cannot; foraging continues past the point it would
  stop for everyone else
- *Swimmer* — forage in water when land-based sources are blocked or depleted; fish, shellfish,
  and water plants
- *Sleeper* — take a recovery break wherever the group is; the cost of conditions resets faster

**A find is too large or too heavy to bring back in one trip — a deer carcass, a fruit-laden
tree, a root cache.** Leaving it means losing half; multiple trips raise Tension significantly.
- *Portage* — haul the find back without the group needing to return; the power is made for
  exactly this weight
- Donkey or cart (if present) — the animal carries the bulk; no Tension cost
- *Ingenuity* — build a drag or carry frame from available wood and cord; multiply what one
  trip can move
- *Scrounger* — identify which parts of the find are most calorie-dense and worth prioritising;
  take the best, leave the rest
