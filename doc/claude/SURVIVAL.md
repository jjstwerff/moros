# Survival — Camp and Forage across the Campaign's Biomes

Moros's camp and forage scenarios (see `doc/claude/SCENARIOS.md` §"Camp" and §"Forage") assume the party is working together through stress. In most of the campaign — the kingdoms, the hills, the settled trade roads — camp and forage are **routine**: a watch is set, a fire is built, someone catches a fish, morning comes. The scenarios are present but the stakes are low.

**The stakes rise in the hostile biomes.** The Great Swamp, the blasted lands, and the southern desert are not places where camping is a mechanic the DM rolls past. They are places where **survival is the scene**, and where the cooperation the tension mechanic rewards is *the only way through*.

This file formalises the biome gradient and presents three cooperative setpieces — one per hostile biome — that demand the party split across tasks and reassemble to succeed.

## The biome gradient

A DM running camp or forage anywhere in the campaign should know where on the gradient they are.

| Biome | Camp difficulty | Forage difficulty | Cooperation demanded |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kingdom towns and farmland** (Elmsfield, Salmonswell, Linar, Scarlet Vale, Hank's farm) | Trivial — Inns, friendly hearths, safe roads | Trivial — markets, hospitality, paid meals | None beyond courtesy |
| **Hills and settled wilderness** (around Elmsfield, Corné's hill routes, Luchebert vale) | Low — standard camp mechanics apply; wild animals as the main risk | Low — a *Hunter* or *Druid* handles it | Routine — watches, a fire, shared cooking |
| **Forbidden woods** (fringe only) | Moderate — short visits at the edge; no overnight past the fringe | Impossible — do not forage here | Emotional, not mechanical — chaos-residue on the party |
| **Great Swamp** | **High** — insects, predators, fungus-poisoning. Worst camp in the near-Lake region. | **High** — most plants and frogs are fungal; water is unsafe; game is scarce | **Required** — watches, fire, repellent, fungus-protection simultaneously |
| **Blasted lands** | **Very high** — every ruin carries chaos-pressure; dreams populate; workings return warped | **Very high** — warped crops mostly inedible; water in warped streams swallows; the fields ate their farmers | **Required** — field-split and reassembly; specific powers per task |
| **Southern desert** | **Very high** — open to elementals; chaos-thick air; sand takes structure; water rare | **Highest** — virtually no natural food; water is the entire calculation | **Required** — water-discipline, shelter-coordination, chaos-pressure rotation |

**The gradient is not a curiosity.** A party pushing from hills to swamp to blasted-lands across a campaign is stepping from *camp as rest* to *camp as survival*. The DM should name the shift explicitly through the narration — a character who has slept in the hills remarks that this *is not camping* when the swamp's first night falls.

## What cooperation looks like in the hostile biomes

Across all three worst biomes, the **cooperation pattern** is the same structurally:

- **Task-split within a shared tile.** Multiple concurrent tasks the party cannot sequence — watches, fire-tending, wound-care, water-discipline, food-prep, chaos-ward, shelter-building. **All within the same world-grid tile**, or at most with one task reaching into the adjacent tile (see RULES.md §"Party scope and cooperative specialization"). Characters cover them in parallel, specializing within shared space. The "split" is *role-split*, not *geographic-split*.
- **Specialists load-bearing.** Each task rewards a specific power or stat. A *Hunter* reads ground-sign; a *Druid* vets food; a *Shamanic* character manages spirit-pressure; a *Relaxed* character holds morale. A party without *any* of these is in trouble; a party with all of them can thrive.
- **Reassembly.** The morning is the scene's end. Did everyone wake? Did anyone take fungus in their lungs? Was the fire held through the worst hour? Did the watcher hear what they had to hear? The morning reassembles the party's night into a condition — full rest, partial, interrupted, bad.

**Specialization-in-shared-space is the register**, not geographic party-split. The party is visible to each other the whole time. The forager at the far edge of the field, the water-drawer at the well, the fire-tender at the camp — all within earshot. Communication is by voice, signal, or trained companion across short distances. A member genuinely out of sight or hearing is *broken from the party* and outside this cooperative frame.

Moros's tension mechanic makes cooperation *costly* (every help raises tension). The hostile biomes make cooperation *mandatory*. The scene's texture is the tension between those two facts.

## Setpiece: Surviving a swamp night

**Biome:** Great Swamp. Close to the Lake of Tears. The party has crossed half a day into the marsh and cannot make the towers by nightfall. They must camp.

**Setup:** A high-ground island — a fifty-pace mound of turf among the reedbeds. Room for the party and a small fire. The reeds are higher than head-height on all sides. Dusk arrives loudly (the blood-gnat drone rising). The fungus-zone is close enough that the air tastes metallic.

**The tasks:**

| Task | What it prevents | Who performs it | How |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Watch** | A mud-lurker kills a sleeper | One character per third of the night; or a *Night*/*Lookout* sleeper-skipper | Eyes on the water-edge, ears through the reeds |
| **Fire-smoke screen** | Blood-gnat swarm pressure | A *Hand* or *Ingenuity* character | Wet wood-smoke; specific herb bundles Bridget stocks; smoke-direction management |
| **Fungus-protection** | Overnight lung-damage (Endu) | A *Druid*, a priest with blessed cloth (Willow's), or *Magic* (air-element) | Faces covered; a specific *Druid* working that holds spores back for a scene |
| **Morale** | Panic-contagion in the night | A *Relaxed* or *Musical* character | Quiet presence, a song, a story — pushes back against the drone and the dark |
| **Wound-tending** | A bitten or gnawed party member's condition worsening by dawn | Healer or *Blood* | Clean what the day's work has left; keep it clean through wet sleep |
| **Water-discipline** | Drinking swamp-water at 3am because everyone is parched | Anyone with *Camp* | Boiled water from daylight kept covered; a watch-turn includes a water-check |

**Run the scene across four rounds** (one hour each, per SCENARIOS.md Camp pacing):

- **Round 1 — setting up.** Tasks assigned. Fire lit. Watches drawn. Tension starts at the base level.
- **Round 2 — first watch.** Insect-drone peaks at dusk. The fire-tender's smoke management is tested. A *mud-lurker* circles the island (the watcher either hears it or doesn't). Decisions about whether to sleep at all ripple through the party.
- **Round 3 — deep watch.** Middle of the night. Morale is most fragile. The fungus-protection is most taxing. A party member wakes with a nightmare (fungus on their own skin); a *Relaxed* presence quiets them without the rest of the camp waking.
- **Round 4 — dawn.** Insect-drone rises again. The reassembly. Who slept? Who did not? What was lost to the gnats? What the fungus took.

**Outcome framework:**
- **Full cooperation, all tasks covered:** Full rest. Tension resolves cleanly.
- **Partial:** *Reduced recovery.* A specific stat did not fully restore. The scene carries weight into the next day.
- **Failed:** An encounter mid-night — a mud-lurker reaches the fire; the fungus wakes someone badly; a watch is blown. The camp ends as combat or crisis.

**What cooperation *looks like* at the table:** Simultaneous specialization within the shared island. Every character is on the same patch of dry ground, within reach of each other; the tasks *differ radically* (fire-smoke, watch, fungus-ward, morale, wound-tending) while the positions stay close. The DM should run the tasks simultaneously rather than in sequence. *"While Irna watches the water, what is Diederik doing with the fire?"* The players must *talk to each other* about task-assignment *before* the round begins. A DM can demand this explicitly: *"I need to hear four people claim four tasks before the night starts."*

## Setpiece: Foraging the blasted-lands fields

**Biome:** Blasted lands. A stretch of overgrown pasture and grain-fields, once communal, now field-flock-occupied. The party is between Commons House and the next site — supplies thin, no Inn ahead for days, nothing at the next ruin but more chaos.

**Setup:** A wide old grain-field, knee-high with the wrong grasses. A copse of fruit-trees at one edge — the trees are fruit-bearing but the fruit is *not right*. Old vines up the slope — once productive, now warped. A well in the field's centre — possibly good; possibly warped.

**The tasks:** forage is **inherently split** — the party cannot work one patch efficiently together. They must cover ground.

| Task | What it yields | Who performs it | Constraint |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Scouting edible crops** | Grain, fruit, tubers — in clean form | A *Druid* or Healer specialist | Must move through the whole field; reads clump-by-clump |
| **Reading wildlife sign** | Knowledge of whether the field-flock is present; where game might be; where *is not* safe to stand | A *Hunter* or *Smell* specialist | Must track; cannot be at the well |
| **Drawing water** | Safe drinking water | *Shamanic* at the well, or Healer with purification | The well-spirit may be warped; the check is the scene |
| **Building a cache** | Food that keeps for the next leg | *Scrounger* or *Ingenuity* | Cannot be done without the forager's finds |
| **Standing spirit-guard** | Prevents chaos-spirit residue accumulating on the foraging party | A *Shamanic* or *Druid* character on spirit-duty | Cannot do any other task; stationary |

**The cooperation-demand is spatial dispersion within one tile.** The field is a single world-grid tile; the party covers it *from the same shared space*. The forager works the grain-rows; the tracker circles the field's inside edge; the well-worker is at the centre; the spirit-guard stands at a chosen high-point with sight-lines to all three. Everyone is within voice across the field. At most, **one character (usually the tracker) may step into the adjacent tile** to read sign from the fringe — still within shouting distance, still rejoining the main party at the field's edge. Nobody is truly *apart*.

**Run the scene across three rounds** (one hour each, per SCENARIOS.md Forage):

- **Round 1 — dispersal within the tile.** The tasks spread the party across the field's interior. The tracker may step out to the fringe. A character calls something out; tension rises because the call is heard by whatever is in the copse — not because of distance, but because of the noise the coordination costs.
- **Round 2 — working.** Each task's check resolves. The forager finds a clean clump *and* a warped clump side-by-side; the tracker reads a fresh field-flock nest and returns to the field's edge to report; the well-worker diagnoses the water as bitter-but-safe; the cache-builder works on what has already been brought.
- **Round 3 — reassembly.** Reconvene at the well. Count the yield. Decide whether to push further or stop here. A **jackalope flock** arrives at the field's edge — drawn by the party's movement. Not a threat; a *signal* that the field is known to them now.

**Outcome framework:**
- **Full cooperation:** Three days' provisions per character. A cache packed. Safe water for the next leg.
- **Partial:** One task failed. One category missing — no water, or no cache, or no tracker-read. The next leg is costly.
- **Failed:** The forager ate something warped; the well was worse than it read; the field-flock arrived earlier. The party continues with *poisoning* on one member and short rations on everyone.

**What cooperation *looks like* at the table:** Simultaneous specialization within the shared tile. The DM narrates the field's *interior* from multiple positions, cutting between them. *"While Diederik reads the third-row grain, Remi is at the well-edge watching the water cloud as she lowers the pot."* Characters are never out of each other's sight. A call from one end of the field reaches the other end in seconds — and everything within earshot that was waiting in the copse hears it too.

## Setpiece: Camping in the desert

**Biome:** Southern desert. Two days out from Lastwater; a day from Fata morgana. Night is falling. The party must camp because the desert crossing cannot be done in one push.

**Setup:** A cluster of large rocks — the only feature on a flat expanse of sand. No water nearby. Any water the party has is what they brought. The air holds the chaos-pressure gradient; workings stutter. The dusk-light goes strange; **fata morganas** appear on the horizon.

**The tasks:** survival here is about **water, chaos-pressure, and shelter** — all concurrent.

| Task | What it prevents | Who performs it | How |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Water-discipline** | Dehydration overnight | *Camp* or any character with *Ingenuity* | Ration-setting; a shaded water-pot under the rocks; morning-dew collection setup |
| **Chaos-pressure rotation** | Accumulated Endu and Will damage from the desert's medium | *Shamanic* or *Druid* characters cycling a ward | One character holds the ward for a watch; another takes over; the party cannot do this with only one such character |
| **Shelter-shading** | Overnight heat-loss; dawn heat-spike | *Ingenuity* or *Hand* | Cloth rigged between rocks; a shaped windbreak; a specific configuration the party must get right |
| **Watch for dust-dervishes and lesser elementals** | Material loss; small creature taking a pack | *Lookout* or *Hearing* | Eyes outward on the sand; ears for the specific whistle-sound of an approaching dervish |
| **Morale** | The fata morganas *will* show a party member something specific | *Relaxed* or *Musical* or a priest | Presence, a song, anchoring the party to the present |

**The specific fata-morgana risk:** during the deep-night hours, the *one most vulnerable* party member sees a fata morgana of somewhere they need to be — home, a lover, a lost friend. The vision is *vivid*. Walking toward it loses them. A party without a morale-watch member may lose someone to the sand.

**Run the scene across four rounds:**

- **Round 1 — setting up.** Tasks assigned. Shade rigged. Water portioned. The dusk light goes strange and the first fata morganas appear on the horizon.
- **Round 2 — first watch.** Chaos-pressure rotation begins. The watcher reports a dust-dervish a hundred paces out; it moves along its own spiral. No contact.
- **Round 3 — deep watch.** The vulnerable party member sees their fata morgana. The morale-watcher intervenes. The chaos-pressure rotation has a gap; the ward briefly thins; workings on other tasks stutter.
- **Round 4 — dawn.** Heat-spike. The shelter holds or does not. Water-audit: how much is left? Mora of the party assessed.

**Outcome framework:**
- **Full cooperation:** All tasks held. Morning comes with water still adequate, no one lost to a fata morgana, chaos-pressure kept at bay. The party continues.
- **Partial:** One task failed. The party is walking thirsty, or with Endu down, or with a member who walked toward a vision at 3am and had to be physically brought back.
- **Failed:** A character walked into the desert and is not with the party at dawn. The day's first work is *finding them*.

**What cooperation *looks like* at the table:** The most acutely coordinated of the three setpieces. The party is physically *clustered* at the rocks — nobody wanders off in the desert, not even to check a vision. Every task is within reach of every other; the specialization is absolute but the geography is tight. The DM should force the question: *"Who is holding the ward while Char sleeps? Who is watching for dervishes while Dex is at the water-pot?"* The tasks genuinely cannot be covered by one person. A party with few specialists *should feel strained*. A party member trying to walk toward their fata morgana is walking *away from the shared space*, and the morale-watcher's intervention is *physical* — catching a hand, blocking a direction — not distant shouting.

## Running these at the table

- **Keep the party in one tile.** The cooperation these setpieces reward is *specialization-in-shared-space*, not geographic dispersion. See RULES.md §"Party scope and cooperative specialization." A DM tempted to send one character off on a distant scout while the rest work the camp is breaking the cooperative frame. A scout can go to the *adjacent tile* at hour-scale — not further. At scene-scale, everyone is on the same ground, doing radically different things, visible to each other throughout.
- **Name the biome shift when it happens.** A party entering the swamp from Elmsfield should be told, in narration, that this is not ordinary country. *"You set down your pack and already it is wet through. The gnats have found you. The reeds are higher than you are. You have half an hour of light and the dusk-drone is rising."*
- **Assign tasks aloud.** Make the players *speak the assignment*. This is the cooperative register rendered legible. *"Who is on fire-tending? Who on watch? Who on fungus-protection?"*
- **Let a task fail if nobody takes it.** A party that does not assign fungus-protection sleeps in the spores. The DM does not have to invent a penalty; the penalty is the mechanic. Moros's tension track will handle the cost.
- **Reward the cooperation specifically.** Not with treasure. With *condition*. A party that cooperated well wakes with full Endu and a clean camp; a party that did not wakes reduced. The memory of *that* morning shapes the next night.
- **These are not every-session mechanics.** The hostile-biome setpieces are **reserved for the hostile biomes**. A DM running every camp as a full survival scene exhausts the mechanic. Run routine camps as routine. Run the swamp night, the blasted-lands forage, the desert camp as the *real scenes they are*.
- **Scale up if the party becomes over-prepared.** A party that has mastered the swamp-camp through repeated visits finds the swamp easier; the DM can then raise the demand (fog, an extra mud-lurker, an encroaching field-flock blown in from the north) to keep the scenario alive.

## Closing — why this matters

The campaign's emotional register lives in *compassion under pressure*. The cooperative camp and forage setpieces put the party's compassion *under pressure that specifically rewards them working together*. A party that has survived a swamp night having distributed the work well — the watcher, the fire-tender, the fungus-carer, the moraler — has *practised* the campaign's central virtue in a scene nobody can individually carry.

This is different from combat's cooperation (flanking, protecting, covering retreats) and different from negotiation's (supporting an argument, backing a position). **Survival cooperation is domestic cooperation.** The party learns to live with each other through a bad night. That learning is what makes the campaign's *friendships* across sessions feel real.

A DM who runs these setpieces well gives the party something they cannot earn anywhere else: the memory of a night they got through together.
