# Moros — Campaign: The Lake of Tears

*A multi-session opening campaign for 4–6 players starting at Elmsfield.*

---

## Background

### The Overseas War

A few years before the campaign begins, ships arrived from across the ocean. The lands
beyond the sea are ruled by mages whose power is largely unchecked — chaotic spirits are
rare there, so workings do not unravel as they do here. But their lands are poorly
cultivated and their people struggle to grow enough food. They came for resources.

The war that followed was brief and inconclusive. It ended not through diplomacy or
decisive battle but through a single unlikely event: John Bean, a boar folk farmer and
ruin-crawler, inadvertently killed the invasion's main instigator. The story of how is
tangled — John lost an arm to an ancient machine in a collapsed tower, gained an ironwood
replacement from a white mouse named Rhianna Linthrope, and ended up in the right place
at the worst time. He is not a hero in any clean sense. He is a stubborn man who survived
things that should have killed him.

The soldiers did not all go home. Some settled in the towns they had occupied. Others
turned pirate, establishing a nest along the southern coast. The war's residue — stranded
soldiers, displaced families, grudges, and unanswered questions — is the soil the campaign
grows from.

### Irna Bean

Irna is John Bean's daughter. She was imprisoned in a mine on account of a political
mishap involving the second prince of Allondo. Her brother has been proclaimed a "valiant
spirit" by the church — a proclamation that originated from Luchebert Monastery and Father
Elliot. Whether this is genuine prophecy, political convenience, or a mistake depends on
who you ask. Rhianna Linthrope arranged Irna's release and sent her toward the monastery
with a letter asking Father Elliot to correct the trouble he caused.

Irna never reached the monastery. She was searching for a small vale leading past Laurent's
downed city toward Luchebert, but could not find the entrance. Instead she wandered near
the Lake of Tears and was exposed to something that left her feverish, half-blind, and
barely alive. Her dog guided her to Elmsfield — the nearest settlement — where the campaign
begins.

### Laurent's Downed City

During the rift, Laurent pledged his flying castle as a refuge. His castle carried survivors
for years. But all flying castles eventually fell — the rift's disruption to mage workings
saw to that. Laurent's city crashed in the hills near the Lake of Tears. Laurent himself
persists as a demi-lich within the ruins, neither fully dead nor meaningfully alive. His
city is a ruin of enormous scale, full of old mage-era construction, iron puppets that may
or may not still function, and whatever Laurent has become in the centuries since.

### Felicia and Luchebert Monastery

Felicia, Laurent's partner in the evacuation, did not survive in body. She is now a great
spirit — one of the vast, diffuse presences the church reaches toward in prayer. Her shrine
is at Luchebert Monastery, tended by Father Elliot and a small order. The monastery sits in
a vale beyond the downed city, reachable through a passage that Irna failed to find.

### The Lake of Tears

The lake sits in a depression fed by a small river from Elmsfield. Around it stand six
wizard towers, erected by the overseas faction during the war. Their purpose: to capture a
giant air elemental. This elemental once powered Laurent's flying city. When the city
crashed, the elemental was trapped — and the overseas mages, recognising what it was, built
the towers to bind it permanently.

The elemental wants to be free. Its struggle fuels a fungal growth spreading outward from
the lake — a crystalline fungus that builds up on skin and in eyes, fed by the elemental's
desperate exertions against its cage. This is what poisoned Irna.

### The Buried Tower

The tower closest to the lake is almost completely buried in sand. It is being consumed by
the fungus spreading from the lake. This tower is not a wizard's study — it is an old
furnace built to create pot-people (small iron puppet constructs). The furnace itself is a
large pot that can move if it needs to. Its exhaust system fights back against the fungus,
blasting it away — but this also spreads fungal spores into the surrounding environment,
which is what contaminated the area and ultimately poisoned Irna.

Inside the tower, a small pot-person may still be active.

## Session 1 — An Unexpected Guest

### Setup

The players are in Elmsfield. They live here or are visiting — they are not yet a group.
Elmsfield is a border town built around a huge tree. It is small enough that a stranger
arriving attracts notice.

### The Arrival

**Scenario type:** Cure (opening into market/negotiation)

Irna Bean stumbles into town at the main gate. She is slumped against a post, feverish,
her cloak pulled tight. A large grey-muzzled dog stands between her and anyone who
approaches. The dog has already driven off two locals. A third is standing at a careful
distance, unsure what to do.

Irna's condition is visible and alarming: crystalline growths on her skin, a film over her
eyes, laboured breathing. She cannot speak.

**Farmer Hendrik** (a local) reacts with fear rather than compassion. He has seen a cholera
outbreak in the aftermath of the last war and fears contamination. He is loud about it. His
fear is not irrational — he watched people die — but it risks turning the town against
helping Irna before anyone understands what is wrong with her.

This is the players' entry point. They are bystanders who can choose to act.

### Crowd Management

**Scenario type:** Negotiation

As the players get involved — calming the dog, examining Irna, managing Hendrik's fear —
the crowd grows. This is where they meet **Elder Diederik**, the local elder. Diederik is
an owl with druid training who was exiled from his druidic order. He has knowledge of
ironwood, of old workings, and of the natural world. He carries authority in Elmsfield.

Diederik can help with crowd management — he has standing and calm that the players likely
do not yet have. He is the first contact the group builds.

**Challenge:** Convince the crowd that Irna is not contagious (she is not — the fungus
requires prolonged direct exposure, not casual contact). Hendrik must be addressed directly;
ignoring his fear lets it spread.

- *Relaxed* — calm Hendrik before his fear infects others
- *Politics* — identify that Hendrik's real concern is the town's safety, not Irna herself;
  address that directly
- Healer specialization — diagnose that this is not cholera or any communicable disease
- *Druid* — recognise the crystalline growth as fungal, not bacterial; it does not spread
  through air or contact

### Saving Irna

**Scenario type:** Cure

Irna can be saved with care, but she cannot communicate. Diederik examines her and
recognises the fungal growths. He needs the source material — the fungus itself — to
compound a treatment. He knows this is something from the land, not from a person, but he
does not know where Irna was or what she encountered.

**What the players can learn in Elmsfield:**
- Irna came from the south, along the river
- She carries a letter from Rhianna Linthrope to Father Elliot (if they can get past the
  dog — Hand check or *Druid* to calm the animal)
- The letter mentions Luchebert Monastery and Father Elliot
- Locals know the river leads to the Lake of Tears, but most avoid it — the area has a
  reputation for strange ailments and bad air
- Diederik has heard of the wizard towers but has never visited them

**Session 1 ends** when the group decides to travel south toward the Lake of Tears to find
the fungus and save Irna. Diederik stays in Elmsfield to keep Irna alive.

### Key NPCs — Session 1

| NPC | Race | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Irna Bean | Boar folk | The patient | Cannot communicate; carries the letter; her dog is fiercely protective |
| Elder Diederik | Owl | Local elder, druid | Exiled from druidic order; knowledge of ironwood and natural medicine; first contact (level 2) |
| Farmer Hendrik | — | Fearful local | Not a villain; a man with trauma from the last war's aftermath |
| Irna's dog | — | Guardian | Grey-muzzled, loyal, aggressive to strangers; can be calmed with *Druid* or patience |

## Session 2 — The River South

### Travel to the Lake

**Scenario type:** Travel

The group follows the small river south from Elmsfield toward the Lake of Tears. The
journey is half a day on foot — 4–5 travel rounds.

**Terrain:** The river valley narrows as it approaches the lake. The vegetation becomes
denser and stranger — fungal growths appear on trees and rocks, increasing as the group
gets closer. The air takes on a metallic quality.

### The Bear Cubs

**Scenario type:** Exploration (shifting to combat or negotiation)

Before reaching the lake, the group encounters two young bear cubs. They are thin, dirty,
and unfed. They are not aggressive — they are frightened and alone. Their behaviour tells
the story: their mother has stopped caring for them.

**Challenge:** The cubs are a warning. Approaching them is safe. Following their trail leads
to their mother.

- *Hunter* or *Smell* — track the mother's location from the cubs' trail
- *Druid* — communicate with the cubs; they are confused and hungry, not hostile
- *Relaxed* — approach without alarming them

### The Mother Bear

**Scenario type:** Combat (avoidable)

The mother bear is raging with the same affliction as Irna — crystalline fungal growths on
her skin and over her eyes, half-blind and in pain. She is not hunting. She is lashing out
at everything that comes near. She has been exposed longer than Irna and is further gone.

**This fight can be avoided.** The mother is not pursuing prey — she is defending a
territory she can barely see. The group can:

- *Druid* — reach the animal beneath the pain; calm her enough to pass
- *Scolding* — draw her attention and lead her away from the group's path
- *Stealth* powers — slip past while she rages at shadows
- Fight her — she is a brown bear (see CREATURES.md) with impaired Perc and Dex from the
  fungal growths; she hits hard but cannot track well

**If the group fights:** The bear is not evil. Killing her is not wrong — she is suffering
and dangerous — but players who find another way should feel that choice mattered. The cubs
need her.

**If the group has a healer or druid:** They may realise the bear needs the same cure as
Irna. This becomes relevant after the tower.

### Reaching the Lake

**Scenario type:** Exploration

The Lake of Tears is visible from a ridge above the river's end. The six wizard towers
stand around it at roughly even intervals — tall, narrow structures of foreign
construction, clearly not built by anyone from this land. Most are intact. The closest
one is almost completely buried in sand, with fungal growth climbing its walls and
spreading outward across the ground toward the lake.

The fungus is thickest between the buried tower and the lake shore. The air here is heavy
with spores. Prolonged exposure (more than one scene without protection) begins the same
process that afflicted Irna — Endu damage accumulating each round.

**What the group can observe:**
- The buried tower's exhaust vents periodically blast hot air, scattering fungus — and
  spreading spores into the surrounding area
- The fungus grows toward the lake, not away from it — it is drawn to something there
- The other five towers are intact and distant, their purpose not immediately clear
- The air above the lake shimmers — not heat, something else; a mage or shaman recognises
  this as a bound elemental straining against confinement

## Session 3 — The Buried Tower

### Approaching the Tower

**Scenario type:** Exploration

The buried tower is half-submerged in sand, with fungal growth covering its exposed upper
sections. The exhaust vents fire periodically — the pattern is roughly regular but not
perfectly predictable.

**Challenge: Reaching the entrance.** The entrance is partially buried. Fungal growth
covers the approach. The exhaust vents create windows of clear air between blasts, but
the timing is tight.

- *Digging* — clear the sand from the entrance directly
- Speed **sprint** — time the exhaust blasts and move between them
- *Fur* or *Hide* — push through the fungal zone with reduced exposure
- *Ingenuity* — improvise face coverings or breathing protection from available materials
  (reduces Endu damage from spores)

### Inside the Tower

**Scenario type:** Exploration (potentially repair)

The tower interior is a furnace — literally. This is not a wizard's library or study. It
is a workshop built to create pot-people, small iron puppet constructs. The central feature
is a large pot (the furnace) that serves as both the forge and the heart of the operation.
The pot can move if it needs to — it has stubby legs and a will of its own, though it has
not moved in a very long time.

The furnace exhaust is what fights the fungus outside. It was not designed for this — the
exhaust is a byproduct of the furnace's normal operation, and the furnace is running on
minimal power because no one has tended it in years. The fungus is winning.

**What the group finds:**
- Fungus samples growing on the tower's inner walls — enough for Diederik to compound
  Irna's cure
- The furnace, dormant but not dead — a *Shamanic* character or one with Magic can sense
  the spirit bound into it
- Tools and materials for pot-person construction, most degraded
- Records of the tower's owner — an overseas mage whose name and purpose can be pieced
  together from surviving notes
- A small pot-person, still active, hiding in a corner of the workshop

### The Pot-Person

The pot-person is small — knee-height — and very old. It has been alone in this tower since
its creator left (or died, or was recalled across the ocean — it does not know which). It
has a spirit bound into it, like all iron puppets, and centuries of solitude have given that
spirit something close to personality: cautious, methodical, deeply attached to the tower
and its furnace.

It does not trust the group immediately. It has not seen people in a very long time, and the
last people it saw were overseas mages who treated it as a tool.

**Building trust:**
- *Shamanic* — communicate with the bound spirit directly; acknowledge it as a person
- *Ingenuity* or Hand — help repair something in the tower; demonstrate competence and
  goodwill
- *Relaxed* — patience and calm; do not rush it; let it observe the group
- *Politics* — recognise that it has interests (the tower, the furnace, survival) and
  address those

**If the group earns the pot-person's trust:** It becomes a contact (level 1) and a source
of information about the towers, the overseas mages, and the air elemental. It knows the
towers were built to trap the elemental. It knows the fungus is getting worse. It does not
know how to stop it.

### Rescuing the Tower

**Scenario type:** Repair (optional)

The group can attempt to rescue the tower by clearing the fungus and restoring the furnace
to fuller operation. This is not required for the main objective (they already have the
fungus samples for Irna's cure) but it earns the pot-person's deep gratitude and
establishes a base of operations near the lake for future sessions.

**Challenge: Clear the fungus.** The fungus is extensive. Cutting it back buys time;
burning it is more effective but risks the tower itself.

- *Magic* (flame) — controlled burn of the fungus around the tower exterior
- *Druid* — ask nature to reclaim the ground; fungus is not natural growth and the land
  may cooperate
- Axes specialization — systematic cutting; effective but slow
- *Ingenuity* — redirect the furnace exhaust to target the densest fungal growth

**Challenge: Restore the furnace.** The furnace needs fuel, maintenance, and possibly a
more skilled hand than anyone present.

- *Ingenuity* or Machinist specialization — diagnose what the furnace needs and provide it
- Hand **improvise tool** — create replacement parts for degraded mechanisms
- The pot-person can direct the work if it trusts the group — it knows the furnace
  intimately

## Session 4 — Return and Recovery

### Curing Irna

**Scenario type:** Cure

The group returns to Elmsfield with fungus samples. Diederik compounds the cure. Irna
recovers over 1–2 days — slowly. As the treatment works, the crystalline growths recede
and her vision clears.

**What Irna can tell the group when she recovers:**
- She was looking for the vale passage to Luchebert Monastery
- She could not find the entrance — it was overgrown or hidden
- She wandered near the lake and the buried tower
- She does not know what the towers are or what poisoned her
- She needs to reach Father Elliot — the letter explains why
- She mentions her father (John Bean) and her brother, but does not explain the full
  political situation unless pressed

### The Mother Bear (optional)

If the group wants to cure the mother bear, they can return with treatment. This is a
meaningful side objective that demonstrates the fungus affects the whole region, not just
one unlucky traveller.

## Sessions 5+ — The Vale and Beyond

With Irna mobile, the campaign opens outward. The following threads are available:

### The Monastery Road

Irna needs to reach Luchebert Monastery. The route passes through the vale beyond
Laurent's downed city. Finding the vale entrance is the first challenge — Irna could not
find it, and the passage may be deliberately hidden or naturally overgrown.

**Scenario types:** Travel, exploration

### Laurent's Downed City

The crashed flying castle is an enormous ruin in the hills near the lake. Laurent persists
inside as a demi-lich. The city contains old mage-era construction, possibly functional
iron puppets, and whatever Laurent has been doing for the past several centuries. Entering
is dangerous. Laurent may or may not be hostile — he was a man who chose to help everyone;
what a demi-lich remembers of that choice is unknown.

**Scenario types:** Exploration, negotiation, combat

### Luchebert Monastery

Father Elliot tends Felicia's shrine. The monastery is small and isolated. A couple of
young farmers from the nearby area have a quest there — a practical problem that gives the
group something to do while Irna's business with Father Elliot plays out.

**Scenario types:** Negotiation, market, exploration

### The Other Towers

Five wizard towers remain around the Lake of Tears. Each was built by a different overseas
mage. Each has its own character, its own defences, and its own secrets. The towers form
the cage that holds the air elemental — understanding them is the key to the larger
question of what to do about the elemental and the spreading fungus.

**Scenario types:** Exploration, repair, combat

### The Hermit Shaman

One of the towers is inhabited by a hermit — an old shaman who has been living there for
reasons of their own. The shaman understands the towers, the elemental, and possibly the
overseas mages better than anyone the group has met. Whether they are willing to share that
understanding depends on how the group approaches.

**Scenario types:** Negotiation, exploration

## DM Notes

### Scenario Progression

The campaign naturally introduces scenario types in order:

| Session | Primary scenarios | New mechanics introduced |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cure, negotiation | Tension in social situations; crowd as challenge |
| 2 | Travel, exploration, combat (optional) | Travel pace; environmental damage; avoidable fights |
| 3 | Exploration, repair (optional) | Dungeon exploration; NPC trust-building; fungal hazard |
| 4 | Cure (resolution) | Payoff for earlier gathering; information from a recovering NPC |
| 5+ | All types available | Full system in play |

### Tone

The campaign opens with compassion, not combat. The first decision is whether to help a
stranger. The first antagonist is fear, not a monster. The bear encounter offers a choice
between violence and patience. The pot-person rewards curiosity and kindness. This sets the
tone for Moros: a world where the most dangerous thing is usually not the creature in front
of you, but the decision you make about it.

### The Fungus as Recurring Threat

The fungus is not a one-session problem. Clearing it from the buried tower buys time. The
real source — the air elemental's struggle against its cage — persists until the elemental
question is resolved. This gives the group a reason to engage with the towers, the overseas
mages' legacy, and eventually the elemental itself. The fungus is the clock. It gets worse
if ignored.
