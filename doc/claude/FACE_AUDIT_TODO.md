# Face-first audit — work plan

**Purpose:** add specific face + condition + reason-to-care to setpieces that currently read as scenario-mechanics.

**This file is temporary.** When every Tier 1 and Tier 2 item is done, delete it. The *principles* the list embodies are preserved permanently:

- **Face-first principle** and **involvement-by-phase gradient** live at `doc/claude/COORDINATION_ROADMAP.md` §"Face-first — the emotional anchor of every tension moment" and, in shorter form, at `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` DM Notes §"Face-first — the emotional anchor."
- **Creature-of-the-week policy** lives at `doc/claude/CREATURES.md` §"Creature usage — early freshness vs late-game fatigue."

The pattern every item below closes against:

> *A named person or being, in a named situation, whose loss the party would feel.*

## Involvement-by-phase — the gradient

The party's emotional investment with a face grows across the campaign. Early, every face is a stranger; late, every encounter should carry accumulated weight. The face-first work must land in whichever slot the item actually sits.

| Phase | Sessions | Expected involvement | What the face must do |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Opening** | 1–3 | Low / fresh — strangers meeting | The face itself *creates* investment; compassion-first register does the work |
| **Mid** | 4–8 | Building — returning NPCs, remembered places | The face connects to earlier scenes; returns deepen |
| **Deep** | 9+ | High / accumulated — histories, commitments, losses | Every encounter must leverage what has been built; new faces carry *inherited* weight |

Involvement is **fresh** here. The face carries the scene by itself.

- [ ] **Swamp night** — reframe around *carrying Irna through* the insects and mud-lurkers while she is dying. Not a generic hostile camp; *her* night. **Involvement: low** (party just met her Session 1); the face-first framing *builds* that investment by making her survival the stake. Refs: `doc/claude/SURVIVAL.md` §"Surviving a swamp night"; `doc/npcs/irna_bean.md`.
- [ ] **Chimney descent (Session 3 opener)** — open from the **pot-boy's** perspective below: footsteps he has not heard in a millennium; his decision to hide as a pot; the tool he forgets to put down. **Involvement: none** (first contact); the face-first opening is what creates the initial bond. Refs: `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"Session 3"; `doc/claude/COORDINATION_ROADMAP.md` §"Buried Tower"; `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`.
- [ ] **Pot-boy's first drawings** — name the specific first four or five images he chooses (pot, window, question mark, hand reaching down). **Involvement: none** (first contact). Refs: `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`.
- [ ] **Furnace restoration** — add a *remembered voice* per round: a specific name, a specific hand on its iron, a specific pride, a specific grief. Centuries of work becomes its first conversation. **Involvement: building** (party has now spent time with the pot-boy; restoration is the first deepening). Refs: `doc/npcs/pot_person.md`; `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At the Great Thresh" as register parallel.
- [ ] **Mother bear + cubs** — differentiate the two cubs' conditions (one still fights; one has gone quiet). Party's choice of which to focus on shapes the mother's response. **Involvement: none** (strangers' suffering); face carries scene. Refs: `doc/claude/CAMPAIGN.md` §"Session 2 — The Bear Cubs / The Mother Bear"; `doc/claude/CREATURES.md`.

## Tier 2 — before towers / blasted lands / desert arcs run

Involvement is **building or deep** by now. Items should leverage accumulated context.

- [ ] **Half-Tower lord** — name him, his wife, his children. The journal gives the party *those specific people*. **Involvement: none direct** (long dead); **retrospective** via journal — a family lost he could not save. Refs: `doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At the Half-Tower".
- [ ] **Three Walls families** — name one or two settler households; well-cache belongs to a specific grandmother; a specific heirloom in a specific house. **Involvement: variable** — none unless party has met descendants in hill-country villages, then **clan-tie transfer** raises it to medium. Refs: `doc/places/southern_desert.md` §"At Three Walls".
- [ ] **Volcano fire elemental — name it.** Heat-pattern register (*"slow-burner"*, *"keeper-of-stones"*). **Involvement: zero-to-building across multi-visit trust arc**; the name is the landing moment when trust has been earned. Refs: `doc/places/volcano_tower.md` §"Open questions".
- [ ] **Sinking tower owner-spirit — name him.** **Involvement: zero-to-high** (name-as-trust-gate — given when earned, after the descent conversation). Refs: `doc/places/sinking_tower.md` §"Open questions".
- [ ] **Silvertrace wooden-man — name the Haven-kin.** Specific puppet-name on the arm the party can return to Haven. **Involvement: transferred from Haven** — if the party has standing there, the puppet's loss *is Haven's loss*. Without Haven, the face is a mystery-shape. Refs: `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Silvertrace"; `doc/places/scattered_kin.md`.
- [ ] **Darkness tower light elemental** — specific movement, release-gesture, gratitude-sign. Not "pale being". **Involvement: brief encounter only**; the face must work fast. Refs: `doc/places/darkness_tower.md`.
- [ ] **Commons House cerberus** — name it, or its three heads, or the community it still guards (e.g. *"Hollen's gate"*). **Involvement: zero direct**; the weight comes from the *duty still performed* and the community *no longer here*. Refs: `doc/places/blasted_lands.md` §"At Commons House".

## Creature-of-the-week — fine early, stale late

Scenarios where the encounter is the creature itself with no deeper face attached. **Fresh Sessions 1–5; becoming stale Sessions 6–10; avoid Sessions 11+.**

### Appropriate for early sessions (Sessions 1–5)

| Scenario | Source | Why it works early |
|---|---|---|
| **Wolf pack on the road** | CREATURES.md §"Common Beasts" | Natural-world threat; party is still learning the world |
| **Bandit ambush** | CREATURES.md §"Humanoid Threats" | Desperate humans; establishes Moros's compassion-first test |
| **Wandering graftbeast** | CREATURES.md §"Chaotic Spirit Constructs" | Introduces rift-ecology; confused rather than evil |
| **Dust-dervish crossing the path** | CREATURES.md §"Creatures of the Southern Desert" | Environmental register; moveable-through |
| **A single encroachment (hearth-dweller, door-kept)** | CREATURES.md §"Encroachments" | First contact with a settled-land threat; compassion-first test |

Each of these can be a session's combat without needing a face — the first time. The party meets the world.

### What takes them out of creature-of-the-week by mid-campaign

Any of these elevates a creature-of-the-week encounter into a face-first scene:

- **A name.** *"That's the wolf the rangers know — she has two pups in the south range."*
- **A specific cause.** *"These bandits are Brumal refugees who did not reach Hank's in time."*
- **Repeat context.** *"The graftbeast from last autumn has come back to the same valley."*
- **Inheritance.** *"The encroachment at the Miller place has children in it now."*
- **Connection.** *"The dust-dervish is the one Nadine sent a message with two weeks ago."*

### Late-campaign rule (Sessions 11+)

**If an encounter does not carry a face or an accumulated stake, redesign it or skip it.** A party that has been through the swamp-night with Irna, the pot-people's restoration, the Laurent audience, should not be presented with "another monster" as a combat. The register the campaign has built would be wasted.

**Exceptions are fine** — travel through the forbidden woods' fringe will still meet Tod; a caravan on the Linar road might still be ambushed by desperate drifters. These work *because* they sit in established threads (Tod is known; the bandits are Brumal-desperate). The test: can the DM name *why this particular creature appears here, now, to these characters?* If yes, the scene is face-first. If no, it is stale.

## Estimated work

- **Tier 1:** ~half a day of focused writing.
- **Tier 2:** ~half a day — mostly naming, small scene-additions.
- **Tier 3:** none (by design).
- **Creature-of-the-week policy:** ongoing, not a one-off task. Flag in session-prep notes.

## Pattern check

An item closes when its setpiece or place-file shows, concretely:

- a **name**,
- a **condition** (specific to them, not general),
- a **reason the party would feel their loss** — weighted by the involvement-phase the encounter sits in.
